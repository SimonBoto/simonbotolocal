## Core Concepts Extracted
**Coronary artery calcium (CAC) scoring stratifies statin benefit** — individuals with CAC = 0 or minimal plaque (<100) derive no benefit from statins, while those with CAC >400 or high-risk plaque features show clear benefit; CAC scoring helps avoid overtreatment in people without atherosclerotic disease and target treatment to those with significant established disease.

## Zettels Extracted

### 1. CAC Score Zero Indicates No Statin Benefit — Avoid Overtreatment
- **Claim:** A coronary artery calcium (CAC) score of zero indicates no benefit from statin therapy — individuals without detectable atherosclerotic plaque do not derive cardiovascular risk reduction from statins, making CAC scoring valuable for avoiding overtreatment in low-risk individuals.
- **Evidence:** JACC Cardiovasc Imaging 2025: CAC = 0 → no statin benefit; CAC 1-100 (minimal plaque) → no benefit from statins; rationale: statins work by stabilizing existing plaque and reducing plaque progression — if no plaque exists, there is no target for statin action; overtreatment common: many low-risk individuals prescribed statins based on LDL alone; CAC = 0 predicts very low 10-year CVD risk regardless of LDL; radiation dose from CAC scan is minimal (~1 mSv, less than annual background); cost-effective for risk stratification; clinical implication: CAC = 0 can reassure patients and avoid unnecessary medication.
- **Reference:** [[Szilveszter_2025_CAC_Statin_Benefit_R-002-0060]] — cited as (Szilveszter et al., JACC 2025)
- **Zettel:** [[CAC_Score_Zero_No_Statin_Benefit_Z-002-0825]]

### 2. CAC Score Above 400 Indicates Clear Statin Benefit
- **Claim:** CAC scores above 400 indicate clear benefit from statin therapy — individuals with moderate to extensive plaque burden, artery stenosis ≥50%, high-risk (vulnerable/unstable) plaque, or multi-vessel disease (>4 vessels) show significant cardiovascular risk reduction with statin treatment.
- **Evidence:** Clear benefit when: CAC >400 (moderate/extensive plaque), artery stenosis ≥50% (any CAC), high-risk plaque features (vulnerable/unstable — any CAC), multi-vessel disease >4 vessels (any CAC); CAC 101-399 (mild plaque): benefit not statistically significant; mechanism: statins stabilize existing plaque, reduce inflammation, prevent plaque rupture; in established disease, benefits outweigh risks; absolute risk reduction greater in high-risk individuals; number needed to treat (NNT) lowest in CAC >400 group; clinical implication: CAC >400 or high-risk features = clear indication for statin therapy.
- **Reference:** [[Szilveszter_2025_CAC_Statin_Benefit_R-002-0060]] — cited as (Szilveszter et al., JACC 2025)
- **Zettel:** [[CAC_Above_400_Clear_Statin_Benefit_Z-002-0826]]

### 3. CAC Stratification Avoids Overtreatment and Targets Therapy Appropriately
- **Claim:** CAC score stratification helps avoid overtreatment in individuals without atherosclerotic plaque and appropriately target treatment to those with significant established disease — personalizing statin therapy based on actual plaque burden rather than population risk estimates.
- **Evidence:** Problem with current approach: statins prescribed based on 10-year risk calculators (FRAMINGHAM, ASCVD) which estimate population risk, not individual plaque burden; many low-risk individuals overtreated, many high-risk individuals undertreated; CAC solution: directly visualizes coronary atherosclerosis; CAC = 0: avoid statins (no target for drug action); CAC 1-100: lifestyle intervention preferred; CAC 101-399: individualized decision (consider age, other risk factors); CAC >400 or high-risk features: clear statin indication; benefit: personalized medicine — treat those who benefit, avoid treating those who don't; cost-effective: CAC scan cost <1 year statin cost; radiation minimal; predictive value high; aligns with "test, don't guess" approach to CVD prevention.
- **Reference:** [[Szilveszter_2025_CAC_Statin_Benefit_R-002-0060]] — cited as (Szilveszter et al., JACC 2025)
