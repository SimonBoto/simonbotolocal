# Fatty Acid Structure, Classification, and Omega System

> **Source:** ChatGPT Conversation CGPT-0586 (August 30, 2023)  
> **Key Themes:** Fatty acid chemistry, omega classification, chain length, saturation, health implications

---

## Core Concepts Extracted

### 1. Basic Fatty Acid Structure

**General Formula:**
```
CH₃(CH₂)nCOOH
```

**Components:**
- **Methyl end (ω/omega):** CH₃- (start of counting)
- **Hydrocarbon chain:** -(CH₂)n-
- **Carboxyl group:** -COOH (acid functional group)

**Saturated Example (Palmitic acid):**
```
CH₃(CH₂)₁₄COOH  or  C16H32O₂
```

**Unsaturated Example (Oleic acid):**
```
CH₃(CH₂)₇CH=CH(CH₂)₇COOH  or  C18H34O₂
```

### 2. Classification by Saturation

#### Saturated Fatty Acids (SFAs)
**Structure:** No double bonds (all single C-C bonds)
**Formula:** CnH₂nO₂
**Physical:** Solid at room temperature (fats)
**Examples:**
- Palmitic acid (C16:0) — 16 carbons, 0 double bonds
- Stearic acid (C18:0)

**Health Notes:**
- Elevated SFAs associated with cardiovascular risk
- But: Not all SFAs behave the same (stearic acid neutral)
- Context matters (carbohydrate intake, overall diet)

#### Monounsaturated Fatty Acids (MUFAs)
**Structure:** One double bond
**Formula:** CnH₂n₋₂O₂
**Physical:** Liquid at room temperature (oils)
**Examples:**
- Oleic acid (C18:1) — Found in olive oil
- Palmitoleic acid (C16:1)

**Health Notes:**
- Cardiovascular protective
- Central to Mediterranean diet benefits

#### Polyunsaturated Fatty Acids (PUFAs)
**Structure:** Two or more double bonds
**Formula:** CnH₂n₋₂ₓO₂ (x = number of double bonds)
**Physical:** Liquid at room temperature, easily oxidized
**Examples:**
- Linoleic acid (C18:2) — Omega-6
- Alpha-linolenic acid (C18:3) — Omega-3

**Health Notes:**
- Essential fatty acids (cannot be synthesized)
- Precursors to eicosanoids (signaling molecules)
- Omega-3: Anti-inflammatory
- Omega-6: Pro-inflammatory (in excess)

---

### 3. The Omega (ω) Classification System

**Definition:** Position of first double bond from methyl (omega) end

| Omega Class | First Double Bond | Key Members | Primary Sources |
|-------------|-------------------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Omega-3** | C3-C4 | EPA, DHA, ALA | Fish, flaxseed, walnuts |
| **Omega-6** | C6-C7 | Linoleic acid, Arachidonic acid | Vegetable oils, nuts |
| **Omega-7** | C7-C8 | Palmitoleic acid | Macadamia nuts, sea buckthorn |
| **Omega-9** | C9-C10 | Oleic acid | Olive oil, avocados |

**Critical Ratio:**
- Historical human diet: Omega-6:Omega-3 ≈ 1:1 to 4:1
- Modern Western diet: Omega-6:Omega-3 ≈ 15:1 to 20:1
- Target ratio: < 4:1 for optimal health

---

### 4. Classification by Chain Length

| Category | Carbons | Examples | Metabolic Fate |
|----------|---------|----------|----------------|
| **Short-chain** | < 6 | Butyrate (C4) | Direct absorption, gut health |
| **Medium-chain** | 6-12 | Caprylic (C8), Capric (C10), Lauric (C12) | Rapid absorption, ketogenesis |
| **Long-chain** | 13-21 | Palmitic (C16), Oleic (C18) | Standard metabolism |
| **Very long-chain** | > 22 | DHA (C22), Nervonic acid (C24) | Specialized functions |

---

### 5. Cis vs Trans Configuration

**Cis:**
- Hydrogens on same side of double bond
- Natural configuration
- Creates "kink" in chain
- Liquid at room temperature

**Trans:**
- Hydrogens on opposite sides
- Mostly industrial (hydrogenation)
- Straight chain (like saturated)
- Solid at room temperature
- **Health concern:** Associated with cardiovascular disease
