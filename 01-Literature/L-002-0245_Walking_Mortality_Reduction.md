# Lit: Walking Reduces All-Cause Mortality Risk by More Than Half

## Source
- **Original:** Facebook Posts Collection, Article 65
- **Reference:** JAMA Network Open. 2023. DOI: 10.1001/jamanetworkopen.2023.5174
- **Type:** Prospective cohort study
- **Language:** Greek post → English extraction

## Reference
[[R-002-0035]] | Paluch AE, et al. Association of Daily Step Patterns With Mortality in US Adults. JAMA Netw Open. 2023.

## Key Finding

**8,000 steps taken 1-2 times per week reduces all-cause mortality risk by over 50% over 10 years:**
- **70% reduction in men**
- **50% reduction in women**

Increasing to 3-7 days/week provides additional risk reduction.

## Clinical Significance

This represents a benefit of enormous magnitude. Few interventions in medicine demonstrate effect sizes of this order.

The study is particularly significant because it shows that even those who cannot walk daily achieve impressive health benefits from 8,000 steps just once or twice weekly.

## Practical Recommendations

- Take stairs whenever possible
- Take 5-minute walking breaks throughout the day
- Walk around the block
- Park farther from home or work
- Gradually increase daily walking
- Even if 8,000 steps isn't achievable daily, add as much walking as possible

## Key Insight

> "The human body functions better when it moves."
