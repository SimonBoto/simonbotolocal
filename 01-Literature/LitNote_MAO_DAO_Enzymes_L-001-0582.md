# MAO and DAO Enzymes — Monoamine and Diamine Oxidase Function and Interactions

## Source

- **Date:** November 04, 2024
- **Scope:** MAO (monoamine oxidase) and DAO (diamine oxidase) enzymes, drug interactions

---

## Key Enzymes

### MAO (Monoamine Oxidase)

**Types:**
- **MAO-A:** Metabolizes serotonin, norepinephrine, epinephrine
- **MAO-B:** Metabolizes dopamine (primary), phenylethylamine

**Location:**
- Mitochondrial outer membrane
- Brain, liver, intestinal mucosa, platelets

**Function:**
- Neurotransmitter degradation
- Dietary amine metabolism
- Drug metabolism

### DAO (Diamine Oxidase)

**Also Known As:**
- Histaminase
- Amiloride-binding protein

**Location:**
- Intestinal mucosa (highest activity)
- Kidney
- Placenta
- White blood cells

**Function:**
- Histamine degradation (primary)
- Other diamine metabolism
- Intestinal barrier function

## Drug Interactions

### MAOIs and DAO

**Question:** Do MAO inhibitors affect DAO?

**Answer:** Generally **no direct effect**

**Reasoning:**
- MAOIs selective for MAO-A/MAO-B
- Different substrate specificities
- No documented direct DAO inhibition at therapeutic doses

**Potential Indirect Effects:**
- Altered amine balance
- Histamine dynamics changes (theoretical)
- Genetic polymorphisms in DAO may influence individual response

### Safinamide (Xadago)

**Selective MAO-B Inhibitor:**
- Used in Parkinson's disease
- No effect on DAO
- No histamine metabolism interference
- Also has sodium/calcium channel blocking properties

## Clinical Relevance

### Histamine Intolerance

**DAO Deficiency:**
- Impaired histamine degradation
- GI symptoms, headaches, flushing
- Dietary histamine accumulation

**Not Related to MAOIs:**
- MAO inhibition doesn't worsen histamine intolerance
- Different enzymatic pathways

### Drug Development

**Selectivity Important:**
- Off-target effects minimized
- Xadago example of clean MAO-B selectivity
- Reduces dietary restrictions and side effects
