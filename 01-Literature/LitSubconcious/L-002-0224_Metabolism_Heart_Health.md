## Core Claim
**Insulin resistance carries 14 times greater risk for coronary artery disease compared to isolated cholesterol elevation** — metabolic factors (energy production, nutrient adequacy, insulin action, inflammation management) dominate cardiovascular health, while cholesterol is only one part and not the main determinant; type 2 diabetes, hypertension, obesity, and kidney dysfunction create ground for CVD years before symptoms appear.

## Zettels Extracted

### 1. Insulin Resistance 14× CAD Risk vs Cholesterol Alone
- **Claim:** Insulin resistance is associated with up to 14 times greater increase in coronary artery disease risk compared to isolated cholesterol elevation — making metabolic health far more significant for cardiovascular outcomes than LDL cholesterol alone.
- **Evidence:** Metabolic cardiology literature demonstrates insulin resistance as dominant CAD risk factor; comparison: insulin resistance = 14× risk increase, isolated cholesterol elevation = baseline risk; endothelial dysfunction, chronic inflammation, and metabolic dysregulation drive atherosclerosis more than lipid levels alone; clinical implication: prioritizing insulin sensitivity (low-carb diets, exercise, weight loss, sleep) more important than cholesterol reduction for many patients.
- **References:** [[Insulin_Resistance_14x_CAD_Risk_Literature_R-002-0058]], [[Pasut_2025_Endothelial_Metabolism_R-001-0082]] — cited as (metabolic cardiology literature; Pasut et al., 2025)
- **Zettel:** [[Insulin_Resistance_14x_CAD_Risk_vs_Cholesterol_Alone_Z-002-0810]]

### 2. Endothelial Metabolism Central to CVD Protection
- **Claim:** Endothelial cell metabolism is central to cardiovascular protection — energy production, nutrient adequacy (micronutrient cofactors), insulin action, and inflammation management are the core processes that maintain vascular health, not cholesterol levels alone.
- **Evidence:** Nature Reviews Cardiology 2025: endothelial cell metabolism critical for vascular function; cardiac muscle requires continuous energy production; micronutrient cofactors essential for metabolic enzymes; insulin action maintains endothelial health; chronic inflammation damages vasculature; these metabolic processes are upstream of cholesterol's role; addressing metabolism (insulin sensitivity, nutrient status, inflammation) more effective than statins alone for prevention.
- **References:** [[Pasut_2025_Endothelial_Metabolism_R-001-0082]], [[Insulin_Resistance_14x_CAD_Risk_Literature_R-002-0058]] — cited as (Pasut et al., 2025; metabolic literature)
- **Zettel:** [[Endothelial_Metabolism_Central_CVD_Protection_Z-002-0811]]

### 3. Silent CVD Progression — Metabolic Disorders Create Ground Before Symptoms
- **Claim:** Cardiovascular disease progresses silently for years before symptoms appear — type 2 diabetes, hypertension, obesity, and kidney dysfunction create the metabolic ground for CVD long before clinical manifestations, making early metabolic intervention essential.
- **Evidence:** T2D acts years before symptoms (insulin resistance → beta-cell failure → hyperglycemia → complications); hypertension causes gradual vascular damage (endothelial dysfunction → arterial stiffness → target organ damage); obesity creates chronic metabolic stress (inflammation, lipotoxicity, insulin resistance); kidney dysfunction has multi-system impact (uremia, electrolyte imbalance, vascular calcification); these factors operate silently; by the time symptoms appear, significant damage has occurred; prevention requires addressing metabolic dysfunction early, not waiting for CVD symptoms.
- **References:** [[Insulin_Resistance_14x_CAD_Risk_Literature_R-002-0058]], [[Pasut_2025_Endothelial_Metabolism_R-001-0082]] — cited as (metabolic cardiology literature; Pasut et al., 2025)
- **Zettel:** [[Silent_CVD_Progression_Metabolic_Disorders_Z-002-0812]]
