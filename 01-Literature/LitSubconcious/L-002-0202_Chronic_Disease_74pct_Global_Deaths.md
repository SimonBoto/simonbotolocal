# Lit: Chronic Diseases Cause 74% of Global Deaths

## Source
- **Full citation:** Global Burden of Disease Collaborative Network. "Global burden and future projections of non-communicable diseases (2000–2050): Progress toward SDG 3.4 and disparities across regions and risk factors." *Lancet*. 2023. doi:10.1016/S0140-6736(20)30752-2. PMID: 36726528.
- **Inbox origin:** [[INBOX-Article-21-Chronic-Disease]]
- **Reference note:** [[GBD_2019_NCD_Global_Burden_R-002-0042]]

## Core Claim
**Non-communicable diseases (NCDs) — cardiovascular disease, cancer, diabetes, chronic respiratory disease — now account for 74.36% of all deaths globally**, representing a fundamental shift from infectious diseases to chronic, largely preventable, lifestyle-related conditions as the dominant causes of mortality.

## Zettels Extracted

### 1. Chronic Disease 74% of Global Mortality
- **Claim:** Non-communicable diseases (NCDs) are the leading cause of death worldwide, accounting for approximately 74% of all global mortality in 2019 — a dramatic epidemiological shift from the early 20th century when infectious diseases dominated.
- **Evidence:** GBD 2019 study of 204 countries demonstrates NCDs cause 3 in 4 deaths globally; cardiovascular disease alone accounts for ~45% of NCD deaths, followed by cancer (~22%), chronic respiratory disease (~10%), and diabetes (~5%).
- **Reference:** [[GBD_2019_NCD_Global_Burden_R-002-0042]] — cited as (GBD 2019)
- **Zettel:** [[Chronic_Disease_74pct_Global_Mortality_Z-002-0744]]

### 2. Developed Countries 90% Chronic Disease Deaths
- **Claim:** In high-income developed countries, approximately 90% of all deaths are from chronic non-communicable diseases — the highest proportion globally — reflecting longer lifespans and lifestyle factors that accumulate over decades.
- **Evidence:** GBD 2019 shows clear socioeconomic gradient: high-income countries ~90% NCD deaths, upper-middle-income ~80%, lower-middle-income ~65%, low-income ~50% (but rising rapidly); as countries develop, NCD burden increases while infectious disease burden decreases.
- **Reference:** [[GBD_2019_NCD_Global_Burden_R-002-0042]] — cited as (GBD 2019)
- **Zettel:** [[Developed_Countries_90pct_Chronic_Deaths_Z-002-0745]]

### 3. Epidemiological Transition to Chronic Disease
- **Claim:** The world has undergone an epidemiological transition — shifting from a pattern where infectious diseases and acute conditions were the primary causes of death to one where chronic, lifestyle-related diseases dominate mortality, driven by aging populations, urbanization, and behavioral risk factors.
- **Evidence:** Historical GBD data shows dramatic shift: in 1900 infectious diseases caused majority of deaths; by 2000 NCDs became dominant; by 2019 NCDs cause 74% of deaths globally; this transition is now occurring in low- and middle-income countries as well.
- **Reference:** [[GBD_2019_NCD_Global_Burden_R-002-0042]] — cited as (GBD 2019)
- **Zettel:** [[Epidemiological_Transition_Chronic_Disease_Z-002-0746]]
