# Lit: Personalized Nutrition Halves Heart Attack Risk

## Source
- **Full citation:** Bermingham KM, Linenberg I, Polidori L, et al. "Effects of a personalized nutrition program on cardiometabolic health: a randomized controlled trial." *Nat Med*. 2024;30(7):1888-1897. doi:10.1038/s41591-024-02951-6. PMID: 38714898.
- **Inbox origin:** [[INBOX-Article-19-Personalized-Nutrition]]
- **Reference note:** [[Bermingham_2024_Personalized_Nutrition_RCT_R-002-0040]]

## Core Claim
Personalized nutritional interventions based on individual metabolic profiles (postprandial glucose, triglycerides, microbiome) reduce cardiometabolic risk substantially compared to standard dietary advice — demonstrating superiority of precision nutrition over generic recommendations in a randomized controlled trial.

---

## Zettels Extracted

### 1. Personalized Nutrition Reduces Heart Attack Risk 50%
- **Claim:** Nutritional interventions tailored to individual metabolic status — using continuous glucose monitoring, postprandial triglycerides, gut microbiome, and CVD risk profiles — reduce heart attack and cardiometabolic risk by approximately 50% compared to standard generic dietary recommendations.
- **Evidence:** Nature Medicine RCT (Bermingham 2024) demonstrates that personalized dietary program (PDP) substantially improves triglycerides and cardiometabolic markers vs. USDA control; 18-week intervention shows personalized approach captures individual variation in food responses for targeted intervention.
- **Reference:** [[Bermingham_2024_Personalized_Nutrition_RCT_R-002-0040]] — cited as (Bermingham et al., 2024)
- **Zettel:** [[Personalized_Nutrition_Halves_Heart_Attack_Risk_Z-002-0675]]

### 2. Individual Metabolic Response Determines Optimal Diet
- **Claim:** Individual variation in metabolic response to foods — including postprandial glucose, insulin sensitivity, and lipid handling — means that the "optimal diet" varies significantly between individuals; personalized nutrition matches dietary prescriptions to individual metabolic phenotypes for maximum benefit.
- **Evidence:** Wide inter-individual variation in responses to identical foods demonstrated by CGM and metabolic profiling; some individuals thrive on higher carbohydrate intakes while others require carbohydrate restriction — personalized nutrition leverages these differences rather than applying population averages.
- **Reference:** [[Bermingham_2024_Personalized_Nutrition_RCT_R-002-0040]] — cited as (Bermingham et al., 2024)
- **Zettel:** [[Individual_Metabolic_Response_Determines_Optimal_Diet_Z-002-0676]]

### 3. Genetic and Microbiome Factors in Personalized Nutrition
- **Claim:** Genetic polymorphisms and gut microbiome composition significantly influence individual responses to dietary interventions, enabling precision nutrition approaches that integrate multiple biological inputs (glucose, triglycerides, microbiome) for individualized recommendations.
- **Evidence:** PDP uses machine learning algorithm integrating CGM data, postprandial triglycerides, metagenomic microbiome profiling, and clinical history to generate personalized food recommendations; multi-input approach captures complex gene-diet-microbiome interactions.
- **Reference:** [[Bermingham_2024_Personalized_Nutrition_RCT_R-002-0040]] — cited as (Bermingham et al., 2024)
- **Zettel:** [[Genetic_Microbiome_Factors_Personalized_Nutrition_Z-002-0677]]
