# Lit: High LDL Not Linked to Mortality in Healthy People

## Source
- **Full citation:** Kip KE, Diamond D, Mulukutla S, Marroquin OC (2024). Is LDL cholesterol associated with long-term mortality among primary prevention adults? A retrospective cohort study from a large healthcare system. *BMJ Open*. 14(3):e077949. doi:10.1136/bmjopen-2023-077949
- **Inbox origin:** [[INBOX-Article-5-LDL-Mortality]]
- **Reference note:** [[Kip_2024_LDL_Mortality_Primary_Prevention_R-002-0028]]

## Core Claim
High LDL cholesterol is **not associated with increased all-cause or cardiovascular mortality** in adults without pre-existing cardiovascular disease (primary prevention population), challenging universal statin prescription and LDL-centric risk assessment.

---

## Zettels Extracted

### 1. LDL Cholesterol Not Predictive in Primary Prevention
- **Claim:** In adults without existing cardiovascular disease, LDL cholesterol levels do not predict long-term all-cause or cardiovascular mortality, contradicting the assumption that lowering LDL universally improves outcomes in healthy populations.
- **Evidence:** Large retrospective cohort study from a major healthcare system found no association between LDL levels and mortality in primary prevention adults over long-term follow-up.
- **Reference:** [[Kip_2024_LDL_Mortality_Primary_Prevention_R-002-0028]] — cited as (Kip et al., 2024)
- **Zettel:** [[LDL_Not_Predictive_Primary_Prevention_Z-002-0647]]

### 2. The LDL-Centric Risk Model is Incomplete
- **Claim:** The medical paradigm of using LDL cholesterol as the primary target for cardiovascular risk assessment and intervention is incomplete and may lead to over-treatment, as LDL alone fails to predict mortality in healthy populations.
- **Evidence:** When LDL is not associated with mortality in primary prevention, other factors (inflammation, insulin resistance, metabolic health) must be more predictive of outcomes than isolated lipid values.
- **Reference:** [[Kip_2024_LDL_Mortality_Primary_Prevention_R-002-0028]] — cited as (Kip et al., 2024)
- **Zettel:** [[LDL_Centric_Risk_Model_Incomplete_Z-002-0648]]

### 3. Metabolic Health More Predictive Than LDL
- **Claim:** Overall metabolic health (insulin sensitivity, inflammation status, body composition) is more predictive of cardiovascular and all-cause mortality than isolated LDL cholesterol values in primary prevention populations.
- **Evidence:** The disconnect between LDL and mortality implies that metabolic dysfunction markers (elevated insulin, CRP, visceral adiposity) drive cardiovascular risk more than cholesterol transport alone.
- **Reference:** [[Kip_2024_LDL_Mortality_Primary_Prevention_R-002-0028]] — cited as (Kip et al., 2024)
- **Zettel:** [[Metabolic_Health_More_Predictive_Than_LDL_Z-002-0649]]

### 4. Rethinking Statin Therapy for Primary Prevention
- **Claim:** The lack of association between LDL and mortality in healthy adults challenges the rationale for universal statin prescription in primary prevention, suggesting need for personalized risk assessment rather than LDL thresholds alone.
- **Evidence:** If high LDL doesn't increase mortality in primary prevention, the risk-benefit ratio of statins (side effects, costs, adherence) must be reconsidered for healthy individuals with elevated LDL but no other risk factors.
- **Reference:** [[Kip_2024_LDL_Mortality_Primary_Prevention_R-002-0028]] — cited as (Kip et al., 2024)
- **Zettel:** [[Rethinking_Statin_Primary_Prevention_Z-002-0650]]
