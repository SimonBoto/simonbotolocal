# Not All Calories Are Equal — Harvard Study

## Source
- **Original:** Γιατί Δεν Μετράνε Όλες οι Θερμίδες το Ίδιο
- **English:** "Why Not All Calories Count the Same"
- **Study:** Harvard Medical School researchers
- **Participants:** 164 adults who had already lost weight

## Key Finding

**When carbohydrates are reduced, the body burns more calories.**

| Diet Type | Carb % | Effect on Metabolism |
|-----------|--------|---------------------|
| **High-carb** | 60% | Slower metabolism |
| **Moderate-carb** | 40% | Intermediate |
| **Low-carb** | 20% | **Faster metabolism** |

## Study Design

| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Participants** | 164 adults |
| **Prerequisite** | Had already lost weight |
| **Duration** | 20 weeks |
| **Groups** | 3 (high/moderate/low carb) |
| **Measurement** | Total energy expenditure |

## The Calorie Source Matters

| Old Thinking | New Evidence |
|--------------|--------------|
| "Calories are calories" | Source affects metabolism |
| "Only quantity matters" | Quality changes energy burn |
| "Weight loss = calories in/out" | Metabolic adaptation varies |

## Metabolic Advantage of Low-Carb

| Mechanism | Effect |
|-----------|--------|
| **Reduced insulin** | Less fat storage signaling |
| **Increased fat oxidation** | More fat burned for fuel |
| **Higher thermic effect** | More energy expended |
| **Preserved muscle** | Better body composition |

## Weight Loss Maintenance

| Challenge | Low-Carb Advantage |
|-----------|-------------------|
| **Metabolic slowdown** | Less pronounced |
| **Hunger** | Better satiety |
| **Weight regain** | Reduced tendency |

## Clinical Implication

| Application | Recommendation |
|-------------|----------------|
| **Weight loss** | Consider carb restriction |
| **Weight maintenance** | Low-carb may be superior |
| **Metabolic health** | Carb quality and quantity matter |

## Evidence

> "It is not only how many calories we eat that matters, but also where they come from. Diets with fewer carbohydrates help the body burn more energy, while diets with many carbohydrates make metabolism slower."
> — Harvard Medical School study
