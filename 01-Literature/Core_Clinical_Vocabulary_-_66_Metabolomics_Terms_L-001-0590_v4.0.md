# Core Clinical Vocabulary — 66 Metabolomics Terms

> **Source:** ChatGPT Archive CGPT-0022 (December 08, 2025)  
> **Core Claim:** This source establishes a foundational clinical vocabulary of 66 metabolomics terms organized by clinical domain to standardize communication in metabolomics training and research settings.

---

## Core Concepts Extracted

### 1. Health Philosophy Core Concepts

**Category:** Health Philosophy

**Key Process:** 
```
Health → Disease → Homeostasis → Adaptation → Self-regulation → Allostatic Load → Oxidative Stress
```

**Mechanism/Steps:**
1. Health represents optimal physiological function and resilience
2. Disease occurs when homeostatic mechanisms fail
3. Homeostasis maintains internal stability through feedback loops
4. Adaptation allows physiological adjustment to environmental challenges
5. Self-regulation enables dynamic control of biological systems
6. Allostatic load accumulates from chronic adaptation demands
7. Oxidative stress results from imbalance between ROS production and antioxidant defenses

**Purpose/Significance:** Provides the philosophical foundation for understanding health-disease transitions and the impact of chronic stress on metabolic function

**Zettel:** [[Health_Philosophy_Core_Concepts_Z-001-1041]]

### 2. Metabolism & Metabolomics Terminology

**Category:** Methodology

**Key Process:** 
```
Metabolism → Metabolites → Metabolomics → Biochemical Pathways → Redox Balance → Energy Flow
```

**Mechanism/Steps:**
1. Metabolism encompasses all biochemical reactions in living organisms
2. Metabolites are small-molecule intermediates and products of metabolism
3. Metabolomics is the systematic study of metabolite profiles
4. Biochemical pathways organize sequential metabolic reactions
5. Redox balance maintains appropriate oxidation-reduction states
6. Energy flow describes ATP production and utilization across pathways

**Purpose/Significance:** Establishes core methodological concepts for analyzing metabolic function and interpreting metabolomic data

**Zettel:** [[Metabolism_Metabolomics_Terminology_Z-001-1042]]

### 3. Mitochondrial Energy Terminology

**Category:** Energy Metabolism

**Key Process:** 
```
Mitochondria → ATP → Krebs Cycle → Glycolysis → OXPHOS → NAD+/NADH → Beta-Oxidation → Ketosis
```

**Mechanism/Steps:**
1. Mitochondria serve as cellular power plants for aerobic metabolism
2. ATP provides the universal energy currency for cellular processes
3. Krebs Cycle oxidizes acetyl-CoA to generate reducing equivalents
4. Glycolysis breaks down glucose to pyruvate in the cytoplasm
5. OXPHOS couples electron transport to ATP synthesis
6. NAD+/NADH ratio reflects cellular redox state and energy status
7. Beta-Oxidation breaks down fatty acids for energy production
8. Ketosis represents metabolic adaptation to low-carbohydrate conditions

**Purpose/Significance:** Defines the core energy metabolism vocabulary essential for understanding metabolic flexibility and mitochondrial function

**Zettel:** [[Mitochondrial_Energy_Terminology_Z-001-1043]]

---

### 4. Hormonal Metabolic Regulation Terms

**Category:** Endocrine Regulation

**Key Process:** 
```
Insulin → Insulin Resistance → Cortisol → PTH-VitD Axis → Lipid Regulation → AA/EPA Ratio
```

**Mechanism/Steps:**
1. Insulin regulates glucose uptake and storage in response to blood sugar levels
2. Insulin resistance impairs insulin signaling and contributes to metabolic syndrome
3. Cortisol modulates glucose metabolism, immune function, and stress response
4. PTH-VitD Axis regulates calcium homeostasis and bone metabolism
5. Lipid regulation maintains appropriate fatty acid profiles and cholesterol metabolism
6. AA/EPA Ratio reflects inflammatory potential of dietary fatty acids

**Purpose/Significance:** Provides vocabulary for understanding systemic hormonal control of metabolic processes and their clinical implications

**Zettel:** [[Hormonal_Metabolic_Regulation_Terms_Z-001-1044]]

---

### 5. Microbiome Gut Brain Terminology

**Category:** Gut-Brain Axis

**Key Process:** 
```
Microbiome → Dysbiosis → Leaky Gut → SIBO → Neurotransmitters
```

**Mechanism/Steps:**
1. Microbiome refers to the community of microorganisms inhabiting the gastrointestinal tract
2. Dysbiosis represents imbalanced microbial composition associated with disease
3. Leaky Gut describes increased intestinal permeability allowing antigen translocation
4. SIBO (Small Intestinal Bacterial Overgrowth) involves abnormal bacterial proliferation in the small intestine
5. Neurotransmitters produced by gut microbes influence brain function and behavior

**Purpose/Significance:** Establishes terminology for understanding gut-brain communication and its impact on systemic health

**Zettel:** [[Microbiome_Gut_Brain_Terminology_Z-001-1045]]

---

### 6. Nutrition Macronutrient Terminology

**Category:** Nutritional Biochemistry

**Key Process:** 
```
Macronutrients → Micronutrients → Essential Fatty Acids → Omega-6/3 Ratio → Metabolic Flexibility
```

**Mechanism/Steps:**
1. Macronutrients (carbohydrates, proteins, fats) provide energy and structural components
2. Micronutrients (vitamins, minerals) serve as cofactors in enzymatic reactions
3. Essential Fatty Acids (linoleic, alpha-linolenic) must be obtained from diet
4. Omega-6/3 Ratio influences inflammatory and anti-inflammatory eicosanoid production
5. Metabolic Flexibility enables efficient switching between fuel sources (glucose/fatty acids)

**Purpose/Significance:** Provides vocabulary for understanding nutritional impacts on metabolic health and disease prevention

**Zettel:** [[Nutrition_Macronutrient_Terminology_Z-001-1046]]

### 7. Patient Communication Core Concepts

**Category:** Clinical Communication

**Key Process:** 
```
Metabolism → Energy → Inflammation → Oxidative Stress → Mitochondria → Nutrients → Hormones → Gut Health
```

**Mechanism/Steps:**
1. Metabolism serves as accessible entry point for patient education
2. Energy concepts help patients understand fatigue and vitality
3. Inflammation provides framework for understanding chronic disease processes
4. Oxidative stress explains cellular damage mechanisms
5. Mitochondria represent tangible cellular structures for patient understanding
6. Nutrients connect diet to physiological function
7. Hormones explain systemic regulation and symptoms
8. Gut Health provides comprehensive framework for systemic wellness

**Purpose/Significance:** Defines patient-facing terminology that facilitates effective communication in clinical settings and participant education

**Zettel:** [[Patient_Communication_Core_Concepts_Z-001-1047]]

## Synthesis Notes

**This vocabulary framework provides the essential linguistic foundation for metabolomics training and research. The organization by clinical domain ensures practical application across different contexts - from philosophical foundations (health/disease concepts) to methodological approaches (metabolomics techniques) to clinical applications (patient communication). The structured terminology enables consistent communication within research teams, standardization of educational materials for the 8-week bootcamp, and effective patient education in clinical settings. The patient-facing subset is particularly valuable for translating complex metabolic concepts into accessible language for participants in the METHAP study.**
