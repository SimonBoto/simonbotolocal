# Metabolomics Clinic Operations Manual

> **Source:** ChatGPT Archive CGPT-0022 (December 08, 2025)  
> **Core Claim:** This source establishes a comprehensive operations manual for metabolomics clinics, covering six-stage patient flow with standard operating procedures, quality standards and checkpoints, follow-up assessment rhythms, and clinician training competency verification protocols.

---

## Core Concepts Extracted

### 1. Six-Stage Patient Flow Operationalized

**Category:** Clinical Operations

**Key Process:** 
```
Assessment → Sample → Analysis → Reporting → Treatment → Follow-up
```

**Mechanism/Steps:**
1. Assessment stage includes comprehensive intake form completion and metabolic story documentation
2. Sample stage follows strict collection protocols with documented chain of custody
3. Analysis stage processes OAT and fatty acid testing with quality control verification
4. Reporting stage uses standardized patient-friendly formats with clear interpretation
5. Treatment stage implements tiered prescribing protocols with drug interaction checks
6. Follow-up stage schedules systematic check-ins with documented progress tracking

**Purpose/Significance:** Provides the operational framework needed to ensure consistent, high-quality patient care across the metabolomics clinic workflow

**Zettel:** [[Clinic_Patient_Flow_Operations_Z-001-1077]]

---

### 2. Quality Standards and Checkpoints

**Category:** Clinical Quality

**Key Process:** 
```
Pre-analytical → Analytical → Post-analytical → Reporting → Clinical Application
```

**Mechanism/Steps:**
1. Pre-analytical quality controls address sample collection variables and integrity
2. Analytical quality controls verify instrument performance and test accuracy
3. Post-analytical quality controls ensure interpretation consistency and accuracy
4. Reporting quality controls confirm patient-friendly language and actionable formats
5. Clinical application quality controls track treatment efficacy and patient outcomes
6. Quality metrics include pre-analytical error rate (< 2%), report turnaround time (< 5 business days), and patient satisfaction (> 90%)

**Purpose/Significance:** Provides the quality assurance framework needed to maintain clinical standards and ensure patient safety across all operational stages

**Zettel:** [[Metabolomics_Quality_Standards_Z-001-1078]]

---

### 3. Follow-Up Assessment Rhythms

**Category:** Clinical Monitoring

**Key Process:** 
```
4 weeks → 8 weeks → 16 weeks → 28 weeks → Ongoing
```

**Mechanism/Steps:**
1. 4-week follow-up evaluates initial treatment response and side effects
2. 8-week follow-up assesses biochemical markers and symptom changes
3. 16-week follow-up evaluates metabolic adaptation and long-term adjustments
4. 28-week follow-up assesses sustained outcomes and maintenance protocols
5. Ongoing follow-up maintains patient engagement and monitors for relapse
6. Each checkpoint evaluates specific metrics including biomarker trends, symptom resolution, and functional improvements

**Purpose/Significance:** Provides the clinical monitoring framework needed to optimize treatment protocols and ensure long-term patient outcomes

**Zettel:** [[Follow_Up_Assessment_Rhythms_Z-001-1079]]

### 4. New Doctor Training Checklist

**Category:** Clinical Training

**Key Process:** 
```
Stage 1 → Stage 2 → Stage 3 → Stage 4 → Stage 5 → Stage 6
Competency → Competency → Competency → Competency → Competency → Competency
```

**Mechanism/Steps:**
1. Stage 1 competency verification covers assessment and intake procedures
2. Stage 2 competency verification covers sample collection and handling
3. Stage 3 competency verification covers analysis interpretation and quality control
4. Stage 4 competency verification covers reporting and patient communication
5. Stage 5 competency verification covers treatment protocols and prescribing
6. Stage 6 competency verification covers follow-up protocols and outcome tracking

**Purpose/Significance:** Provides the training framework needed to ensure consistent, high-quality clinical practice across the metabolomics team
