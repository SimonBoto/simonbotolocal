# Lit: Minimal Exercise Dose Reduces Mortality

## Source
- **Full citation:** Ahmadi MN, Clare PJ, Katzmarzyk PT, Lee IM, Stamatakis E. "Vigorous physical activity, incident heart disease, and cancer: How little is enough?" *European Heart Journal*. 2022.
- **Original Greek:** "Ακόμη και 15–20 λεπτά έντονης άσκησης την εβδομάδα συνδέονται με 16–40% χαμηλότερο κίνδυνο θανάτου"
- **Inbox origin:** [[INBOX-Article-35-Minimal-Exercise]]
- **Reference note:** [[Ahmadi_2022_Vigorous_Physical_Activity_R-001-0041]]
- **Study size:** 72,000 participants followed ~6 years

## Core Claim
**Just 15-20 minutes of vigorous exercise per week reduces all-cause mortality risk by 16-40%** — the European Heart Journal study demonstrates that minimal doses of vigorous physical activity provide substantial protection, with optimal benefits at ~25 minutes/week (46% lower mortality) and diminishing returns beyond 50-57 minutes.

## Zettels Extracted

### 1. Minimal Exercise Dose: 15-20 Minutes Weekly Provides Major Mortality Benefit
- **Claim:** As little as 15-20 minutes of vigorous physical activity per week is associated with 16-40% lower risk of death from all causes — demonstrating that substantial health benefits can be achieved with minimal time investment, making exercise accessible even to the busiest individuals.
- **Evidence:** European Heart Journal study of 72,000 people followed ~6 years; 15-20 min/week vigorous activity associated with 16-40% mortality reduction; benefits observed for cardiovascular disease and cancer mortality; minimal effective dose is far below current guidelines (150-300 min/week moderate or 75-150 min/week vigorous).
- **Reference:** [[Ahmadi_2022_Vigorous_Physical_Activity_R-001-0041]] — cited as (Ahmadi et al., 2022)
- **Zettel:** [[Minimal_Exercise_15min_Mortality_Z-002-0798]]

### 2. Exercise Benefits Follow Diminishing Returns Curve
- **Claim:** Exercise benefits follow a diminishing returns pattern — the largest mortality reductions occur with relatively small amounts of vigorous activity, with continued but more gradual improvements as exercise volume increases beyond the optimal point.
- **Evidence:** Study found "sweet spot" at ~25 min/week vigorous activity (46% lower all-cause mortality, 58% lower CVD mortality); cancer optimal at ~45 min/week (37% lower); increasing to 50-57 min/week achieved ~50% risk reduction; benefits continue beyond this but at slower rate; suggests minimum threshold effect with plateau.
- **Reference:** [[Ahmadi_2022_Vigorous_Physical_Activity_R-001-0041]] — cited as (Ahmadi et al., 2022)
- **Zettel:** [[Exercise_Diminishing_Returns_Z-002-0799]]

### 3. Vigorous Activity More Efficient Than Moderate for Time-Limited Individuals
- **Claim:** Vigorous physical activity is more time-efficient than moderate activity for mortality reduction — small amounts of high-intensity exercise provide substantial benefits, making it practical for time-limited individuals to achieve health protection.
- **Evidence:** Study focused on vigorous (not moderate) activity; 15-20 min/week vigorous = significant protection; equivalent moderate activity would require 2-4x more time; practical example: 1 hour walking 4x/week + 15-30 min vigorous 2x/week (weights, sprints, bodyweight) achieves optimal benefits; time efficiency makes exercise adherence more realistic.
- **Reference:** [[Ahmadi_2022_Vigorous_Physical_Activity_R-001-0041]] — cited as (Ahmadi et al., 2022)
- **Zettel:** [[Vigorous_vs_Moderate_Exercise_Z-002-0800]]
