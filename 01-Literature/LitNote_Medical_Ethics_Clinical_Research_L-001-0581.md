## Seven Requirements for Ethical Research (Emanuel et al.)

### The Framework

**Core Insight:** Informed consent alone is neither necessary nor sufficient for ethical research. Seven requirements must be satisfied.

### The Seven Requirements

| Requirement | Key Element | Purpose |
|-------------|-------------|---------|
| **1. Value** | Enhance health or knowledge | Justify risks undertaken |
| **2. Scientific Validity** | Sound methodology | Ensure reliable results |
| **3. Fair Subject Selection** | Scientific objectives guide selection | Avoid exploitation |
| **4. Favorable Risk-Benefit Ratio** | Benefits outweigh risks | Protect participants |
| **5. Independent Review** | IRB/ethics committee oversight | Unbiased evaluation |
| **6. Informed Consent** | Voluntary, informed participation | Respect autonomy |
| **7. Respect for Enrolled Subjects** | Ongoing protection, privacy | Continued ethical obligation |

### Application

**Universal Framework:**
- Applies across cultures and contexts
- May require adaptation to local conditions
- Forms basis for ethical evaluation

## Historical Ethical Guidelines

### Nuremberg Code (1947)

**Context:**
- Post-WWII Nazi medical trials
- Nuremberg Military Tribunal
- Response to atrocities

**Key Principles:**
- Voluntary informed consent essential
- Avoid unnecessary harm
- Scientific validity required
- Right to withdraw
- Qualified investigators

**Significance:**
- First international research ethics code
- Foundation for subsequent guidelines

### Declaration of Helsinki (1964, revised periodically)

**Source:** World Medical Association

**Evolution:**
- 1964: Original
- 1975, 1983, 1989, 1996, 2000, 2008, 2013: Revisions

**Key Principles:**
- Informed consent
- Ethical review (IRB/EC)
- Patient welfare priority
- Therapeutic vs non-therapeutic research distinction
- Post-trial access to interventions

**Current Version (2013):**
- 37 paragraphs
- Greater emphasis on:
  - Vulnerable populations
  - Placebo use limitations
  - Post-trial obligations
  - Registration and publication

### Belmont Report (1979)

**Source:** National Commission (USA)

**Three Core Principles:**

**1. Respect for Persons:**
- Autonomy
- Informed consent
- Protection of vulnerable

**2. Beneficence:**
- Do no harm
- Maximize benefits
- Minimize risks

**3. Justice:**
- Fair distribution of burdens and benefits
- No exploitation of vulnerable

**Impact:**
- Basis for U.S. research regulations
- IRB establishment
- 45 CFR 46 (Common Rule)

### CIOMS Guidelines (1982, revised 1993, 2002, 2016)

**Source:** Council for International Organizations of Medical Sciences + WHO

**Focus:**
- International research
- Vulnerable populations
- Resource-limited settings
- Collaborative partnerships

## Regulatory Frameworks

### U.S. Regulations

**45 CFR 46 (Common Rule):**
- Federal policy for human subjects research
- Subparts:
  - A: Basic HHS Policy
  - B: Additional protections for pregnant women, fetuses
  - C: Additional protections for prisoners
  - D: Additional protections for children

**FDA Regulations (21 CFR 50, 56):**
- Informed consent requirements
- IRB requirements
- Clinical trial standards

### International Standards

**ICH-GCP (Good Clinical Practice):**
- E6(R2) guideline
- Harmonized standard (EU, USA, Japan)
- Quality and ethical standards
- Essential for pharmaceutical trials

**European Convention on Human Rights and Biomedicine (1997):**
- Council of Europe treaty
- Legally binding in ratifying countries
- Comprehensive bioethical framework
