# Anabolic vs Antiresorptive Therapy Selection — Clinical Decision Framework

## Core Concepts Extracted

### Therapy Selection Rationale
> "The choice between anabolic and antiresorptive therapy should be based on fracture risk, disease severity, and patient-specific factors."

**Key Concept:** Therapy selection is not one-size-fits-all; risk stratification and patient factors determine optimal approach.

**Clinical Imperative:** High-risk patients benefit most from anabolic therapy, while moderate-risk patients may start with antiresorptives.

---

### 1. Fracture Risk Stratification

**Zettel extracted:** [[Fracture_Risk_Stratification_Anabolic_Selection_Z-001-0129]]

#### High-Risk Indicators (Anabolic First)
| Indicator | Definition | Evidence Strength |
|-----------|------------|-------------------|
| **Multiple fractures** | ≥2 fragility fractures | High |
| **T-score < -3.5** | Hip or spine DXA | High |
| **Severe osteoporosis** | T-score < -3.0 + fracture | High |
| **Rapid bone loss** | >10% spine in 1 year | Moderate |
| **Glucocorticoid use** | ≥7.5 mg/day prednisone | High |

#### Moderate-Risk Indicators (Antiresorptive First)
| Indicator | Definition | Evidence Strength |
|-----------|------------|-------------------|
| **Single fracture** | One fragility fracture | Moderate |
| **T-score -2.5 to -3.5** | Hip or spine DXA | High |
| **Osteopenia + FRAX high** | FRAX >20% major or >3% hip | Moderate |
| **Age >75** | With osteopenia | Low |

### 2. Patient-Specific Factors

**Zettel extracted:** [[Patient_Specific_Factors_Therapy_Selection_Z-001-0130]]

#### Administration Preferences
| Factor | Anabolic Preference | Antiresorptive Preference |
|--------|----------------------|---------------------------|
| **Injections** | Comfortable with daily (TPTD, ABL) or monthly (ROMO) | Prefers oral or yearly IV |
| **GI tolerance** | No GI issues | History of esophagitis, ulcers |
| **Renal function** | Any renal impairment | eGFR <35 (avoid oral bisphosphonates) |
| **Cardiac history** | No CV disease | Recent MI/stroke (avoid ROMO) |

#### Comorbidities and Contraindications
| Comorbidity | Anabolic Contraindication | Antiresorptive Contraindication |
|-------------|---------------------------|------------------------------|
| **Multiple myeloma** | Relative contraindication | Not contraindicated |
| **Osteonecrosis risk** | Not associated | Dental procedures require caution |
| **Hypercalcemia** | Contraindicated | Not contraindicated |
| **Hypersensitivity** | Teriparatide allergy | Denosumab allergy |

### 3. Treatment Goals and Outcomes

**Zettel extracted:** [[Treatment_Goals_Outcomes_Selection_Z-001-0131]]

#### Goal Alignment
| Treatment Goal | Preferred Therapy | Rationale |
|----------------|-------------------|-----------|
| **Rapid fracture reduction** | Anabolic (ROMO, TPTD) | Highest vertebral fracture reduction (70%+) |
| **Preserve bone mass** | Antiresorptive (BISPHOS, DENOS) | Slows loss, preserves existing mass |
| **Stabilize severe disease** | Anabolic first, then antiresorptive | Builds then preserves |
| **Maintenance therapy** | Antiresorptive | Long-term safety established |
| **Bridge after anabolic** | Antiresorptive | Prevents rapid bone loss |

#### Outcome Comparison
| Outcome | Anabolic | Antiresorptive | Notes |
|---------|----------|----------------|-------|
| **12-month spine BMD gain** | +7–15% | +2–6% | Anabolic superior |
| **12-month hip BMD gain** | +2–4% | +1–3% | Anabolic superior |
| **Vertebral fracture reduction** | 60–73% | 40–50% | Anabolic superior |
| **Non-vertebral fracture reduction** | 35–45% | 20–35% | Anabolic superior |
| **Time to effect** | 3–6 months | 6–12 months | Anabolic faster |

### 4. Shared Decision-Making Framework

**Zettel extracted:** [[Shared_Decision_Making_Framework_Z-001-0132]]

#### Clinical Conversation Structure
```
1. Assess fracture risk (FRAX, DXA, clinical factors)
2. Discuss treatment goals (fracture prevention, BMD gain)
3. Review administration preferences (oral vs injection)
4. Evaluate contraindications and comorbidities
5. Present evidence-based options with risks/benefits
6. Confirm patient priorities and preferences
7. Collaborate on final decision
```

#### Decision Aid Elements
| Element | Anabolic | Antiresorptive |
|---------|----------|----------------|
| **Efficacy** | Higher fracture reduction | Good fracture reduction |
| **Speed** | Rapid effect | Slower effect |
| **Duration** | Limited (12–24 months) | Long-term (5–10+ years) |
| **Cost** | High ($1,000+/month) | Lower ($50–300/month) |
| **Adherence** | Daily injections (TPTD/ABL) | Weekly/monthly oral or yearly IV |
| **Risk profile** | Osteosarcoma (theoretical), hypercalcemia | Osteonecrosis, atypical fractures |
