## My Notes

**Synthesis:** The ADA represents the complex intersection of medical progress, institutional influence, and pharmaceutical industry relationships. Founded with noble goals (1940), it has become a dominant force in diabetes care, but faces legitimate criticism about pharmaceutical funding potentially influencing guidelines. The insulin manufacturers (Lilly, Novo Nordisk, Sanofi) contributing $53M+ creates clear conflict of interest, especially given insulin pricing controversies. This institutional context matters for understanding why certain guidelines exist and why alternative approaches (like metabolic-focused protocols) may face resistance.
