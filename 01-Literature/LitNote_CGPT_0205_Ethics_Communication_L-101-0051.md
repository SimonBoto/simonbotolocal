## Context
PhD candidate experiencing 6-month delay in ethics committee response; seeking guidance on direct communication with committee head.

## Recommended Communication Strategy

### Draft Message Structure

**Subject:** Follow-Up Regarding Ethics Committee Application

**Elements:**
1. **Professional greeting** — Respectful tone
2. **Self-identification** — Name, supervisor, institution
3. **Context** — Submission date, duration elapsed
4. **Concern** — Impact on research timeline
5. **Request** — Guidance on next steps
6. **Availability** — Openness to meeting
7. **Professional close** — Gratitude, contact information

### Key Principles
| Principle | Application |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Respectful persistence** | Polite but clear about urgency |
| **Documentation** | Reference submission date, previous attempts |
| **Professional tone** | No accusation or frustration |
| **Solution-oriented** | Request specific next steps |
| **Multiple channels** | Email, phone, formal letter as needed |

### Escalation Pathway
| Stage | Action | Timeline |
|-------|--------|----------|
| 1 | Formal email to committee head | Week 1 |
| 2 | Phone follow-up | Week 2 |
| 3 | Formal letter | Week 3 |
| 4 | Supervisor intervention | Week 4 |
| 5 | Institutional ombudsman | Week 6+ |

## Prevention Strategies

### For Future Submissions
- [ ] Confirm receipt of application
- [ ] Request expected timeline
- [ ] Schedule pre-submission consultation
- [ ] Identify committee liaison
- [ ] Document all communications

### Proactive Communication
- Check application completeness before submission
- Address potential concerns preemptively
- Maintain professional relationship with committee
