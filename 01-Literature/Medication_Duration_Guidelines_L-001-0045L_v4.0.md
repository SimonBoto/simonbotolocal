## Core Concepts Extracted

### Duration Rationale
> "Medication duration guidelines balance efficacy, safety, and cost-effectiveness in long-term osteoporosis management."

**Key Concept:** Duration guidelines are evidence-based recommendations to maximize benefit while minimizing risk.

**Clinical Imperative:** Duration decisions should be individualized, not automatic.

---

### 1. Bisphosphonate Duration Guidelines

**Zettel extracted:** [[Bisphosphonate_Duration_Guidelines_Z-001-0137]]

#### Oral Bisphosphonates
| Medication | Standard Duration | Extended Duration | Criteria for Extension |
|------------|-------------------|---------------------|------------------------|
| **Alendronate** | 5 years | Up to 10 years | High fracture risk, T-score < -3.5, ongoing GC therapy |
| **Risedronate** | 5 years | Up to 10 years | Same as alendronate |
| **Ibandronate** | 3 years | Up to 5 years | Same criteria, less data |

#### IV Bisphosphonates
| Medication | Standard Duration | Extended Duration | Criteria for Extension |
|------------|-------------------|---------------------|------------------------|
| **Zoledronic acid** | 3 years | Up to 6 years | High fracture risk, T-score < -3.5, ongoing GC therapy |
| **Pamidronate** | 2–3 years | Up to 5 years | Same criteria, less data |

#### Drug Holiday Considerations
- **Indications for holiday:** Low fracture risk, improved BMD, no new fractures
- **Monitoring during holiday:** DXA every 2 years, clinical assessment annually
- **Restart criteria:** BMD decline >5%, new fracture, FRAX increase >20%

### 2. Denosumab Duration Guidelines

**Zettel extracted:** [[Denosumab_Duration_Guidelines_Z-001-0138]]

#### Standard Duration
- **No fixed duration limit** — designed for long-term use
- **Minimum:** 5 years for high-risk patients
- **Continuation:** Based on risk reassessment every 3–5 years

#### Discontinuation Considerations
- **High risk of rebound bone loss:** Up to 5–7% BMD loss in first year
- **Mandatory bridging:** Start oral bisphosphonate before final denosumab dose
- **No drug holiday:** Unlike bisphosphonates, denosumab has no "accumulated effect"

#### Monitoring During Therapy
| Parameter | Frequency | Target |
|-----------|-----------|--------|
| **DXA** | Every 3–5 years | Stabilization or improvement |
| **CTX** | Every 6 months | Suppressed (<0.2 ng/mL) |
| **Calcium** | Annually | Normal range |

### 3. Anabolic Therapy Duration Guidelines

**Zettel extracted:** [[Anabolic_Therapy_Duration_Guidelines_Z-001-0139]]

#### Teriparatide and Abaloparatide
| Parameter | Guideline | Rationale |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| **Maximum duration** | 2 years lifetime | Theoretical osteosarcoma risk in rats |
| **Standard duration** | 18–24 months | Optimal BMD gains |
| **Early discontinuation** | If severe hypercalcemia, orthostatic hypotension | Safety concerns |

#### Romosozumab
| Parameter | Guideline | Rationale |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| **Maximum duration** | 12 months | CV safety signal in FRAME extension |
| **Standard duration** | 12 months | Optimal BMD and fracture reduction |
| **Early discontinuation** | If CV events, severe reaction | Safety concerns |

#### Sequential Therapy Timing
- **Teriparatide → antiresorptive:** Within 1 week of final dose
- **Abaloparatide → antiresorptive:** Within 1 week of final dose
- **Romosozumab → antiresorptive:** At 12 months

### 4. Individualized Duration Decision Framework

**Zettel extracted:** [[Individualized_Duration_Framework_Z-001-0140]]

#### Decision Factors
| Factor | Duration Extension Indication | Duration Limitation Indication |
|--------|--------------------------------|--------------------------------|
| **Fracture risk** | High risk (T-score < -3.5, multiple fractures) | Low risk (T-score > -1.5, no fractures) |
| **BMD change** | Continued improvement or stabilization | Decline during therapy |
| **Patient age** | Older age (>75) | Younger age (<50) |
| **Comorbidities** | Ongoing glucocorticoid therapy | Renal impairment (bisphosphonates) |
| **Adherence** | Excellent adherence | Poor adherence |

#### Clinical Decision Tree
```
Duration reassessment (annually or at standard duration end)
                ↓
      High fracture risk? (T-score, FRAX, clinical factors)
          ┌─────┴─────┐
          ↓           ↓
     Yes → Continue    No → Consider holiday or switch
     therapy             (if bisphosphonate)
```
