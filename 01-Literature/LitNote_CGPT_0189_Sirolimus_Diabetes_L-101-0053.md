# Lit: Sirolimus — Mitochondrial Toxicity and Metabolic Effects

## Overview
Sirolimus (rapamycin) inhibits mTOR, causing mitochondrial dysfunction and metabolic disruption. Understanding these mechanisms provides insight into insulin resistance pathways.

## Mechanisms of Mitochondrial Toxicity

### 1. mTORC1 Inhibition → Impaired Mitochondrial Biogenesis
| Effect | Mechanism | Outcome |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| **↓ PGC-1α** | mTORC1 normally promotes PGC-1α expression | Reduced mitochondrial DNA replication |
| **↓ Mitochondrial protein synthesis** | Translation regulation disrupted | Decreased mitochondrial mass |
| **↓ Biogenesis overall** | Master regulator suppressed | Reduced mitochondrial capacity |

### 2. Suppressed Oxidative Phosphorylation (OXPHOS)
| Effect | Mechanism | Outcome |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| **↓ ETC components** | mTORC1 regulates translation of electron transport chain proteins | Reduced ATP production |
| **Metabolic stress** | Energy deficit in high-demand tissues | Cell dysfunction/death |

### 3. Increased Reactive Oxygen Species (ROS)
| Effect | Mechanism | Outcome |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| **ETC impairment** | Electron leakage from dysfunctional chain | ROS generation |
| **Oxidative damage** | ROS attacks membranes, proteins, DNA | Mitochondrial damage |
| **mPTP opening** | Mitochondrial permeability transition pore activation | Apoptosis pathway |

### 4. Autophagy/Mitophagy Dysregulation
| Effect | Mechanism | Outcome |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| **↑ Autophagy induction** | mTORC1 inhibition triggers autophagy | Initially protective |
| **Excessive mitophagy** | Chronic mTOR inhibition | Depletion of healthy mitochondria |
| **Energy deficit** | Clearance exceeds biogenesis | Cellular energy crisis |

### 5. Calcium Dysregulation
| Effect | Mechanism | Outcome |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| **Disrupted Ca²⁺ buffering** | mTORC1 role in calcium homeostasis | Mitochondrial calcium overload |
| **mPTP opening** | High Ca²⁺ triggers pore | Mitochondrial depolarization |
| **Cell death** | Apoptosis cascade activation | Tissue damage |

## Connection to Diabetes

### Mechanisms of Sirolimus-Induced Diabetes

#### 1. Insulin Resistance (Peripheral Tissues)
```
Sirolimus → mTORC1 inhibition → ↓ GLUT4 translocation → ↓ Glucose uptake → Hyperglycemia
```
| Tissue | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| **Muscle** | Reduced glucose uptake |
| **Adipose** | Impaired glucose uptake, altered adipokines |
| **Liver** | Altered gluconeogenesis regulation |

#### 2. β-cell Dysfunction (Pancreas)
| Mechanism | Effect |
|-----------|--------|
| **Mitochondrial toxicity** | ↓ ATP production for insulin secretion |
| **↑ ROS** | Oxidative damage to β-cells |
| **Excessive autophagy** | Clearance of functional mitochondria |
| **↓ β-cell mass** | Reduced insulin-producing capacity |

#### 3. mTORC2 Inhibition (Chronic/High Dose)
```
Sirolimus → mTORC2 inhibition → ↓ Akt phosphorylation → Insulin resistance
```
- mTORC2 normally maintains insulin sensitivity
- Disrupted insulin signaling cascade
- Further contributes to glucose intolerance

#### 4. Lipid Metabolism Disruption
| Effect | Mechanism | Outcome |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| **↓ Adiponectin** | mTORC1 suppression | Reduced insulin sensitization |
| **↑ Lipolysis** | Altered adipose function | Elevated free fatty acids |
| **Lipotoxicity** | FFA accumulation in muscle/liver | Worsened insulin resistance |

## Clinical Evidence

### Transplant Patients (NODAT)
- **New-onset diabetes after transplantation** risk increased with sirolimus
- Dose-dependent effect
- More pronounced with prolonged therapy
- Monitoring: fasting glucose, HbA1c

### Laboratory Findings
| Parameter | Change |
|-----------|--------|
| Fasting glucose | ↑ |
| HbA1c | ↑ |
| Insulin levels | Variable (compensatory then ↓) |
| Glucose tolerance | Impaired |

## Paradoxical Effects

### Protective Contexts
| Context | Mechanism | Benefit |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| **Cancer** | Mitochondrial dysfunction → cell death | Anti-tumor effect |
| **Aging (intermittent)** | Autophagy activation | Lifespan extension |
| **Neuroprotection** | Selective autophagy | Reduced protein aggregation |

### Dose-Dependence
| Dose | Effect |
|------|--------|
| **Low/intermittent** | Primarily mTORC1; beneficial autophagy |
| **High/chronic** | mTORC2 inhibition; metabolic toxicity |
