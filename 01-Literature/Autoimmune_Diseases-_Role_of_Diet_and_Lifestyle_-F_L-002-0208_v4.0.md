# Lit: Autoimmune Diseases — Role of Diet and Lifestyle

## Source
- **Full citation:** Fasano A. "Leaky gut and autoimmune diseases." *Clin Rev Allergy Immunol*. 2012;42(1):71-78. Plus AIP clinical trials 2017-2024.
- **Inbox origin:** [[INBOX-Article-27-Autoimmune]]
- **Reference note:** [[Autoimmune_Diet_Lifestyle_R-002-0048]]

## Core Concepts Extracted
**Autoimmune diseases have dietary and lifestyle root causes** — environmental triggers, intestinal permeability (leaky gut), and gut dysbiosis are primary drivers that can be modified. The Autoimmune Protocol (AIP) and elimination diets produce significant symptom improvement and potential disease remission by removing triggers and healing the gut barrier.

## Zettels Extracted

### 1. Autoimmune Root Causes — Diet and Lifestyle Primary Drivers
- **Claim:** Autoimmune diseases are not purely genetic — environmental factors including diet, gut health, sleep, stress, and toxins are primary drivers that can be modified to prevent, halt, or reverse autoimmune conditions; genetics account for only 10-30% of risk.
- **Evidence:** Fasano's triad demonstrates autoimmunity requires genetic susceptibility + environmental trigger + intestinal permeability — two of three factors are modifiable; twin studies show low concordance for autoimmunity, implicating environmental factors; lifestyle interventions produce measurable improvements.
- **Reference:** [[Autoimmune_Diet_Lifestyle_R-002-0048]] — cited as (Fasano, AIP trials)
- **Zettel:** [[Autoimmune_Root_Causes_Diet_Lifestyle_Z-002-0762]]

### 2. Elimination Diets for Autoimmune Improvement — AIP Protocol
- **Claim:** Elimination diets, particularly the Autoimmune Protocol (AIP), produce significant symptom improvement and disease modification in autoimmune conditions including IBD, Hashimoto's thyroiditis, rheumatoid arthritis, and psoriasis by removing dietary triggers and supporting gut healing.
- **Evidence:** Clinical trials of AIP show symptom remission in IBD patients, reduced thyroid antibodies in Hashimoto's, decreased inflammation in rheumatoid arthritis, and skin clearance in psoriasis; elimination of gluten, dairy, nightshades, and processed foods removes common triggers.
- **Reference:** [[Autoimmune_Diet_Lifestyle_R-002-0048]] — cited as (AIP clinical trials)
- **Zettel:** [[Elimination_Diets_Autoimmune_Improvement_Z-002-0763]]

### 3. Leaky Gut and Autoimmune Connection — Intestinal Permeability Mechanism
- **Claim:** Intestinal permeability (leaky gut) is a prerequisite for autoimmune disease — tight junction dysfunction allows antigen entry, triggering immune activation and molecular mimicry that leads to self-tissue attack; healing the gut barrier can prevent or halt autoimmune processes.
- **Evidence:** Fasano's research demonstrates zonulin-mediated tight junction opening precedes autoimmune onset; celiac disease shows direct gluten-zonulin-leaky gut mechanism; elevated zonulin found in T1D, MS, and IBD; gut barrier healing correlates with symptom improvement.
- **Reference:** [[Autoimmune_Diet_Lifestyle_R-002-0048]] — cited as (Fasano leaky gut research)
- **Zettel:** [[Leaky_Gut_Autoimmune_Connection_Z-002-0764]]
