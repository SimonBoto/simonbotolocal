# Lit: Ethics Committee Communication — Follow-Up Protocol

## Source
- **Full citation:** ChatGPT conversation CGPT-0205, January 9, 2025
- **Context:** PhD candidate seeking guidance on ethics committee non-response
- **Content:** Template message for IRB follow-up, professional communication

## Core Concepts Extracted

### 1. IRB Non-Response Protocol
> "Despite my best efforts to follow up through official channels, I have not yet received any updates regarding the status of my application."

When ethics committees don't respond:
- Document all follow-up attempts
- Escalate through official channels first
- Mobile/direct contact as last resort
- Maintain professionalism regardless of frustration

**Zettel extracted:** [[IRB_Non_Response_Protocol_Z-002-0613]]

### 2. Professional Communication Template
> Formal message structure: greeting → context → concern → request → closing

Key elements:
- Identify yourself and supervisor
- Reference submission date
- State concern without accusation
- Request specific next steps
- Offer additional information
- Maintain respectful tone

**Zettel extracted:** [[IRB_Communication_Template_Z-002-0614]]

### 3. Administrative Persistence
Six months without response requires escalation:
- Multiple follow-up attempts documented
- Direct contact when appropriate
- Alternative pathways (supervisor, department)
- Consider institutional ombudsman

**Zettel extracted:** [[IRB_Escalation_Pathways_Z-002-0615]]

## Key Insight

**Administrative vs. Substantive Ethics:**
This conversation addresses the *process* of ethics review (communication, follow-up) rather than *substantive* ethics principles (which were covered in CGPT-0131). Both are necessary for successful IRB navigation.

## Application to METHAP

If NKUA ethics committee delays:
1. Document all submissions and follow-ups
2. Use formal, respectful communication
3. Escalate through supervisor and department
4. Consider whether delay indicates substantive concerns
