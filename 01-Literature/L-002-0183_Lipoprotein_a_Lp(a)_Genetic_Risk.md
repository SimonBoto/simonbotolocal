## Core Claim
**Lipoprotein(a) [Lp(a)]** is a genetically determined, independent cardiovascular risk factor present in ~20% of the population that increases heart disease and stroke risk 2-3 fold — unlike other lipid risk factors, Lp(a) is not significantly modified by diet or lifestyle, though emerging therapies show promise for targeted reduction.

## Zettels Extracted

### 1. Lp(a) is a Genetic Cardiovascular Risk Factor
- **Claim:** Lipoprotein(a) is an LDL-like particle with an additional apolipoprotein(a) component that is 90-95% genetically determined, present in approximately 20% of the population at levels (>50 mg/dL) that confer 2-3 fold increased risk of cardiovascular disease, stroke, and aortic stenosis — independent of LDL cholesterol and other traditional risk factors.
- **Evidence:** Mendelian randomization studies confirm causality: genetically elevated Lp(a) directly causes CVD; population studies show dose-response relationship between Lp(a) levels and cardiovascular events; unlike LDL, Lp(a) is not significantly responsive to diet, exercise, or statins — making it a unique, genetically fixed risk factor.

### 2. Lp(a) Not Modified by Diet or Lifestyle
- **Claim:** Unlike LDL cholesterol and triglycerides, Lp(a) levels are remarkably resistant to modification through dietary interventions, exercise, weight loss, or statin therapy — representing a fixed genetic risk that requires different management strategies than modifiable lipid risk factors.
- **Evidence:** Dietary interventions (low-fat, low-carb, Mediterranean), exercise programs, and statin therapy have minimal to no effect on Lp(a) levels; the only established modulators are hormones (estrogen reduces, testosterone increases) and rare medical conditions — management focuses on aggressive control of other modifiable risk factors rather than Lp(a) reduction itself.
- **Reference:** (Pending) — cited as (Primary study, year)
- **Zettel:** [[Lp(a)_Not_Modified_Diet_Lifestyle_Z-002-0685]]

### 3. Emerging Therapies Targeting Lp(a) Reduction
- **Claim:** Novel therapeutics specifically targeting Lp(a) reduction — including antisense oligonucleotides (ASOs), siRNA therapies (pelacarsen), and oral small molecules — are in advanced clinical trials and show promise for directly addressing this genetic risk factor for the first time.
- **Evidence:** Phase 2/3 trials of pelacarsen (siRNA) and other Lp(a)-targeting therapies demonstrate 80-95% Lp(a) reduction; outcomes trials (HORIZON, ASSA) are underway to determine if Lp(a) reduction translates to CVD event reduction — if positive, these would be first therapies to address this major residual risk factor.
