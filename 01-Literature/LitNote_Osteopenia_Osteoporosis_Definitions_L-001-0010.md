## Core Concepts Extracted

### 1. Osteopenia Definition (T-score -1.0 to -2.5)
> "Osteopenia is defined by a T-score between −1.0 and −2.5 on dual-energy X-ray absorptiometry (DXA)."

### 2. Osteoporosis Definition (T-score ≤ -2.5)
> "Osteoporosis is diagnosed when the T-score is ≤ −2.5. The microarchitectural deterioration of bone, in addition to the loss of bone mass, contributes significantly to fracture risk."

**Zettel extracted:** [[Osteoporosis_T_score_Definition_Minus_2.5_Z-001-0077]

## Key Figures & Data
- **Osteopenia:** T-score -1.0 to -2.5
- **Osteoporosis:** T-score ≤ -2.5
- **Global impact:** 1 in 3 women, 1 in 5 men over 50 affected
- **Gold standard:** DXA (dual-energy X-ray absorptiometry)


## My Notes

**Synthesis:** T-score classification creates clear diagnostic categories, but clinical decision-making requires FRAX integration with BMD. The continuum from normal → osteopenia → osteoporosis guides intervention intensity.
