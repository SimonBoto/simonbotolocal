## Core Concepts Extracted

### 1. Methionine — The Essential Sulfur Amino Acid
> "Methionine is an essential amino acid containing a sulfur atom, serving as the precursor for SAM and, through transsulfuration, cysteine synthesis."

**Structure:**
- Sulfur-containing amino acid
- Thioether bond (methyl-thio group)
- Essential (cannot be synthesized de novo)

**Dietary Sources:**
- Animal proteins (meat, eggs, dairy)
- Plant proteins (legumes, nuts, seeds)
- Lower in plant-based diets

**Metabolic Fates:**
1. Protein synthesis (incorporation)
2. SAM synthesis (methylation)
3. Cysteine synthesis (transsulfuration)
4. Energy production (complete oxidation)

**Zettels extracted:** [[Methionine_Essential_Sulfur_Amino_Acid_Z-001-0432]

### 2. The Methionine Cycle — Activation and Regeneration
> "Methionine is activated to SAM for methylation reactions, then converted to SAH and homocysteine, which is either remethylated to methionine or enters transsulfuration."

**Step 1: SAM Synthesis**
```
Methionine + ATP → SAM + PPi + Pi
```
- Enzyme: Methionine adenosyltransferase (MAT)
- Irreversible, commits methionine to metabolic fate

**Step 2: Methyl Donation**
```
SAM + Acceptor → Methylated product + SAH
```
- >100 different methyltransferases
- Universal methyl donor

**Step 3: SAH Hydrolysis**
```
SAH ↔ Homocysteine + Adenosine
```
- Enzyme: SAH hydrolase
- Reversible (product removal drives forward)

**Step 4: Branch Point**
- **Remethylation:** Homocysteine → Methionine (B12/folate)
- **Transsulfuration:** Homocysteine → Cysteine (B6-dependent)

**Zettels extracted:** [[Methionine_Cycle_Activation_Regeneration_Z-001-0433]

### 3. Transsulfuration Pathway — Cysteine Synthesis
> "The transsulfuration pathway converts homocysteine to cysteine via cystathionine, requiring vitamin B6 and providing the sulfur atom for cysteine and glutathione synthesis."

**Step 1: Cystathionine β-Synthase (CBS)**
```
Homocysteine + Serine → Cystathionine
```
- **Cofactor:** Pyridoxal phosphate (vitamin B6)
- **Regulation:** Activated by SAM (high methionine pushes pathway)

**Step 2: Cystathionine γ-Lyase (CGL)**
```
Cystathionine → Cysteine + α-Ketobutyrate + NH₃
```
- **Cofactor:** Pyridoxal phosphate (vitamin B6)
- **Products:** Cysteine (useful), α-ketobutyrate (degraded)

**Regulation:**
- **High SAM:** Activates CBS (promotes transsulfuration when methionine abundant)
- **Low SAM:** CBS inhibited (conserves methionine)
- **Vitamin B6:** Required for both steps

**Zettels extracted:** [[Transsulfuration_Pathway_Cysteine_Synthesis_Z-001-0434]

### 4. Cysteine — Metabolic Fates
> "Cysteine serves as precursor for glutathione, taurine, and inorganic sulfate, or is degraded to pyruvate for energy production."

**Pathway 1: Glutathione Synthesis**
```
Cysteine + Glutamate + Glycine → Glutathione (GSH)
```
- Major antioxidant
- Detoxification
- Cellular redox regulation

**Pathway 2: Taurine Synthesis**
```
Cysteine → Cysteine sulfinic acid → Hypotaurine → Taurine
```
- Bile acid conjugation
- Osmoregulation
- Neuromodulation

**Pathway 3: Sulfate Production**
```
Cysteine → Inorganic sulfate + Pyruvate
```
- Sulfation reactions
- Excreted in urine

**Pathway 4: Energy Production**
```
Cysteine → Pyruvate → Acetyl-CoA → Krebs cycle
```
- Glucogenic amino acid
- Complete oxidation possible

**Zettels extracted:** [[Cysteine_Metabolic_Fates_Glutathione_Z-001-0435]

### 5. Glutathione — The Master Antioxidant
> "Glutathione (GSH) is synthesized from cysteine, glutamate, and glycine, serving as the primary intracellular antioxidant and detoxification agent."

**Synthesis:**
```
Cysteine + Glutamate → γ-Glutamylcysteine (GCL enzyme)
    ↓
+ Glycine → Glutathione (GSS enzyme)
```

**Functions:**

**Antioxidant:**
```
2 GSH + H₂O₂ → GSSG + 2 H₂O
```
- Neutralizes reactive oxygen species
- Regenerates other antioxidants (vitamin C, E)

**Detoxification:**
- Conjugates with toxins for excretion
- Phase II drug metabolism
- Heavy metal chelation

**Amino Acid Transport:**
- γ-Glutamyl cycle
- Membrane transport

**Regulation:**
- Cysteine availability rate-limiting
- Oxidative stress upregulates synthesis
- GSH:GSSG ratio indicates redox status

**Zettels extracted:** [[Glutathione_Master_Antioxidant_Z-001-0436]

### 6. Complete Methionine Oxidation
> "Methionine can be completely oxidized for energy, producing propionyl-CoA (glucogenic) and sulfate, with B12 required for the propionate pathway."

**Complete Degradation Path:**

**Step 1-3:** To Homocysteine (as above)
- SAM → SAH → Homocysteine

**Step 4:** Transsulfuration
- Homocysteine + Serine → Cystathionine → Cysteine

**Step 5:** Cysteine to Pyruvate
```
Cysteine → Pyruvate + NH₃ + H₂S
```
- Multiple enzymatic steps
- Pyruvate enters Krebs cycle
- H₂S oxidized to sulfate

**Step 6:** Propionate Pathway (from methyl group)
- Methionine methyl group → 5-MTHF
- Eventually contributes to one-carbon pool

**Energy Yield:**
- Complete oxidation → CO₂ + H₂O + energy
- Glucogenic (produces glucose precursors)
- Sulfur excreted as sulfate

## Key Figures & Data
- **Methionine:** Essential, sulfur-containing
- **SAM:** Universal methyl donor (>100 reactions)
- **Transsulfuration:** B6-dependent (2 steps)
- **Cysteine:** Rate-limiting for glutathione
- **Glutathione:** Most abundant intracellular antioxidant (1-10 mM)

## My Notes

**Synthesis:** The transsulfuration pathway represents a critical metabolic intersection linking methionine metabolism to antioxidant defense through cysteine and glutathione synthesis. When methionine is abundant, SAM activates cystathionine β-synthase, diverting homocysteine toward cysteine production rather than remethylation. This cysteine becomes the rate-limiting substrate for glutathione synthesis, the primary cellular antioxidant. The pathway requires vitamin B6 for both transsulfuration steps, creating another nutritional dependency. Complete methionine oxidation generates both energy (via pyruvate and propionate pathways) and inorganic sulfate, demonstrating the integrated nature of amino acid catabolism. For METHAP, adequate B6 and B12 status ensures efficient transsulfuration, supporting glutathione-mediated antioxidant defense during high-dose nutrient therapy.

**Connection to METHAP/PhD:**
- **Antioxidant support:** Glutathione production requires B6/B12
- **High-dose nutrients:** Increase oxidative stress
- **Methylation-transsulfuration balance:** Affects homocysteine levels
- **Monitoring:** Glutathione status (if available)
