# High LDL Not Associated with Mortality in People Without CVD

## Source
- **Original:** Τα Υψηλά Επίπεδα LDL Χοληστερίνης Δεν Συνδέονται με Αυξημένο Κίνδυνο Θανάτου
- **English:** "High LDL Cholesterol Not Associated with Increased Death Risk"
- **Study:** BMJ Open, March 2024

## Study Design

| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Participants** | 177,000 adults |
| **Age range** | 50-89 years |
| **Exclusions** | No diabetes, no cholesterol-lowering drugs |
| **Follow-up** | 6 years |
| **Data source** | Real-world healthcare records |

## Unexpected Finding

| LDL Level | Mortality Risk |
|-----------|----------------|
| **100-189 mg/dL** | **Lowest risk** |
| **Below current "optimal"** | Higher mortality |
| **Very low LDL** | Higher mortality |

## The Paradox

| Assumption | Reality |
|------------|---------|
| "Lower LDL is always better" | Not supported |
| "High LDL increases death risk" | Not in healthy people |
| "Very low LDL optimal" | Associated with higher mortality |

## Clinical Implication

| Old Approach | Evidence-Based Approach |
|--------------|------------------------|
| LDL drives all decisions | Consider metabolic context |
| Treat high LDL aggressively | Assess overall risk |
| Ignore other factors | Prioritize insulin, inflammation |

## Authors' Conclusion

> "In these adults, LDL cholesterol should **not by itself guide medical decisions**."

## Context Matters

| Population | LDL Interpretation |
|------------|-------------------|
| **Without CVD** | Less predictive |
| **With established CVD** | More relevant |
| **With metabolic syndrome** | Context-dependent |

## Evidence

> "The lowest mortality risk was observed in individuals with LDL cholesterol between 100 and 189 mg/dL — clearly higher than what current guidelines often consider 'optimal'."
> — BMJ Open 2024, 177,000 participants
