## Zettels Extracted

### 1. Walking 8,000 Steps Reduces All-Cause Mortality by More Than Half
- **Claim:** Taking 8,000 steps 1-2 times per week reduces all-cause mortality risk by over 50% over 10 years — with 70% reduction in men and 50% reduction in women, representing one of the most powerful health interventions available.
- **Evidence:** Key finding: 8,000 steps 1-2x/week → >50% mortality risk reduction over 10 years; sex differences: men = 70% reduction, women = 50% reduction; dose-response: increasing to 3-7 days/week provides additional risk reduction; clinical significance: benefit of enormous magnitude — few interventions demonstrate effect sizes of this order; practical recommendations: take stairs whenever possible, take 5-minute walking breaks throughout the day, walk around the block, park farther from home or work, gradually increase daily walking; key insight: "The human body functions better when it moves"; clinical implication: walking is one of the highest-impact health interventions; minimal frequency (1-2x/week) still provides dramatic benefit; accessible to virtually everyone regardless of fitness level.
- **Reference:**  — cited as (Paluch et al., JAMA Netw Open 2023)
- **Zettel:** [[Walking_8000_Steps_Mortality_Reduction_Z-002-0875]]

### 2. Physical Activity Minimum Effective Dose — 1-2 Times Per Week
- **Claim:** Even minimal physical activity frequency provides substantial health benefits — the JAMA study shows that 8,000 steps taken just 1-2 times per week achieves over 50% mortality risk reduction, making this an achievable "minimum effective dose" for people who cannot exercise daily.
- **Evidence:** The study is particularly significant because: even those who cannot walk daily achieve impressive health benefits, 8,000 steps just once or twice weekly provides >50% mortality reduction, removes barrier of "daily exercise requirement" that discourages many people; minimum effective dose: 1-2 days/week = substantial benefit (foundation), 3-7 days/week = additional incremental benefit; comparison with other interventions: few drugs or medical interventions achieve 50%+ mortality reduction, walking is free and accessible to all, no side effects unlike medications; practical implementation: start with 1-2 days/week if daily walking not achievable, 8,000 steps = approximately 6-7 km or 45-60 minutes, can be accumulated throughout the day; clinical implication: "some walking" vastly better than "no walking"; remove perfectionism barrier — 1-2x/week still transformative; focus on consistency over intensity.
- **Reference:**  — cited as (Paluch et al., JAMA 2023)
- **Zettel:** [[Physical_Activity_Minimum_Effective_Dose_Z-002-0876]]

### 3. Movement Is Fundamental to Human Body Function
- **Claim:** The human body is designed to move — walking and physical activity are not optional "extras" but fundamental requirements for optimal physiological function, gene expression, metabolic health, and longevity.
- **Evidence:** Key insight: "The human body functions better when it moves"; evolutionary perspective: humans evolved as walking/ endurance animals, sedentary lifestyle is evolutionary mismatch, modern environment designed for sitting (cars, desks, screens); physiological mechanisms: movement improves insulin sensitivity, enhances cardiovascular function, supports brain health and cognition, reduces inflammation, optimizes gene expression; metabolic benefits: improved glucose disposal, enhanced fat oxidation, better mitochondrial function, hormonal optimization; connections: [[MOC-Metabolic-Syndrome]] — walking improves metabolic health, [[MOC-Exercise-Physiology]] — foundational movement pattern, [[MOC-Longevity]] — walking extends lifespan; clinical implication: movement should be viewed as essential nutrient, not optional lifestyle choice; prescribe walking like medication — specific dose (8,000 steps), frequency (minimum 1-2x/week); sitting is the new smoking — movement breaks essential throughout day.
- **Reference:**  — cited as (JAMA walking study)
- **Zettel:** [[Movement_Fundamental_Human_Body_Function_Z-002-0877]]
