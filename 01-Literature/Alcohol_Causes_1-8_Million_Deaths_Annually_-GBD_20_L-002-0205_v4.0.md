## Core Concepts Extracted
**Alcohol consumption causes 1.8 million deaths annually** (2019 data), making it the 9th leading risk factor for death globally — and critically, **there is no safe level of alcohol consumption**, with risk increasing progressively from the first drink.

## Zettels Extracted

### 1. Alcohol 1.8 Million Annual Deaths
- **Claim:** Alcohol use is responsible for approximately 1.8 million deaths annually worldwide, ranking as the 9th leading risk factor for death globally — with the majority (2.07 million) being male deaths and a significant burden (374,000) among females.
- **Evidence:** GBD 2019 analysis of 195 countries shows alcohol causes death through multiple mechanisms: liver disease (~400,000), cancer (~250,000), cardiovascular disease (~200,000), injuries (~350,000), and infectious diseases (~150,000).
- **Reference:** [[GBD_2019_Alcohol_1.8M_Deaths_R-002-0045]] — cited as (GBD 2019 Alcohol)
- **Zettel:** [[Alcohol_1.8_Million_Annual_Deaths_Z-002-0753]]

### 2. Alcohol Chronic Disease Link
- **Claim:** Alcohol contributes to chronic disease through multiple mechanisms — direct liver toxicity causing cirrhosis and hepatitis, carcinogenic effects increasing risk of multiple cancers (oral, throat, esophageal, liver, breast), and metabolic effects contributing to cardiovascular disease.
- **Evidence:** Dose-response relationships exist for most alcohol-related diseases; even low-level consumption increases risk of certain cancers and cardiovascular conditions; chronic heavy use causes progressive organ damage.
- **Reference:** [[GBD_2019_Alcohol_1.8M_Deaths_R-002-0045]] — cited as (GBD 2019 Alcohol)
- **Zettel:** [[Alcohol_Chronic_Disease_Link_Z-002-0754]]

### 3. Zero Alcohol Safer Than Low Consumption
- **Claim:** The GBD 2019 study demonstrates that **zero alcohol consumption is safer than any level of consumption** — challenging previous beliefs about protective effects of moderate drinking and establishing that health risks increase progressively from the first drink.
- **Evidence:** Population-level analysis shows J-shaped or linear risk curves for most outcomes; protective effects observed in some studies attributed to confounding (healthier lifestyles of moderate drinkers); when properly controlled, any alcohol increases risk relative to abstinence.
- **Reference:** [[GBD_2019_Alcohol_1.8M_Deaths_R-002-0045]] — cited as (GBD 2019 Alcohol)
- **Zettel:** [[Zero_Alcohol_Safer_Than_Low_Z-002-0755]]
