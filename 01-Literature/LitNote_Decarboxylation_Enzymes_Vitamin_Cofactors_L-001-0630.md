# Decarboxylation Enzymes and Vitamin Cofactors

> **Source:** ChatGPT Conversation CGPT-0586 (August 30, 2023)  
> **Key Themes:** Decarboxylation reactions, B-vitamin cofactors, enzyme mechanisms, cofactor deficiencies

## Core Concepts Extracted

### 1. What Is Decarboxylation?

**Definition:** Removal of a carboxyl group (-COOH) from an organic molecule, releasing CO₂

**Importance:**
- Critical for energy extraction (Krebs cycle)
- Amino acid metabolism
- Neurotransmitter synthesis
- Fatty acid metabolism

---

### 2. Key Decarboxylation Enzymes by Pathway

#### Krebs Cycle Decarboxylases

**Isocitrate Dehydrogenase:**
- Substrate: Isocitrate → α-ketoglutarate + CO₂
- Cofactor: NAD⁺ (NADH produced)
- Dual function: Dehydrogenation + decarboxylation
- Regulation: Activated by ADP, inhibited by ATP/NADH

**α-Ketoglutarate Dehydrogenase Complex:**
- Substrate: α-ketoglutarate → Succinyl-CoA + CO₂
- Cofactors: 
  - **Thiamine pyrophosphate (TPP)** — Vitamin B1
  - **Lipoic acid**
  - **Coenzyme A**
  - **FAD**
  - **NAD⁺**
- Similar to pyruvate dehydrogenase complex

#### Pyruvate Metabolism

**Pyruvate Dehydrogenase Complex:**
- Substrate: Pyruvate → Acetyl-CoA + CO₂
- Cofactors: TPP, lipoic acid, CoA, FAD, NAD⁺
- **Critical link:** Glycolysis → Krebs cycle

**Pyruvate Decarboxylase (yeast):**
- Substrate: Pyruvate → Acetaldehyde + CO₂
- Cofactor: TPP
- Function: Fermentation (alcohol production)

#### Amino Acid Metabolism

**Glutamate Decarboxylase (GAD):**
- Substrate: Glutamate → GABA + CO₂
- Cofactor: **Pyridoxal phosphate (PLP)** — Vitamin B6
- Function: Neurotransmitter synthesis

**Aromatic L-amino Acid Decarboxylase (AADC):**
- Substrates: L-DOPA → Dopamine, 5-HTP → Serotonin
- Cofactor: PLP
- Function: Catecholamine/serotonin synthesis

**Histidine Decarboxylase:**
- Substrate: Histidine → Histamine + CO₂
- Cofactor: PLP
- Function: Immune response, gastric acid secretion

#### Fatty Acid Metabolism

**Methylmalonyl-CoA Decarboxylase:**
- Substrate: Methylmalonyl-CoA → Propionyl-CoA + CO₂
- Cofactor: **Biotin** (Vitamin B7) in reverse reaction
- Function: Odd-chain fatty acid metabolism

### 3. Vitamin Cofactors Required

| Vitamin | Active Form | Enzymes Using It | Function |
|---------|-------------|------------------|----------|
| **B1 (Thiamine)** | TPP | Pyruvate DH, α-ketoglutarate DH, transketolase | Decarboxylation, C-C bond cleavage |
| **B5 (Pantothenic Acid)** | CoA | All acyl-CoA reactions | Acyl group carrier |
| **B6 (Pyridoxine)** | PLP | Amino acid decarboxylases | Amino group transfer, decarboxylation |
| **B7 (Biotin)** | Biotin-lysine | Carboxylases (reverse = decarboxylation) | CO₂ fixation/release |
| **B12 (Cobalamin)** | Adenosylcobalamin | Methylmalonyl-CoA mutase | Carbon skeleton rearrangement |

### 4. Mineral Cofactors

**Magnesium (Mg²⁺):**
- Required by many decarboxylases
- Stabilizes enzyme-substrate complexes
- Examples: Pyruvate carboxylase, phosphoenolpyruvate carboxykinase

**Manganese (Mn²⁺):**
- Some enzymes prefer Mn²⁺ over Mg²⁺
- Arginase, pyruvate carboxylase (in some tissues)

**Iron (Fe²⁺/Fe³⁺):**
- Aconitase (Fe-S cluster)
- Some hydroxylases

---

### 5. Dehydrogenases vs. Decarboxylases

**Dehydrogenases:**
- Remove hydrogen atoms (H⁺ + e⁻)
- Redox reactions (oxidation-reduction)
- Cofactors: NAD⁺, NADP⁺, FAD
- Example: Alcohol dehydrogenase, lactate dehydrogenase

**Decarboxylases:**
- Remove carboxyl group (-COOH)
- Release CO₂
- Typically not redox reactions (unless dual-function)
- Cofactors: TPP, PLP, biotin

**Dual-Function Enzymes:**
**Isocitrate Dehydrogenase:**
- Step 1: Dehydrogenation (isocitrate → oxalosuccinate) — NAD⁺ reduced
- Step 2: Decarboxylation (oxalosuccinate → α-ketoglutarate) — CO₂ released

### 6. Clinical Deficiency States

**Thiamine (B1) Deficiency — Beriberi:**
- Impaired pyruvate and α-ketoglutarate dehydrogenase
- Lactic acidosis
- Neurological symptoms (Wernicke-Korsakoff)

**Vitamin B6 Deficiency:**
- Impaired neurotransmitter synthesis
- Seizures, depression, peripheral neuropathy
- Impaired amino acid metabolism

**Biotin Deficiency:**
- Rare (avidin in raw egg whites)
- Dermatitis, alopecia, neurological symptoms
