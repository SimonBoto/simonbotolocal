## Core Concepts Extracted
**Personalized vitamin D dosing based on blood levels reduces heart attack risk by 52%** in adults with established cardiovascular disease — the TARGET-D study demonstrates that individualized dosing to achieve 40-80 ng/mL is superior to standard care, with most participants requiring >5,000 IU/day (6x FDA recommendations).

## Zettels Extracted

### 1. Personalized Vitamin D Dosing Reduces Heart Attack Risk by 52%
- **Claim:** Personalized vitamin D supplementation with dosing adjusted every 3 months to maintain blood levels of 40-80 ng/mL reduces heart attack risk by 52% compared to standard care in adults with established cardiovascular disease.
- **Evidence:** TARGET-D study randomized participants to personalized dosing vs usual care; personalized group had 52% lower MI risk over ~4 years; 85% started below 40 ng/mL despite "adequate" levels by conventional standards; demonstrates that one-size-fits-all dosing fails many patients.
- **Reference:**  — cited as (May et al., AHA 2025)
- **Zettel:** [[Personalized_Nutrition_50pct_Risk_Reduction_Z-002-0789]]

### 2. Standard Vitamin D Dosing Is Inadequate for Most Patients
- **Claim:** Standard fixed-dose vitamin D supplementation (e.g., 800 IU/day) is inadequate for most patients to reach optimal levels — the TARGET-D study found 85% of participants started below 40 ng/mL, and nearly half needed >5,000 IU/day to reach target, suggesting current FDA recommendations are insufficient for many individuals.
- **Evidence:** TARGET-D baseline data: 85% below 40 ng/mL; dosing requirements: nearly 50% needed >5,000 IU/day (6x FDA's 800 IU recommendation); dose adjustments made every 3 months based on blood testing; individualized approach required 4-6x higher doses than standard recommendations for many participants.
- **Reference:**  — cited as (May et al., AHA 2025)
- **Zettel:** [[Metabolomic_Personalization_Z-002-0790]]

### 3. Targeted Micronutrient Repletion Requires Blood Testing and Adjustment
- **Claim:** Effective micronutrient repletion requires regular blood testing and dose adjustment — the TARGET-D protocol of measuring levels at baseline, adjusting dose every 3 months until target reached (40-80 ng/mL), then annual monitoring represents a model for personalized nutrition intervention.
- **Evidence:** TARGET-D protocol: baseline measurement → dose adjustment every 3 months → target 40-80 ng/mL → annual monitoring once stable; this personalized approach achieved 52% risk reduction vs no intervention; contrast with standard care where 85% remained suboptimal; demonstrates that "test, treat, retest" model outperforms empirical dosing.
- **Reference:**  — cited as (May et al., AHA 2025)
- **Zettel:** [[Targeted_Micronutrient_Repletion_Z-002-0791]]
