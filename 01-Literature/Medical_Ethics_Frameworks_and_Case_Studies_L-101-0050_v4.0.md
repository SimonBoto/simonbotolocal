## Core Concepts Extracted

### 1. Emanuel's Seven Requirements for Ethical Research (2000)
> "Informed consent alone is necessary but not sufficient. All seven requirements must be satisfied."

| # | Requirement | Core Principle |
|---|-------------|----------------|
| **1** | **Social/Clinical Value** | Research answers important question; benefits justify risks |
| **2** | **Scientific Validity** | Methodologically sound; incompetent research is unethical |
| **3** | **Fair Subject Selection** | Scientific objectives drive selection; avoid exploiting vulnerable |
| **4** | **Favorable Risk-Benefit Ratio** | Benefits outweigh risks; minimize harm |
| **5** | **Independent Review** | IRB/ethics committee evaluates impartially |
| **6** | **Informed Consent** | Voluntary participation with full disclosure |
| **7** | **Respect for Enrolled Subjects** | Ongoing protection of privacy, confidentiality, well-being |

**Key Insight:** Poor-quality research is inherently unethical because it exposes participants to risk without reliable benefit to society.

### 2. Historical Ethics Violations

#### Tuskegee Syphilis Study (1932-1972)
| Aspect | Violation |
|--------|-----------|
| **Deception** | Told they received treatment; actually observed |
| **Withholding care** | Penicillin withheld even after available |
| **Exploitation** | Poor African American men targeted |
| **Outcome** | 128 deaths directly attributable |

**Legacy:** Led to National Research Act (1974), IRB requirements

#### Nazi Medical Experiments (1941-1945)
- Unconsented experiments on prisoners
- Extreme cruelty, torture, murder
- Led to Nuremberg Code (1947)

#### Willowbrook Hepatitis Study (1956-1980)
- Mentally disabled children intentionally infected
- Coercive consent from parents (preferential admission)
- Questionable risk-benefit ratio

### 3. Informed Consent Components
| Element | Requirement |
|---------|-------------|
| **Disclosure** | Full information about study |
| **Comprehension** | Participant understands |
| **Voluntariness** | No coercion or undue influence |
| **Competence** | Capacity to decide |
| **Decision** | Clear choice to participate or not |

### 4. Vulnerable Populations
| Population | Special Protections |
|------------|---------------------|
| **Children** | Assent + parental permission |
| **Prisoners** | Extra scrutiny, limited coercion |
| **Pregnant women** | Fetal protection considerations |
| **Mentally disabled** | Legally authorized representative |
| **Economically disadvantaged** | Avoid exploitation |
