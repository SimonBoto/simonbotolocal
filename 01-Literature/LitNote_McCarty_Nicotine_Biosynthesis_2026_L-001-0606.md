# Lit: Nicotine Complete Biosynthesis Pathway (McCarty Summary)

## Source
- **Full citation:** McCarty, N. (2026). *Complete biosynthesis of nicotine* [Summary of Cell paper]. X. https://x.com/nikomccarty/status/2040126879341125918
- **Original paper:** Cell — "Complete biosynthesis of nicotine" (Chinese research group)
- **Inbox origin:** [[INBOX-2026-04-04-001_Nicotine_Biosynthesis_X_Post]]
- **Reference note:** [[McCarty_2026_R-001-0606]]

## Core Claim
After nearly 200 years, the complete nicotine biosynthesis pathway has been elucidated through massive crossing experiments (643 tobacco lines), revealing a two-ring fusion mechanism and enabling pathway reconstitution in four heterologous species.

## Zettels Extracted

### 1. Nicotine Biosynthesis Two-Ring Structure
- **Claim:** Nicotine consists of a pyrrolidine ring (5 carbons) fused to a pyridine ring (6 carbons), with the fusion mechanism being the key discovery.
- **Evidence:** "Nicotine is made from two 'ring-shaped' molecules fused together. One ring has five carbons (the 'pyrrolidine ring') and the second has six carbons (the 'pyridine ring.')"
- **Reference:** [[McCarty_2026_R-001-0606]] — cited as (McCarty, 2026)
- **Zettel:** [[Nicotine_Two_Ring_Structure_Z-001-0615A]]

### 2. Pyrrolidine Ring Biosynthesis from Ornithine
- **Claim:** The pyrrolidine ring originates from ornithine via putrescine through decarboxylation, methylation, oxidation, and intramolecular cyclization.
- **Evidence:** "The pyrrolidine ring starts when ornithine, an amino acid that is not used to make proteins, gets its carbon dioxide clipped off by an enzyme, called ornithine decarboxylase, to make putrescine."
- **Reference:** [[McCarty_2026_R-001-0606]] — cited as (McCarty, 2026)
- **Zettel:** [[Pyrrolidine_Ring_Biosynthesis_Z-001-0615B]]

### 3. Pyridine Ring Biosynthesis from Aspartate via Niacin
- **Claim:** The pyridine ring derives from aspartate through oxidation to nicotinic acid mononucleotide (NAMN), with NAMN hydrolase releasing free vitamin B3 (niacin).
- **Evidence:** "To make the pyridine ring, plant cells first take aspartate (the amino acid) and oxidize it. The resulting molecule is then transformed into nicotinic acid mononucleotide... NAMN hydrolase clips off the sugar and phosphate to release pure vitamin B3"
- **Reference:** [[McCarty_2026_R-001-0606]] — cited as (McCarty, 2026)
- **Zettel:** [[Pyridine_Ring_Biosynthesis_Z-001-0615C]]

### 4. Nicotine Ring Fusion Mechanism via A622 Enzyme
- **Claim:** The two rings fuse when A622 enzyme strips CO2 from sugar-bound nicotinic acid, creating a reactive intermediate that attacks the pyrrolidine ring.
- **Evidence:** "Another enzyme, called A622, then strips off a CO2 group, making the molecule reactive again. And finally, that reactive intermediate 'attacks' the five-membered pyrrolidine ring to join the two halves together."
- **Reference:** [[McCarty_2026_R-001-0606]] — cited as (McCarty, 2026)
- **Zettel:** [[Nicotine_Ring_Fusion_A622_Z-001-0615D]]

### 5. Vacuolar Compartmentalization of Nicotine Biosynthesis
- **Claim:** Nicotine biosynthesis occurs on plant vacuole surfaces with toxic intermediates sequestered and final product stored in vacuoles via transporters.
- **Evidence:** "All of this happens on the surface of plant vacuoles. Many of the chemical intermediates are toxic, so they need to be sequestered and converted quickly. And as soon as the final nicotine gets made, a transporter pumps it into the vacuole"
- **Reference:** [[McCarty_2026_R-001-0606]] — cited as (McCarty, 2026)
- **Zettel:** [[Nicotine_Vacuolar_Compartmentalization_Z-001-0615E]]

### 6. Large-Scale Plant Crossing for Metabolic Discovery
- **Claim:** Discovery of nicotine-deficient mutant required crossing 643 inbred lines from 26 parent tobacco plants, followed by backcrossing and inbreeding.
- **Evidence:** "The authors — a Chinese group — ended up crossing 643 lines of tobacco plants to find a single mutant incapable of making nicotine. They next backcrossed and inbred that plant"
- **Reference:** [[McCarty_2026_R-001-0606]] — cited as (McCarty, 2026)
- **Zettel:** [[Plant_Crossing_Metabolic_Discovery_Z-001-0615F]]

### 7. Single Point Mutation Blocking Nicotine Production
- **Claim:** A single C→T nucleotide substitution is sufficient to completely block nicotine biosynthesis.
- **Evidence:** "They also crossed back this plant and inbred it for two generations to find the mutation responsible; a single C-to-T swap"
- **Reference:** [[McCarty_2026_R-001-0606]] — cited as (McCarty, 2026)
- **Zettel:** [[Single_Mutation_Nicotine_Block_Z-001-0615G]]

### 8. Heterologous Pathway Reconstitution in Multiple Species
- **Claim:** The complete nicotine biosynthesis pathway was functionally reconstituted in yeast, tomato, eggplant, and peas.
- **Evidence:** "RECONSTITUTED THE ENTIRE PATHWAY IN FOUR DIFFERENT SPECIES: YEAST, TOMATO, EGGPLANTS, AND PEAS"
- **Reference:** [[McCarty_2026_R-001-0606]] — cited as (McCarty, 2026)
- **Zettel:** [[Heterologous_Nicotine_Pathway_Reconstitution_Z-001-0615H]]
