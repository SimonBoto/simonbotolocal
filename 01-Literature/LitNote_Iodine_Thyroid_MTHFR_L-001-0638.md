# Iodine, Thyroid, and MTHFR Support

## Source
- **ChatGPT conversation:** [[CGPT_0199_Dysbiosis_Treatment_Side_Effects_CGPT-0199]]
- **Date:** January 4, 2025
- **Messages:** 40-120 (iodine, thyroid, MTHFR)

## Core Concepts

### 1. Iodine Supplementation Protocol
**Benefits:**
- Thyroid hormone synthesis (T3, T4)
- Detoxification (halide displacement)
- Immune defense
- Breast/prostate health

**Dosing:**
- Start: 500 mcg/day
- Target: 1 mg/day (1,000 mcg)
- Maximum: 12.5 mg (with supervision)

**Mandatory Co-factors:**
| Nutrient | Dose | Why |
|----------|------|-----|
| **Selenium** | 200-400 mcg | Protects thyroid from oxidative stress |
| **Vitamin C** | 2-3 g | Supports iodine metabolism |
| **Magnesium** | 400-600 mg | Cofactor for thyroid enzymes |
| **B-complex** | Standard | Energy, methylation support |

### 2. Selenium Considerations
**Sources:**
- Brazil nuts: 68-91 mcg/nut (variable!)
- Oysters: 77 mcg/100g
- Beef liver: 40 mcg/100g
- Supplements: 80-200 mcg

**Toxicity Risk (Selenosis):**
- UL: 400 mcg/day
- Symptoms: Brittle hair/nails, garlic odor, fatigue
- Brazil nuts: 1-2/day max (unpredictable content)

### 3. MTHFR Homozygous Considerations
**Additional Needs:**
- **Methylated folate (5-MTHF):** Avoid folic acid
- **Methylcobalamin (B12):** Not cyanocobalamin
- **Riboflavin (B2):** MTHFR cofactor
- **Magnesium:** Methylation support
- **Avoid:** Synthetic folic acid, unmethylated B12

**My Health Suitability:**
- Contains methylfolate ✓
- Contains methylcobalamin ✓
- High B2 (riboflavin) ✓
