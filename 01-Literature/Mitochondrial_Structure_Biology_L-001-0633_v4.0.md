### 1. Outer Membrane
- **Structure:** Smooth outer layer
- **Key Feature:** Contains porins (protein channels)
- **Function:** Allows passage of ions and small molecules between cytoplasm and intermembrane space

### 2. Intermembrane Space
- **Location:** Between inner and outer membranes
- **Function:** Critical for ETC and oxidative phosphorylation
- **Significance:** Proton gradient establishment

### 3. Inner Membrane
- **Structure:** Extensively folded into cristae
- **Function:** Houses ETC complexes, ATP synthase, transport proteins
- **Key Concept:** Increased surface area = enhanced ATP production capacity

### 4. Cristae
- **Definition:** Folds of inner membrane
- **Function:** House proteins/enzymes for ETC and ATP synthesis
- **Clinical Significance:** Essential for energy-producing functions

### 5. Matrix
- **Location:** Innermost compartment (enclosed by inner membrane)
- **Contents:**
  - Krebs cycle enzymes
  - Mitochondrial DNA (mtDNA)
  - Ribosomes
  - Other components for mitochondrial function

### 6. ATP Synthase
- **Location:** Inner membrane
- **Function:** Converts ADP + Pi → ATP
- **Mechanism:** Utilizes proton gradient from ETC
- **Critical Role:** The actual energy production machinery

### 7. Mitochondrial DNA (mtDNA)
- **Location:** Matrix
- **Encoding:** Some proteins for mitochondrial function
- **Limitation:** Most mitochondrial proteins encoded by nuclear DNA and imported

### 8. Ribosomes
- **Location:** Matrix
- **Function:** Translate mitochondrial-encoded proteins


## Bioenergetics Integration

### Energy Production Flow
```
Matrix (Krebs) → Intermembrane Space (ETC) → ATP Synthase (ATP)
```

### Key Efficiency Factor
**Cristae folding:** Maximizes surface area for oxidative phosphorylation machinery


## Isoprenol Biochemistry (Messages 156-162)

### Structure Identification
**Molecule:** Isoprenol (3-methyl-3-buten-1-ol)
- 5-carbon chain
- Double bond between carbons 3-4
- Hydroxyl group (-OH) at carbon 1

### Not a Ketone Body
**Ketone bodies:** Acetoacetate, Beta-hydroxybutyrate, Acetone  
**Isoprenol:** Organic alcohol (not ketone)  
**Difference:** Contains alcohol functional group (-OH), no ketone group (C=O)

### Thomas Seyfried Connection
- Used as cover image symbolism
- Represents metabolic pathway focus (ketone bodies vs other molecules)
- Symbolic of metabolic therapy approach to cancer
