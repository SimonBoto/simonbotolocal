## The Carbohydrate Hierarchy

```
Trioses (3C): Glyceraldehyde-3-P, Dihydroxyacetone-P
    ↓
Pentoses (5C): Ribose-5-P, Ribulose-5-P, Xylulose-5-P
    ↓
Hexoses (6C): Glucose-6-P, Fructose-6-P, Glucose, Fructose
```

## Pathway Integration
| Pathway | Function | Triose Connection |
|---------|----------|-------------------|
| **Glycolysis** | Energy extraction | G3P and DHAP as intermediates |
| **PPP** | Ribose + NADPH | Generates ribose-5-phosphate |
| **Krebs** | Complete oxidation | Pyruvate (from glycolysis) feeds in |

## My Notes

**Synthesis:** Trioses represent metabolic "crossroads"—simplest sugars connecting multiple fundamental pathways. Their ancient evolutionary origin suggests these were among the first metabolic processes to evolve.
