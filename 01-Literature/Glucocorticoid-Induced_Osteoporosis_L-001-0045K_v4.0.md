# Glucocorticoid-Induced Osteoporosis — Pathogenesis and Management

## Source

## Core Concepts Extracted

### Pathogenesis Overview
> "Glucocorticoids cause rapid bone loss through direct effects on osteoblasts and osteocytes, and indirect effects on calcium homeostasis."

**Key Concept:** Glucocorticoid-induced osteoporosis (GIOP) is the most common secondary cause of osteoporosis, with unique pathophysiology and management requirements.

**Clinical Imperative:** GIOP requires earlier and more aggressive intervention than primary osteoporosis.

---

### 1. Pathophysiological Mechanisms

**Zettel extracted:** [[Glucocorticoid_Pathophysiology_Osteoblast_Osteocyte_Effects_Z-001-0125]]

#### Direct Effects on Bone Cells
- **Osteoblasts:** Apoptosis, reduced differentiation, inhibited collagen synthesis
- **Osteocytes:** Apoptosis, loss of mechanosensing, reduced sclerostin regulation
- **Osteoclasts:** Initial stimulation (early phase), then suppression (late phase)

#### Indirect Effects on Calcium Homeostasis
- **Intestinal calcium absorption:** Reduced (vitamin D metabolism impaired)
- **Renal calcium excretion:** Increased
- **Parathyroid hormone:** Secondary hyperparathyroidism develops

#### Temporal Pattern
| Time | Bone Loss Pattern | Dominant Mechanism |
|------|-------------------|--------------------|
| **0–6 months** | Rapid loss (10–15%) | Osteoblast apoptosis, osteocyte death |
| **6–12 months** | Slowing of loss | Continued osteoblast suppression |
| **>12 months** | Plateau, then continued loss | Secondary hyperparathyroidism, reduced bone formation |

---

### 2. Risk Assessment and Screening

**Zettel extracted:** [[Glucocorticoid_Risk_Assessment_Screening_Z-001-0126]]

#### Risk Stratification
| Risk Level | Criteria | Intervention Timing |
|------------|----------|---------------------|
| **High** | ≥7.5 mg/day prednisone equivalents, age >65, prior fracture, T-score < -2.5 | Immediate (same day as starting GC) |
| **Moderate** | 2.5–7.5 mg/day, age 30–65, no prior fracture, T-score > -1.5 | Within 1 month |
| **Low** | <2.5 mg/day, age <30, no risk factors | Consider monitoring only |

#### Baseline Assessment
- **DXA:** Lumbar spine and hip (spine most sensitive for GIOP)
- **FRAX:** Calculated (but underestimates GIOP risk)
- **Bone turnover markers:** P1NP and CTX (baseline for monitoring)

#### Monitoring Frequency
- **High risk:** DXA every 12 months
- **Moderate risk:** DXA every 18–24 months
- **Low risk:** Clinical assessment only

---

### 3. Management Guidelines

**Zettel extracted:** [[Glucocorticoid_Management_Guidelines_Z-001-0127]]

#### Non-Pharmacologic
- **Vitamin D:** 1000–2000 IU/day (target 25(OH)D >30 ng/mL)
- **Calcium:** 1000–1200 mg/day
- **Exercise:** Weight-bearing and resistance training
- **Fall prevention:** Home assessment, vision evaluation

#### Pharmacologic Therapy
| Risk Level | First-Line Therapy | Alternatives |
|------------|-------------------|--------------|
| **High** | Bisphosphonate (alendronate, risedronate) | Teriparatide (if severe) |
| **Moderate** | Bisphosphonate or Calcium/Vitamin D | Calcitonin (limited evidence) |
| **Low** | Calcium/Vitamin D only | Monitor |

#### Special Considerations
- **Teriparatide:** Preferred for severe GIOP (T-score < -3.5 or fragility fracture)
- **Denosumab:** Alternative for patients intolerant to oral bisphosphonates
- **Avoid:** Estrogen therapy (not approved for GIOP)

---

### 4. Treatment Monitoring and Outcomes

**Zettel extracted:** [[Glucocorticoid_Treatment_Monitoring_Outcomes_Z-001-0128]]

#### Monitoring Parameters
| Parameter | Frequency | Target |
|-----------|-----------|--------|
| **DXA** | Every 12–24 months | Stabilization or improvement |
| **P1NP/CTX** | At 3 and 6 months | >30% change indicates response |
| **Serum calcium** | Annually | Normal range |
| **Renal function** | Annually | Normal |

#### Expected Outcomes
| Therapy | 12-Month DXA Change | Fracture Risk Reduction |
|---------|---------------------|------------------------|
| **Bisphosphonate** | +2–4% spine, +1–2% hip | 40–50% vertebral |
| **Teriparatide** | +7–10% spine, +2–4% hip | 60–70% vertebral |
| **Calcium/Vit D** | +0.5–1.0% spine | 20–30% vertebral |

#### Treatment Failure Criteria
- **DXA:** Continued bone loss despite therapy
- **BTMs:** No significant change at 3 months
- **Fracture:** New fragility fracture on therapy
