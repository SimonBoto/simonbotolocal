## Core Concepts Extracted

### Monitoring Rationale
> "Regular monitoring ensures treatment efficacy, identifies non-responders, and guides therapy adjustments."

**Key Concept:** Osteoporosis management requires systematic follow-up to optimize outcomes and prevent treatment failure.

**Clinical Imperative:** Monitoring is not optional — it's essential for quality care.

---

### 1. Clinical Monitoring Components

**Zettel extracted:** [[Clinical_Monitoring_Components_Z-001-0133]]

#### History and Physical
- **Fracture assessment:** New fragility fractures, location, mechanism
- **Fall assessment:** Frequency, circumstances, risk factors
- **Medication adherence:** Pill count, injection technique, side effects
- **Lifestyle assessment:** Exercise, diet, smoking, alcohol

#### Physical Examination
- **Height measurement:** Annual (loss >2 cm suggests vertebral fractures)
- **Spinal examination:** Kyphosis, tenderness, deformity
- **Neurologic assessment:** Balance, gait, fall risk
- **Dental examination:** Prior to antiresorptives (ONJ risk)

#### Laboratory Assessment
- **Baseline:** 25(OH)D, calcium, PTH, creatinine, LFTs
- **Annual:** 25(OH)D, calcium, renal function
- **As needed:** Bone turnover markers (P1NP, CTX), PTH

### 2. Imaging Monitoring Protocol

**Zettel extracted:** [[Imaging_Monitoring_Protocol_Z-001-0134]]

#### DXA Scanning
| Parameter | Frequency | Criteria |
|-----------|-----------|----------|
| **Baseline** | At diagnosis | Lumbar spine and proximal femur |
| **Anabolic therapy** | 12–18 months | Assess BMD gains |
| **Antiresorptive therapy** | 18–24 months | Assess stabilization |
| **High-risk patients** | 12 months | Monitor rapid bone loss |
| **Stable patients** | 24–36 months | Long-term monitoring |

#### DXA Interpretation
- **BMD change:** ≥3% change at spine, ≥4% at hip (least significant change)
- **Trend analysis:** Use same machine, same technologist, same analysis software
- **Vertebral fracture assessment (VFA):** When DXA done, assess for vertebral fractures

#### Alternative Imaging
| Modality | Indication | Frequency |
|----------|------------|-----------|
| **VFA** | Suspected vertebral fractures | With DXA |
| **Lateral spine X-ray** | Confirm VFA findings | As needed |
| **MRI** | Suspected acute fracture or malignancy | As needed |

### 3. Treatment Response Assessment

**Zettel extracted:** [[Treatment_Response_Assessment_Z-001-0135]]

#### Response Criteria
| Parameter | Responding | Non-responding | Action |
|-----------|------------|----------------|--------|
| **DXA (spine)** | ≥3% increase or stabilization | >3% decrease | Reassess adherence, consider switch |
| **DXA (hip)** | ≥4% increase or stabilization | >4% decrease | Reassess adherence, consider switch |
| **Fractures** | No new fragility fractures | New fragility fracture | Comprehensive assessment |
| **BTMs** | >30% change (P1NP↑, CTX↓) | <30% change | Assess adherence, consider switch |

#### Non-Response Algorithm
```
Non-response on DXA or BTMs
                ↓
      Assess adherence (pill count, injection log)
                ↓
      If adherent → Assess secondary causes
                ↓
      If secondary causes → Treat underlying condition
                ↓
      If no secondary causes → Switch therapy
```

#### Secondary Causes to Evaluate
- **Malabsorption:** Celiac disease, IBD, gastric bypass
- **Endocrine:** Hyperparathyroidism, hyperthyroidism, diabetes
- **Medications:** Glucocorticoids, anticonvulsants, proton pump inhibitors
- **Lifestyle:** Smoking, alcohol, sedentary lifestyle
