# Lit — Ribose Biosynthesis: Pentose Phosphate Pathway and Metabolic Integration

> **Source:** ChatGPT conversation [[CGPT_0445_Advanced_Biochemistry_Exploration_CGPT-0445]] (March 25, 2024)  
> **Core Claim:** The pentose phosphate pathway (PPP) provides ribose-5-phosphate for nucleotide synthesis and NADPH for reductive biosynthesis and antioxidant defense, with G6PD deficiency representing a common evolutionary trade-off with malaria protection.

---

## Core Concepts Extracted

### 1. PPP Oxidative Phase

**Category:** Pathway

**Key Process:** 
```
Glucose-6-phosphate → Ribose-5-phosphate + 2 NADPH + CO₂
```

**Mechanism/Steps:**
1. Glucose-6-phosphate dehydrogenase (G6PD) oxidizes G6P to 6-phosphogluconolactone, reducing NADP+ to NADPH
2. 6-Phosphogluconolactone is hydrolyzed to 6-phosphogluconate
3. 6-Phosphogluconate dehydrogenase oxidizes 6PG to ribulose-5-phosphate, producing another NADPH and CO₂

**Purpose/Significance:** Generates NADPH for reductive biosynthesis and antioxidant systems, and ribose-5-phosphate for nucleotide synthesis

**Zettel:** [[PPP_Oxidative_Phase_Z-001-0457]]

---

### 2. Ribose Structure and RNA Backbone

**Category:** Biochemistry

**Key Process:** 
```
Ribose (C5H10O5) → RNA backbone and ATP structure
```

**Mechanism/Steps:**
1. Ribose forms the 5-carbon sugar backbone of RNA nucleotides
2. In ATP, ribose connects to adenine base and three phosphate groups
3. Deoxyribose (lacking 2'-OH) forms DNA backbone

**Purpose/Significance:** Provides structural scaffold for genetic information storage and energy metabolism

**Zettel:** [[Ribose_Structure_RNA_Backbone_Z-001-0458]]

---

### 3. Triose Metabolism (G3P and DHAP)

**Category:** Metabolism

**Key Process:** 
```
Glyceraldehyde-3-phosphate (G3P) ↔ Dihydroxyacetone phosphate (DHAP)
```

**Mechanism/Steps:**
1. Triose phosphate isomerase interconverts G3P and DHAP
2. G3P enters lower glycolysis to produce ATP
3. DHAP can be converted to glycerol-3-phosphate for lipid synthesis

**Purpose/Significance:** Central hub connecting glycolysis, lipid synthesis, and PPP

**Zettel:** [[Triose_Metabolism_G3P_DHAP_Z-001-0459]]

---

### 4. NADPH Functions

**Category:** Biochemistry

**Key Process:** 
```
NADPH → Reducing power for biosynthesis and antioxidant defense
```

**Mechanism/Steps:**
1. NADPH donates electrons for reductive biosynthesis (fatty acids, cholesterol, nucleotides)
2. NADPH regenerates glutathione (GSH) from oxidized glutathione (GSSG) via glutathione reductase
3. NADPH powers NADPH oxidase for immune function

**Purpose/Significance:** Essential for anabolic processes and protection against oxidative stress

**Zettel:** [[NADPH_Functions_Antioxidant_Defense_Z-001-0460]]

---

### 5. G6PD Deficiency

**Category:** Genetic Disorder

**Key Process:** 
```
G6PD enzyme deficiency → Impaired NADPH production → Hemolytic anemia
```

**Mechanism/Steps:**
1. X-linked genetic mutation reduces G6PD enzyme activity
2. Impaired NADPH production compromises antioxidant defense
3. Oxidative stress damages red blood cell membranes
4. Results in hemolytic anemia, especially with oxidative triggers

**Purpose/Significance:** Most common human enzyme deficiency (400M+ people), with evolutionary trade-off providing malaria protection

**Zettel:** [[G6PD_Deficiency_Evolutionary_Trade-offs_Z-001-0461]]

---

### 6. Nucleobase Phosphorylation

**Category:** Biochemistry

**Key Process:** 
```
Nucleobase + PRPP → Nucleotide + PPi
```

**Mechanism/Steps:**
1. Phosphoribosyl pyrophosphate (PRPP) provides ribose-5-phosphate moiety
2. Nucleobase phosphoribosyltransferases (e.g., HGPRT) attach nucleobase to PRPP
3. Forms nucleotide monophosphates for DNA/RNA synthesis

**Purpose/Significance:** Salvage pathway for nucleotide synthesis, critical for rapidly dividing cells

**Zettel:** [[Nucleobase_Phosphorylation_Nucleotide_Formation_Z-001-0465]]
