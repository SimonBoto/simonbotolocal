## Core Concepts Extracted

### 1. Pharmacometabolomics Framework
> "Patients can be categorized as good or poor responders to therapy through the determination of 'metabotypes.'"

**Key insight:** Clayton's experiments with paracetamol established that pre-treatment metabolic profiles predict drug response.

**Three axes of Clinical Metabolomics:**
1. Treatment selection based on metabolic profile (type, dose, duration)
2. Diagnosis/management of underlying metabolic disorders (IR, dysbiosis)
3. Targeting metabolic pathways — recognizing metabolic reprogramming enables new drug development

**Zettel extracted:** [[Pharmacometabolomics_Framework_Metabotypes_Z-101-0001.md]

### 2. Metformin: AMPK/mTOR Immunometabolism
> "Metformin inhibits reactive oxygen species production and IL-1β cytokine production in activated macrophages."

**Mechanism:**
- Inhibits mitochondrial Complex I → ↓ ATP → AMPK activation
- AMPK activates fatty acid oxidation, inhibits lipogenesis
- Inhibits mTORC1 → blocks c-myc and Hif-1α
- ↓ T-cell proliferation, ↑ B-cell function

**Clinical applications:** Systemic lupus erythematosus, colitis, arthritis, obesity

**Zettel extracted:** [[Metformin_AMPK_mTOR_Immunometabolism_Z-101-0002.md]

---

### 3. Rapamycin: mTOR Inhibition & Immune Tolerance
> "Rapamycin is an mTOR inhibitor resulting in induction of immune tolerance."

**Key findings:**
- mTOR activation precedes SLE onset
- Rapamycin improves clinical picture in active disease
- Restores cellular homeostasis

**Mechanism in T-cells:**
- Inhibits mTOR → blocks IL-2 and IFN-γ production
- Promotes regulatory T-cell functions
- Blocks M2 macrophage differentiation

**Zettel extracted:** [[Rapamycin_mTOR_Immune_Tolerance_Z-101-0003.md]

---

### 4. Immunometabolism: Warburg Effect in Inflammation
> "Cell dependence on glycolysis at the expense of oxidative phosphorylation has been observed in M1 macrophages."

**The Warburg Effect in Immunity:**
| Phenotype | Metabolism | Cell Types |
|-----------|------------|------------|
| Pro-inflammatory | Aerobic glycolysis | M1 macrophages, activated T-cells |
| Anti-inflammatory | Oxidative phosphorylation | M2 macrophages, regulatory T-cells |

**Therapeutic targets:** 2-deoxyglucose (2-DG), Glut-1 inhibitors, GAPDH modulation

**Zettel extracted:** [[Immunometabolism_Warburg_Inflammation_Z-101-0004.md]

---

### 5. Methotrexate Low-Dose Mechanism
> "Methotrexate's immunosuppressive action in autoimmunity is achieved at 1/100th the dose used for cancer."

**Mechanism (low-dose):**
- Inhibits AICAR transformylase → AICAR accumulates
- AICAR releases extracellular adenosine (paracrine)
- Adenosine has anti-inflammatory effects (M2 differentiation, blocks neutrophils, promotes T-reg proliferation)
- Also: AICAR activates AMPK

**Key point:** Folic acid does NOT impair efficacy — mechanism NOT folate antagonism at this dose

**Zettel extracted:** [[Methotrexate_Low_Dose_Mechanism_Adenosine_Z-101-0005.md]

---

### 6. Dimethyl Fumarate (DMF): Nrf2 Antioxidant Pathway
> "DMF induces expression of genes contributing to antioxidant mechanisms, acting on transcription factor Nrf2."

**Mechanism:**
- Activates Nrf2 transcription factor
- Induces glutamate-cysteine ligase, glutathione transferase, PPP enzymes
- Modulates lipid metabolism
- Affects CD8+ T-cell numbers

**Clinical:** Multiple sclerosis, psoriasis

**Zettel extracted:** [[DMF_Nrf2_Antioxidant_Pathway_Z-101-0006.md]

---

### 7. Precision Medicine: 30-60% Response Rate Problem
> "Effectiveness of approved therapies for chronic diseases ranges 30-60%."

**Key statistics:**
- Anti-TNFα: 40% non-responders (2017 data)
- Biologics: Only for DMARD non-responders due to cost
- Adverse effects: Dementia, severe infections, lymphoma

**The Problem:** Empirical/reactive medicine — treating chronic diseases like acute conditions

**Solution:** Pharmacometabolomics — matching therapy to metabolic profile

**Zettel extracted:** [[Precision_Medicine_Response_Rate_Problem_Z-101-0007.md]

## My Notes

**Synthesis:** This is the most METHAP-relevant course unit. It provides:
1. Pharmacometabolomics framework validating adipic acid as drug response biomarker
2. Metformin/VitD shared AMPK/mTOR pathways
3. Immunometabolism explaining chronic inflammation in IR
4. Therapeutic targets beyond immunosuppression

**Connection to METHAP/PhD:**
- **Pharmacometabolomics Cycle:** Pre-treatment metabotype → predict response → select therapy → post-treatment metabotype → measure efficacy
- **Adipic acid role:** Both predictive and pharmacodynamic biomarker for VitD response
- **AMPK/mTOR:** Shared pathway between metformin and VitD
- **Warburg Effect:** Explains M1 macrophage polarization in adipose tissue IR

**Questions:**
- Can adipic acid stratify VitD responders vs non-responders?
- Does VitD metabolic-reprogram immune cells like metformin?
