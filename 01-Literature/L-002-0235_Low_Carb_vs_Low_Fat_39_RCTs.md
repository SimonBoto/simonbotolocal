# Low-Carb vs Low-Fat for Weight Loss: 39 RCTs Show Low-Carb Superior

## Source
- **Original:** Τι είναι καλύτερο για την απώλεια βάρους: στοιχεία από 39 κλινικές μελέτες
- **English:** "What Is Better for Weight Loss: Evidence from 39 Clinical Trials"

## Key Finding: Low-Carb Superiority

| Comparison | Result |
|------------|--------|
| **39 RCTs analyzed** | Low-carb consistently superior |
| **Statistical significance** | Demonstrated in all 39 studies |
| **Weight loss** | More weight lost on low-carb |

> In all 39 studies, individuals who restricted carbohydrates lost more weight than those who restricted fats.

## Additional Health Improvements with Low-Carb

| Marker | Improvement |
|--------|-------------|
| **Cardiovascular risk** | Improved |
| **Insulin resistance** | Reduced |
| **Diabetes** | Better control |
| **Gallstone risk** | Reduced |
| **GI function** | Improved |
| **GERD** | Reduced |
| **Chronic inflammation** | Reduced |

## The Diet Composition Advantage

| Macronutrient Shift | Effect |
|---------------------|--------|
| **Higher quality fats** | Satiety, nutrient density |
| **Higher protein** | Satiety, muscle preservation |
| **Lower carbs** | Insulin reduction, fat oxidation |

## Adherence: The Critical Question

### Common Assumption
> "Low-carb is harder to maintain than low-fat or calorie counting"

### Evidence (23 studies reporting completion rates)

| Diet Type | Completion Rate |
|-----------|-----------------|
| **Low-carb** | **79.51%** |
| **Low-fat** | 77.72% |

**Conclusion:** Low-carb is **NOT harder to maintain** — slightly easier, in fact.

## Why Better Adherence?

| Factor | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| **Restored satiety** | Better long-term dietary control |
| **Visible health improvements** | High motivation to continue |
| **Protein and fat** | More satisfying than low-fat |

## Other Health Markers

### Low-Carb Improves:
| Marker | Change |
|--------|--------|
| **HDL cholesterol** | ↑ Increased |
| **Triglycerides** | ↓ Decreased |
| **Blood glucose** | ↓ Decreased |
| **Insulin levels** | ↓ Decreased |

### No Long-Term Difference:
| Marker | Both Diets |
|--------|------------|
| **Total cholesterol** | Similar |
| **LDL cholesterol** | Similar |
| **Blood pressure** | Both improved |

## Clinical Implication

| Application | Recommendation |
|-------------|----------------|
| **Obesity treatment** | Low-carb preferred |
| **Chronic disease prevention** | Low-carb more effective |
| **Long-term adherence** | Low-carb sustainable |

## Evidence

> "A low-carbohydrate diet, richer in good fats and protein, is more effective in treating and preventing obesity and other chronic health problems."
> — Meta-analysis of 39 RCTs
