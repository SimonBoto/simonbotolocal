## Zettels Extracted

### 1. LDL is Number 10 in CVD Risk Factor Hierarchy
- **Claim:** Elevated LDL cholesterol ranks #10 in cardiovascular risk factor importance — below diabetes (#1), insulin resistance (#2), hypertension (#3), smoking (#4), low HDL (#5), elevated triglycerides (#6), small dense LDL (#7), increased body weight (#8), and elevated Lp(a) (#9) — challenging the LDL-centric paradigm of cardiovascular prevention.
- **Evidence:** Risk factor hierarchy based on epidemiological evidence and clinical outcomes data; diabetes and insulin resistance have highest population attributable risk; hypertension and smoking are major independent drivers; lipid parameters (HDL, triglycerides, sdLDL) rank above total LDL; Lp(a) genetic risk exceeds total LDL risk; total cholesterol ranks #11 (least predictive); this ranking suggests current guideline focus on LDL is misplaced.
- **References:** [[Dugani_2021_Biomarkers_CHD_Women_R-001-0103]], [[Diamond_2022_Statin_Low_Carb_R-001-0101]] — cited as (Dugani et al., 2021; Diamond et al., 2022)
- **Zettel:** [[LDL_Number_10_CVD_Risk_Hierarchy_Z-002-0807]]

### 2. Metabolic Cluster Covers 80 Percent of CVD Risk
- **Claim:** The metabolic cluster — hypertension + high triglycerides + low HDL + small dense LDL + increased body weight — are not separate independent risk factors but **manifestations of underlying insulin resistance**, together covering approximately 80% of cardiovascular disease risk.
- **Evidence:** These five factors cluster together metabolically; all improve with insulin-sensitizing interventions (low-carb diets, exercise, weight loss, metformin); treating them as separate leads to polypharmacy (BP meds, statins, fibrates) rather than addressing root cause; insulin resistance is the common denominator; population attributable risk for metabolic factors ~80% vs. small fraction for LDL alone; guideline imbalance: pharmaceutical treatment detailed, lifestyle interventions vague.
- **References:** [[Dugani_2021_Biomarkers_CHD_Women_R-001-0103]], [[Diamond_2022_Statin_Low_Carb_R-001-0101]] — cited as (Dugani et al., 2021; Diamond et al., 2022)
- **Zettel:** [[Metabolic_Cluster_80_Percent_CVD_Risk_Z-002-0808]]

### 3. Small Dense LDL Quality Matters More Than Total LDL Quantity
- **Claim:** Small dense LDL (sdLDL) is more atherogenic than total LDL cholesterol quantity — sdLDL represents "damaged" cholesterol created by glucose exposure and oxidation, making it more dangerous than the total LDL number alone.
- **Evidence:** Framingham Offspring Study: sdLDL most atherogenic lipoprotein parameter; sdLDL characteristics: more easily oxidized, more easily penetrates arterial wall, more strongly associated with CVD than total LDL; created by glucose exposure (glycation), smoking, alcohol, processed foods; sdLDL ranks #7 in risk hierarchy vs. total LDL at #10; clinical implication: quality of LDL particles matters more than quantity; reducing sdLDL requires addressing metabolic dysfunction (glucose control, smoking cessation, reducing processed foods) not just lowering total LDL.
- **References:** [[Ikezaki_2021_Small_Dense_LDL_Framingham_R-001-0007]], [[Diamond_2022_Statin_Low_Carb_R-001-0101]] — cited as (Ikezaki et al., 2021; Diamond et al., 2022)
