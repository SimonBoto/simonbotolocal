### 2. Linoleic Acid Metabolic Fate — The Delta-6 Desaturase Bottleneck
> "Excessive intake of omega-6 fatty acids can lead to an imbalance in the omega-6 to omega-3 ratio, promoting the synthesis of pro-inflammatory eicosanoids."

**Zettel extracted:** [[Linoleic_Acid_Metabolic_Fate_Desaturase_Competition_Z-001-0946]

**Pathway:**
```
Linoleic Acid (18:2 n-6)
    ↓ Delta-6 Desaturase (rate-limiting, shared with omega-3)
Gamma-Linolenic Acid (GLA, 18:3 n-6)
    ↓ Elongase
Dihomo-Gamma-Linolenic Acid (DGLA, 20:3 n-6)
    ↓ Delta-5 Desaturase
Arachidonic Acid (AA, 20:4 n-6) ← PRIMARY END PRODUCT (95%+)
    ↓ COX-2 / LOX enzymes
Pro-inflammatory eicosanoids (PGE2, LTB4, TXA2)
```

**Critical Competition:**
- Delta-6 desaturase processes BOTH omega-6 AND omega-3
- High linoleic acid intake (20-30g/day) saturates enzyme capacity
- Reduced conversion of ALA (omega-3) to EPA/DHA
- Result: Pro-inflammatory AA-derived products dominate

### 3. Tissue Incorporation and Membrane Composition
**Zettel extracted:** [[Omega_6_Tissue_Incorporation_Membrane_Fluidity_Z-001-0947]

**Adipose Tissue Storage:**
- Linoleic acid half-life in human adipose: **600-680 days**
- Modern adipose tissue: 15-25% linoleic acid (vs. 2-3% in 1900)
- Adipose serves as "reservoir" releasing linoleic acid during fasting

**Cell Membrane Effects:**
- High omega-6 intake → Increased AA in phospholipid bilayer
- Membrane fluidity changes → Receptor function altered
- Increased susceptibility to lipid peroxidation
- Endothelial dysfunction → Atherosclerosis risk

### 4. Omega-6 to Omega-3 Ratio — Evolutionary Context
**Zettel extracted:** [[Omega_6_Omega_3_Ratio_Evolutionary_Context_Z-001-0948]

| Era / Diet | Omega-6:Omega-3 Ratio | Health Outcome |
|------------|----------------------|----------------|
| **Hunter-gatherer** | 1:1 to 4:1 | Low inflammatory disease |
| **Traditional Mediterranean** | 4:1 to 8:1 | Cardiovascular protection |
| **Modern Western** | 15:1 to 25:1 | High metabolic disease |
| **Optimal target** | ≤4:1 | Anti-inflammatory effects |

**Research Evidence:**
- 4:1 ratio: 70% reduction in total mortality
- 2-3:1: Suppressed inflammation in rheumatoid arthritis
- 5:1: Positive outcomes in asthma
- 10:1: Upper limit of metabolic safety

### 5. Cardiolipin and Mitochondrial Function
**Zettel extracted:** [[Cardiolipin_Omega_6_Mitochondrial_Dysfunction_Z-001-0949]

**Cardiolipin Role:**
- Unique mitochondrial phospholipid (4 acyl chains)
- Essential for ETC complex stability and OXPHOS
- High linoleic acid → Cardiolipin enriched with omega-6
- Oxidized cardiolipin → Cytochrome c release → Apoptosis
- Result: Mitochondrial dysfunction, insulin resistance
