## Hormones Covered

| Hormone | Source | Primary Function |
|---------|--------|------------------|
| **Estradiol** | Testes (aromatization), peripheral tissues | Bone health, libido, erectile function |
| **Testosterone** | Leydig cells (testes) | Masculinization, muscle, bone, libido |
| **Prolactin** | Anterior pituitary | Lactation, testosterone regulation |
| **SHBG** | Liver | Transport, bioavailability regulation |
| **DHEA-S** | Adrenal cortex | Adrenal androgen, precursor |

## Clinical Scenarios Overview

### Scenario 1: Hypogonadism + Hyperprolactinemia

**Pattern:**
- ↑ Estradiol, ↓ Testosterone
- ↑ Prolactin
- ↑ SHBG
- ↓ DHEA-S

**Differential:**
- Prolactinoma
- Secondary hypogonadism
- Adrenal insufficiency

### Scenario 2: Steroid Abuse/Androgen Excess

**Pattern:**
- ↓ Estradiol (low aromatization)
- ↑ Testosterone
- ↓ Prolactin
- ↓ SHBG
- ↑ DHEA-S

**Differential:**
- Anabolic steroid use
- Testicular tumor
- Adrenal tumor

### Scenario 3: Severe Combined Deficiency

**Pattern:**
- ↓↓ Estradiol
- ↑ Testosterone (erratic)
- ↑↑ Prolactin
- ↑↑ SHBG
- ↓↓ DHEA-S

**Differential:**
- Large pituitary adenoma
- Severe adrenal insufficiency
- Aromatase deficiency

### Scenario 4: Aromatase Excess + Secondary Hypogonadism

**Pattern:**
- ↑ Estradiol
- ↓ Testosterone
- ↑ Prolactin
- ↓ SHBG
- ↑ DHEA-S

**Differential:**
- Aromatase excess syndrome
- Secondary hypogonadism
- Adrenal hyperplasia

## Normal Reference Ranges

| Hormone | Normal Range (Men) | Notes |
|---------|-------------------|-------|
| **Estradiol** | 10-50 pg/mL | Age-dependent |
| **Testosterone** | 300-1000 ng/dL | Morning sample preferred |
| **Prolactin** | <20 ng/mL | Can rise with stress |
| **SHBG** | 10-57 nmol/L | Varies with age, liver function |
| **DHEA-S** | 280-640 μg/dL | Age-declining |

## Erectile Dysfunction Workup

### Essential Tests
1. **Testosterone** (total and free)
2. **Prolactin**
3. **FSH and LH** (if hypogonadism suspected)
4. **Thyroid function**
5. **Metabolic panel** (diabetes, lipids)

### Role of FSH and LH
- **Primary hypogonadism:** High FSH/LH + low testosterone
- **Secondary hypogonadism:** Low/normal FSH/LH + low testosterone

### ACTH Role
- **Not routine** for ED evaluation
- Consider if adrenal insufficiency suspected
