# Vitamin D RDA Statistical Error

## Source
- **Original:** Το Στατιστικό Λάθος που Οδήγησε στις Χαμηλές Συστάσεις βιταμίνης D
- **English:** "The Statistical Error That Led to Low Vitamin D Recommendations Still Used Today"
- **Primary:** Veugelers PJ, Ekwaru JP. A Statistical Error in the Estimation of the Recommended Dietary Allowance for Vitamin D. *Nutrients*. 2014;6(10):4472-4475.

## The 2011 IOM Error

### What They Recommended
| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **RDA** | 600 IU/day (ages 1-70) |
| **Goal** | 25(OH)D >20 ng/mL in 97.5% of population |
| **Method** | Based on dose-response studies |

### The Statistical Mistake
| Error | Impact |
|-------|--------|
| **Used mean requirement** | Instead of 97.5th percentile |
| **Underestimated need** | By factor of ~15× |
| **Result** | Inadequate recommendations for most people |

## Corrected Calculation

### Veugelers & Ekwaru 2014
| Finding | Value |
|---------|-------|
| **Actual RDA needed** | ~8,900 IU/day |
| **Multiple** | 15× higher than IOM |
| **Statistical method** | Proper population coverage calculation |

### Heaney 2015 Confirmation
| Finding | Value |
|---------|-------|
| **For >30 ng/mL** | 6,201 IU/day needed |
| **Method** | Meta-analysis of dose-response |
| **Conclusion** | IOM underestimates requirement |

## Why Vitamin D Matters

### Hormone Function
| Function | Scope |
|----------|-------|
| **Gene regulation** | 2,000+ genes |
| **Immune system** | Innate and adaptive |
| **Metabolism** | Insulin sensitivity |
| **Cellular differentiation** | Cancer prevention |

### Natural Production
| Source | Amount |
|--------|--------|
| **Summer sun exposure** | 15,000-20,000 IU/day possible |
| **Evolutionary context** | Humans adapted to higher levels |

## Health Outcomes

### With Adequate Levels
| Outcome | Evidence |
|---------|----------|
| **All-cause mortality** | Reduced |
| **Cancer death** | Lower risk |
| **Autoimmune disease** | Reduced incidence |
| **Metabolic health** | Less insulin resistance |

## Individual Variation

| Population | Optimal Level |
|------------|---------------|
| **General** | 40-60 ng/mL |
| **Children** | May benefit at 30 ng/mL |
| **Autoimmune patients** | May need above "normal" range |
