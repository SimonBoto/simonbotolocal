# Drug Transition Protocols — Sequential Therapy in Osteoporosis

## Source
- **ChatGPT conversation:** [[CGPT_0133_Osteopenia_Osteoporosis_Bone_Remodeling_CGPT-0133]]
- **Date:** March 07, 2025
- **Scope:** Clinical pharmacology section on switching medications

---

## Core Concepts Extracted

### Sequential Therapy Rationale
> "Switching from anabolic to antiresorptive therapy preserves bone mass gains after the anabolic phase concludes."

**Key Concept:** Anabolic agents build bone but have duration limits; antiresorptives preserve gains but don't create new bone.

**Clinical Imperative:** Without sequential therapy, rapid bone loss occurs after anabolic discontinuation.

---

### 1. Teriparatide → Antiresorptive Transition

**Zettel extracted:** [[Teriparatide_to_Antiresorptive_Transition_Z-001-0113]]

**Standard Protocol:**
- **Timing:** Begin antiresorptive therapy immediately after final teriparatide dose
- **Preferred agents:** Alendronate (oral) or Zoledronic acid (IV) 
- **Rationale:** Prevents rapid bone loss that occurs in first 6–12 months post-teriparatide

**Evidence:**
- ALGOB study: Teriparatide → alendronate superior to either alone
- BONE study: Teriparatide → zoledronic acid maintains BMD gains

**Monitoring:** DEXA at 12 months to assess preservation of gains

---

### 2. Abaloparatide → Antiresorptive Transition

**Zettel extracted:** [[Abaloparatide_to_Antiresorptive_Transition_Z-001-0114]]

**Standard Protocol:**
- **Timing:** Same as teriparatide — immediate transition
- **Preferred agents:** Alendronate (oral) or denosumab (SC)
- **Rationale:** Similar pharmacokinetics to teriparatide; identical timing requirements

**Evidence:**
- ACTIVE study: Abaloparatide → alendronate superior to either alone
- Abaloparatide → denosumab shows similar BMD preservation

**Considerations:** Denosumab may be preferred for patients with GI issues (no oral administration)

---

### 3. Romosozumab → Antiresorptive Transition

**Zettel extracted:** [[Romosozumab_to_Antiresorptive_Transition_Z-001-0115]]

**Standard Protocol:**
- **Timing:** Start antiresorptive at end of 12-month romosozumab course
- **Preferred agents:** Denosumab (SC) or alendronate (oral)
- **Rationale:** Romosozumab's dual action (anabolic + antiresorptive) provides unique transition window

**Evidence:**
- FRAME study extension: Romosozumab → denosumab maintains and extends BMD gains
- ARCH study: Romosozumab → alendronate preserves gains

**Monitoring:** DEXA at 12 months (end of romosozumab) and 24 months (12 months after transition)

---

### 4. Antiresorptive → Antiresorptive Transition

**Zettel extracted:** [[Antiresorptive_to_Antiresorptive_Transition_Z-001-0116]]

**Common Scenarios:**
- **Bisphosphonate → Denosumab:** Due to GI intolerance, inadequate response, or need for SC administration
- **Denosumab → Bisphosphonate:** Due to infection risk, cost, or preference for oral therapy
- **Bisphosphonate → Teriparatide:** Due to inadequate response or severe osteoporosis

**Critical Considerations:**
- **Drug Holiday:** For oral bisphosphonates, no washout needed
- **Denosumab discontinuation:** Requires immediate bridging (high risk of rapid bone loss)
- **Teriparatide after bisphosphonates:** May have blunted response (consider drug holiday)

**Evidence:**
- DISCOVERY study: Denosumab → alendronate maintains BMD
- DUAL study: Bisphosphonate → teriparatide shows improved response with longer drug holiday

## Clinical Pitfalls

### Pitfall 1: Delayed Transition After Teriparatide
- **Risk:** Up to 5–7% BMD loss in first 6 months
- **Solution:** Schedule follow-up within 1 week of last teriparatide dose

### Pitfall 2: Discontinuing Denosumab Without Bridging
- **Risk:** Rapid bone loss, rebound vertebral fractures
- **Solution:** If denosumab must be stopped, start oral bisphosphonate same day

### Pitfall 3: Overlooking Dental Precautions
- **Risk:** Osteonecrosis of jaw (ONJ) with antiresorptives
- **Solution:** Dental exam before starting any antiresorptive
