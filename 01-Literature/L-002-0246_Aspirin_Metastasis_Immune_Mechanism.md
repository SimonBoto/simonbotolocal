## Core Finding

**Aspirin reduces cancer metastasis by 36% and cancer death by 51% through an immune mechanism involving platelet TXA2 and T-cell activation.**

## The Metastasis Problem

| Statistic | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Cancer deaths from metastasis** | 90% worldwide |
| **Metastasis definition** | Spread from primary tumor to distant organs |
| **Vulnerability window** | Circulating cancer cells in blood are exposed to immune attack |
| **Current problem** | Most cancer cells survive immune surveillance |

## Mechanism: The TXA2-T-Cell Axis

### Step 1: Platelet Suppression of Immunity
- Platelets produce **thromboxane A2 (TXA2)**
- TXA2 suppresses T-cell function
- Creates immunosuppressive environment for circulating cancer cells

### Step 2: Aspirin's Intervention
- Aspirin **irreversibly inhibits COX-1** in platelets
- Platelets have no nucleus → cannot regenerate COX-1
- Result: Permanent blockade of TXA2 synthesis

### Step 3: Immune Release
- Reduced TXA2 → T-cells released from suppression
- Effective immune surveillance of micrometastases
- Cancer cells become vulnerable to attack

## Clinical Evidence

| Outcome | Hazard Ratio | Interpretation |
|---------|--------------|----------------|
| **Metastasis reduction** | 0.64 (95% CI 0.48-0.84) | 36% risk reduction |
| **Cancer death reduction** | 0.49 (95% CI 0.30-0.79) | 51% risk reduction |
| **Effective dose** | 75-300mg daily | Low-dose sufficient |

## Why This Matters

### Paradigm Shift
- **Old view:** Aspirin = anti-platelet, anti-inflammatory
- **New view:** Aspirin = **immunomodulator** in cancer

### Clinical Implications
- Potential adjuvant therapy for cancer patients
- Low-cost, widely available intervention
- Risk-benefit assessment needed (bleeding risk)

### Research Significance
- First mechanistic explanation for aspirin's anti-cancer effect
- Opens door to combination immunotherapy approaches
- HLA class I expression may predict response

## Safety Considerations

> **Warning:** Aspirin use requires medical supervision due to increased bleeding risk.

- GI bleeding risk
- Hemorrhagic stroke risk
- Individual risk-benefit analysis essential
