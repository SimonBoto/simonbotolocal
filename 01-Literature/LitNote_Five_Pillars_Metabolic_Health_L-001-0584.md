## The Five Pillars

### Pillar 1: Mitochondrial Health & Metabolic Flexibility

**Core Concept:**
Metabolic flexibility is the ability to switch between glucose and fatty acids as fuel sources, which determines energy efficiency and disease resilience.

**Key Markers:**
- RER (Respiratory Exchange Ratio)
- Fasting insulin and glucose
- Ketone bodies
- Lactate/pyruvate ratio

**Clinical Application:**
- Assess fuel utilization
- Identify metabolic inflexibility
- Targeted interventions

### Pillar 2: Precision Metabolomics

**Core Concept:**
Move beyond single markers to comprehensive metabolic profiling for personalized interventions.

**Components:**
- Urine organic acids
- Plasma/serum metabolomics
- Targeted vs untargeted approaches
- Integration with genomics

### Pillar 3: Glycation Control

**Target:** HbA1c <5.5%

**Rationale:**
- Advanced glycation end-products (AGEs)
- Vascular and tissue damage
- Accelerated aging

**Measurement:**
- HbA1c
- Fructosamine
- Skin autofluorescence

### Pillar 4: Magnesium & Core Mineral Status

**Focus Minerals:**
- Magnesium (ATP synthesis, 300+ enzymes)
- Zinc (immune, hormonal)
- Selenium (antioxidant, thyroid)
- Copper (mitochondrial)
- Manganese (SOD2 cofactor)

**Assessment:**
- RBC minerals (intracellular)
- Serum (limited value)
- Functional markers

### Pillar 5: Omega-3/Omega-6 Balance

**Target Ratio:** 2-4:1 (omega-6:omega-3)

**Current Reality:** 15-25:1 (Western diet)

**Interventions:**
- EPA/DHA supplementation
- Seed oil reduction
- Fatty acid testing

## Clinical Integration

**Assessment Flow:**
1. Mitochondrial function markers
2. Comprehensive metabolomics
3. Glycation status
4. Mineral profiling
5. Fatty acid balance

**Intervention Hierarchy:**
- Foundation: Diet, sleep, movement
- Targeted: Supplementation based on testing
- Advanced: Pharmaceutical when indicated
