## Core Claim
Personalized nutritional interventions based on individual metabolic profiles, genetics, and cardiovascular risk factors reduce heart attack risk by approximately **50%** in adults with established cardiovascular disease — demonstrating superiority of individualized approaches over generic dietary advice.

## Zettels Extracted

### 1. Personalized Nutrition Reduces Heart Attack Risk 50%
- **Claim:** Nutritional interventions tailored to individual metabolic status, genetic polymorphisms, and specific cardiovascular risk profiles reduce heart attack incidence by approximately 50% in adults with established CVD, compared to standard generic dietary recommendations.
- **Evidence:** Clinical intervention studies demonstrate that personalized nutrition — accounting for individual glucose response, lipid profiles, inflammation markers, and genetic variants — achieves dramatic risk reduction that one-size-fits-all approaches cannot match; the personalization may include macronutrient ratios, specific food recommendations, and nutrient timing based on individual metabolic phenotypes.

- **Zettel:** [[Personalized_Nutrition_Halves_Heart_Attack_Risk_Z-002-0675]]

### 2. Individual Metabolic Response Determines Optimal Diet
- **Claim:** Individual variation in metabolic response to foods — including postprandial glucose, insulin sensitivity, and lipid handling — means that the "optimal diet" varies significantly between individuals; personalized nutrition matches dietary prescriptions to individual metabolic phenotypes for maximum benefit.
- **Evidence:** Research demonstrates wide inter-individual variation in metabolic responses to identical foods; continuous glucose monitoring and metabolic profiling reveal that some individuals thrive on higher carbohydrate intakes while others require carbohydrate restriction — personalized nutrition leverages these individual differences rather than applying population averages.
- **Reference:** (Pending) — cited as (Primary study, year)
- **Zettel:** [[Individual_Metabolic_Response_Determines_Optimal_Diet_Z-002-0676]]

### 3. Genetic and Microbiome Factors in Personalized Nutrition
- **Claim:** Genetic polymorphisms (APOE, FTO, MTHFR, etc.) and gut microbiome composition significantly influence individual responses to dietary interventions, enabling precision nutrition approaches that account for genetic predisposition and microbial metabolic capacity.
- **Evidence:** Nutrigenomics research identifies specific gene-diet interactions that modify cardiovascular risk; microbiome analysis reveals bacterial species that influence nutrient metabolism, inflammation, and cardiometabolic outcomes — integrating these factors enables truly personalized dietary prescriptions beyond simple phenotype assessment.
