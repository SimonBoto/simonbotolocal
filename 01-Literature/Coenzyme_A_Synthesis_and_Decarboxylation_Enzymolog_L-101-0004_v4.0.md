## Core Concepts Extracted

### 1. Coenzyme A Biosynthesis from Vitamin B5
> "Vitamin B5 (Pantothenic Acid) is the essential precursor for Coenzyme A (CoA) synthesis — a molecule critical for fatty acid metabolism, citric acid cycle, and steroid synthesis."

**Complete Synthesis Pathway:**

**Step 1: Phosphorylation**
- **Pantothenic acid + ATP → 4'-phosphopantothenate + ADP**
- Enzyme: Pantothenate kinase (PanK)
- Rate-limiting step
- Feedback inhibited by CoA and acyl-CoAs

**Step 2: Cysteine Addition**
- **4'-phosphopantothenate + L-cysteine + ATP → 4'-phosphopantothenoylcysteine + ADP + Pi**
- Enzyme: Phosphopantothenoylcysteine synthetase (PPCS)

**Step 3: Decarboxylation**
- **4'-phosphopantothenoylcysteine → 4'-phosphopantetheine + CO₂**
- Enzyme: Phosphopanthenoylcysteine decarboxylase (PPCDC)
- Requires: Mg²⁺ as cofactor

**Step 4: Adenylation**
- **4'-phosphopantetheine + ATP → dephospho-CoA + PPi**
- Enzyme: Pantetheine phosphate adenylyltransferase (PPAT)

**Step 5: Phosphorylation**
- **dephospho-CoA + ATP → CoA + ADP**
- Enzyme: Dephospho-CoA kinase (DPCK)

### 2. Decarboxylation vs Dehydrogenase Enzymes

| Feature | Decarboxylases | Dehydrogenases |
|---------|---------------|----------------|
| **Reaction** | Remove CO₂ | Transfer electrons |
| **Cofactors** | TPP, PLP, biotin | NAD⁺, FAD |
| **Energy yield** | None directly | NADH/FADH₂ → ATP |
| **Examples** | Pyruvate decarboxylase, GAD | PDH, IDH, MDH |

### 3. Clinical Relevance
- **B5 deficiency:** Rare but causes fatigue, irritability, paresthesias
- **PanK-associated neurodegeneration (PKAN):** Genetic disorder of CoA synthesis
- **CoA compartmentalization:** Mitochondrial vs cytosolic pools
