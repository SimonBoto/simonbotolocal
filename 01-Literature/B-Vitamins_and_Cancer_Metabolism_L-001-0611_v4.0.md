# B-Vitamins and Cancer Metabolism

> **Source:** ChatGPT CGPT-0264 (October 27, 2024)  
> **Key Themes:** One-carbon metabolism, methylation, folate, B12, nucleotide synthesis

---

## Core Concepts Extracted

### 1. One-Carbon Metabolism in Cancer
> "Rapidly dividing cancer cells have high demand for one-carbon units for nucleotide synthesis and methylation reactions."

**High Demand:**
- DNA replication requires nucleotides
- Methylation reactions for epigenetic regulation
- Rapid proliferation = high metabolic need
- One-carbon metabolism essential

### 2. Methylation and Epigenetics
> "B-vitamins (folate, B12) influence DNA methylation patterns—both deficiency and excess may affect cancer risk."

**Dual Role:**
- Adequate methylation: Maintains normal gene expression
- Hypomethylation: Genome instability
- Hypermethylation: Tumor suppressor silencing

### 3. Clinical Evidence and Paradox
> "High folate intake may reduce cancer incidence but potentially promote growth in existing tumors."

**The Folate Paradox:**
- Deficiency: Increases cancer risk
- Supplementation: May prevent initiation
- High intake with existing cancer: May fuel progression

## The B-Vitamin-Cancer Connection

| Vitamin | Role | Cancer Relevance |
|---------|------|------------------|
| **Folate (B9)** | DNA synthesis, methylation | Urgent need in proliferation |
| **B12** | Methionine synthase | Methylation capacity |
| **B6** | One-carbon metabolism | Nucleotide synthesis |
| **B2** | FAD for MTHFR | Methylation regulation |

## Evidence Status

| Finding | Interpretation |
|---------|----------------|
| **Folate deficiency** | Associated with cancer risk (DNA instability) |
| **Folate supplementation** | May prevent initiation (adequate methylation) |
| **High folate in existing cancer** | May promote progression (fuels proliferation) |
| **Timing matters** | Critical window hypothesis—different effects by stage |
