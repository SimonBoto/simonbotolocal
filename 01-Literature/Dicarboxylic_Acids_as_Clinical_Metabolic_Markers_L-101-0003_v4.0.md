## Core Concepts Extracted

### 1. Dicarboxylic Aciduria: Definition and Significance
> "Dicarboxylic aciduria is the abnormal excretion of dicarboxylic acids (adipate, suberate, sebacate) in urine, indicating impaired mitochondrial β-oxidation and compensatory ω-oxidation activity."

**Clinical Significance:**
- Marker of fatty acid oxidation disorders (FAODs)
- Indicator of mitochondrial dysfunction
- Early warning sign in insulin resistance progression
- Distinguishable from other organic acidemias

### 2. Complete Dicarboxylic Acid Inventory
> "The pattern of dicarboxylic acid elevation reveals the severity and type of fatty acid oxidation impairment."

| Acid | Carbons | Formula | Primary Source | Clinical Relevance |
|------|---------|---------|----------------|-------------------|
| **Oxalic** | C2 | HOOC-COOH | Endogenous (glyoxylate), dietary | Kidney stones, primary hyperoxaluria |
| **Malonic** | C3 | HOOC-CH₂-COOH | Fatty acid synthesis inhibitor | Rare genetic disorder (MAL deficiency) |
| **Succinic** | C4 | HOOC-(CH₂)₂-COOH | TCA cycle intermediate | Mitochondrial dysfunction marker |
| **Glutaric** | C5 | HOOC-(CH₂)₃-COOH | Lysine metabolism | Glutaric aciduria type I |
| **Adipic** | C6 | HOOC-(CH₂)₄-COOH | ω-oxidation (C6-C10 FAs) | Primary IR/metabolic marker |
| **Pimelic** | C7 | HOOC-(CH₂)₅-COOH | Minor ω-oxidation product | Rarely clinically significant |
| **Suberic** | C8 | HOOC-(CH₂)₆-COOH | ω-oxidation (C8-C12 FAs) | Secondary marker |
| **Azelaic** | C9 | HOOC-(CH₂)₇-COOH | Minor ω-oxidation product | Uncommon elevation |
| **Sebacic** | C10 | HOOC-(CH₂)₈-COOH | ω-oxidation (C10-C16 FAs) | Tertiary marker |

### 3. Differential Diagnosis of Dicarboxylic Aciduria

| Condition | Pattern | Key Features |
|-----------|---------|--------------|
| **Insulin resistance** | Adipate ↑ | Early marker, metabolic syndrome |
| **Carnitine deficiency** | Adipate ↑↑, suberate ↑ | Hypoketotic hypoglycemia |
| **MCAD deficiency** | All C6-C10 ↑↑↑ | Crisis during fasting |
| **Ethanol consumption** | Adipate ↑ | Acute mitochondrial damage |
| **Ketogenic diet** | All dicarboxylics ↑ | Physiological adaptation |
