# Fatty Acid Structure, Classification, and Omega System

> **Source:** ChatGPT Conversation CGPT-0586 (August 30, 2023)

## Core Concepts Extracted

### 1. Basic Fatty Acid Structure

**General Formula:**
```
CH₃(CH₂)nCOOH
```

**Components:**
- **Methyl end (ω/omega):** CH₃- (start of counting)
- **Hydrocarbon chain:** -(CH₂)n-
- **Carboxyl group:** -COOH (acid functional group)

**Saturated Example (Palmitic acid):**
```
CH₃(CH₂)₁₄COOH  or  C16H32O₂
```

**Unsaturated Example (Oleic acid):**
```
CH₃(CH₂)₇CH=CH(CH₂)₇COOH  or  C18H34O₂
```

### 2. Classification by Saturation

#### Saturated Fatty Acids (SFAs)
**Structure:** No double bonds (all single C-C bonds)
**Formula:** CnH₂nO₂
**Physical:** Solid at room temperature (fats)
**Examples:**
- Palmitic acid (C16:0) — 16 carbons, 0 double bonds
- Stearic acid (C18:0)

**Health Notes:**
- Elevated SFAs associated with cardiovascular risk
- But: Not all SFAs behave the same (stearic acid neutral)
- Context matters (carbohydrate intake, overall diet)

#### Monounsaturated Fatty Acids (MUFAs)
**Structure:** One double bond
**Formula:** CnH₂n₋₂O₂
**Physical:** Liquid at room temperature (oils)
**Examples:**
- Oleic acid (C18:1) — Found in olive oil
- Palmitoleic acid (C16:1)

**Health Notes:**
- Cardiovascular protective
- Central to Mediterranean diet benefits
- Improves insulin sensitivity

#### Polyunsaturated Fatty Acids (PUFAs)
**Structure:** Two or more double bonds
**Formula:** CnH₂n₋₄O₂ (for 2 double bonds)
**Physical:** Liquid, prone to oxidation
**Examples:**
- Linoleic acid (C18:2) — Omega-6
- Alpha-linolenic acid (C18:3) — Omega-3
- Arachidonic acid (C20:4)

**Health Notes:**
- Essential fatty acids (cannot be synthesized)
- Precursors to eicosanoids (signaling molecules)
- Balance of omega-6:omega-3 important

### 3. The Omega (ω) Classification System

**Definition:** Position of first double bond counted from methyl (omega) end

#### Omega-3 Fatty Acids
**Structure:** First double bond at C3 from methyl end
**Examples:**
- **ALA (Alpha-linolenic acid):** C18:3, ω-3
- **EPA (Eicosapentaenoic acid):** C20:5, ω-3
- **DHA (Docosahexaenoic acid):** C22:6, ω-3

**Sources:**
- ALA: Flaxseeds, walnuts, chia seeds
- EPA/DHA: Fatty fish (salmon, mackerel, sardines), fish oil, algae

**Functions:**
- Anti-inflammatory (pro-resolving mediators)
- Brain development (DHA in neural membranes)
- Cardiovascular protection
- Lower triglycerides

#### Omega-6 Fatty Acids
**Structure:** First double bond at C6 from methyl end
**Examples:**
- **LA (Linoleic acid):** C18:2, ω-6
- **AA (Arachidonic acid):** C20:4, ω-6

**Sources:**
- Vegetable oils (corn, soybean, safflower)
- Nuts and seeds
- Poultry and eggs

**Functions:**
- Pro-inflammatory eicosanoids (from AA)
- Cell membrane structure
- Essential for skin and hair

**Concern:** Excessive ω-6 (modern diet 15:1 to 20:1 ω-6:ω-3) promotes inflammation

#### Omega-9 Fatty Acids
**Structure:** First double bond at C9 from methyl end
**Examples:**
- Oleic acid (C18:1, ω-9)
- Erucic acid (C22:1, ω-9)

**Sources:**
- Olive oil (75% oleic acid)
- Canola oil
- Avocado
- Almonds

**Characteristics:**
- **Non-essential** — body can synthesize
- Most abundant fatty acid in human diet
- Cardiovascular benefits when replacing saturated fats

### 4. Classification by Chain Length

| Category | Carbons | Examples | Metabolism |
|----------|---------|----------|------------|
| **Short-Chain (SCFA)** | < 6 | Butyric acid (C4:0) | Absorbed directly, gut bacteria produce |
| **Medium-Chain (MCFA)** | 6-12 | Caprylic (C8), Capric (C10), Lauric (C12) | Rapid absorption, ketone production |
| **Long-Chain (LCFA)** | 13-21 | Palmitic (C16), Oleic (C18), EPA (C20) | Standard β-oxidation, carnitine shuttle |
| **Very Long-Chain (VLCFA)** | > 22 | DHA (C22), Nervonic acid (C24) | Peroxisomal β-oxidation |

**Clinical Relevance:**
- **MCFAs:** Ketogenic diet, malabsorption disorders
- **LCFAs:** Most dietary fats, standard metabolism
- **VLCFAs:** Peroxisomal disorders (Zellweger syndrome)

### 5. Cis vs. Trans Configuration

#### Cis Fatty Acids
**Structure:** Hydrogens on same side of double bond
**Result:** Kink/bend in carbon chain
**Physical:** Lower melting point (liquid at room temp)
**Natural:** Most natural unsaturated fats

#### Trans Fatty Acids
**Structure:** Hydrogens on opposite sides of double bond
**Result:** Linear chain (like saturated)
**Physical:** Higher melting point (semi-solid)
**Sources:**
- **Natural:** Ruminant fats (small amounts)
- **Artificial:** Partial hydrogenation (margarines, processed foods)

**Health Impact:**
- Artificial trans fats: Strong CVD risk
- Raise LDL, lower HDL
- Inflammation promotion
- **Banned/restricted** in many countries

---

### 6. Dicarboxylic Acids in Context

**Definition:** Fatty acids with carboxyl groups at BOTH ends
**General formula:** HOOC-(CH₂)n-COOH

**Examples:**
- Oxalic acid (C2): HOOC-COOH
- Malonic acid (C3): HOOC-CH₂-COOH
- Succinic acid (C4): HOOC-(CH₂)₂-COOH
- **Adipic acid (C6):** HOOC-(CH₂)₄-COOH
- **Suberic acid (C8):** HOOC-(CH₂)₆-COOH
- **Sebacic acid (C10):** HOOC-(CH₂)₈-COOH

**Formation:** ω-oxidation of monocarboxylic fatty acids
**Clinical:** Urinary markers of β-oxidation impairment
