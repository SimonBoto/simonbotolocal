# Lit: High-Fat Cheese Linked to Reduced Dementia Risk

## Source
- **Full citation:** Du Y, Borné Y, Samuelsson J, et al. (2026). High- and low-fat dairy consumption and long-term risk of dementia: evidence from a 25-year prospective cohort study. *Neurology*. 104(2):e2104343. doi:10.1212/WNL.0000000000214343
- **Inbox origin:** [[Τυρί-Υψηλών-Λιπαρών-Προστασία-Άνοιας]]
- **Reference note:** [[Du_2026_HighFat_Dairy_Dementia_25yr_R-002-0031]]

## Core Claim
High-fat dairy consumption, particularly cheese, is associated with **reduced long-term dementia risk** over 25-year follow-up, while low-fat dairy shows neutral effects — challenging conventional dietary advice to avoid saturated fat for brain health.

## Zettels Extracted

### 1. High-Fat Cheese Associated with Lower Dementia Risk
- **Claim:** Consumption of high-fat dairy products, particularly cheese, is associated with significantly reduced risk of developing dementia over 25-year follow-up compared to low or no dairy consumption, based on prospective cohort evidence.
- **Evidence:** 25-year prospective cohort study demonstrates inverse association between high-fat dairy intake and dementia incidence; high-fat cheese consumption shows protective association while low-fat dairy shows no significant association.
- **Reference:** [[Du_2026_HighFat_Dairy_Dementia_25yr_R-002-0031]] — cited as (Du et al., 2026)
- **Zettel:** [[High_Fat_Cheese_Lower_Dementia_Risk_Z-002-0663]]

### 2. Saturated Fat Dairy Not Harmful for Brain Health
- **Claim:** The association between high-fat dairy and reduced dementia risk challenges the conventional recommendation to limit saturated fat for cognitive health, suggesting that dairy-derived saturated fats may have neutral or protective effects on brain aging.
- **Evidence:** Long-term cohort data shows no increased dementia risk with high saturated fat dairy intake; potential mechanisms include fat-soluble vitamins (K2, D), bioactive lipids, and fermentation products in cheese that may support brain health.
- **Reference:** [[Du_2026_HighFat_Dairy_Dementia_25yr_R-002-0031]] — cited as (Du et al., 2026)
- **Zettel:** [[Saturated_Fat_Dairy_Not_Harmful_Brain_Z-002-0664]]

### 3. Low-Fat Dairy Shows Neutral Effect on Dementia
- **Claim:** Low-fat dairy consumption shows no significant association with dementia risk — neither protective nor harmful — suggesting that the potential cognitive benefits of dairy are specifically associated with the fat component or fat-soluble factors removed in low-fat processing.
- **Evidence:** Direct comparison in 25-year cohort shows high-fat dairy protective, low-fat dairy neutral, implying fat-soluble nutrients (vitamins K2, D, A, E), bioactive lipids, or fermentation compounds in full-fat products may drive the protective association.
- **Reference:** [[Du_2026_HighFat_Dairy_Dementia_25yr_R-002-0031]] — cited as (Du et al., 2026)
- **Zettel:** [[Low_Fat_Dairy_Neutral_Dementia_Z-002-0665]]
