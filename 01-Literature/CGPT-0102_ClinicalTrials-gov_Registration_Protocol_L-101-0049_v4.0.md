### 5. Study Description

**Brief Summary:**
> This study investigates whether urinary organic acids and plasma free fatty acids can serve as early biomarkers for insulin resistance detection. Two hundred adults will be enrolled in an observational phase; a subset will receive a 12-week nutritional intervention (low-carbohydrate diet + targeted supplementation) to assess metabolic marker changes.

**Detailed Description:**
- **Background:** Insulin resistance precedes type 2 diabetes by years; current tools detect it late
- **Innovation:** Metabolomics may enable earlier, more precise detection
- **Intervention:** Dietary + supplement approach targeting metabolic dysregulation
- **Significance:** Potential for personalized prevention strategies

### 8. Arms and Interventions

**Arm 1: Control Group**
- No intervention
- Standard dietary recommendations
- Monitoring only

**Arm 2: Intervention Group**
- Low-carbohydrate diet (12 weeks)
- Supplementation:
  - Vitamin D3 (2000-4000 IU/day)
  - Magnesium (200-400 mg/day)
  - Omega-3 fatty acids (1-2 g EPA+DHA/day)
  - Glutamine (5-10 g/day)
  - Vitamin C (500-1000 mg/day)
  - Probiotics (10-50 billion CFU/day)

### 10. Eligibility Criteria

**Inclusion:**
- Adults aged 18-65 years
- Ability to provide informed consent
- For intervention arm: confirmed insulin resistance (HOMA-IR >2.5 or physician diagnosis)

**Exclusion:**
- Diabetes mellitus (HbA1c ≥6.5%)
- Severe renal or hepatic disease
- Pregnancy or lactation
- Current use of glucose-lowering medications
- Allergies to study supplements
- Inability to comply with dietary requirements

## Key Registration Principles

- Avoid promotional language
- Present hypotheses as hypotheses (not facts)
