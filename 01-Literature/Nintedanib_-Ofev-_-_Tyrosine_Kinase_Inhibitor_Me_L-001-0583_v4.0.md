# Lit — Nintedanib (Ofev) — Tyrosine Kinase Inhibitor Mechanism and Clinical Use

> **Source:** ChatGPT conversation [[CGPT_0064_Ofev_MOA_and_effects_CGPT-0064]] (September 01, 2025)  
> **Core Claim:** This clinical synthesis establishes nintedanib (Ofev) as a multi-targeted tyrosine kinase inhibitor with distinct mechanisms against FGFR, VEGFR, and PDGFR pathways, explaining its antifibrotic effects in IPF and SSc-ILD while clarifying its safety profile and clinical applications.

---

## Core Concepts Extracted

### 1. Nintedanib Drug Overview Framework

**Category:** Pharmacology

**Key Process:** 
```
Nintedanib (Ofev) → Multi-targeted TKI → IPF, SSc-ILD, progressive fibrosing ILDs
```

**Mechanism/Steps:**
1. Generic name nintedanib, brand name Ofev
2. Class: Multi-targeted tyrosine kinase inhibitor (TKI)
3. Primary indications: Idiopathic pulmonary fibrosis (IPF) and systemic sclerosis-associated interstitial lung disease (SSc-ILD)
4. Also used for other progressive fibrosing interstitial lung diseases and advanced NSCLC in EU

**Purpose/Significance:** Provides the clinical context for nintedanib use and establishes its position in the antifibrotic therapeutic landscape

**Zettel:** [[Nintedanib_Drug_Overview_Framework_Z-001-0583A]]

---

### 2. FGFR Inhibition Mechanism Framework

**Category:** Molecular Pharmacology

**Key Process:** 
```
FGFR 1-3 inhibition → Fibroblast proliferation/migration ↓ → Myofibroblast differentiation ↓ → Fibrogenesis ↓
```

**Mechanism/Steps:**
1. Targets fibroblast growth factor receptors (FGFR 1-3)
2. Inhibits fibroblast proliferation and migration
3. Reduces myofibroblast differentiation
4. Decreases fibrogenesis and extracellular matrix deposition

**Purpose/Significance:** Explains nintedanib's primary antifibrotic mechanism in lung tissue

**Zettel:** [[FGFR_Inhibition_Mechanism_Framework_Z-001-0583B]]

---

### 3. VEGFR Inhibition Mechanism Framework

**Category:** Molecular Pharmacology

**Key Process:** 
```
VEGFR 1-3 inhibition → Angiogenesis ↓ → Vascular permeability ↓ → Vascular remodeling ↓
```

**Mechanism/Steps:**
1. Targets vascular endothelial growth factor receptors (VEGFR 1-3)
2. Inhibits pathological angiogenesis in fibrotic tissue
3. Reduces vascular permeability
4. Prevents vascular remodeling associated with fibrosis

**Purpose/Significance:** Explains nintedanib's effects on lung vasculature and its contribution to antifibrotic activity

**Zettel:** [[VEGFR_Inhibition_Mechanism_Framework_Z-001-0583C]]

---

### 4. PDGFR Inhibition Mechanism Framework

**Category:** Molecular Pharmacology

**Key Process:** 
```
PDGFR α/β inhibition → Fibroblast recruitment/proliferation ↓ → Collagen deposition ↓ → ECM accumulation ↓
```

**Mechanism/Steps:**
1. Targets platelet-derived growth factor receptors (PDGFR α/β)
2. Inhibits fibroblast recruitment and proliferation
3. Reduces collagen deposition in fibrotic tissue
4. Decreases extracellular matrix accumulation

**Purpose/Significance:** Explains nintedanib's mechanism for targeting the core cellular drivers of fibrosis

**Zettel:** [[PDGFR_Inhibition_Mechanism_Framework_Z-001-0583D]]

---

### 5. Clinical Outcomes Framework

**Category:** Clinical Pharmacology

**Key Process:** 
```
IPF: ~50% reduction in FVC decline → Slows progression → No mortality benefit
SSc-ILD: Improves progression-free survival
```

**Mechanism/Steps:**
1. In IPF patients, reduces annual forced vital capacity (FVC) decline by ~50%
2. Slows disease progression but does not reverse established fibrosis
3. In SSc-ILD, improves progression-free survival
4. Clinical benefits observed without mortality benefit demonstrated

**Purpose/Significance:** Provides evidence-based clinical outcomes to guide therapeutic decision-making

**Zettel:** [[Clinical_Outcomes_Framework_Z-001-0583E]]

---

### 6. Safety and Metabolism Framework

**Category:** Clinical Pharmacology

**Key Process:** 
```
Metabolism: Esterases → BIBF 1202 → Glucuronidation → Fecal elimination (93%)
Safety: Diarrhea (gut kinase inhibition), Hepatotoxicity (ALT/AST monitoring), Bleeding risk (VEGFR)
```

**Mechanism/Steps:**
1. Primary metabolism by esterases to active metabolite BIBF 1202
2. Secondary metabolism by glucuronidation (UGT1A1, UGT1A7, UGT1A9, UGT2B7)
3. Minimal CYP3A4 involvement
4. 93% fecal elimination, minimal renal excretion
5. Common side effects: diarrhea, nausea, vomiting
6. Serious risks: hepatotoxicity, bleeding, cardiovascular events

**Purpose/Significance:** Enables safe clinical use through appropriate monitoring and risk management

**Zettel:** [[Safety_and_Metabolism_Framework_Z-001-0583F]]
