## The Dose Distinction

| Dose Type | Route | Plasma Level | Effect |
|-----------|-------|--------------|--------|
| **Dietary** | Oral | <100 μM | Antioxidant, essential nutrient |
| **High-dose** | IV | >1,000 μM | Pro-oxidant, therapeutic |

## Administration Protocols

| Protocol | Dose | Frequency | Setting |
|----------|------|-----------|---------|
| **Standard IV** | 10-100g | 2-3x/week | Outpatient |
| **RI Protocol** | 1.5 g/kg | 3x/week | Integrated with chemo |
| **Cameron-Pauling** | 10g daily | Daily | Historical |

## Evidence Summary

| Study Type | Finding |
|------------|---------|
| **Preclinical** | Consistent anti-cancer effects |
| **Early clinical** | Improved quality of life |
| **Controlled trials** | Mixed results, methodology issues |
| **Combination** | Most promising with standard therapy |

## My Notes

**Synthesis:** High-dose vitamin C represents a metabolic approach to cancer therapy—generating oxidative stress selectively in cancer cells while potentially enhancing epigenetic regulation. Best evidence supports use as adjunctive therapy, not standalone.
