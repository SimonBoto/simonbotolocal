# Saturated Fat Stability: Oxidation Resistance and Health

## Source
- **Original:** Κορεσμένα Λιπαρά — Σταθερότητα και Μη Οξείδωση
- **English:** "Saturated Fats: Stability and Oxidation Resistance"

## Why Saturated Fats Are Stable

### Chemical Structure
| Feature | Result |
|---------|--------|
| **All bonds saturated** | No double bonds to react with oxygen |
| **Chemically stable** | Resistant to oxidation |
| **Like iron that doesn't rust** | No oxidative damage |

### Stability In Practice
| Environment | Saturated Fat Behavior |
|-------------|------------------------|
| **Cooking** | Stable at high heat |
| **In the body** | Doesn't form harmful oxidized compounds |
| **Storage** | Long shelf life |

## Comparison: Fat Types and Oxidation

| Fat Type | Double Bonds | Oxidation Risk |
|----------|--------------|----------------|
| **Saturated** | 0 | **Lowest** — most stable |
| **Monounsaturated (MUFA)** | 1 | Moderate — olive oil, avocado |
| **Polyunsaturated (PUFA)** | Multiple | **Highest** — seed oils |

### Seed Oil Problem
| Oil | Issue |
|-----|-------|
| Sunflower oil | High PUFA, oxidizes easily |
| Corn oil | High PUFA, oxidizes easily |
| Soybean oil | High PUFA, oxidizes easily |

> When heated, PUFAs cause cellular and tissue damage through oxidation.

## MUFA: The Middle Ground

| Source | Stability |
|--------|-----------|
| **Olive oil** | More stable than PUFA |
| **Avocado oil** | More stable than PUFA |
| **Nuts** | Protected by antioxidants |

Antioxidants in these foods provide additional protection from oxidation.

## Practical Considerations

### Caloric Density
| Consideration | Recommendation |
|---------------|----------------|
| **High caloric density** | All fats are calorie-dense |
| **Increased saturated fat** | Should accompany **reduced carbohydrate intake** |

### Carbohydrate Interaction
| High-Carb Foods | Effect on Lipid Metabolism |
|-----------------|---------------------------|
| Potatoes | Impair fat metabolism |
| Bread, sweets | Impair fat metabolism |
| Soda, alcohol | Impair fat metabolism |
| Pasta, rice (large amounts) | Impair fat metabolism |

## Clinical Context: Inflammation and Autoimmunity

| Condition | Saturated Fat Role |
|-----------|-------------------|
| **Chronic inflammation** | Central role in health improvement |
| **Autoimmunity** | Central role in health improvement |
| **Context** | Within healthy diet, not isolated |

## Evidence

> "Saturated fats are called saturated because all their bonds are occupied (saturated), making them not prone to oxidation. For this reason they remain stable both in cooking and in the body, not easily forming harmful oxidized compounds."
> — Fat chemistry and nutrition
