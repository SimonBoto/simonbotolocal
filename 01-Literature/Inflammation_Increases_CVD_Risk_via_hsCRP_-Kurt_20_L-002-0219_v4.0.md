# Lit: Inflammation Increases CVD Risk via hsCRP

## Source
- **Full citation:** Kurt B, Reugels M, Schneider KM, et al. "C-reactive protein and cardiovascular risk in the general population." *Eur Heart J*. 2025. doi:10.1093/eurheartj/ehaf937. PMID: 41378999.
- **Inbox origin:** 
- **Reference note:** [[Kurt_2025_CRP_CVD_Risk_R-002-0025]]

## Core Concepts Extracted
**Elevated C-reactive protein (CRP) predicts cardiovascular events even in people without known cardiovascular disease** — hsCRP serves as a critical biomarker for low-grade chronic inflammation that drives atherosclerosis and cardiovascular risk independent of traditional risk factors.

---

## Zettels Extracted

### 1. hsCRP Predicts CVD Risk in Healthy Population
- **Claim:** High-sensitivity CRP (hsCRP) predicts heart attack and cardiovascular events even in apparently healthy individuals without known CVD, making it a valuable early warning biomarker for subclinical atherosclerosis and inflammation-driven risk.
- **Evidence:** Kurt 2025 study in general population demonstrates CRP is independently associated with cardiovascular events; elevated hsCRP identifies individuals at risk who may have normal cholesterol, blood pressure, and other traditional markers — filling a critical gap in risk stratification.
- **Reference:** [[Kurt_2025_CRP_CVD_Risk_R-002-0025]] — cited as (Kurt et al., 2025)
- **Zettel:** [[hsCRP_CVD_Predictor_Healthy_Population_Z-002-0795]]

### 2. Chronic Inflammation Root Causes and Mechanisms
- **Claim:** Chronic low-grade inflammation arises from ongoing metabolic damage (ultra-processed foods, smoking, excess alcohol) combined with nutritional deficiencies (vitamins, minerals, omega-3s) that prevent proper healing and resolution of inflammatory processes.
- **Evidence:** UPF consumption, smoking, and alcohol create persistent inflammatory stimuli; micronutrient deficiencies impair resolution pathways; the combination creates a pro-inflammatory state measurable by elevated hsCRP that drives insulin resistance, endothelial dysfunction, and atherosclerosis.
- **Reference:** [[Kurt_2025_CRP_CVD_Risk_R-002-0025]] — cited as (Kurt et al., 2025)
- **Zettel:** [[Chronic_Inflammation_Root_Causes_Z-002-0796]]

### 3. Resolving Inflammation Central to CVD Prevention
- **Claim:** Addressing and resolving chronic inflammation — through dietary changes, smoking cessation, alcohol reduction, and micronutrient repletion — is a central strategy for cardiovascular disease prevention, with hsCRP serving as a measurable target for intervention success.
- **Evidence:** Clinical approach targeting root inflammatory causes (not just symptoms) reduces CVD risk; hsCRP monitoring provides feedback on intervention effectiveness; anti-inflammatory lifestyle modifications (Mediterranean diet, omega-3s, exercise) lower CRP and improve cardiovascular outcomes.
- **Reference:** [[Kurt_2025_CRP_CVD_Risk_R-002-0025]] — cited as (Kurt et al., 2025)
- **Zettel:** [[Resolving_Inflammation_CVD_Prevention_Z-002-0797]]
