# Early Detection of Insulin Resistance Improves Intervention Outcomes

**Core Claim:** Advancing early diagnostic strategies for insulin resistance enables timely interventions that are more effective than late-stage treatment.

**Evidence:**
- Current tools (HOMA-IR, HbA1c) fail to provide early detection
- Late diagnosis means metabolic syndrome already established
- Reversing established IR is harder than preventing progression

**Mechanism:**
Early detection → lifestyle/pharmacological intervention → preserved β-cell function → prevented progression to T2DM

**Implication for METHAP/UoC:**
Metabolomic biomarkers may identify "pre-pre-diabetic" state when intervention most effective.

**Quote:**
> "advance early diagnostic strategies for insulin resistance... providing a basis for timely interventions"
