# Urine Organic Acids as Real-Time Metabolic Snapshot

**Concept:** Urine organic acids reflect instantaneous metabolic pathway activity, providing a "real-time" picture of cellular metabolism.

**Why Urine:**
- Non-invasive collection
- Reflects systemic metabolism (kidney filtration)
- Accumulates over hours (integrated signal)
- Stable metabolites (when properly preserved)

**What They Show:**
- Mitochondrial function (citric acid cycle intermediates)
- Fatty acid oxidation (adipic, suberic, sebacic)
- Amino acid metabolism
- Gut microbiome activity
- Detoxification pathways

**Key Organic Acids in IR:**
| Acid | Pathway | IR Association |
|------|---------|----------------|
| Adipic | Omega-oxidation | Elevated (mitochondrial stress) |
| Suberic | Omega-oxidation | Elevated (carnitine deficiency) |
| Lactic | Glycolysis | Elevated (anaerobic shift) |
| Pyruvic | Glycolysis | Altered (PDH dysfunction) |

**Advantage Over Blood:**
- Accumulates low-abundance metabolites
- Less invasive for repeated sampling
- Reflects 24-hour metabolic flux

**Limitations:**
- Diet influences (controlled by fasting)
- Hydration effects (normalized by creatinine)
- Gut microbiome contributions (variable)
