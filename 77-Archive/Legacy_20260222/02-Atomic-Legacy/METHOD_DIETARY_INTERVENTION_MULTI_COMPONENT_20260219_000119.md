# Multi-Component Dietary Intervention Protocol

**Rationale:** Address multiple pathways of insulin resistance simultaneously through synergistic interventions

**Components:**

### 1. Mediterranean Diet Optimization
**Elements:**
- High olive oil (monounsaturated fats)
- Increased vegetables/fruits
- Whole grains over refined carbs
- Fish/legumes as primary proteins
- Limited processed foods/sugar

**Monitoring:** Dietary questionnaires, adherence scoring

### 2. Omega-3 Supplementation
**Target:** EPA/DHA
**Mechanism:**
- Improve insulin receptor signaling
- Anti-inflammatory (resolvins, protectins)
- Reduce hepatic lipogenesis

**Dose:** Not specified (literature: 2-4g EPA+DHA)

### 3. Vitamin D Supplementation
**Target:** 25(OH)D >30 ng/mL
**Mechanism:**
- Insulin receptor expression
- Anti-inflammatory immunomodulation

**Dose:** Not specified (context: 50,000 IU/week in [[20260218_Hashimoto_VitD_Protocol]])

### 4. Magnesium Supplementation
**Role:** Enzyme cofactor for glucose metabolism
**Mechanism:**
- Tyrosine kinase activity at insulin receptor
- Correct deficiency common in IR

**Dose:** Not specified (literature: 200-400mg elemental)

**Synergistic Rationale:**
- Vitamin D improves magnesium absorption
- Omega-3 may increase vitamin D bioavailability
- Mediterranean diet provides natural sources
