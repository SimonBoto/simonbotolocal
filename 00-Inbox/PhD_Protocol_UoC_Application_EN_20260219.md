## Abstract

### Background
The proposed PhD research aims to advance early diagnostic strategies for insulin resistance (IR), a critical medical condition with inadequate detection methods. Existing diagnostic tools, such as the Homeostatic Model Assessment for Insulin Resistance (HOMA-IR) and glycated hemoglobin (HbA1c), often fail to provide early and precise detection.

### Objective
This study will explore the potential of metabolomics—specifically, the analysis of urine organic acids and free fatty acids—as alternative markers for insulin resistance.

### Study Design
We will compare these markers across two distinct groups:
1. Healthy individuals (control group)
2. Insulin-resistant individuals (case group)

### Interventions
This research will assess the impact of dietary interventions, including:
- Mediterranean diet modifications
- Supplements: Omega-3, Vitamin D, and Magnesium

### Methods
- **Analytical Techniques:** Gas Chromatography-Mass Spectrometry (GC-MS)
- **Statistical Analysis:** PCA, multivariate analysis, network analysis
- **Data Management:** Large dataset handling from metabolomic analysis

### Expected Outcomes
- Dramatically improve current early diagnostic methodologies
- Provide basis for timely interventions
- Validate the role of metabolomics in clinical practice
- Assess effectiveness of diet modification and supplementation
