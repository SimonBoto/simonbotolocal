# Article 20: Artificial Sweeteners Increase Body Fat

## Key Findings

### Study Design (CARDIA 25-Year Study)
| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Participants** | 3,088 men and women |
| **Duration** | 25 years follow-up |
| **Assessment** | Diet history at baseline, year 7, year 20 |
| **Outcome** | Adipose tissue volumes via CT |

### Fat Accumulation Findings
| Fat Depot | Association with Artificial Sweeteners |
|-----------|----------------------------------------|
| **Visceral fat** | Increased |
| **Intramuscular fat** | Increased |
| **Subcutaneous fat** | Increased |

### Critical Finding: Independent of Calories
| Factor | Result |
|--------|--------|
| **Calorie intake** | Does NOT explain the association |
| **Diet quality** | Does NOT explain the association |
| **BMI** | Artificial sweeteners predict fat beyond BMI |

### Specific Sweeteners
| Sweetener | Effect |
|-----------|--------|
| **Aspartame** | Visceral & subcutaneous fat accumulation |
| **Saccharin** | Visceral & subcutaneous fat accumulation |
| **Diet beverages** | Same associations as sweeteners |

## Key References
- Steffen BT, et al. Int J Obes (Lond), 2023. PMID: 37443272
