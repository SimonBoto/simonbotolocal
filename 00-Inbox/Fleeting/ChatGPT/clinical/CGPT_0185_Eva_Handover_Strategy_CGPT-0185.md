## 2. Potential Challenges & Dangers

1. **Loss of Tacit Knowledge**  
   - Eva’s experience with GC-MS interpretation and formatting goes beyond what is likely written in a standard operating procedure (SOP). Tacit knowledge—her “insider” understanding of subtle data interpretations and troubleshooting—can be difficult to transfer quickly.

2. **Disruption in Daily Workflow**  
   - If Eva was the go-to person for coordinating test results, overseeing daily tasks, or editing and refining research outputs, her departure may lead to bottlenecks in these areas.

3. **Delay in PhD Progress**  
   - Your PhD coordination might suffer if her support on design, data analysis, or strategic planning suddenly becomes less available or more fragmented.

4. **Administrative Gaps**  
   - The clinic’s ability to maintain updated paperwork, ethics approvals, and other documentation may face delays if no one is assigned to pick up these tasks immediately.

5. **Reduced Momentum in Collaborative Research**  
   - Ongoing or newly planned projects may stall or slow if they depended heavily on her daily input.

## 3. Recommendations for Handover and Continued Collaboration

### A. Formal Handover Process

1. **Define Key Responsibilities**

2. **Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs) & Process Documentation**

3. **Data Repositories and File Management**  
   - Ensure all research data, records, and relevant software credentials are backed up and stored securely in a shared repository (e.g., a cloud-based drive or a laboratory information management system).  
   - This includes your PhD documents, drafts, published manuscripts, and any associated references.

4. **Contact and Communication Structures**  
   - Determine a designated communication channel for quick queries (e.g., a dedicated email thread, shared Slack channel, or scheduled Zoom/Teams calls).  
   - If your clinic uses project management software (such as Trello, Asana, or MS Planner), ensure Eva is included in the relevant boards or tasks—at least for the transition period.

5. **Handoff Meetings or Workshops**  
   - Conduct a series of transition meetings where Eva provides training and addresses specific questions from the team or from you personally regarding the PhD.

4. **Leverage Digital Tools**  
   - Use collaboration tools (e.g., Microsoft Teams, Zoom, Google Suite) so that even in a freelance role, Eva remains integrated in relevant discussions or decisions that require her specific expertise.
