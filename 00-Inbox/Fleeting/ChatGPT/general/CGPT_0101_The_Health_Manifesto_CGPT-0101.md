# 2. The Fundamental Role of Water in Life and Disease

## A. Scientific Basis

- **Water is the primary solvent** in biological systems:  
  Almost **all biochemical reactions** — from enzymatic activities to metabolic transformations — occur **in aqueous solutions**.
  
- **Proportion of water in the human body**:
  - ~60% of total body weight in adults (varies: ~55–65% depending on age, sex, body composition)  
  - Higher in infants (~75–80%), decreases with age.
  - Distribution:
    - **Intracellular Fluid (ICF)**: ~2/3 of total body water (inside cells)
    - **Extracellular Fluid (ECF)**: ~1/3 (blood plasma, interstitial fluid)

- **Role of water**:
  - **Solvent**: dissolves and transports ions, nutrients, gases, and waste products.
  - **Medium for chemical reactions**: facilitates reactant mobility, enables molecular interactions.
  - **Thermal buffer**: regulates temperature through high specific heat.
  - **Structural component**: provides turgor (pressure) inside cells.
  - **Signal transduction**: hydration shells around proteins and membranes are crucial for signal transmission.

> Without adequate water, **metabolism slows**, **toxins accumulate**, **enzymes malfunction**, and **cellular structure collapses**.

---

## B. Dehydration as a Major Cause of Disease

- **Definition**: Dehydration is a net loss of body water, impairing normal physiological functions.
  
- **Consequences of dehydration**:
  - Decreased blood volume → impaired nutrient and oxygen delivery to tissues.
  - Increased blood viscosity → greater cardiovascular strain, risk of clotting.
  - Impaired kidney function → toxin buildup, acidosis risk.
  - Impaired thermoregulation → overheating, systemic inflammation.
  - Impaired cellular function → reduced energy production, autophagy disruption.
  - Neurological impact → cognitive dysfunction, fatigue, headaches.
  - Chronic low-grade dehydration → associated with hypertension, kidney stones, constipation, urinary tract infections, migraines.

- **Key Insight**:
  Many modern chronic diseases can be **exacerbated or triggered** by persistent **mild dehydration**, which is often unnoticed.
