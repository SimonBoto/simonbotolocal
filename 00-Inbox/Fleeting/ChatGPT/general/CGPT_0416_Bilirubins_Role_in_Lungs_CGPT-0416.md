### 6. **ChatGPT:**

Bilirubin functions as an effective antioxidant mainly through its ability to scavenge various reactive oxygen species, thus protecting cells from oxidative damage. Here’s how it works:

1. Bilirubin can efficiently scavenge peroxyl radicals, which are reactive oxygen species known to cause oxidative stress in cells. This activity is especially potent at lower oxygen concentrations, which are more physiologically relevant, making bilirubin a crucial antioxidant under normal biological conditions [(Stocker et al., 1987)](https://consensus.app/papers/bilirubin-importance-stocker/d29b45051a8e50e8840cb37967dbbf7f/?utm_source=chatgpt).
2. Additionally, bilirubin interacts with other radicals like peroxynitrite. It acts by scavenging these radicals and, interestingly, has shown effectiveness in protecting proteins in human blood plasma from oxidative modifications, further underscoring its role in cellular defense against oxidative stress [(Minetti et al., 1998)](https://consensus.app/papers/bilirubin-antioxidant-peroxynitritemediated-oxidation-minetti/b7143449e2f25472986bb54bbf259499/?utm_source=chatgpt).
3. On a structural level, bilirubin's antioxidant properties also involve the suppression of lipid peroxidation, a process that damages cell membranes. Studies have shown that bilirubin is more effective than some vitamins in inhibiting the oxidation of lipids, which are key components of cellular membranes [(Asad et al., 2001)](https://consensus.app/papers/prooxidant-activities-bilirubin-precursor-biliverdin-asad/7e3079874103515badffee2ac31951be/?utm_source=chatgpt).

These mechanisms collectively highlight how bilirubin contributes to cellular protection and overall physiological health by mitigating oxidative stress at various levels within the body.
