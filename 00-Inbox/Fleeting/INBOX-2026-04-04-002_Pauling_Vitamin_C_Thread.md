### Thread Overview

**Opening:** Linus Pauling—the only person to win TWO unshared Nobel Prizes—spent the last 30 years of his life trying to tell us something about vitamin C. The medical establishment called him a quack. Turns out, he might have been right all along.

### 1. Who Was Linus Pauling?
- Nobel Prize in Chemistry (1954)
- Nobel Peace Prize (1962)
- Discovered the nature of chemical bonds
- Predicted protein structures
- One of the greatest scientists of the 20th century
- Then became obsessed with vitamin C

### 2. The 1970 Publication
- Published "Vitamin C and the Common Cold"
- Claim: humans need FAR more vitamin C than RDA (90mg)
- Recommended 2,000-10,000mg daily
- Medical community ridiculed him — called it pseudoscience

### 3. Pauling's Key Insight
Most animals MAKE their own vitamin C:
- Goats produce 13,000mg per day
- During stress/illness: up to 100,000mg
- Humans lost the gene millions of years ago
- We MUST get it from food

### 4. Ancestral Intake
- Primate ancestors: 2,000-10,000mg daily from fruits/plants
- Modern RDA (90mg): just enough to prevent scurvy
- Not enough for OPTIMAL function
- Bare minimum to not die

### 5. The RDA Problem
- Based on preventing scurvy in sailors (18th century)
- Hardtack and salt pork diet
- Using deficiency standards for optimal health
- Pauling saw this as insane

### 6. What Vitamin C Actually Does
- Produces collagen (holds body together)
- Neutralizes free radicals (electron donor)
- Regenerates other antioxidants (vitamin E, glutathione)
- Required for neurotransmitter synthesis
- Essential for immune function

### 7. Evolutionary Biology
- Humans lost GULO gene
- Only handful of species can't make vitamin C: guinea pigs, fruit bats, some primates, humans
- Enzyme: L-gulonolactone oxidase (converts glucose to vitamin C)
- "Use it or lose it" — ancestors ate so much fruit, making C became wasteful

### 8. Modern Problem
- Modern food has 87% less vitamin C than wild fruits
- We evolved for abundance, live in deficiency
- Mismatch between evolutionary needs and modern diet

### 9. Cancer Controversy
- Pauling's most controversial claim: High-dose vitamin C (10,000mg+) can prevent/treat cancer
- Medical establishment went ballistic — called him dangerous
- Recent research starting to validate parts of this
- High-dose IV vitamin C shows promise in cancer treatment
