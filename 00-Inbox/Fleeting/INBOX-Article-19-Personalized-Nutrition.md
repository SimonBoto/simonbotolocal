# Article 19: Personalized Nutrition Halves Heart Attack Risk

## Key Findings

### Main Result
**Personalized nutrition program reduces heart attack risk by ~50%** in adults with established cardiovascular disease vs. standard dietary advice.

### Study Design (Bermingham et al. 2024, Nature Medicine)
| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Design** | Randomized controlled trial |
| **Intervention** | Personalized dietary program (PDP) |
| **Control** | General USDA dietary advice |
| **Follow-up** | 18 weeks |
| **Primary outcome** | Cardiometabolic health markers |

### Personalization Factors
| Input | Measurement |
|-------|-------------|
| **Postprandial glucose** | CGM data |
| **Triglycerides** | Post-meal response |
| **Gut microbiome** | Metagenomic sequencing |
| **CVD risk profile** | Clinical history |
| **Health history** | Individual context |

### Key Outcomes
| Outcome | PDP vs Control |
|---------|----------------|
| **Triglycerides** | Substantially improved |
| **Cardiometabolic risk** | Significantly reduced |
| **Weight** | Comparable to control (both lost) |

### Why It Works
- **Individual metabolic response varies widely** to identical foods
- **One-size-fits-all fails** — personalization matches diet to individual phenotype
- **Multiple biological inputs** create precision recommendations

## Key References
