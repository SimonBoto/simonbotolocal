# Article 26: LDL Lowering Does Not Always Mean Greater Protection

## Key Findings

### The LDL Paradox
| Context | LDL Lowering Effect |
|---------|---------------------|
| **High CAC, established CVD** | Clear benefit |
| **Low CAC, healthy metabolic** | Minimal/no benefit |
| **Insulin resistant** | Less benefit |
| **Inflammatory** | Less benefit |

### What Matters More Than LDL
| Factor | Predictive Power |
|--------|------------------|
| **Triglycerides** | Strong CVD predictor |
| **HDL** | Inverse association |
| **Insulin resistance** | Stronger than LDL |
| **Inflammation (hsCRP)** | Critical |
| **Metabolic health** | Comprehensive |

### Clinical Shift
| From | To |
|------|-----|
| **LDL-centric** | Comprehensive risk assessment |
| **Statin for all** | Stratified by risk |
| **Number chasing** | Context matters |

### Key Insight
LDL is **necessary but not sufficient** for CVD risk assessment. Metabolic context determines statin benefit.
