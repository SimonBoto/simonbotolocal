# Article 14: Lipoprotein(a) [Lp(a)] Genetic Risk

## Key Findings

### What is Lp(a)?
**Lipoprotein(a)** is an LDL-like particle with an additional apolipoprotein(a) component — a genetically determined cardiovascular risk factor present in ~20% of the population.

### Genetic Determination
| Characteristic | Detail |
|----------------|--------|
| **Heritability** | 90-95% genetically determined |
| **Population prevalence** | ~20% have elevated levels (>50 mg/dL) |
| **Stability** | Levels stable throughout life |
| **Inheritance** | Autosomal codominant |

### Cardiovascular Risk
| Lp(a) Level | Risk Increase |
|-------------|---------------|
| >50 mg/dL | 2-3× CVD, stroke, aortic stenosis |
| >90th percentile | 1.6-1.7× MI risk |
| Very high (>180 mg/dL) | Significant lifetime risk |

### Key Distinction: NOT Lifestyle Modifiable
Unlike LDL cholesterol, Lp(a) is **resistant to:**
- Dietary changes (low-fat, low-carb, Mediterranean)
- Exercise programs
- Weight loss
- Statin therapy

### Management Strategy
Since Lp(a) itself is not modifiable:
1. **Aggressive control of other risk factors**
2. **Lower LDL targets** (compensate for Lp(a) risk)
3. **Blood pressure optimization**
4. **Smoking cessation**
5. **Diabetes prevention/management**

### Emerging Therapies
| Therapy | Lp(a) Reduction | Status |
|---------|-----------------|--------|
| Pelacarsen (siRNA) | ~80% | Phase 3 trials |
| Olpasiran (siRNA) | ~95% | Phase 3 trials |
| PCSK9 inhibitors | ~25-30% | Currently available |
