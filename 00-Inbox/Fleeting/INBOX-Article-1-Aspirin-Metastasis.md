# Article 1: Aspirin Reduces Cancer Metastases via Immune Mechanism

## Key Findings

### The Metastasis Problem
- **90% of cancer deaths** worldwide are due to metastases
- Metastasis = spread of cancer cells from primary tumor to distant organs
- Circulating cancer cells in blood are vulnerable to immune attack
- Most cancer cells survive immune surveillance and establish metastases

### The Platelet-TXA2 Mechanism
- Platelets produce **thromboxane A2 (TXA2)** which suppresses T-cell function
- This creates an immunosuppressive environment for circulating cancer cells
- T-cells cannot effectively attack cancer cells when TXA2 is high

### Aspirin's Mechanism
- Aspirin **irreversibly inhibits COX-1** in platelets
- This permanently blocks TXA2 synthesis (platelets have no nucleus to regenerate COX-1)
- Reduced TXA2 → T-cell release from suppression → effective immune surveillance
- Cancer cells become vulnerable to immunological attack

### Clinical Evidence
- **Nature (March 2025):** Mechanism study (Yang et al.)
- **NEJM (Dec 2025):** Clinical implications
- Meta-analysis HR for metastasis: **0.64** (36% reduction)
- Cancer death reduction: **HR 0.49** (51% reduction) in non-metastatic patients
- Low-dose (75-300mg daily) sufficient due to platelet-specific effect

### Safety Note
- Aspirin use requires medical supervision
- Increased bleeding risk is the main concern
- Risk-benefit analysis needed for each patient
