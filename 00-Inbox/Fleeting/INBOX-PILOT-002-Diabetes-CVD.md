## Key Claims
- Cardiovascular deaths = #1 cause of death worldwide (32% of all deaths)
- NEJM study analyzed 5 modifiable risk factors: BMI, systolic BP, non-HDL cholesterol, smoking, diabetes
- 112 studies, 1.5 million people, 34 countries, mean age 54
- Combined, these 5 factors account for:
  - 53-57% of new CVD cases (57.2% women, 52.6% men)
  - 19-22% of all-cause deaths (22.2% women, 19.1% men)
- **Diabetes = strongest risk factor for all-cause death** (~3x increased risk at younger ages)
- Smoking = ~2.5x increased death risk
- Hypertension & obesity = ~1.5x increased death risk each
- Non-HDL cholesterol: HIGH levels → 1.6x CVD risk (but NOT all-cause death)
- Non-HDL cholesterol: VERY LOW levels → 1.6x all-cause death risk (reverse association)
- **Metabolic factors (diabetes, hypertension, obesity) = largest CVD risk component**
- These are effectively treatable with lifestyle/diet changes
