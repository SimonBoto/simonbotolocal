# Article 23: Tobacco and Sugar Industry Tactics

## Key Findings

### The Playbook (Same for Both Industries)
| Tactic | Implementation |
|--------|----------------|
| **Hide evidence** | Internal documents show harm known for decades |
| **Fund favorable research** | Pay experts to produce conflicting studies |
| **Attack the science** | Cast doubt on harmful findings |
| **Delay regulation** | Lobby against restrictions |
| **Shift blame** | Blame fat (sugar) / blame users (tobacco) |

### Sugar Industry Manipulation (1960s-2016)
| Action | Result |
|--------|--------|
| **1967 NEJM review** | Blamed fat/cholesterol, exonerated sugar |
| **Funded research** | $50k+ to Harvard scientists |
| **Shifted focus** | Heart disease blamed on saturated fat |
| **Documents hidden** | Internal memos revealed in 2016 |

### Tobacco Precedent
- **1950s-1990s:** Knew cancer link, hid it
- **Same tactics:** Attack science, fund doubt, delay
- **Sugar copied:** Same playbook, different product

### Outcome
- Public health action delayed decades
- Misinformation became "common knowledge"
- Regulation still weaker than evidence supports
