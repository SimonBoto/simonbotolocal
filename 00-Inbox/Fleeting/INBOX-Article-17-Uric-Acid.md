# Article 17: Elevated Uric Acid — Fructose, Alcohol & Processed Foods

## Key Findings

### Fructose: Primary Driver of Uric Acid
| Mechanism | Effect |
|-----------|--------|
| **Bypasses PFK regulation** | Uncontrolled glycolysis entry |
| **Rapid ATP depletion** | Generates AMP |
| **AMP degradation** | Produces uric acid |
| **Unique to fructose** | Glucose metabolism is regulated |

**Result:** Fructose metabolism in liver depletes ATP within minutes, triggering uric acid production.

### Alcohol: Multiple Mechanisms
| Mechanism | Effect |
|-----------|--------|
| **Accelerated ATP degradation** | Increased purine production |
| **Lactate competition** | Reduced renal uric acid excretion |
| **Beer purines** | Direct guanosine contribution |

**Beer strongest association** — both ethanol effects AND high purine content.

### Dietary Modification Works
| Intervention | Effect |
|--------------|--------|
| **Eliminate fructose-sweetened beverages** | Significant uric acid reduction |
| **Alcohol restriction** | Lower serum uric acid |
| **Avoid purine-rich processed foods** | Reduced gout flare risk |

## Clinical Message
- **Fructose is the primary dietary culprit** — not just "sugar"
- **Beer worse than other alcohol** for uric acid
- **Dietary modification is effective first-line treatment**
