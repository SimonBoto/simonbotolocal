# Article 8: Lifestyle and Diet as Disease Determinants

## Key Findings

### NEJM Study (2 million people, 39 countries)
Five modifiable risk factors analyzed:
1. High blood pressure
2. Cholesterol
3. Body weight (very low or high)
4. Diabetes
5. Smoking

### Impact on Lifespan
| Risk Factor Profile | Extra Years (Women) | Extra Years (Men) |
|---------------------|---------------------|-------------------|
| All 5 present | Baseline | Baseline |
| None present | +14.5 years | +11.8 years |
| Healthy life years | +13.3 years | +10.6 years |

### Key Insights
- **Smoking cessation:** Greatest benefit
- **Blood pressure control:** Second greatest
- **Cholesterol:** Minimal independent impact
- **Weight:** Confounded by diabetes and hypertension

### Reversibility
All major factors are modifiable through:
- Diet changes
- Lifestyle interventions
- Micronutrient repletion
