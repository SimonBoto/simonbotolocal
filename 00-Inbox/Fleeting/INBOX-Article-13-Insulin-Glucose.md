# Article 13: High Glucose vs High Insulin

## Key Findings

### Primary Claim
**Elevated insulin levels are more predictive of cardiovascular mortality than elevated glucose levels** — challenging the glucose-centric model of diabetes complications.

### Evidence from Zhao 2019 Meta-analysis
| Comparison | Finding |
|------------|---------|
| **Insulin vs glucose** | Insulin more predictive of CVD mortality |
| **HOMA-IR** | 111% greater CVD mortality risk (highest vs lowest) |
| **All-cause mortality** | 34% greater risk with high HOMA-IR |
| **Independent effect** | Risk begins in pre-diabetes (normal glucose, high insulin) |

### Mechanisms: Hyperinsulinemia Drives CVD
| Mechanism | Effect |
|-----------|--------|
| **Endothelial dysfunction** | Impaired vascular function |
| **Sodium retention** | Hypertension |
| **Smooth muscle proliferation** | Atherosclerosis |
| **Dyslipidemia** | High triglycerides, low HDL |
| **Inflammation** | Vascular damage |

### Paradigm Shift
- **Glucose-centric:** Focus on blood sugar control (HbA1c targets)
- **Insulin-centric:** Focus on insulin resistance reduction
- **Clinical implication:** Interventions reducing insulin (low-carb, fasting, weight loss) may be more cardioprotective than glucose-lowering alone

### Supporting Evidence
- ACCORD, ADVANCE, VADT trials: Intensive glucose lowering didn't reduce CVD mortality
- Disconnect between glucose control and CVD outcomes
- Insulin resistance precedes hyperglycemia by years/decades

## Key References
