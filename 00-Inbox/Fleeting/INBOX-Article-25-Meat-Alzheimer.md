# Article 25: Meat Consumption Eliminates Genetic Alzheimer Risk

## Key Findings

### Study (Norgren et al. 2026)
| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Population** | APOE4 carriers (3/4 and 4/4 genotypes) |
| **Finding** | Higher meat intake = lower dementia risk |
| **Risk reduction** | 55% lower dementia risk (HR 0.45) |
| **Cognitive decline** | Reduced in APOE4 carriers |

### Why Meat Protects APOE4 Carriers
| Nutrient | Role |
|----------|------|
| **Vitamin B12** | Critical for brain health, cognition |
| **Heme iron** | Bioavailable, prevents deficiency |
| **Zinc** | Neuroprotection, bioavailable form |
| **Creatine** | Brain energy metabolism |
| **Omega-3s** | DHA for neuronal membranes |

### APOE4 Evolutionary Context
- APOE4 is the **evolutionarily oldest** APOE variant
- May have been adaptive in **meat-rich environments**
- Modern low-meat diets may **disadvantage** APOE4 carriers

### Clinical Implication
- APOE4 carriers may **require meat** for optimal brain health
- Plant-based diets may **increase risk** for this genotype
- Personalized nutrition based on **genetics**
