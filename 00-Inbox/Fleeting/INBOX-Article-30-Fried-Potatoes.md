# Article 30: Fried Potatoes in Seed Oils Increase Diabetes Risk 20%

## Key Findings

### The Risk
| Exposure | Risk Increase |
|----------|---------------|
| **Fried potatoes in seed oils** | +20% T2D |
| **Frequency** | Dose-dependent |
| **Mechanism** | Oxidized omega-6, AGEs |

### Why Seed Oils Are Problematic
| Factor | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| **High omega-6** | Pro-inflammatory |
| **Heat unstable** | Oxidizes at frying temps |
| **Creates AGEs** | Advanced glycation end-products |
| **Trans fats** | Partial hydrogenation |

### Better Alternatives
| Option | Why Better |
|--------|------------|
| **Olive oil** | More stable, monounsaturated |
| **Animal fats** | Heat-stable (tallow, lard) |
| **Coconut oil** | Saturated, very stable |
| **Air frying** | Minimal oil needed |
| **Baking/roasting** | No frying required |

### Key Insight
It's not the potato — it's the **oil and cooking method**.
