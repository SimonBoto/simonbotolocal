# Article 2: Vitamin D RDA Statistical Error

## Key Findings

### The 2011 Institute of Medicine Error
- **Recommended:** 600 IU/day for ages 1-70
- **Goal:** Maintain 25(OH)D >20 ng/mL in 97.5% of population
- **Problem:** Statistical calculation used MEAN instead of 97.5th percentile

### Corrected Calculation (Veugelers & Ekwaru 2014)
- **Actual need:** ~8,900 IU/day (15× higher)
- **Reason:** Proper statistical approach for 97.5% population coverage
- **Heaney 2015 confirmation:** 6,201 IU/day needed for >30 ng/mL

### Why It Matters
Vitamin D functions as a **hormone**, not just a vitamin:
- Regulates 2,000 genes
- Affects immune system, metabolism, cellular differentiation
- Produced via sun exposure: 15,000-20,000 IU/day possible

### Health Outcomes with Adequate Vitamin D
- Reduced all-cause mortality
- Lower cancer death risk
- Reduced autoimmune disease
- Better metabolic health (less insulin resistance)

### Individual Variation
- Children: may benefit at 30 ng/mL
- Autoimmune patients: may need levels above "normal" range
- Optimal levels vary significantly by individual
