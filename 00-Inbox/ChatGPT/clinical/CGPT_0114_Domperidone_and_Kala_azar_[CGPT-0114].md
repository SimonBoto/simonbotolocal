**Domperidone** is a **dopamine D2 receptor antagonist** that primarily acts **peripherally**, with limited penetration across the blood–brain barrier. Its classical clinical use is to **enhance gastrointestinal motility** and **reduce nausea and vomiting** due to its **prokinetic and antiemetic effects**.

Let’s break this into two parts:

---

### **1. Mechanism of Action of Domperidone**
- **D2 Receptor Antagonist**: Domperidone blocks **dopamine D2 receptors**, mainly in the **chemoreceptor trigger zone (CTZ)** located in the **area postrema**, which lies outside the blood-brain barrier. This helps **prevent emesis**.
- **GI Motility**: By antagonizing D2 receptors in the **gut**, domperidone **increases lower esophageal sphincter tone**, improves **gastric emptying**, and enhances **anterograde motility**. This is because dopamine has an inhibitory effect on gut motility via D2 receptors; blocking these enhances cholinergic activity.

---

### **2. Role in **Kala-azar** (Visceral Leishmaniasis)**

Domperidone is **not a standard or first-line treatment for kala-azar**, which is caused by *Leishmania donovani* and transmitted by sandflies. The main therapeutic agents are:

- **Amphotericin B (liposomal)**
- **Miltefosine**
- **Paromomycin**
- **Sodium stibogluconate**

However, some **experimental or supportive roles** of domperidone have been explored:

#### **A. Immunomodulatory Hypothesis**:
- Some animal studies suggest that domperidone **enhances host immune response** via modulation of **prolactin secretion**.
- Domperidone increases **prolactin levels** by blocking dopamine’s inhibitory effect on the anterior pituitary.
- **Prolactin** has been shown in some in-vitro and animal studies to have **immunostimulatory properties**, enhancing **macrophage activation**, which could hypothetically aid in **Leishmania clearance**.

#### **B. Experimental/Adjunctive Use**:
- Certain **experimental studies in murine models** have shown **increased survival** or **parasite clearance** when domperidone was used **in combination** with conventional anti-leishmanial drugs.
- However, **no large-scale clinical trial** or **guideline** supports domperidone for routine use in kala-azar.

#### **C. GI Supportive Role**:
- Patients with kala-azar may experience **nausea, vomiting**, or **gastroparesis** due to systemic illness or drug toxicity.
- Domperidone can be used **supportively** to alleviate these symptoms during **treatment with emetogenic drugs** like amphotericin B.
