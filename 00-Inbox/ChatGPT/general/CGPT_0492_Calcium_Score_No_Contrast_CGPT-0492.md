### 2. **ChatGPT:**

A calcium score, also known as a coronary calcium scan, does not require intravenous contrast. This test is a specific type of CT scan (computed tomography) designed to measure the amount of calcified plaque in the coronary arteries. The scan is non-invasive and does not use contrast dye, which is typically required in contrast-enhanced CT scans or coronary angiography for detailed visualization of the blood vessels. The calcium score is used to assess the risk of coronary artery disease. Contrast is used in CT angiography, not in a standard calcium score scan.
