Here’s a list of **classic and famous movies set in Rome**, many of which showcase the Eternal City with its stunning landmarks and streets:

---

### **1. Roman Holiday (1953)**  
- **Director**: William Wyler  
- **Stars**: Audrey Hepburn, Gregory Peck  
- **Plot**: A princess escapes her royal duties for a day of freedom in Rome and is accompanied by an American journalist.  
- **Key Rome Locations**:  
  - Piazza di Spagna (Spanish Steps)  
  - Fontana di Trevi  
  - Castel Sant'Angelo  
  - Via Margutta  

This film introduced many to Rome’s romantic atmosphere and iconic landmarks.

---

### **2. La Dolce Vita (1960)**  
- **Director**: Federico Fellini  
- **Stars**: Marcello Mastroianni, Anita Ekberg  
- **Plot**: A journalist navigates the decadent nightlife of Rome in search of love and meaning.  
- **Key Rome Locations**:  
  - Fontana di Trevi (the famous scene of Anita Ekberg in the fountain)  
  - Via Veneto (symbol of post-war Roman luxury)  
  - Basilica of St. Peter’s Dome  
  - Baths of Caracalla  

A defining film for Italian cinema and a portrayal of 1960s Rome.

---

### **3. Angels & Demons (2009)**  
- **Director**: Ron Howard  
- **Stars**: Tom Hanks, Ewan McGregor  
- **Plot**: A Harvard symbologist tries to stop a plot against the Vatican.  
- **Key Rome Locations**:  
  - Pantheon  
  - Piazza Navona  
  - Vatican City (including digitally recreated interiors)  
  - Castel Sant'Angelo  

The movie captures many historical and religious landmarks tied to secret societies and symbolism.

---

### **4. Eat Pray Love (2010)**  
- **Director**: Ryan Murphy  
- **Stars**: Julia Roberts, Javier Bardem  
- **Plot**: A woman embarks on a self-discovery journey across Italy, India, and Indonesia. In Rome, she indulges in food and self-love.  
- **Key Rome Locations**:  
  - Piazza Navona  
  - Via dei Coronari (for its charm)  
  - The Pantheon  
  - Piazza Venezia  

Rome’s beauty is captured in leisurely shots of its streets, piazzas, and food culture.

---

### **5. The Great Beauty (La Grande Bellezza) (2013)**  
- **Director**: Paolo Sorrentino  
- **Stars**: Toni Servillo, Carlo Verdone  
- **Plot**: A writer reflects on his life while wandering through the glamorous but decadent cultural scene of Rome.  
- **Key Rome Locations**:  
  - Colosseum  
  - Janiculum Terrace  
  - Palazzo Spada (the Borromini illusion)  
  - Aventine Keyhole  
  - Terrazza del Gianicolo  

This visually stunning film is a poetic homage to the splendor of Rome.

---

### **6. Gladiator (2000)** *(although filmed in multiple locations)*  
- **Director**: Ridley Scott  
- **Stars**: Russell Crowe, Joaquin Phoenix  
- **Plot**: A betrayed Roman general seeks revenge after being enslaved.  
- **Key Rome Representations**:  
  - Colosseum (digitally recreated for battle scenes)  

Although much was digitally created, the ancient Roman Colosseum plays a central symbolic role.

---

### **7. When in Rome (2010)**  
- **Director**: Mark Steven Johnson  
- **Stars**: Kristen Bell, Josh Duhamel  
- **Plot**: A woman steals coins from a "love fountain" in Rome, causing suitors to fall in love with her.  
- **Key Rome Locations**:  
  - Piazza Navona  
  - Fontana di Trevi  
  - Villa Borghese  

A romantic comedy that plays with Rome’s magic and charm.

---

### **8. The Talented Mr. Ripley (1999)** *(partial scenes in Rome)*  
- **Director**: Anthony Minghella  
- **Stars**: Matt Damon, Jude Law  
- **Plot**: A young man becomes obsessed with a wealthy playboy in 1950s Italy.  
- **Key Rome Locations**:  
  - Piazza Navona  
  - Church of Sant'Agostino  

---

### **9. To Rome with Love (2012)**  
- **Director**: Woody Allen  
- **Stars**: Alec Baldwin, Penélope Cruz, Roberto Benigni  
- **Plot**: Multiple interwoven stories of love, dreams, and fame set against the backdrop of Rome.  
- **Key Rome Locations**:  
  - Spanish Steps  
  - Via Condotti  
  - Trastevere  

A tribute to Rome’s enchantment and timeless allure.

---

### **10. Ben-Hur (1959)** *(Rome depicted in iconic sets)*  
- **Director**: William Wyler  
- **Stars**: Charlton Heston  
- **Plot**: A Jewish prince is betrayed and enslaved by the Romans, culminating in an epic chariot race.  
- **Key Rome Representations**:  
  - Circus Maximus-inspired chariot race arena  

---

### **11. Cleopatra (1963)** *(featuring ancient Rome-inspired scenes)*  
- **Director**: Joseph L. Mankiewicz  
- **Stars**: Elizabeth Taylor, Richard Burton  
- **Plot**: The story of Cleopatra and her entanglements with Julius Caesar and Mark Antony.  
- **Key Rome Representations**:  
  - Forum Romanum-inspired set pieces
