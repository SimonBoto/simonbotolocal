It’s difficult to find **IV infusion suppliers in Europe** for several tightly interlinked **regulatory, legal, and market reasons**. Below is a structured breakdown of why this is the case — especially when you're seeking *bulk suppliers* of **injectable vitamins or micronutrients** (e.g., glutathione, B-complex, vitamin C):

---

### 1. **Regulatory Restrictions (GMP / EMA / National Law)**

- **Injectables are classified as pharmaceutical products**, not nutraceuticals, even if the compound is something as "natural" as vitamin C.
- In the **EU**, these products must comply with:
  - **Good Manufacturing Practice (GMP)**
  - **EMA (European Medicines Agency)** regulations
  - **National authorities’ rules** (e.g., ANSM in France, AEMPS in Spain, EOF in Greece, MHRA in UK)
- Therefore, **only licensed pharmaceutical manufacturers** can produce and distribute these injectables, limiting your supplier pool.

**Consequence:** You can’t just buy injectable vitamins from nutraceutical suppliers or vitamin companies unless they’re licensed for parenteral use.

---

### 2. **Controlled Distribution Channels**

- Many injectables are **prescription-only medications (POM)**, especially:
  - Glutathione
  - Vitamin B12 (methylcobalamin/hydroxycobalamin)
  - NAD+
  - Magnesium sulfate
- This means:
  - **Direct-to-clinic** wholesale sales may be restricted
  - Some suppliers require you to **go through a local distributor or pharmacy**
  - Others may only sell to **hospitals or authorized medical centers**

---

### 3. **Small Market, High Risk**

- The **IV wellness market** (vitamin drips, NAD+, aesthetic treatments) is still **not officially regulated** in many EU countries.
- Most players operate in a legal gray zone, especially **non-hospital wellness clinics**.
- Suppliers **fear liability** and avoid selling to providers without:
  - A fully registered clinic
  - Physician oversight
  - Explicit authorization to administer IVs

**Example:** In Greece, even a fully licensed doctor can’t legally administer IV vitamins in a wellness setting unless under very specific conditions.

---

### 4. **Low Public Visibility of Suppliers**

- Legitimate suppliers (e.g., German or Italian pharma companies) often:
  - **Do not advertise online**
  - Require **email/fax inquiries and business registration**
  - Operate via **distributors or brokers**
- The few that do sell online are often:
  - **Cosmetic clinics selling off-brand or Korean imports**
  - Or **non-EU based** (which adds customs and compliance risks)

---

### 5. **Fear of Abuse and Repackaging**

- Some compounds (e.g., glutathione, vitamin B12, even vitamin C) are associated with:
  - **Skin-lightening abuse**
  - **DIY injection**
  - **Resale and rebranding**
- As a result, **European producers often avoid bulk sales** to unverified buyers, especially from wellness or alternative medicine circles.

---

### 6. **Fragmented Logistics and Compliance Costs**

- **Cold chain requirements** (fridges, validated shipping, EU GDP compliance)
- **Batch traceability**, **sterility documentation**, **expiry and lot matching**
- Many smaller players **don’t have infrastructure** for this or **don’t want to deal with it** for vitamin infusions

---

### 7. **Rise of Compounders and Off-Label Clinics**

- In places like the **UK or Germany**, the IV market is often served by:
  - **Compound pharmacies** (e.g., using semi-custom blends under prescription)
  - **Private wellness chains** who have their own internal supply chains
- These are **closed-loop systems**, not open to public wholesale buyers.

---

### Conclusion

Finding a supplier is hard because you’re looking for a product class that lies at the **intersection of pharma, wellness, and high regulation**. You’re essentially trying to source **injectables** that are either:
- **Pharmaceutical-grade** (and thus highly controlled)
- Or **wellness-grade** (but not allowed to be IV legally)

---

2. **Partner with a compounding pharmacy** that can prepare IVs for your clinic.

   - *Unifarm (Italy)*

   - *Alliance Healthcare (UK)* – if you have a doctor’s prescription or registered clinic.
4. **Register your clinic** officially and get physician license + cold chain infrastructure.
5. Consider **offshore suppliers** (e.g., Swiss, Korean, or South African) only if you're sure about EU customs clearance and sterility.
