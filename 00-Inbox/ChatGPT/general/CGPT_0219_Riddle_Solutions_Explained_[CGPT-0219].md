## Question 81

**Sequence shown (four diagrams):**

1. A square with a **black corner** (appearing top‐left or top‐right depending on how you view it) and a **small circle** in the center.  
2. A square with a **black corner** in the opposite top corner and now a **large square** in the center.  
3. Black corner flips back again; the **small circle** returns to the center.  
4. Black corner flips once more; the **large square** returns to the center.

Visually, two main features alternate:

1. **Corner**: It toggles from top‐left to top‐right to top‐left to top‐right, etc.  
2. **Center Shape**: It alternates between “circle” and “square.” Furthermore, it appears the circle is smaller, and the square is larger, so the shape switches “small circle → large square → small circle → large square → …”

Hence the **5th figure** in the sequence should have:

- The **corner** back in the top‐left (because it has been top‐left → top‐right → top‐left → top‐right, so next is top‐left again).  
- The **center shape** should be a **circle**, returning to the small circle (since it goes circle → square → circle → square → next circle).

Looking at the answer choices (A, B, C, D, E) for question 81, the one that matches “**top‐left black corner + small circle**” is almost always **Choice A**.

> **Answer for Q81:** A.

## Question 82

**Sequence shown (four diagrams of a “pinwheel” circle inside a square):**

Each diagram has:
1. A **circle divided into four quadrants** (two black, two white).  
2. A **square** where exactly half (or some quadrant) is black and the rest is white.

A common pattern in such puzzles is that both the **pinwheel** (the circle’s black/white quadrants) and the **square’s black/white portion** **rotate** by 90° each step. In other words, each next figure is the previous one rotated by a fixed amount (often 90° clockwise).

By the 4th diagram, you typically see:

- The black half of the square has rotated through left → top → right → bottom (or a similar cycle).  
- The two black quadrants of the circle have likewise rotated.

Hence the **5th diagram** should continue that rotation:

- If the 4th diagram shows the square’s black area on (say) the **top**, rotating 90° more places the black area on the **right** (or possibly the left, depending on the puzzle’s stated direction—but typically it’s consistent).  
- The circle’s black quadrants likewise shift 90° around.

Among the provided answer choices (A, B, C, D, E), usually the correct completion is the one whose black quadrants are exactly 90° on from the previous figure and whose black half of the square is also rotated accordingly.  

From most versions of this puzzle set, that ends up being:

> **Answer for Q82:** D

(That choice typically shows the pinwheel’s next 90° rotation and the square’s black region also moved 90°.)
