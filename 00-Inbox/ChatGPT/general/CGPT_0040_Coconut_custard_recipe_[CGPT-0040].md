## ✅ Texture Logic
- **Coconut flour absorbs liquid** → thickens the custard.
- **Gelatin gives body and creaminess without starch.**
- Result: *smooth, firm, sliceable custard—like panna cotta meets galaktoboureko cream.*

---

## ✅ Preparation Steps

### Step 1 — Hydrate Gelatin
1. In a small bowl add **3–4 tbsp cold milk** (from the 1 L).
2. Sprinkle gelatin on top.
3. Let bloom for **5–10 minutes**.

### Step 2 — Warm the Milk
1. Heat remaining milk gently in a pot (medium-low heat).
2. Add coconut sugar + xylitol and whisk until dissolved.
3. Add vanilla if using.

### Step 3 — Add Coconut Flour
1. Sprinkle coconut flour gradually while whisking to avoid clumps.
2. Continue simmering very gently for **3–4 minutes** until slightly thickened.

### Step 4 — Add Eggs (Tempering)
1. Beat the 5 eggs in a separate bowl.
2. Add a ladle of the hot milk mixture slowly into the eggs while whisking (tempering).
3. Then pour the egg mixture back into the main pot slowly, whisking continuously.
4. Cook on LOW heat for **3–5 minutes** until lightly thickened (do NOT boil, or eggs will scramble).

### Step 5 — Incorporate Gelatin
1. Remove pot from heat.
2. Stir in the bloomed gelatin until fully dissolved.

### Step 6 — Set
1. Pour mixture into a glass or ceramic dish (or 6–8 ramekins).
2. Cool to room temperature.
3. Then refrigerate **minimum 3–4 hours** to set.

---

### Equipment
- clean surface or large bowl  
- fork + rolling pin or pasta machine  
- knife or pizza cutter

---

### Step-by-Step
1. **Form a flour mound**  
   On your counter, make a small crater in the middle of the flour.

2. **Add eggs + salt + oil** into the crater.  
   Beat them lightly with a fork.

3. **Gradually pull flour into the eggs** until a rough dough forms.  
   When it starts holding together, knead with your hands.

4. **Knead 8–10 min**  
   The dough should become smooth and elastic—not sticky.

5. **Rest 20–30 min** covered (at room temp).

6. **Roll out**

   Roll the sheet loosely and slice into ribbons, or use a cutter.

8. **Cook immediately** in plenty of salted boiling water—  
   only **2–3 minutes** until tender but firm (fresh pasta cooks fast).

9. **Drain & toss** directly with sauce or butter/oil (never let it sit dry).
