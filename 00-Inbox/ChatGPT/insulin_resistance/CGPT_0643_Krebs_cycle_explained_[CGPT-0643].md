The cycle begins with the oxidation of acetyl-CoA, a two-carbon molecule that is derived from the breakdown of carbohydrates, fats, and proteins. This step is catalyzed by the enzyme citrate synthase, which combines the acetyl group with oxaloacetate to form citrate. Citrate is then isomerized to isocitrate by the enzyme aconitase.

Next, isocitrate is oxidized and decarboxylated by the enzyme isocitrate dehydrogenase, producing alpha-ketoglutarate, CO2, and NADH. Alpha-ketoglutarate is then oxidized and decarboxylated by the enzyme alpha-ketoglutarate dehydrogenase, producing CO2, NADH, and succinyl-CoA.

Succinyl-CoA then undergoes a series of reactions catalyzed by the enzymes succinyl-CoA synthetase, succinate dehydrogenase, fumarase, and malate dehydrogenase, ultimately regenerating oxaloacetate and producing ATP and NADH.
