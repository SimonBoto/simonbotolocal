The **Dr. William Davis** *Lactobacillus reuteri* yogurt recipe is a high-potency, long-fermented probiotic yogurt that aims to deliver massive CFU counts (in the range of hundreds of billions to trillions) of *L. reuteri*, with documented benefits including increased **oxytocin**, improved **skin health**, **bone density**, and **insulin sensitivity**.

Below is the **standard Davis L. reuteri yogurt recipe**, adapted from his book *Super Gut* and his official blog:

---

### **Ingredients**:
- **Lactobacillus reuteri** culture:  
  Use either  
  - BioGaia *Gastrus* tablets (contains L. reuteri DSM 17938 and ATCC PTA 6475), crushed  
  OR  
  - A pure *L. reuteri* starter culture from a reputable supplier (many now provide it online).

- **Half and half** or **whole milk** (preferably organic, A2, or raw if available): **1 quart (about 1 liter)**  
  - Must be high-fat for best fermentation and texture.
  - Avoid ultra-pasteurized milk.

- **Prebiotic fiber** (optional but strongly recommended): **1 tablespoon**  
  - Inulin or acacia fiber work best.  
  - Serves as a fermentation substrate.

- **Starter**:  
  - If first batch: 10 crushed *Gastrus* tablets or starter powder.  
  - If second+ batch: use 2–3 tablespoons of the previous yogurt as inoculant.

---

### **Instructions**:

1. **Mix**:
   - Heat the milk to **110°F (43°C)** (no higher).
   - Dissolve the prebiotic fiber and the starter culture (crushed tablets or powder) thoroughly in the warm milk.

2. **Ferment**:
   - Place in a **yogurt maker**, **Instant Pot**, or other device that maintains a steady **100–106°F (38–41°C)**.
   - **Ferment for 36 hours** — this extended time is critical to achieve high bacterial counts and thick texture.
   - Ensure **anaerobic conditions** as much as possible (cover tightly, use a lid, etc.)

3. **Cool and Refrigerate**:
   - After fermentation, transfer to the fridge. It will thicken further.
   - Save a few spoonfuls for the next batch (up to 10–15 times before needing to restart with fresh starter).

---

### **Tips**:
- Use a **water bath** or yogurt maker that guarantees **steady temperature**.
- Adding gelatin or agar can help thicken if the result is too runny.
- Avoid stirring during fermentation — this disrupts the process.

---

### **Benefits Observed in Literature & Davis’s Work**:
- Boost in **oxytocin** levels  
- Enhanced **skin dermal thickness and collagen**  
- Improved **bone density** (via oxytocin and vitamin D synergy)  
- Reduction in **abdominal fat** and insulin resistance  
- Increased **muscle mass** (in some rodent and anecdotal human data)

(References: Davis W. *Super Gut*, 2022; Lebeer et al., Microbiome 2018; Poutahidis et al., PLoS ONE 2013)
