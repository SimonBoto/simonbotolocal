## 1. Fostering Social Connections

1. **Community Activities**  
   - Encourage her to explore community groups in Edessa or surrounding areas, such as local cultural associations, knitting circles, or volunteer initiatives. Research has consistently shown that social interaction can protect against depressive symptoms in older adults (Santini et al., 2020).

2. **Volunteering**  
   - Even if her professional nursing responsibilities are minimal, she could volunteer her expertise or general assistance at local clinics, community centers, or with charity organizations. Doing so can restore a sense of identity and purpose.

3. **Online Support and Hobby Groups**  
   - While she already spends time on her smartphone, redirecting some of that time to online communities focused on positive topics, hobbies, or skill-building may be beneficial. Virtual book clubs, animal care forums, or Greek cultural heritage groups are examples that can help transform passive screen time into a more interactive, fulfilling experience.

---

## 2. Physical Activity and Outdoor Exposure

1. **Daily Walks with Her Dog**  
   - She is already walking her dog twice a day, which is beneficial. Encouraging slightly longer or more brisk walks can boost endorphin release and improve cardiovascular health. There is a well-established link between regular aerobic activity and improved mood as well as better metabolic and cognitive health (World Health Organization, 2020).

2. **Light Exercise Indoors**  
   - Simple routines, such as chair-based exercises, light resistance training, or yoga tailored to her age group, can be performed at home. Regular movement can also mitigate age-related decline and improve insulin sensitivity (Fiuza-Luces et al., 2013).

3. **Vitamin D and Sunlight**  
   - Given that she lives in an apartment with limited outdoor space, she might consider spending more time outdoors during midday hours to obtain natural sunlight (which helps with vitamin D synthesis), if weather and safety allow. A balanced vitamin D level has been associated with improved mood and metabolic health (Anglin et al., 2013). If her vitamin D levels are low, supplementation in consultation with a healthcare provider may be beneficial.

## 3. Mental and Emotional Well-being

1. **Purpose-Focused Activities**  
   - Adopting a new hobby—such as painting, cooking, gardening in pots on the apartment balcony, or learning a musical instrument—can instill a sense of accomplishment and mental stimulation.

2. **Mindfulness and Stress-Management Techniques**  
   - Practices such as guided meditation or gentle stretching have demonstrated efficacy in reducing stress and improving emotional well-being. Smartphone apps featuring mindfulness exercises (e.g., Calm, Headspace, or Greek-based alternatives) could transform part of her phone usage into a therapeutic activity.

3. **Professional Counseling or Group Therapy**  
   - If feelings of hopelessness or persistent low mood are evident, speaking with a mental health professional (e.g., psychologist or psychiatrist) is advisable. Tele-mental health options (video counseling sessions) are now widely available, which could be advantageous in a rural environment.

## 4. Nutrition and Metabolic Health

1. **Structured Dietary Approach**  
   - Following a nutrient-dense eating plan can positively impact both mental and physical well-being. A Mediterranean-style low-carbohydrate plan with quality animal-based protein, healthy fats, and nutrient-rich vegetables is often advantageous for metabolic and cognitive health (Estruch et al., 2018).

2. **Adequate Protein and Micronutrients**  
   - Ensuring sufficient high-quality protein intake supports muscle mass and general health. Prioritizing foods rich in B vitamins, magnesium, zinc, and omega-3 fatty acids—such as liver, sardines, or other nutrient-dense animal sources—may be beneficial for mood and cognition.

3. **Vitamin D Supplementation**  
   - If her serum vitamin D is indeed low (often the case in individuals who spend more time indoors), supplementation with higher-dose vitamin D can be considered under medical supervision. Numerous studies link improved vitamin D status with better mood regulation and possibly decreased risk of depressive symptoms (Zhang et al., 2022).

## 5. Structured Daily Routine

1. **Creating a Schedule**  
   - Having a predictable routine that includes set times for meals, exercise, and leisure can provide structure and reduce idle time spent on passive screen usage (TV, social media).

2. **Goal-Setting**  
   - Small, achievable goals—such as walking an extra 1,000 steps per day, finishing a knitting project, or completing a volunteer shift—can help reinforce a sense of progress and daily purpose.
