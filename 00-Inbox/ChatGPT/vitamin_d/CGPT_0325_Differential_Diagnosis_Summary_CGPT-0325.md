### 1. **Technetium-99m Bone Scintigraphy (Bone Scan):**
   - **Indication:** This nuclear medicine imaging technique is highly sensitive for detecting bone metastases, Paget's disease, and other areas of increased bone turnover.
   - **Procedure:** It involves intravenous injection of a small amount of radioactive tracer (Technetium-99m), which accumulates in areas of high bone activity. The whole-body scan is then performed to detect these areas.
   - **Advantages:** High sensitivity for detecting metastatic bone disease and areas of increased osteoblastic activity.

### 2. **Positron Emission Tomography (PET) Scan:**
   - **Indication:** PET scans, often combined with CT (PET/CT), can detect metabolic activity associated with cancer metastases, including bone involvement.
   - **Procedure:** Uses a radioactive glucose analog (Fluorodeoxyglucose, FDG) to detect high metabolic activity typical of malignancies.
   - **Advantages:** Provides both metabolic and anatomical information, useful for detecting malignancies and assessing the extent of disease.

### 3. **Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI):**
   - **Indication:** MRI is excellent for evaluating bone marrow and soft tissue involvement. It is particularly useful if there is a suspicion of multiple myeloma or primary bone tumors.
   - **Procedure:** Uses strong magnetic fields and radio waves to generate detailed images of bones and surrounding tissues.
   - **Advantages:** High resolution and excellent contrast for soft tissues and bone marrow.

### 4. **Computed Tomography (CT) Scan:**
   - **Indication:** CT scans provide detailed images of the bone and are useful for detecting lytic lesions, cortical bone integrity, and guiding biopsy if needed.
   - **Procedure:** Uses X-rays to create detailed cross-sectional images of the body.
   - **Advantages:** Excellent for detecting structural bone changes and guiding interventional procedures.

### Recommended Approach:

   - This should be the initial imaging modality due to its high sensitivity for detecting areas of increased bone turnover or metastasis.

   - If multiple myeloma or primary bone tumor is suspected, MRI should be considered for detailed evaluation of bone marrow and soft tissue involvement.

**3. PET/CT:**
   - If there is a high suspicion of malignancy, particularly with systemic involvement, a PET/CT scan can provide comprehensive metabolic and anatomical information.
