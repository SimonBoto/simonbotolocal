Micronutrients are essential vitamins and minerals required by the body in small amounts to maintain various physiological functions, including metabolism. These micronutrients serve as cofactors or coenzymes in enzymatic reactions involved in energy production, macronutrient metabolism, and cellular signaling pathways. For instance, B vitamins, such as Biotin and Thiamine, participate in carbohydrate, fat, and protein metabolism, while minerals like zinc and magnesium are essential for insulin synthesis and action.

In normal metabolism, micronutrients support glucose homeostasis by influencing insulin sensitivity, pancreatic function, and cellular glucose uptake. Deficiencies in these micronutrients can disrupt metabolic processes, leading to insulin resistance and impaired glucose metabolism.
