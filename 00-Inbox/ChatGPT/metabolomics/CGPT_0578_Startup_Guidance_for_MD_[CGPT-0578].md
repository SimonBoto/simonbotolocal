Yes, there are several standardized questionnaires specifically designed for diabetics. These questionnaires are often used in clinical settings and research to assess various aspects of diabetes management, quality of life, and self-care behaviors. Here are some well-known ones:

1. **Diabetes Quality of Life (DQOL) Measure**: This questionnaire assesses the impact of diabetes and its treatment on patients' quality of life. It covers multiple domains, including satisfaction with treatment, impact on daily life, and worries about the future.

2. **Diabetes Self-Care Activities Measure (DSCA)**: This measures the frequency of certain diabetes self-care activities over the past week, such as diet, exercise, blood sugar testing, foot care, and medication adherence.

3. **Diabetes Distress Scale (DDS)**: This is used to measure the emotional burden and distress often associated with managing diabetes. It evaluates various domains like emotional burden, regimen distress, interpersonal distress, and physician-related distress.

4. **Problem Areas in Diabetes Scale (PAID)**: This is a 20-item questionnaire that measures diabetes-related emotional distress. It can help healthcare professionals identify patients who might benefit from further psychological assessment or intervention.

5. **Audit of Diabetes-Dependent Quality of Life (ADDQoL)**: This tool measures the impact of diabetes on a person's quality of life. It covers a broad range of life domains impacted by diabetes.

6. **Diabetes Treatment Satisfaction Questionnaire (DTSQ)**: As the name suggests, it assesses satisfaction with diabetes treatment. This can be especially valuable when assessing the efficacy and acceptability of new treatments or interventions.

7. **Michigan Neuropathy Screening Instrument (MNSI)**: This tool helps screen for diabetic peripheral neuropathy, a common complication of diabetes. It involves both a questionnaire and a physical examination.

8. **Fear of Hypoglycemia Questionnaire**: Many diabetics, especially those on insulin, have concerns about hypoglycemia. This questionnaire assesses the worries and fears related to experiencing low blood sugar.

If you're considering using or adapting elements from these standardized questionnaires, you should be aware of any licensing or permissions required. Many of these instruments are copyrighted. However, they can provide valuable frameworks and insights for developing your own questionnaire tailored to your app's goals and the needs of your user base.
