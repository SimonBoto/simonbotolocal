### 17. **ChatGPT:**

A nutraceutical is a term combining “nutrition” and “pharmaceutical.” It refers to any substance that is a food or part of a food and provides medical or health benefits, including the prevention and treatment of disease. Nutraceuticals can be classified into several categories, such as dietary supplements, functional foods, medicinal foods, and farmaceuticals.

1. **Dietary Supplements:** These include vitamins, minerals, herbs, and other botanicals, amino acids, and substances such as enzymes, organ tissues, glandulars, and metabolites. They typically come in the form of capsules, tablets, or liquids, and are used to supplement the diet.

2. **Functional Foods:** These are similar to conventional foods and are consumed as part of a regular diet. However, they have been enhanced or fortified to provide a specific health benefit beyond their basic nutritional value. Examples include calcium-enriched orange juice or omega-3 enriched eggs.

3. **Medicinal Foods:** These are formulated to be consumed or administered internally under the supervision of a physician. They are intended for the specific dietary management of a disease or condition for which distinctive nutritional requirements are established by medical evaluation.

4. **Farmaceuticals:** These are medically valuable compounds produced from modified agricultural crops or animals. The term is also used for plants that have been genetically modified to produce pharmaceuticals.

Nutraceuticals are often used in the management and prevention of life-style related diseases such as hypertension, diabetes, and obesity. The use of nutraceuticals has grown rapidly over the past decades, with an increasing focus on achieving wellness through natural products and a balanced diet. However, it is important to approach nutraceuticals with a critical eye, as the regulatory frameworks for these products can vary significantly from those for pharmaceutical drugs, leading to variations in quality, efficacy, and safety.
