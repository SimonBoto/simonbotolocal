## 1️⃣ **What is ergothioneine? (Very precise definition)**

- A naturally occurring **low molecular weight thiol derivative** of histidine: **2-mercaptohistidine trimethylbetaine**.
- Synthesized only by some bacteria and fungi.
- Humans acquire it exclusively from the diet (mushrooms, fermented foods, some animal tissues).
- Transported into cells via **OCTN1 (SLC22A4)** transporter — highly conserved across mammalian tissues.
- Accumulates preferentially in tissues with high oxidative turnover: gut, liver, kidneys, bone marrow, brain.

> Reference: Cheah IK, Halliwell B. Ergothioneine; antioxidant potential, physiological function and role in disease. *Biochim Biophys Acta Gen Subj.* 2012.

---

## 2️⃣ **Known Biological Functions (Human data)**

- Potent scavenger of **reactive oxygen and nitrogen species (ROS/RNS)**.
- Protection against mitochondrial oxidative damage.
- Stabilization of proteins and lipids under oxidative stress.
- Anti-inflammatory properties via NF-κB and Nrf2 modulation.
- Potential cytoprotective effects in endothelial, neuronal, and hepatic tissues.

> Reference: Ames BN. Prolonging healthy aging: longevity vitamins. *Proc Natl Acad Sci USA.* 2018.

---

| **Human observational data** | Lower plasma ergothioneine associated with metabolic syndrome, cognitive decline, cardiovascular risk | Indirect clinical association |
