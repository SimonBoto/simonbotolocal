Elevated levels of calcium, alkaline phosphatase (ALP), gamma-glutamyl transferase (GGT), and lactate dehydrogenase (LDH) can be indicative of several malignancies, but they are most commonly associated with metastatic cancers, particularly those involving the bone and liver.

1. **Bone Metastases**: Various cancers can metastasize to the bone, leading to elevated calcium and ALP. These include:
   - **Breast Cancer**: Often metastasizes to the bone.
   - **Prostate Cancer**: Another common cancer that metastasizes to the bone.
   - **Lung Cancer**: Can also metastasize to the bone.

2. **Liver Metastases**: When cancers spread to the liver, they can elevate ALP, GGT, and LDH levels. Cancers commonly associated with liver metastases include:
   - **Colorectal Cancer**: Frequently metastasizes to the liver.
   - **Pancreatic Cancer**: Can spread to the liver.
   - **Breast Cancer**: Can also metastasize to the liver.

3. **Multiple Myeloma**: This hematologic malignancy is associated with elevated calcium (due to bone resorption) and can sometimes elevate ALP and LDH, though GGT elevation is less common.

4. **Primary Liver Cancer (Hepatocellular Carcinoma)**: This can elevate ALP, GGT, and LDH levels, although hypercalcemia is less typical unless there is concomitant paraneoplastic syndrome.

**Pathophysiology:**
- **Elevated Calcium**: This is often due to bone metastases causing osteolysis or in some cancers, due to paraneoplastic hypercalcemia mediated by PTH-related peptide.
- **Elevated ALP**: ALP is elevated in conditions affecting bone and liver. Bone metastases cause increased osteoblastic activity, and liver metastases or primary liver tumors increase hepatic ALP production.
- **Elevated GGT**: GGT is primarily a marker of hepatic function and can be elevated in liver metastases or primary liver cancer.
- **Elevated LDH**: LDH is a nonspecific marker of cell turnover and can be elevated in many malignancies due to high tumor burden and cell lysis.

### Summary
Cancers commonly associated with concurrent elevation of calcium, ALP, GGT, and LDH are typically those that metastasize to bone or liver, such as breast cancer, prostate cancer, lung cancer, colorectal cancer, and pancreatic cancer. Multiple myeloma and hepatocellular carcinoma may also present with some of these elevations. Each marker's elevation reflects different aspects of tumor biology and organ involvement.
