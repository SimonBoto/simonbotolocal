## **Training Material: Understanding Therapeutic Diets**

### **1. Understanding Macronutrients**

- **Proteins and Fats:** Essential for body functions, including muscle repair and hormone production.
- **Carbohydrates:** Primary energy source; focus on complex carbs for sustained energy.
- **Balanced Intake:** While carbs are not classified as essential, they play a crucial role in energy provision and fiber intake.

### **2. Prioritizing Nutrient-Dense Foods**

- **High-Quality Proteins:** Red meat from ruminants, eggs, seafood, and fish provide essential amino acids and micronutrients.
- **Variety:** Incorporate different protein sources to ensure a wide range of nutrients.

### **3. Understanding Fats and Cholesterol**

- **Healthy Fats:** Include sources like olive oil, avocados, nuts, and fatty fish.
- **Saturated Fats:** Consume in moderation; focus on whole-food sources.
- **Cholesterol:** Essential for hormone production but should be balanced within dietary guidelines.

### **4. Grains and Their Preparation**

- **Whole Grains:** Provide fiber and essential nutrients.
- **Anti-Nutrients:** Fermentation (e.g., sourdough) reduces compounds that inhibit nutrient absorption.
- **Glycemic Impact:** Be mindful of portion sizes to manage blood sugar levels.

### **5. Oxalate Awareness**

- **High-Oxalate Foods to Limit:**
  - Spinach, peanuts, cashews, almonds.
- **Preparation Methods:**
  - Soaking and peeling nuts like almonds can reduce oxalate content.
- **Nightshades:**
  - Tomatoes, eggplants, and peppers should be peeled and de-seeded if sensitive.
  - Pair with healthy fats and acids like olive oil and vinegar to enhance nutrient absorption.

- **Collaboration:** Encourage open communication between patients, dietitians, and healthcare providers.

### **Understanding Dietary Patterns and Carbohydrate Intake**

#### **Dietary Categories:**

- **Carnivore Diet:** Focuses exclusively on animal products.
- **Animal-Based Diet:** Emphasizes animal products but includes some plant foods.
- **Paleo Diet:** Excludes grains, legumes, dairy, and processed foods; focuses on whole foods.
- **Gluten-Free Diet:** Eliminates all forms of gluten.
- **Mediterranean Diet:** Rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, legumes, and healthy fats.

#### **Carbohydrate Intake Levels:**

- **Ketogenic Diet:** Less than 30 grams of net carbs per day.
- **Low-Carb Diet:** Less than 60 grams of net carbs per day.
- **Medium-Carb Diet:** Less than 100 grams of net carbs per day.

A **Minimal Residue Diet**, also known as a **Low Residue Diet**, is designed to reduce the size and frequency of bowel movements by minimizing the intake of foods that increase intestinal activity. This diet aims to lessen the workload on the gastrointestinal tract by limiting fiber, fat, and other foods that can be difficult to digest. It is often recommended for patients with inflammatory bowel diseases during flare-ups, before and after surgeries of the gastrointestinal tract, or for other conditions where it's beneficial to reduce bowel volume.

Here’s a general guideline on how to follow a minimal residue diet:

### **Foods to Eat:**

#### **1. Grains**
- **Allowed:** Refined grains like white bread, white rice, plain crackers, and cereals with less than 1 gram of fiber per serving.
- **Avoid:** Whole grains, brown rice, and any grain products with seeds or nuts.

#### **2. Proteins**
- **Allowed:** Tender cuts of meat (such as chicken, turkey, and fish), eggs, and tofu.
- **Avoid:** Tough meats, meats with casings, and all legumes (beans, lentils).

#### **3. Dairy Products**
- **Allowed:** Lactose may be tolerable in small amounts; options include plain yogurt, mild cheeses, and lactose-free dairy products.

- **Allowed:** Well-cooked and skinless vegetables; canned or cooked fruits without skins or seeds.
- **Avoid:** All raw fruits and vegetables, especially those high in fiber like berries, and cruciferous vegetables like broccoli and cauliflower.

- **Allowed:** Butter, oils, and margarine in moderation.

#### **6. Beverages**
- **Allowed:** Water, tea (without caffeine), and clear juices like apple or cranberry.
