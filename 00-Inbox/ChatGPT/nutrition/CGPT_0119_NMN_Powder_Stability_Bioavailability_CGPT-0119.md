Nicotinamide mononucleotide (NMN) powder's **stability** and **bioavailability** depend on multiple factors, including storage conditions, formulation, and the route of administration.

### **1. Stability of NMN Powder**
NMN is relatively **unstable** when exposed to heat, light, and humidity because it degrades into nicotinamide (NAM), especially in aqueous solutions. Here are key points regarding its stability:

- **Temperature Sensitivity:** NMN degrades faster at high temperatures. Studies show that NMN should be stored at **room temperature or lower** (preferably **below 25°C**) to maintain its potency.
- **Moisture Sensitivity:** NMN is hygroscopic, meaning it absorbs moisture from the air. This can accelerate its breakdown into nicotinamide.
- **Light Sensitivity:** UV light can degrade NMN over time, so it should be stored in an opaque or dark container.
- **Formulation Matters:** Some NMN supplements include stabilizers like trehalose or microencapsulation, which improve its shelf life.

### **2. Bioavailability of NMN**
NMN is **more bioavailable than nicotinamide riboside (NR)** but less efficient than **intravenous NAD+ administration**. The main factors affecting its bioavailability include:

- **Oral Absorption:** NMN is absorbed in the small intestine via the Slc12a8 transporter, which facilitates direct NMN uptake. However, some NMN may still convert to nicotinamide (NAM) before reaching circulation.
- **Sublingual Administration:** Some studies suggest that **sublingual NMN** bypasses the digestive system and is more bioavailable than capsules.
- **Intravenous or Intranasal NMN:** These methods result in much higher bioavailability but are less practical for daily use.
- **Gut Microbiome Influence:** The composition of gut bacteria can affect NMN metabolism, possibly altering its conversion rate to nicotinamide.

### **3. Enhancing NMN Stability and Bioavailability**
To optimize NMN supplementation:
- **Choose well-packaged NMN powder** (vacuum-sealed or in desiccant-containing containers).
- **Store in a cool, dry place**, ideally a refrigerator.
- **Opt for sublingual NMN formulations** for better absorption.
- **Consider NMN precursors** like NR if stability is a concern, though NMN has better direct absorption.

### **Conclusion**
NMN powder is **somewhat unstable**, requiring proper storage to maintain potency. Its **bioavailability is decent**, especially when taken sublingually or in stabilized formulations. However, rapid conversion into NAD+ and its breakdown into nicotinamide must be considered when choosing an administration method.
