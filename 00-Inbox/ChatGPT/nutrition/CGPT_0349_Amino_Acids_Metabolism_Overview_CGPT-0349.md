Here's a table listing amino acids that metabolize into specific intermediates including glycolic acid, oxalate, phenylpyruvic acid, acetyl-CoA, alpha-ketoglutarate, and ketoisovaleric acid:

| Amino Acid         | Metabolic Intermediate      |
|--------------------|-----------------------------|
| Glycine            | Glycolic Acid               |
| Glycine            | Oxalate                     |

| Isoleucine         | Acetyl-CoA                  |
| Leucine            | Acetyl-CoA                  |

| Valine             | Ketoisovaleric Acid         |

### Notes:

1. **Glycine**:
   - **Glycolic Acid**: Glycine is converted to glycolic acid through a pathway involving glyoxylate.
   - **Oxalate**: Glycine can also be metabolized to oxalate, often via glyoxylate as an intermediate.

2. **Phenylalanine**:
   - **Phenylpyruvic Acid**: Phenylalanine is transaminated to form phenylpyruvic acid.

3. **Amino acids producing Acetyl-CoA**:
   - **Isoleucine, Leucine, Lysine, Tryptophan, Alanine**: These amino acids undergo catabolic pathways that ultimately produce acetyl-CoA.

4. **Amino acids producing Alpha-Ketoglutarate**:
   - **Arginine, Glutamate, Glutamine, Histidine, Proline**: These amino acids are catabolized to produce alpha-ketoglutarate, an intermediate in the TCA cycle.

5. **Valine**:
   - **Ketoisovaleric Acid**: Valine is transaminated to form ketoisovaleric acid.

This table provides a concise overview of the metabolic fates of these amino acids.
