What forms of magnesium are present in hemp seeds and which antinutrient inhibit its absorption 

---

### 2. **ChatGPT:**

Hemp seeds are a notable source of magnesium, and they contain several forms of this essential mineral. The primary forms of magnesium in hemp seeds include:

1. **Magnesium Oxide**: This is the most common form found in dietary sources and is known for its high magnesium content.
2. **Magnesium Citrate**: While less common than magnesium oxide, magnesium citrate can also be present in hemp seeds, albeit in smaller quantities.
3. **Magnesium Chloride**: This form is highly bioavailable but is present in minor amounts in hemp seeds.
4. **Magnesium Sulfate**: Present in trace amounts, this form contributes to the overall magnesium content in hemp seeds.

Regarding antinutrients that inhibit magnesium absorption, hemp seeds contain several compounds that can interfere with the bioavailability of magnesium:

1. **Phytic Acid**: This is the most significant antinutrient in hemp seeds. Phytic acid can bind to magnesium (as well as other minerals like calcium, zinc, and iron), forming insoluble complexes that the body cannot absorb. This reduces the bioavailability of magnesium from the seeds.
   
2. **Oxalates**: Hemp seeds contain oxalates, which can also bind to magnesium and form insoluble salts, thereby reducing magnesium absorption.

3. **Fiber**: While dietary fiber is beneficial for health, excessive fiber can sometimes bind to minerals like magnesium and inhibit their absorption in the digestive tract.

Strategies to mitigate the impact of these antinutrients include soaking or sprouting hemp seeds, which can reduce the levels of phytic acid and oxalates, thereby enhancing the bioavailability of magnesium and other minerals.
