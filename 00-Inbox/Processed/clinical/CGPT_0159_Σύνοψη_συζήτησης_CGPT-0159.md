### **1. Person-Centered Medicine**  

#### **Through the Patient’s Eyes**  

There is a significant difference between medicine that focuses on the individual and that which focuses on the disease. In recent years, we have witnessed radical changes in the medical field, and if we were to summarize what has happened, we would say that observing things from within the patient’s perspective is now considered the only possible way to address the challenges that medicine faces today.  

More specifically, we have moved from the paradigm of therapy to therapeutic collaboration, from treating the patient to caring for them, from the concept of **sickness**, which is associated with a disease (**disease**) in the body, to that of **illness**, which considers the overall condition of the patient as a person.  

A key differentiation is **compliance**, where the patient "tolerates" the doctor’s recommendations, versus **adherence**, where the patient accepts the doctor’s recommendations as their own. This transition is crucial in shifting from a doctor-centered system to a patient-centered system, where the patient plays an increasingly active role in improving their health.  

Furthermore, communication has become even more critical. It is essential to **communicate** with the patient rather than simply **inform** them. Good communication is based on an emotional connection, on the doctor understanding the patient’s needs, and sometimes, it is necessary to establish a good relationship with their family as well.
