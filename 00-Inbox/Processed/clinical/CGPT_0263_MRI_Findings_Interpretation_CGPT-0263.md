The **PI-RADS (Prostate Imaging Reporting and Data System)** is a standardized scoring system used in MRI to evaluate the risk of clinically significant prostate cancer. It ranges from 1 to 5:

- **PI-RADS 1**: Very low likelihood of clinically significant cancer.
- **PI-RADS 2**: Low likelihood of clinically significant cancer.
- **PI-RADS 3**: Intermediate likelihood – the findings are equivocal, and further evaluation is often needed.
- **PI-RADS 4**: High likelihood of clinically significant cancer.
- **PI-RADS 5**: Very high likelihood of clinically significant cancer.

In this case:

- **PI-RADS 4**: Indicates a high likelihood of clinically significant prostate cancer in the identified lesion, with an emphasis on the need for further evaluation, typically through a biopsy.
