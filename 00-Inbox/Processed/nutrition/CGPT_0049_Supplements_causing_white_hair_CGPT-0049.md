## 1. Melanocyte loss and pigment reduction  
- In pigmented hair, specialized cells called **melanocytes** sit next to the keratin-producing cells in the hair bulb. They pass melanin granules into the keratinocytes, which become the actual hair shaft.

- Result: the hair shaft is built mostly of keratin without melanin granules.  

---

## 2. Structural role of melanin  
- Melanin isn’t just color — it also changes **mechanical properties** of the shaft.  
- It helps **bind keratin fibers** more tightly and contributes to moisture balance.  
- Without melanin, hair shafts have altered cross-linking → less flexible, more brittle.  

---

## 3. Keratin matrix shifts  
- Gray hairs show altered **keratin-associated proteins** and **lipid composition**.  
- Keratin fibrils align more rigidly without the “space-filling” effect of melanin granules.  
- That rigidity translates to a coarser, stiffer texture.

- Pigment loss is linked with **oxidative stress** (hydrogen peroxide buildup in the follicle, lowered catalase/antioxidant defense).
