Metabolic Syndrome (MetS) is increasingly recognized as a critical condition impacting public health due to its strong association with various cardiovascular diseases and type 2 diabetes mellitus. It is characterized by a cluster of interrelated risk factors including insulin resistance, hypertension, dyslipidemia, and central obesity. The defining criteria for MetS have evolved, with key organizations like the International Diabetes Federation and the American Heart Association providing slightly varying frameworks which generally include measurements of waist circumference, fasting glucose levels, triglyceride levels, HDL cholesterol, and blood pressure.

A comprehensive review of these criteria reveals that despite minor variations, there is a consensus on the inclusion of these core components, which reflect the underlying pathophysiology of insulin resistance and the risk it poses for cardiovascular and metabolic disorders. The diagnostic criteria emphasize the role of central obesity and insulin resistance, highlighting the importance of these factors in clinical assessments and interventions aimed at managing MetS.
