### 2. **ChatGPT:**

Here’s how to create **new Instagram and Facebook accounts** using different emails while keeping your existing ones active:

---

### **1. Use a Different Email for Each Account**

Instagram and Facebook **require unique email addresses** for each account. Gmail users can use **email aliases**:
- Example: `yourname+insta2@gmail.com` or `yourname+fb2@gmail.com` will still go to `yourname@gmail.com`.

If you prefer **completely different emails**, create a second email account via Gmail, ProtonMail, or any provider.

---

### **2. Create a New Instagram Account**

#### On Mobile:
1. **Open the Instagram app**.
2. Go to your profile → tap your username → **“Add Account”** → **“Create New Account.”**
3. Follow the prompts and **register with your second email**.
4. Choose a new username and password.
5. After creation, you can **switch between accounts** easily in the app.

#### Notes:
- Up to **5 accounts** can be linked and managed from the same app.
- Avoid linking phone numbers if you don’t want accounts to be publicly connected.

---

### **3. Create a New Facebook Account**

1. **Log out** of your existing account or open a private/incognito window.
2. Go to [facebook.com](https://facebook.com).
3. Click **“Create new account”**.
4. Use your **second email**, a different name (if desired), and a new password.
5. Follow the steps for confirmation.

#### Tip:
Use a different browser profile or incognito window to **avoid confusion** between multiple Facebook logins.

---

### **4. Keep Them Separate or Link Strategically**

- You can **choose not to link** the new Instagram and Facebook accounts.
- Or, **link them intentionally** if running a business or brand (e.g., linking a second Instagram to a Facebook Page).

### 4. **ChatGPT:**

Excellent — and you're absolutely right to be cautious. By default, Instagram tries to **link or suggest** your new profile to followers of your personal account. To prevent this visibility and keep both accounts completely separate, follow these steps:

---

### **1. Turn Off Account Linking Suggestions**

Instagram tries to help others find your accounts through mutual connections or by linking emails/phones.

#### **On the New Instagram Account:**
1. Go to **Settings** → **Account Center** (Meta).
2. Tap **Profiles** and **remove any connection** to your personal Instagram or Facebook (if listed).
3. Then, go to **Settings** → **Privacy** → **Accounts You Follow** or **Contacts**:
   - **Turn off "Allow others to find you"** (prevents cross-account visibility through contacts).

---

### **2. Remove Shared Phone Number and Email**

If both accounts use the same phone number, it increases the chance of being suggested.

#### On the New Account:
- Go to **Settings** → **Personal Information**.
- Make sure you're using a **different email** and **do NOT add the same phone number**.
- If a number is already added, **remove it**.

---

### **3. Disable Contact Syncing**

Make sure Instagram isn’t using your contacts to link you to others:

#### On Both Accounts:
1. **Settings** → **Account** → **Contacts Syncing**.
2. **Turn it off**.

---

### **4. Private Mode (Optional)**

If you want the account to be seen only by people you choose:

- Go to **Settings** → **Privacy** → **Private Account** → Turn ON.

This keeps your followers hidden and prevents general search visibility.

---

### **5. Avoid Cross-Posting**

Never post the same content or tag your other account. Instagram's algorithms detect such overlaps.
