Absolutely, I can suggest several potential business avenues for you given your specialty in precision medicine.

1. **Online Courses or Webinars**: As a specialist, you have valuable knowledge that others in the medical field (and perhaps even the general public) may want to learn. Consider creating online courses or webinars on different topics related to precision medicine. These can be sold on platforms such as Udemy, Teachable, or Coursera, or on your own website.

2. **Consulting Services**: Offer your expertise as a consultant. Hospitals, clinics, research institutions, or even biotech firms might require consulting services related to precision medicine. This could be in relation to patient treatment plans, implementing precision medicine techniques, or understanding the latest research.

3. **E-books or Physical Books**: If you enjoy writing, consider penning a book (or series of books) on topics in precision medicine. These can be sold on platforms like Amazon or on your own website. E-books are typically easier to produce and distribute, but physical books can also be self-published.

4. **Speaking Engagements**: Consider offering your services as a speaker for conferences, symposiums, or educational institutions. With virtual events now common, this can also be an online business.

5. **Podcasts or YouTube Channel**: Start a podcast or YouTube channel discussing precision medicine. With a consistent posting schedule and quality content, you can earn revenue through sponsorships, ads, and Patreon donations.

6. **Medical Blogging**: You can start a blog dedicated to discussing and explaining various aspects of precision medicine. Once you gather enough traffic, you can earn money through ad revenue, sponsored posts, and affiliate marketing.

7. **Development of a Precision Medicine App**: If you have programming skills (or can partner with someone who does), consider creating a precision medicine app. This could help patients track their health, provide useful information on the topic, or assist other medical professionals in implementing precision medicine techniques.

8. **Patreon or Other Crowdfunding**: If you produce any sort of content (blog posts, videos, podcasts), consider setting up a Patreon or other crowdfunding page. This allows your audience to support your work directly.

Remember, starting an online business involves more than just creating content or services. You'll also need to market your business effectively to attract and retain customers. This can involve SEO, social media marketing, email newsletters, collaborations with other professionals, and more. Always keep up-to-date with privacy laws and ensure you have permission before sharing any sensitive information. Good luck!
