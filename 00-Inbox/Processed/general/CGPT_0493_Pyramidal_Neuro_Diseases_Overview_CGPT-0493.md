Pyramidal neurological diseases are those that affect the pyramidal tract in the brain, which is primarily responsible for controlling voluntary motor activity. Common diseases and conditions affecting the pyramidal tract include:

1. **Stroke:** A stroke can affect the pyramidal tract, particularly if it occurs in the motor cortex or the internal capsule. This often leads to hemiplegia or hemiparesis.

2. **Multiple Sclerosis (MS):** This autoimmune disease of the central nervous system can cause lesions in the pyramidal tract, leading to muscle weakness and spasticity.

3. **Amyotrophic Lateral Sclerosis (ALS):** ALS, also known as Lou Gehrig's disease, is a progressive neurodegenerative disease that affects nerve cells in the brain and the spinal cord, including the pyramidal tracts, leading to loss of muscle control.

4. **Cerebral Palsy:** This group of disorders affects movement and muscle tone or posture. It's caused by damage to the immature brain as it develops, often affecting the pyramidal tract.

5. **Spinal Cord Injury:** Damage to the spinal cord can affect the descending pathways of the pyramidal tract, leading to paralysis or weakness below the level of injury.

6. **Primary Lateral Sclerosis (PLS):** A rare neuromuscular disease characterized by progressive muscle weakness in the voluntary muscles, caused by degeneration of neurons in the upper motor neurons of the pyramidal tracts.

7. **Brain Tumors:** Tumors in certain areas of the brain can impact the pyramidal tract, leading to symptoms like weakness or paralysis on one side of the body.

These conditions can have various etiologies and present with a range of symptoms, but they all involve the pyramidal tract and typically manifest with motor deficits like weakness, spasticity, and changes in reflexes. Diagnosis and management depend on the specific condition and the overall health and needs of the patient.
