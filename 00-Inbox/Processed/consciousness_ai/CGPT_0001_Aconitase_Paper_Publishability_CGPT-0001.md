### 1. The Core Identity

Aconitase catalyzes the reversible isomerization of citrate to isocitrate via cis-aconitate in the TCA cycle.

That sounds pedestrian. It is not.

The reaction depends on a **[4Fe–4S] iron–sulfur cluster**. This cluster is both catalytic and fragile. Oxidize it slightly, remove one iron, and the enzyme transforms.

Mitochondrial aconitase (ACO2) → TCA enzyme.  
Cytosolic aconitase (ACO1) → loses one iron → becomes **IRP1 (Iron Regulatory Protein 1)** → binds mRNA iron response elements.

Same protein. Two identities. Metabolism vs gene regulation.

That duality alone justifies a high-level review.

---

### 2. The Redox Sensor

Aconitase is exquisitely sensitive to:

- Superoxide
- Nitric oxide
- Peroxynitrite
- Iron depletion
- Oxidative stress

The Fe-S cluster acts like a molecular Geiger counter. When ROS rise, aconitase activity drops early — often before ATP collapses or mitochondria fragment.

In other words, aconitase is not just a TCA enzyme.  
It is an **early redox sentinel**.

In ischemia-reperfusion, neurodegeneration, metabolic syndrome, and inflammation, aconitase inhibition is often one of the first measurable mitochondrial defects.

That makes it conceptually important for metabolomics. Citrate accumulation and downstream TCA alterations can be interpreted as *functional redox footprints*.

---

### 3. Iron Metabolism and Systems Control

When cytosolic aconitase loses its Fe-S cluster:

- It stops catalyzing citrate.
- It binds iron-response elements (IREs) in mRNAs.
- It increases transferrin receptor expression.
- It decreases ferritin translation.

This is elegant systems biology.

Low iron → IRP1 binds RNA → more iron uptake → less iron storage.  
High iron → Fe-S cluster reassembled → IRP1 returns to enzyme form → iron homeostasis stabilizes.

This is metabolism directly regulating gene expression without transcription factors in the classical sense.

It is a post-transcriptional metabolic rheostat.

### 4. Disease Landscapes

**Neurodegeneration**

- Fe-S sensitivity + high ROS load makes it a target in Parkinsonian models.

**Cancer**
- Altered TCA flux.
- Citrate shunting to lipid synthesis.

- Some tumors show altered reliance on aconitase activity.

**Inflammation**
- Macrophage metabolic rewiring affects citrate export.
- Nitric oxide can inactivate aconitase.
- Citrate becomes a substrate for inflammatory mediator production.

**Aging**
- Progressive decline in Fe-S cluster integrity.
- Accumulated oxidative hits.
- Possible role in mitochondrial signaling collapse.

The enzyme becomes a crossroad between redox, iron, inflammation, and mitochondrial integrity.

### 5. Methodological Reality

A serious review must address measurement pitfalls:

- Aconitase is labile during extraction.
- Freeze–thaw cycles destroy Fe-S clusters.
- Activity assays are sensitive to oxygen exposure.
- Metabolomics inference from citrate/isocitrate ratio is indirect and context dependent.

No masterpiece ignores technical nuance.
