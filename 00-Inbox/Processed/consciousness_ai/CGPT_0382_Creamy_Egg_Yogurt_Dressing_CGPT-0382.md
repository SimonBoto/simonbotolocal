### Ingredients:
- 2 hard-boiled eggs
- 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
- Juice of half a lemon
- 1/2 cup olive oil
- 2 tablespoons finely chopped cornichons (small pickles)
- 1 tablespoon capers, finely chopped
- 2 tablespoons chopped fresh herbs (like tarragon, parsley, and chervil)
- Salt and pepper to taste

### Instructions:

1. **Prepare the eggs**:
   - Boil the eggs for about 9-12 minutes until hard-boiled.
   - Cool them under cold running water, then peel and chop them. Separate the yolks from the whites. Chop the whites finely and mash the yolks with a fork.

2. **Mix the base**:
   - In a medium bowl, add the mashed egg yolks.
   - Add 1 tablespoon of Dijon mustard and the juice of half a lemon to the yolks.
   - Mix well until you have a smooth paste.

3. **Add the olive oil**:
   - Slowly whisk in the 1/2 cup of olive oil a little at a time. This helps create a smooth, emulsified sauce. Keep whisking until all the oil is incorporated and the sauce is creamy.

4. **Combine other ingredients**:
   - Add the chopped egg whites, 2 tablespoons of finely chopped cornichons, 1 tablespoon of finely chopped capers, and 2 tablespoons of chopped fresh herbs.
   - Stir everything together gently to combine.

5. **Season**:
   - Add salt and pepper to taste. Start with a small pinch of each and adjust as needed.

6. **Chill**:
   - Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and let the sauce chill in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

7. **Serve**:
   - Your Gribiche sauce is ready to be served! It pairs well with cold meats, fish, or vegetables.

### Egg Salad
A classic egg salad is always a hit.

#### Ingredients:
- 4 hard-boiled eggs
- 1/4 cup mayonnaise
- 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
- 1 teaspoon lemon juice
- 1 tablespoon finely chopped celery
- 1 tablespoon finely chopped red onion
- Salt and pepper to taste
- Optional: a pinch of paprika or fresh herbs like chives or parsley

#### Instructions:
1. **Prepare the eggs**: Chop the hard-boiled eggs into small pieces.
2. **Mix the base**: In a bowl, combine the mayonnaise, Dijon mustard, and lemon juice.
3. **Combine ingredients**: Add the chopped eggs, celery, and red onion to the bowl. Mix everything together gently.
4. **Season**: Add salt and pepper to taste. Optionally, sprinkle with a pinch of paprika or fresh herbs.
5. **Serve**: Chill the egg salad for at least 15 minutes before serving on bread, lettuce leaves, or crackers.

### Deviled Eggs
Another classic way to enjoy boiled eggs.

#### Ingredients:
- 6 hard-boiled eggs
- 3 tablespoons mayonnaise
- 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
- 1 teaspoon white vinegar
- Salt and pepper to taste
- Paprika for garnish

#### Instructions:
1. **Prepare the eggs**: Peel the hard-boiled eggs and cut them in half lengthwise. Remove the yolks and place them in a bowl.
2. **Make the filling**: Mash the yolks with a fork. Add the mayonnaise, Dijon mustard, and white vinegar. Mix until smooth.
3. **Season**: Add salt and pepper to taste.
4. **Fill the eggs**: Spoon or pipe the yolk mixture back into the egg whites.
5. **Garnish**: Sprinkle with paprika for a bit of color and flavor.
6. **Serve**: Chill the deviled eggs for at least 15 minutes before serving.

### Scotch Eggs

#### Ingredients:
- 4 large eggs (for boiling)
- 1 egg (for coating)
- 1 pound sausage meat
- 1/2 teaspoon mustard powder
- 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
- 1/2 teaspoon salt
- 1 cup all-purpose flour
- 1 cup breadcrumbs
- Oil for frying

#### Instructions:

1. **Boil the eggs**:
   - Place the 4 eggs in a saucepan and cover them with water.
   - Bring to a boil and cook for about 6-8 minutes for slightly soft yolks or 10 minutes for fully hard yolks.
   - Transfer the eggs to a bowl of cold water to cool, then peel them.

2. **Prepare the sausage meat**:
   - In a bowl, mix the sausage meat with mustard powder, black pepper, and salt until well combined.

3. **Wrap the eggs**:
   - Divide the sausage meat into 4 equal portions.
   - Flatten each portion into a thin patty.
   - Place a boiled egg in the center of each patty and carefully wrap the sausage around the egg, ensuring it is fully covered.

4. **Prepare the coating**:
   - Beat the remaining egg in a small bowl.
   - Place the flour on a plate and the breadcrumbs on another plate.

5. **Coat the eggs**:
   - Roll each sausage-wrapped egg in flour, shaking off any excess.
   - Dip it into the beaten egg, allowing any excess to drip off.
   - Roll it in breadcrumbs, ensuring it is evenly coated.

6. **Fry the Scotch eggs**:
   - Heat oil in a deep fryer or a large saucepan to 350°F (175°C).
   - Carefully lower each Scotch egg into the hot oil and fry for about 5-7 minutes, turning occasionally, until golden brown and cooked through.
   - Transfer the fried Scotch eggs to a paper towel-lined plate to drain any excess oil.

7. **Serve**:
   - Allow the Scotch eggs to cool slightly before serving. They can be enjoyed warm or at room temperature, often with a side of mustard or pickle.
