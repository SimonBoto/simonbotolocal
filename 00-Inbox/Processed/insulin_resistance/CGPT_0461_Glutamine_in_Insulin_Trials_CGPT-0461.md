**Physiological Explanation**
Glutamine serves as the precursor for nitric oxide production in β-cells, enhancing insulin secretion. Additionally, it contributes to glutathione production, crucial for mitigating oxidative stress, a central factor in the pathogenesis of diabetes complications. Recent studies have shown glutamine's efficacy in improving glycemic control, evidenced by its impact on incretin hormones like glucagon-like peptide-1 (GLP-1) and gastric inhibitory polypeptide (GIP). These hormones are integral to insulin secretion and glycemic regulation.
