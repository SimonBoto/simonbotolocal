### 1. **Input layer: Clinical–Analytical Mapping**

4. **GI/Barrier module** – glutamine, mastiha, probiotic, postbiotic.

6. **Targeted module** – thyroid, testosterone, senolytics, etc.

### 4. **Calibration layer: Ratio Logic and Safety Nets**

Here’s where metabolomics shines: **ratios, not absolute values.**

Example:
- Cholesterol/HDL, HDL/TG → lipid flux.
- Lactate/pyruvate → redox.
- GSH/GSSG → antioxidant capacity.
- Citric/isocitric → TCA function.

Your regimen is considered “balanced” when **the ratios normalize**, not necessarily when absolute numbers enter reference ranges.

Safety nets are enforced simultaneously:
- Check for cumulative D, K2, Fe, B6, iodine, Ca, herbal interactions.
- Respect hepatic/renal elimination capacity (avoid 10+ poly-herbals concurrently).
- Keep redox balance: avoid pro-oxidant paradox of too-high antioxidants in low ROS contexts.

### 5. **Feedback layer: Clinical–Biochemical Loop**

The regimen must be **iterative**, not static.

1. **Clinical feedback:** energy, sleep, digestion, mood, menses, muscle recovery.
2. **Biochemical feedback:** repeat OAT/FFA/labs at 3–6 months.
3. **Dynamic adjustment:**  
   - If baseline deficits corrected → move to maintenance.  
   - If new compensations appear → re-target modules.

In practice:  
- Foundation stays stable (multi + D/K2/Mg + omega + glutamine).  
- Corrective modules cycle 8–12 weeks.  
- Maintenance focuses on diet, fewer capsules.

Three core rules govern this process:

1. **Systems coherence:** treat the whole network, not the node.

2. **Cofactor redundancy is not benign:** every duplication changes flux.  
   → The difference between synergy and toxicity is redundancy without rationale.

3. **Adaptive cycling:** the body is a dynamic system.  
   → No formula is sacred; everything is contingent on new data.
