## 13.1 Therapies

### Stem Cell Therapy

**What it is:**  
The use of pluripotent cells to regenerate and repair tissues. They can differentiate into various cell types and restore damaged tissue.

**Recommended Therapies:**  
• Mesenchymal Stem Cells (MSC): Used for tissue regeneration and repair of joints, cartilage, and muscles.  
• Adipose-Derived Stem Cells: Harvested from adipose tissue and used for skin rejuvenation and joint restoration.

**Benefits:** Tissue regeneration, reduced inflammation, deceleration of aging at cellular and organ level.

**Contraindications:**  
Careful selection of both the cell source and the clinic is essential, as the quality of the cells and the procedure directly impacts therapeutic success.

---

### Rapamycin

**What it is:**  
A drug that inhibits the mTOR pathway, a major regulator of aging. Primarily used post-transplant to prevent organ rejection, but shows promising longevity effects.

**Benefits:** Slows cellular aging, enhances immune function, and may extend lifespan.

**Contraindications:**  
Not suitable for everyone; may suppress immune function. Medical supervision is required.

---

### Bioregulatory Peptides

**What they are:**  
Peptides that regulate the function of specific organs or tissues, supporting regeneration and improved performance.

**Recommended Peptides:**  
● Epitalon: Regulates melatonin production and supports telomere maintenance.

**Benefits:** May slow aging, improve organ function, and boost immune health.

**Contraindications:**  
Should be administered by specialists; excessive or improper use may cause adverse effects.

---

### Plasma Exchange (Therapeutic Plasma Exchange)

**What it is:**  
Removal of old plasma and replacement with fresh plasma or plasma components. This procedure has shown potential to rejuvenate tissues and improve brain function.

**Benefits:** Tissue rejuvenation and reduction of circulating aging factors.

**Contraindications:**  
More complex and costly; must be performed in specialized medical centers.

**Contraindications:**  
Generally safe; IV use requires medical supervision.
