To calculate the **pH of a solution** with 4 grams of ascorbic acid dissolved in 300 grams (or milliliters) of water:

### Step 1: Determine the concentration
1. Molar mass of ascorbic acid (C₆H₈O₆) = **176.12 g/mol**.
2. Moles of ascorbic acid in 4 g:  
   \[
   \text{Moles} = \frac{\text{Mass}}{\text{Molar mass}} = \frac{4}{176.12} \approx 0.0227 \, \text{moles}.
   \]
3. Volume of water is approximately 300 mL (300 g of water, assuming density = 1 g/mL), so the molarity (M) is:  
   \[
   \text{Molarity} = \frac{\text{Moles}}{\text{Volume in liters}} = \frac{0.0227}{0.3} \approx 0.0757 \, \text{M}.
   \]

### Step 2: Use the dissociation of ascorbic acid
Ascorbic acid is a weak acid with a **pKa of 4.1**. The equilibrium expression is:
\[
\text{pH} = -\log \left( \sqrt{\text{C} \cdot K_a} \right),
\]
where:
- \( C \) = concentration = 0.0757 M,
- \( K_a \) = \( 10^{-\text{pKa}} \) = \( 10^{-4.1} \approx 7.94 \times 10^{-5} \).

### Step 3: Solve for pH
\[
\text{pH} = -\log \left( \sqrt{0.0757 \cdot 7.94 \times 10^{-5}} \right).
\]
First, calculate the product:  
\[
0.0757 \cdot 7.94 \times 10^{-5} \approx 6.01 \times 10^{-6}.
\]
Now take the square root:  
\[
\sqrt{6.01 \times 10^{-6}} \approx 2.45 \times 10^{-3}.
\]
Finally, calculate the pH:  
\[
\text{pH} = -\log(2.45 \times 10^{-3}) \approx 2.6.
\]

### Final Answer:
The **pH of 4 g of ascorbic acid in 300 g of water** is approximately **2.6**.

\[

- **Sodium ascorbate** is the sodium salt of ascorbic acid, which is less acidic (pH ~7 when fully neutralized).
- The reaction releases carbon dioxide (CO₂), causing fizzing or bubbling.

### 2. **Effect on pH**
Adding baking soda raises the pH of the solution, making it closer to neutral (depending on the amount of baking soda added). This can:
- Reduce stomach irritation caused by the acidity of ascorbic acid.
- Allow for higher doses of ascorbic acid to be consumed comfortably.

### 3. **Does It Affect the Efficacy of Ascorbic Acid?**
- **Sodium ascorbate** retains the antioxidant and vitamin C activity of ascorbic acid because the active component is the ascorbate ion, not the free acid.
- Studies suggest that sodium ascorbate is equally effective as ascorbic acid in supporting physiological functions, such as:
  - Acting as an antioxidant.
  - Supporting collagen synthesis.
  - Enhancing immune function.

### 4. **Possible Negative Effects**

### Conclusion:
