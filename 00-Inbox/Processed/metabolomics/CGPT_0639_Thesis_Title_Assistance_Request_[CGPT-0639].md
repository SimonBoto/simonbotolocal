The HOMA-IR is calculated using the formula:

    HOMA-IR = (Fasting insulin (μU/mL) x Fasting glucose (mmol/L)) / 22.5

A higher HOMA-IR score indicates greater insulin resistance.

It's important to note that while HOMA-IR is a useful tool, it has its limitations. It's based on a number of assumptions and simplifications about glucose and insulin dynamics, and it may not be accurate in all situations (for example, in individuals with type 1 diabetes or in the setting of severe insulin deficiency). It's also less accurate than more complex methods for assessing insulin resistance, like the euglycemic-hyperinsulinemic clamp, but its simplicity and low cost make it a practical choice for many situations.
