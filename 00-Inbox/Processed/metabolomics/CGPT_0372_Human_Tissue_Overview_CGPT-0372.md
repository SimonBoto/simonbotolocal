In human biology, tissues are groups of cells that perform a similar function. There are four primary types of tissues, but these can be further divided into subtypes. Here are the primary tissue types and their major subtypes, totaling seven key tissue types often discussed in the context of human anatomy and physiology:

1. **Epithelial Tissue**
   - **Covering and Lining Epithelium**: Covers body surfaces and lines body cavities, such as the skin and the lining of the digestive tract.
   - **Glandular Epithelium**: Forms glands that secrete substances such as hormones, enzymes, and sweat.

2. **Connective Tissue**
   - **Loose Connective Tissue**: Includes areolar, adipose, and reticular tissues.
   - **Dense Connective Tissue**: Includes dense regular, dense irregular, and elastic tissues.
   - **Specialized Connective Tissues**: Includes cartilage, bone, and blood.

3. **Muscle Tissue**
   - **Skeletal Muscle**: Voluntary muscle that attaches to bones and allows for movement.
   - **Cardiac Muscle**: Involuntary muscle found in the heart.
   - **Smooth Muscle**: Involuntary muscle found in the walls of internal organs such as the stomach and intestines.

4. **Nervous Tissue**
   - Composed of neurons and supporting cells called glial cells, this tissue is responsible for transmitting electrical signals throughout the body.

To summarize, the seven key types of tissues often highlighted in human anatomy are:

1. Covering and Lining Epithelium
2. Glandular Epithelium
3. Loose Connective Tissue
4. Dense Connective Tissue
5. Specialized Connective Tissues (cartilage, bone, blood)
6. Muscle Tissue (skeletal, cardiac, smooth)
7. Nervous Tissue

These classifications encompass the diversity of tissue types found in the human body, each playing a crucial role in maintaining physiological functions.

In Ayurveda, the traditional system of medicine from India, the concept of tissues is referred to as "Dhatus." 1. **Rasa Dhatu (Plasma)**

2. **Rakta Dhatu (Blood)**
   - The second tissue, Rakta Dhatu is associated with the function of blood and is responsible for the transportation of oxygen and nutrients to all body parts, as well as vitality and complexion.

4. **Meda Dhatu (Fat)**

6. **Majja Dhatu (Bone Marrow and Nerve Tissue)**
