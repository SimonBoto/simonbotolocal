Preparing for a clinical trial as part of your PhD, especially in the field of insulin resistance and metabolomics, is a significant undertaking, and it's great that you're considering various platforms to increase the visibility and impact of your research.

In addition to applying to your university's ethical committee and registering with ClinicalTrials.gov, which is a widely recognized registry for clinical trials, there are several other international platforms and registries where you can register your clinical trial:

1. **World Health Organization (WHO) International Clinical Trials Registry Platform (ICTRP)**: The ICTRP aims to ensure that a complete view of research is accessible to all those involved in health care decision making. This can help improve the transparency of clinical research and also increase the likelihood of your study being found by international researchers.

2. **European Union Clinical Trials Register (EUCTR)**: If your study is being conducted in the European Union, or if you are considering including sites in the EU, registering with the EUCTR is important.

3. **ISRCTN registry**: This is a globally recognized registry that contains a wide range of clinical trials. It's an ideal platform for researchers looking to enhance the visibility of their study on an international level.

4. **National Registries**: Depending on the countries where your research will be conducted, there may be specific national registries where you should consider registering. For example, many countries in Europe, Asia, and Latin America have their own clinical trial registries.

5. **Specialized Registries**: Depending on the specific area of your research, there might be specialized registries focused on certain diseases or types of interventions. For instance, trials in cancer research are often registered with registries like the US National Cancer Institute's database.

Remember, the primary purpose of these registries is to promote transparency and to ensure that the scientific community and the public are informed about your research. Before registering, ensure that your trial meets the requirements of each registry, as they can vary. Additionally, maintaining up-to-date and accurate information on these registries is crucial for the credibility and visibility of your research. 

Good luck with your clinical trial and your PhD journey!
