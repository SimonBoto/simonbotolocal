Chickens fed predominantly on corn and grain-based diets can indeed exhibit an increased proportion of omega-6 polyunsaturated fatty acids (PUFAs) in their tissues. Under such feeding regimes, it is plausible that the total fatty acid profile in the chicken’s fat could have omega-6 levels reaching or even exceeding 36% of the total fatty acids.

Several factors influence this outcome:

1. **Feed Composition:** Corn and grain-based feeds are typically high in omega-6 fatty acids, which, when ingested by chickens, are incorporated into their tissues. In contrast, diets richer in sources of omega-3 fatty acids (e.g., flaxseed or fish oil) tend to shift the balance towards a more favorable omega-6/omega-3 ratio.

2. **Metabolic Processing:** The efficiency with which chickens metabolize and deposit these dietary fatty acids in muscle and adipose tissues can vary depending on genetic factors, overall health, and the specific composition of the feed. Some studies have noted that chickens raised on conventional grain diets can accumulate a significantly higher proportion of omega-6 fatty acids compared to pasture-raised or specially formulated diets.

3. **Implications for Inflammation:** While omega-6 fatty acids are essential for various physiological functions, an excessive dietary intake, especially when not balanced with omega-3 fatty acids, may promote a pro-inflammatory milieu. The conversion of linoleic acid to arachidonic acid and subsequently to eicosanoids (such as prostaglandins and leukotrienes) is a well-recognized pathway in the inflammatory response (Simopoulos, 2002; Calder, 2010).

In summary, it is scientifically plausible that chickens fed predominantly on corn and grains could reach high levels of omega-6 fatty acids—potentially around 36% or more—depending on the exact feed composition and rearing conditions. This alteration in fatty acid profile may have implications for the inflammatory potential of the meat, particularly if the consumption of such meat contributes to an imbalanced dietary omega-6/omega-3 ratio in humans.
