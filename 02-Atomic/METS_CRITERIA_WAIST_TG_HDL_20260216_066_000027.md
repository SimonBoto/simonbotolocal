# Metabolic Syndrome Diagnostic Criteria: IDF and ATP III Definitions

## Purpose

Standardized diagnostic criteria for metabolic syndrome (MetS) enable consistent identification of high-risk patients for early intervention and research enrollment. Multiple definitions exist; harmonized criteria most widely used.

## Harmonized IDF/ATP III Criteria (2009)

MetS diagnosed by **≥3 of 5** criteria:

| Criterion | Threshold (Men) | Threshold (Women) | Measurement |
|-----------|----------------|-------------------|-------------|
| **1. Elevated waist circumference** | >94 cm (>102 cm US*) | >80 cm (>88 cm US*) | At iliac crest, end-expiration |
| **2. Elevated triglycerides** | ≥150 mg/dL (1.7 mmol/L) | Same | Fasting preferred |
| **3. Reduced HDL cholesterol** | <40 mg/dL (1.0 mmol/L) | <50 mg/dL (1.3 mmol/L) | Fasting preferred |
| **4. Elevated blood pressure** | ≥130/85 mmHg | Same | Seated, 3 measurements |
| **5. Elevated fasting glucose** | ≥100 mg/dL (5.6 mmol/L) | Same | 8-12 hour fast |

*US-specific cutoffs for waist (ATP III original) often used; IDF recommends ethnicity-specific values.

## Ethnicity-Specific Waist Circumference Cutoffs (IDF)

| Population | Men | Women |
|------------|-----|-------|
| **Europids** | ≥94 cm | ≥80 cm |
| **South Asians, Chinese, Japanese** | ≥90 cm | ≥80 cm |
| **South/Central Americans** | ≥90 cm | ≥80 cm |
| **Sub-Saharan Africans, Eastern Mediterranean, Middle East** | ≥94 cm | ≥80 cm |
| **US (ATP III)** | ≥102 cm | ≥88 cm |

**Rationale:** Different body composition and risk profiles across ethnicities at same waist circumference.

## Detailed Criterion Specifications

### 1. Waist Circumference

**Why waist, not BMI?**
- Visceral fat more metabolically active than subcutaneous
- Waist predicts CVD risk better than BMI
- Simple, low-cost measurement

**Measurement protocol:**
1. Patient standing, feet together
2. Locate top of iliac crest
3. Measure at end of normal expiration
4. Tape parallel to floor, snug but not compressing skin
5. Record to nearest 0.1 cm

**Confounders:**
- Postprandial distension (measure fasting)
- Clothing (light clothing or none)
- Pregnancy (different threshold)
- Ascites (measure invalid)

### 2. Triglycerides

| Category | Level (mg/dL) | Level (mmol/L) |
|----------|---------------|----------------|
| **Normal** | <150 | <1.7 |
| **Borderline high** | 150-199 | 1.7-2.2 |
| **High** | 200-499 | 2.3-5.6 |
| **Very high** | ≥500 | ≥5.7 |

**Note:** ≥150 mg/dL = MetS criterion (no borderline allowance)

### 3. HDL Cholesterol

| Category | Men (mg/dL) | Women (mg/dL) |
|----------|-------------|---------------|
| **Low (MetS)** | <40 | <50 |
| **Borderline** | 40-59 | 50-59 |
| **Optimal** | ≥60 | ≥60 |

**Note:** Higher HDL is protective; ≥60 mg/dL is negative risk factor for CVD.

### 4. Blood Pressure

| Category | Systolic | Diastolic |
|----------|----------|-----------|
| **Normal** | <120 | <80 |
| **Elevated** | 120-129 | <80 |
| **Stage 1 HTN** | 130-139 | 80-89 |
| **Stage 2 HTN** | ≥140 | ≥90 |

**MetS criterion:** ≥130/85 mmHg OR on antihypertensive medication

**Measurement protocol:**
1. Seated, back supported, feet flat
2. Arm at heart level
3. Cuff appropriate size (bladder 80% arm circumference)
4. Rest 5 minutes
5. Take 3 readings, 1 minute apart
6. Record average of last 2

### 5. Fasting Glucose

| Category | FPG (mg/dL) | FPG (mmol/L) | HbA1c |
|----------|-------------|--------------|-------|
| **Normal** | <100 | <5.6 | <5.7% |
| **Impaired (IFG/MetS)** | 100-125 | 5.6-6.9 | 5.7-6.4% |
| **Diabetes** | ≥126 | ≥7.0 | ≥6.5% |

**Note:** MetS criterion is ≥100 mg/dL (pre-diabetes range)

## Alternative Definitions (Historical Context)

| Organization | Year | Key Difference |
|--------------|------|----------------|
| **WHO** | 1998 | Required insulin resistance (hyperinsulinemic-euglycemic clamp or fasting insulin) |
| **EGIR** | 1999 | European; insulin resistance required; lower waist threshold |
| **ATPIII** | 2001 | No insulin resistance required; US-centric waist |
| **IDF** | 2005 | Required central obesity as mandatory; ethnicity-specific waist |
| **Harmonized** | 2009 | Any 3 of 5; ethnicity-specific waist; most widely adopted |

## METHAP/PhD Application

### Cohort Eligibility

**Inclusion (MetS suspected):**
- ≥2 criteria present OR
- 1 criterion + insulin resistance (HOMA-IR >2.5)

**Stratification:**
- **Group A:** 3+ criteria (established MetS)
- **Group B:** 2 criteria + HOMA-IR elevated (pre-MetS)
- **Group C:** 1-2 criteria (at risk)

### Outcome Assessment

**Primary endpoint:** Change in number of MetS criteria (0-5 scale)
- Baseline: X criteria
- 12-week: Y criteria
- Response: Reduction by ≥1 criterion

**Secondary:**
- Resolution of MetS (dropping below 3 criteria)
- Individual criterion changes
- Time to resolution

### Metabolic Improvement vs. MetS Resolution

| Baseline | 12-Week | Interpretation |
|----------|---------|----------------|
| 4 criteria | 2 criteria | Major improvement, no longer MetS |
| 3 criteria | 3 criteria | Improved within criteria but still MetS |
| 5 criteria | 4 criteria | Improvement, still high risk |

## Limitations of MetS Criteria

| Limitation | Explanation |
|------------|-------------|
| **Arbitrary thresholds** | Binary cutoffs miss gradations of risk |
| **Missing inflammation** | CRP not included despite importance |
| **Static snapshot** | Doesn't capture metabolic trajectory |
| **Treatment target unclear** | Treat MetS or individual components? |
| **Ethnic variations** | Universal cutoffs imperfect |
