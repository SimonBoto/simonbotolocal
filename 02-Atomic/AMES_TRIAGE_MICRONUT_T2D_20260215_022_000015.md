# Ames Triage Theory: Micronutrient Prioritization Explains T2D Chronicity

## Core Claim

Bruce Ames' "triage theory" posits that when micronutrients are scarce, evolutionarily conserved mechanisms prioritize short-term survival and reproduction over long-term repair — leading to accelerated aging and chronic disease (including T2D) when "longevity vitamins" like vitamin D are chronically underprovided.

## The Triage Mechanism

### Evolutionary Logic

**Scarcity → Prioritization → Trade-offs**

| Priority Level | Functions Protected | Micronutrients | When Scarce |
|----------------|---------------------|----------------|-------------|
| **Immediate survival** | DNA repair (acute), ATP production, immune response | Iron, zinc, magnesium | Non-negotiable |
| **Reproduction** | Fertility, pregnancy, lactation | Folate, iron, iodine | Strongly protected |
| **Long-term repair** | Telomere maintenance, mitochondrial biogenesis, protein repair | Vitamin D, K2, selenium, omega-3 | **Sacrificed first** |

### The T2D Connection

**Step-by-step pathway:**

1. **Micronutrient insufficiency** (common in modern diets)
   - "Hidden hunger" — adequate calories, poor micronutrition
   - Soil depletion, processed foods, reduced diversity

2. **Triage activation**
   - Short-term proteins get cofactors first
   - Longevity proteins (SIRT, PARP, FOXO) underfunded

3. **Mitochondrial dysfunction**
   - Impaired β-oxidation (carnitine, CoQ10, B-vitamin dependent)
   - Alternative: ω-oxidation → adipic acid production

4. **Insulin resistance cascade**
   - Incomplete fatty acid oxidation → lipotoxicity
   - Oxidative stress → inflammation → IR
   - β-cell dysfunction (chronic demand exceeds supply)

5. **T2D chronicity**
   - Metabolic flexibility never restored
   - Progressive β-cell failure
   - Vascular complications (long-term repair deficit)

## METHAP/PhD Application

### Research Hypothesis
> Hashimoto's patients have compounded triage burden: autoimmune inflammation increases micronutrient demand (antioxidants, repair) while dietary restrictions may reduce intake. Adipic acid serves as a biomarker of triage failure (ω-oxidation overflow).

### Intervention Design

**Comprehensive Repletion Protocol:**

| Nutrient | Dose | Rationale | Monitoring |
|----------|------|-----------|------------|
| Vitamin D3 | 50,000 IU/week | Primary intervention | 25(OH)D monthly |
| Magnesium | 400 mg/day | Cofactor for 300+ enzymes | RBC Mg at 12w |
| Selenium | 200 μg/day | Thyroid function, antioxidant | Monitor if Hashimoto's |
| Omega-3 | 2g EPA+DHA/day | Inflammation resolution | Not measured |
| B-complex | RDA+ | Mitochondrial support | Clinical response |

**Outcome Measures:**
- **Primary:** HOMA-IR, TPO-Ab
- **Secondary:** Adipic acid (triage marker), quality of life
- **Mechanistic:** Oxidative stress markers (8-OHdG, F2-isoprostanes)

### Expected Outcome
Multi-nutrient repletion restores metabolic flexibility faster than single-nutrient intervention by addressing multiple triage bottlenecks simultaneously.

## Clinical Implications

### Beyond Single-Nutrient Thinking

**Traditional approach:** Test → Deficient → Supplement one nutrient

**Triage-informed approach:**
- Assume multiple suboptimal nutrients (modern diet)
- Address whole spectrum (foundation + longevity)
- Monitor functional outcomes (adipic, HOMA-IR) not just levels

**Patient Education:**
- "Your body is rationing nutrients — we're ending the rationing"
- Emphasize food diversity + targeted supplementation
