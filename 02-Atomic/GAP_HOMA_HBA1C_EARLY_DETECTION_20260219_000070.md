# Diagnostic Gap: HOMA-IR and HbA1c Limitations in Early IR Detection

## Core Claim

Current diagnostic tools for insulin resistance (HOMA-IR, HbA1c) frequently **fail to provide early and precise detection**, creating a critical need for novel biomarkers.

## The Problem

| Tool | Measures | Limitation |
|------|----------|------------|
| **HOMA-IR** | Fasting glucose × fasting insulin / 405 | Insensitive to early β-cell dysfunction; varies with insulin assay |
| **HbA1c** | 3-month average glucose | Late marker; affected by hemoglobin variants, anemia, ethnicity |

## Why It Matters

- **Window of opportunity missed:** By the time HOMA-IR or HbA1c are abnormal, IR has been progressing for years
- **Metabolic syndrome already established:** Late diagnosis means comorbidities (dyslipidemia, hypertension) already present
- **Intervention less effective:** Reversing established IR harder than preventing progression

## The Metabolomics Alternative

**Hypothesis:** Urine organic acids and plasma free fatty acids provide **earlier, more sensitive** detection of metabolic dysfunction before conventional markers change.

## Evidence Base

- Organic acids reflect mitochondrial function, fatty acid oxidation, and metabolic pathway activity
- Free fatty acids directly implicated in lipotoxicity and IR pathogenesis
- GC-MS enables simultaneous measurement of hundreds of metabolites

## METHAP Application

**Study Design:**
- Compare metabolomic profiles between healthy controls and IR patients
- Identify metabolite signatures that change **before** HOMA-IR elevation
- Validate against gold-standard clamp studies (subset)

## Clinical Translation

If validated, metabolomic screening could:
1. Identify at-risk individuals earlier
2. Enable preventive interventions (diet, exercise, supplements)
3. Monitor treatment response at metabolic level
