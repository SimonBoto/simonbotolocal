# Plasma Free Fatty Acids (FFA) and Lipotoxicity in IR

**Concept:** Elevated plasma free fatty acids (FFA) directly contribute to insulin resistance through lipotoxicity mechanisms.

**Mechanism of Lipotoxicity:**
1. **Elevated FFA** → adipose tissue insulin resistance → unrestrained lipolysis
2. **FFA uptake** by liver and muscle
3. **Intracellular accumulation** → lipid intermediates (DAG, ceramides)
4. **PKC activation** → serine phosphorylation of IRS-1
5. **Insulin signaling blocked** → glucose uptake impaired

**FFA Composition Matters:**
| Type | Effect on IR |
|------|--------------|
| Saturated (palmitate, stearate) | Pro-inflammatory, pro-IR |
| Monounsaturated (oleate) | Neutral or protective |
| Omega-3 (EPA, DHA) | Anti-inflammatory, insulin-sensitizing |
| Trans fats | Strongly pro-IR |

**Why Measure FFA Profile (Not Just Total):**
- Composition predicts IR risk better than concentration
- Omega-3 index correlates with insulin sensitivity
- SFA/MUFA ratio as biomarker

**Intervention Target:**
- Omega-3 supplementation shifts FFA profile
- Mediterranean diet reduces SFA, increases MUFA
- Weight loss reduces total FFA flux
