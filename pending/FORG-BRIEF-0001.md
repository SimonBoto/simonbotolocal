## Summary
ChatGPT conversation on fatty acid metabolism pathways, focusing on dicarboxylic acids (adipate/C6, suberate/C8, sebacate/C10) formed via omega-oxidation when beta-oxidation is impaired (e.g., carnitine deficiency). Starts from palmitate (C16), explains incomplete beta-ox leading to medium-chain acyl-CoAs, then omega-ox to dicarbs. Adipate further beta-oxidized to acetyl-CoA entering TCA cycle. Covers vitamins (B5 for CoA, B1/B6 for decarboxylation), enzymes (IDH dual dehydrogenase/decarboxylase, aconitase isomerase, IRP1 iron regulation), Krebs intermediates.

## Key Points
- Beta-oxidation (mito): primary FA breakdown to acetyl-CoA; impaired -> omega-ox (ER): hydroxylates omega-C to dicarbs.
- Palmitate -> hexanoyl-CoA (after 5 beta cycles) -> omega-hydroxy -> adipate via P450.
- Adipate -> adipyl-CoA -> glutaryl-CoA + acetyl-CoA -> succinyl-CoA + acetyl-CoA -> TCA.
- Dicarbs as markers for FA oxidation defects.
- Krebs: not all dicarbs (citrate tricarboxylic); IDH: deh + decarb isocitrate -> aKG + CO2 + NADH.
- Aconitase: citrate <-> isocitrate; IRP1 dual role iron sensor/aconitase.
- Omega-3/6/9 classification by first double bond from omega end.
