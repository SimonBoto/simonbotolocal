## Summary
Critique of cross-sectional study (n=178 elderly Tehran health centers, 2-stage cluster sampling prorated by region/budget): sampling bias (health center attendees != general pop), small n limits power, gender imbalance (51M/127F), broad age 60-83, arbitrary samples/center, self-report bias (lifestyle/demo), no confounder control, cross-sec no causality.

## Key Points
- Cluster from 25 centers (5 regions): unrep, budget/time compromise.
- Questionnaire: demo/lifestyle/med hist/diet self-report.
- Limits: validity/reliability/generalizability.
