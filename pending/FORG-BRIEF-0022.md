## Summary
Detailed chemical pathway: Ascorbic acid (C6H8O6) oxidizes to dehydroascorbic acid (DHA, C6H6O6) via oxygen/metal ions/enzymes, then further to oxalate (C2O4^2-) with loss of 4 carbons (as CO2). Not all ascorbic converts; depends on oxidative stress, enzymes, diet. High intake may interfere with oxalate urine tests (false positives). Other DHA metabolites: 2,3-diketogulonic acid, ascorbate-2-sulfate.

## Key Points
- Step 1: Ascorbic -> DHA (loss of 2H, enediol -> diketone).
- Step 2: DHA -> oxalate (cleavage, 4C lost as CO2/H2O).
- 1 DHA yields 1 oxalate molecule.
- Factors: ROS, ascorbate oxidase, individual variability.
- Formulas: Oxalate dicarboxylic ion; DHA ring w/ double bond.
