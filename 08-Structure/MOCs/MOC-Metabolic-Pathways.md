# MOC: Iron Metabolism

Iron is essential for oxygen transport, energy production, and methylation. **If iron is low, nothing works.** But inflammation creates "functional iron deficiency" — ferritin high, iron unavailable.

---

## 🎯 Core Principle

> "Iron status must be interpreted in context of inflammation. Ferritin is both iron storage AND acute phase reactant. Low iron breaks heme synthesis, cytochromes, and methylation."

## 🩺 Clinical Protocols

### Assessment Sequence
1. CBC (hemoglobin, MCV, MCH)
2. Ferritin (storage iron + inflammation)
3. Serum iron + TIBC (transport)
4. CRP or ESR (inflammation context)
5. If complex: Soluble transferrin receptor (sTfR)

### Intervention
- **True deficiency:** Iron bisglycinate (better tolerated), with Vitamin C
- **Anemia of chronic disease:** Treat inflammation first, consider IV iron if severe
- **Overload:** Phlebotomy, avoid supplementation

---

## 🔬 PhD-METHAP Relevance

**Biomarker inclusion:**
- Ferritin → Iron status + inflammation composite
- Soluble transferrin receptor → True iron deficiency (inflammation-independent)

**Hypothesis:**
- Iron deficiency predicts poor metabolic response
- Inflammation-driven functional iron deficiency common in IR
