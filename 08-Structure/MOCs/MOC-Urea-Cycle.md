# MOC — Urea Cycle

> Nitrogen disposal, ammonia detoxification, and clinical disorders

---

## Cycle Overview
**Purpose:** Convert toxic ammonia to excretable urea

**Location:** Liver (primarily)

**Steps:**
1. Carbamoyl phosphate synthetase I (CPS I)
2. Ornithine transcarbamylase (OTC)
3. Argininosuccinate synthetase (ASS)
4. Argininosuccinase (ASL)
5. Arginase

## Nitrogen Sources
- **First nitrogen:** Ammonia (from glutamate)
- **Second nitrogen:** Aspartate

## Key Connections
- [[LDL_Cholesterol_Structure_Function_Risk_Z-001-0503]] Glutamate-Arginine-Urea Cycle
- [[Amino_Acids_Oxaloacetate_Aspartate_Z-001-0493]] Aspartate Role
- [[Lipoproteins_Transport_System_Z-001-0502]] Glutamine-Ammonia Cycle

## Regulation
- N-acetylglutamate (NAG) activates CPS I
- Glutamate provides NAG carbon skeleton
- Dietary protein intake regulates expression

## Clinical: Urea Cycle Defects
- CPS I deficiency
- OTC deficiency (most common, X-linked)
- ASS deficiency
- ASL deficiency
- Arginase deficiency

### Presentation
- Hyperammonemia
- Encephalopathy
- Vomiting, lethargy, seizures
- Neonatal onset (severe) or later (milder)

### Treatment
- Low-protein diet
- Sodium phenylbutyrate
- L-citrulline or L-arginine supplementation
- Liver transplant (curative)
