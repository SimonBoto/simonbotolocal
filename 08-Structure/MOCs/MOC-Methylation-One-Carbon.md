# MOC: Methylation & One-Carbon Metabolism

The methylation cycle is the body's methyl group (CH3) supply system. It powers DNA repair, neurotransmitter synthesis, detoxification, and cardiovascular health. **If methylation fails, nothing works.**

---

## 🎯 Core Principle

> "Methylation is the metabolic backbone. Iron deficiency or MTHFR variants break the cycle → elevated homocysteine → inflammation → insulin resistance → disease."

## 🩺 Functional B12 Deficiency

**The Problem:**
- Serum B12 can be normal (200-900 pg/mL) but tissue deficiency exists
- MMA (methylmalonic acid) >271 nmol/L = functional deficiency
- Homocysteine >10 μmol/L = impaired methylation

## 🩺 Clinical Protocols

### Assessment
| Marker | Target | Interpretation |
|--------|--------|----------------|
| Homocysteine | <10 μmol/L | Composite methylation status |
| Serum B12 | >600 pg/mL | Minimum (functional may still be low) |
| MMA | <271 nmol/L | Tissue B12 status |
| 5-Methylfolate | >10 ng/mL | Active folate (MTHFR bypass) |

### Intervention
- **MTHFR C677T homozygous:** Methylfolate (not folic acid)
- **Functional B12 deficiency:** Methylcobalamin + adenosylcobalamin
- **High homocysteine:** B6 (P5P) + B12 + folate + TMG
- **Iron deficiency:** Correct first (cofactor for methionine synthase)
