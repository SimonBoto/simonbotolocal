### Target Ratios by Condition
| Population | Target Omega-6:3 | Rationale |
|------------|------------------|-----------|
| **General health** | <5:1 | Balanced inflammation |
| **Metabolic syndrome** | <4:1 | Reduce inflammatory load |
| **Autoimmune disease** | <3:1 | Anti-inflammatory protocol |
| **Cardiovascular disease** | <4:1 | AHA/ISSFAL guidance |

---

## 🔬 Mechanisms

### The Biochemical Pathway
1. **Linoleic acid (omega-6)** → Arachidonic acid
2. **Arachidonic acid** → Pro-inflammatory eicosanoids
   - Prostaglandins (PGE₂)
   - Leukotrienes (LTB₄)
   - Thromboxanes (TXA₂)
3. **Result:** Pro-inflammatory state when omega-6 dominates

### Vs. Omega-3 Pathway
- **EPA/DHA** → Anti-inflammatory eicosanoids (PGE₃, LTB₅)
- **Competes** with arachidonic acid for enzymes
- **Modulates** inflammatory response

---

## 🍽️ Food Sources

### High Omega-6 (Limit/Avoid)
- Corn-fed chicken meat (35-40% omega-6)
- Conventional eggs (25-30% omega-6)
- Vegetable oils (corn, soybean, sunflower)
- Processed foods

### Balanced/Good Omega-3
- Pasture-raised poultry
- Omega-3 enriched eggs
- Fatty fish (salmon, sardines, mackerel)
- Flaxseed, chia, walnuts

### Optimal Omega-3 Sources
- Wild-caught fatty fish
- Fish oil supplements
- Algae oil (vegan option)

## 💡 Clinical Applications

### Patient Counseling Points
1. **Quality matters:** Feed determines poultry fatty acid profile
2. **Eggs are vehicles:** Hens' diet directly affects egg yolk composition
3. **Ratio over absolute:** Balance omega-6 with omega-3 intake
4. **Upgrade priority:** Frequent poultry consumers benefit most from quality upgrade

### METHAP Protocol Integration
- **Baseline assessment:** Current omega-6:3 intake estimation
- **Intervention:** Upgrade poultry sources, add fatty fish
- **Monitoring:** Consider red blood cell omega-3 index
- **Target:** <5:1 omega-6:3 ratio in overall diet
