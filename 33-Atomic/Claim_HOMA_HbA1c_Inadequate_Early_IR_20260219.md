# HOMA-IR and HbA1c Are Inadequate for Early IR Detection

**Core Claim:** Existing diagnostic tools (HOMA-IR, HbA1c) often fail to provide early and precise detection of insulin resistance.

**Evidence:**
- HOMA-IR: Static measure, misses dynamic metabolic changes
- HbA1c: 3-month average, late indicator of dysfunction
- Both normal in early IR stages

**Mechanism of Failure:**
HOMA-IR = fasting glucose × fasting insulin / constant
- Compensatory hyperinsulinemia maintains normal glucose
- β-cell dysfunction progresses silently
- Only detectable when compensation fails

**Implication:**
Need biomarkers that detect mitochondrial dysfunction, lipotoxicity, and metabolic pathway shifts BEFORE glucose abnormalities.

**Quote:**
> "Existing diagnostic tools, such as the Homeostatic Model Assessment for Insulin Resistance (HOMA-IR) and glycated hemoglobin (HbA1c), often fail to provide early and precise detection"
