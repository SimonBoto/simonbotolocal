# UoC PhD Intervention — Diet and Supplementation Protocol

## Overview

Secondary objective: Assess impact of dietary interventions on insulin resistance biomarkers.

## Intervention Components

### 1. Mediterranean Diet Optimization

**Rationale:** Mediterranean diet associated with improved insulin sensitivity, reduced inflammation, and lower diabetes risk.

**Key Elements (Implied):**
- High olive oil intake (monounsaturated fats)
- Increased vegetable and fruit consumption
- Whole grains over refined carbohydrates
- Fish and legumes as primary protein sources
- Limited processed foods and added sugars

**Monitoring:** Dietary intake questionnaires, adherence scoring

### 2. Omega-3 Supplementation

**Rationale:**
- EPA/DHA improve insulin receptor signaling
- Anti-inflammatory effects (resolvins, protectins)
- Reduce hepatic lipogenesis

**Protocol (To Be Specified):**
- Dose: ___ mg EPA+DHA daily (typically 2-4g for metabolic effects)
- Duration: ___ weeks (assumed 12 based on standard protocols)
- Form: Fish oil or algae oil capsules

### 3. Vitamin D Supplementation

**Rationale:**
- Vitamin D deficiency associated with IR and diabetes risk
- Improves insulin receptor expression
- Anti-inflammatory immunomodulation

**Protocol (To Be Specified):**
- Target level: 25(OH)D >30 ng/mL (optimal 40-60)
- Dosing strategy: Daily vs. weekly bolus
- Monitoring: 25(OH)D at baseline, mid-study, endpoint

**Note:** Our [[20260218_Hashimoto_VitD_Protocol]] suggests 50,000 IU/week × 12 weeks for autoimmune applications. IR may require different dosing.

### 4. Magnesium Supplementation

**Rationale:**
- Magnesium essential for glucose metabolism (enzyme cofactor)
- Hypomagnesemia common in IR and diabetes
- Improves insulin receptor tyrosine kinase activity

**Protocol (To Be Specified):**
- Form: Magnesium glycinate, citrate, or oxide
- Dose: ___ mg elemental magnesium daily (typically 200-400mg)
- Monitoring: Serum magnesium (poor indicator; consider RBC magnesium)

## Combined Approach

**Rationale for Multi-Component:**
- Synergistic effects on insulin signaling
- Address multiple pathways simultaneously
- Mimics real-world lifestyle interventions

**Potential Interactions:**
- Vitamin D improves magnesium absorption
- Omega-3 may increase vitamin D bioavailability
- Mediterranean diet provides natural sources of all three

## Outcome Measures

### Primary (Metabolomic)
- Changes in urine organic acid profile
- Changes in plasma free fatty acid composition
- Shift in metabolic pathway activity (PCA components)

### Secondary (Clinical)
- HOMA-IR change
- Fasting glucose/insulin
- HbA1c (if applicable)
- Lipid profile
- Inflammatory markers (hs-CRP, IL-6)
