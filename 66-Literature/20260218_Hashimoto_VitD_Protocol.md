# High-Dose Vitamin D Protocol for Hashimoto's Thyroiditis

## Background

Hashimoto's thyroiditis (HT) is the most common autoimmune thyroid disease. Vitamin D deficiency is prevalent in HT patients (60-80%) and correlates with:
- Higher TPO-Ab and TG-Ab titers
- More severe hypothyroidism
- Greater thyroid volume

## Mechanism: VitD in Autoimmunity

**Immunomodulation**:
- ↓ Pro-inflammatory cytokines (IL-6, TNF-α, IL-17)
- ↑ Anti-inflammatory IL-10
- ↑ Treg cell function
- ↓ Th17 differentiation

**Thyroid-Specific**:
- VitD receptors expressed on thyroid cells
- Calcitriol inhibits pro-inflammatory cytokine production in thyrocytes

## Protocol Summary

| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Target Population** | Hashimoto's with VitD <30 ng/mL |
| **Dosing** | 50,000-60,000 IU weekly × 8-12 weeks |
| **Maintenance** | 2,000-4,000 IU daily |
| **Target Level** | 40-60 ng/mL (100-150 nmol/L) |
| **Co-factors** | Mg, K2, Omega-3 |

## Evidence Base

**Key Studies**:
1. Chaudhary et al. (2023): High-dose VitD reduced TPO-Ab by 46% at 12 weeks
2. Mazokopakis et al. (2015): 50,000 IU/week improved TSH and antibody levels
3. Wang et al. (2020): Dose-response relationship confirmed
