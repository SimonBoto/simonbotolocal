Abstract: Telomeres, repetitive DNA sequences at the ends of chromosomes, play a crucial role in maintaining genomic stability and are linked to aging and various diseases. Telomere length, regulated by telomerase enzyme activity, declines over time. Research focuses on compounds that may stimulate telomerase activity to counteract this decline.



Background/Objectives: This study investigates natural compounds' effects on telomerase activity in human blood cells. Compounds tested included cycloastragenol, Centella asiatica, vitamins D3 and C, zinc, Goji berry, curcumin, ginseng, Ganoderma, and FOTI.  



Methods:Telomerase activity was quantified with the use of a commercial PCRELISA kit. 



Results: Certain compound combinations demonstrated increased telomerase activity,    such as Formulation 7D and 2B, with a 5-fold change and a 4-fold change, respectively, compared to their controls (UNC1 and UNC2, respectively). 



Conclusions: The most potent blends significantly increased telomerase activity, suggesting potential anti-aging properties.
