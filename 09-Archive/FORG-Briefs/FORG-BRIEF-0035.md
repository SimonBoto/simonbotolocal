MSM, or Methylsulfonylmethane, is an organic sulfur-containing compound with the chemical formula (CH)SO. It's known for its presence in green plants, animals, and humans. Here's a detailed biochemistry-focused explanation:

**Chemical Structure and Properties**: MSM consists of two methyl groups attached to a sulfone group. Its sulfur content, around 34% by weight, is a significant feature as sulfur is a crucial element in many biochemical processes.

**Biosynthesis and Natural Sources**: In nature, MSM is derived from dimethyl sulfide (DMS), a volatile organic sulfur compound produced by plankton in the ocean. DMS is oxidized in the atmosphere to dimethyl sulfoxide (DMSO) and subsequently to MSM, which finds its way into the terrestrial and marine food chains.

**Biological Role and Mechanisms**: MSM's role in the body isn't fully elucidated, but it's believed to be involved in sulfur metabolism. Sulfur is integral for synthesizing amino acids like cysteine and methionine, and in forming disulfide bonds which are vital for the structural integrity of connective tissues and proteins. MSM might provide a source of sulfur for these essential processes.

**Therapeutic Potential and Uses**: Clinically, MSM is used as a dietary supplement, often claimed to help with joint pain, inflammation, and allergies. Its mechanism of action in these uses is not completely understood, but it's proposed to have anti-inflammatory, antioxidative, and analgesic properties.

**Safety and Metabolism**: Generally considered safe when used as a dietary supplement, MSM is metabolized in the body to sulfur-containing compounds. The excess is excreted in urine. Its toxicity appears to be relatively low, but as with all supplements, it should be used under appropriate clinical guidance, especially in special populations.

**Research and Clinical Trials**: Studies on MSM have looked at its effects on osteoarthritis, exercise recovery, and allergic reactions, among others. However, the research is mixed, and more rigorous clinical trials are needed to establish its efficacy and the mechanisms by which it works.
