# Inflammatory Bowel Disease — Crohn's vs Ulcerative Colitis Pathophysiology

## Source
- **ChatGPT conversation:** [[CGPT_0209_Gastroenterology_Registrar_Assessment_CGPT-0209]] (Question 1)
- **Date:** November 18, 2025
- **Scope:** IBD mechanisms, Crohn's vs UC differences

---

## Pathophysiology Overview

### Common Mechanisms

**Dysregulated Immune Response:**
- Inappropriate activation against gut microbiota
- Defective intestinal barrier
- Genetic susceptibility + environmental triggers
- Chronic relapsing inflammation

**Key Components:**
1. Genetic predisposition (NOD2/CARD15, IL23R, etc.)
2. Gut microbiome alterations
3. Mucosal barrier defects
4. Aberrant immune activation

### Crohn's Disease

**Pathological Features:**
- **Transmural inflammation** (full thickness)
- **Skip lesions** (discontinuous)
- **Any GI segment** (ileum most common)
- **Granulomas** (non-caseating, 40-60%)
- **Fistulas and strictures**

**Immune Profile:**
- Th1 and Th17 predominant
- IFN-γ, TNF-α, IL-12, IL-23 elevated
- IL-17, IL-22 increased

**Risk Factors:**
- Smoking (increases risk, worsens disease)
- NSAIDs
- Western diet
- Family history

### Ulcerative Colitis

**Pathological Features:**
- **Mucosal inflammation only** (superficial)
- **Continuous involvement** (no skip lesions)
- **Colon only** (rectum always involved)
- **No granulomas**
- **Crypt abscesses**

**Immune Profile:**
- Th2 predominant
- IL-5, IL-13 elevated
- Humoral immunity (autoantibodies)
- p-ANCA positive in 60-70%

**Risk Factors:**
- Smoking (protective for UC, but don't recommend!)
- Appendectomy (protective)
- Urban living
- Family history

## Comparative Features

| Feature | Crohn's Disease | Ulcerative Colitis |
|---------|-----------------|-------------------|
| **Distribution** | Any GI segment | Colon only |
| **Pattern** | Skip lesions, focal | Continuous, diffuse |
| **Depth** | Transmural | Mucosal only |
| **Rectal involvement** | Variable | Universal (100%) |
| **Granulomas** | Present (40-60%) | Absent |
| **Fistulas/strictures** | Common | Rare |
| **Perianal disease** | Common (30%) | Rare |
| **Cancer risk** | Increased (small bowel, colon) | Increased (colon) |
| **p-ANCA** | 10-20% | 60-70% |
| **ASCA** | 50-60% | 10-15% |

## Serological Markers

### p-ANCA (Perinuclear Anti-Neutrophil Cytoplasmic Antibody)

**UC Association:**
- 60-70% positive in UC
- 10-20% positive in Crohn's
- Suggests UC-like Crohn's (colonic)

### ASCA (Anti-Saccharomyces cerevisiae Antibody)

**Crohn's Association:**
- 50-60% positive in Crohn's
- 10-15% positive in UC
- Associated with small bowel disease, strictures

### Clinical Utility

**Diagnostic:**
- Not definitive alone
- Supportive with clinical picture
- p-ANCA+/ASCA- suggests UC
- p-ANCA-/ASCA+ suggests Crohn's
- Both negative or both positive less specific

## Extraintestinal Manifestations

### Common to Both

**Musculoskeletal:**
- Peripheral arthritis (type 1 and 2)
- Ankylosing spondylitis
- Sacroiliitis

**Dermatologic:**
- Erythema nodosum
- Pyoderma gangrenosum

**Ocular:**
- Uveitis
- Episcleritis

**Hepatobiliary:**
- Primary sclerosing cholangitis (more common in UC)
- Fatty liver

**Differentiating:**
- Primary sclerosing cholangitis: Stronger UC association
- Ankylosing spondylitis: More Crohn's

## Treatment Approach

### Crohn's Disease

**Targets:**
- Mucosal healing (deeper healing needed)
- Transmural healing (imaging)
- Prevent strictures/fistulas
- Surgery often needed (70% lifetime)

**Biologics:**
- Anti-TNF (infliximab, adalimumab)
- Anti-integrin (vedolizumab)
- Anti-IL12/23 (ustekinumab)
- Anti-IL23 (risankizumab)

### Ulcerative Colitis

**Targets:**
- Mucosal healing (endoscopic)
- Prevent colectomy
- Rectal therapy important
- Surgery curative (proctocolectomy)

**Biologics:**
- Anti-TNF
- Anti-integrin
- JAK inhibitors (tofacitinib)
- S1P modulators (ozanimod)

## Summary

**Key Distinctions:**
1. Crohn's = transmural, any location, skip lesions, granulomas
2. UC = mucosal only, colon only, continuous, no granulomas
3. Smoking opposite effects (CD: harmful, UC: protective)
4. Serology: ASCA-CD, p-ANCA-UC
5. Treatment differs: CD needs deeper healing, surgery different
