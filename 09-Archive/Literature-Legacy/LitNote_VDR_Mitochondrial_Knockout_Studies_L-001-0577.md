## Part 1: Vitamin D Receptor (VDR) Knockout

### Model Overview

**Gene:** VDR (Vitamin D Receptor)
**Function:** Nuclear receptor for 1,25-dihydroxyvitamin D3
**Role:** Regulates gene expression for calcium homeostasis, immune function, cell proliferation

### Phenotypic Findings

| System | Phenotype | Mechanism |
|--------|-----------|-----------|
| **Calcium/Bone** | Hypocalcemia, secondary hyperparathyroidism, rickets, osteomalacia | Impaired calcium absorption |
| **Immune** | Dysregulated immune response, T-cell dysfunction, autoimmune susceptibility | Loss of immunomodulatory signaling |
| **Cancer** | Increased tumor susceptibility (colon) | Loss of antiproliferative/apoptotic signaling |
| **Metabolic** | Insulin resistance, obesity, altered lipid metabolism | Disrupted metabolic regulation |
| **Cardiovascular** | Hypertension, impaired cardiac function | Vascular and cardiac effects |
| **Development** | Skin/hair abnormalities, impaired development | Tissue-specific VDR functions |

### Key Studies

**Li YC et al. (1997):** Seminal VDR knockout mouse model
**Bouillon R et al. (2008):** Comprehensive VDR review
**Feldman D et al. (2014):** VDR in cancer and immune regulation


## Part 2: Mitochondrial Gene Knockouts

### POLG (Mitochondrial DNA Polymerase)

**Function:** mtDNA replication and repair
**Knockout:** Embryonic lethal
**Heterozygous:** Mitochondrial dysfunction, neurodegeneration, muscle weakness
**Human disease:** POLG-related disorders, Alpers syndrome

### TFAM (Mitochondrial Transcription Factor A)

**Function:** mtDNA transcription and maintenance
**Knockout:** Rapid mtDNA depletion, respiratory chain deficiency
**Cardiac-specific:** Dilated cardiomyopathy, heart failure
**Tissue-specific models:** Demonstrate organ-specific vulnerabilities

### Mitochondrial Dynamics Genes

**Mitofusin 1/2 (Mfn1, Mfn2):**
- Fusion proteins
- Knockout: Fragmented mitochondria, neurodegeneration
- Mfn2: Charcot-Marie-Tooth disease type 2A

**Drp1 (Dynamin-Related Protein 1):**
- Fission protein
- Knockout: Embryonic lethal
- Tissue-specific: Enlarged dysfunctional mitochondria, cerebellar neurodegeneration

### Respiratory Chain Complexes

**NDUFS4 (Complex I):**
- Knockout: Severe neurodegeneration, ataxia, premature death
- Model for Leigh syndrome

**COX10 (Complex IV):**
- Assembly factor
- Knockout: Energy failure, ROS increase, neurodegeneration

### Antioxidant Defenses

**SOD2 (Mitochondrial Superoxide Dismutase):**
- Key antioxidant enzyme
- Knockout: Neonatal lethality from oxidative stress
- Tissue-specific models: Cancer, neurodegeneration, cardiovascular disease


## Part 3: Mitochondrial DNA in Cancer

### mtDNA Mutations in Cancer

**Frequency:** Common in multiple cancer types (breast, lung, colorectal, ovarian)
**Locations:** D-loop (replication control), respiratory chain genes
**Effects:** Respiratory defects, ROS increase, metabolic reprogramming

### mtDNA Copy Number Alterations

**Dual Role:**
- Some cancers: Increased copy number
- Others: Decreased copy number
- Context-dependent effects on tumorigenesis

### Metastasis Connection

**mtDNA Mutations Enhance Metastasis:**
- Altered cellular signaling
- Increased ROS
- Metabolic flexibility
- Mitochondrial transfer from stroma

### Therapeutic Targeting

**Strategies:**
- Metformin (complex I inhibition)
- Dichloroacetate (PDK inhibition)
- Mitochondrial poisons
- Exploiting oxidative stress

### Biomarker Potential

**Circulating mtDNA:**
- Released from tumor cells
- Reflects tumor burden
- Non-invasive detection
- Prognostic significance


## Integration with Clinical Metabolomics

**Your Clinical Context:**
- VDR polymorphisms affect vitamin D response
- Mitochondrial dysfunction underlies metabolic disease
- mtDNA damage measurable in clinical practice
- Therapeutic implications for personalized medicine
