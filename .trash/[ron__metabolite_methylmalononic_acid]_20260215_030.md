Definition:
Methylmalonic acid (MMA) is a metabolite that rises when the B12-dependent conversion of methylmalonyl-CoA to succinyl-CoA is functionally limited.

Clinical meaning:
- Often a more B12-specific functional marker than homocysteine.
- Links B12 status to mitochondrial throughput (succinyl-CoA feeds energy metabolism).

Common confusion:
- “MMA is only about kidneys.” Renal function can affect MMA interpretation, but the primary clinical use is B12 functional status.

Use pattern:
- Symptoms/risk + ambiguous serum B12 → MMA is a strong clarifier.
- Pair with [[Homocysteine]] to map whether the issue is “B12-only” or “network.”
