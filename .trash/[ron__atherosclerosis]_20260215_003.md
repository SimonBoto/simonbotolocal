Definition:
Atherosclerosis is a chronic arterial wall disease characterized by plaque formation and vascular remodeling, driven by endothelial dysfunction, inflammation, lipid/lipoprotein retention and modification, and immune activation.

Clinical meaning:
- Atherosclerosis behaves like an inflammatory wound-healing process gone wrong; lipids participate, but the inflammatory/oxidative environment often governs progression and instability.
- Risk evaluation should include metabolic state and inflammatory drivers, not only cholesterol values.

Common confusion:
- “Atherosclerosis is just cholesterol sludge clogging pipes.” The “hydraulic” model is incomplete; biology is messier and inflammatory.
- “Normal LDL-C means no risk.” Many events occur at “acceptable” LDL-C when other drivers are present.
