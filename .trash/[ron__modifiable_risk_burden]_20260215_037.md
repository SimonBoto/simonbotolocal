**Definition**  
The cumulative load of biological and lifestyle factors that can be altered and that influence disease expression and progression.

**Clinical meaning**
Includes metabolic, nutritional, inflammatory, and behavioral contributors that shape disease trajectory.

**Typical components**
- Insulin resistance
- Micronutrient deficiencies
- Gut dysbiosis
- Sedentary behavior
- Smoking
- Chronic inflammatory exposure

**Why it matters**
Disease severity and progression correlate more strongly with total modifiable burden than with diagnosis alone.

**Common confusion**
- Mistaken for “alternative medicine”
- Misunderstood as denying disease reality

**Clinical framing**
Modifying risk burden does not replace treatment; it alters trajectory.
