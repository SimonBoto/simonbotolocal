Definition:
Homocysteine is an intermediate amino-acid metabolite whose level reflects flux through remethylation (back to methionine) and transsulfuration pathways.

Clinical meaning:
- Elevated homocysteine is a *signal* of disrupted one-carbon pathway throughput, often involving B12/folate/B6 status and other stressors.
- Useful as a functional marker when interpreting methylation-related symptoms or risk contexts.

Common confusion:
- “Homocysteine = heart disease only.” It also reflects methylation network strain.
- “High homocysteine means B12 deficiency.” It may be folate/B6 or mixed; pair with [[Metabolite_Methylmalononic_Acid] when B12 question matters.

Interpretation anchor:
- If homocysteine is high and MMA is high → stronger B12 signal.
- If homocysteine is high and MMA is normal → look harder at folate/B6 and other drivers.
