Definition:
Stress is the body’s hormonal response to a factor perceived as a threat to survival (real or apparent).

Clinical meaning:
- The stress response can be triggered by perception and interpretation, not only by objective danger.
- Chronic perceived threat can sustain stress physiology and degrade sleep, mood, and resilience.

Common confusion:
- “Stress is only emotional.” (False: can be biological + emotional.)
- “If it’s perception, it’s imaginary.” (False: perception triggers real hormones.)
- “You can just decide not to be stressed.” (Usually false; inputs and habits matter.)
