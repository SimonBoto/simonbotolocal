# Empty calories

## Definition (simple)
“Empty calories” = foods that provide substantial energy (calories) with low amounts of essential nutrients (especially micronutrients and high-quality protein).

## Clinical meaning
- Raises the probability of **simultaneous overfeeding and undernourishment** (high calories, low cofactors).
- Pushes metabolism toward dysregulation by increasing energy intake without supplying the micronutrients required for repair, detox/biotransformation, mitochondrial throughput, and endocrine balance.

## Common confusion
- “Calories are empty” is not a moral label; it’s a **nutrient density** label.
- A food can be calorie-dense and still not “empty” if it carries micronutrients/protein (context dependent).
- The key issue is the **calorie-to-micronutrient ratio** and the person’s current metabolic context.
