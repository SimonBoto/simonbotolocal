Definition:
LDL cholesterol (LDL-C) is the cholesterol carried within low-density lipoproteins. It is a commonly used surrogate for atherogenic lipoprotein burden.

Clinical meaning:
- LDL-C is a useful risk proxy but imperfect: atherogenicity depends on particle number, particle characteristics, inflammation/oxidation, endothelial state, and metabolic context.
- Patients can have ASCVD events with “near-target” LDL-C, especially if HDL is low and metabolic/inflammatory drivers persist.

Common confusion:
- “LDL-C alone determines risk.” Risk is multi-causal; context and additional markers can matter.
- “Lowering LDL-C always equals fixing cause.” LDL lowering can reduce risk, but may not correct the upstream pathology producing atherogenesis.
