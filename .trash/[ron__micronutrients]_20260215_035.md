Definition:
Micronutrients are nutrients required in small amounts that enable normal physiology (mainly as enzyme cofactors, signaling molecules, and structural helpers). They include [[Dictionary_Vitamins_20260215_008] and [[Dictionary_Vitamins_20260215_008].

Clinical meaning:
- Micronutrients rarely provide calories, but they often decide whether pathways “run” or “stall.”
- Deficiency can masquerade as disease (fatigue, low resilience, immune dysregulation, impaired recovery).
- Many metabolomics “abnormalities” are downstream reflections of missing cofactors.

Common confusion:
- “Micronutrients = only vitamins.” Minerals and trace elements are equally foundational.
- Thresholds like “<100 mg” vary by source; use functional meaning clinically.
- “Not produced endogenously” is broadly true for vitamins/minerals, with important exceptions (e.g., vitamin D is produced in skin; some vitamins can be produced by gut microbes in variable amounts).
