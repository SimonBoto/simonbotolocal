Definition:
The gut–brain axis is the bidirectional communication network between the gut (microbiome, mucosa, enteric nervous system) and the central nervous system via neural, immune, endocrine, and metabolic signaling.

Clinical meaning:
- Explains why gut perturbations can manifest as mood, fatigue, sleep, and cognition changes.
- Stress and autonomic tone can change motility, secretion, permeability, and microbial ecology.

Common confusion:
- “It’s just serotonin.” Neurotransmitters are part of it, but immune and metabolic signals are major channels.
- “Psychological stress is the only driver.” Often a trigger on top of metabolic/barrier load.
