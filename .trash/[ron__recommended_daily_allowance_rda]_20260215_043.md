Definition:
Recommended_Daily_Allowance (RDA) is a population-level intake estimate designed to prevent deficiency in most healthy people.

Clinical meaning:
- RDA is best treated as a minimum “anti-deficiency” benchmark, not a performance/optimization target.
- In chronic disease, stress, inflammation, pregnancy, malabsorption, or high toxic load, functional requirements can differ from population averages.
- For clinic communication: RDA answers “how little prevents overt deficiency,” not “how much optimizes physiology.”

Common confusion:
- "RDA is the maximum safe dose." Incorrect.
- "If I exceed RDA I will harm liver/kidneys." Not automatically—depends on compound, dose, duration, and fat-solubility.
- "RDA is personalized." It is not; it is a statistical construct.
