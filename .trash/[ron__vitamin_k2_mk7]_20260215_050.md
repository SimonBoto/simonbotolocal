## Definition (simple)
**Vitamin K2 (MK-7)** is a fat-soluble vitamin form that helps activate proteins involved in calcium placement and tissue maintenance.

## Clinical meaning (how we use it)
In our Vitamin D doctrine, MK-7 is a **calcium-handling co-factor**:
- baseline support when giving D3
- escalated when D3 dosing is high or calcification burden exists

## Common confusion
- K2 is not a “vitamin D antidote.” It’s **part of the steering wheel**, not the brake.
- We do **not** tag MK-7 as a supplement tag; we link it.
