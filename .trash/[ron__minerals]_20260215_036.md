Definition:
Minerals are inorganic micronutrients (elements) required in small amounts for structure, signaling, and enzyme function (e.g., magnesium, zinc, selenium, iodine).

Clinical meaning:
- Mineral status shapes energy production, thyroid function, immunity, neurotransmission, and detox/biotransformation capacity.
- Deficiency can be intake-related, absorption-related (gut), or demand-related (inflammation/stress).

Common confusion:
- “Blood levels always reflect tissue status.” Often false (compartmentalization).
- “All forms are equal.” Bioavailability and tolerability vary by salt/chelate form.
