Definition:
Cytochrome_P450 (CYP450) is a large family of enzymes (primarily hepatic) involved in metabolizing drugs, hormones, and other xenobiotics.

Clinical meaning:
- CYP450 activity is a major node for biotransformation: converting lipophilic compounds into more excretable forms.
- Functional capacity depends on substrate load, genetics, inflammation status, and cofactor availability (micronutrients).
- Explains a key clinic narrative: micronutrients can support detox/elimination capacity rather than “overload the liver.”

Common confusion:
- "Detox enzymes are only relevant for drug users." False; they process endogenous and exogenous compounds.
- "Taking vitamins forces the liver to work harder." Mechanistically, deficiency may impair hepatic processing more than repletion.
