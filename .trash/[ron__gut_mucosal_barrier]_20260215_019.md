Definition:
The gut mucosal barrier is the multi-layer defense system (mucus, epithelial cells, tight junctions, immune layer, microbiota) separating the intestinal lumen from the bloodstream.

Clinical meaning:
- Primary gatekeeper for antigen exposure; barrier failure increases immune activation.
- Relevant to systemic inflammation, food sensitivity patterns, and autoimmune flare propensity.

Common confusion:
- “Barrier = just mucus.” It’s structural + immune + microbial.
- “Leaky gut is a diagnosis.” Better treated as a mechanism with measurable proxies and context.
