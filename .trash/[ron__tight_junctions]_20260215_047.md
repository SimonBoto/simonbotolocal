Definition:
Tight junctions are protein complexes between intestinal epithelial cells that regulate paracellular permeability (what passes “between” cells).

Clinical meaning:
- Tight junction disruption increases passage of antigens/microbial products, amplifying immune signaling.
- Often discussed in relation to intestinal permeability and inflammatory cascades.

Common confusion:
- “Tight junctions open/close like doors.” Reality is graded regulation via signaling, inflammation, and barrier repair dynamics.
- “One marker proves leaky gut.” Prefer multi-signal context (symptoms + labs + triggers + response).
