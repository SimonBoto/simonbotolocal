Definition: The study of how health-related states/events are distributed in populations and what determines them.

Clinical meaning: Epidemiology is the main tool that lets EBM estimate risk, prognosis, and treatment effects **on average** (e.g., relative risk, absolute risk, NNT).

Common confusion:
- Epidemiology describes **group patterns**, not a person’s full causal story.
- A strong population signal can still be weakly predictive for a specific individual if heterogeneity is high.
