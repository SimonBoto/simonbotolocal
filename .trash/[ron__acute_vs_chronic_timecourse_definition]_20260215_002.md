Definition (simple):
- Acute: a health problem with a short time course (often defined as < 3 months).
- Chronic: a health problem with a prolonged time course (often defined as > 3 months).

Clinical meaning:
- Acute problems often benefit from rapid, structural stabilization and symptom control.
- Chronic problems usually require identifying and correcting sustaining drivers (metabolic, immune, repair capacity, exposures), while still managing symptoms.

Common confusion:
- “Severe symptoms = acute.” (Not necessarily; chronic disease can flare.)
- “Acute care solves chronic disease.” (Acute tools can stabilize, but rarely reverse chronic drivers alone.)
