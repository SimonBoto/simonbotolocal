Definition:
Protein is a macronutrient made of amino acids, used primarily as structural and functional material (tissues, enzymes, transporters), and secondarily as an energy source.

Clinical meaning:
- Key for maintenance/repair, immune proteins, detox/biotransformation enzymes, and neurotransmitter precursors.
- Adequate protein often stabilizes appetite and supports recovery (especially when mitochondrial function is stressed).

Common confusion:
- “Protein is just for muscles.” It’s for *everything* that’s built from amino acids.
- “More is always better.” Needs individualized context (renal impairment, appetite, goals).
