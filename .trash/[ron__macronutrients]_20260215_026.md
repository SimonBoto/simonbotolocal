Definition:
Macronutrients are nutrients required in relatively large amounts that provide energy and/or structural material for the body. In practice: carbohydrates, protein, and fat.

Clinical meaning:
- They determine the bulk of caloric intake and strongly influence satiety, glycemic load, and body composition.
- “Macros” set the metabolic context; micronutrients often determine whether pathways run smoothly within that context.

Common confusion:
- “Macros = only calories.” Not quite: protein is also structural; fats are hormonal/membrane substrates; carbs are context-dependent fuel.
- Threshold definitions (e.g., “>100 mg”) vary by source; use functional meaning rather than a rigid cutoff.
