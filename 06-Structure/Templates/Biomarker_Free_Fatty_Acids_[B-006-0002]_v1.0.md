# Free Fatty Acids Panel (RBC Membrane) - B-006-0002

## CRITICAL PRINCIPLE

> **"Free fatty acids are the ROOT. Lipids in the blood are the symptom. Fix the fatty acid composition of cell membranes, and metabolism follows."

**RBC membrane fatty acids reflect:**
- 3-4 months of dietary intake (long-term status)
- Cell membrane fluidity and function
- Receptor sensitivity (insulin, etc.)
- Inflammatory potential
- Hormone signaling

---

**Specimen:** RBC membrane (not plasma — reflects long-term status)
**Collection:** EDTA whole blood

## 2. SIMOS CLINICAL TARGETS

### CRITICAL RATIOS

#### Omega-6:Omega-3 Ratio
| Range | Interpretation | Action |
|-------|----------------|--------|
| **<4:1** | Excellent | Maintain |
| **4-7:1** | **ACCEPTABLE** | Monitor |
| **>7:1** | **HIGH** | Aggressive omega-3, reduce omega-6 |
| **>15:1** | Very high | Standard American diet |

**Simos Target: Omega-6:3 <7 (ideally <4)**

**Evolutionary context:** Hunter-gatherers ~1-2:1

#### AA:EPA Ratio (Arachidonic:EPA)
| Range | Interpretation | Action |
|-------|----------------|--------|
| **<3:1** | Excellent | Maintain |
| **3-5:1** | **ACCEPTABLE** | Increase EPA |
| **>5:1** | **HIGH** | Aggressive EPA supplementation |
| **>15:1** | Very high | High inflammation risk |

**Simos Target: AA:EPA <5 (ideally <3)**

**Clinical significance:** Direct measure of pro vs anti-inflammatory eicosanoid precursors

---

### INDIVIDUAL FATTY ACIDS

#### DGLA (Dihomo-γ-linolenic Acid) - μg/mL
| Range | Interpretation | Index |
|-------|----------------|-------|
| **<80** | **OPTIMAL** | **Insulin sensitivity good** |
| **80-120** | Elevated | Monitor insulin |
| **>120** | High | Insulin resistance likely |

**Simos Target: DGLA <80 (INSULIN INDEX)**

**Why it matters:**
- DGLA precursor to AA (pro-inflammatory)
- Elevated DGLA = impaired delta-5-desaturase = insulin resistance pattern
- **More sensitive than fasting glucose for early IR**

#### Arachidonic Acid (AA) - μg/mL
| Range | Interpretation | Index |
|-------|----------------|-------|
| **<550** | **OPTIMAL** | **Low inflammation** |
| **550-700** | Elevated | Monitor inflammation |
| **>700** | High | High inflammation risk |

**Simos Target: AA <550 (INFLAMMATION INDEX)**

**Sources:**
- Endogenous: DGLA conversion
- Exogenous: Meat, eggs (minimal compared to endogenous)

**Clinical:** High AA = substrate for pro-inflammatory eicosanoids (PGE2, LTB4)

#### Linoleic Acid (LA) - μg/mL
| Range | Interpretation |
|-------|----------------|
| **<2400** | **OPTIMAL** |
| **2400-3000** | Elevated |
| **>3000** | High (excess seed oil intake) |

**Simos Target: LA <2400**

**Why it matters:**
- Primary omega-6 in diet (seed oils)
- Bioaccumulates in adipose (2-year half-life)
- High LA = high substrate for pro-inflammatory pathway

#### GLA (γ-linolenic Acid) - μg/mL
| Range | Interpretation |
|-------|----------------|
| **15-60** | **OPTIMAL** |
| **<15** | Low (poor Δ6-desaturase) |
| **>60** | High |

**Simos Target: GLA 15-60**

**Clinical:** GLA is the first step in omega-6 conversion. Low GLA = impaired Δ6-desaturase (zinc deficiency, aging, diabetes)

#### ALA (α-linolenic Acid) - μg/mL
| Range | Interpretation |
|-------|----------------|
| **15-50** | **OPTIMAL** |
| **<15** | Low intake |
| **>50** | **EXCESS (don't want more)** |

**Simos Target: ALA 15-50 (DON'T WANT MORE)**

**Why not more:**
- ALA is omega-3 precursor
- Conversion to EPA/DHA is <5% in most people
- High ALA without conversion = useless
- Better to get EPA/DHA directly from fish

#### EPA (Eicosapentaenoic Acid) - μg/mL
| Range | Interpretation |
|-------|----------------|
| **<35** | Deficient |
| **35-100** | Low-normal |
| **100-250** | **OPTIMAL** |
| **>250** | **EXCELLENT (more is better)** |

**Simos Target: EPA 35-250 (MORE IS BETTER)**

**Clinical:**
- Anti-inflammatory eicosanoid precursor
- Competes with AA for enzymes
- Higher EPA = lower inflammation
- No upper limit mentioned (safe)

#### DHA (Docosahexaenoic Acid) - μg/mL
| Range | Interpretation |
|-------|----------------|
| **<90** | Deficient |
| **90-180** | Low-normal |
| **180-280** | **OPTIMAL** |
| **>280** | **EXCELLENT (more is better)** |

**Simos Target: DHA 90-280 (MORE IS BETTER)**

**Clinical:**
- Brain structure (30% of brain fat)
- Retina integrity
- Anti-inflammatory signaling
- No upper limit mentioned (safe)

#### Palmitoleic Acid (16:1) - μg/mL
| Range | Interpretation | Index |
|-------|----------------|-------|
| **<60** | **OPTIMAL** | **Low cortisol/stress** |
| **60-100** | Elevated | Monitor stress |
| **>100** | High | High cortisol/de novo lipogenesis |

**Simos Target: Palmitoleic <60 (CORTISOL INDEX)**

**Mechanism:**
- Palmitoleic = de novo lipogenesis marker
- High carbohydrate intake → palmitoleic synthesis
- Also elevated by cortisol (stress)
- **Independent of dietary intake**

#### Oleic Acid (18:1) - μg/mL
| Range | Interpretation |
|-------|----------------|
| **<1400** | Low |
| **>1400** | **OPTIMAL** |
| **>2000** | Excellent |

**Simos Target: Oleic >1400**

**Clinical:**
- Primary fat in olive oil
- Cardioprotective
- Mediterranean diet marker
- Higher = better

#### C15:0 (Pentadecanoic Acid) - μg/mL
| Range | Interpretation |
|-------|----------------|
| **<8** | Low |
| **>8** | **OPTIMAL** |

**Simos Target: C15 >8**

**Clinical:**
- Odd-chain saturated fatty acid
- Marker of dairy fat intake (butter, whole milk)
- Emerging marker of metabolic health
- Higher = better

## 4. INTERVENTIONS

### To Lower Omega-6:3 Ratio
| Intervention | Mechanism |
|--------------|-----------|
| **Eliminate seed oils** | ↓ LA intake |
| **Omega-3 2-4g EPA+DHA** | ↑ EPA, DHA |
| **Fatty fish 3x/week** | ↑ EPA, DHA |
| **Time** | 3-6 months for RBC turnover |

### To Lower DGLA (Insulin Index)
| Intervention | Mechanism |
|--------------|-----------|
| **Low-carb diet** | ↓ Insulin, ↓ DGLA synthesis |
| **Weight loss** | ↓ Insulin resistance |
| **Exercise** | ↑ Insulin sensitivity |
| **Omega-3** | Competitive inhibition |

### To Lower Palmitoleic (Cortisol Index)
| Intervention | Mechanism |
|--------------|-----------|
| **Low-carb** | ↓ De novo lipogenesis |
| **Stress management** | ↓ Cortisol |
| **Sleep** | ↓ Cortisol |
