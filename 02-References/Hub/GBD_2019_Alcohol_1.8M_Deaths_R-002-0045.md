## Key Findings

### Mortality (2019)
| Metric | Figure |
|--------|--------|
| **Total deaths** | 1.8 million |
| **Male deaths** | 2.07 million |
| **Female deaths** | 374,000 |
| **Global rank** | #9 risk factor |

### Disease Burden
| Condition | Attributable Deaths |
|-----------|---------------------|
| **Cirrhosis/liver disease** | ~400,000 |
| **Cancer** | ~250,000 |
| **Cardiovascular disease** | ~200,000 |
| **Injuries/accidents** | ~350,000 |
| **Infectious diseases** | ~150,000 |

### Key Conclusion
**No safe level of alcohol consumption** — risk increases with any amount consumed.

### Zero vs Low
| Consumption Level | Relative Risk |
|-------------------|---------------|
| **Zero** | 1.0 (baseline) |
| **Low (1 drink/day)** | >1.0 (increased) |
| **Moderate** | Progressive increase |
| **High** | Highest risk |

## Clinical Significance

### Public Health Message
- **Previous belief:** Moderate drinking protective
- **GBD 2019 finding:** Any drinking increases risk
- **Recommendation:** Zero alcohol is safest

### Risk Progression
| Drinks/Day | Risk Increase |
|------------|---------------|
| **1** | Small but measurable |
| **2** | Moderate increase |
| **3+** | Significant increase |

### Policy Implications
- Alcohol taxes
- Marketing restrictions
- Warning labels
- Zero-tolerance for driving
