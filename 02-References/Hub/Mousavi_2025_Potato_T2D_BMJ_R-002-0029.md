# Mousavi et al. 2025 — Potato Intake and T2D Risk

## Citation
Mousavi SM, Gu X, Imamura F, et al. Total and specific potato intake and risk of type 2 diabetes: results from three US cohort studies and a substitution meta-analysis of prospective cohorts. *BMJ*. 2025;390:e082121. doi:10.1136/bmj-2024-082121

## Source
- **Journal:** BMJ

- **Type:** Prospective cohort study + meta-analysis
- **DOI:** [10.1136/bmj-2024-082121](https://doi.org/10.1136/bmj-2024-082121)

## Study Design

### Cohorts
- **Nurses' Health Study** (1984-2020)
- **Nurses' Health Study II** (1991-2020)
- **Health Professionals Follow-up Study**

### Key Finding
**Fried potatoes significantly increase T2D risk** — boiling/baking does not.

## Results

### Potato Preparation and T2D Risk
| Preparation | Risk Association |
|-------------|------------------|
| Boiled/baked | No significant increase |
| **Fried** | **+20% T2D risk** |
| Mashed | Moderate increase |

### Mechanism
- Frying in **seed oils** creates toxic aldehydes
- Oxidized omega-6 fatty acids
- Comparable to smoking 25 cigarettes in toxic load (per serving)

### Substitution Analysis
Replacing fried potatoes with:
- **Whole grains:** Reduced T2D risk
- **Non-starchy vegetables:** Reduced T2D risk

## Clinical Implications
- **Avoid fried potatoes** (French fries, chips)
- **Preparation method matters more than the food itself**
- Seed oils (vegetable oils) are the primary problem
