# Low-Carbohydrate Diet Meta-Analysis — 174 RCTs on CVD Risk Factors

## Citation
Largest meta-analysis to date: 174 randomized controlled trials on carbohydrate restriction and cardiovascular risk factors. Various studies (2000-2024).

## Source
- **Primary sources:** 174 randomized controlled trials
- **Citation extracted from:** Facebook Posts Collection
- **Type:** Meta-analysis synthesis
- **Status:** Citation from source

## Key Findings
- Triglycerides: reduced
- Blood pressure (systolic and diastolic): reduced
- TG/HDL ratio: improved
- LDL/HDL ratio: improved
- ApoB/ApoA1 ratio: improved
- Inflammation markers (CRP, TNF-α, IL-6): reduced
- HDL cholesterol: increased
- LDL cholesterol: minor increase (+5 mg/dL)
- Total cholesterol: minor increase (+4 mg/dL)
- Body weight, fat, BMI, waist circumference: all reduced
- Ketogenic diets: greater weight loss, larger LDL increase, but NOT increased CVD risk

## Clinical Relevance
Low-carbohydrate diets improve metabolic health markers despite minor LDL increases. Net effect is CVD risk reduction.
