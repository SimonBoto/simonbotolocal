# Not All Calories Equal — Metabolic Effects of Macronutrients

## Key Studies

### Ebbeling et al. — BMJ 2018 (Framingham Feeding Study)
- **Design:** Randomized controlled trial, 164 adults
- **Intervention:** Three diets with different carb content, same calories
- **Finding:** Energy expenditure highest on low-carb diet
- **Result:** 278 kcal/day more burned on low-carb vs high-carb

### Ludwig et al. — JAMA 2012
- **Design:** Overfeeding study followed by weight loss
- **Finding:** Metabolic adaptation varies by diet composition
- **Result:** Low-carb diets preserve metabolic rate better

### Thermic Effect of Food (TEF) Research
- **Protein:** 20-30% of calories burned in processing
- **Carbohydrates:** 5-10% TEF
- **Fats:** 0-3% TEF

## The Calorie Myth

### "Calories In, Calories Out" Problems
| Assumption | Reality |
|------------|---------|
| All calories absorbed equally | ❌ Different TEF |
| Metabolism constant | ❌ Adapts to intake |
| Weight loss linear | ❌ Plateaus occur |
| Macronutrients irrelevant | ❌ Hormonal effects |

### Why Calories Differ
| Factor | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| **Thermic effect** | Protein costs more to process |
| **Hormonal response** | Carbs spike insulin |
| **Satiety** | Protein most filling |
| **Metabolic adaptation** | Varies by diet |

## Clinical Implications

### For Weight Loss
| Strategy | Effect |
|----------|--------|
| **Higher protein** | Higher TEF, better satiety |
| **Lower carb** | Preserves metabolic rate |
| **Don't just count calories** | Quality matters |

### Metabolic Advantage
| Diet | Estimated Advantage |
|------|---------------------|
| **Low-carb vs high-carb** | 200-300 kcal/day |
| **High-protein vs low-protein** | 100-150 kcal/day |
| **Whole foods vs processed** | Variable |

## Quality Assessment
- **Study quality:** High (RCT, controlled feeding)
- **Effect size:** Clinically meaningful (~300 kcal/day)
- **Consistency:** Multiple studies support
- **Limitation:** Short-term (20 weeks)
- **Clinical relevance:** High
