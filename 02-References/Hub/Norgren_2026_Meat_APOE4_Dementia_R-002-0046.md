## Key Findings

### Main Result
**Higher meat consumption associated with 55% lower dementia risk in APOE4 carriers.**

### Risk Reduction
| Genotype | Finding |
|----------|---------|
| **APOE4/4 (homozygous)** | Lower dementia risk with high meat intake |
| **APOE3/4 (heterozygous)** | Lower dementia risk with high meat intake |
| **Hazard ratio** | 0.45 (55% risk reduction) |
| **P-value** | 0.04 (significant) |

### Cognitive Decline
- Reduced cognitive decline with higher meat intake
- Effect specific to APOE4 carriers
- P=0.01 for cognitive decline outcome

### Hypothesis
APOE4 is the **evolutionarily oldest** APOE variant:
- Adapted to **meat-rich, low-carbohydrate** environments
- May require meat nutrients for optimal brain function
- Modern plant-based diets may mismatch APOE4 metabolism

## Mechanisms (Proposed)

### Meat Nutrients for Brain Health
| Nutrient | Function |
|----------|----------|
| **Vitamin B12** | Myelin synthesis, neurotransmission |
| **Heme iron** | Oxygen transport, neurotransmitter synthesis |
| **Zinc** | Synaptic function, neuroprotection |
| **Creatine** | Brain energy metabolism |
| **DHA/EPA** | Neuronal membrane integrity |

### APOE4 Specific
- APOE4 carriers have **impaired neuronal lipid metabolism**
- May require **dietary cholesterol and fats** for brain health
- Plant-based diets may **exacerbate** APOE4 disadvantages

## Clinical Implications

### For APOE4 Carriers (~15% population)
- **Consider meat consumption** for brain health
- **Monitor B12, iron, zinc** levels
- **Plant-based diets** may increase dementia risk
- **Personalized nutrition** based on genotype

### Precision Nutrition
- **Genotype matters:** Same diet different effects
- **APOE4:** May need meat for optimal cognition
- **APOE3:** More flexible dietary patterns
