## Study Design

### Inclusion Criteria
- Randomized controlled trials (RCTs)
- Adults with type 2 diabetes
- Compared low-carbohydrate vs low-fat diets
- Minimum 12 weeks duration

### Outcomes Measured
- Glycemic control (HbA1c, fasting glucose)
- Weight loss
- Lipid profiles
- Blood pressure

## Key Findings

### Low-Carbohydrate Diet Superior For:
| Outcome | Effect |
|---------|--------|
| **HbA1c reduction** | Greater improvement |
| **Fasting glucose** | Greater reduction |
| **Weight loss** | Greater loss |
| **Triglycerides** | Greater reduction |

### No Significant Difference:
- Total cholesterol
- HDL cholesterol
- LDL cholesterol
- Blood pressure

## Clinical Implications
- **Low-carb diets outperform low-fat** for glycemic control in T2D
- **Weight loss is a key mechanism** — both diets work, low-carb works better
- **Lipid effects are mixed** — triglycerides improve, other lipids neutral
- **Practical advantage:** Low-carb may be easier to sustain for some patients

## Note on "39 RCTs" Claim
The original Facebook post referenced "39 RCTs." This meta-analysis includes **22 unique RCTs**. The discrepancy may be due to:
- Counting multiple publications from same trials
- Including non-randomized studies
- Different inclusion criteria
- Possible error in original post

**This is the highest-quality available evidence** comparing LCD vs LFD specifically in T2D.
