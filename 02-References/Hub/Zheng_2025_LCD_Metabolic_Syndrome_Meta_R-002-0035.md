# Zheng et al. 2025 — Low-Carbohydrate Diets and Metabolic Syndrome

## Citation
Zheng Q, Gao X, Ruan X, et al. Are low carbohydrate diet interventions beneficial for metabolic syndrome and its components? A systematic review and meta-analysis of randomized controlled trials. *Int J Obes*. 2025. doi:10.1038/s41366-025-01822-5

## Key Findings

### Metabolic Syndrome Components Improved
| Component | Effect of Low-Carb Diet |
|-----------|------------------------|
| **Waist circumference** | Reduced |
| **Triglycerides** | Reduced |
| **HDL cholesterol** | Increased |
| **Blood pressure** | Reduced |
| **Fasting glucose** | Reduced |

### CVD Risk Implications
- Metabolic syndrome increases T2DM and CVD risk
- Low-carb diets improve all 5 diagnostic criteria
- Superior to low-fat for metabolic syndrome reversal

## Clinical Implications
- **First-line dietary intervention** for metabolic syndrome
- **Multi-factorial benefit** — addresses root cause (insulin resistance)
- **Sustainable** — better adherence than low-fat in long-term studies
