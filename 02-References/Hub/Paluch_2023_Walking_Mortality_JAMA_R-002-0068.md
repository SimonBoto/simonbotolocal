# Paluch 2023 — Association of Daily Step Patterns With Mortality in US Adults

## Citation
Paluch AE, et al. "Association of Daily Step Patterns With Mortality in US Adults." *JAMA Netw Open*. 2023. doi:10.1001/jamanetworkopen.2023.5174

## Source
- **Primary source:** JAMA Network Open
- **Citation extracted from:** Facebook Posts Collection, Article 65
- **Type:** Prospective cohort study
- **DOI:** 10.1001/jamanetworkopen.2023.5174
- **Status:** Verified DOI, citation from source

## Key Findings
- 8,000 steps taken 1-2 times per week reduces all-cause mortality risk by over 50% over 10 years
- 70% reduction in men
- 50% reduction in women
- Increasing to 3-7 days/week provides additional risk reduction

## Clinical Relevance
Few interventions in medicine demonstrate effect sizes of this magnitude. Even those who cannot walk daily achieve impressive health benefits from 8,000 steps just once or twice weekly.
