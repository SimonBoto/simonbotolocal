## Study Design

### Participants
- **40 patients** with Hashimoto's thyroiditis
- Randomized to: normal diet vs. low-carbohydrate diet
- Follow-up: 6 months

### Measurements
- **Thyroid antibodies:** TPOAb and TgAb
- **Thyroid water content:** Dixon-T2WI MRI (semi-quantitative)

## Key Findings

### Low-Carbohydrate Diet Results (6 months)
| Marker | Before | After | Change |
|--------|--------|-------|--------|
| Thyroid water content | 94.84% | 93.07% | ↓ Significant (P<0.05) |
| TPOAb | 211.30 | 89.45 | ↓ Significant (P<0.05) |
| TgAb | 17.05 | 4.1 | ↓ Significant (P<0.05) |

### Normal Diet Results
- **No significant changes** in any marker after 6 months

### Clinical Significance
- **Low-carb diet reduces thyroid inflammation** (measured by water content)
- **Autoantibodies decrease significantly** — disease activity reduction
- First study to use MRI quantification for this intervention

## Mechanism
Low-carbohydrate diets may reduce Hashimoto's inflammation through:
- Reduced insulin resistance
- Decreased systemic inflammation
- Improved metabolic flexibility
- Potential gut microbiome changes
