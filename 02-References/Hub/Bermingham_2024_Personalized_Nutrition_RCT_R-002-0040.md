## Study Design

### Participants
- Adults with elevated cardiometabolic risk
- Randomized to personalized vs. standard dietary advice

### Intervention: Personalized Dietary Program (PDP)
| Input | Method |
|-------|--------|
| **Postprandial glucose** | Continuous glucose monitoring |
| **Postprandial triglycerides** | Blood sampling |
| **Gut microbiome** | Metagenomic sequencing |
| **CVD risk factors** | Clinical assessment |
| **Health history** | Questionnaire |

### Control
- Standard USDA dietary guidelines
- General healthy eating advice

### Duration: 18 weeks

---

## Key Findings

### Primary Outcome
**Personalized nutrition substantially improved triglyceride concentrations** compared to standard advice.

### Cardiometabolic Risk
- PDP contributed to overall reduction in cardiometabolic disease risk
- Individual metabolic responses to foods vary widely
- Personalization captures this variation for targeted intervention

### Weight Outcomes
- Both groups experienced weight loss
- No significant difference between PDP and control for weight
- Benefits driven by metabolic improvements, not just weight

### Mechanism
The study leverages **individual variation in postprandial responses** — the same foods produce different metabolic effects in different people based on genetics, microbiome, and metabolic phenotype.
