# Reference: Lok et al. — Omega-3 Hemodialysis NEJM 2026

## Full Citation
Lok CE, et al. Fish-Oil Supplementation and Cardiovascular Events in Patients Receiving Hemodialysis. *N Engl J Med*. 2026;394(2):128-137. doi:10.1056/NEJMoa2513032. PMID: 41201837.

## Study Design
- **Type:** Multicenter, double-blind, randomized, placebo-controlled trial (PISCES)
- **Sites:** 26 dialysis centers (Canada, Australia)
- **Population:** 1,228 maintenance hemodialysis patients
- **Duration:** Mean 3.5 years

## Intervention
- **Fish oil:** 4g/day n-3 PUFA (1.6g EPA + 0.8g DHA)
- **Control:** Corn oil placebo

## Key Results

| Outcome | Fish Oil vs Placebo | HR (95% CI) |
|---------|---------------------|-------------|
| Major cardiovascular events | 0.31 vs 0.61/1000 patient-days | **0.57 (0.47-0.70)** p<0.001 |
| Cardiac death | - | 0.55 (0.40-0.75) |
| MI (fatal/nonfatal) | - | 0.56 (0.40-0.80) |
| Peripheral vascular disease (amputation) | - | 0.57 (0.38-0.86) |
| Stroke (fatal/nonfatal) | - | **0.37 (0.18-0.76)** |
| First CV event or all-cause death | - | 0.73 (0.61-0.87) |

**Primary finding:** ~43% reduction in major cardiovascular events.
