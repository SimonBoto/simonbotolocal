# Glucose vs Insulin CVD Risk — ACCORD and Insulin Resistance Literature

## Citation
Multiple sources:

2. Various studies on insulin resistance and cardiovascular disease (2008-2024)

- **Citation extracted from:** Facebook Posts Collection
- **Type:** Literature synthesis
- **Status:** Citation from source

## Key Findings
- ACCORD 2008: Intensive glucose control increased mortality, did not reduce CVD events
- Excess insulin (hyperinsulinemia) linked to increased CVD and all-cause mortality
- Metformin and lifestyle improve insulin sensitivity, reduce mortality
- Glucose damages small vessels (kidneys, eyes, nerves)
- Insulin damages large vessels (heart, brain — MI, stroke)
- Ideal: good glucose control with LOW insulin levels
- Fasting insulin reference ranges: 2-10 μIU/mL optimal; >10-12 suggests insulin resistance

## Clinical Relevance
The glucose-centric paradigm (focus on blood sugar) may be less important than the insulin-centric paradigm (focus on insulin resistance) for cardiovascular outcomes.
