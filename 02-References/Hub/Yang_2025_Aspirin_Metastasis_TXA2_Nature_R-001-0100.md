# Yang et al. 2025 — Aspirin Prevents Metastasis via Platelet TXA2 Mechanism

## Full Citation
Yang J, Yamashita-Kanemaru Y, Morris BI, Contursi A, Trajkovski D, Xu J, Chen M, Li J, Anderson G, Gao Y, Patrignani P, Adams DJ, Roychoudhuri R. Aspirin prevents metastasis by limiting platelet TXA2 suppression of T cell immunity. *Nature*. 2025. doi:10.1038/s41586-025-08626-7

## Abstract Summary
Cyclooxygenase-1 (COX-1) inhibitors, including aspirin, enhance anti-metastatic immunity by releasing T cells from platelet-derived thromboxane A2 (TXA2)-mediated suppression. This explains the clinical observation that daily aspirin reduces metastasis and cancer mortality.

## Key Mechanisms
1. **Platelet TXA2 suppresses T-cell function** — Creates immunosuppressive environment for circulating cancer cells
2. **Aspirin irreversibly inhibits COX-1** — In anucleated platelets, this permanently blocks TXA2 synthesis
3. **T-cell release from suppression** — Enables effective immune surveillance of micrometastases
4. **HLA class I dependency** — Effect strongest in tumors with high HLA-I expression

## Clinical Relevance
- **Meta-analysis HR for metastasis:** 0.64 (95% CI 0.48-0.84)
- **Cancer death reduction:** HR 0.49 (95% CI 0.30-0.79) in non-metastatic patients
- **Dose:** Low-dose (75-300mg daily) sufficient due to platelet-specific effect
