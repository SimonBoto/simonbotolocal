# Fatigue and Mitochondrial Dysfunction — Metabolomic Literature

## Citation
Various studies on fatigue, mitochondrial dysfunction, and metabolomic interventions (2010-2025).

## Source
- **Primary sources:** Various journals on mitochondrial medicine
- **Citation extracted from:** Facebook Posts Collection
- **Type:** Literature synthesis
- **Status:** Citation from source

## Key Findings
- Persistent fatigue despite rest = mitochondrial dysfunction
- Mitochondria produce ATP for muscles, brain, immune system, organs
- Mitochondrial dysfunction linked to most chronic diseases (autoimmune, CVD, diabetes, cancer)
- Factors affecting mitochondria: vitamin deficiencies, processed foods, smoking, alcohol, seed oils, inflammation, metabolic disorders, low sun exposure
- Metabolomic analysis can assess mitochondrial function
- Metabolomic-based interventions improve health in >90% of patients with autoimmune/chronic diseases

## Clinical Relevance
Fatigue is not normal — it's a message from the body about mitochondrial dysfunction that can be identified and corrected.
