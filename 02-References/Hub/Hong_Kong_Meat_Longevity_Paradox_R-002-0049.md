# Hong Kong Meat-Longevity Paradox

## The Data

### Meat Consumption (FAO 2020-2022)
| Rank | Country/Region | kg/person/year |
|------|----------------|----------------|
| **1** | Hong Kong | ~145 |
| **2** | Australia | ~120 |
| **3** | USA | ~100 |
| **4** | Argentina | ~95 |

### Life Expectancy (WHO 2023)
| Rank | Country/Region | Years |
|------|----------------|-------|
| **1** | Hong Kong | 85.3 |
| **2** | Japan | 84.8 |
| **3** | Switzerland | 84.0 |
| **4** | Singapore | 83.9 |

## The Paradox

### Contradicts Assumptions
| Assumption | Hong Kong Data |
|------------|----------------|
| "Meat reduces lifespan" | ❌ Highest meat, longest life |
| "Red meat is harmful" | ❌ Pork is staple |
| "Plant-based only healthy" | ❌ Omnivorous diet |

### Alternative Explanations
| Factor | Hong Kong Advantage |
|--------|---------------------|
| **Meat quality** | Fresh, less processed |
| **Dietary pattern** | Whole foods, vegetables |
| **Lifestyle** | Walking, urban density |
| **Healthcare** | Excellent access |
| **Social factors** | Family, community |

## Dietary Context

### Hong Kong Diet Characteristics
| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Primary meat** | Pork (fresh, not processed) |
| **Cooking** | Steaming, stir-frying |
| **Vegetables** | High variety, daily |
| **Rice** | Staple carbohydrate |
| **Sugar** | Lower than Western diets |
| **Processed foods** | Less prevalent |

### Comparison to Western Diets
| Factor | Hong Kong | USA/UK |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| **Meat processing** | Minimal | High (bacon, sausages) |
| **Sugar intake** | Lower | Higher |
| **Seed oils** | Less | More |
| **Vegetables** | More variety | Less |
| **Ultra-processed** | Less | More |

## Limitations

### Confounders
| Factor | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| **Genetics** | East Asian longevity |
| **Healthcare** | Universal, high quality |
| **Income** | High GDP per capita |
| **Education** | High health literacy |
| **Smoking** | Lower rates |

### Ecological Fallacy
- Population data ≠ individual advice
- Correlation ≠ causation
- Multiple factors contribute
