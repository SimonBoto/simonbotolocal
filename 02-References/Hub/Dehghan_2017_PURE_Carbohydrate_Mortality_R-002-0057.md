# Dehghan 2017 — PURE Study: Associations of fats and carbohydrate intake with cardiovascular disease and mortality

## Citation
Dehghan M, Mente A, Zhang X, et al. Associations of fats and carbohydrate intake with cardiovascular disease and mortality in 18 countries from five continents (PURE): a prospective cohort study. *Lancet*. 2017;390(10107):2050-2062. doi:10.1016/S0140-6736(17)32252-3

## Source
- **Primary source:** The Lancet
- **External source:** Not from Facebook Collection
- **DOI:** 10.1016/S0140-6736(17)32252-3
- **PMID:** 28864332
- **Status:** Verified PMID/DOI, external reference

## Key Findings
- PURE study: 135,335 participants from 18 countries
- Higher carbohydrate intake associated with INCREASED mortality
- Higher fat intake associated with REDUCED mortality
- Challenges conventional low-fat dietary guidelines
- 7.4 years follow-up

## Clinical Relevance
The largest global dietary study challenges the low-fat paradigm, showing higher carbohydrate intake (not lower) is associated with increased mortality.
