## Key Findings

### The 1967 NEJM Review
| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Funded by** | Sugar Research Foundation ($50,000+) |
| **Authors** | Harvard scientists |
| **Conclusion** | Blamed fat/cholesterol for CHD |
| **Exonerated** | Sugar as a cause |
| **Result** | Shifted 50+ years of nutrition policy |

### Industry Tactics Identified
| Tactic | Evidence |
|--------|----------|
| **Fund favorable research** | Paid experts to minimize sugar role |
| **Attack the science** | Cast doubt on sugar-CHD link |
| **Shift blame** | Focus on saturated fat instead |
| **Hide conflicts** | Funding not disclosed in publications |

- Internal memos from 1950s-1970s
- Sugar Research Foundation records

- Funding agreements

## Historical Impact

### Nutrition Policy Shift
- **1960s-2010s:** Fat demonized, sugar ignored
- **Dietary guidelines:** Low-fat recommendations
- **Food industry:** Replaced fat with sugar
- **Health outcome:** Obesity/diabetes epidemic

### Revelation Consequences
- **2016:** Documents published
- **Aftermath:** Sugar taxes proposed
- **Industry response:** Continued denial
- **Current status:** Slow policy change

### Tobacco Parallel
| Tobacco | Sugar |
|---------|-------|
| Knew cancer link 1950s | Knew CHD link 1960s |
| Funded doubt research | Funded fat-blame research |
| Attacked scientists | Attacked sugar critics |
| Delayed regulation 40+ years | Delayed regulation 50+ years |
