## Study Design

### Participants
- **177,000+ adults** aged 50-89 years
- No diabetes
- No cholesterol-lowering medication
- 6-year follow-up

### Key Finding
**Lowest mortality at LDL 100-189 mg/dL** — higher than current guidelines recommend.

## Results

### LDL and Mortality
| LDL Level | Mortality Risk |
|-----------|----------------|
| Very low | Higher mortality |
| 100-189 mg/dL | **Lowest risk** |
| Current "optimal" (<100) | Not associated with longevity |

### Better Predictors Than LDL
- **Triglycerides:** Higher levels consistently associated with increased mortality
- **HDL:** Low HDL linked to higher mortality; higher HDL protective

### Clinical Implication
> "Health professionals should promote established (causal) mechanisms that reduce future risk of major ASCVD events, rather than focusing on LDL-C level, per se."

## Key Conclusions
- LDL alone should not guide medical decisions in primary prevention
- Metabolic health (triglycerides, HDL) more predictive
- Very low LDL may be harmful
