# Patrono & Rocca 2025 — Understanding How Aspirin Prevents Metastasis

## Full Citation
Patrono C, Rocca B. Understanding How Aspirin Prevents Metastasis. *N Engl J Med*. 2025;392:e47. doi:10.1056/NEJMcibr2502386

## Article Type
Clinical Implications / Clinical Practice Commentary

## Context
This NEJM article provides clinical commentary on the Yang et al. Nature 2025 study, explaining the clinical implications of aspirin's anti-metastatic mechanism through platelet TXA2 inhibition and T-cell activation.

## Key Clinical Points

### Mechanism Summary
- Aspirin enhances antimetastatic immunity by decreasing platelet activation
- This releases T cells from suppression by thromboxane A2 (TXA2)
- COX-1 inhibition in platelets is the key molecular target

### Clinical Relevance
- Explains observational data showing reduced metastasis with aspirin use
- Provides mechanistic rationale for adjuvant aspirin in cancer patients
- Supports low-dose aspirin (75-100mg) as sufficient for platelet effect

### Connection to Nature Study
- Commentary on: Yang J et al. Aspirin prevents metastasis by limiting platelet TXA2 suppression of T cell immunity. *Nature*. 2025. doi:10.1038/s41586-025-08626-7
- Translates basic science findings into clinical practice considerations
