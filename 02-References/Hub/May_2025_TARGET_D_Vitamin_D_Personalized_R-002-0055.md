# May 2025 — TARGET-D: Personalized Vitamin D Halves Heart Attack Risk

## Citation
May HT, et al. Heart attack risk halved in adults with heart disease taking tailored vitamin D doses. American Heart Association Scientific Sessions 2025, Abstract 4382525. November 9, 2025.

## Source
- **Primary source:** American Heart Association Scientific Sessions 2025
- **Citation extracted from:** Facebook Posts Collection
- **Type:** Conference abstract
- **Status:** Citation from source
- **Note:** Conference presentation, not peer-reviewed publication

## Key Findings
- TARGET-D study: personalized vitamin D dosing vs standard care
- 52% lower heart attack risk with personalized dosing
- Target range: 40-80 ng/mL vitamin D3
- 85% started below 40 ng/mL
- Many needed >5,000 IU/day (6x FDA recommendation)
- Dose adjusted every 3 months based on blood levels

## Clinical Relevance
Personalized vitamin D dosing based on blood levels may be more effective than standard dosing for CVD prevention in adults with established heart disease.
