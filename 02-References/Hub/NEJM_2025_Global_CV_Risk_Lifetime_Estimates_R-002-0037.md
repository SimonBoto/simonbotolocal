## Key Findings

### Five Modifiable Risk Factors
1. High blood pressure
2. Elevated cholesterol
3. Abnormal body weight
4. Diabetes
5. Smoking

### Lifetime Impact
| Profile | Women | Men |
|---------|-------|-----|
| All 5 present | Baseline | Baseline |
| None present | +14.5 years | +11.8 years |
| Healthy life years | +13.3 years | +10.6 years |

### Hierarchy of Impact
1. **Smoking cessation** — Greatest benefit
2. **Blood pressure control** — Second greatest
3. **Diabetes prevention** — Substantial
4. **Weight normalization** — Moderate
5. **Cholesterol management** — Minimal independent impact
