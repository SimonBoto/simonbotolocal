# Low-Carb vs Low-Fat for Weight Loss — Meta-Analysis of 39 RCTs

## Citation
Meta-analysis of 39 randomized controlled trials comparing low-carbohydrate vs low-fat diets for weight loss and metabolic health (2010-2025).

## Source
- **Primary sources:** Various RCTs and meta-analyses
- **Citation extracted from:** Facebook Posts Collection
- **Type:** Meta-analysis synthesis
- **Status:** Citation from source

## Key Findings
- 39 RCTs analyzed: low-carb consistently superior for weight loss
- Statistical significance demonstrated in all 39 studies
- Low-carb completion rate: 79.51% vs low-fat: 77.72%
- Low-carb improves: HDL cholesterol (↑), triglycerides (↓), blood glucose (↓), insulin levels (↓)
- No long-term difference in: total cholesterol, LDL cholesterol, blood pressure
- Additional benefits: reduced insulin resistance, better diabetes control, reduced gallstone risk, improved GI function, reduced GERD, reduced chronic inflammation

## Clinical Relevance
Low-carbohydrate diets are more effective than low-fat diets for weight loss and metabolic health, with better adherence rates.
