# Animal and Saturated Fat Not Linked to Cardiovascular Death — Tokyo University

## Citation
University of Tokyo. "Restriction of Animal and Saturated Fats Not Associated with Reduced Death Risk."

## Source
- **Primary source:** University of Tokyo Research
- **Citation extracted from:** Facebook Posts Collection
- **Type:** Clinical research / epidemiological study
- **Status:** Citation from source

## Key Findings
- Saturated fat increases CVD risk: NOT supported
- Saturated fat increases mortality: NOT supported
- Reducing animal fat improves health: NOT supported
- Consumption of animal and saturated fats not associated with increased cardiovascular risk or mortality
- Current guidelines based on weak evidence from observational studies with limitations

## Clinical Relevance
Challenges conventional dietary guidelines on saturated fat restriction. Evidence suggests overall dietary pattern and metabolic context more important than single nutrient restriction.
