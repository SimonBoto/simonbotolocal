## Key Findings

### Main Result
**Genetically elevated Lp(a) is causally associated with increased risk of myocardial infarction** — providing Mendelian randomization evidence that Lp(a) is not just a marker but a causal risk factor.

### Specific Findings
| Lp(a) Level | Odds Ratio for MI |
|-------------|-------------------|
| >90th percentile | 1.6-1.7× increased risk |
| KIV-2 variants (low Lp(a)) | Protective |
| KIV-2 null alleles | Lower CVD risk |

### Genetic Evidence
- **KIV-2 kringle repeats:** Inversely associated with Lp(a) levels
- **Mendelian randomization:** Genetic variants affecting Lp(a) levels showed consistent association with MI risk
- **Causality:** Genetic elevation of Lp(a) → increased MI risk (supporting causation, not just correlation)

### Population Impact
- ~20% of population has elevated Lp(a) levels
- Lp(a) is **90-95% genetically determined**
- Not significantly modified by diet, exercise, or statins

## Clinical Significance

### Why This Study Matters
1. **Established causality** — Lp(a) is not just a marker, it causes CVD
2. **Genetic basis** — 90-95% heritable, not lifestyle-modifiable
3. **Screening indication** — One-time measurement sufficient (stable throughout life)
4. **Treatment implications** — Focus on aggressive control of other modifiable risk factors

### Current Therapeutics
| Therapy | Lp(a) Effect | Status |
|---------|--------------|--------|
| Statins | Minimal/none | Standard care (for other risk factors) |
| Niacin | ~20% reduction | Limited use due to side effects |
| PCSK9 inhibitors | ~25-30% reduction | Approved, expensive |
| **Pelacarsen (siRNA)** | **~80% reduction** | Phase 3 trials ongoing |
| **Olpasiran (siRNA)** | **~95% reduction** | Phase 3 trials ongoing |
