# Lifestyle Disease Factors Are Modifiable

## The 80/20 Rule

### CDC/WHO Estimates
| Factor | Chronic Disease Contribution |
|--------|------------------------------|
| **Lifestyle/behavior** | 80% |
| **Genetics** | 15-20% |
| **Healthcare access** | 10% |
| **Environment** | 20% |

*Note: Categories overlap; lifestyle dominates.*

### Leading Causes of Death (USA)
| Cause | % Preventable by Lifestyle |
|-------|---------------------------|
| **Heart disease** | 80% |
| **Cancer** | 40-50% |
| **Stroke** | 80% |
| **Diabetes T2** | 90% |
| **Dementia** | 40% |

---

## Modifiable Risk Factors

### High Impact
| Factor | Disease Impact | Modification |
|--------|----------------|--------------|
| **Smoking** | CVD, cancer, COPD | Cessation |
| **Diet** | All chronic diseases | Whole foods |
| **Physical inactivity** | CVD, diabetes, cancer | Daily movement |
| **Alcohol** | Cancer, liver, CVD | Limit/eliminate |

### Moderate Impact
| Factor | Disease Impact | Modification |
|--------|----------------|--------------|
| **Sleep** | CVD, obesity, depression | 7-9 hours |
| **Stress** | CVD, immune, mental health | Management |
| **Social isolation** | Mortality, CVD, dementia | Connection |

## Genetics vs Lifestyle

### Twin Studies
| Finding | Implication |
|---------|-------------|
| **Identical twins, different lifestyles** | Different disease outcomes |
| **Concordance for lifestyle diseases** | Low (30-40%) |
| **Environment > genes for most diseases** | Modifiable determinants |

### Epigenetics
| Mechanism | Lifestyle Effect |
|-----------|------------------|
| **DNA methylation** | Diet, stress modify |
| **Histone modification** | Exercise, fasting |
| **miRNA expression** | Multiple factors |
