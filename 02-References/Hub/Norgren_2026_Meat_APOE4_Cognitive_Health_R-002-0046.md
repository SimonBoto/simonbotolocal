## Key Findings

### APOE4 Background
- **APOE ε4 allele** present in ~25% of population
- Increases Alzheimer's disease risk ~2.5×
- Genetic predisposition ≠ destiny

### Main Result
**Higher meat consumption eliminated APOE4-associated dementia risk completely.**

- Low meat consumption + APOE4: ~2.5× increased dementia risk
- High unprocessed meat consumption + APOE4: **Risk eliminated** (cognitive function similar to non-APOE4 individuals)
- Hazard ratio 0.45 (P=0.04) for dementia risk reduction
- Cognitive decline also reduced (P=0.01)

### Processed vs. Unprocessed Meat
- **Unprocessed meat:** Protective (even with APOE4)
- **Processed meat:** Negative effects regardless of genotype

### Clinical Implications
- Dietary intervention can overcome genetic risk
- Unprocessed meat contains nutrients critical for cognitive health (B12, iron, zinc, omega-3s)
- Public health recommendations should distinguish processed vs. unprocessed meat
