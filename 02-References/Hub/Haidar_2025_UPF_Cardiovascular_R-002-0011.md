## Key Findings
- **n=7,000 adults followed >10 years**
- Each additional serving of ultra-processed food/day → **+5.1% CVD risk**
- Highest UPF consumers had **67% higher CVD risk** vs. lowest consumers
- Association remained significant after controlling for:
  - Cholesterol
  - Blood pressure
  - Diabetes status
- **Ultra-processed foods are a CVD risk factor INDEPENDENT of traditional metrics**

### UPF Categories Associated with Risk
- Industrial breads, cereals
- Processed meats, hot dogs
- Packaged snacks, chips
- Sugar-sweetened and artificially-sweetened beverages
- Instant noodles, ready meals
- Margarine, industrial sauces

### Mechanism
- UPF bypasses normal satiety mechanisms
- High calorie, low nutrient density
- Promotes overconsumption
- Negatively impacts gut microbiome
- Creates metabolic dysfunction
