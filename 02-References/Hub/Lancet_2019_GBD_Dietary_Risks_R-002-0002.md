## Key Findings
- **Dietary risks responsible for 11 million deaths annually (22% of all adult deaths)**
- High intake of sodium, low intake of whole grains, and low intake of fruits were leading dietary risks
- Poor diet now a leading risk factor for death globally
- Suboptimal diet is a leading cause of non-communicable diseases
- Universal need for improving diet composition across all populations
