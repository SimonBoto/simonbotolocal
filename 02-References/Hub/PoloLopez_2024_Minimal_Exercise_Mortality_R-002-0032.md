## Key Finding
**Significant reductions in all-cause mortality and CVD incidence can be achieved with as little as 10-15 minutes of physical activity per day.**

## Results

### Minimum Effective Dose
| Activity Duration | Outcome |
|-------------------|---------|
| **10 min/day** | Significant reduction in all-cause mortality |
| **15 min/day** | Significant reduction in CVD incidence |
| 250 min/week (MVPA) | Optimal target |
| 100 min/week | Possible first target |

### Study Design
- **Accelerometer-measured** physical activity (objective, not self-reported)
- Prospective cohort methodology
- Dose-response analysis

## Clinical Implications
- **Any amount of exercise is beneficial** — no minimum threshold
- **10-15 minutes/day** is a realistic starting point for sedentary patients
- **Small amounts compound** — consistency beats intensity for beginners
- Message: "Start with 10 minutes, build from there"
