## The Omega-6 Pathway
```
Linoleic Acid (LA) → Gamma-Linolenic Acid (GLA) → 
Dihomo-GLA (DGLA) → Anti-inflammatory prostaglandins (PGE1)
```

### Why GLA Matters
- **Anti-inflammatory** — precursor to PGE1 (anti-inflammatory prostaglandin)
- **Skin health** — supports skin barrier function
- **Hormonal balance** — PMS and menopause support
- **Nerve function** — myelin membrane component

### Delta-6-Desaturase Issue
Many people have impaired conversion of LA → GLA due to:
- Aging
- Stress
- High sugar intake
- Trans fat consumption
- Alcohol
- Zinc deficiency

**Direct GLA supplementation bypasses this bottleneck.**

## Clinical Applications

### Women's Health
- **PMS** — breast tenderness, mood, bloating
- **Menopause** — hot flashes, skin dryness
- **Fibrocystic breast disease** — reduces cysts

### Skin Conditions
- **Atopic eczema** — reduces inflammation, improves barrier
- **Acne** — anti-inflammatory, hormone-balancing
- **Dry skin** — improves moisture retention
- **Dermatitis** — anti-inflammatory support

### Other Uses
- **Diabetic neuropathy** — nerve membrane support
- **Rheumatoid arthritis** — anti-inflammatory
- **Osteoporosis** — bone density support

## Clinical Notes
- **GLA dose** — 270 mg at 3 softgels is therapeutic
- **Takes time** — effects often seen after 4-12 weeks
- **Well-tolerated** — minimal side effects
- **Evening dosing** — some prefer taking at night (not required)
- **Not for everyone** — avoid in epilepsy, schizophrenia (rare reports)
