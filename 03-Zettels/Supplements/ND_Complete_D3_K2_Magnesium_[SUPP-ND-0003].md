## The D3-K2-Magnesium Synergy

### Vitamin D3 (4000 IU)
- Increases intestinal calcium absorption
- Requires magnesium for conversion to active form (1,25-OH-D)
- Supports immune function, bone health

### Vitamin K2 (MK7) — 200 μg
- Activates osteocalcin (bone-building protein)
- Activates matrix Gla protein (prevents arterial calcification)
- Works with D3 to ensure calcium goes to bones, not soft tissues

### Magnesium (56 mg bisglycinate)
- **Required cofactor** for vitamin D activation
- Converts 25(OH)D → 1,25(OH)₂D (active form)
- Supports bone matrix formation
- Calms nervous system, supports sleep

## Clinical Notes
- **High-dose D3** — 4000 IU is therapeutic dose for deficiency
- **K2 essential** — prevents hypercalcemia, arterial calcification when taking high-dose D
- **Mg modest** — 56 mg is supportive; may need additional magnesium
- **Bisglycinate form** — gentle on stomach, good absorption

## Ideal For
- Vitamin D deficiency with bone health concerns
- Osteopenia/osteoporosis prevention
- Cardiovascular protection (prevents arterial calcification)
- Patients on high-dose D3 therapy
