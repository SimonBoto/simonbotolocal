## Nattokinase Explained

### What is Nattokinase?
- **Potent fibrinolytic enzyme** — breaks down fibrin
- **Discovered in natto** (fermented soybeans)
- **Produced by Bacillus subtilis natto**
- **Orally bioactive** — survives stomach acid
- **Systemic effects** — enters bloodstream

### Mechanisms
1. **Direct fibrinolysis** — breaks down fibrin clots
2. **Plasmin activation** — enhances body's own clot-busting
3. **Blood viscosity reduction** — improves flow
4. **Anti-thrombotic** — prevents excessive clotting

### Research Areas
- **Cardiovascular health** — circulation support
- **Blood pressure** — ACE inhibition (modest)
- **Deep vein thrombosis (DVT)** — prevention support
- **Stroke prevention** — fibrinolytic support
- **Atherosclerosis** — plaque stabilization

## Clinical Notes
- **2000 FU standard dose** — common maintenance dose
- **Higher doses exist** — 4000-8000 FU for therapeutic use
- **Take on empty stomach** — better absorption
- **Caution with:** Blood thinners (warfarin, aspirin, etc.)
- **Surgery** — discontinue 2 weeks before
- **Well-tolerated** — few side effects reported

## Contraindications
- **Bleeding disorders** — hemophilia, von Willebrand
- **Anticoagulant medications** — warfarin, heparin, DOACs
- **Antiplatelet drugs** — aspirin, clopidogrel
- **Surgery** — bleeding risk
- **Pregnancy/breastfeeding** — insufficient safety data
