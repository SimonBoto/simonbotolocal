## Description
Active form of vitamin B12 — methylcobalamin. Most B12 supplements contain cyanocobalamin, which requires liver detoxification and methylation to become active. Methylcobalamin is immediately active upon absorption and stays longer in the body.

## Methylcobalamin vs Cyanocobalamin

| Feature | Methylcobalamin | Cyanocobalamin |
|---------|-----------------|----------------|
| Form | Active | Inactive (synthetic) |
| Absorption | Direct | Requires conversion |
| Retention | Longer in body | Shorter |
| Cyanide | None | Must be detoxified |
| Methylation | Supports | Requires |
| Cost | Higher | Lower |

## B12 Functions
- **Nervous system** — myelin synthesis, nerve function
- **Brain health** — cognitive function, mood
- **Red blood cells** — prevents megaloblastic anemia
- **Homocysteine metabolism** — cardiovascular protection
- **DNA synthesis** — cell division
- **Energy** — reduces fatigue

## Who Needs B12?
- **Vegans/vegetarians** — B12 only in animal foods
- **Elderly** — reduced absorption with age
- **Pernicious anemia** — intrinsic factor deficiency
- **GI disorders** — malabsorption (Crohn's, celiac)
- **MTHFR variants** — methylation issues
- **Long-term PPI use** — reduced stomach acid

## Clinical Notes
- **High dose** — 1000 μg is therapeutic
- **Sublingual alternative** — some prefer under tongue
- **No toxicity** — water-soluble, excess excreted
- **Check levels** — aim B12 >400 pg/mL
- **With folate** — works synergistically
