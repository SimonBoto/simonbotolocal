## Red Yeast Rice & Monacolins

### What is Red Yeast Rice?
- **Rice fermented with Monascus purpureus** — traditional Chinese food/medicine
- **Contains monacolins** — HMG-CoA reductase inhibitors
- **Monacolin K** — identical to lovastatin (statin drug)
- **Natural alternative** — to prescription statins

### How It Works
- **Inhibits HMG-CoA reductase** — same enzyme as statins
- **Reduces cholesterol synthesis** — in liver
- **Lowers LDL** — primary effect
- **May raise HDL** — modestly

## Critical Cautions

### DO NOT TAKE WITH:
- **Statins** (atorvastatin, lovastatin, simvastatin) — additive effect, liver risk
- **Antibiotics** (especially tetracycline) — B12/folate interferes with absorption
- **Pregnancy/breastfeeding** — contraindicated
- **Children under 18** — not recommended
- **Adults over 70** — not recommended
- **Those already taking red yeast rice products**

### Monitoring Required:
- **Liver enzymes** — check periodically
- **Cholesterol levels** — verify efficacy
- **Muscle symptoms** — report pain/weakness

## Clinical Notes
- **3 mg monacolins** — below prescription statin doses
- **Gentler than statins** — but same mechanism
- **Take at different time** from antibiotics
- **Consult physician** — especially if on medications
- **Not for everyone** — strict contraindications

## Ideal For
- Mild to moderate cholesterol elevation
- Statin-intolerant individuals (with medical supervision)
- Those seeking natural alternatives
- Cardiovascular prevention
