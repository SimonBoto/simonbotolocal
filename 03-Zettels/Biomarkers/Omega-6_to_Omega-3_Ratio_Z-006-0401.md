# Omega-6 to Omega-3 Ratio — Inflammatory Balance Index

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**Omega-6:3 ratio:** Ratio of omega-6 to omega-3 fatty acids

**Also called:**
- n6:n3 ratio
- Omega-6/3 index

source-litnote: Synthesized from clinical practice
---

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**Balance of essential fatty acids:**
| Family | Primary Fatty Acid | Function |
|--------|-------------------|----------|
| **Omega-6** | Arachidonic acid (AA) | Pro-inflammatory (when excessive) |
| **Omega-3** | EPA, DHA | Anti-inflammatory |

**Evolutionary perspective:**
- Hunter-gatherers: ~1:1 to 4:1
- Modern Western diet: ~15:1 to 20:1
- **Imbalance drives chronic inflammation**

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### Omega-6:3 Ratio

| Ratio | Status | Interpretation |
|-------|--------|----------------|
| **1:1 to 4:1** | Optimal | Anti-inflammatory balance |
| **4:1 to 10:1** | Acceptable | Moderate balance |
| **10:1 to 15:1** | Elevated | Pro-inflammatory tendency |
| **>15:1** | High | Significant inflammatory risk |

### Clinical Significance

**High ratio associated with:**
- Cardiovascular disease
- Inflammatory conditions
- Autoimmune disease
- Depression
- Cancer risk

**Optimal ratio protective for:**
- Heart disease
- Inflammatory disorders
- Cognitive function

## 4. COFACTORS

### Factors Affecting Ratio

| Factor | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| **Vegetable oils** | High omega-6 (corn, soybean, sunflower) |
| **Fish intake** | Increases omega-3 |
| **Fish oil** | Increases omega-3 |
| **Grain-fed meat** | High omega-6 |
| **Grass-fed meat** | Better omega-6:3 ratio |

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### For High Omega-6:3 Ratio

**Reduce omega-6:**
- Eliminate vegetable oils (corn, soybean, sunflower, safflower)
- Reduce processed foods
- Choose grass-fed meat

**Increase omega-3:**
- Fatty fish 2-3x/week
- Fish oil: 2-4g EPA+DHA/day
- Algae oil (vegan)

**Target:** <4:1 ratio
