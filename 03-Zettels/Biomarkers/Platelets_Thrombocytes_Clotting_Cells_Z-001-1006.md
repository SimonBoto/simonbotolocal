# Platelets — Thrombocytes, Clotting Cells

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**Platelets:** From Greek "platys" (flat) — small, flat cell fragments

**Thrombocytes:** From Greek "thrombos" (clot) + "kytos" (cell)

**Also called:**
- PLT
- Thrombocytes (technically cell fragments, not cells)

**Discovery:** 1882 by Giulio Bizzozero

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**Primary hemostasis:**
- Form platelet plug at injury site
- Release granules (ADP, serotonin, growth factors)
- Provide surface for coagulation cascade

**Production:**
- Bone marrow megakaryocytes
- Thrombopoietin (TPO) regulates production
- Lifespan: 7-10 days

**Normal count:** 150,000-400,000/μL

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### Platelet Levels

| Level (/μL) | Status | Interpretation |
|-------------|--------|----------------|
| **150,000-400,000** | Normal | Adequate hemostasis |
| **100,000-150,000** | Mild thrombocytopenia | Usually asymptomatic |
| **50,000-100,000** | Moderate | Risk with surgery/trauma |
| **20,000-50,000** | Severe | Spontaneous bleeding risk |
| **<20,000** | Critical | High bleeding risk |
| **>400,000** | Thrombocytosis | Clotting risk |

### Thrombocytopenia (Low Platelets)

**Causes:**
| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| **Decreased production** | Bone marrow failure, chemotherapy, B12/folate deficiency |
| **Increased destruction** | ITP, TTP, HUS, DIC, drug-induced |
| **Sequestration** | Hypersplenism |
| **Dilution** | Massive transfusion |

**Immune Thrombocytopenia (ITP):**
- Autoimmune destruction
- Isolated low platelets
- Normal RBC, WBC

### Thrombocytosis (High Platelets)

**Reactive (secondary):**
- Infection, inflammation
- Iron deficiency
- Post-splenectomy
- Malignancy

**Primary:**
- Essential thrombocythemia
- Polycythemia vera
- Myelofibrosis

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### For Thrombocytopenia

**Identify cause:**
- Peripheral smear
- Bone marrow biopsy (if indicated)
- Autoimmune markers
- Infection workup

**Treatment:**
- ITP: Steroids, IVIG, splenectomy
- TTP/HUS: Plasma exchange
- Drug-induced: Stop offending drug
- Severe: Platelet transfusion

### For Thrombocytosis

**Reactive:**
- Treat underlying cause
- Usually resolves

**Primary (myeloproliferative):**
- Hydroxyurea
- Aspirin (if low risk)
- Cytoreductive therapy

## 6. CONNECTED BIOMARKERS

| Biomarker | Relationship | Pattern |
|-----------|--------------|---------|
| **MPV** | Platelet size | High MPV = young, active platelets |
| **PDW** | Size variation | Width of distribution |
| **Ferritin** | Iron status | Low iron often → high platelets |
| **CRP** | Inflammation | Both elevated in reactive thrombocytosis |

### Clinical Patterns

**Iron Deficiency:**
- Platelets: Often elevated
- Ferritin: Low
- MCV: Low
- **Reactive thrombocytosis**

**ITP:**
- Platelets: Low
- MPV: Often high (young platelets)
- RBC, WBC: Normal
- **Isolated thrombocytopenia**

**Essential Thrombocythemia:**
- Platelets: Very high (>450,000)
- JAK2 mutation: Often positive
- **Primary thrombocytosis**
