# Albumin — Liver Synthesis, Nutrition & Transport

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**Albumin:** From Latin "albus" (white) — egg white contains albumin

**Also called:**
- Serum albumin
- Human serum albumin (HSA)

---

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**Most abundant plasma protein (60% of total protein):**
| Function | Mechanism |
|----------|-----------|
| **Oncotic pressure** | Maintains fluid in vessels |
| **Transport** | Binds fatty acids, bilirubin, calcium, drugs, hormones |
| **Nutritional marker** | Reflects protein status |
| **Antioxidant** | Free radical scavenger |
| **Buffer** | pH regulation |

**Synthesis:**
- Produced by liver (15g/day)
- Half-life: 20 days
- Sensitive to liver function and nutrition

---

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### Albumin Levels

| Level (g/dL) | Status | Interpretation |
|--------------|--------|----------------|
| **4.0-5.0** | Optimal | Normal synthesis/nutrition |
| **3.5-4.0** | Low-normal | Mild decrease |
| **3.0-3.5** | Low | Moderate deficiency |
| **2.5-3.0** | Very low | Severe deficiency |
| **<2.5** | Critical | Life-threatening |

### Causes of Low Albumin (Hypoalbuminemia)

**Decreased synthesis:**
| Cause | Mechanism |
|-------|-----------|
| **Liver disease** | Hepatitis, cirrhosis |
| **Malnutrition** | Protein deficiency |
| **Malabsorption** | Celiac, IBD |

**Increased loss:**
| Cause | Mechanism |
|-------|-----------|
| **Nephrotic syndrome** | Kidney protein loss |
| **Protein-losing enteropathy** | Gut protein loss |
| **Burns** | Skin loss |
| **Hemorrhage** | Blood loss |

**Increased catabolism/dilution:**
| Cause | Mechanism |
|-------|-----------|
| **Inflammation** | Acute phase response |
| **Pregnancy** | Hemodilution |
| **IV fluids** | Dilution |

### Causes of High Albumin (Hyperalbuminemia)

- Dehydration (hemoconcentration)
- Rare genetic variants

---

## 4. COFACTORS

### Albumin Synthesis Requirements

| Nutrient | Role | Deficiency Effect |
|----------|------|-------------------|
| **Protein** | Amino acid substrate | Reduced synthesis |
| **Liver function** | Synthesis site | Reduced production |
| **Zinc** | Protein synthesis | Impaired production |

---

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### For Low Albumin

**Address underlying cause:**
- Liver disease: Treat hepatitis, cirrhosis support
- Malnutrition: Increase protein intake
- Malabsorption: Treat celiac, IBD
- Nephrotic syndrome: Treat kidney disease

**Nutritional support:**
- Protein: 1.2-1.5 g/kg/day
- Zinc: 15-30 mg/day
- Treat inflammation

**IV albumin:**
- Severe hypoalbuminemia with edema
- Cirrhosis with ascites
- Nephrotic syndrome

---

## 6. CONNECTED BIOMARKERS

| Biomarker | Relationship | Pattern |
|-----------|--------------|---------|
| **Total protein** | Component | Albumin + globulins |
| **Globulins** | Other proteins | Inverse in liver disease |
| **A/G ratio** | Albumin/globulin | Normal >1.0 |
| **Prealbumin** | Nutrition marker | Shorter half-life |
| **CRP** | Inflammation | Elevated = low albumin |
| **Liver enzymes** | Liver function | Elevated in liver disease |

### Clinical Patterns

**Liver Disease:**
- Albumin: Low
- Globulins: High (immune response)
- A/G ratio: <1.0 (reversed)
- **Chronic liver dysfunction**

**Nephrotic Syndrome:**
- Albumin: Very low
- Proteinuria: High (>3.5g/day)
- Edema: Present
- **Kidney protein loss**

**Malnutrition:**
- Albumin: Low
- Prealbumin: Low
- Transferrin: Low
- **Protein deficiency**

**Inflammation:**
- Albumin: Low (acute phase)
- CRP: High
- **Catabolic state**

| Population | Optimal |
|------------|---------|
| **Adults** | 4.0-5.0 g/dL |

## 8. ACCEPTED RANGE

| Category | Range |
|----------|-------|
| **Normal** | 3.5-5.0 g/dL |

---

## 9. PROBLEMATIC RANGE

| Level | Concern | Action |
|-------|---------|--------|
| **<3.5 g/dL** | Low | Investigate cause |
| **<3.0 g/dL** | Moderate | Nutritional intervention |
| **<2.5 g/dL** | Severe | Consider IV albumin |
| **>5.5 g/dL** | High | Check dehydration |

---

## 10. CLINICAL WEIGHT

**Significance: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (5/5 — Critical)**

**Primary nutrition and liver function marker**

**Critical for fluid balance and transport**

**Always with:**
- Total protein
- Globulins (A/G ratio)
- Prealbumin (acute nutrition)
- Liver enzymes
