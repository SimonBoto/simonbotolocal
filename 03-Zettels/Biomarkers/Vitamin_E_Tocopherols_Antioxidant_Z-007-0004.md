# Vitamin E — Tocopherols, Fat-Soluble Antioxidant

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**Vitamin E:** Discovered 1922, essential for fertility ("tocopherol" from Greek "tokos" childbirth + "pherein" to bear)

**Tocopherols:** Four forms (alpha, beta, gamma, delta)

**Tocotrienols:** Four forms with unsaturated side chain

---

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**Primary fat-soluble antioxidant:**
| Function | Mechanism |
|----------|-----------|
| **Lipid protection** | Prevents PUFA oxidation in membranes |
| **LDL protection** | Prevents oxidative modification |
| **Cell signaling** | Regulates protein kinase C |
| **Immune function** | T-cell enhancement |
| **Skin health** | UV protection, wound healing |

**Forms:**
| Form | Activity |
|------|----------|
| **Alpha-tocopherol** | Highest vitamin E activity |
| **Gamma-tocopherol** | Anti-inflammatory, nitrosative stress |
| **Tocotrienols** | Cholesterol-lowering, neuroprotective |

---

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### Vitamin E Levels

| Level (mg/L) | Status | Interpretation |
|--------------|--------|----------------|
| **12-20** | Optimal | Adequate antioxidant status |
| **5-12** | Low-normal | Suboptimal |
| **<5** | Deficient | Increased oxidative risk |
| **>30** | High | Possible excess |

### Clinical Significance

**Deficiency symptoms:**
- Peripheral neuropathy
- Hemolytic anemia (premature infants)
- Retinopathy
- Immune dysfunction
- Skin problems

**High-risk groups:**
- Fat malabsorption (cystic fibrosis, cholestasis)
- Premature infants
- Genetic defects (abetalipoproteinemia)

---

## 4. COFACTORS

### Vitamin E Metabolism

| Nutrient | Role | Interaction |
|----------|------|-------------|
| **Vitamin C** | Regenerates oxidized E | Synergistic |
| **Selenium** | GPx enzyme | Protects E |
| **Vitamin A** | Shares antioxidant | Competes if excess |
| **Fat** | Absorption | Required for uptake |

---

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### For Deficiency (<5 mg/L)

**Supplementation:**
- Mixed tocopherols: 400-800 IU/day
- Gamma-tocopherol: 200-400 mg/day
- Tocotrienols: 50-100 mg/day

**Food sources:**
- Wheat germ oil
- Sunflower seeds
- Almonds
- Spinach
- Avocado

**Caution:**
- High dose (>1000 IU) may increase bleeding risk
- Monitor if on anticoagulants

---

## 6. CONNECTED BIOMARKERS

| Biomarker | Relationship | Pattern |
|-----------|--------------|---------|
| **Oxidized LDL** | Protection | High oxLDL = low E |
| **F2-isoprostanes** | Oxidation marker | Elevated if E low |
| **Vitamin C** | Regeneration | Both needed |
| **Selenium** | GPx enzyme | Protects E |

### Clinical Patterns

**Oxidative Stress:**
- Vitamin E: Low
- Oxidized LDL: High
- F2-isoprostanes: High
- **Antioxidant depletion**

**Fat Malabsorption:**
- Vitamin E: Low
- Vitamin A: Low
- Vitamin D: Low
- **All fat-soluble vitamins low**
