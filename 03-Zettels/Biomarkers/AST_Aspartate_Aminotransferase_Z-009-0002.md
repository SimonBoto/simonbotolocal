# AST — Aspartate Aminotransferase

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**AST:** Aspartate Aminotransferase

**Formerly:** SGOT (Serum Glutamic-Oxaloacetic Transaminase)

**Enzyme class:** Aminotransferase (transaminase)

**Discovered:** 1950s — recognition as diagnostic enzyme

---

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**Intracellular enzyme:**
- Catalyzes: Aspartate + α-ketoglutarate ↔ Oxaloacetate + Glutamate
- Role in amino acid metabolism and urea cycle
- Found in mitochondria and cytoplasm

**Tissue distribution (widespread):**
| Tissue | Relative Amount |
|--------|-----------------|
| **Liver** | High |
| **Heart** | High |
| **Skeletal muscle** | High |
| **Kidney** | Moderate |
| **Brain** | Moderate |
| **Red blood cells** | Present |

### Release into Blood

**Cell injury (any tissue):**
- Mitochondrial and cytoplasmic release
- Elevates with damage to any AST-rich tissue
- **Less specific to liver than ALT**

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### AST Levels

| Level (U/L) | Status | Interpretation |
|-------------|--------|----------------|
| **<20 (women), <30 (men)** | Optimal | Minimal tissue stress |
| **<40** | Normal | Standard reference |
| **40-80** | Mild elevation | Fatty liver, mild injury |
| **80-200** | Moderate elevation | Hepatitis, muscle injury |
| **200-1000** | High elevation | Acute hepatitis, MI, rhabdomyolysis |
| **>1000** | Very high | Severe tissue necrosis |

### The AST:ALT Ratio

**Critical diagnostic tool:**

| Ratio | Interpretation |
|-------|---------------|
| **<1** | Typical of viral hepatitis, fatty liver |
| **1-2** | Nonspecific liver disease |
| **>2** | Suggests alcoholic liver disease |
| **>3** | Highly suggestive of alcohol |

**Why ratio matters:**
- Alcohol damages mitochondria → releases mitochondrial AST
- ALT is purely cytoplasmic
- AST elevation out of proportion to ALT suggests alcohol

### Causes of Elevation

**Liver causes:**
- Fatty liver
- Alcohol
- Viral hepatitis
- Drug-induced injury
- Ischemic hepatitis

**Non-liver causes (important):**
| Cause | Mechanism |
|-------|-----------|
| **Myocardial infarction** | Cardiac muscle release |
| **Rhabdomyolysis** | Skeletal muscle breakdown |
| **Intense exercise** | Muscle release |
| **Hemolysis** | RBC release |
| **Muscle injury** | Trauma, surgery |

## 4. COFACTORS

### Factors Affecting AST

| Factor | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| **Vitamin B6** | Cofactor | Deficiency lowers AST |
| **Exercise** | Significant increase | Muscle release |
| **Hemolysis** | Falsely elevates | RBC AST |
| **Muscle mass** | Higher baseline | More muscle = more AST |

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### For Elevated AST

**Identify tissue source:**

**Liver source (with elevated ALT):**
- Same as ALT management
- Weight loss, alcohol reduction, medication review

**Cardiac source (with cardiac symptoms):**
- Troponins more specific
- Cardiac workup

**Muscle source (with CK elevation):**
- Rest
- Hydration
- Avoid strenuous exercise before testing

### Monitoring

**Recheck:** After addressing cause
**Target:** Normalization

## 6. CONNECTED BIOMARKERS

| Biomarker | Relationship | Pattern |
|-----------|--------------|---------|
| **ALT** | Paired liver enzyme | AST:ALT ratio diagnostic |
| **CK** | Muscle enzyme | Elevated CK + elevated AST = muscle source |
| **Troponin** | Cardiac marker | Elevated troponin + AST = cardiac source |
| **LDH** | Tissue injury | Nonspecific, supports tissue damage |

### Clinical Patterns

**Alcoholic Liver Disease:**
- AST: Elevated
- ALT: Elevated (less than AST)
- **AST:ALT ratio >2**
- GGT: Elevated
- MCV: Elevated

**Viral Hepatitis:**
- AST: Elevated
- ALT: Elevated (higher than AST)
- **AST:ALT ratio <1**

**Myocardial Infarction:**
- AST: Elevates 8-12 hours post-MI
- Troponin: More specific, elevates earlier
- CK-MB: Cardiac specific

**Rhabdomyolysis:**
- CK: Very high (>5x ULN, often >1000)
- AST: Elevated proportionally
- ALT: Normal or mildly elevated
- **CK >> AST suggests muscle source**

## 10. CLINICAL WEIGHT

**Significance: ⭐⭐⭐ (3/5 — Moderate)**

**Less specific than ALT for liver**

**Best for:**
- AST:ALT ratio (alcoholic liver disease)
- Tissue injury assessment (with CK, troponin)
- Not as standalone liver marker

**Always with:**
- ALT (for ratio)
- CK (to rule out muscle)
- Troponin (if cardiac suspected)
