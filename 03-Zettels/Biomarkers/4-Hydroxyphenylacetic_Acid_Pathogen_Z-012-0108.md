# 4-Hydroxyphenylacetic Acid — Pathogen & Anaerobe Marker

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**4-Hydroxyphenylacetic acid:** Phenylacetic acid with hydroxy group at position 4

**Also called:**
- 4-HPAA
- p-Hydroxyphenylacetic acid

source-litnote: Synthesized from clinical practice
---

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**Tyrosine metabolite:**
- Tyrosine → 4-hydroxyphenylpyruvate → 4-hydroxyphenylacetate
- Gut bacteria produce from tyrosine

**Pathogen marker:**
- Produced by certain pathogenic bacteria
- Indicates anaerobic bacterial overgrowth
- **Marker of gut dysbiosis**

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### 4-HPAA Levels

| Level | Status | Interpretation |
|-------|--------|----------------|
| **Normal** | Low | Balanced gut flora |
| **Elevated** | High | Pathogen overgrowth |

### Causes of Elevation

**Gut dysbiosis:**
| Organism | Association |
|----------|-------------|
| **Clostridia species** | Common producers |
| **Anaerobic bacteria** | Overgrowth |
| **Pathogenic strains** | Dysbiosis marker |

**Dietary factors:**
- High tyrosine intake
- Protein malabsorption

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### For Elevated 4-HPAA

**Gut restoration:**
- Probiotics (Lactobacillus, Bifidobacterium)
- Prebiotics (inulin, FOS, GOS)
- Fermented foods

**Antimicrobials (if severe dysbiosis):**
- Herbal antimicrobials (oregano, berberine)
- Rifaximin (prescription)

**Dietary:**
- Reduce excessive protein if malabsorption
- Ensure adequate stomach acid
