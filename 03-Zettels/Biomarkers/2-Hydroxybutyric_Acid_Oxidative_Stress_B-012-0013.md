## 2. PHYSIOLOGY & FUNCTION

### 2.1 Normal Physiological Role

**Primary Function:**
Metabolic intermediate in methionine and threonine degradation pathways

**Formation Pathways:**
```
Methionine → 2-ketobutyrate → 2-HB
Threonine → 2-ketobutyrate → 2-HB
```

**Clinical Significance:**
- Marker of oxidative stress
- Indicator of glutathione synthesis demand
- Associated with insulin resistance

### 2.2 Regulation

**Homeostatic Control:**
- Driven by methionine cycle activity
- Increased with glutathione demand
- Elevated in oxidative stress states

## 4. CLINICAL INTERPRETATION

### 4.1 Elevated Levels

**Mild to Moderate Elevation:**
- **Common causes:** Oxidative stress, early insulin resistance
- **Clinical significance:** Early warning marker
- **Next steps:** Antioxidant support, metabolic evaluation

**Significant Elevation:**
- **Common causes:** High oxidative stress, significant IR
- **Clinical significance:** Active glutathione depletion
- **Next steps:** Comprehensive antioxidant protocol
