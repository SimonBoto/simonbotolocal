# 3-Hydroxybutyric Acid — Ketone Body, Fat Oxidation Marker

| **Specimen** | Serum (primary), Urine (OAT) |
| **Collection Requirements** | Fasting preferred for baseline |

### 1.2 Biochemistry

**Molecular Structure:**
- Formula: C₄H₈O₃
- Reduced form of acetoacetate (not technically a ketone)
- 70% of circulating ketones

**Physical Properties:**
- Solubility: Water soluble
- Stable in serum

---

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY & FUNCTION

### 2.1 Normal Physiological Role

**Primary Ketone Body:**
- Alternative fuel for brain, heart, muscle
- Spares glucose during fasting/starvation
- Neuroprotective effects

**Formation:**
```
Acetoacetate + NADH → BHB (via BHB dehydrogenase)
```

### 2.2 Ketogenesis Triggers

| Condition | Mechanism |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Fasting** | Glycogen depletion |
| **Low-carbohydrate diet** | Insufficient glucose |
| **Prolonged exercise** | Glycogen depletion |
| **Diabetic ketoacidosis** | Insulin deficiency |

### 2.3 Utilization

- Converted to acetoacetate → acetyl-CoA
- Enters Krebs cycle
- Brain fuel during starvation

## 3. REFERENCE RANGES

### 3.1 Serum BHB (mmol/L)

| Population | Lower Limit | Upper Limit | Units |
|------------|-------------|-------------|-------|
| Fed adults | 0.02 | 0.20 | mmol/L |
| After 12h fast | 0.10 | 0.50 | mmol/L |
| After 24h fast | 0.50 | 3.00 | mmol/L |

### 3.2 Clinical Interpretation Ranges

| Level (mmol/L) | Status | Clinical Significance |
|----------------|--------|----------------------|
| **<0.5** | Normal fed | Glucose sufficient |
| **0.5-3.0** | Nutritional ketosis | Low-carb, fasting, therapeutic |
| **3.0-5.0** | High ketosis | Extended fast, strict keto |
| **>5.0** | Very high | Monitor for acidosis |
| **>10.0 + pH<7.3** | DKA | Medical emergency |

### 3.3 Factors Affecting Levels

**Physiological:**
- Fasting duration: Increases
- Carbohydrate intake: Decreases
- Exercise: May increase

**Pathological:**
- Insulin deficiency (DKA): Markedly elevated
- Alcoholism: May elevate

---

## 4. CLINICAL INTERPRETATION

### 4.1 Elevated Levels

**Nutritional Ketosis (0.5-3.0 mmol/L):**
- **Common causes:** Ketogenic diet, intermittent fasting
- **Clinical significance:** Normal physiological adaptation
- **Next steps:** Monitor, ensure adequate nutrition

**Starvation Ketosis:**
- **Common causes:** Extended fasting, malnutrition
- **Clinical significance:** Adaptive response
- **Next steps:** Nutritional support

**Diabetic Ketoacidosis (>5.0 mmol/L with acidosis):**
- **Common causes:** Insulin deficiency (Type 1 DM)
- **Clinical significance:** Medical emergency
- **Next steps:** IV insulin, fluids, electrolytes

### 4.2 Pattern Recognition

| Pattern | Associated Findings | Interpretation |
|---------|---------------------|----------------|
| Nutritional Ketosis | BHB 0.5-3.0, Glucose 70-90, pH normal | Desired on keto diet |
| DKA | BHB >5.0, Glucose >250, pH <7.3 | Emergency |
| Starvation | BHB 1-3, Glucose low-normal, pH normal | Adaptive |

## 6. INTERVENTIONS

### For Low BHB (Poor Ketogenesis)

**Support Fat Oxidation:**
- Carnitine: 1-3 g/day
- B2, B3, B5
- CoQ10
- MCT oil for rapid ketones

### For High BHB (DKA)

**Medical Emergency:**
- IV insulin
- IV fluids
- Electrolyte replacement
- Bicarbonate if pH <7.0
