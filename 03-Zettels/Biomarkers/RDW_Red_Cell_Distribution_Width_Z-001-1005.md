# RDW — Red Cell Distribution Width

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**RDW:** Red Cell Distribution Width

**Distribution width:** Measure of variation in RBC size

**Also indicates:**
- Anisocytosis (variation in cell size)

source-litnote: Synthesized from clinical practice
---

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**Coefficient of variation of RBC volume:**
- Measures heterogeneity in RBC size
- Normal: <14.5%

**Clinical utility:**
- Early indicator of iron deficiency (before anemia)
- Distinguishes mixed deficiencies
- Prognostic marker in various conditions

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### RDW Levels

| Level (%) | Status | Interpretation |
|-----------|--------|----------------|
| **<14.5** | Normal | Uniform RBC size |
| **14.5-17** | Borderline | Mild variation |
| **>17** | High | Significant anisocytosis |

### Causes of Elevated RDW

**Nutritional deficiencies:**
| Cause | Mechanism |
|-------|-----------|
| **Iron deficiency** | Early sign; mix of normal and microcytic cells |
| **B12 deficiency** | Mix of normal and macrocytic cells |
| **Folate deficiency** | Mixed cell populations |
| **Combined deficiency** | Very high RDW |

**Other causes:**
- Hemolysis (reticulocytosis)
- Blood transfusion (mixed populations)
- Myelodysplastic syndrome
- Liver disease
- Thalassemia (usually normal RDW despite microcytosis)

### RDW-CV vs. RDW-SD

| Type | Measurement |
|------|-------------|
| **RDW-CV** | Coefficient of variation (%) |
| **RDW-SD** | Standard deviation (fL) |

**RDW-CV most commonly reported**

## 4. COFACTORS

### Factors Affecting RDW

| Factor | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| **Nutrient deficiency** | Increases (mixed cell populations) |
| **Hemolysis** | Increases (reticulocytes larger) |
| **Transfusion** | Increases (donor + recipient cells) |
| **Thalassemia** | Normal (uniformly microcytic) |

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### Elevated RDW

**Workup:**
- Iron studies
- B12, folate
- Reticulocyte count
- Hemolysis markers

**Clinical significance:**
- Early iron deficiency (RDW rises before Hb falls)
- Mixed deficiency states
- Response to treatment (RDW normalizes as cells become uniform)

## 6. CONNECTED BIOMARKERS

| Biomarker | Relationship | Pattern |
|-----------|--------------|---------|
| **MCV** | Size | High RDW + low MCV = iron deficiency; High RDW + high MCV = B12/folate |
| **Ferritin** | Iron stores | Low ferritin + high RDW = early iron deficiency |
| **Reticulocytes** | Young RBCs | High retics increase RDW |

### Clinical Patterns

**Early Iron Deficiency:**
- Hb: Normal
- MCV: Normal
- RDW: Elevated
- Ferritin: Low
- **RDW is first abnormal parameter**

**Combined Deficiency:**
- MCV: Normal (80-100)
- RDW: Very high (>17)
- **Iron + B12/folate deficiency**

**Thalassemia Trait:**
- MCV: Low
- RDW: Normal
- **Key distinguishing feature from iron deficiency**
