# Hemoglobin — Oxygen Transport Protein

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**Hemoglobin:** From Greek "haima" (blood) + Latin "globus" (sphere)

**Also called:**
- Hb, HGB
- Heme (iron-containing) + globin (protein)

**Discovery:** 1864 by Felix Hoppe-Seyler

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**Primary oxygen transport protein:**
- 4 heme groups (each with iron)
- Binds O2 in lungs, releases in tissues
- Also transports CO2 and nitric oxide

**Structure:**
- 2 alpha + 2 beta globin chains (adult HbA)
- Each chain has heme group with Fe2+
- Binds 4 O2 molecules total

**Production:**
- Bone marrow erythropoiesis
- Requires: Iron, B12, folate, protein
- Lifespan: ~120 days

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### Hemoglobin Levels

| Population | Low | Normal | Optimal | High |
|------------|-----|--------|---------|------|
| **Men** | <13.5 | 13.5-17.5 | 14-16 | >17.5 |
| **Women** | <12.0 | 12.0-16.0 | 12.5-14.5 | >16.0 |

### Clinical Significance

**Low Hemoglobin (Anemia):**
| Severity | Hb (g/dL) | Symptoms |
|----------|-----------|----------|
| **Mild** | 10-12 (W) / 10-13.5 (M) | Fatigue, pallor |
| **Moderate** | 8-10 | Dyspnea, tachycardia |
| **Severe** | <8 | Chest pain, heart failure |

**Causes of Anemia:**
- **Iron deficiency** (most common)
- **B12/folate deficiency** (megaloblastic)
- **Chronic disease** (inflammation)
- **Hemolysis** (destruction)
- **Blood loss** (acute/chronic)
- **Bone marrow failure**

**High Hemoglobin (Polycythemia):**
- Dehydration (hemoconcentration)
- Chronic hypoxia (COPD, high altitude)
- Smoking
- Polycythemia vera
- Testosterone therapy

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### For Low Hemoglobin

**Identify cause first:**
- Iron studies (ferritin, TIBC)
- B12, folate
- Reticulocyte count
- Stool for occult blood

**Iron deficiency:**
- Ferrous sulfate: 65 mg elemental iron daily
- With vitamin C
- Empty stomach (if tolerated)
- Recheck in 8 weeks

**B12/folate deficiency:**
- B12: 1000 mcg/day or injections
- Folate: 400-800 mcg/day

**Dietary:**
- Iron-rich: Red meat, liver, spinach, lentils
- B12: Animal products only
- Folate: Leafy greens, legumes

### For High Hemoglobin

**Address cause:**
- Hydration if dehydrated
- Smoking cessation
- Evaluate for polycythemia vera if persistent
- Adjust testosterone dose if on TRT

## 6. CONNECTED BIOMARKERS

| Biomarker | Relationship | Pattern |
|-----------|--------------|---------|
| **Hematocrit** | Proportional | Hct ≈ 3× Hb |
| **MCV** | RBC size | Low MCV = iron deficiency; High MCV = B12/folate |
| **Ferritin** | Iron stores | Low ferritin = iron deficiency anemia |
| **B12/Folate** | DNA synthesis | Low = megaloblastic anemia |
| **Reticulocytes** | Production | Low = underproduction; High = hemolysis/loss |

### Clinical Patterns

**Iron Deficiency Anemia:**
- Hb: Low
- MCV: Low (<80)
- Ferritin: Low
- **Microcytic, hypochromic**

**B12/Folate Deficiency:**
- Hb: Low
- MCV: High (>100)
- B12/Folate: Low
- **Macrocytic anemia**

**Anemia of Chronic Disease:**
- Hb: Low
- MCV: Normal (80-100)
- Ferritin: Normal/high
- **Normocytic anemia**
