# 4-Hydroxyphenylpyruvic Acid — Oxidation & Tyrosine Metabolism Marker

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**4-Hydroxyphenylpyruvic acid:** Tyrosine metabolite with hydroxyphenyl group

**Also called:**
- 4-HPPA
- p-Hydroxyphenylpyruvate

source-litnote: Synthesized from clinical practice
---

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**Tyrosine metabolism intermediate:**
- Tyrosine → 4-hydroxyphenylpyruvate → homogentisate
- Enzyme: 4-hydroxyphenylpyruvate dioxygenase (HPPD)

**Oxidation sensitivity:**
- HPPD requires antioxidant protection
- **Elevated with oxidative stress or antioxidant deficiency**

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### 4-HPPA Levels

| Level | Status | Interpretation |
|-------|--------|----------------|
| **Normal** | Low | Normal metabolism |
| **Elevated** | High | Oxidative stress or cofactor deficiency |

### Causes of Elevation

**Antioxidant deficiency:**
| Nutrient | Role |
|----------|------|
| **Vitamin C** | Protects HPPD |
| **Vitamin E** | Protects HPPD |
| **B6** | Cofactor for related enzymes |

**Oxidative stress:**
- Damages HPPD enzyme
- Accumulation of substrate

**Genetic (tyrosinemia type III):**
- HPPD deficiency
- Severe elevation
- Rare disorder

## 4. COFACTORS

### HPPD Protection

| Nutrient | Role | Deficiency Effect |
|----------|------|-------------------|
| **Vitamin C** | Antioxidant | HPPD damage |
| **Vitamin E** | Antioxidant | HPPD damage |
| **B6** | Enzyme cofactor | Impaired metabolism |
| **Iron** | HPPD cofactor | Reduced activity |

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### For Elevated 4-HPPA

**Antioxidant support:**
- Vitamin C: 1-2 g/day
- Vitamin E: 400 IU/day
- Mixed tocopherols

**B6 support:**
- B6: 50-100 mg/day

**Dietary:**
- Fruits, vegetables (vitamin C)
- Nuts, seeds (vitamin E)
