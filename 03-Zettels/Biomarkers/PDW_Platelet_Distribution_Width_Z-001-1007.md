# PDW — Platelet Distribution Width

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**PDW:** Platelet Distribution Width

**Distribution width:** Measure of variation in platelet size

source-litnote: Synthesized from clinical practice
---

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**Coefficient of variation of platelet volume:**
- Measures heterogeneity in platelet size
- Normal: 9-14 fL

**Clinical utility:**
- Marker of platelet activation
- Prognostic in cardiovascular disease
- Reflects platelet turnover

source-litnote: Synthesized from clinical practice
---

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### PDW Levels

| Level (fL) | Status | Interpretation |
|------------|--------|----------------|
| **9-14** | Normal | Uniform platelet size |
| **>14** | Elevated | Increased variation |

### Causes of Elevated PDW

**Increased platelet turnover:**
| Cause | Mechanism |
|-------|-----------|
| **Immune thrombocytopenia (ITP)** | Young platelets released |
| **Recovery from thrombocytopenia** | Reactive thrombopoiesis |
| **Myeloproliferative disorders** | Dysplastic production |

**Platelet activation:**
- Cardiovascular disease
- Inflammation
- Infection

### Clinical Significance

**Elevated PDW associated with:**
- Increased cardiovascular risk
- Poor prognosis in coronary artery disease
- Active inflammation
- Platelet consumption disorders

## 4. COFACTORS

### Factors Affecting PDW

| Factor | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| **Platelet turnover** | High turnover → high PDW |
| **Platelet activation** | Activation changes morphology |
| **Inflammation** | Increases PDW |

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### Elevated PDW

**Clinical context:**
- Evaluate for cardiovascular risk
- Assess platelet function if indicated
- Consider anti-platelet therapy if high CV risk

## 6. CONNECTED BIOMARKERS

| Biomarker | Relationship | Pattern |
|-----------|--------------|---------|
| **MPV** | Mean platelet volume | Often elevated together |
| **Platelet count** | Number | Inverse relationship (high turnover) |

### Clinical Patterns

**ITP:**
- Platelets: Low
- MPV: High
- PDW: High
- **Young, heterogeneous platelets**

**Cardiovascular Risk:**
- PDW: Elevated
- Associated with plaque instability
