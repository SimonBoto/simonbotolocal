# Platelets (PLT) and PDW - B-001-0003

## 1. IDENTIFICATION

| Marker | Full Name | What It Measures |
|--------|-----------|------------------|
| **PLT** | Platelet Count | Number of thrombocytes per volume |
| **PDW** | Platelet Distribution Width | Variation in platelet size |
| **MPV** | Mean Platelet Volume | Average platelet size |

**Specimen:** Whole blood (EDTA)
**Collection:** No fasting required

## 2. REFERENCE RANGES (Simos Clinical Targets)

### Platelet Count (PLT)
| Range | Standard | Simos Optimal | Clinical Significance |
|-------|----------|---------------|----------------------|
| **Thrombocytopenia** | <150,000 | <150,000 | Bleeding risk |
| **Low-normal** | 150-200k | 150-200k | Monitor |
| **Optimal** | 200-400k | **200-350k** | **Ideal hemostasis** |
| **Elevated** | 400-450k | **>350k** | **Inflammation or iron loss** |
| **Thrombocytosis** | >450,000 | >400,000 | Hypercoagulable risk |

**Simos Rules:**
- **Optimal PLT: 200-350k**
- **>350k:** Check for inflammation (CRP) or iron deficiency (ferritin)
- **<200k:** Connected to worse health outcomes, investigate cause

**Simos Clinical Interpretation:**
- **PLT 200-350k:** Optimal range
- **PLT >350k:** Indicates inflammation (check CRP) or iron deficiency/loss (check ferritin, transferrin)
- **PLT <200k:** Associated with worse health outcomes, requires investigation

### PDW (Platelet Distribution Width)
| Range | Interpretation |
|-------|----------------|
| **<15%** | Normal, uniform platelet size |
| **15-17%** | Mild variation |
| **>17%** | High variation (mixed populations) |

**Clinical:** High PDW = activated platelets, platelet turnover

### MPV (Mean Platelet Volume)
| Range | Interpretation |
|-------|----------------|
| **<7 fL** | Small platelets |
| **7-11 fL** | Normal |
| **>11 fL** | Large platelets (young, active) |

**Clinical:** High MPV = increased platelet activity, cardiovascular risk

## 3. PLATELET FUNCTION & PHYSIOLOGY

**Primary Functions:**
1. **Hemostasis:** Primary clot formation (platelet plug)
2. **Coagulation cascade activation:** Provide phospholipid surface
3. **Inflammation:** Release cytokines, recruit immune cells
4. **Tissue repair:** Release growth factors (PDGF, TGF-β)
5. **Antimicrobial defense:** NET formation, direct pathogen killing

**Production (Thrombopoiesis):**
- Site: Bone marrow megakaryocytes
- Regulation: Thrombopoietin (TPO)
- Lifespan: 7-10 days
- 1/3 sequestered in spleen

## 4. CLINICAL INTERPRETATION

### Thrombocytopenia (Low Platelets) <150,000

**Severity:**
| Count | Risk Level | Clinical |
|-------|-----------|----------|
| 100-150k | Mild | Usually asymptomatic |
| 50-100k | Moderate | Risk with surgery/trauma |
| 20-50k | Severe | Spontaneous bleeding risk |
| <20k | Critical | High bleeding risk |

**Causes (Simos Workup):**

| Category | Causes | Workup |
|----------|--------|--------|
| **Production defect** | B12/folate deficiency, marrow failure, alcohol | B12, MMA, folate, marrow biopsy if persistent |
| **Destruction** | ITP, TTP, HUS, DIC, drugs | Peripheral smear, coagulation studies |
| **Sequestration** | Hypersplenism (cirrhosis) | Liver enzymes, ultrasound |
| **Dilution** | Massive transfusion | History |
| **Consumption** | Major bleeding, surgery | Clinical context |

**Key Patterns:**
- ↓ PLT + ↑ MPV = Destruction (ITP, young platelets)
- ↓ PLT + ↓ MPV = Bone marrow failure
- ↓ PLT + splenomegaly = Hypersplenism

### Thrombocytosis (High Platelets) >450,000

**Reactive (Secondary):**
| Cause | Mechanism | Associated Findings |
|-------|-----------|---------------------|
| **Iron deficiency** | EPO effect on megakaryocytes | ↓ Ferritin, ↑ TIBC |
| **Inflammation/infection** | IL-6 stimulates TPO | ↑ CRP, ↑ ESR |
| **Malignancy** | Paraneoplastic | Weight loss, night sweats |
| **Postsplenectomy** | Loss of sequestration | History |
| **Exercise** | Acute demargination | Transient |

**Primary (Myeloproliferative):**
- Essential thrombocythemia
- Polycythemia vera
- Primary myelofibrosis
- CML

**Workup for persistent thrombocytosis:**
- JAK2 mutation (V617F)
- CALR mutation
- MPL mutation
- BCR-ABL (CML)
- Bone marrow biopsy

### PDW/MPV Interpretation

**High PDW (>17%):**
- Indicates platelet activation
- Mixed population (young + old platelets)
- Cardiovascular risk marker
- Inflammation

**High MPV (>11 fL):**
- Young, active platelets
- Increased reactivity
- Cardiovascular risk
- Response to bleeding (compensatory)

**Low MPV (<7 fL):**
- Old, inactive platelets
- Bone marrow suppression
- Wiskott-Aldrich syndrome

## 5. CARDIOVASCULAR RISK

**Platelets as CV Risk Marker:**
| Finding | CV Risk | Mechanism |
|---------|---------|-----------|
| PLT >400k | ↑↑ | Hypercoagulability |
| MPV >11 fL | ↑↑ | Large, reactive platelets |
| PDW >17% | ↑ | Platelet activation |
| PLT <150k | Variable | Bleeding vs thrombosis paradox |

**Platelet Function in Atherosclerosis:**
- Adhere to damaged endothelium
- Release pro-inflammatory cytokines
- Promote smooth muscle proliferation
- Form thrombus on ruptured plaque

## 6. NUTRITIONAL CONNECTIONS

### Deficiency States Affecting Platelets
| Nutrient | Role | Deficiency Effect |
|----------|------|-------------------|
| **B12** | DNA synthesis | Thrombocytopenia |
| **Folate** | DNA synthesis | Thrombocytopenia |
| **Iron** | Heme synthesis | Reactive thrombocytosis |
| **Vitamin K** | Coagulation factors | Bleeding (not platelet count) |

### Supplements Affecting Platelet Function
| Supplement | Effect | Clinical Note |
|------------|--------|---------------|
| **Omega-3 (EPA/DHA)** | ↓ Platelet aggregation | Antithrombotic |
| **Curcumin** | ↓ Platelet activation | Caution with anticoagulants |
| **Garlic** | ↓ Platelet aggregation | Caution with anticoagulants |
| **Ginkgo** | ↓ Platelet aggregation | Caution with anticoagulants |
| **Vitamin E** | ↓ Platelet aggregation | High doses only |
| **Papaya leaf extract** | ↑ Platelet count | Dengue, ITP (traditional) |

## 7. SUPPLEMENT INTERVENTIONS

### For High Platelets (>400k)
| Goal | Supplement | Dose | Mechanism |
|------|------------|------|-----------|
| Reduce aggregation | Omega-3 | 2-4 g EPA+DHA | ↓ TXA2, anti-inflammatory |
| Reduce activation | Curcumin | 500-1000 mg | ↓ Platelet activation |
| Improve flow | Garlic | 600-1200 mg | ↓ Aggregation |

**Caution:** If on anticoagulants (warfarin, apixaban), monitor closely

### For Low Platelets (<150k)
| Cause | Supplement | Dose |
|-------|------------|------|
| B12 deficiency | Methylcobalamin | 1000 mcg |
| Folate deficiency | Methylfolate | 800-1600 mcg |
| Functional support | Vitamin C | 1-2 g |
| Traditional | Papaya leaf | Per product |

**Note:** Supplements help nutritional causes, not ITP or marrow failure

## 8. RED FLAGS

🚩 **Critical Values:**
- PLT <20,000 (spontaneous bleeding risk)
- PLT >1,000,000 (thrombosis risk, check for ET)
- PLT <50,000 with active bleeding
- Acute drop >50% (DIC, TTP, HUS)

⚠️ **Urgent Workup:**
- Unexplained thrombocytopenia
- Thrombocytosis >600k
- PLT abnormalities with bleeding/clotting

## 9. PATTERN RECOGNITION

| Pattern | Indicates | Action |
|---------|-----------|--------|
| ↑ PLT + ↓ Ferritin | Iron deficiency | Iron workup |
| ↑ PLT + ↑ CRP | Inflammation | Find source |
| ↓ PLT + ↑ MPV | Destruction (ITP) | Hematology referral |
| ↓ PLT + ↓ MPV | Marrow failure | Marrow biopsy |
| ↑ PLT + splenectomy history | Reactive, expected | Monitor |
| ↑ PLT + JAK2+ | Essential thrombocythemia | Hematology management |
