# Cystatin C — Superior Kidney Function Marker

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**Cystatin C:** Cysteine protease inhibitor

**Also called:**
- Cys-C
- Gamma trace
- Post-gamma-globulin

**Discovered:** 1961 as trace protein in urine

---

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**Protease inhibitor:**
- Inhibits cysteine proteases
- Produced by all nucleated cells at constant rate
- Freely filtered by glomerulus
- Not secreted or reabsorbed by tubules
- **Ideal GFR marker**

**Advantages over creatinine:**
| Feature | Creatinine | Cystatin C |
|---------|------------|------------|
| **Production** | Muscle-dependent | Constant (all cells) |
| **Diet** | Affected by meat | Independent |
| **Age** | Decreases with age | Stable |
| **Sex** | Higher in men | Same |
| **Race** | Different | Same |
| **Body composition** | Affected | Independent |

---

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### Cystatin C Levels

| Level (mg/L) | eGFR (mL/min) | Status | CKD Stage |
|--------------|---------------|--------|-----------|
| **<0.8** | >90 | Normal | None |
| **0.8-1.0** | 60-90 | Mild decrease | 2 |
| **1.0-1.3** | 45-60 | Moderate | 3a |
| **1.3-1.8** | 30-45 | Severe | 3b |
| **>1.8** | <30 | Very severe | 4-5 |

### Clinical Significance

**Superior GFR estimation:**
- More accurate than creatinine-based eGFR
- Especially in:
  - Elderly
  - Extreme body composition
  - Liver disease
  - Malnutrition

**Conditions affecting creatinine but not cystatin C:**
- High meat intake
- Creatine supplementation
- Muscle wasting
- Amputations
- Extreme obesity/cachexia

---

## 4. COFACTORS

### Factors Affecting Cystatin C

| Factor | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| **Thyroid disease** | Hypothyroidism increases Cys-C |
| **Corticosteroids** | May increase Cys-C |
| **Inflammation** | Minimal effect |
| **Smoking** | May slightly increase |

---

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### For Elevated Cystatin C

**Same as CKD management:**
- Blood pressure control (<130/80)
- ACE inhibitors/ARBs
- Diabetes control
- Reduce proteinuria
- Avoid nephrotoxins

**Monitoring:**
- More accurate trend than creatinine
- Use for drug dosing adjustments

---

## 6. CONNECTED BIOMARKERS

| Biomarker | Relationship | Pattern |
|-----------|--------------|---------|
| **Creatinine** | Alternative marker | May be discordant |
| **eGFR** | Calculated | Cys-C eGFR more accurate |
| **BUN** | Kidney function | Elevated together |
| **Urine albumin** | Kidney damage | Combined assessment |

### Clinical Patterns

**True Kidney Dysfunction:**
- Cystatin C: Elevated
- Creatinine: Elevated
- eGFR: Reduced
- **Confirmed CKD**

**Creatinine Misleading:**
- Cystatin C: Normal
- Creatinine: Elevated (muscular, meat intake)
- **False positive with creatinine**

**Early CKD:**
- Cystatin C: Elevated
- Creatinine: Normal
- **Earlier detection with Cys-C**
