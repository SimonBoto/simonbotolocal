# Total Cholesterol — Complete Lipid Pool

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**Cholesterol:** From Greek "chole" (bile) + "stereos" (solid) + "-ol" (alcohol)

**Named for:** First isolated from gallstones (solid in bile)

**Discovery:** 1815 by Michel Eugène Chevreul

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**Essential for life:**
| Function | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Cell membranes** | Structural component, fluidity |
| **Steroid hormones** | Precursor ([[Cortisol_Primary_Stress_Hormone_Z-011-0005]|cortisol], aldosterone, [[Testosterone_Primary_Male_Androgen_Z-011-0001]|sex hormones]) |
| **[[Vitamin_D_Calciferol_Steroid_Hormone_Z-007-0001]|Vitamin D]** | Synthesis from 7-dehydrocholesterol |
| **Bile acids** | Synthesis in liver |
| **Myelin** | Nerve insulation |

### Sources

**Endogenous:**
- Liver synthesis: ~80% of total
- All cells can synthesize
- HMG-CoA reductase rate-limiting

**Exogenous:**
- Dietary intake: ~20%
- Animal products only

### Transport

- Lipid-insoluble → requires lipoproteins
- VLDL → LDL → HDL
- Total = HDL + LDL + VLDL + IDL + chylomicrons

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### Total Cholesterol Levels

| Level (mg/dL) | Status | Interpretation |
|---------------|--------|----------------|
| **<150** | Low | May be concerning (hemorrhagic stroke risk) |
| **150-200** | Optimal | Ideal range |
| **200-240** | Borderline | Moderate risk |
| **240-280** | High | High risk |
| **>280** | Very high | Very high risk |

### Clinical Significance

**Elevated total cholesterol:**
- Cardiovascular disease risk factor
- Atherosclerosis progression
- But: must consider fractions (LDL, HDL)

**Low total cholesterol:**
- Malnutrition
- Hyperthyroidism
- Liver disease
- Malabsorption
- Rarely: genetic disorders

**Limitations:**
- Less predictive than LDL or ApoB
- Can be misleading (high HDL raises total)
- **Always interpret with lipid fractions**

## 4. COFACTORS

### Cholesterol Metabolism

| Factor | Role | Effect |
|--------|------|--------|
| **Saturated fat** | Increases synthesis | Raises cholesterol |
| **Dietary cholesterol** | Modest effect | Modest raise |
| **Fiber** | Reduces absorption | Lowers cholesterol |
| **Thyroid** | Regulates metabolism | Hypo → high; Hyper → low |
| **Insulin** | Stimulates synthesis | High insulin → high cholesterol |
| **Genetics** | LDL receptor function | Familial hypercholesterolemia |

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### For High Total Cholesterol

**Lifestyle:**
- Reduce saturated fat (<7% calories)
- Increase fiber (25-30g/day)
- Exercise regularly
- Weight loss
- Limit refined carbohydrates

**Medications:**
- Statins (first line)
- Ezetimibe
- PCSK9 inhibitors
- Bile acid sequestrants

### For Low Total Cholesterol

**Evaluate cause:**
- Thyroid function
- Liver function
- Nutritional status
- Malabsorption

## 6. CONNECTED BIOMARKERS

| Biomarker | Relationship | Pattern |
|-----------|--------------|---------|
| **LDL** | Major component | Usually correlates |
| **HDL** | Protective fraction | High HDL raises total |
| **Triglycerides** | VLDL component | Elevated TG raises total |
| **ApoB** | Particle count | Better predictor than total |
| **Non-HDL-C** | Atherogenic pool | Total - HDL |

### Clinical Patterns

**High Total, High LDL, Low HDL:**
- High cardiovascular risk
- Aggressive treatment indicated

**High Total, High HDL, Normal LDL:**
- Lower risk than appears
- "Good" high cholesterol

**Low Total, Low LDL, Low HDL:**
- Concerning pattern
- Evaluate for secondary causes
