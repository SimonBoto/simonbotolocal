# CRP (C-Reactive Protein) - B-002-0001

## 1. IDENTIFICATION

| Marker | Full Name | What It Measures |
|--------|-----------|------------------|
| **CRP** | C-Reactive Protein | Acute phase reactant, inflammation |
| **hs-CRP** | High-sensitivity CRP | Lower range for CV risk assessment |

**Specimen:** Serum
**Collection:** No fasting required

## 2. SIMOS MINIMUM TARGET

| Range | Interpretation | Action |
|-------|----------------|--------|
| **<1.0 mg/L** | **MINIMUM - Low risk** | Maintain |
| **1.0-3.0** | Moderate risk | Address causes |
| **>3.0** | High risk | Active intervention |
| **>10** | Acute inflammation | Find source |

**Simos Rule: CRP should be <1.0 mg/L (minimum target)**

## 3. WHAT CRP TELLS US

**Acute Phase Reactant:**
- Produced by liver in response to IL-6
- Rises within 6-8 hours of inflammation
- Falls rapidly when inflammation resolves

**Sources of Elevation:**
- Infection (bacterial, viral)
- Chronic inflammation (obesity, metabolic syndrome)
- Tissue injury
- Autoimmune disease
- Cancer
- Smoking

**Cardiovascular Risk:**
- CRP >3 = 2x CV risk
- CRP >1 = subclinical inflammation
- Independent of cholesterol

## 4. INTERVENTIONS TO LOWER CRP

| Approach | Mechanism |
|----------|-----------|
| **Weight loss** | ↓ Adipose inflammation |
| **Omega-3 (2-4g)** | ↓ Pro-inflammatory cytokines |
| **Exercise** | ↓ Systemic inflammation |
| **Sleep optimization** | ↓ Inflammatory markers |
| **Curcumin** | ↓ NF-κB |
| **Vitamin D** | Immune modulation |
