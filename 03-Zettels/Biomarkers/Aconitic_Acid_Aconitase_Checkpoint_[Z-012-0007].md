# Aconitic Acid — Aconitase Checkpoint, Citric Acid Cycle

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**Aconitic acid:** First isolated from Aconitum napellus (monkshood) plant

**Aconitase:** Enzyme converting citrate to isocitrate via aconitate intermediate

source-litnote: Synthesized from clinical practice
---

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**Krebs cycle intermediate:**
- Citrate → **Cis-aconitate** → Isocitrate
- Catalyzed by aconitase enzyme
- Iron-sulfur cluster enzyme (requires Fe)

**Metabolic significance:**
- Isomerization step in TCA cycle
- Rate-limiting under some conditions
- Sensitive to oxidative stress

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### Aconitic Acid Levels

| Level | Status | Interpretation |
|-------|--------|----------------|
| **Normal** | Low | Properly converted to isocitrate |
| **Elevated** | High | Aconitase inhibition or overload |

### Causes of Elevation

**Aconitase inhibition:**
| Cause | Mechanism |
|-------|-----------|
| **Iron deficiency** | Aconitase requires Fe-S cluster |
| **Oxidative stress** | Damages Fe-S cluster |
| **Mitochondrial dysfunction** | Impaired enzyme activity |
| **Fluoride toxicity** | Inhibits aconitase |

**Metabolic overload:**
- High citrate production
- Impaired downstream metabolism

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### For Elevated Aconitic Acid

**Address iron status:**
- Check ferritin, iron panel
- Supplement if deficient

**Antioxidant support:**
- Protect Fe-S clusters
- Reduce oxidative stress

**Mitochondrial support:**
- B vitamins
- Magnesium
- CoQ10
