# Mitochondrial Dysfunction Pattern Recognition
## OAT Krebs Cycle Clinical Guide

---

## The Krebs Cycle as a Diagnostic Window

Unlike static lab markers, **Krebs cycle metabolites are dynamic** — they reveal real-time mitochondrial function. The pattern of elevations and depressions tells a story about:
- **Where** the bottleneck occurs
- **Why** it's happening (nutrient deficiency, toxin, hypoxia)
- **What** to do about it

---

## The Six Markers — Quick Reference

| Marker | Position | Key Enzyme | Critical Cofactors | Primary Interpretation |
|--------|----------|------------|-------------------|----------------------|
| **Citrate** | Entry | Citrate synthase | Magnesium | Energy surplus vs. entry block |
| **Isocitrate** | Early | Aconitase | Iron (Fe-S cluster) | Oxidative stress, iron status |
| **α-KG** | Nitrogen hub | α-KGDH | Thiamine, lipoic acid | Nitrogen metabolism, B1 status |
| **Succinate** | FADH2 point | SDH/Complex II | Iron, CoQ10 | Hypoxia, SDH mutations |
| **Fumarate** | Urea link | Fumarase | — | Urea cycle stress |
| **Malate** | Exit/Redox | Malate DH | Magnesium, NAD+ | Redox balance, shuttle function |

---

## Pattern Recognition Framework

### Pattern 1: The Upstream Block (Early Krebs)
**Signature:** Citrate ↑, Isocitrate ↑, α-KG ↑ (or normal), downstream normal/low

**Mechanism:** Bottleneck at aconitase or α-KGDH

**Common Causes:**
- Iron deficiency (aconitase)
- Oxidative stress (Fe-S cluster damage)
- Thiamine deficiency (α-KGDH)
- B-vitamin insufficiency generally

**Clinical Context:**
- Fatigue, exercise intolerance
- Brain fog
- Poor stress tolerance

**Priority Interventions:**
1. Iron studies (ferritin, TIBC, saturation)
2. B-complex with emphasis on B1, B2, B3, B5
3. Antioxidant support (glutathione, NAC, vitamin C/E)
4. CoQ10 for downstream support

---

### Pattern 2: The Full Krebs Slowdown
**Signature:** All markers elevated proportionally

**Mechanism:** Generalized mitochondrial dysfunction

**Common Causes:**
- CoQ10 deficiency
- Multiple B-vitamin deficiencies
- Environmental toxicity (mold, heavy metals, solvents)
- Chronic inflammation
- Mitochondrial DNA mutations
- Medication effects (statins, metformin in some)

**Clinical Context:**
- Severe fatigue
- Multi-system symptoms
- Exercise intolerance
- Cognitive impairment

**Priority Interventions:**
1. Comprehensive mitochondrial support (all cofactors)
2. Environmental assessment (mold, toxins)
3. Inflammation reduction
4. Consider mitochondrial DNA testing if severe

---

### Pattern 3: The Hypoxia Signature
**Signature:** Succinate ↑, Lactate ↑, Malate may be elevated

**Mechanism:** Oxygen limitation or pseudo-hypoxia

**Common Causes:**
- Sleep apnea
- Lung disease
- Heart failure
- Poor circulation
- SDH mutations (pseudo-hypoxia)

**Clinical Context:**
- Fatigue, especially morning
- Brain fog
- Exercise intolerance
- Cardiovascular symptoms

**Priority Interventions:**
1. Sleep study if snoring/fatigue
2. Cardiopulmonary evaluation
3. CoQ10 for SDH support
4. Address root cause (CPAP, cardiovascular support)

---

### Pattern 4: The Nitrogen Overload
**Signature:** α-KG ↓, Fumarate ↑, possibly ammonia elevated

**Mechanism:** Urea cycle overwhelmed

**Common Causes:**
- Very high protein intake
- Liver dysfunction
- Constipation (ammonia absorption)
- Urea cycle partial defects

**Clinical Context:**
- "Toxic" feeling
- Brain fog (ammonia neurotoxicity)
- Digestive issues

**Priority Interventions:**
1. Moderate protein intake (1.2-1.6g/kg for most)
2. Bowel regularity (magnesium, fiber)
3. Liver support
4. Consider α-KG supplementation

---

### Pattern 5: The Metabolic Inflexibility
**Signature:** Citrate ↑, Malate ↑, Lactate ↑, Pyruvate ↑, ketones ↓

**Mechanism:** Locked in glycolysis, can't access fat oxidation

**Common Causes:**
- Insulin resistance
- Chronic overfeeding
- Excessive carbohydrate intake
- Hypothyroidism
- Mitochondrial dysfunction preventing fat oxidation

**Clinical Context:**
- Weight loss resistance
- Post-meal fatigue
- Inability to fast
- Energy crashes

**Priority Interventions:**
1. Time-restricted eating (16:8 minimum)
2. Carbohydrate reduction/timing
3. Zone 2 training (fat oxidation)
4. Insulin sensitizers (berberine, metformin if appropriate)
5. Sleep optimization

---

### Pattern 6: The Depletion Pattern
**Signature:** Multiple Krebs markers low

**Mechanism:** Hypermetabolism or substrate limitation

**Common Causes:**
- Hyperthyroidism
- Fever, infection
- Excessive exercise/overtraining
- Starvation/malnutrition
- Malignancy

**Clinical Context:**
- Weight loss (unintentional)
- Heat intolerance
- Rapid heart rate
- Anxiety

**Priority Interventions:**
1. Address root cause (thyroid, infection, training volume)
2. Adequate nutrition
3. Mitochondrial support to meet demand
4. Rest and recovery

## The Marker Ratios — Advanced Interpretation

### Citrate:Isocitrate Ratio
| Ratio | Interpretation |
|-------|---------------|
| >2:1 | Possible aconitase impairment |
| <1:1 | Normal or accelerated aconitase |

### α-KG:Succinate Ratio
| Ratio | Interpretation |
|-------|---------------|
| >2:1 | α-KGDH block (thiamine, lipoic acid) |
| <1:1 | SDH impairment or hypoxia |

### Fumarate:Malate Ratio
| Ratio | Interpretation |
|-------|---------------|
| >1:1 | Fumarase impairment |
| <0.5:1 | Rapid malate conversion or MDH acceleration |

### Lactate:Pyruvate Ratio
| Ratio | Interpretation |
|-------|---------------|
| >20:1 | Hypoxia, severe mitochondrial dysfunction |
| 10-20:1 | Mild hypoxia, NADH excess |
| <10:1 | Normal or hypermetabolic |

## Clinical Decision Tree

```
START: Review all 6 Krebs markers

Q1: Are all markers elevated?
├── YES → Full Krebs Slowdown Pattern
│   └── Action: Comprehensive mitochondrial support, toxin screen
│
└── NO → Q2: Which markers are elevated?

Q2: Are citrate/isocitrate/α-KG elevated with normal/low downstream?
├── YES → Upstream Block Pattern
│   └── Action: Check iron, B-vitamins, oxidative stress
│
└── NO → Q3: Is succinate disproportionately elevated?

Q3: Succinate high with or without lactate elevation?
├── YES → Hypoxia/SDH Pattern
│   └── Action: Sleep study, cardiopulmonary eval, CoQ10
│
└── NO → Q4: Is α-KG low with fumarate high?

Q4: α-KG ↓ + Fumarate ↑ pattern?
├── YES → Nitrogen Overload Pattern
│   └── Action: Moderate protein, bowel regularity, liver support
│
└── NO → Q5: Are citrate/malate high with lactate/pyruvate?

Q5: Citrate ↑ Malate ↑ Lactate ↑ pattern?
├── YES → Metabolic Inflexibility Pattern
│   └── Action: TRE, carb reduction, Zone 2 training
│
└── NO → Q6: Are markers generally low?

Q6: Multiple markers low?
├── YES → Depletion Pattern
│   └── Action: Check thyroid, infection, training load, nutrition
│
└── NO → Mixed/Unclear Pattern
    └── Action: Context-dependent, consider retest
```

## Integration with Other Biomarkers

### Always Check With Elevated Krebs Markers
| Marker | Why It Matters |
|--------|---------------|
| **Ferritin/Iron studies** | Iron for aconitase, SDH |
| **B-vitamin markers** | Cofactors for multiple enzymes |
| **Lactate** | Hypoxia indicator, pairs with succinate |
| **Pyruvate** | Entry point assessment |
| **8-OHdG** | Oxidative stress confirmation |
| **Glutathione** | Antioxidant capacity |

### Cross-Reference Patterns
| Krebs Pattern + | Suggests |
|-----------------|----------|
| High CRP | Inflammation driving oxidative stress |
| High insulin | Metabolic inflexibility confirmed |
| Low T3 | Hypothyroid slowdown |
| High methylmalonate | B12 deficiency compounding |
| High FIGLU | Folate deficiency compounding |

## Intervention Hierarchy

### Step 1: Remove Obstacles
- Address sleep apnea
- Reduce toxin exposure
- Moderate protein if excessive
- Optimize sleep
- Manage stress

### Step 2: Provide Cofactors (Universal)
| Nutrient | Dose | Targets |
|----------|------|---------|
| Thiamine (B1) | 100-300mg | PDH, α-KGDH |
| Riboflavin (B2) | 100-400mg | FAD synthesis |
| Niacin (B3) | 50-100mg | NAD+ synthesis |
| Pantothenic acid (B5) | 100-500mg | CoA synthesis |
| Lipoic acid | 300-600mg | α-KGDH, antioxidant |
| Magnesium | 400-600mg | Multiple enzymes |
| CoQ10 (ubiquinol) | 200-400mg | ETC, SDH |

### Step 3: Targeted Support (Pattern-Specific)
| Pattern | Additional Interventions |
|---------|-------------------------|
| Upstream block + low iron | Iron repletion (if deficient) |
| Upstream block + oxidative stress | Glutathione, NAC, vitamin C/E |
| Hypoxia/SDH | Sleep study, CPAP if needed, cardiopulmonary eval |
| Nitrogen overload | α-KG 1-3g, ornithine, moderate protein |
| Metabolic inflexibility | TRE, carb timing, berberine, Zone 2 training |
| Depletion | Address root cause, adequate calories |

### Step 4: Advanced (When Needed)
- NAD+ precursors (NMN, NR)
- D-ribose for ATP substrate
- Ketogenic diet for metabolic reset
- Mitochondrial peptides (SS-31, MOTS-c — experimental)

## Retesting and Monitoring

### When to Retest
- **8-12 weeks** after starting interventions
- **Sooner** if symptoms worsen
- **Annually** for chronic conditions

### Expected Changes
| Intervention | Expected Timeline |
|--------------|-------------------|
| B-vitamins | 4-8 weeks |
| CoQ10 | 6-12 weeks |
| Iron (if deficient) | 8-12 weeks |
| Antioxidants | 4-12 weeks |
| Lifestyle (sleep, TRE, exercise) | 4-12 weeks |

### Success Criteria
- **25-50% reduction** in elevated markers
- **Symptom improvement** correlating with marker improvement
- **Sustained pattern** on repeat testing

---

## Special Populations

### Athletes
- Expect higher baseline due to training load
- Focus on recovery markers (sleep, HRV)
- Watch for overtraining pattern (depletion + elevated lactate)

### Elderly
- Reduced mitochondrial biogenesis
- Higher baseline oxidative stress
- More conservative dosing
- Emphasize CoQ10, B-vitamins

### Children
- Lower baseline values
- Genetic disorders more likely with severe elevations
- Pediatric metabolic referral for extreme patterns

### Pregnancy
- Increased metabolic demand
- Modified reference ranges
- Consult obstetrician before supplements

---

## Key Clinical Pearls

1. **Patterns > Individual Markers** — Always interpret the cluster
2. **Context is King** — Diet, exercise, sleep, stress all affect results
3. **Cofactors First** — Most mitochondrial issues respond to B-vitamins + CoQ10
4. **Iron Caution** — Check status before supplementing; inflammation can mimic deficiency
5. **Retest to Verify** — Single snapshot may miss dynamic issues
6. **Symptom Correlation** — Markers should match clinical picture
7. **The Krebs is a Cycle** — Block anywhere affects everywhere
