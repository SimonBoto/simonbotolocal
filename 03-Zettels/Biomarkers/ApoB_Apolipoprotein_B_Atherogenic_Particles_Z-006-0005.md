# ApoB — Apolipoprotein B, Atherogenic Particle Count

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**ApoB:** Apolipoprotein B

**Apolipoprotein:** Protein component of lipoproteins

**Two forms:**
- **ApoB-48:** Intestinal chylomicrons
- **ApoB-100:** Liver-derived VLDL, IDL, LDL

source-litnote: Synthesized from clinical practice
---

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**One ApoB per atherogenic particle:**
- VLDL, IDL, LDL each contain one ApoB molecule
- **ApoB = particle number**

**Why better than LDL-C:**
- LDL-C measures cholesterol mass
- ApoB measures particle count
- Small dense LDL = more particles, same cholesterol
- **Particle number drives atherosclerosis**

### Atherogenic Particles

| Lipoprotein | ApoB Content | Atherogenicity |
|-------------|--------------|----------------|
| **Chylomicrons** | ApoB-48 | Low (rapidly cleared) |
| **VLDL** | ApoB-100 | Moderate |
| **IDL** | ApoB-100 | High |
| **[[LDL_Cholesterol_Low-Density_Lipoprotein_Z-006-0001]|LDL]** | ApoB-100 | High |
| **[[Lp-a_Lipoprotein-a_Genetic_CV_Risk_Z-006-0006]|Lp(a)]** | ApoB-100 | Very high |

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### ApoB Levels

| Level (mg/dL) | Status | Risk |
|---------------|--------|------|
| **<60** | Optimal | Very low |
| **60-80** | Good | Low |
| **80-100** | Borderline | Moderate |
| **100-120** | High | High |
| **>120** | Very high | Very high |

### Clinical Significance

**Superior to LDL-C for risk prediction:**
- Better predictor of cardiovascular events
- Accounts for small dense LDL
- Reflects total atherogenic burden

**Discordance with LDL-C:**
| Scenario | LDL-C | ApoB | Interpretation |
|----------|-------|------|----------------|
| **Pattern A** | Normal | Normal | Low risk |
| **Pattern B** | Normal | High | High risk (small dense LDL) |
| **Pattern C** | High | Normal | Lower risk than appears |

**Pattern B:** High ApoB with normal LDL-C = **hidden risk**

## 4. COFACTORS

### Factors Affecting ApoB

| Factor | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| **Insulin resistance** | Increases small dense LDL → high ApoB |
| **Saturated fat** | Increases production |
| **Carbohydrates** | High glycemic → increases VLDL/ApoB |
| **Genetics** | Familial hypercholesterolemia |
| **Hypothyroidism** | Increases ApoB |

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### For Elevated ApoB

**Lifestyle:**
- Reduce saturated fat
- Reduce refined carbohydrates
- Increase fiber
- Exercise
- Weight loss

**Medications:**
- Statins (reduce ApoB ~30-50%)
- Ezetimibe
- PCSK9 inhibitors (reduce ApoB ~50-60%)
- Fibrates (reduce VLDL-ApoB)

**Targets:**
| Risk Level | ApoB Target |
|------------|-------------|
| **Low risk** | <100 mg/dL |
| **Moderate risk** | <90 mg/dL |
| **High risk** | <80 mg/dL |
| **Very high risk** | <65 mg/dL |

## 6. CONNECTED BIOMARKERS

| Biomarker | Relationship | Pattern |
|-----------|--------------|---------|
| **LDL-C** | Cholesterol mass | ApoB better predictor |
| **LDL-P** | Particle number | Correlates with ApoB |
| **Non-HDL-C** | Atherogenic cholesterol | Similar information |
| **Triglycerides** | VLDL marker | High TG often → high ApoB |
| **Insulin** | Metabolic | High insulin → high ApoB |

### Clinical Patterns

**Insulin Resistance:**
- ApoB: Elevated
- LDL-C: Normal or mildly elevated
- Triglycerides: Elevated
- HDL: Low
- **Pattern B dyslipidemia**

**Familial Hypercholesterolemia:**
- ApoB: Very high
- LDL-C: Very high
- **Genetic LDL receptor defect**
