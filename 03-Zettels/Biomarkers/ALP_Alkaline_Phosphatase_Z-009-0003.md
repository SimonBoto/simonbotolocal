# ALP — Alkaline Phosphatase

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**ALP:** Alkaline Phosphatase

**"Alkaline":** Optimal activity at alkaline pH (9-10)

**"Phosphatase":** Removes phosphate groups from molecules

**Discovery:** Early 1900s — recognition in bone and liver

---

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**Enzyme activity:**
- Hydrolyzes phosphate esters at alkaline pH
- Role in:
  - Bone mineralization (osteoblasts)
  - Biliary excretion (hepatocytes)
  - Intestinal absorption
  - Placental function

**Tissue distribution:**
| Isoenzyme | Source | Clinical Significance |
|-----------|--------|----------------------|
| **Liver ALP** | Hepatocytes, bile ducts | Cholestasis marker |
| **Bone ALP** | Osteoblasts | Bone formation marker |
| **Intestinal ALP** | Intestinal epithelium | Dietary fat absorption |
| **Placental ALP** | Placenta | Pregnancy elevation |

### Normal Variation

**Physiologic elevations:**
- **Children/adolescents:** Higher (bone growth)
- **Pregnancy:** Elevated (placental)
- **Postprandial:** Elevated (intestinal)
- **Blood type O/B:** Higher (intestinal)

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### ALP Levels

| Level (U/L) | Status | Interpretation |
|-------------|--------|----------------|
| **<100** | Normal | Typical adult |
| **100-150** | Mild elevation | Physiologic or mild cholestasis |
| **150-300** | Moderate elevation | Cholestasis, bone disease |
| **300-1000** | High elevation | Biliary obstruction, Paget's disease |
| **>1000** | Very high | Severe cholestasis, metastatic bone disease |

### Causes of Elevation

**Hepatobiliary causes:**
| Cause | Mechanism |
|-------|-----------|
| **Biliary obstruction** | Regurgitation from bile ducts |
| **Primary biliary cholangitis** | Autoimmune bile duct injury |
| **Primary sclerosing cholangitis** | Bile duct inflammation/fibrosis |
| **Drug-induced cholestasis** | Medication effect |
| **Infiltrative diseases** | Liver metastases, granulomas |

**Bone causes:**
| Cause | Mechanism |
|-------|-----------|
| **Paget's disease** | Increased bone turnover |
| **Bone metastases** | Osteoblastic activity |
| **Healing fractures** | Bone formation |
| **Rickets/osteomalacia** | Attempted bone formation |
| **Hyperparathyroidism** | Increased bone turnover |

**Physiologic:**
- Growth (children)
- Pregnancy (2nd-3rd trimester)
- Postprandial (intestinal)
- Aging (slight increase)

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### For Elevated ALP

**Identify source:**
- GGT (if elevated = liver source)
- 5'-nucleotidase (liver specific)
- Bone ALP fraction
- Clinical context

**Liver source:**
- Address cholestasis cause
- Imaging (ultrasound, MRCP)
- Consider PBC/PSC workup

**Bone source:**
- Treat underlying bone disease
- Vitamin D if deficient
- Calcium if deficient

### Monitoring

**Recheck:** After addressing cause
**Target:** Normalization or <1.5x ULN

## 6. CONNECTED BIOMARKERS

| Biomarker | Relationship | Pattern |
|-----------|--------------|---------|
| **GGT** | Confirms liver source | Elevated GGT + elevated ALP = liver |
| **ALT/AST** | Hepatocellular enzymes | Normal ALT/AST + elevated ALP = cholestasis |
| **Bilirubin** | Excretory function | Elevated bilirubin + elevated ALP = obstruction |
| **5'-nucleotidase** | Liver specific | Elevated = confirms liver source |
| **Calcium/Phosphate** | Bone metabolism | Abnormal + elevated ALP = bone disease |

### Clinical Patterns

**Cholestatic Pattern:**
- ALP: Elevated
- GGT: Elevated
- ALT/AST: Normal or mildly elevated
- Bilirubin: May be elevated
- **Biliary obstruction or intrahepatic cholestasis**

**Hepatocellular Pattern:**
- ALP: Normal or mildly elevated
- ALT/AST: Elevated
- **Hepatocyte injury**

**Isolated ALP Elevation:**
- Consider bone source
- Check GGT
- If GGT normal → bone or physiologic
