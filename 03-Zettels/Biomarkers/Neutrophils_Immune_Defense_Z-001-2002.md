# Neutrophils — Immune Defense, Phagocytic Warriors

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**Neutrophil:** From Latin "neutral" (neutral staining) + Greek "philein" (to love)

**Named for:** Stains neutral (lilac) with Wright's stain

**Also called:**
- PMN (polymorphonuclear leukocytes)
- Segs (segmented neutrophils)
- Polys

---

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**Primary phagocytic cells:**
- 40-70% of WBCs
- First responders to bacterial infection
- Short lifespan (6-48 hours in circulation)

**Mechanisms:**
| Function | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Phagocytosis** | Engulf and destroy bacteria |
| **Degranulation** | Release antimicrobial enzymes |
| **NET formation** | Neutrophil extracellular traps |
| **Chemotaxis** | Migrate to infection site |

**Production:**
- Bone marrow (myelopoiesis)
- Stimulated by G-CSF, GM-CSF
- Storage pool in marrow (band cells)

---

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### Neutrophil Levels

| Level (%) | Absolute (K/μL) | Status | Interpretation |
|-----------|-----------------|--------|----------------|
| **40-60%** | **2.0-7.0** | Optimal | Normal defense |
| **<40%** | **<1.8** | Low | Neutropenia |
| **>70%** | **>7.0** | High | Neutrophilia |

### Causes of Elevated Neutrophils (Neutrophilia)

| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| **Bacterial infection** | Most common cause |
| **Inflammation** | Tissue injury, surgery |
| **Stress** | Exercise, corticosteroids |
| **Smoking** | Chronic elevation |
| **Myeloproliferative** | CML (very high) |

**Left shift:** Immature neutrophils (bands) in circulation
- Indicates active marrow response
- Seen in severe infection

### Causes of Low Neutrophils (Neutropenia)

| Severity | Absolute Count | Risk |
|----------|----------------|------|
| **Mild** | 1.0-1.5 K/μL | Moderate |
| **Moderate** | 0.5-1.0 K/μL | Significant |
| **Severe** | <0.5 K/μL | Life-threatening |

**Causes:**
- Viral infections
- Chemotherapy
- Autoimmune (lupus, Felty's)
- B12/folate deficiency
- Drug-induced

## 4. COFACTORS

### Neutrophil Production & Function

| Nutrient | Role | Deficiency Effect |
|----------|------|-------------------|
| **Copper** | Hematopoiesis | Neutropenia |
| **Zinc** | Immune function | Impaired phagocytosis |
| **Folate** | DNA synthesis | Neutropenia |
| **B12** | DNA synthesis | Neutropenia |
| **Vitamin C** | Immune function | Impaired function |
| **Selenium** | Antioxidant defense | Impaired function |

---

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### For Neutropenia

**Nutritional:**
- Copper: 1-2 mg/day (if deficient)
- Zinc: 15-30 mg/day
- Folate, B12 as needed

**Medical:**
- Treat underlying cause
- G-CSF (filgrastim) if severe
- Prophylactic antibiotics if <0.5 K/μL

**Neutropenic precautions:**
- Avoid raw foods
- Good hygiene
- Monitor for fever

---

## 6. CONNECTED BIOMARKERS

| Biomarker | Relationship | Pattern |
|-----------|--------------|---------|
| **WBC** | Total count | Neutrophils major component |
| **Bands** | Immature form | Left shift = active response |
| **CRP** | Inflammation | Elevated with neutrophilia |
| **Procalcitonin** | Bacterial infection | Elevated with bacterial neutrophilia |
| **Lymphocytes** | Other WBC | Inverse ratio in bacterial vs viral |

### Clinical Patterns

**Bacterial Infection:**
- Neutrophils: Elevated
- Bands: Elevated (left shift)
- Toxic granulation: Present
- **Acute bacterial response**

**Viral Infection:**
- Neutrophils: Normal or low
- Lymphocytes: Elevated
- **Lymphocytic response**

**Corticosteroid Effect:**
- Neutrophils: Elevated
- Lymphocytes: Low
- Eosinophils: Low
- **Stress leukogram**
