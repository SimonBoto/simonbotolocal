# Lp(a) — Lipoprotein(a), Genetic Cardiovascular Risk

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**Lp(a):** Lipoprotein(a)

**Discovered:** 1963 by Kare Berg

**Also called:**
- Lipoprotein little-a
- Lp little-a

---

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**LDL-like particle with apolipoprotein(a):**
- Similar to LDL but with additional apo(a) protein
- Apo(a) is homologous to plasminogen
- **Pro-thrombotic and pro-atherogenic**

**Key characteristics:**
| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Structure** | [[LDL_Cholesterol_Low-Density_Lipoprotein_Z-006-0001]|LDL] particle + apo(a) |
| **Apo(a)** | Kringle domains (like plasminogen) |
| **Size variation** | Genetically determined (isoforms) |
| **Levels** | 80-90% genetically determined |

**Pathogenic mechanisms:**
- Atherogenic (like [[LDL_Cholesterol_Low-Density_Lipoprotein_Z-006-0001]|LDL])
- Pro-thrombotic (interferes with fibrinolysis)
- Pro-inflammatory

---

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### Lp(a) Levels

| Level (mg/dL) | Status | Risk |
|---------------|--------|------|
| **<14** | Optimal | Low risk |
| **14-30** | Borderline | Moderate risk |
| **30-50** | High | High risk |
| **>50** | Very high | Very high risk |
| **>100** | Severe | Extreme risk |

### Clinical Significance

**Independent cardiovascular risk factor:**
- Not affected by diet or lifestyle
- Not reduced by statins
- Genetic determination

**Risk associations:**
| Level | Risk Increase |
|-------|---------------|
| **>30 mg/dL** | 2x cardiovascular risk |
| **>50 mg/dL** | 3x risk |
| **>100 mg/dL** | 4-5x risk |

**Associated conditions:**
- Atherosclerotic cardiovascular disease
- Aortic stenosis
- Ischemic stroke
- Venous thromboembolism

---

## 4. COFACTORS

### Lp(a) Determinants

| Factor | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| **Genetics** | 80-90% of variation |
| **Estrogen** | Lowers Lp(a) |
| **Testosterone** | May raise Lp(a) |
| **Niacin** | May lower Lp(a) |
| **PCSK9 inhibitors** | May lower Lp(a) 20-30% |

---

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### For Elevated Lp(a)

**Lifestyle (limited effect):**
- Exercise
- Diet changes
- **Minimal impact on Lp(a)**

**Medications:**
| Drug | Effect |
|------|--------|
| **Niacin** | May lower 20-30% |
| **PCSK9 inhibitors** | May lower 20-30% |
| **Statins** | No effect on Lp(a) |
| **Ezetimibe** | No effect |
| **Apheresis** | Dramatic reduction (severe cases) |

**Management strategy:**
- Aggressively treat other risk factors
- Lower LDL-C to very low levels
- Consider PCSK9 inhibitors
- Niacin (if tolerated)
- Apheresis for extreme cases (>100 mg/dL with CVD)

---

## 6. CONNECTED BIOMARKERS

| Biomarker | Relationship | Pattern |
|-----------|--------------|---------|
| **LDL-C** | Similar particle | Both atherogenic |
| **ApoB** | Particle count | Lp(a) contributes to ApoB |
| **hs-CRP** | Inflammation | Elevated with high Lp(a) |

### Clinical Patterns

**High Genetic Risk:**
- Lp(a): >50 mg/dL
- LDL-C: Variable
- Family history: CVD
- **Aggressive risk factor modification needed**

**Combined Risk:**
- Lp(a): Elevated
- LDL-C: Elevated
- **Multiplicative risk**
