# Free T4 — Thyroxine, Prohormone

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**T4:** Contains 4 iodine atoms

**Thyroxine:** From thyroid + oxygen (chemical suffix)

**Also called:**
- Tetraiodothyronine (chemical name)
- L-thyroxine
- Levothyroxine (synthetic form)

**Discovery:** 1914 by Edward Kendall

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**Primary thyroid hormone (by quantity):**
- 90% of thyroid hormone produced is T4
- Prohormone (less active than T3)
- Converted to T3 (active form) in peripheral tissues

**Transport:**
- >99% bound to proteins (TBG, transthyretin, albumin)
- <1% free (biologically active)
- **Free T4 measured clinically**

### Conversion to T3

**Deiodination (removes one iodine):**
- Type 1 deiodinase: Liver, kidney, thyroid
- Type 2 deiodinase: Brain, pituitary, brown fat, muscle
- Type 3 deiodinase: Inactivates (converts to rT3)

**Factors affecting conversion:**
- Selenium (cofactor for deiodinases)
- Stress, illness (reduce conversion)
- Fasting (reduces conversion)
- Beta-blockers (reduce conversion)
- Steroids (reduce conversion)

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### Free T4 Levels

| Level (ng/dL) | Status | Interpretation |
|---------------|--------|----------------|
| **1.0-1.5** | Optimal | Ideal thyroid function |
| **0.8-1.8** | Normal | Standard reference |
| **<0.8** | Low | Hypothyroidism |
| **>1.8** | High | Hyperthyroidism |

### Clinical Patterns

**Primary Hypothyroidism:**
- Free T4: Low
- TSH: Elevated
- **Thyroid failure**

**Subclinical Hypothyroidism:**
- Free T4: Normal
- TSH: Elevated (4-10)
- **Early or compensated**

**Primary Hyperthyroidism:**
- Free T4: High
- TSH: Suppressed
- **Excess production**

**Secondary Hypothyroidism (Pituitary):**
- Free T4: Low
- TSH: Low or inappropriately normal
- **Pituitary failure**

**T3 Toxicosis:**
- Free T4: Normal
- Free T3: High
- TSH: Suppressed
- **Early or treated hyperthyroidism**

## 4. COFACTORS

### T4 Synthesis Requirements

| Nutrient | Role | Deficiency Effect |
|----------|------|-------------------|
| **Iodine** | Essential component | Goiter, hypothyroidism |
| **Tyrosine** | Amino acid backbone | Reduced synthesis |
| **Iron** | TPO cofactor | Impaired hormone synthesis |
| **Selenium** | Deiodinase cofactor | Poor T4→T3 conversion |
| **Zinc** | TSH receptor function | Impaired signaling |

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### For Low Free T4 (Hypothyroidism)

**Levothyroxine (T4) replacement:**
- Start: 25-50 mcg/day (elderly, cardiac: 12.5-25 mcg)
- Titrate: Every 6-8 weeks
- Target: Free T4 mid-normal, TSH 0.5-2.5

**Nutritional support:**
- Iodine: 150 mcg/day (if deficient)
- Selenium: 100-200 mcg/day
- Iron: If ferritin low
- Zinc: 15-30 mg/day

### For High Free T4 (Hyperthyroidism)

**Treatment depends on cause:**
- Antithyroid medications (methimazole, PTU)
- Radioactive iodine
- Surgery
- Beta-blockers for symptoms

## 6. CONNECTED BIOMARKERS

| Biomarker | Relationship | Pattern |
|-----------|--------------|---------|
| **TSH** | Inverse feedback | Low T4 → high TSH; high T4 → low TSH |
| **Free T3** | Active hormone | May be low when T4 normal (conversion issue) |
| **Reverse T3** | Inactive form | Elevated in stress, illness |
| **Anti-TPO** | Autoimmune marker | Positive + low T4 = Hashimoto's |

### Clinical Patterns

**Hashimoto's Thyroiditis:**
- Free T4: Low or normal
- TSH: Elevated
- Anti-TPO: Positive
- **Autoimmune destruction**

**Conversion Problem:**
- Free T4: Normal
- Free T3: Low
- TSH: Normal or slightly elevated
- Symptoms: Hypothyroid
- **Poor T4→T3 conversion**
