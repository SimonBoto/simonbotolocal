# Insulin — Pancreatic Beta-Cell Response

## 1. IDENTIFICATION

### 1.1 Basic Information

| **Tier Classification** | GOLD |
| **Specimen** | Serum (fasting required) |
| **Collection Requirements** | 8-12 hour fast essential |

### 1.2 Biochemistry

**Molecular Structure:**
- Protein hormone (51 amino acids)
- Two chains (A and B) connected by disulfide bonds
- Secreted as proinsulin, cleaved to insulin + C-peptide

**Physical Properties:**
- Peptide hormone
- Short half-life (~5 minutes)
- Cleared by liver and kidneys

---

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY & FUNCTION

### 2.1 Normal Physiological Role

**Primary Anabolic Hormone:**
- Promotes glucose uptake (muscle, adipose)
- Inhibits hepatic glucose output
- Promotes glycogen synthesis
- Inhibits lipolysis (prevents fatty acid release)
- Promotes protein synthesis
- Stimulates LDL production by liver

### 2.2 Secretion Pattern

**Basal Secretion:**
- Continuous low-level release (50% of daily insulin)
- Pulsatile: Every 4-6 minutes

**Meal-Stimulated Secretion:**
- **First phase:** Rapid (5-10 min), sharp peak
- **Second phase:** Sustained, gradual rise
- Return to baseline: 2-3 hours

### 2.3 Regulation

**Stimuli for Secretion:**
| Stimulus | Mechanism |
|----------|-----------|
| **Glucose** | Primary stimulus (ATP/K+ channel closure) |
| **Amino acids** | Secondary stimulus (arginine, leucine) |
| **GIP, GLP-1** | Incretin hormones (gut-derived) |
| **Acetylcholine** | Parasympathetic stimulation |

**Inhibition of Secretion:**
| Factor | Mechanism |
|--------|-----------|
| **Low glucose** | ATP depletion |
| **Somatostatin** | Paracrine inhibition |
| **Epinephrine** | Sympathetic inhibition |
| **FFA, ketones** | Metabolic inhibition |

## 3. REFERENCE RANGES

### 3.1 Standard Laboratory Ranges

| Population | Lower Limit | Upper Limit | Units |
|------------|-------------|-------------|-------|
| Fasting Adults | 2 | 25 | μIU/mL |

### 3.2 Optimal/Functional Ranges (Simos Clinical)

| Level (μIU/mL) | Status | Interpretation |
|----------------|--------|----------------|
| **<3** | Low | Possible beta-cell dysfunction (late diabetes) |
| **3-6** | Optimal | Excellent insulin sensitivity |
| **6-10** | Normal | Acceptable |
| **10-15** | Elevated | Early insulin resistance |
| **15-25** | High | Significant insulin resistance |
| **>25** | Very high | Severe insulin resistance, diabetes risk |

### 3.3 Insulin Resistance Progression

**Stage 1: Compensatory Hyperinsulinemia**
- Fasting glucose: Normal
- Fasting insulin: Elevated (>10)
- HOMA-IR: Elevated (>2.5)
- **Body compensating for peripheral resistance**

**Stage 2: Prediabetes**
- Fasting glucose: 100-125 mg/dL
- Fasting insulin: Higher (>15)
- Beta-cells working harder

**Stage 3: Type 2 Diabetes**
- Fasting glucose: >126 mg/dL
- Fasting insulin: Variable (high early, low late)
- Beta-cell exhaustion

### 3.4 Factors Affecting Levels

**Physiological:**
- Fasting duration: Must be 8-12 hours
- Time of day: Morning peak (dawn phenomenon)
- Recent exercise: May acutely lower

**Pathological:**
- Insulin resistance: Elevated fasting
- Type 2 diabetes: Elevated early, low late
- Type 1 diabetes: Low/absent
- Obesity: Elevated

**Pharmacological:**
- Exogenous insulin: Elevated (measure C-peptide instead)
- Corticosteroids: Elevate
- Beta-blockers: May mask hypoglycemia

## 4. CLINICAL INTERPRETATION

### 4.1 Elevated Levels (Hyperinsulinemia)

**Early Insulin Resistance (10-15 μIU/mL):**
- **Common causes:** Obesity, sedentary lifestyle, high-carb diet
- **Clinical significance:** Earliest detectable metabolic dysfunction
- **Next steps:** Lifestyle intervention, recheck 8-12 weeks

**Significant Insulin Resistance (15-25 μIU/mL):**
- **Common causes:** Metabolic syndrome, prediabetes
- **Clinical significance:** Active compensation, diabetes risk
- **Next steps:** Aggressive lifestyle + supplements, consider metformin

**Severe Insulin Resistance (>25 μIU/mL):**
- **Common causes:** Severe obesity, PCOS, severe metabolic syndrome
- **Clinical significance:** High diabetes risk, cardiovascular risk
- **Next steps:** Medical management, consider medications

### 4.2 Decreased Levels (Hypoinsulinemia)

**Low with Normal Glucose:**
- **Common causes:** Ketogenic diet, prolonged fasting
- **Clinical significance:** Normal adaptation
- **Next steps:** None (expected)

**Low with High Glucose (Type 1 Diabetes):**
- **Common causes:** Autoimmune beta-cell destruction
- **Clinical significance:** Absolute insulin deficiency
- **Next steps:** Insulin therapy mandatory

**Low-Normal with High Glucose (Late Type 2):**
- **Common causes:** Beta-cell exhaustion
- **Clinical significance:** Relative insulin deficiency
- **Next steps:** Insulin therapy likely needed

### 4.3 Pattern Recognition

**Insulin Resistance Pattern:**
| Biomarker | Finding |
|-----------|---------|
| Fasting insulin | >10 μIU/mL |
| Fasting glucose | 90-125 mg/dL |
| HOMA-IR | >2.5 |
| Triglycerides | >150 mg/dL |
| HDL | <40 mg/dL (men), <50 mg/dL (women) |

**Beta-Cell Exhaustion (Late T2D):**
| Biomarker | Finding |
|-----------|---------|
| Fasting insulin | Low-normal or low |
| Fasting glucose | >126 mg/dL |
| C-peptide | Low |
| **Management** | Requires insulin therapy |

## 6. INTERVENTIONS

### For Elevated Insulin (Insulin Resistance)

**Tier 1: Lifestyle (Most Effective)**
- **Carbohydrate restriction:** Reduce refined carbs, increase fiber
- **Time-restricted eating:** 12-16 hour fasts daily
- **Exercise:** Resistance training especially effective
- **Sleep:** 7-9 hours, consistent schedule
- **Stress management:** Cortisol drives insulin resistance

**Tier 2: Supplements**
| Nutrient | Dose | Mechanism |
|----------|------|-----------|
| **Chromium** | 200-400 mcg/day | Glucose tolerance factor |
| **Magnesium** | 400-600 mg/day | Insulin receptor function |
| **Berberine** | 500mg 2-3x/day | AMPK activation, insulin sensitization |
| **Alpha-lipoic acid** | 300-600 mg/day | Insulin sensitivity |
| **Zinc** | 15-30 mg/day | Beta-cell function |
| **Vitamin D** | 2000-5000 IU/day | Beta-cell support |

**Tier 3: Medications**
- **Metformin:** Insulin sensitizer (first-line)
- **GLP-1 agonists:** Enhance insulin secretion, weight loss
- **SGLT2 inhibitors:** Independent of insulin
- **Thiazolidinediones:** PPAR-gamma agonists

### Monitoring

**Retest:** 8-12 weeks after intervention
**Target:** Fasting insulin <6 μIU/mL
**Along with:** Glucose, HOMA-IR, HbA1c
