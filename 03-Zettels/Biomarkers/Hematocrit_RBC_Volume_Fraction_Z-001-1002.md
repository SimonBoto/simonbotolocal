# Hematocrit — RBC Volume Fraction

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**Hematocrit:** From Greek "haima" (blood) + "krites" (judge/separator)

**Also called:**
- Hct, HCT
- Packed cell volume (PCV)

**Discovery:** Early 20th century centrifugation method

source-litnote: Synthesized from clinical practice
---

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**Percentage of blood volume occupied by RBCs:**
- Normal: ~40-50% of blood volume
- Plasma: ~50-60%
- Buffy coat: <1% (WBCs, platelets)

**Calculated from:**
- RBC count × MCV ÷ 10
- Or measured directly (centrifugation)

**Relationship to hemoglobin:**
- Hct ≈ 3 × Hb (roughly)
- Example: Hb 15 g/dL → Hct ~45%

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### Hematocrit Levels

| Population | Low | Normal | Optimal | High |
|------------|-----|--------|---------|------|
| **Men** | <41% | 41-50% | 43-47% | >50% |
| **Women** | <36% | 36-44% | 38-42% | >44% |

### Clinical Significance

**Low Hematocrit:**
- **Anemia** (same causes as low Hb)
- Overhydration (dilutional)
- Pregnancy (physiologic hemodilution)

**High Hematocrit:**
- **Dehydration** (most common)
- Polycythemia vera
- Chronic hypoxia (COPD, high altitude)
- Smoking
- Testosterone therapy
- Cushing's syndrome

**Dehydration vs. True Polycythemia:**
| Feature | Dehydration | True Polycythemia |
|---------|-------------|-------------------|
| Hct | Elevated | Elevated |
| Hb | Elevated | Elevated |
| RBC count | Normal | Elevated |
| Plasma volume | Low | Normal |
| Resolution | Normalizes with fluids | Persistent |

## 4. COFACTORS

### Factors Affecting Hematocrit

| Factor | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| **Hydration status** | Major effect on plasma volume |
| **Altitude** | Chronic hypoxia increases RBCs |
| **Testosterone** | Stimulates erythropoiesis |
| **EPO** | Stimulates RBC production |
| **Iron status** | Required for Hb synthesis |

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### For Low Hematocrit

**Same as anemia workup:**
- Identify cause (iron, B12, folate, blood loss)
- Treat underlying condition

### For High Hematocrit

**Dehydration:**
- Oral or IV fluids
- Rehydrate and recheck

**True polycythemia:**
- Phlebotomy (therapeutic blood removal)
- Hydroxyurea (if high risk)
- Address underlying cause
