# Suberic Acid — Fatty Acid Oxidation Marker (8-Carbon)

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**Suberic acid:** From Latin "suber" (cork oak)

**Also called:**
- Suberate
- Octanedioic acid (8-carbon dicarboxylic acid)

source-litnote: Synthesized from clinical practice
---

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**Dicarboxylic acid (8 carbons):**
- Formed from incomplete fatty acid oxidation
- Longer chain than adipic acid
- **Indicates impaired beta-oxidation**

**Pattern with other dicarboxylics:**
- Adipic (6C), Suberic (8C), Sebacic (10C)
- Elevated together in oxidation defects

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### Suberic Acid Levels

| Level | Status | Interpretation |
|-------|--------|----------------|
| **Normal** | Low | Normal fat oxidation |
| **Elevated** | High | Impaired beta-oxidation |

### Causes of Elevation

Same as adipic acid:
- Insulin resistance
- Mitochondrial dysfunction
- Carnitine deficiency
- B-vitamin deficiencies
- Genetic fatty acid oxidation disorders
