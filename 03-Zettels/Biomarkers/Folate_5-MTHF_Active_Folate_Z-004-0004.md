# Folate (5-MTHF) — Active Folate for Methylation

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**Folate:** From Latin "folium" (leaf) — found in leafy greens

**5-MTHF:** 5-Methyltetrahydrofolate (active form)

**Also called:**
- Vitamin B9
- Folic acid (synthetic form)
- L-methylfolate (active supplement)

**Discovery:** 1931 by Lucy Wills — identified as anti-anemia factor

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**Essential for:**
| Function | Mechanism |
|----------|-----------|
| **DNA synthesis** | Purine and thymidine synthesis |
| **Cell division** | Rapidly dividing tissues (RBCs, fetus) |
| **Methylation** | Methyl donor regeneration (with B12) |
| **Homocysteine metabolism** | Converts homocysteine to methionine |
| **Neural tube development** | Critical in early pregnancy |

### Forms of Folate

| Form | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Folic acid** | Synthetic, oxidized (requires conversion) |
| **Dihydrofolate (DHF)** | Intermediate |
| **Tetrahydrofolate (THF)** | Reduced form |
| **5-MTHF (L-methylfolate)** | Active form, crosses blood-brain barrier |

**Preferred form:** 5-MTHF (bypasses MTHFR enzyme)

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### Folate Levels

| Level (ng/mL) | Status | Interpretation |
|---------------|--------|----------------|
| **>6** | Optimal | Adequate status |
| **3-6** | Borderline | Suboptimal |
| **<3** | Deficient | Clear deficiency |

### RBC Folate vs. Serum Folate

| Type | Reflects | Timeframe |
|------|----------|-----------|
| **Serum folate** | Recent intake | Days |
| **RBC folate** | Tissue stores | Months |

**RBC folate more reliable** for status assessment

### Clinical Significance

**Deficiency causes:**
- Megaloblastic anemia (with B12 deficiency)
- Neural tube defects (pregnancy)
- Elevated homocysteine
- Cognitive impairment
- Depression

**High-risk groups:**
- Pregnancy (increased need)
- Alcoholics (reduced absorption)
- Malabsorption (celiac, Crohn's)
- Medications (methotrexate, phenytoin)
- MTHFR polymorphism (reduced conversion)

## 4. COFACTORS

### Folate Metabolism

| Nutrient | Role | Deficiency Effect |
|----------|------|-------------------|
| **B12** | Regenerates 5-MTHF | Functional folate deficiency |
| **B2 (riboflavin)** | MTHFR cofactor | Impaired conversion |
| **B6** | Homocysteine metabolism | Elevated homocysteine |
| **Zinc** | Folate metabolism | Impaired absorption |

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### For Deficiency (<3 ng/mL)

**Supplementation:**
- 5-MTHF (L-methylfolate): 400-1000 mcg/day
- Preferred over folic acid (especially if MTHFR variant)
- Food sources: Leafy greens, legumes, fortified grains

**Pregnancy:**
- 400-800 mcg/day preconception and first trimester
- Prevents neural tube defects
- Critical in first 28 days (often before pregnancy known)

**High-dose indications:**
- MTHFR C677T homozygous: 1-5 mg 5-MTHF
- Depression: 7.5-15 mg (prescription Deplin)
- Recurrent miscarriage: 1-5 mg

### Caution

**Always check B12 before folate supplementation:**
- Folate corrects anemia but not B12 neurological damage
- Can mask B12 deficiency
- **Supplement both together**

## 6. CONNECTED BIOMARKERS

| Biomarker | Relationship | Pattern |
|-----------|--------------|---------|
| **B12** | Partner vitamin | Both needed for methylation |
| **Homocysteine** | Methylation marker | Elevated if either B12 or folate low |
| **RBC folate** | Tissue stores | Better indicator than serum |
| **MCV** | RBC size | Elevated in folate deficiency |

### Clinical Patterns

**Folate Deficiency:**
- Serum/RBC folate: Low
- Homocysteine: Elevated
- MCV: Elevated (if anemia)
- B12: Normal

**Combined B12 + Folate Deficiency:**
- Both vitamins low
- Severe megaloblastic anemia
- Neurological symptoms (from B12)

**MTHFR Polymorphism:**
- Folate: May be normal
- Homocysteine: Elevated
- **Impaired conversion to active form**
