# Magnesium — Essential Mineral, 300+ Enzyme Cofactor

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**Magnesium:** From Magnesia (region in Greece)

**Element symbol:** Mg (atomic number 12)

**Also called:**
- Mg
- Mg2+ (ionized form)

---

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**Critical cofactor for 300+ enzymes:**
| Function | Mechanism |
|----------|-----------|
| **Energy production** | ATP-Mg complex, Krebs cycle enzymes |
| **Muscle function** | Calcium channel regulation, contraction |
| **Nerve function** | Neurotransmitter release, membrane stability |
| **Heart rhythm** | Cardiac conduction |
| **Bone health** | Bone matrix, vitamin D activation |
| **DNA/RNA synthesis** | Polymerase cofactor |
| **Blood pressure** | Vascular tone regulation |
| **Glucose metabolism** | Insulin receptor function |

**Forms measured:**
| Form | Reflects |
|------|----------|
| **Serum magnesium** | <1% of total, tightly regulated |
| **RBC magnesium** | Intracellular status (better indicator) |
| **Ionized magnesium** | Biologically active |

**Note:** Serum can be normal while tissues are deficient

---

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### Magnesium Levels

| Level (mg/dL) | Status | Interpretation |
|---------------|--------|----------------|
| **1.8-2.4** | Optimal | Adequate status |
| **1.6-1.8** | Low-normal | Suboptimal |
| **<1.6** | Deficient | Functional deficiency |
| **<1.2** | Severe | Critical deficiency |

### RBC Magnesium (better indicator)

| Level (mg/dL) | Status |
|---------------|--------|
| **4.0-6.4** | Normal |
| **<4.0** | Deficient |

### Clinical Significance

**Deficiency symptoms:**
- Muscle cramps, twitching, weakness
- Fatigue
- Arrhythmias
- Hypertension
- Insomnia, anxiety
- Constipation
- Osteoporosis
- Migraines
- Insulin resistance

**Severe deficiency (hypomagnesemia):**
- Tetany, seizures
- Cardiac arrest
- Hypokalemia (refractory to K+)
- Hypocalcemia (refractory to Ca2+)

**High-risk groups:**
- Diuretic users
- PPI users
- Alcoholics
- Diabetics
- Elderly
- Athletes (sweat loss)

---

## 4. COFACTORS

### Magnesium Metabolism

| Nutrient | Role | Interaction |
|----------|------|-------------|
| **Calcium** | Antagonist | High Ca:Mg ratio problematic |
| **Vitamin D** | Absorption | D requires Mg for activation |
| **Potassium** | Balance | Low Mg → refractory low K |
| **B6** | Absorption | Enhances Mg uptake |

---

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### For Deficiency (<1.6 mg/dL serum or <4.0 mg/dL RBC)

**Supplementation:**
- Magnesium glycinate: 200-400 mg elemental Mg/day
- Magnesium citrate: 200-400 mg/day (laxative effect)
- Magnesium threonate: 144 mg/day (brain penetration)
- Magnesium chloride: Transdermal option

**Food sources:**
- Pumpkin seeds
- Almonds
- Spinach
- Dark chocolate
- Avocado
- Legumes

**Caution:**
- Reduce dose if diarrhea
- Reduce/stop if kidney disease
- Balance with calcium

---

## 6. CONNECTED BIOMARKERS

| Biomarker | Relationship | Pattern |
|-----------|--------------|---------|
| **Calcium** | Antagonist | Ideal ratio 1:2 (Ca:Mg) |
| **Potassium** | Balance | Low Mg → refractory low K |
| **Vitamin D** | Activation | Mg required for D metabolism |
| **PTH** | Regulation | Low Mg → low PTH |

### Clinical Patterns

**Magnesium Deficiency:**
- Magnesium: Low
- Potassium: Low (refractory to supplementation)
- Calcium: Normal/low
- **Intracellular depletion**

**Diuretic Effect:**
- Magnesium: Low
- Potassium: Low
- **Renal losses**

**PPI Effect:**
- Magnesium: Low
- B12: Low
- **Malabsorption**
