# AA to EPA Ratio — Inflammatory Precursor Balance

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**AA:EPA ratio:** Ratio of arachidonic acid to eicosapentaenoic acid

**AA:** Arachidonic acid (omega-6, pro-inflammatory precursor)

**EPA:** Eicosapentaenoic acid (omega-3, anti-inflammatory)

source-litnote: Synthesized from clinical practice
---

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**Eicosanoid precursors:**
| Fatty Acid | Eicosanoid Type | Effect |
|------------|-----------------|--------|
| **AA** | Series-2 prostaglandins, thromboxanes, leukotrienes | Pro-inflammatory |
| **EPA** | Series-3 prostaglandins, thromboxanes, leukotrienes | Anti-inflammatory |

**Competition:**
- AA and EPA compete for same enzymes (COX, LOX)
- More EPA = less inflammatory AA-derived products
- **Ratio determines inflammatory tone**

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### AA:EPA Ratio

| Ratio | Status | Interpretation |
|-------|--------|----------------|
| **<3:1** | Optimal | Anti-inflammatory |
| **3:1 to 6:1** | Acceptable | Moderate balance |
| **6:1 to 10:1** | Elevated | Pro-inflammatory tendency |
| **>10:1** | High | Significant inflammatory risk |

### Clinical Significance

**High ratio associated with:**
- Cardiovascular disease
- Depression
- Inflammatory disorders
- Cancer risk

**Low ratio protective for:**
- Heart disease
- Mood disorders
- Chronic inflammation

## 4. COFACTORS

### Factors Affecting Ratio

| Factor | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| **Fish oil** | Increases EPA, lowers ratio |
| **Vegetable oils** | Increases AA, raises ratio |
| **Grain-fed meat** | High AA |
| **Grass-fed meat** | Better AA:EPA ratio |
| **Delta-6-desaturase** | Converts LA to AA; genetics vary |

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### For High AA:EPA Ratio

**Increase EPA:**
- Fish oil: 2-4g EPA+DHA/day
- High-EPA fish oil preferred
- Fatty fish 2-3x/week

**Reduce AA:**
- Limit vegetable oils
- Choose grass-fed meat
- Moderate egg consumption

**Target:** <3:1 ratio
