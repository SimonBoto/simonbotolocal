## 2. PHYSIOLOGY & FUNCTION

### 2.1 Normal Physiological Role

**Leucine Metabolism Intermediate:**
- Leucine → HMG-CoA → 3-methylcrotonyl-CoA
- **Biotin-dependent step:** 3-methylcrotonyl-CoA carboxylase (MCC)
- MCC requires biotin as cofactor

### 2.2 Biotin-Dependent Enzyme

**3-methylcrotonyl-CoA carboxylase (MCC):**
- Converts 3-methylcrotonyl-CoA → 3-methylglutaconyl-CoA
- **Biotin deficiency → MCC impaired → 3-HIA accumulates**

### 2.3 Regulation

**Biotin Status:**
- Adequate biotin: Normal 3-HIA
- Deficient biotin: Elevated 3-HIA

### 3.3 Factors Affecting Levels

**Pathological:**
- Biotin deficiency: Elevated
- Genetic MCC deficiency: Markedly elevated

**Dietary:**
- Raw egg white consumption (avidin binds biotin)
- Long-term TPN without biotin

**Pharmacological:**
- Anticonvulsants: May increase need
- Antibiotics: May reduce gut biotin production

**Gut Health:**
- Dysbiosis: May reduce biotin-producing bacteria

## 4. CLINICAL INTERPRETATION

### 4.1 Elevated Levels

**Mild Elevation:**
- **Common causes:** Suboptimal biotin, mild dysbiosis
- **Clinical significance:** Early deficiency indicator
- **Next steps:** Biotin supplementation, dietary review

**Significant Elevation:**
- **Common causes:** Biotin deficiency, genetic MCC deficiency
- **Clinical significance:** Functional biotin deficiency
- **Next steps:** High-dose biotin, evaluate for genetic disorder if very high

### 4.2 Pattern Recognition

**Biotin Deficiency Pattern:**
| Biomarker | Finding |
|-----------|---------|
| 3-HIA | Elevated |
| Methylcitric acid | Elevated |
| Clinical symptoms | Hair loss, dermatitis, neurological |

**Genetic MCC Deficiency (Rare):**
- 3-HIA: Very high
- Metabolic acidosis
- Requires specialist management

## 6. INTERVENTIONS

### For Elevated 3-HIA

**Biotin Supplementation:**
- B7: 1-5 mg/day (high doses safe, water-soluble)
- Higher doses if severe deficiency
- Retest in 4-8 weeks

**Dietary Sources:**
- Egg yolks (not whites)
- Liver
- Nuts and seeds
- Salmon
- Avocado
- Sweet potato

**Gut Health:**
- Probiotics to support biotin-producing bacteria
- Avoid raw egg whites (avidin binds biotin)
