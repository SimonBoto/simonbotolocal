# LH & FSH — Pituitary Gonadotropins, Hormonal Regulation

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**LH:** Luteinizing Hormone — stimulates corpus luteum formation

**FSH:** Follicle-Stimulating Hormone — stimulates follicle development

**Gonadotropins:** Hormones acting on gonads (ovaries/testes)

---

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**Pituitary hormones regulating reproduction:**

**LH (Luteinizing Hormone):**
| Sex | Function |
|-----|----------|
| **Women** | Triggers ovulation, maintains corpus luteum |
| **Men** | Stimulates testosterone production (Leydig cells) |

**FSH (Follicle-Stimulating Hormone):**
| Sex | Function |
|-----|----------|
| **Women** | Stimulates follicle growth, estrogen production |
| **Men** | Stimulates sperm production (Sertoli cells) |

**Regulation:**
```
Hypothalamus (GnRH) → Pituitary (LH/FSH) → Gonads (sex hormones)
                              ↑___________________________|
                                    (Negative feedback)
```

---

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### LH/FSH Levels (Adults)

| Hormone | Men | Women (follicular) | Women (mid-cycle) | Women (postmenopausal) |
|---------|-----|-------------------|-------------------|----------------------|
| **LH** | 1.5-9.3 | 1.9-12.5 | 8.7-76.3 | 15.9-54.0 |
| **FSH** | 1.4-18.1 | 2.5-10.2 | 3.4-33.4 | 23.0-116.3 |

### Clinical Significance

**Elevated LH/FSH (Hypergonadotropic):**
| Cause | Mechanism |
|-------|-----------|
| **Primary hypogonadism** | Gonads fail, pituitary compensates |
| **Menopause** | Ovarian failure (normal) |
| **Klinefelter's** | Testicular failure |
| **Turner's** | Ovarian failure |

**Low LH/FSH (Hypogonadotropic):**
| Cause | Mechanism |
|-------|-----------|
| **Hypothalamic/pituitary disease** | Brain not signaling |
| **Functional hypothalamic amenorrhea** | Stress, exercise, weight loss |
| **Hyperprolactinemia** | Prolactin suppresses GnRH |
| **Kallmann's syndrome** | Genetic GnRH deficiency |
| **Anabolic steroids** | Exogenous testosterone suppresses |

**LH:FSH ratio:**
- PCOS: LH:FSH >2:1 (elevated LH relative to FSH)

---

## 4. COFACTORS

### LH/FSH Regulation

| Factor | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| **GnRH** | Stimulates release |
| **Sex hormones** | Negative feedback |
| **Prolactin** | Suppresses GnRH |
| **Stress** | Suppresses GnRH |
| **Nutrition** | Required for normal function |

---

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### Based on LH/FSH Pattern

**Primary hypogonadism (high LH/FSH):**
- Hormone replacement (testosterone/estrogen)
- Treat underlying cause if possible

**Secondary hypogonadism (low LH/FSH):**
- Address pituitary/hypothalamic cause
- GnRH therapy or gonadotropin injections
- Treat prolactinoma if present

**PCOS (high LH:FSH):**
- Metformin
- OCPs
- Lifestyle modification

---

## 6. CONNECTED BIOMARKERS

| Biomarker | Relationship | Pattern |
|-----------|--------------|---------|
| **Testosterone** | Target hormone | Low T + high LH = primary hypogonadism |
| **Estradiol** | Target hormone | Low E2 + high FSH = menopause |
| **Prolactin** | Regulator | High prolactin → low LH/FSH |
| **SHBG** | Binding protein | Affects free hormone levels |

### Clinical Patterns

**Primary Hypogonadism (Men):**
- Testosterone: Low
- LH/FSH: Elevated
- **Testes not responding**

**Secondary Hypogonadism (Men):**
- Testosterone: Low
- LH/FSH: Low
- **Brain not signaling**

**Menopause:**
- Estradiol: Low
- FSH: Very high (>25)
- LH: Elevated
- **Normal physiological state**

**PCOS:**
- LH: Elevated
- FSH: Normal/low
- LH:FSH ratio: >2:1
- Testosterone: Elevated
