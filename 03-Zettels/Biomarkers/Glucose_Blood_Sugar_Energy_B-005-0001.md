# Glucose — Blood Sugar, Energy Currency

## 1. IDENTIFICATION

| **Tier Classification** | GOLD |
| **Specimen** | Serum (fasting preferred) |
| **Collection Requirements** | 8-12 hour fast for baseline; random acceptable for screening |

### 1.2 Biochemistry

**Molecular Structure:**
- Formula: C₆H₁₂O₆
- Monosaccharide (simple sugar)
- Primary energy source for cells

**Physical Properties:**
- Solubility: Water soluble
- Stable in serum (glycolysis inhibited by fluoride in grey-top tubes)

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY & FUNCTION

### 2.1 Normal Physiological Role

**Primary Energy Source:**
- Brain: 120g/day required (obligate glucose user)
- Red blood cells: Only fuel source (no mitochondria)
- Muscle: Anaerobic and aerobic metabolism
- All cells: ATP production via glycolysis, Krebs cycle

### 2.2 Regulation

**Hormonal Control:**
| Hormone | Trigger | Action |
|---------|---------|--------|
| **Insulin** | High glucose | Promotes glucose uptake, storage |
| **Glucagon** | Low glucose | Promotes glycogenolysis, gluconeogenesis |
| **Cortisol** | Stress | Increases glucose production |
| **Epinephrine** | Emergency | Rapid glucose release |

**Normal Kinetics:**
- **Fasting:** 70-90 mg/dL (hepatic glucose output)
- **Postprandial:** Peak 30-60 min, return to baseline by 2 hours
- **Dawn phenomenon:** Slight morning elevation

### 2.3 Metabolic Pathways

**Glucose Utilization:**
```
Glycolysis → Pyruvate → Krebs Cycle → ATP
          → Lactate (anaerobic)
```

**Glucose Storage:**
```
Glucose → Glycogen (liver, muscle)
Glucose → Fat (adipose tissue)
```

## 3. REFERENCE RANGES

### 3.1 Standard Laboratory Ranges

| Population | Lower Limit | Upper Limit | Units |
|------------|-------------|-------------|-------|
| Fasting Adults | 70 | 99 | mg/dL |
| Random | — | 200 | mg/dL (screening) |

### 3.2 Optimal/Functional Ranges (Simos Clinical)

| Level (mg/dL) | Status | Clinical Significance |
|---------------|--------|----------------------|
| **<70** | Hypoglycemia | Low energy, symptoms |
| **70-85** | Optimal | Ideal metabolic health |
| **86-99** | Normal | Acceptable |
| **100-125** | Prediabetes | Insulin resistance developing |
| **>126 (fasting)** | Diabetes | Diagnostic threshold |

### 3.3 ADA Diagnostic Criteria

| Test | Normal | Prediabetes | Diabetes |
|------|--------|-------------|----------|
| **Fasting glucose** | <100 mg/dL | 100-125 mg/dL | ≥126 mg/dL |
| **2h OGTT** | <140 mg/dL | 140-199 mg/dL | ≥200 mg/dL |
| **HbA1c** | <5.7% | 5.7-6.4% | ≥6.5% |
| **Random glucose** | — | — | ≥200 mg/dL + symptoms |

### 3.4 Factors Affecting Levels

**Physiological:**
- Fasting duration: Decreases with prolonged fast
- Time of day: Dawn phenomenon (morning elevation)
- Stress: Acute elevation (cortisol, epinephrine)
- Exercise: Acute decrease, chronic improvement

**Pathological:**
- Insulin resistance: Elevated fasting
- Diabetes: Markedly elevated
- Liver disease: Variable (impaired gluconeogenesis)
- Sepsis: Variable

**Pharmacological:**
- Corticosteroids: Elevate
- Beta-blockers: May mask hypoglycemia symptoms
- Thiazides: May elevate

## 4. CLINICAL INTERPRETATION

### 4.1 Elevated Levels (Hyperglycemia)

**Prediabetes (100-125 mg/dL fasting):**
- **Common causes:** Insulin resistance, metabolic syndrome
- **Clinical significance:** Reversible stage
- **Next steps:** Lifestyle intervention, recheck in 3-6 months

**Diabetes (≥126 mg/dL fasting, confirmed):**
- **Common causes:** Type 2 DM (insulin resistance), Type 1 DM (autoimmune)
- **Clinical significance:** Chronic hyperglycemia, complications risk
- **Next steps:** Comprehensive diabetes management

**Severe Hyperglycemia (>200-300 mg/dL):**
- **Common causes:** Uncontrolled diabetes, acute illness
- **Clinical significance:** Dehydration, electrolyte imbalance, DKA risk
- **Next steps:** Urgent medical evaluation

### 4.2 Decreased Levels (Hypoglycemia)

**Mild (60-70 mg/dL):**
- **Common causes:** Prolonged fasting, reactive hypoglycemia
- **Clinical significance:** Usually asymptomatic
- **Next steps:** Dietary modification

**Moderate (40-60 mg/dL):**
- **Common causes:** Insulin excess, sulfonylureas, alcohol
- **Clinical significance:** Adrenergic symptoms (sweating, tremors)
- **Next steps:** Glucose administration, cause investigation

**Severe (<40 mg/dL):**
- **Common causes:** Insulin overdose, insulinoma, sepsis
- **Clinical significance:** Neuroglycopenia (confusion, seizures, coma)
- **Next steps:** Emergency glucose, hospitalization

### 4.3 Pattern Recognition

**Insulin Resistance Pattern:**
| Biomarker | Finding |
|-----------|---------|
| Fasting glucose | 100-125 mg/dL |
| Fasting insulin | >10 μIU/mL |
| HOMA-IR | >2.5 |
| Triglycerides | Elevated |
| HDL | Low |

**Metabolic Syndrome (3+ criteria):**
- Glucose: ≥100 mg/dL
- Plus: High BP, high TG, low HDL, or central obesity

**Diabetes with Complications Risk:**
- Glucose: >126 mg/dL
- HbA1c: >6.5%
- Duration: >10 years
- Additional: Hypertension, dyslipidemia, smoking

## 6. INTERVENTIONS

### For Elevated Glucose (Prediabetes/Diabetes)

**Tier 1: Lifestyle (Foundation)**
- **Carbohydrate modification:** Reduce refined carbs, increase fiber
- **Time-restricted eating:** 12-16 hour fasts
- **Exercise:** 150 min/week aerobic + 2x/week resistance
- **Sleep:** 7-9 hours, consistent schedule
- **Stress management:** Cortisol reduction

**Tier 2: Supplements**
| Nutrient | Dose | Mechanism |
|----------|------|-----------|
| **Chromium** | 200-400 mcg/day | Glucose tolerance factor |
| **Magnesium** | 400-600 mg/day | Insulin sensitivity |
| **Berberine** | 500mg 2-3x/day | AMPK activation, glucose-lowering |
| **Alpha-lipoic acid** | 300-600 mg/day | Insulin sensitivity, antioxidant |
| **Cinnamon** | 1-3g/day | Mild glucose reduction |
| **Vitamin D** | 2000-5000 IU/day | Insulin sensitivity |

**Tier 3: Medications**
- **Metformin:** First-line, insulin sensitization
- **SGLT2 inhibitors:** Glucose excretion
- **GLP-1 agonists:** Insulin secretion, weight loss
- **Insulin:** When oral agents insufficient

### For Hypoglycemia

**Reactive Hypoglycemia:**
- Reduce simple carbohydrates
- Increase protein and fat at meals
- Smaller, more frequent meals
- Chromium supplementation

**Drug-Induced:**
- Adjust medication timing/dose
- Glucose monitoring
- Medical supervision
