# Pyruvic Acid — Glycolysis Endpoint, Mitochondrial Gateway

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**Pyruvic acid:** From Greek "pyr" (fire) + Latin "uva" (grape)

**Named for:** Discovered in fermented grapes

**Also called:**
- Pyruvate
- 2-oxopropanoic acid

---

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**End product of glycolysis:**
- Glucose → 2 pyruvate + 2 ATP + 2 NADH
- Crossroads of metabolism

**Fates of pyruvate:**
| Pathway | Condition | Product |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| **Mitochondrial entry** | Aerobic, fed | Acetyl-CoA → Krebs cycle |
| **Lactate conversion** | Anaerobic, hypoxic | Lactate |
| **Gluconeogenesis** | Fasting | Glucose |
| **Alanine synthesis** | Protein metabolism | Alanine |

**Pyruvate dehydrogenase complex:**
- Converts pyruvate → acetyl-CoA
- Requires: Thiamine (B1), lipoic acid, CoA, NAD+, FAD
- **Critical gateway to mitochondria**

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPOLATION

### Pyruvic Acid Levels

| Level | Status | Interpretation |
|-------|--------|----------------|
| **Normal** | Low | Properly metabolized |
| **Elevated** | High | PDH block or overload |

### Causes of Elevation

**Thiamine (B1) deficiency:**
- Impaired pyruvate dehydrogenase
- Pyruvate cannot enter mitochondria
- Accumulates → lactate or alanine
- **Classic B1 deficiency marker**

**Mitochondrial dysfunction:**
- Impaired pyruvate entry/utilization
- Multiple enzyme defects

**Lactic acidosis:**
- Often elevated with lactate

## 4. COFACTORS

### Pyruvate Dehydrogenase Complex

| Nutrient | Role | Deficiency Effect |
|----------|------|-------------------|
| **Thiamine (B1)** | Cofactor (TPP) | Pyruvate accumulation |
| **Lipoic acid** | Cofactor | Impaired conversion |
| **Pantothenic acid (B5)** | CoA synthesis | Impaired acetyl-CoA formation |
| **Riboflavin (B2)** | FAD synthesis | Impaired complex function |
| **Niacin (B3)** | NAD+ synthesis | Impaired complex function |
| **Magnesium** | Cofactor | Impaired enzyme activity |

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### For Elevated Pyruvic Acid

**Thiamine supplementation:**
- B1: 100-300 mg/day
- Critical if deficiency suspected

**Mitochondrial support:**
- B-complex
- Lipoic acid
- CoQ10
- Magnesium

## 6. CONNECTED BIOMARKERS

| Biomarker | Relationship | Pattern |
|-----------|--------------|---------|
| **Lactate** | Conversion product | Elevated together |
| **Lactate:Pyruvate ratio** | Redox state | Normal ~10:1; High ratio = hypoxia |
| **B1 status** | Cofactor | Deficiency → elevated pyruvate |

### Clinical Patterns

**B1 Deficiency:**
- Pyruvate: Elevated
- Lactate: Elevated
- B1: Low
- **Beriberi or Wernicke's**

**Mitochondrial Dysfunction:**
- Pyruvate: Elevated
- Lactate: Elevated
- TCA intermediates: Abnormal
- **Multiple deficiencies**
