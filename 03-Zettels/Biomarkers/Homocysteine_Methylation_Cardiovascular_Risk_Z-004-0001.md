# Homocysteine — Methylation & Cardiovascular Risk

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**Homocysteine:** From "homo-" (similar to) + "cysteine" (amino acid)

**Structure:** Homolog of cysteine with additional methylene group

**Discovery:** 1932 by V. du Vigneaud (Nobel Prize 1955)

**Clinical significance recognized:** 1960s-70s — link to vascular disease

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**Not a functional endpoint — metabolic intermediate:**
- Product of methionine demethylation
- Re-methylated to methionine (requires B12, folate)
- Or transsulfurated to cysteine (requires B6)

**The Methionine Cycle:**
Methionine → SAM (methyl donor) → SAH → Homocysteine → (re-methylated) → Methionine

### Why Elevated Homocysteine Matters

**Pro-atherogenic effects:**
- Endothelial dysfunction
- Oxidative stress
- Smooth muscle proliferation
- Thrombosis promotion
- Collagen cross-linking

**Neurotoxic effects:**
- NMDA receptor activation
- Excitotoxicity
- White matter damage

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### Homocysteine Levels

| Level | Status | Risk |
|-------|--------|------|
| **<5 μmol/L** | Optimal | Minimal |
| **<7 μmol/L** | Acceptable | Low |
| **7-10 μmol/L** | Borderline | Moderate |
| **10-15 μmol/L** | Elevated | High |
| **>15 μmol/L** | High | Very high |

### Causes of Elevation

**Nutritional (most common):**
- B12 deficiency
- Folate deficiency
- B6 deficiency
- Riboflavin (B2) deficiency

**Genetic:**
- MTHFR polymorphism (C677T)
- CBS deficiency
- MTR/MTRR mutations

**Medical conditions:**
- Hypothyroidism
- Kidney disease (reduced clearance)
- Psoriasis
- Cancer

**Medications:**
- Methotrexate
- Carbamazepine
- Phenytoin
- Metformin (B12 depletion)

**Lifestyle:**
- Smoking
- High coffee intake
- High methionine diet (without B-vitamins)

## 4. COFACTORS

### Required for Metabolism

| Nutrient | Role | Deficiency Effect |
|----------|------|-------------------|
| **B12 (cobalamin)** | MTR enzyme cofactor (re-methylation) | Severe elevation |
| **Folate (5-MTHF)** | Methyl donor for re-methylation | Moderate elevation |
| **B6 (pyridoxine)** | CBS enzyme cofactor (transsulfuration) | Moderate elevation |
| **B2 (riboflavin)** | MTHFR cofactor | Mild elevation |
| **Zinc** | Enzyme function | Mild elevation |

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### For Elevated Homocysteine

**Tier 1: B-Vitamins**
| Nutrient | Dose | Notes |
|----------|------|-------|
| **Methylcobalamin (B12)** | 1000-2000 mcg/day | Active form |
| **5-MTHF (folate)** | 400-800 mcg/day | Active form, not folic acid |
| **P5P (B6)** | 25-50 mg/day | Active form |
| **Riboflavin (B2)** | 10-25 mg/day | If MTHFR variant |

**Tier 2: Supportive**
- Zinc: 15-30 mg/day
- Betaine (TMG): 500-1000 mg/day (alternative methyl donor)
- Choline: 250-500 mg/day

**Tier 3: Lifestyle**
- Reduce coffee (<2 cups/day)
- Stop smoking
- Moderate methionine intake (balanced with B-vitamins)

### Monitoring

**Retest:** 8-12 weeks
**Target:** <7 μmol/L (optimal <5)

## 6. CONNECTED BIOMARKERS

| Biomarker | Relationship | Pattern |
|-----------|--------------|---------|
| **Methylmalonic Acid (MMA)** | B12 status | Both elevated = B12 deficiency |
| **5-MTHF (folate)** | Methyl donor | Low with high homocysteine = folate deficiency |
| **B12 (serum)** | Cofactor | Low with high homocysteine = B12 deficiency |
| **CRP** | Inflammation | Both elevated = cardiovascular risk |
| **LDL** | Lipid marker | Both elevated = additive cardiovascular risk |
| **Creatinine** | Kidney function | Elevated creatinine + high homocysteine = reduced clearance |

### Clinical Patterns

**B12 Deficiency:**
- Homocysteine: Elevated
- MMA: Elevated
- B12: Low or normal-low
- **Treat with B12**

**Folate Deficiency:**
- Homocysteine: Elevated
- MMA: Normal
- Folate: Low
- **Treat with 5-MTHF**

**B6 Deficiency:**
- Homocysteine: Elevated
- MMA: Normal
- B6: Low
- **Treat with P5P**

**Combined Deficiency:**
- Homocysteine: Very elevated
- Multiple B-vitamins low
- **Treat with B-complex**

**MTHFR Variant:**
- Homocysteine: Elevated
- B-vitamins: Normal
- **Treat with 5-MTHF + B12 + B2**

## 10. CLINICAL WEIGHT

**Significance: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (5/5 — Critical)**

**Independent cardiovascular risk factor**

**Associated with:**
- Cardiovascular disease
- Stroke
- Dementia/Alzheimer's
- Osteoporosis
- Pregnancy complications

**Always with:**
- B12
- Folate (5-MTHF)
- MMA (if B12 status unclear)
