# Transferrin Saturation — Functional Iron Assessment

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**Transferrin Saturation:** Percentage of transferrin binding sites occupied by iron

**Also called:**
- TSAT
- Iron saturation
- Transferrin iron saturation

**Clinical significance:** Best functional indicator of iron availability

source-litnote: Synthesized from clinical practice
---

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Calculation

**Formula:**
```
Transferrin Saturation = ([[Serum_Iron_Circulating_Availability_Z-003-0004]|Serum Iron] ÷ [[TIBC_Total_Iron_Binding_Capacity_Z-003-0002]|TIBC]) × 100
```

Or:
```
Transferrin Saturation = (Serum Iron ÷ Transferrin × 1.25) × 100
```

### What It Measures

**Functional iron availability:**
- How much of the transport capacity is actually carrying iron
- Reflects immediate iron supply to tissues
- Dynamic marker (changes faster than ferritin)

### Normal Kinetics

**Daily variation:**
- Morning: Higher (diurnal rhythm)
- After meals: Lower
- Menstruation: Lower in women

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### Transferrin Saturation Levels

| Saturation | Status | Interpretation |
|------------|--------|----------------|
| **<20%** | Deficient | Insufficient iron delivery |
| **20-50%** | Normal | Adequate iron transport |
| **>50%** | Elevated | Iron overload risk |
| **>80%** | Dangerous | Hemochromatosis, organ damage risk |

### Clinical Patterns

**Early Iron Deficiency:**
- Transferrin sat drops first
- Before ferritin falls significantly
- Most sensitive functional marker

**Iron Deficiency Anemia:**
- Transferrin sat: <15%
- Ferritin: <30 ng/mL
- Hemoglobin: Low

**Hemochromatosis Screening:**
- Transferrin sat: >45% (fasting)
- Best screening test for hereditary hemochromatosis
- More sensitive than ferritin alone

**Inflammatory Block:**
- Transferrin sat: Low (<20%)
- Ferritin: Normal or high
- Serum iron: Low
- **Iron trapped, not usable**

## 4. COFACTORS

### Factors Affecting Saturation

| Factor | Effect | Mechanism |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| **Time of day** | Morning higher | Diurnal variation |
| **Meals** | Postprandial decrease | Iron distribution |
| **Menstruation** | Lower in women | Blood loss |
| **Inflammation** | Lower | Hepcidin elevation |
| **Supplementation** | Higher | Increased iron availability |

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### For Low Saturation (<20%)

**Iron supplementation:**
- See [[Ferritin_Iron_Storage_Systemic_Status_Z-003-0001|Ferritin] for protocols
- Target: >20% saturation
- Monitor with ferritin

### For High Saturation (>50%)

**Investigate cause:**
1. **Hemochromatosis** — genetic testing (HFE gene)
2. **Iron overload** — reduce intake, therapeutic phlebotomy
3. **Liver disease** — impaired transferrin synthesis

**Therapeutic phlebotomy:**
- If ferritin >1000 ng/mL
- If confirmed hemochromatosis
- Target: Saturation <50%

## 6. CONNECTED BIOMARKERS

| Biomarker | Relationship | Pattern |
|-----------|--------------|---------|
| **Ferritin** | Storage indicator | Low sat + low ferritin = deficiency |
| **TIBC** | Transport capacity | Used in calculation |
| **Serum Iron** | Numerator | Direct measurement |
| **CRP** | Inflammation | High CRP + low sat = functional deficiency |
| **Hemoglobin** | End result | Low Hgb + low sat = iron deficiency anemia |

### Key Patterns

**Deficiency:**
- Saturation: <20%
- Ferritin: <30 ng/mL
- TIBC: High

**Overload:**
- Saturation: >50%
- Ferritin: >300 ng/mL (men), >200 ng/mL (women)
- TIBC: Normal or low

**Inflammatory Block:**
- Saturation: <20%
- Ferritin: Normal/high
- CRP: Elevated
