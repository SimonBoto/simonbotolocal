# Calcium — Mineral, Bone & Cellular Signaling

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**Calcium:** From Latin "calx" (lime)

**Element symbol:** Ca (atomic number 20)

**Discovery:** Isolated by Humphry Davy in 1808

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**Critical for life:**
| Function | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Bone structure** | Hydroxyapatite mineralization |
| **Muscle contraction** | Excitation-contraction coupling |
| **Nerve transmission** | Action potentials |
| **Blood clotting** | Coagulation cascade |
| **Cell signaling** | Second messenger |
| **Enzyme function** | Cofactor for many enzymes |

### Forms in Blood

| Form | Percentage | Clinical Utility |
|------|------------|------------------|
| **Ionized (free)** | 50% | Biologically active (preferred) |
| **Protein-bound** | 40% | Inactive |
| **Complexed** | 10% | Inactive |

**Total calcium affected by albumin:**
- Low albumin → artificially low total calcium
- Corrected Ca = Measured Ca + 0.8 × (4 - Albumin)

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### Calcium Levels

| Level (mg/dL) | Status | Interpretation |
|---------------|--------|----------------|
| **<8.5** | Low | Hypocalcemia |
| **8.5-10.5** | Normal | Adequate |
| **10.5-12** | Borderline high | Monitor |
| **>12** | High | Hypercalcemia |

### Ionized Calcium (Preferred)

| Level (mmol/L) | Status |
|----------------|--------|
| **<1.0** | Low |
| **1.0-1.25** | Normal |
| **>1.25** | High |

### Clinical Significance

**Hypocalcemia (Low calcium):**
| Cause | Mechanism |
|-------|-----------|
| **Hypoparathyroidism** | Low PTH |
| **Vitamin D deficiency** | Poor absorption |
| **Chronic kidney disease** | Poor activation |
| **Hypomagnesemia** | Required for PTH |
| **Pancreatitis** | Saponification |
| **Hungry bone syndrome** | Post-parathyroidectomy |

**Symptoms:** Tetany, paresthesias, seizures, QT prolongation

**Hypercalcemia (High calcium):**
| Cause | Mechanism |
|-------|-----------|
| **Primary hyperparathyroidism** | Most common (outpatient) |
| **Malignancy** | Most common (inpatient), PTHrP |
| **Vitamin D toxicity** | Excess absorption |
| **Milk-alkali syndrome** | Excess intake |
| **Thiazide diuretics** | Reduced excretion |
| **Granulomatous disease** | Extra-renal vitamin D activation |

**Symptoms:** "Stones, bones, groans, moans, thrones"
- Kidney stones
- Bone pain
- Abdominal pain
- Confusion/depression
- Constipation
- Polyuria

## 4. COFACTORS

### Calcium Metabolism

| Nutrient | Role | Deficiency Effect |
|----------|------|-------------------|
| **Vitamin D** | Absorption | Poor absorption |
| **PTH** | Regulation | Dysregulated calcium |
| **Magnesium** | PTH secretion | Low PTH → low calcium |
| **Albumin** | Transport | Low albumin → low total Ca |
| **Phosphate** | Balance | High phosphate → low calcium |

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### For Hypocalcemia

**Acute (tetany, seizures):**
- IV calcium gluconate
- Cardiac monitoring

**Chronic:**
- Calcium supplements (carbonate, citrate)
- Vitamin D (cholecalciferol or calcitriol)
- Magnesium repletion

### For Hypercalcemia

**Acute severe (>14 mg/dL):**
- IV fluids (saline)
- Loop diuretics (after rehydration)
- Calcitonin (rapid, short-acting)
- Bisphosphonates (zoledronic acid)

**Chronic:**
- Treat underlying cause
- Surgery for primary hyperparathyroidism

## 6. CONNECTED BIOMARKERS

| Biomarker | Relationship | Pattern |
|-----------|--------------|---------|
| **PTH** | Primary regulator | Inverse relationship |
| **Vitamin D** | Absorption | Low D → low calcium |
| **Albumin** | Binding protein | Correct total calcium |
| **Phosphate** | Inverse balance | High Ca → low phosphate |
| **Magnesium** | Cofactor | Low Mg → low PTH → low Ca |
| **Creatinine** | Kidney function | CKD affects calcium |

### Clinical Patterns

**Primary Hyperparathyroidism:**
- Calcium: High
- PTH: High or inappropriately normal
- Phosphate: Low
- **Adenoma/hyperplasia**

**Vitamin D Deficiency:**
- Calcium: Low or low-normal
- PTH: Elevated
- Vitamin D: Low
- **Secondary hyperparathyroidism**

**Malignancy Hypercalcemia:**
- Calcium: High
- PTH: Low (suppressed)
- PTHrP: Elevated
- **Paraneoplastic**
