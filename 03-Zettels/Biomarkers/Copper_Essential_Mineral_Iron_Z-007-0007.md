# Copper — Essential Mineral, Iron Metabolism & Energy

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**Copper:** From Latin "cuprum" (Cyprus, where mined)

**Element symbol:** Cu (atomic number 29)

**Ceruloplasmin:** From Latin "caeruleus" (blue) — copper-containing protein

---

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**Essential trace mineral:**
| Function | Mechanism |
|----------|-----------|
| **Iron metabolism** | Ceruloplasmin oxidizes Fe2+ to Fe3+ for transport |
| **Energy production** | Cytochrome c oxidase (Complex IV) |
| **Connective tissue** | Lysyl oxidase (collagen/elastin cross-linking) |
| **Neurotransmitters** | Dopamine beta-hydroxylase, tyrosinase |
| **Antioxidant** | Copper-zinc SOD |
| **Melanin synthesis** | Tyrosinase enzyme |

**Copper transport:**
- Absorbed in intestine
- Bound to albumin in blood
- Delivered to liver
- Incorporated into ceruloplasmin
- Ceruloplasmin transports copper to tissues

---

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### Copper Levels

| Level (μg/dL) | Status | Interpretation |
|---------------|--------|----------------|
| **80-120** | Optimal | Adequate status |
| **70-80** | Low-normal | Suboptimal |
| **<70** | Deficient | Functional deficiency |
| **<50** | Severe | Significant deficiency |
| **>150** | High | Possible excess/Wilson's |

### Ceruloplasmin Levels

| Level (mg/dL) | Status |
|---------------|--------|
| **20-40** | Normal |
| **<20** | Low (copper deficiency or Wilson's) |
| **>50** | Elevated (inflammation, pregnancy, estrogen) |

### Clinical Significance

**Deficiency symptoms:**
- Anemia (iron-resistant)
- Neutropenia
- Osteoporosis
- Connective tissue problems
- Neurological symptoms
- Hypopigmentation

**Excess (Wilson's disease):**
- Liver disease
- Neurological symptoms
- Kayser-Fleischer rings
- Psychiatric symptoms

---

## 4. COFACTORS

### Copper Metabolism

| Nutrient | Role | Interaction |
|----------|------|-------------|
| **Zinc** | Antagonist | High zinc depletes copper |
| **Iron** | Requires copper | Copper needed for iron transport |
| **Molybdenum** | Antagonist | High molybdenum reduces copper |
| **Vitamin C** | Absorption | High dose may reduce absorption |

---

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### For Deficiency (<70 μg/dL)

**Supplementation:**
- Copper bisglycinate: 1-2 mg/day
- Balance with zinc (8-10:1 zinc:copper ratio)

**Food sources:**
- Oysters, shellfish
- Beef liver
- Dark chocolate
- Cashews
- Sunflower seeds

**Caution:**
- Don't supplement if zinc is high without balancing
- Check ceruloplasmin

### For Excess

**Wilson's disease:**
- Chelation therapy (D-penicillamine, trientine)
- Zinc acetate (blocks absorption)
- Low copper diet
- Liver transplant if severe

---

## 6. CONNECTED BIOMARKERS

| Biomarker | Relationship | Pattern |
|-----------|--------------|---------|
| **Zinc** | Antagonist | High zinc → low copper |
| **Ceruloplasmin** | Copper transport | Reflects copper status |
| **Iron/Ferritin** | Metabolism | Low copper → iron dysregulation |
| **Hemoglobin** | Anemia | Copper deficiency → anemia |
| **Neutrophils** | Immune | Copper deficiency → neutropenia |

### Clinical Patterns

**Copper Deficiency:**
- Copper: Low
- Ceruloplasmin: Low
- Iron: Normal/high (not usable)
- Hemoglobin: Low (anemia)
- **Iron-resistant anemia**

**Zinc-Induced Copper Deficiency:**
- Zinc: Very high (supplementation)
- Copper: Low
- Ceruloplasmin: Low
- **Iatrogenic deficiency**

**Wilson's Disease:**
- Copper: High (tissue)
- Ceruloplasmin: Low
- Urine copper: High
- **Genetic copper accumulation**
