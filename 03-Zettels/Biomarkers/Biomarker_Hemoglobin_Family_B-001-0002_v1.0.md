# Hemoglobin Family (Hb, Hct, MCV, MCH, MCHC, RDW) - B-001-0002

## 1. IDENTIFICATION

| Marker | Full Name | What It Measures |
|--------|-----------|------------------|
| **Hb** | Hemoglobin | Oxygen-carrying protein in RBCs |
| **Hct** | Hematocrit | % of blood volume that is RBCs |
| **MCV** | Mean Corpuscular Volume | Average RBC size |
| **MCH** | Mean Corpuscular Hemoglobin | Average Hb per RBC |
| **MCHC** | Mean Corpuscular Hb Concentration | Hb concentration in RBCs |
| **RDW** | Red Cell Distribution Width | Variation in RBC size |

**Specimen:** Whole blood (EDTA)
**Collection:** No fasting required

## 2. REFERENCE RANGES (Simos Clinical Targets)

### Hemoglobin (Hb) - Simos Clinical Targets
| Population | Minimum Acceptable | Simos Optimal | Notes |
|------------|-------------------|---------------|-------|
| Adult Male | >12.0 g/dL | **>13.0** | Ideally 14-16 |
| Adult Female | >12.0 g/dL | **>13.0** | Ideally 13-15 |
| Pregnancy | >11.0 g/dL | **>12.0** | Higher target for fetal oxygenation |

**Simos Rule: Hb should ALWAYS be >12, ideally >13**

### Hematocrit (Hct) - Simos Clinical Targets
| Population | Standard Range | Simos Optimal | Hct:Hb Ratio |
|------------|----------------|---------------|--------------|
| Adult Male | 38.3-48.6% | 40-48% | Should be ~3× Hb |
| Adult Female | 35.5-44.9% | 38-45% | Should be ~3× Hb |

**Simos Rule: Hct should be approximately 3× Hb**
- Example: Hb 14 → Hct should be ~42%
- **If Hct <3× Hb:** Check hydration status

**Dehydration Workup (when Hct:Hb ratio elevated):**
| Marker | Elevated In Dehydration | Notes |
|--------|------------------------|-------|
| **Urea (BUN)** | ↑ | Prerenal azotemia |
| **Uric acid** | ↑ | Concentrated |
| **Creatinine** | ↑ (mild) | Less than urea rise |
| **BUN:Creatinine ratio** | **>20:1** | Classic for prerenal |
| **Electrolytes** | ↑ sodium, chloride | Hemoconcentration |

**Clinical:** Low Hct relative to Hb = hemodilution OR check for hydration

### MCV (Mean Corpuscular Volume) - Simos Targets
| Range | Interpretation | Clinical Significance |
|-------|----------------|----------------------|
| <80 fL | Microcytic | Iron deficiency, thalassemia |
| 80-90 fL | **Optimal** | Ideal RBC size |
| 90-95 fL | Upper normal | Monitor trend |
| **>95 fL** | **Macrocytic** | **B12/folate deficiency, liver, thyroid** |

**Simos Rule: MCV should be <95, ideally <90**

**Simos Note:** Always interpret MCV with RDW

### MCH & MCHC
- **MCH:** 27-33 pg (correlates with MCV)
- **MCHC:** 32-36 g/dL (hypochromic <32)

### RDW (Red Cell Distribution Width) - Simos Target
| Range | Interpretation |
|-------|----------------|
| <14% | **Within range (optimal)** |
| 14-16% | Mild anisocytosis |
| >16% | Significant anisocytosis |

**Simos Rule: RDW should be within normal range (<14%)**

**Clinical Meaning:**
- **High RDW** = Mixed RBC population = Early deficiency OR response to treatment
- **Normal RDW** = Uniform RBC size = Chronic deficiency OR thalassemia
- **Always interpret with MCV**

## 3. PATTERN RECOGNITION (Simos Diagnostic Algorithm)

### Microcytic Anemia (MCV <80)
| Pattern | Indicates | Next Steps |
|---------|-----------|------------|
| ↓ MCV, ↓ MCH, ↑ RDW, ↓ Ferritin | **Iron deficiency** | Iron supplementation, find cause |
| ↓ MCV, ↓ MCH, **Normal RDW**, Normal ferritin | **Thalassemia trait** | Hemoglobin electrophoresis |
| ↓ MCV, ↓ MCH, ↑ Ferritin | **Anemia of chronic disease** | Check inflammation, chronic disease |

**Iron Deficiency Workup:**
- Ferritin (low <30)
- Transferrin saturation (low <20%)
- TIBC (high)
- Soluble transferrin receptor (high)
- **Causes:** Blood loss (GI, menstrual), malabsorption, low intake

### Macrocytic Anemia (MCV >100)
| Pattern | Indicates | Next Steps |
|---------|-----------|------------|
| ↑ MCV, **↑ RDW**, hypersegmented neutrophils | **B12/Folate deficiency** | Methylmalonic acid, homocysteine |
| ↑ MCV, normal RDW, liver enzymes ↑ | **Liver disease** | Liver function tests |
| ↑ MCV, normal RDW, TSH ↑ | **Hypothyroidism** | Thyroid panel |
| ↑ MCV, alcohol history | **Alcohol effect** | History, GGT, MCV often 100-110 |
| ↑ MCV, medications (methotrexate, Bactrim) | **Drug-induced** | Medication review |

**B12/Folate Deficiency Workup (Simos Protocol):**
- **Methylmalonic acid (MMA)** — functional B12 status
- **Homocysteine** — B12/folate/methylation
- **5-Methylfolate** — functional folate status
- **Intrinsic factor antibodies** — pernicious anemia
- **Parietal cell antibodies** — autoimmune gastritis
- **Gastrin** — high in pernicious anemia

### Normocytic Anemia (MCV 80-100)
| Pattern | Indicates | Next Steps |
|---------|-----------|------------|
| Normal MCV, ↓ Hb, ↑ Creatinine | **Renal anemia** | EPO level, kidney function |
| Normal MCV, ↓ Hb, chronic disease | **Anemia of chronic disease** | CRP, ferritin (normal/high), disease workup |
| Normal MCV, ↓ Hb, ↑ LDH, retic ↓ | **Bone marrow failure** | Marrow biopsy |
| Normal MCV, ↓ Hb, ↑ Retic | **Hemolysis** | LDH, haptoglobin, bilirubin, Coombs |

## 4. NUTRITIONAL CONNECTIONS

### Iron Deficiency
| Nutrient | Role | Deficiency Signs |
|----------|------|------------------|
| **Iron** | Hb synthesis, O2 transport | Fatigue, pallor, brittle nails, pica |
| **Copper** | Iron mobilization | Refractory iron deficiency |
| **Vitamin A** | Iron mobilization | Night blindness with anemia |
| **Vitamin C** | Iron absorption | Poor iron absorption |

**Iron Sources:**
- Heme: Liver, beef, oysters, clams (best absorbed)
- Non-heme: Lentils, spinach, fortified foods (absorb with vitamin C)

### B12/Folate Deficiency
| Nutrient | Role | Deficiency Signs |
|----------|------|------------------|
| **B12** | DNA synthesis, methylation | Fatigue, neuropathy, glossitis, dementia |
| **Folate** | DNA synthesis | Fatigue, glossitis, neural tube defects (pregnancy) |

**B12 Sources:** Liver, clams, beef, eggs, dairy
**Folate Sources:** Liver, legumes, leafy greens, fortified grains

### Other Nutrients
| Nutrient | Role in RBC Production |
|----------|------------------------|
| **Vitamin B6** | Heme synthesis |
| **Vitamin E** | RBC membrane stability |
| **Riboflavin (B2)** | Iron absorption, RBC production |
| **Protein** | Globin chain synthesis |

## 5. SUPPLEMENT INTERVENTIONS

### Iron Deficiency
| Supplement | Dose | Notes |
|------------|------|-------|
| **Iron bisglycinate** | 25-50 mg elemental | Best tolerated, absorb with vitamin C |
| **Iron sulfate** | 65 mg elemental | Cheap, more GI side effects |
| **Vitamin C** | 250-500 mg | Enhances absorption |
| **Copper** | 1-2 mg | If refractory to iron |

**Monitoring:** Recheck Hb, ferritin in 8 weeks

### B12 Deficiency
| Supplement | Dose | Notes |
|------------|------|-------|
| **Methylcobalamin** | 1000 mcg sublingual | Active form, neurological protection |
| **Hydroxocobalamin** | 1000 mcg IM | Severe deficiency, pernicious anemia |
| **B-complex** | With other B vitamins | Cofactors for methylation |

**Monitoring:** MMA, homocysteine, Hb in 8-12 weeks

### Folate Deficiency
| Supplement | Dose | Notes |
|------------|------|-------|
| **Methylfolate (5-MTHF)** | 800-1600 mcg | Active form, bypasses MTHFR |
| **Folic acid** | 1 mg | Only if no MTHFR variant |
| **B12 ALWAYS with folate** | 1000 mcg | Prevents masking B12 deficiency |

**CRITICAL:** Never give folate alone — can mask/worsen B12 deficiency neuropathy

## 6. RED FLAGS

🚩 **Immediate Action:**
- Hb <7 g/dL (severe anemia)
- Hb <8 g/dL with cardiac symptoms
- Acute drop >2 g/dL (bleeding)
- Pancytopenia (all cell lines low)

⚠️ **Urgent Workup:**
- Iron deficiency without clear cause → GI workup (colonoscopy, endoscopy)
- B12 deficiency → pernicious anemia workup
- Macrocytosis with neurological symptoms

## 7. MEMORY AIDS

**MCV Classification:**
- **Micro**cytic = **Micro** = **M**issing **I**ron
- **Macro**cytic = **Macro** = **M**issing **B**12

**RDW Interpretation:**
- **High RDW** = Mixed population = Early deficiency or response to treatment
- **Normal RDW** = Uniform population = Chronic deficiency or thalassemia

**Iron Studies Pattern:**
- **Ferritin low** = Iron deficiency
- **Ferritin normal/high + low iron** = Anemia of chronic disease
- **Ferritin high** = Iron overload (hemochromatosis)
