# C15 Pentadecanoic Acid — Odd-Chain Saturated Fat Marker

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**Pentadecanoic acid:** From Greek "pente" (five) + "deca" (ten) = 15 carbons

**C15:** 15-carbon fatty acid

**Also called:**
- C15:0 (saturated)

source-litnote: Synthesized from clinical practice
---

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**Odd-chain saturated fatty acid:**
- Not synthesized by humans
- Must come from diet
- **Marker of dairy fat intake**

**Emerging research:**
- Inversely associated with metabolic disease
- Cardioprotective associations
- **Essential fatty acid candidate**

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### C15 Levels

| Level (% of total fatty acids) | Status | Interpretation |
|--------------------------------|--------|----------------|
| **<0.2%** | Low | Low dairy intake |
| **0.2-0.5%** | Optimal | Adequate dairy fat |
| **>0.5%** | High | High dairy intake |

### Clinical Significance

**Low C15 associated with:**
- Low dairy consumption
- Higher metabolic disease risk (emerging data)

**C15 as biomarker:**
- Objective measure of dairy fat intake
- More reliable than dietary recall

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### For Low C15

**Increase dairy fat (if tolerated):**
- Butter
- Whole milk
- Cheese
- Full-fat yogurt

**Or:**
- C15 supplement (emerging)
