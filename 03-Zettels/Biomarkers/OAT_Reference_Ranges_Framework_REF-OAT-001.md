# OAT Reference Ranges and Interpretation Framework

*Extracted from legacy clinic prototype — formalized for TheOptimizedBrain v4.1*

---

## 1. OPTIMAL RANGES (Ideal Targets)

### Krebs Cycle — Energy Production Markers
| Metabolite | Unit | Optimal Range | Notes |
|------------|------|---------------|-------|
| **Citric** | mmol/mol Crea | 67.0 – 171.0 | Entry marker; low = insufficient substrate, high = block |
| **Aconitic** | mmol/mol Crea | 6.0 – 22.0 | Early Krebs; citric:aconitic ratio should be <20% difference |
| **Isocitric** | mmol/mol Crea | 3.0 – 6.5 | Oxidative stress/iron marker |
| **2-Ketoglutaric** | mmol/mol Crea | 2.7 – 10.5 | Nitrogen hub; B1/lipoic acid dependent |
| **Succinic** | mmol/mol Crea | 1.4 – 10.0 | Hypoxia/SDH marker; FADH2 transition |
| **Fumaric** | mmol/mol Crea | Not detectable | Urea cycle link; any detection = stress |
| **Malic** | mmol/mol Crea | Not detectable | Redox shuttle; any detection = stress |

### Carbohydrate Metabolism Markers
| Metabolite | Unit | Optimal Range | Notes |
|------------|------|---------------|-------|
| **Lactic** | mmol/mol Crea | < 25.0 | Hypoxia/metabolic stress marker |
| **Pyruvic** | mmol/mol Crea | < 15.0 | Glycolysis endpoint; entry to Krebs |
| **3-Hydroxybutyric** | mmol/mol Crea | Not detectable | Ketone; indicates fat burning or carb excess issues |
| **3-Hydroxy-3-methylglutaric** | mmol/mol Crea | 1.4 – 2.9 | CoQ10/B3 marker; cholesterol synthesis pathway |

## 2. CLINICAL INTERPRETATION PATTERNS

### Citrate Patterns
| Finding | Interpretation | Mechanism |
|---------|---------------|-----------|
| **Low Citrate** | Insufficient substrate | Amino acid deficiency (especially glutamine), magnesium deficiency, increased oxidation, possible iron deficiency; indicates digestive difficulty and malabsorption |
| **Normal Citrate** | Adequate supply | Entry marker within desired limits |
| **High Citrate** | Block in cycle | Oxidation, mitochondrial dysfunction, amino acid deficiency (glutamine), possible iron deficiency |

### Aconitate Patterns
| Finding | Interpretation | Mechanism |
|---------|---------------|-----------|
| **Block detected** | Aconitase impairment | Increased oxidation, glutamine deficiency |
| **Normal** | Adequate flux | Within desired range |
| **Ratio check** | Citric:Aconitic difference should be <20% | Greater difference indicates aconitase dysfunction |

### Full Krebs Block Pattern
When multiple markers show blocks (Isocitric, 2-Ketoglutaric, Succinic elevated; Fumaric/Malic detectable):
- **Mechanism:** Mitochondrial dysfunction
- **Deficiencies:** B-complex vitamins, manganese, magnesium, CoQ10

### HMG (3-Hydroxy-3-methylglutaric) Interpretation
| Finding | Interpretation | Mechanism |
|---------|---------------|-----------|
| **Block detected** | CoQ10/B3 deficiency | CoQ10 works with molecular oxygen for mitochondrial function; primary energy extraction and cellular antioxidant |
| **Normal** | Adequate | Within desired range |

### Carbohydrate Metabolism Interpretation
| Pattern | Interpretation | Mechanism |
|---------|---------------|-----------|
| **Easy metabolism** | Both Lactic and Pyruvic low, no ketones | Efficient glucose utilization |
| **Difficulty with simple carbohydrates** | 3-Hydroxybutyric elevated OR Lactic/Pyruvic elevated | Excess carb intake (sugar, honey, bread, potatoes, rice, fruit, juice, soda), insulin resistance, α-lipoic acid deficiency, B-complex deficiency |

### Metabolic Stress Cascade
Blocks in Krebs cycle → metabolic stress → body struggles metabolically → compensatory hormone release:
- **Dopamine increase**
- **Adrenaline increase**  
- **Cortisol increase**

**Effects of elevated stress hormones:**
- Digestive impairment
- Altered gastric/intestinal motility
- Mood changes
- Insulin resistance
- Lipid metabolism disruption
- Protein metabolism disruption
- Calcium metabolism disruption
- Bacterial flora alteration
- Alternating hyperarousal and exhaustion

## 3. INTERVENTIONS

### For Krebs Cycle Blocks
| Deficiency | Nutrient | Role |
|------------|----------|------|
| Amino acids | Glutamine | Krebs substrate, nitrogen donor |
| Minerals | Magnesium | Citrate synthase, multiple enzymes |
| Minerals | Manganese | Mitochondrial SOD, urea cycle |
| Minerals | Iron | Aconitase, SDH (Fe-S clusters) |
| Vitamins | B-complex | Multiple cofactors (B1, B2, B3, B5) |
| Antioxidant | CoQ10 | Electron transport, cellular antioxidant |
| Antioxidant | α-Lipoic acid | Mitochondrial antioxidant, glucose metabolism |

### For Carbohydrate Metabolism Issues
| Issue | Intervention |
|-------|-------------|
| Excess carb intake | Dietary modification (reduce sugar, refined carbs, fruit juice, soda) |
| Insulin resistance | α-Lipoic acid, B-complex, dietary intervention |
| Nutrient deficiency | α-Lipoic acid supplementation, B-complex |

---

## 4. PROGRESSIVE TESTING CONCEPT

**Key Principle:** As current deficiencies are corrected and metabolic pathway nutrient supply improves, retests may reveal additional deficiencies or metabolic disturbances — **increasing sensitivity of results**.

**Interpretation:**
- First test shows current layer of dysfunction
- Correction allows deeper/more subtle patterns to emerge
- This is expected and desirable — indicates improving metabolic function
