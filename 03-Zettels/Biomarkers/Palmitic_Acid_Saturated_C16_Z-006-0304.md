# Palmitic Acid — Saturated Fat, C16:0

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**Palmitic acid:** From "palm" (palm oil)

**Also called:**
- C16:0 (16 carbons, saturated)
- Palmitate

source-litnote: Synthesized from clinical practice
---

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**Most abundant saturated fatty acid:**
- Found in palm oil, animal fats
- Endogenously synthesized (de novo lipogenesis)

**Metabolic significance:**
- Elevated with high carbohydrate intake
- Pro-inflammatory
- Raises LDL cholesterol

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### Palmitic Acid Levels

| Level (% of total fatty acids) | Status | Interpretation |
|--------------------------------|--------|----------------|
| **15-25%** | Normal | Typical range |
| **>30%** | High | High saturated fat or carb intake |

### Clinical Significance

**High palmitic associated with:**
- High carbohydrate diet (converted to fat)
- High saturated fat intake
- Insulin resistance
- Inflammation

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### For High Palmitic

**Reduce sources:**
- Limit palm oil
- Moderate saturated fat
- Reduce refined carbohydrates

**Increase:**
- Omega-3 fatty acids
- MUFAs (olive oil)
