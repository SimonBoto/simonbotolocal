# Anti-TPO — Thyroid Peroxidase Antibodies

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**Anti-TPO:** Antibodies against Thyroid Peroxidase

**Thyroid Peroxidase (TPO):**
- Enzyme in thyroid hormone synthesis
- Iodinates tyrosine residues
- Couples iodotyrosines to form T3/T4

**Also called:**
- TPO antibodies
- Antimicrosomal antibodies (older term)

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**Autoantibodies:**
- Produced by immune system
- Attack thyroid peroxidase enzyme
- Marker of autoimmune thyroid disease
- **Not normally present**

**Pathophysiology:**
- Antibodies → lymphocytic infiltration → thyroid destruction
- Gradual loss of thyroid function
- May cause transient hyperthyroidism (Hashitoxicosis)
- Eventually leads to hypothyroidism

### Conditions Associated

**Hashimoto's thyroiditis:**
- 90-95% have elevated Anti-TPO
- Most common cause of hypothyroidism
- Chronic lymphocytic thyroiditis

**Graves' disease:**
- 70-80% have elevated Anti-TPO
- Also has TSI/TRAb antibodies

**Postpartum thyroiditis:**
- 50% have elevated Anti-TPO
- Transient dysfunction after pregnancy

**General population:**
- 10-15% have elevated Anti-TPO (often subclinical)
- More common in women
- Increases with age

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### Anti-TPO Levels

| Level (IU/mL) | Status | Interpretation |
|---------------|--------|----------------|
| **<10** | Negative | No autoimmune thyroid disease |
| **10-35** | Borderline | Possible early autoimmune process |
| **35-100** | Positive | Autoimmune thyroid disease likely |
| **>100** | Strongly positive | Active autoimmune thyroiditis |
| **>1000** | Very high | Severe autoimmune activity |

### Clinical Significance

**Positive Anti-TPO indicates:**
- Autoimmune thyroid disease present or developing
- Risk of progression to hypothyroidism
- May have normal thyroid function currently
- Higher risk in pregnancy (postpartum thyroiditis)

**Negative Anti-TPO:**
- Does not rule out autoimmune disease (check Anti-Tg)
- Other causes of thyroid dysfunction

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### For Elevated Anti-TPO

**No specific treatment to lower antibodies:**
- Focus on thyroid function management
- Some evidence for selenium (200 mcg/day)
- Vitamin D optimization
- Gluten-free diet (controversial, may help some)

**Monitor thyroid function:**
- Check TSH, Free T4 every 6-12 months
- Treat hypothyroidism if develops
- Earlier intervention if pregnancy planned

**Pregnancy considerations:**
- Higher risk of postpartum thyroiditis
- Monitor closely during and after pregnancy
- Levothyroxine may be started if TSH elevated

## 6. CONNECTED BIOMARKERS

| Biomarker | Relationship | Pattern |
|-----------|--------------|---------|
| **Anti-Tg** | Another thyroid antibody | Both positive = strong autoimmune |
| **TSH** | Thyroid function | May be normal early, elevated later |
| **Free T4** | Thyroid output | Low if hypothyroidism developed |
| **TSI/TRAb** | Graves' specific | Positive = Graves' disease |

### Clinical Patterns

**Hashimoto's Thyroiditis:**
- Anti-TPO: Positive (usually high)
- Anti-Tg: May be positive
- TSH: Elevated (or normal early)
- Free T4: Low or normal
- **Chronic autoimmune destruction**

**Graves' Disease:**
- Anti-TPO: Positive (70-80%)
- TSI/TRAb: Positive
- TSH: Suppressed
- Free T4: Elevated
- **Stimulating autoimmunity**

**Euthyroid with Antibodies:**
- Anti-TPO: Positive
- TSH: Normal
- Free T4: Normal
- **Risk of future hypothyroidism**
