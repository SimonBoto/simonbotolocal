# Glycolic Acid — Protein Digestion & SIBO Marker

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**Glycolic acid:** From "glyco-" (sweet) — simplest alpha-hydroxy acid

**Also called:**
- Glycolate
- Hydroxyacetic acid

source-litnote: Synthesized from clinical practice
---

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**Multiple sources:**
1. **Protein digestion** — amino acid metabolite
2. **SIBO** — bacterial production
3. **Oxalate metabolism** — glyoxylate → glycolate → oxalate

**Clinical significance:**
- Marker of protein malabsorption
- SIBO indicator
- Oxalate precursor

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### Glycolic Acid Levels

| Level | Status | Interpretation |
|-------|--------|----------------|
| **Normal** | Low | Normal digestion |
| **Elevated** | High | Protein malabsorption or SIBO |

### Causes of Elevation

**Protein malabsorption:**
| Cause | Mechanism |
|-------|-----------|
| **Low stomach acid** | Impaired protein digestion |
| **Pancreatic insufficiency** | Reduced proteases |
| **Gut dysbiosis** | Bacterial fermentation |
| **SIBO** | Bacterial overgrowth |

**Oxalate metabolism:**
- Glyoxylate → glycolate → oxalate
- Elevated glycolate may precede oxalate elevation

## 4. COFACTORS

### Digestive Support

| Factor | Role |
|--------|------|
| **Stomach acid** | Protein denaturation |
| **Pepsin** | Protein breakdown |
| **Pancreatic enzymes** | Protein digestion |
| **Brush border enzymes** | Final digestion |

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### For Elevated Glycolic Acid

**Digestive support:**
- Betaine HCl (if low stomach acid)
- Digestive enzymes (proteases)
- Pancreatic enzymes (if insufficiency)

**SIBO treatment:**
- Rifaximin or herbal antimicrobials
- Prokinetics
- Dietary modification

**Oxalate management:**
- If elevated with oxalate
- B6 supplementation
- Probiotic support (Oxalobacter)
