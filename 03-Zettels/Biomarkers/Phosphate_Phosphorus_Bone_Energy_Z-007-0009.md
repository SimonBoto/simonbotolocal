# Phosphate — Phosphorus, Bone & Energy Metabolism

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**Phosphate:** From Greek "phosphoros" (light-bearer)

**Phosphorus:** Element name

**Also called:**
- PO4
- Inorganic phosphorus
- Phosphorus

---

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**Essential for multiple systems:**
| Function | Mechanism |
|----------|-----------|
| **Bone health** | Hydroxyapatite (Ca-P crystals) |
| **Energy metabolism** | ATP, creatine phosphate |
| **Cell membranes** | Phospholipids |
| **DNA/RNA** | Sugar-phosphate backbone |
| **Acid-base balance** | Buffer system |
| **Cell signaling** | Phosphorylation |

**Regulation:**
- PTH: Decreases phosphate (increases excretion)
- Vitamin D: Increases phosphate (increases absorption)
- FGF-23: Decreases phosphate

---

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### Phosphate Levels

| Level (mg/dL) | Status | Interpretation |
|---------------|--------|----------------|
| **3.0-4.5** | Optimal | Normal balance |
| **2.5-3.0** | Low-normal | Mild decrease |
| **<2.5** | Low | Hypophosphatemia |
| **<1.0** | Severe | Critical |
| **>4.5** | High | Hyperphosphatemia |

### Causes of Low Phosphate (Hypophosphatemia)

| Cause | Mechanism |
|-------|-----------|
| **Malnutrition** | Poor intake |
| **Vitamin D deficiency** | Impaired absorption |
| **Hyperparathyroidism** | Increased renal excretion |
| **Alcoholism** | Poor intake, renal losses |
| **Diabetic ketoacidosis** | Cellular uptake |
| **Respiratory alkalosis** | Cellular shift |
| **Diuretics** | Renal losses |
| **Refeeding syndrome** | Cellular uptake |

### Causes of High Phosphate (Hyperphosphatemia)

| Cause | Mechanism |
|-------|-----------|
| **Chronic kidney disease** | Reduced excretion |
| **Hypoparathyroidism** | Reduced renal excretion |
| **Vitamin D toxicity** | Increased absorption |
| **Tumor lysis syndrome** | Cellular release |
| **Phosphate supplements** | Excessive intake |

---

## 4. COFACTORS

### Phosphate Metabolism

| Nutrient | Role | Interaction |
|----------|------|-------------|
| **Calcium** | Bone mineralization | Inverse relationship |
| **Vitamin D** | Absorption | Increases phosphate |
| **PTH** | Regulation | Decreases phosphate |
| **Magnesium** | Cofactor | Required for PTH function |

---

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### For Low Phosphate (<2.5 mg/dL)

**Supplementation:**
- Phosphate salts: 500-1000 mg/day
- Vitamin D if deficient
- Address underlying cause

**Food sources:**
- Dairy
- Meat
- Fish
- Nuts
- Beans

**Caution:**
- Monitor calcium (risk of hypocalcemia)
- Check kidney function

### For High Phosphate (>4.5 mg/dL)

**CKD management:**
- Phosphate binders (calcium acetate, sevelamer)
- Dietary restriction
- Vitamin D analogs

---

## 6. CONNECTED BIOMARKERS

| Biomarker | Relationship | Pattern |
|-----------|--------------|---------|
| **Calcium** | Bone partner | Ca × P product important |
| **PTH** | Regulator | Inverse relationship |
| **Vitamin D** | Absorption | Direct relationship |
| **Magnesium** | Cofactor | Low Mg impairs PTH |
| **Creatinine** | Kidney function | Elevated in CKD |

### Clinical Patterns

**CKD-Mineral Bone Disorder:**
- Phosphate: High
- Calcium: Low
- PTH: High
- Vitamin D: Low
- **Renal osteodystrophy**

**Hyperparathyroidism:**
- Phosphate: Low
- Calcium: High
- PTH: High
- **Parathyroid overactivity**

**Vitamin D Deficiency:**
- Phosphate: Low
- Calcium: Low/normal
- PTH: Elevated
- Vitamin D: Low
- **Secondary hyperparathyroidism**
