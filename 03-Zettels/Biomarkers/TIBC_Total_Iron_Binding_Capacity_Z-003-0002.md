# TIBC — Total Iron Binding Capacity

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**TIBC:** Total Iron Binding Capacity  
**Transferrin:** From Latin "transferre" (to carry across) + "-in" (protein)

**Discovery:** 1940s — identification of iron-transporting protein in blood

**Evolution:**
- Initially: "siderophilin" (iron-loving)
- Later: β-1 metal-binding globulin
- Now: Transferrin (primary name)

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**Primary Role: Iron Transport**
- Binds Fe³⁺ (ferric iron) in circulation
- Transports iron from:
  - Intestine (absorption) to tissues
  - Macrophages (recycling) to erythroid precursors
  - [[Ferritin_Iron_Storage_Systemic_Status_Z-003-0001]|Liver stores] to sites of use

**Transferrin Characteristics:**
- Molecular weight: 80 kDa
- Synthesized primarily in [[ALT_Alanine_Aminotransferase_Z-009-0001]|liver]
- Two iron-binding sites per molecule
- Binds Fe³⁺ with high affinity (pH-dependent release)

### Iron Kinetics

**Daily iron flux:**
- 25-30 mg iron recycled daily via transferrin
- Only 1-2 mg from diet (absorption)
- Transferrin makes 7-10 cycles/day

**Half-life:** 7-10 days (affected by iron status)

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### TIBC as Transferrin Amount

**TIBC indirectly measures transferrin concentration**

| TIBC | Interpretation |
|------|----------------|
| **High (>400 μg/dL)** | Transferrin elevated → body seeking iron |
| **Normal (250-400 μg/dL)** | Adequate transferrin |
| **Low (<250 μg/dL)** | Transferrin reduced → iron overload or inflammation |

### Patterns

**Iron Deficiency Pattern:**
- TIBC: **Elevated**
- Mechanism: Liver produces more transferrin to capture scarce iron
- Like calling more taxis when there aren't enough passengers

**Iron Overload/Inflammation Pattern:**
- TIBC: **Reduced**
- Mechanism: Liver downregulates transferrin synthesis
- Inflammation → hepcidin elevation → reduced iron mobilization → less transferrin needed

### TIBC vs. Transferrin Direct

| Measurement | Equivalent |
|-------------|------------|
| **TIBC** | Total binding sites available |
| **Transferrin (direct)** | Protein concentration |
| **Conversion** | TIBC ÷ 1.25 = Transferrin (mg/dL) |

## 4. COFACTORS

### Transferrin Synthesis Requirements

| Factor | Role | Deficiency Effect |
|--------|------|-------------------|
| **Liver function** | Transferrin synthesis site | Low TIBC in liver disease |
| **Protein status** | Amino acid availability | Low TIBC in malnutrition |
| **Iron status** | Regulatory signal | High TIBC when iron low |
| **Inflammation** | IL-6 suppresses synthesis | Low TIBC in inflammation |

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### For Elevated TIBC (Iron Deficiency)

**Address iron deficiency:**
- See [[Z-003-0001|Ferritin] interventions
- TIBC will normalize as iron status improves

### For Reduced TIBC

**Identify cause:**
1. **Inflammation** → Treat underlying
2. **Iron overload** → Phlebotomy if hemochromatosis
3. **Liver disease** → Hepatic support
4. **Malnutrition** → Protein repletion

## 6. CONNECTED BIOMARKERS

| Biomarker | Relationship | Pattern |
|-----------|--------------|---------|
| **Ferritin** | Storage vs. transport | High TIBC + low ferritin = deficiency |
| **Serum Iron** | What's being transported | Low iron + high TIBC = deficiency |
| **Transferrin Saturation** | Functional assessment | Calculated from TIBC + iron |
| **CRP** | Inflammation affects TIBC | High CRP + low TIBC = inflammatory block |
| **Liver enzymes** | Synthesis site | Elevated ALT/AST + low TIBC = hepatic dysfunction |

### Clinical Patterns

**Pattern 1: Iron Deficiency**
- TIBC: High (>400)
- Ferritin: Low (<30)
- Serum iron: Low
- Transferrin sat: Low (<20%)

**Pattern 2: Inflammatory Block**
- TIBC: Low-normal or low
- Ferritin: Normal or elevated
- Serum iron: Low
- CRP: Elevated
- **Iron trapped, not deficient**

**Pattern 3: Hemochromatosis**
- TIBC: Low-normal
- Ferritin: Very high (>1000)
- Transferrin sat: High (>50%)
- Serum iron: High
