# Free T3 — Triiodothyronine, Active Thyroid Hormone

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**T3:** Contains 3 iodine atoms

**Triiodothyronine:** From "tri-" (three) + "iodo-" (iodine) + "thyronine" (thyroid amino acid)

**Also called:**
- L-triiodothyronine
- Liothyronine (synthetic form)

**Discovery:** 1952 by Rosalind Pitt-Rivers and J. Gross

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**Most active thyroid hormone:**
- 3-4x more potent than T4
- Binds nuclear thyroid receptors
- Regulates gene expression
- Controls metabolic rate

**Production:**
- 20% directly from thyroid
- 80% from peripheral T4 conversion (deiodination)

**Actions:**
- Increases basal metabolic rate
- Stimulates protein synthesis
- Promotes carbohydrate metabolism
- Enhances lipolysis
- Critical for brain development
- Required for normal growth

### Transport

- >99% protein-bound (TBG, transthyretin)
- <1% free (active)
- **Free T3 measured clinically**

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### Free T3 Levels

| Level (pg/mL) | Status | Interpretation |
|---------------|--------|----------------|
| **3.0-4.0** | Optimal | Ideal function |
| **2.3-4.2** | Normal | Standard reference |
| **<2.3** | Low | Hypothyroidism or conversion issue |
| **>4.2** | High | Hyperthyroidism |

### Clinical Patterns

**Low Free T3 with Normal Free T4:**
- **Conversion problem** (common)
- Causes: Stress, illness, selenium deficiency, medications
- Symptoms: Hypothyroid despite "normal" T4

**T3 Toxicosis:**
- Free T3: Elevated
- Free T4: Normal
- TSH: Suppressed
- **Early or treated hyperthyroidism**

**Euthyroid Sick Syndrome:**
- Free T3: Low
- Free T4: Normal or low
- TSH: Normal or low
- **Non-thyroidal illness**

## 4. COFACTORS

### T3 Production Requirements

| Nutrient | Role | Deficiency Effect |
|----------|------|-------------------|
| **Selenium** | Deiodinase cofactor | Poor T4→T3 conversion |
| **Zinc** | T3 receptor function | Impaired hormone action |
| **Iron** | TPO cofactor | Reduced synthesis |
| **Iodine** | Hormone component | Reduced production |

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### For Low Free T3

**Address conversion issues:**
- Selenium: 100-200 mcg/day
- Zinc: 15-30 mg/day
- Reduce stress
- Optimize sleep
- Review medications (beta-blockers, steroids)

**Liothyronine (T3) therapy:**
- Controversial
- May help some with conversion issues
- Risk: Cardiac, bone loss
- Use with caution

### For High Free T3

**Hyperthyroidism treatment:**
- Same as T4 elevation
- Often T3 elevates first or disproportionately

## 6. CONNECTED BIOMARKERS

| Biomarker | Relationship | Pattern |
|-----------|--------------|---------|
| **Free T4** | Prohormone | T4 converts to T3 |
| **TSH** | Feedback | Low T3 → elevated TSH |
| **Reverse T3** | Inactive competitor | High rT3 + low T3 = conversion blocked |
| **Selenium** | Cofactor | Deficiency impairs conversion |

### Clinical Patterns

**Conversion Problem:**
- Free T4: Normal
- Free T3: Low
- Reverse T3: Elevated
- Symptoms: Hypothyroid
- **T4 not converting to T3**

**T3 Toxicosis:**
- Free T3: High
- Free T4: Normal
- TSH: Suppressed
- **T3-predominant hyperthyroidism**
