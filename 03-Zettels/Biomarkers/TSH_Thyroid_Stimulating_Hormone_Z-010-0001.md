# TSH — Thyroid Stimulating Hormone

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**TSH:** Thyroid Stimulating Hormone

**Also called:** Thyrotropin

**Origin:** Greek "thyreos" (shield-shaped) + "trope" (turning/stimulating)

**Discovery:** 1950s — isolation and characterization

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**Pituitary hormone:**
- Synthesized in anterior pituitary
- Stimulates thyroid gland to produce T3/T4
- Primary regulator of thyroid function

**Hypothalamic-pituitary-thyroid axis:**
```
Hypothalamus (TRH) → Pituitary (TSH) → Thyroid (T3/T4)
                        ↑___________________________|
                              (Negative feedback)
```

**Negative feedback:**
- High T3/T4 → suppresses TSH
- Low T3/T4 → stimulates TSH

### TSH Secretion

**Pulsatile:**
- Higher amplitude at night
- Peak around 2-4 AM
- Nadir in afternoon

**Influenced by:**
- Circadian rhythm
- Stress (can transiently elevate)
- Fasting (can transiently elevate)
- Illness (can suppress)

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### TSH Levels

| Level (mIU/L) | Status | Interpretation |
|---------------|--------|----------------|
| **0.5-2.0** | Optimal | Ideal thyroid function |
| **0.4-4.0** | Normal | Standard reference |
| **4.0-10.0** | Subclinical hypothyroidism | Early thyroid dysfunction |
| **>10.0** | Overt hypothyroidism | Clear deficiency |
| **<0.4** | Hyperthyroidism | Excess thyroid hormone |
| **<0.01** | Suppressed | Significant excess |

### Clinical Patterns

**Primary Hypothyroidism (thyroid failure):**
- TSH: Elevated
- Free T4: Low
- **Most common cause:** Hashimoto's thyroiditis

**Subclinical Hypothyroidism:**
- TSH: Elevated (4-10)
- Free T4: Normal
- **May progress to overt, may resolve**

**Primary Hyperthyroidism:**
- TSH: Suppressed (<0.01)
- Free T4: Elevated
- **Causes:** Graves' disease, toxic nodule, thyroiditis

**Secondary Hypothyroidism (pituitary failure):**
- TSH: Low or inappropriately normal
- Free T4: Low
- **Pituitary problem, not thyroid**

**T3 Toxicosis:**
- TSH: Suppressed
- Free T4: Normal
- Free T3: Elevated
- **Early or treated hyperthyroidism**

## 4. COFACTORS

### Factors Affecting TSH

| Factor | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| **Time of day** | Higher in morning |
| **Fasting** | May elevate slightly |
| **Illness (euthyroid sick)** | Suppresses TSH |
| **Medications** | Dopamine, glucocorticoids suppress; lithium, amiodarone elevate |
| **Biotin** | Falsely suppresses (high dose) |

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### For Elevated TSH (Hypothyroidism)

**Subclinical (TSH 4-10):**
- Consider treatment if:
  - Symptoms present
  - Positive antibodies
  - Pregnancy planned
  - Cardiovascular disease
- Otherwise monitor

**Overt (TSH >10 or low Free T4):**
- Levothyroxine (T4)
- Start low, titrate up
- Target TSH 0.5-2.5

### For Suppressed TSH (Hyperthyroidism)

**Treatment depends on cause:**
- Antithyroid medications
- Radioactive iodine
- Surgery
- Beta-blockers for symptoms

## 6. CONNECTED BIOMARKERS

| Biomarker | Relationship | Pattern |
|-----------|--------------|---------|
| **Free T4** | Thyroid output | Inverse relationship with TSH |
| **Free T3** | Active hormone | May be abnormal when T4 normal |
| **Anti-TPO** | Autoimmune marker | Positive + high TSH = Hashimoto's |
| **Anti-Tg** | Autoimmune marker | Supports Hashimoto's diagnosis |
| **Reverse T3** | Inactive form | Elevated in illness, stress |

### Clinical Patterns

**Hashimoto's Thyroiditis:**
- TSH: Elevated
- Free T4: Low or normal
- Anti-TPO: Positive
- Anti-Tg: May be positive

**Graves' Disease:**
- TSH: Suppressed
- Free T4: Elevated
- TSI/TRAb: Positive

**Euthyroid Sick Syndrome:**
- TSH: Low-normal or suppressed
- Free T4: Low-normal
- Free T3: Low
- **Non-thyroidal illness**

## 10. CLINICAL WEIGHT

**Significance: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (5/5 — Critical)**

**Primary thyroid screening test**

**Best screening tool:**
- Sensitive to thyroid dysfunction
- Reflects feedback loop
- Inexpensive

**Limitations:**
- Doesn't distinguish primary vs. secondary
- Affected by non-thyroidal illness
- Must be interpreted with Free T4

**Always with:**
- Free T4
- Consider Free T3
- Anti-TPO if autoimmune suspected
