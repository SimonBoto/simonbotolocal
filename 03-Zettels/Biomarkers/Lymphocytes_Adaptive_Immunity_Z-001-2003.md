# Lymphocytes — Adaptive Immunity, B & T Cells

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**Lymphocyte:** From Latin "lympha" (water/spring) + Greek "kytos" (cell)

**Named for:** Found in lymph fluid

**Also called:**
- Lymphs
- Lymphoid cells

---

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**Adaptive immune cells:**
| Type | Percentage | Function |
|------|------------|----------|
| **T cells** | 60-80% of lymphocytes | Cell-mediated immunity |
| **B cells** | 10-20% | Antibody production |
| **NK cells** | 5-10% | Natural killer, viral defense |

**T cell subsets:**
| Subset | Function |
|--------|----------|
| **CD4+ (Helper T)** | Coordinate immune response |
| **CD8+ (Cytotoxic T)** | Kill infected cells |
| **Regulatory T** | Suppress autoimmunity |

**Long lifespan:**
- Memory cells: Years to lifetime
- Naive cells: Months to years

---

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### Lymphocyte Levels

| Level (%) | Absolute (K/μL) | Status | Interpretation |
|-----------|-----------------|--------|----------------|
| **25-35%** | **1.5-3.0** | Optimal | Normal adaptive immunity |
| **20-40%** | **1.0-4.0** | Normal | Standard range |
| **>40%** | **>4.0** | High | Lymphocytosis |
| **<20%** | **<1.0** | Low | Lymphopenia |

### Causes of Elevated Lymphocytes (Lymphocytosis)

| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| **Viral infection** | Most common (EBV, CMV, HIV acute) |
| **Atypical lymphocytes** | Infectious mononucleosis |
| **Chronic infections** | TB, toxoplasmosis |
| **Lymphoproliferative** | CLL, lymphoma |
| **Smoking** | Mild elevation |

### Causes of Low Lymphocytes (Lymphopenia)

| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| **Corticosteroids** | Most common cause |
| **Viral infections** | HIV, influenza |
| **Autoimmune** | Lupus, RA |
| **Immunodeficiency** | Primary or acquired |
| **Malnutrition** | Protein deficiency |

---

## 4. COFACTORS

### Lymphocyte Function Requirements

| Nutrient | Role | Deficiency Effect |
|----------|------|-------------------|
| **Zinc** | T cell function | Impaired immunity |
| **Vitamin D** | Immune regulation | Autoimmune risk |
| **Vitamin A** | Mucosal immunity | Impaired defense |
| **Vitamin C** | Immune function | Impaired response |
| **Selenium** | Antioxidant | Impaired function |
| **Protein** | Cell production | Lymphopenia |
| **B12/Folate** | DNA synthesis | Impaired proliferation |

---

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### For Lymphopenia

**Nutritional:**
- Zinc: 15-30 mg/day
- Vitamin D: 2000-5000 IU/day (target 50-80 ng/mL)
- Vitamin C: 1-2 g/day
- Selenium: 100-200 mcg/day
- Adequate protein

**Medical:**
- Reduce corticosteroids if possible
- Treat underlying infection
- IVIG if severe hypogammaglobulinemia

### For Lymphocytosis

**Address underlying cause:**
- Viral infection: Supportive care
- CLL: Oncology referral
- Monitor for transformation

## 6. CONNECTED BIOMARKERS

| Biomarker | Relationship | Pattern |
|-----------|--------------|---------|
| **WBC** | Total count | Lymphocytes component |
| **Neutrophils** | Other WBC | Inverse in viral vs bacterial |
| **CD4 count** | T helper cells | HIV monitoring |
| **CD4:CD8 ratio** | T cell balance | Normal ~1.5-2.5 |
| **Immunoglobulins** | B cell function | Hypogammaglobulinemia |

### Clinical Patterns

**Viral Infection:**
- Lymphocytes: Elevated
- Atypical lymphocytes: Present
- Neutrophils: Normal or low
- **Lymphocytic response**

**Bacterial Infection:**
- Lymphocytes: Normal or low
- Neutrophils: Elevated
- **Neutrophilic response**

**HIV Infection:**
- CD4 count: Decreased
- CD4:CD8 ratio: Inverted (<1)
- **Immunodeficiency**

**CLL:**
- Lymphocytes: Very high
- Smudge cells on smear
- **Monoclonal B cells**

## 9. PROBLEMATIC RANGE

| Level | Concern | Action |
|-------|---------|--------|
| **>4.0 K/μL** | Lymphocytosis | Evaluate for viral/CLL |
| **>5.0 K/μL** | Significant | CLL workup |
| **<1.0 K/μL** | Lymphopenia | Evaluate cause |
| **<0.5 K/μL** | Severe | Immunocompromised |
