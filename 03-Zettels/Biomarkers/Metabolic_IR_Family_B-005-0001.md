# Metabolic / Insulin Resistance Family - B-005-0001

## 1. IDENTIFICATION

| Marker | Full Name | What It Measures |
|--------|-----------|------------------|
| **Glucose** | Fasting Plasma Glucose | Blood sugar after 8-12h fast |
| **Insulin** | Fasting Insulin | Pancreatic insulin production |
| **HbA1c** | Glycated Hemoglobin | 3-month average glucose |
| **TG** | Triglycerides | Blood lipids (fat) |
| **Uric Acid** | Uric Acid | Purine metabolism, antioxidant |
| **Waist/Height** | Waist-to-Height Ratio | Central adiposity |

**Specimen:** Serum (fasting required for glucose, insulin, TG)
**Collection:** Morning, 8-12 hour fast

## 2. SIMOS CLINICAL TARGETS (INSULIN RESISTANCE SPECTRUM)

### Glucose (Fasting) - mg/dL
| Range | Simos Category | Interpretation | Action |
|-------|----------------|----------------|--------|
| **70-90** | **IDEAL** | Optimal metabolic health | Maintain |
| **90-100** | **Accepted** | Normal, monitor trend | Lifestyle focus |
| **100-110** | **High** | Early insulin resistance | Active intervention |
| **>110** | **Very high** | Significant IR, prediabetes | Aggressive treatment |
| **>126** | Diabetes threshold | Diabetes (confirm with repeat) | Medical management |

**Simos Rule: Glucose should be 70-90, accepted to 100, concern >100**
- **Every point >100 = worse metabolic health**
- **Analog increase in risk as glucose rises**

### Insulin (Fasting) - μIU/mL (or mU/L)
| Range | Simos Category | Interpretation | Action |
|-------|----------------|----------------|--------|
| **<7** | **IDEAL** | Optimal insulin sensitivity | Maintain |
| **7-10** | **Accepted** | Normal, watch trend | Lifestyle |
| **10-15** | **Elevated** | Early insulin resistance | Active intervention |
| **15-20** | **High** | Significant IR | Aggressive treatment |
| **>20** | **Very high** | Severe IR, β-cell stress | Urgent intervention |

**Simos Rule: Insulin should be <7, accepted <10, concern >10**
- **Every unit >10 = worse insulin sensitivity**
- **Analog increase in IR severity**
- **Hyperinsulinemia precedes hyperglycemia by years**

### HOMA-IR (Calculated)
| Range | Interpretation |
|-------|----------------|
| **<2.0** | Optimal insulin sensitivity |
| **2.0-2.5** | Accepted |
| **2.5-3.0** | Early insulin resistance |
| **>3.0** | Significant insulin resistance |
| **>4.0** | Severe insulin resistance |

**Formula:** (Glucose × Insulin) / 405 (US units)

### HbA1c - %
| Range | Simos Category | Interpretation | Action |
|-------|----------------|----------------|--------|
| **4.9-5.5** | **OPTIMAL** | Excellent glucose control | Maintain |
| **5.5-5.7** | **Accepted high** | Upper normal, monitor | Lifestyle focus |
| **5.7-5.8** | **Very high** | Prediabetes range | Active intervention |
| **>5.8** | **Worse** | Significant glycation | Aggressive treatment |
| **>6.5** | Diabetes threshold | Diabetes | Medical management |

**Simos Rule: HbA1c 4.9-5.5% optimal, accepted to 5.7%, concern >5.7%**
- **Every 0.1% >5.7 = worse metabolic health**
- **Analog increase in complication risk**

**Note:** HbA1c can be falsely low with anemia, high with iron deficiency

### Triglycerides - mg/dL
| Range | Simos Category | Interpretation |
|-------|----------------|----------------|
| **<65** | **IDEAL** | Optimal lipid metabolism |
| **65-90** | **Accepted** | Normal range |
| **90-150** | **Elevated** | Carbohydrate excess, IR |
| **150-200** | **High** | Significant dyslipidemia |
| **>200** | **Very high** | Severe hypertriglyceridemia |

**Simos Rule: TG should be <90, ideally <65**
- **TG <65 = excellent metabolic health**
- **TG >150 = significant carbohydrate excess**

**TG/HDL Ratio (important):**
| Ratio | Risk |
|-------|------|
| **<1.0** | Optimal |
| **<2.0** | Low risk |
| **2.0-4.0** | Moderate risk |
| **>4.0** | High risk |

### Uric Acid - mg/dL
| Range | Simos Category | Interpretation |
|-------|----------------|----------------|
| **<5.5** | **OPTIMAL** | Ideal purine metabolism |
| **5.5-6.5** | **Accepted** | Upper normal |
| **6.5-7.0** | **Elevated** | Early metabolic syndrome |
| **>7.0** | **High** | Significant risk |
| **>7.0** | **Worse** | Gout risk, CV risk, metabolic syndrome |

**Simos Rule: Uric acid <5.5 optimal, accepted <6.5, concern >7.0**
- **>7.0 = analog increase in worse outcomes**
- **Fructose metabolism drives uric acid**

### Waist-to-Height Ratio (WHtR)
| Ratio | Risk Category | Interpretation |
|-------|---------------|----------------|
| **<0.40** | Very low | Excellent |
| **0.40-0.50** | Healthy | Optimal body composition |
| **0.50-0.55** | Acceptable | Monitor |
| **0.55-0.60** | Increased | Overweight, central adiposity |
| **>0.60** | High | Obesity, high metabolic risk |

**Simos Rule: WHtR should be <0.50, ideally <0.50**
- **Universal measure (works across heights)**
- **Better than BMI for metabolic risk**

**Measurement:**
- Waist: At level of navel, after exhale
- Height: Standing straight
- Calculation: Waist (cm) / Height (cm)

## 3. INSULIN RESISTANCE PATHOPHYSIOLOGY

### The IR Spectrum (Simos Staging)

**Stage 0: Optimal (Metabolically Healthy)**
| Marker | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Glucose | 70-90 |
| Insulin | <7 |
| HbA1c | 4.9-5.5% |
| TG | <65 |
| Uric acid | <5.5 |
| WHtR | <0.50 |

**Stage 1: Early IR (Compensatory Hyperinsulinemia)**
| Marker | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Glucose | 90-100 (normal) |
| Insulin | 10-15 (↑) |
| HbA1c | 5.5-5.7% |
| TG | 90-150 |
| Uric acid | 5.5-6.5 |
| WHtR | 0.50-0.55 |

**Characteristics:**
- β-cells compensating with more insulin
- Glucose still normal
- This stage lasts YEARS
- **Critical intervention window**

**Stage 2: Prediabetes (Failing Compensation)**
| Marker | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Glucose | 100-126 |
| Insulin | 15-25 (high but failing) |
| HbA1c | 5.7-6.5% |
| TG | 150-200 |
| Uric acid | 6.5-7.0 |
| WHtR | 0.55-0.60 |

**Characteristics:**
- β-cells can't keep up
- Glucose rising
- Reversible with aggressive intervention

**Stage 3: Type 2 Diabetes (β-cell Exhaustion)**
| Marker | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Glucose | >126 |
| Insulin | Variable (may fall as β-cells fail) |
| HbA1c | >6.5% |
| TG | >200 |
| Uric acid | >7.0 |
| WHtR | >0.60 |

**Characteristics:**
- Significant β-cell dysfunction
- Medical management usually required
- Still reversible early with aggressive intervention

### Mechanisms of Insulin Resistance

**Lipotoxicity:**
- Excess fatty acids → ceramide accumulation
- Interferes with insulin signaling (IRS-1, Akt)
- [[Lipotoxicity_Pathway_IR_Z-001-0029]]

**Mitochondrial Dysfunction:**
- Impaired fatty acid oxidation
- Incomplete β-oxidation → adipic acid elevation
- [[Mitochondrial_Dysfunction_IR_Z-001-0015]]

**Inflammation:**
- Adipose tissue macrophages → cytokines (TNF-α, IL-6)
- Serine phosphorylation of IRS-1
- [[Inflammatory_Signaling_IR_Z-001-0031]]

**ER Stress:**
- Unfolded protein response
- JNK activation → IRS-1 serine phosphorylation
- [[ER_Stress_Induced_IR_Z-001-0014]]

**Gut Dysbiosis:**
- LPS translocation → metabolic endotoxemia
- TLR4 activation → inflammation
- [[Gut_Derived_Mechanisms_IR_Z-001-0032]]

## 4. METABOLOMICS CONNECTIONS

**When Metabolic Markers Abnormal, Check OAT:**

| Metabolic Finding | OAT Markers | Connection |
|-------------------|-------------|------------|
| **High glucose/insulin** | [[Adipic_Acid_Z-001-0041]] | Impaired β-oxidation |
| **High TG** | ] | Fatty acid transport |
| **High uric acid** | ] | Lactic acidosis, mitochondrial stress |
| **IR + fatigue** | [[Mitochondrial_Markers_Z-001-1067]] | Energy failure |
| **IR + brain fog** | [[BHB_Z-001-0483]] | Metabolic flexibility |

**Key OAT-Metabolic Correlations:**

**Adipic Acid (Early IR Marker):**
- Elevated in insulin resistance (before glucose rises)
- Indicates impaired mitochondrial fatty acid oxidation
- **More sensitive than fasting glucose**

**Beta-Hydroxybutyrate (BHB):**
- Low fasting BHB = impaired metabolic flexibility
- Should be >0.1 mmol/L in fasting state
- [[BHB_Hydroxy_Acid_Not_Ketone_Z-001-0483]]

**Organic Acids Pattern in IR:**
- Elevated adipic, suberic (impaired β-oxidation)
- Normal or low BHB (impaired ketogenesis)
- Elevated citric acid (mitochondrial stress)

**See also:** [[IR_Playbook_Clinical_Decision_Tool_S-001-0001] for complete integration

## 5. NUTRITIONAL CONNECTIONS

### Carbohydrate Impact
| Carb Type | Effect on IR | Recommendation |
|-----------|--------------|----------------|
| **Refined sugar** | ↑↑ Glucose, ↑↑ Insulin | Eliminate |
| **Refined grains** | ↑ Glucose, ↑ Insulin | Minimize |
| **Whole grains** | Moderate | Limit if IR |
| **Legumes** | Moderate | OK in moderation |
| **Non-starchy veg** | Minimal | Unlimited |
| **Fiber** | ↓ Glucose spike | Increase |

### Fat Impact
| Fat Type | Effect on IR | Recommendation |
|----------|--------------|----------------|
| **Trans fats** | ↑↑ IR | Eliminate |
| **Industrial seed oils** | ↑ Inflammation, ↑ IR | Eliminate |
| **Saturated (excess)** | ↑ Lipotoxicity | Moderate |
| **Monounsaturated** | Neutral/beneficial | OK |
| **Omega-3** | ↓ Inflammation, ↑ Sensitivity | Increase |

### Protein Impact
- Moderate protein: No significant glucose/insulin effect
- High protein: Minimal glucose, ↑ insulin (but less than carbs)
- **Recommendation:** 1.2-1.6 g/kg, distributed across meals

### Specific Nutrients
| Nutrient | Role in Glucose Metabolism | Deficiency Effect |
|----------|---------------------------|-------------------|
| **Chromium** | Insulin receptor sensitivity | Impaired glucose tolerance |
| **Magnesium** | Insulin receptor, glucose transport | Insulin resistance |
| **Zinc** | Insulin synthesis, secretion | Impaired insulin production |
| **Vanadium** | Insulin mimetic | (Supplement use) |
| **Alpha-lipoic acid** | Insulin sensitivity | Oxidative stress |
| **Berberine** | AMPK activation, insulin sensitivity | (Therapeutic use) |
| **Vitamin D** | Insulin receptor expression | Insulin resistance |

## 6. SUPPLEMENT INTERVENTIONS

### For Insulin Resistance (Glucose >100, Insulin >10)

**Tier 1: Foundation**
| Supplement | Dose | Mechanism | Evidence |
|------------|------|-----------|----------|
| **Magnesium** | 400-600 mg | Insulin receptor sensitivity | Strong |
| **Vitamin D** | 4000-10,000 IU | Insulin receptor expression | Strong |
| **Omega-3** | 2-4 g EPA+DHA | ↓ Inflammation, ↑ Sensitivity | Strong |
| **Chromium** | 200-1000 mcg | Glucose tolerance factor | Moderate |
| **Zinc** | 15-30 mg | Insulin synthesis | Moderate |

**Tier 2: Advanced**
| Supplement | Dose | Mechanism | Evidence |
|------------|------|-----------|----------|
| **Berberine** | 500 mg 2-3x/day | AMPK activation | Strong (metformin-like) |
| **Alpha-lipoic acid** | 300-600 mg | Insulin sensitivity, antioxidant | Strong |
| **Cinnamon** | 1-6 g | Insulin mimetic | Moderate |
| **Gymnema** | 200-400 mg | Glucose uptake | Traditional |
| **Fenugreek** | 5-10 g | ↓ Carb absorption | Moderate |

**Tier 3: Mitochondrial Support**
| Supplement | Dose | Mechanism |
|------------|------|-----------|
| **CoQ10** | 200-400 mg | Mitochondrial function |
| **B-vitamins** | B-complex | Energy metabolism |
| **Carnitine** | 1-2 g | Fatty acid oxidation |

### For High Triglycerides (>150)
| Supplement | Dose | Mechanism |
|------------|------|-----------|
| **Omega-3** | 2-4 g EPA+DHA | ↓ VLDL production |
| **Niacin** | 500-2000 mg | ↓ TG synthesis (flushing form) |
| **Berberine** | 500 mg 2-3x/day | ↓ Hepatic TG |
| **Low-carb diet** | <100g/day | ↓ TG production |

### For High Uric Acid (>7.0)
| Intervention | Mechanism |
|--------------|-----------|
| **Eliminate fructose** | Fructose → uric acid |
| **Reduce alcohol** | Especially beer, spirits |
| **Limit purines** | Organ meats, shellfish |
| **Vitamin C** | 500-1000 mg | ↑ Uric acid excretion |
| **Cherry extract** | Traditional uric acid lowering |
| **Quercetin** | ↓ Uric acid production |

## 7. LIFESTYLE INTERVENTIONS

### Diet (Most Important)
| Approach | Target | Effectiveness |
|----------|--------|---------------|
| **Low-carb** | <100g/day | Very high |
| **Ketogenic** | <50g/day | Very high |
| **Time-restricted eating** | 16:8 or 20:4 | High |
| **Mediterranean** | Whole foods, olive oil | Moderate |
| **Eliminate sugar** | All added sugars | Essential |
| **Eliminate seed oils** | Industrial omega-6 | Essential |

### Exercise
| Type | Effect on IR | Recommendation |
|------|--------------|----------------|
| **Resistance training** | ↑↑ Muscle insulin sensitivity | 3x/week minimum |
| **HIIT** | ↑ Mitochondrial function | 2x/week |
| **Walking post-meal** | ↓ Glucose spike | 10-15 min after meals |
| **NEAT** | ↑ Calorie expenditure | Move throughout day |

### Sleep
| Aspect | Target | Effect on IR |
|--------|--------|--------------|
| **Duration** | 7-9 hours | <6h = ↑ IR |
| **Quality** | Uninterrupted | Poor sleep = ↑ cortisol, ↑ glucose |
| **Timing** | Regular schedule | Circadian disruption = ↑ IR |

### Stress
| Factor | Effect | Management |
|--------|--------|------------|
| **Chronic stress** | ↑ Cortisol, ↑ glucose | Meditation, nature, social |
| **Poor sleep** | ↑ IR | Sleep hygiene |
| **Sedentary** | ↓ Muscle glucose uptake | Movement breaks |

## 8. RED FLAGS

🚩 **Critical:**
- Glucose >200 (diabetic emergency risk)
- HbA1c >9% (poor control, complications)
- TG >500 (pancreatitis risk)
- Uric acid >9 (gout, kidney stones)

⚠️ **Urgent:**
- Glucose 126-200 (diabetes range)
- Insulin >20 (severe IR)
- TG 200-500 (high risk)
- Rapid rise in any marker

## 9. MONITORING PROTOCOL

### Initial Assessment
- Fasting glucose, insulin, HbA1c
- Lipid panel (TG, HDL, LDL)
- Uric acid
- Waist circumference
- Weight
- OAT (adipic acid, BHB)

### Follow-up
| Timeline | Check | Target |
|----------|-------|--------|
| 4 weeks | Glucose, insulin | Trend down |
| 8 weeks | HbA1c | Should improve |
| 12 weeks | Complete panel | All optimal |
| 3 months | OAT | Adipic ↓, BHB ↑ |

### Success Markers
| Marker | Target Achieved |
|--------|-----------------|
| Glucose | <90 |
| Insulin | <7 |
| HbA1c | <5.5% |
| TG | <90 (ideally <65) |
| Uric acid | <5.5 |
| Weight | 5-10% loss if overweight |
| WHtR | <0.50 |
