# Methylation Family - B-004-0001

## 1. IDENTIFICATION

| Marker | Full Name | What It Measures |
|--------|-----------|------------------|
| **5-MTHF** | 5-Methyltetrahydrofolate | Active folate (functional status) |
| **MMA** | Methylmalonic Acid | Functional B12 status |
| **Homocysteine** | Homocysteine | Methylation efficiency, CV risk |
| **Active B12** | Holotranscobalamin | B12 available for cells |

**Specimen:** Serum (5-MTHF, Active B12), Serum/Urine (MMA), Plasma (Homocysteine)
**Collection:** Fasting preferred for homocysteine

## 2. SIMOS FUNCTIONAL TARGETS (CRITICAL)

### 5-Methylfolate (5-MTHF) - ACTIVE FOLATE
| Range | Interpretation | Action |
|-------|----------------|--------|
| **<10 ng/mL** | Deficiency | Supplement 5-MTHF |
| **10-25** | Suboptimal | Consider supplementation |
| **>25** | **OPTIMAL** | **Simos target achieved** |
| **>40** | High | Check B12 status (masking risk) |

**Simos Rule: 5-MTHF should be >25 ng/mL**
- **Why not folic acid?** Folic acid is synthetic, not biologically active
- **Why not serum folate?** Measures all forms, not just active
- **5-MTHF is the only form that matters for methylation**

### Methylmalonic Acid (MMA) - FUNCTIONAL B12
| Range | Interpretation | Action |
|-------|----------------|--------|
| **Detectable** | B12 insufficiency | Supplement methylcobalamin |
| **Low-normal** | Borderline | Monitor, consider B12 |
| **UNDETECTABLE** | **OPTIMAL** | **Simos target** |
| **Elevated** | Deficiency | Urgent B12 replacement |

**Simos Rule: MMA should be UNDETECTABLE**
- **Why not serum B12?** Serum B12 is artifact — measures inactive forms, supplement carryover
- **Why not holotranscobalamin?** Better than serum B12 but still not functional
- **MMA is the ONLY true functional B12 marker**
- **MMA accumulates when B12 insufficient for methionine synthase**

### Homocysteine - METHYLATION EFFICIENCY
| Range | Interpretation | CV Risk | Action |
|-------|----------------|---------|--------|
| **<5 μmol/L** | **IDEAL** | Minimal | Maintain |
| **5-7** | **OPTIMAL** | Low | Acceptable |
| **7-10** | Elevated | Moderate | Address methylation |
| **10-15** | High | High | Active intervention |
| **>15** | Very high | Very high | Urgent treatment |

**Simos Rules:**
- **Max: 7 μmol/L**
- **Ideally: <5 μmol/L**
- **Every 1 μmol/L increase = ~10% CV risk increase**

### Active B12 (Holotranscobalamin) - OPTIONAL
| Range | Notes |
|-------|-------|
| **>50 pmol/L** | Adequate cellular delivery |
| **35-50** | Borderline |
| **<35** | Deficiency |

**Note:** Active B12 is better than serum B12 but MMA is still superior for functional assessment

## 3. METHYLATION BIOCHEMISTRY

### The Methionine Cycle
```
Methionine → SAM (S-adenosylmethionine) → [METHYLATION REACTIONS] → SAH → Homocysteine
                                                            ↓
                              5-MTHF + B12 ←—— Methionine Synthase ——→ Methionine (recycled)
                                  ↑
                            MTHFR enzyme (folate metabolism)
```

**Key Enzymes:**
| Enzyme | Function | Cofactors | Polymorphisms |
|--------|----------|-----------|---------------|
| **MTHFR** | Converts folate to 5-MTHF | Riboflavin (B2) | C677T, A1298C |
| **Methionine Synthase** | Remethylates homocysteine | B12, 5-MTHF | Common variants |
| **Methionine Synthase Reductase** | Maintains B12 active form | — | Rare variants |
| **CBS** | Transsulfuration pathway | B6 | C699T |
| **MTRR** | Methionine synthase reductase | — | Common variants |

### One-Carbon Metabolism
**Sources of methyl groups:**
- 5-MTHF (primary methyl donor)
- Betaine (TMG - trimethylglycine)
- Choline
- SAM (ultimate methyl donor)

**Methylation reactions:**
- DNA methylation (gene expression)
- Neurotransmitter synthesis (dopamine → norepinephrine, serotonin → melatonin)
- Phospholipid synthesis (myelin)
- Creatine synthesis
- Detoxification (phase II)

## 4. CLINICAL INTERPRETATION

### Optimal Methylation Pattern (Simos Target)
| Marker | Value | Interpretation |
|--------|-------|----------------|
| **5-MTHF** | >25 ng/mL | Adequate active folate |
| **MMA** | Undetectable | Adequate functional B12 |
| **Homocysteine** | <7 (ideally <5) | Efficient methylation |

### Deficiency Patterns

**Folate Deficiency (Low 5-MTHF)**
| Marker | Value | Clinical |
|--------|-------|----------|
| 5-MTHF | <10 | Low active folate |
| Homocysteine | >7 (often >10) | Elevated |
| MMA | Normal | B12 adequate |
| MCV | >100 (macrocytic) | Megaloblastic |

**Causes:**
- MTHFR polymorphism (C677T, A1298C)
- Low intake (folate from food, not synthetic folic acid)
- Malabsorption
- Medications (methotrexate, anticonvulsants, OCPs)
- Alcohol
- Pregnancy/lactation (increased needs)

**B12 Deficiency (Elevated MMA)**
| Marker | Value | Clinical |
|--------|-------|----------|
| MMA | Detectable/elevated | Functional B12 deficiency |
| Homocysteine | >7 (often >10) | Elevated |
| 5-MTHF | Normal or high | Folate trapping |
| MCV | >100 (macrocytic) | Megaloblastic |
| Neurological | Variable | Neuropathy, dementia |

**Causes:**
- Pernicious anemia (autoimmune)
- Gastric surgery (bypass, resection)
- Vegan diet (no animal products)
- Malabsorption (celiac, Crohn's)
- Medications (PPIs, H2 blockers, metformin)
- Nitrous oxide exposure

**Combined Deficiency**
| Marker | Value |
|--------|-------|
| 5-MTHF | Low |
| MMA | Elevated |
| Homocysteine | Very high (>15) |
| MCV | Very high (>110) |

**MTHFR Polymorphism Pattern**
| Marker | Value |
|--------|-------|
| 5-MTHF | Low-normal or low |
| Homocysteine | Elevated |
| MMA | Normal |
| Genetic | C677T or A1298C positive |

**Treatment:** 5-MTHF bypasses MTHFR defect

### High Homocysteine Differential
| Cause | Mechanism | Markers |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| **B12 deficiency** | Impaired remethylation | ↑ MMA, ↑ Hcy |
| **Folate deficiency** | Impaired remethylation | ↓ 5-MTHF, ↑ Hcy |
| **MTHFR defect** | Reduced 5-MTHF production | ↓ 5-MTHF, ↑ Hcy |
| **B6 deficiency** | Impaired transsulfuration | ↑ Hcy, normal MMA, normal 5-MTHF |
| **B2 deficiency** | MTHFR dysfunction | Variable |
| **Hypothyroidism** | Reduced methylation | ↑ Hcy, check TSH |
| **Renal failure** | Reduced clearance | ↑ Hcy, ↑ creatinine |
| **Medications** | Interference | Drug history |
| **Genetic (CBS)** | Transsulfuration defect | Rare, very high Hcy |

## 5. CARDIOVASCULAR RISK

### Homocysteine as CV Risk Factor
| Level | Risk | Evidence |
|-------|------|----------|
| <5 | Minimal | Optimal |
| 5-7 | Low | Acceptable |
| 7-10 | Moderate | 1.5x risk |
| 10-15 | High | 2x risk |
| >15 | Very high | 3x risk |

**Mechanisms:**
- Endothelial dysfunction
- Oxidative stress
- Increased thrombosis
- Vascular inflammation
- LDL oxidation

**Clinical Trials:**
- B vitamin supplementation lowers homocysteine
- BUT: Large trials (VITATOPS, HOPE-2) showed mixed CV outcomes
- **Interpretation:** Lowering Hcy helps, but not sufficient alone
- **Simos approach:** Target <7 as part of comprehensive CV prevention

## 7. SUPPLEMENT INTERVENTIONS

### 5-MTHF (Active Folate) - PREFERRED
| Form | Dose | Notes |
|------|------|-------|
| **5-MTHF (Quatrefolic®)** | 400-800 mcg | Best form, bypasses MTHFR |
| **5-MTHF (Metafolin®)** | 400-800 mcg | Good quality |
| **Folinic acid** | 400-800 mcg | Alternative (not 5-MTHF but active) |

**NEVER USE:** Folic acid (synthetic, can mask B12, problematic with MTHFR)

**Dosing:**
- Maintenance: 400-800 mcg daily
- Deficiency: 800-1600 mcg daily
- MTHFR polymorphism: 800-1600 mcg daily
- Pregnancy: 800-1600 mcg daily (critical for neural tube)

### Methylcobalamin (Active B12) - PREFERRED
| Form | Dose | Route | Notes |
|------|------|-------|-------|
| **Methylcobalamin** | 1000 mcg | Sublingual | Active form, neurological protection |
| **Methylcobalamin** | 1000 mcg | Oral | If sublingual not tolerated |
| **Hydroxocobalamin** | 1000 mcg | IM injection | Severe deficiency, pernicious anemia |
| **Adenosylcobalamin** | 1000 mcg | Oral | Mitochondrial B12 (alternative) |

**NEVER USE:** Cyanocobalamin (synthetic, requires conversion, cyanide byproduct)

**Dosing:**
- Maintenance: 1000 mcg daily
- Deficiency: 1000-2000 mcg daily
- Pernicious anemia: 1000 mcg IM weekly until normal, then monthly

### B-Complex Support
| Nutrient | Dose | Role |
|----------|------|------|
| **Riboflavin (B2)** | 10-25 mg | MTHFR cofactor |
| **P5P (B6)** | 25-50 mg | CBS cofactor |
| **B2 + B6 + B12 + 5-MTHF** | Combined | Synergistic |

### Betaine (TMG) - Alternative Methyl Donor
| Form | Dose | Use Case |
|------|------|----------|
| **Betaine HCl or TMG** | 500-3000 mg | High homocysteine unresponsive to B vitamins |

**Mechanism:** Alternative remethylation pathway (betaine-homocysteine methyltransferase)

### Monitoring Response
| Timeline | Check | Expected |
|----------|-------|----------|
| 4 weeks | Homocysteine | Should decrease |
| 8 weeks | 5-MTHF, MMA | Should normalize |
| 12 weeks | Complete panel | All optimal |

## 8. RED FLAGS

🚩 **Critical:**
- Homocysteine >15 μmol/L (very high CV risk)
- Homocysteine >20 (urgent treatment)
- B12 deficiency + neurological symptoms (irreversible if delayed)
- Pregnancy with low 5-MTHF (neural tube defect risk)

⚠️ **Urgent:**
- Homocysteine 10-15
- MMA elevated with normal serum B12
- Macrocytic anemia with neurological symptoms

## 9. SPECIAL POPULATIONS

### MTHFR Polymorphism (40% population)
| Variant | Effect | Treatment |
|---------|--------|-----------|
| **C677T heterozygous** | 30-40% reduced activity | 5-MTHF 800 mcg |
| **C677T homozygous** | 70% reduced activity | 5-MTHF 1600 mcg + B2 |
| **A1298C** | Reduced BH4 recycling | 5-MTHF + consider BH4 |
| **Compound** | C677T + A1298C | 5-MTHF 1600 mcg + B2 |

**Testing:** 23andMe, functional medicine labs

### Pregnancy
- **Critical period:** First 28 days (neural tube formation)
- **Target 5-MTHF:** >25 ng/mL before conception
- **Dose:** 800-1600 mcg 5-MTHF (not folic acid)
- **Also check:** B12 (neural tube risk if deficient)

### Elderly
- Reduced absorption (atrophic gastritis)
- Medications (PPIs, metformin)
- Cognitive protection (B12, folate)
- Target: Homocysteine <7

### Cardiovascular Disease
- Target homocysteine <7 as part of prevention
- Combine with omega-3, CoQ10, magnesium
- Not sufficient alone, but important component
