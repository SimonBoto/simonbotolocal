# MCHC — Mean Corpuscular Hemoglobin Concentration

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**MCHC:** Mean Corpuscular Hemoglobin Concentration

**Concentration:** Amount of hemoglobin per unit volume of RBC

source-litnote: Synthesized from clinical practice
---

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**Average concentration of hemoglobin in RBCs:**
- Calculated: Hb ÷ Hct × 100
- Normal: 32-36 g/dL

**Clinical utility:**
- Less commonly used than MCV
- Helps distinguish hypochromic anemias

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### MCHC Categories

| Category | MCHC (g/dL) | Description |
|----------|-------------|-------------|
| **Hypochromic** | <32 | Pale RBCs, low Hb content |
| **Normochromic** | 32-36 | Normal color |
| **Hyperchromic** | >36 | Dark RBCs (usually artifact) |

### Causes by Category

**Hypochromic (<32 g/dL):**
| Cause | Mechanism |
|-------|-----------|
| **Iron deficiency** | Most common; impaired heme |
| **Thalassemia** | Reduced globin synthesis |
| **Sideroblastic anemia** | Iron utilization defect |
| **Anemia of chronic disease** | Iron sequestration |

**Hyperchromic (>36 g/dL):**
- Usually artifact (spherocytes, hemolysis)
- True hyperchromia rare
- Spherocytosis (hereditary or autoimmune)

## 4. COFACTORS

### Factors Affecting MCHC

| Factor | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| **Iron availability** | Deficiency → hypochromic |
| **Globin synthesis** | Defect → hypochromic |
| **RBC membrane** | Spherocytes → artifactual high |

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### Based on MCHC

**Hypochromic:**
- Same workup as microcytic anemia
- Iron studies
- Hemoglobin electrophoresis

**Hyperchromic:**
- Peripheral smear review
- Osmotic fragility test (spherocytosis)
- Direct Coombs test (autoimmune hemolysis)
