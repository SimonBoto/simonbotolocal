# Free Fatty Acids Complete Analysis

**Source:** Extended clinical guide (Greek)  
**Scope:** Complete biochemistry, metabolism, clinical interpretation of all fatty acids  
**Extraction date:** March 31, 2026

---

## 1. FATTY ACID FUNDAMENTALS

### Definition
Organic compounds consisting of:
- **Carbon skeleton** (6-22 carbons)
- **Carboxyl group** (-COOH) at one end
- **Methyl group** (-CH3) at the other end

### Functions
| Function | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Energy storage** | Triglycerides in adipose tissue |
| **Energy production** | Beta-oxidation → Acetyl-CoA → ATP |
| **Cell membrane structure** | Phospholipid bilayer components |
| **Eicosanoid precursors** | Inflammatory cascade ("inflammatory waterfall") |
| **Circulation** | As triglycerides in blood |

### Sources
- **Dietary absorption** — from food
- **Endogenous synthesis** — from carbohydrates (insulin-driven)

### Metabolic Processes
| Process | Enzyme | Description |
|---------|--------|-------------|
| **Synthesis** | — | Carbohydrates → Acetyl-CoA → Fatty acids (insulin-dependent) |
| **Elongation** | Elongases | Add carbon atoms to skeleton |
| **Desaturation** | Delta-desaturases | Add double bonds |

### Classification by Saturation
| Type | Double Bonds | Examples |
|------|--------------|----------|
| **Saturated** | 0 | Palmitic (C16:0), Stearic (C18:0) |
| **Monounsaturated** | 1 | Oleic (C18:1 ω9) |
| **Polyunsaturated** | 2+ | Linoleic (C18:2 ω6), ALA (C18:3 ω3) |

### Omega (ω) Nomenclature
- Numbering starts from **methyl end** (ω)
- Indicates position of first double bond
- **ω3:** First double bond at carbon 3
- **ω6:** First double bond at carbon 6
- **ω9:** First double bond at carbon 9

## 2. ESSENTIAL FATTY ACIDS

**Two essential fatty acids** — cannot be synthesized by the body:

### Omega-3 Essential
**Alpha-Linolenic Acid (ALA)** — C18:3 ω3

### Omega-6 Essential
**Linoleic Acid (LA)** — C18:2 ω6

**Both ω3 and ω6:**
- Use common desaturases and elongases
- **Compete** for these enzymes
- Ratio matters significantly

---

## 3. OMEGA-3 FATTY ACIDS

Associated with health throughout lifespan — from embryonic development to neurodegenerative disease prevention.

**Actions:**
- Anti-inflammatory
- Cell membrane production and function
- Gene regulation

**Western diet deficiency:** Low in beneficial ω3 found in:
- Flaxseed
- Fatty fish
- Nuts
- Green leafy vegetables

**Deficiency leads to:**
- Neurodegenerative diseases
- Mood disorders
- Cognitive decline
- Cardiovascular disease
- Skin disorders
- Vision changes
- Malignancies

**Low levels caused by:**
- Insufficient intake
- Difficult digestion/absorption
- Cofactor deficiency: Zinc (Zn), Niacin (B3), Magnesium, Vitamin C
- Alcohol
- Cortisol and adrenocorticoids

---

### 3.1 Alpha-Linolenic Acid (ALA) — C18:3 ω3

**Structure:** 18 carbons, 3 double bonds starting at carbon 3

**Sources:**
- Green leafy vegetables
- Flaxseed
- Walnuts
- Chia seeds
- Soybean & canola oil

**Metabolism:**
- Precursor to EPA and DHA
- **Mainly used for energy production**
- Very limited conversion to EPA/DHA (<5-10%)

**Clinical note:** Direct EPA/DHA intake preferred over ALA supplementation

---

### 3.2 Eicosapentaenoic Acid (EPA) — C20:5 ω3

**Structure:** 20 carbons, 5 double bonds

**Sources:**
- Fatty fish (salmon, mackerel, cod, sardines)
- Minimal conversion from ALA (desaturase + elongase)

**Functions:**
- Precursor to **Prostaglandin E3 (PGE3)** — inhibits platelet aggregation
- Precursor to Thromboxane 3
- Precursor to Leukotriene-5 (anti-inflammatory eicosanoids)
- **Participates in inflammatory cascade**

**Clinical effects:**
- Reduces triglycerides without increasing LDL
- Cardiovascular protection:
  - Stabilizes atherosclerotic plaque
  - Prevents progression
  - Reduces macrophage concentration
  - Vasodilation

**Elevation:** From supplements or increased dietary intake — no side effects, preventive for above conditions

---

### 3.3 Docosapentaenoic Acid (DPA) — C22:5 ω3

**Structure:** 22 carbons, 5 double bonds

**Formation:** From EPA via elongase

**Status:** Less studied than EPA + DHA

**Functions:**
- COX-1 inhibitor (associated with platelet aggregation)
- Reduces lipogenesis
- Regulates lipolysis

---

### 3.4 Docosahexaenoic Acid (DHA) — C22:6 ω3

**Structure:** 22 carbons, 6 double bonds

**Functions:**
- Brain development and function
- Retinal health
- Anti-inflammatory
- Cell membrane fluidity

**Clinical:** Usually studied together with EPA

---

## 4. OMEGA-6 FATTY ACIDS

### The Evolutionary Problem

**Historical ratio:** ω6:ω3 = **1:1**  
**Modern Western diet:** ω6:ω3 = **20:1**

This dramatic shift contributes to:
- Obesity pandemic
- Metabolic syndrome
- Chronic inflammation

**Primary concern:** Arachidonic Acid (AA) — pro-inflammatory precursor

**However:** Some ω6 fatty acids are **anti-inflammatory** (GLA, DGLA)

---

### 4.1 Linoleic Acid (LA) — C18:2 ω6

**Structure:** 18 carbons, 2 double bonds

**Status:** **Only essential ω6 fatty acid**

**Sources:**
- Nuts
- Vegetable oils (corn, soybean, canola, sunflower)
- Most meats

**Dual nature:**
- **Balanced intake:** Reduces total cholesterol, improves all-cause mortality
- **Excessive intake:** Promotes pro-inflammatory ω6 pathway
- **Main component of LDL** — one of first to oxidize
- **Involved in atherogenesis** when from commercial vegetable oils

**Elevation causes:**
- Vegetable oil consumption
- Conjugated LA supplements
- Reduced cofactors → inability to metabolize → accumulation

**Clinical associations with high LA:**
- Inflammatory diseases
- Malignancy
- Cardiovascular disease
- Neural healing (context-dependent)

**Reduction causes:**
- Rarely from low intake
- Skin lesions
- Impaired wound healing

---

### 4.2 Gamma-Linolenic Acid (GLA) — C18:3 ω6

**Formation:** From LA via **delta-6-desaturase**

**Cofactors required:** Zinc, Cobalt

**Inhibitors of conversion:**
- Smoking
- Stress
- Alcohol
- Systemic inflammation

**Sources:**
- Animal organs
- Borage oil
- Black currant
- Evening primrose oil

**Clinical applications:**
- Rheumatoid arthritis
- Dermatitis
- Diabetic neuropathy

**Mechanism:** Reduces inflammation, improves bone health, regulates lipid metabolism

**Critical importance:** Rapid conversion to **DGLA** (anti-inflammatory)

**Note:** DGLA can become pro-inflammatory (AA) if pathway continues

---

### 4.3 Dihomo-gamma-linoleic Acid (DGLA) — C20:3 ω6

**Formation:** LA → (desaturase) → GLA → (elongase) → DGLA

**Difficult conversion associated with:**
- Aging
- Diabetes
- Alcoholism
- Atopic dermatitis
- Rheumatoid arthritis
- Cancer
- Cardiovascular disease

**Functions:**
- Precursor to **Prostaglandin E1 (PGE1)**:
  - Inhibits platelet aggregation
  - Promotes vasodilation
  - Inhibits cholesterol synthesis
  - Regulates immune response
  - Reduces arterial hypertension
- Inhibits AA production
- **Inhibits growth and differentiation of malignancies**

**Elevation:** From DGLA/GLA supplements

**Clinical correlation:**
- Assess with AA ratio
- Associated with central obesity
- Insulin increase

**Reduction:** Diabetes, alcoholism, atopic dermatitis, cardiovascular disease

---

### 4.4 Arachidonic Acid (AA) — C20:4 ω6

**Formation:** From DGLA via elongase and desaturase

**Status:** **Pro-inflammatory precursor**

**Eicosanoid production:** Via COX or LOX enzymes
- Prostaglandin E2 (PGE2) — pro-inflammatory
- Thromboxane A2 — platelet aggregation
- Leukotrienes — inflammation

**Clinical significance:**
- Excessive AA drives chronic inflammation
- Balanced by sufficient EPA (ω3)
- Target for anti-inflammatory interventions

---

## 5. THE OMEGA-6:3 RATIO

### Historical vs. Modern
| Era | Ratio | Health Status |
|-----|-------|---------------|
| Evolutionary | 1:1 | Optimal |
| Traditional diets | 2-4:1 | Good |
| Modern Western | 20:1 | Pathological |

### Clinical Implications of High Ratio
- Chronic low-grade inflammation
- Insulin resistance
- Obesity
- Cardiovascular disease
- Autoimmune conditions
- Mood disorders

### Optimal Target
**ω6:ω3 ratio = 2-4:1**

### Achieving Balance
**Reduce ω6:**
- Minimize vegetable oils (corn, soybean, sunflower)
- Avoid processed foods
- Limit grain-fed meat

**Increase ω3:**
- Fatty fish 2-3x/week
- Flaxseed, walnuts
- Consider EPA/DHA supplements

## 6. CLINICAL INTERPRETATION SUMMARY

### Low Fatty Acids — Causes
- Insufficient intake
- Malabsorption
- Cofactor deficiency (Zn, B3, Mg, Vitamin C)
- Alcohol
- Cortisol excess

### High Specific Fatty Acids

| Fatty Acid | High Indicates |
|------------|---------------|
| **LA** | Vegetable oil excess, poor metabolism |
| **ALA** | Flaxseed/oil supplementation |
| **EPA/DHA** | Fish oil supplementation (beneficial) |
| **GLA/DGLA** | Borage/evening primrose supplements |
| **AA** | High ω6 intake, inflammation |

### Pattern Recognition

#### High LA + Low EPA/DHA
- Typical Western diet pattern
- Pro-inflammatory risk
- Priority: Increase ω3, reduce ω6

#### High DGLA + High AA
- DGLA converting to AA (pro-inflammatory)
- Block delta-5-desaturase (EPA, zinc)
- Consider EPA supplementation

#### Low DGLA + Low AA
- Impaired ω6 metabolism
- Cofactor deficiency
- Skin issues, poor healing

---

## 7. INTERVENTION PROTOCOLS

### For High ω6:3 Ratio
1. **Eliminate vegetable oils** — cook with olive oil, butter, coconut oil
2. **Increase fatty fish** — salmon, mackerel, sardines
3. **Add flaxseed/walnuts** — ALA source
4. **Consider EPA/DHA supplements** — 1-3g/day
5. **Check cofactors** — zinc, B3, magnesium, vitamin C

### For Low DGLA/Impaired Conversion
1. **GLA supplements** — borage oil or evening primrose
2. **Cofactor support** — zinc, magnesium
3. **Reduce inhibitors** — alcohol, smoking, stress

### For Inflammation (High AA)
1. **EPA/DHA** — 2-4g/day (competes with AA)
2. **Reduce ω6** — especially vegetable oils
3. **Curcumin** — anti-inflammatory support
4. **Address root cause** — gut health, stress, sleep
