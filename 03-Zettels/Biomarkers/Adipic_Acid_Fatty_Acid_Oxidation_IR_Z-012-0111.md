# Adipic Acid — Fatty Acid Oxidation & Insulin Resistance Marker

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**Adipic acid:** From Latin "adeps" (fat)

**Also called:**
- Adipate
- Hexanedioic acid (6-carbon dicarboxylic acid)

source-litnote: Synthesized from clinical practice
---

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**Dicarboxylic acid (6 carbons):**
- Formed from incomplete fatty acid oxidation
- Normally fully oxidized to CO2
- **Elevated when beta-oxidation impaired**

**Insulin resistance connection:**
- Impaired fat oxidation in IR
- Adipic acid accumulates
- **Early marker of metabolic dysfunction**

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### Adipic Acid Levels

| Level | Status | Interpretation |
|-------|--------|----------------|
| **Normal** | Low | Normal fat oxidation |
| **Elevated** | High | Impaired beta-oxidation or IR |

### Causes of Elevation

**Insulin resistance:**
- Impaired mitochondrial fat oxidation
- Preferential glucose metabolism
- **Early IR marker**

**Mitochondrial dysfunction:**
- Impaired beta-oxidation enzymes
- Carnitine deficiency
- CoQ10 deficiency

**Fasting/ketogenic diet:**
- Increased fat oxidation
- May transiently elevate

**Genetic (fatty acid oxidation disorders):**
- MCAD, LCAD deficiency
- Severe elevation
- Hypoglycemia risk

## 4. COFACTORS

### Beta-Oxidation Requirements

| Nutrient | Role | Deficiency Effect |
|----------|------|-------------------|
| **Carnitine** | Fatty acid transport | Impaired oxidation |
| **Riboflavin (B2)** | FAD synthesis | Impaired oxidation |
| **Niacin (B3)** | NAD+ synthesis | Impaired oxidation |
| **CoQ10** | Electron transport | Impaired oxidation |
| **Magnesium** | Enzyme cofactor | Impaired metabolism |

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### For Elevated Adipic Acid

**Insulin sensitivity:**
- Weight loss
- Exercise
- Low-carb diet
- Metformin

**Mitochondrial support:**
- Carnitine: 1-3 g/day
- CoQ10: 100-300 mg/day
- B2, B3
- Magnesium

**Caution with fasting/keto:**
- May transiently elevate
- Context matters
