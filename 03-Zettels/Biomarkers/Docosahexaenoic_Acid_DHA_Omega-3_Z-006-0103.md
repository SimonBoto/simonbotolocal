# DHA — Docosahexaenoic Acid, Brain Omega-3

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**Docosahexaenoic acid:** 22 carbons, 6 double bonds

**Also called:**
- DHA
- C22:6 n-3

---

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**Long-chain omega-3:**
- Major brain fatty acid (40% of brain PUFA)
- Retina component (60% of retina PUFA)
- Essential for neurological development

**Functions:**
| System | Role |
|--------|------|
| **Brain** | Membrane fluidity, neurogenesis |
| **Retina** | Photoreceptor function |
| **Heart** | Anti-arrhythmic |

---

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### DHA Levels

| Level (% of total fatty acids) | Status | Interpretation |
|--------------------------------|--------|----------------|
| **3-5%** | Optimal | Adequate |
| **<2%** | Low | Insufficient intake |
| **>6%** | High | High intake |

### Clinical Significance

**Low DHA associated with:**
- Cognitive decline
- Depression
- Vision problems
- Cardiovascular risk

---

## 4. COFACTORS

### DHA Sources

- Direct from fish/algae
- Poor conversion from ALA (<0.5%)

---

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### For Low DHA

**Direct sources:**
- Fatty fish
- Fish oil
- Algae oil (vegan)

**Target:** 1-2g DHA/day

---
