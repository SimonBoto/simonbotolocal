# Triglycerides — Lipid Energy Storage

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**Triglycerides:** Three (tri-) fatty acids attached to glycerol backbone

**Also called:**
- Triacylglycerols
- Neutral fats
- TG (abbreviation)

**Structure:** Glycerol + 3 fatty acids

---

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**Primary energy storage:**
- Most concentrated energy form (9 kcal/g)
- Stored in adipose tissue
- Transported in blood as lipoproteins

**Metabolic roles:**
- Energy reserve
- Insulation/protection
- [[Vitamin_A_Retinol_Vision_Immune_Z-007-0005]|Fat-soluble vitamin] transport

### Normal Metabolism

**After meal:**
1. Dietary fats → chylomicrons
2. Triglycerides hydrolyzed by lipoprotein lipase (LPL)
3. Fatty acids taken up by tissues
4. Remnants cleared by liver

**Fasting:**
- VLDL transports endogenous triglycerides
- LPL releases fatty acids for energy

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### Triglyceride Levels

| Level (mg/dL) | Status | Interpretation |
|---------------|--------|----------------|
| **<100** | Optimal | Low risk |
| **<150** | Normal | Acceptable |
| **150-199** | Borderline | Moderate risk |
| **200-499** | High | Increased risk |
| **>500** | Very high | Pancreatitis risk, severe metabolic dysfunction |

### Clinical Significance

**Elevated triglycerides indicate:**
- Insulin resistance
- Carbohydrate excess
- Alcohol consumption
- Hypothyroidism
- Kidney disease
- Genetic disorders

**Very high (>500 mg/dL):**
- Risk of acute pancreatitis
- Requires immediate intervention

### Postprandial Effect

**Triglycerides increase after meals:**
- Fasting measurement preferred
- Peak at 2-4 hours post-meal
- Return to baseline by 8-12 hours

## 4. COFACTORS

### Factors Affecting Triglycerides

| Factor | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| **Carbohydrate intake** | Increases (especially simple sugars) |
| **Alcohol** | Increases significantly |
| **Fasting status** | Lower when fasting |
| **Exercise** | Decreases |
| **Omega-3** | Decreases |
| **Insulin resistance** | Increases |
| **Hypothyroidism** | Increases |

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### For Elevated Triglycerides

**Tier 1: Lifestyle (most effective)**
- Reduce simple carbohydrates
- Eliminate sugar/fructose
- Reduce/eliminate alcohol
- Exercise regularly
- Weight loss (if overweight)
- Omega-3 fatty acids (2-4g EPA+DHA)

**Tier 2: Supplements**
| Supplement | Dose | Effect |
|------------|------|--------|
| **Omega-3 (EPA/DHA)** | 2-4g/day | Potent triglyceride reduction |
| **Niacin** | 1-3g/day | Significant reduction |
| **Berberine** | 500mg 2-3x/day | Moderate reduction |
| **Fiber** | 10-25g/day | Modest reduction |

**Tier 3: Medications**
- Fibrates (first-line for high TG)
- Omega-3 prescription (Lovaza, Vascepa)
- Niacin
- Statins (modest effect)

### Targets

| Risk Level | Target |
|------------|--------|
| **Optimal** | <100 mg/dL |
| **Acceptable** | <150 mg/dL |
| **High risk** | <150 mg/dL (aggressive if >200) |

## 6. CONNECTED BIOMARKERS

| Biomarker | Relationship | Pattern |
|-----------|--------------|---------|
| **HDL** | Inverse relationship | High TG + low HDL = metabolic syndrome |
| **LDL** | Often elevated in metabolic syndrome | Both elevated = high risk |
| **Glucose** | Metabolic partner | Both elevated = insulin resistance |
| **Insulin** | Regulatory hormone | High insulin + high TG = metabolic syndrome |
| **HbA1c** | Glycemic control | Both elevated = poor metabolic control |

### Clinical Patterns

**Metabolic Syndrome:**
- TG: ≥150 mg/dL
- HDL: <40 (men), <50 (women)
- Glucose: ≥100 mg/dL
- Plus 2 of: high BP, central obesity

**Severe Hypertriglyceridemia:**
- TG: >500 mg/dL
- Risk: Acute pancreatitis
- Requires immediate treatment
