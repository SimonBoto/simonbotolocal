# Master Biomarker Reference
## All Simos Clinical Targets - Quick Reference

---

## CBC / HEMATOLOGY

### WBC (B-001-0001)
| Marker | Optimal | Accepted | Concerning |
|--------|---------|----------|------------|
| **WBC** | 4.5-8.0 | 4.5-10.0 | >11 or <3.5 |
| **Neutrophils** | 40-60% | 40-70% | >70% (infection) |
| **Lymphocytes** | 25-35% | 20-40% | <20% (chronic) |
| **Monocytes** | 2-6% | 2-8% | >8% (chronic inflammation) |
| **Eosinophils** | 1-3% | 1-4% | >4% (allergies/parasites) |
| **Basophils** | 0.5-1% | 0.5-1% | >1% (rare) |

**Key Rules:**
- Always check ratios AND absolute numbers
- Eosinophils >4% = allergies
- Monocytes >8% = chronic inflammation
- Lymphocytes low = clinical concern

**Low WBC Workup:** Methylmalonic (B12), 5-methylfolate, vitamin D, mitochondria, MCV, ferritin, homocysteine

---

### Hemoglobin Family (B-001-0002)
| Marker | Optimal | Accepted | Notes |
|--------|---------|----------|-------|
| **Hb** | >13 (M&F) | >12 | Always >12, ideally >13 |
| **Hct** | ~3× Hb | ~3× Hb | If <3× Hb = dehydration |
| **MCV** | <90 | <95 | Macrocytic if >95 |
| **MCH** | 27-33 pg | 27-33 pg | With MCV |
| **MCHC** | 32-36 g/dL | 32-36 g/dL | Hypochromic <32 |
| **RDW** | <14% | <14% | High = mixed population |

**Dehydration Check:** If Hct <3× Hb → check urea, urate, creatinine, BUN:Cr ratio

**Patterns:**
- Microcytic (MCV <80): Iron deficiency vs thalassemia
- Macrocytic (MCV >95): B12/folate vs liver vs thyroid
- Normocytic: Renal, chronic disease, hemolysis

---

### Platelets (B-001-0003)
| Marker | Optimal | Accepted | Concerning |
|--------|---------|----------|------------|
| **PLT** | 200-350k | 200-400k | >400k or <150k |
| **PDW** | <15% | <17% | >17% (activation) |
| **MPV** | 7-11 fL | 7-11 fL | >11 (young, active) |

**Key Rules:**
- PLT >400k = inflammation or iron loss (check CRP, ferritin)
- PLT <200k = worse health outcomes
- PLT 200-350k = optimal

## IRON STUDIES (B-003-0001)

| Marker | Women | Men | Notes |
|--------|-------|-----|-------|
| **Ferritin** | ~70 optimal | 50-100 | <30 = deficiency; >120 (W) / >300 (M) = check methylation/inflammation |
| **Iron** | 60-120 | 60-120 | |
| **TIBC** | 250-400 | 250-400 | High in deficiency |
| **Transferrin Sat** | 20-50% | 20-50% | <20% = deficiency; >50% = overload |

**Key Rules:**
- Ferritin <30 = iron deficiency (both sexes)
- Women >120: Check methylation (5-methylfolate, methylmalonic, homocysteine)
- Men >300: Watchful, investigate cause

---

## METHYLATION (B-004-0001)

| Marker | Optimal | Accepted | Critical |
|--------|---------|----------|----------|
| **5-MTHF** | >25 | >25 | Active folate only |
| **Methylmalonic** | Undetectable | Low | Functional B12 |
| **Homocysteine** | <5 | <7 | Max 7 |

**Key Rules:**
- Never use folic acid (synthetic) — only 5-MTHF
- Serum B12 is artifact — use methylmalonic acid
- B12 ALWAYS with folate (prevents masking neuropathy)

## METABOLIC / INSULIN RESISTANCE (B-005-0001)

| Marker | Optimal | Accepted | High | Very High |
|--------|---------|----------|------|-----------|
| **Glucose** | 70-90 | 90-100 | 100-110 | >110 |
| **Insulin** | <7 | <10 | 10-15 | >15 |
| **HbA1c** | 4.9-5.5% | 5.5-5.7% | 5.7-5.8% | >5.8% |
| **TG** | <65 | <90 | 90-150 | >150 |
| **Uric Acid** | <5.5 | <6.5 | 6.5-7.0 | >7.0 |
| **Waist/Height** | <0.50 | <0.55 | 0.55-0.60 | >0.60 |

**Key Rules:**
- Glucose >100 = analog increase in risk
- Insulin >10 = analog increase in IR
- HbA1c every 0.1% >5.7 = worse outcomes
- TG:HDL ratio <2.0 (ideally <1.0)

## LIPID RATIOS (B-006-0001)

| Ratio | Optimal | Accepted | High |
|-------|---------|----------|------|
| **LDL:ApoB** | >1.5 | >1.2 | <1.2 |
| **TG:HDL** | <1.0 | <2.0 | >3.0 |
| **Total:HDL** | <4.0 | <5.0 | >5.0 |

**Key Rules:**
- LDL:ApoB >1.2 minimum, higher is better (particle size)
- Don't focus on absolute LDL or total cholesterol
- Lipids come SECOND to insulin resistance and free fatty acids

## FREE FATTY ACIDS (B-006-0002)

| Marker | Optimal | Accepted | High |
|--------|---------|----------|------|
| **Omega-6:3** | <4 | <7 | >7 |
| **AA:EPA** | <3 | <5 | >5 |
| **DGLA** | <80 | <80 | >80 (Insulin Index) |
| **AA** | <550 | <550 | >550 (Inflammation Index) |
| **LA** | <2400 | <2400 | >2400 |
| **GLA** | 15-60 | 15-60 | >60 |
| **ALA** | 15-50 | 15-50 | >50 (don't want more) |
| **EPA** | >250 | 100-250 | <100 (more is better) |
| **DHA** | >280 | 180-280 | <180 (more is better) |
| **Palmitoleic** | <60 | <60 | >60 (Cortisol Index) |
| **Oleic** | >1400 | >1400 | <1400 |
| **C15** | >8 | >8 | <8 |

**Key Rules:**
- EPA/DHA: More is better, no upper limit
- DGLA >80 = insulin resistance (more sensitive than glucose)
- AA >550 = inflammation substrate
- Palmitoleic >60 = cortisol high or carb excess

## VITAMIN D / CALCIUM / PTH (B-007-0001)

| Marker | Optimal | Accepted | Minimum |
|--------|---------|----------|---------|
| **25(OH)D** | 80-150 | 80-100 | >50 |
| **PTH** | <20 | <40 | <40 |
| **Calcium** | 9-10 | 9-10.5 | 8.5-10.5 |
| **Magnesium** | 2.0-2.5 | 2.0-2.5 | >1.8 |

**Key Rules:**
- PTH <20 = ideal suppression, confirms adequate D
- Check magnesium FIRST (required for D activation)
- PTH elevated + normal D = low magnesium

## LIVER FUNCTION (B-009-0001)

| Marker | Optimal | Normal | Elevated |
|--------|---------|--------|----------|
| **ALT** | <20 | 20-40 | >40 |
| **AST** | <20 | 20-40 | >40 |
| **GGT** | <20 | 20-60 | >60 |
| **ALP** | 40-120 | 40-120 | >120 |
| **Bilirubin** | 0.1-1.2 | 0.1-1.2 | >1.2 |

**AST:ALT Ratio:**
- <1.0: Fatty liver, viral hepatitis
- >2.0: Alcoholic liver disease

## THYROID (B-010-0001)

| Marker | Optimal | Accepted | Concerning |
|--------|---------|----------|------------|
| **TSH** | 0.5-2.0 | 2.0-4.0 | >4.0 |
| **FT4** | 1.0-1.5 | 0.8-1.8 | <0.8 or >1.8 |
| **FT3** | 2.5-3.5 | 2.0-4.0 | <2.0 or >4.5 |
| **Anti-TPO** | <10 | <35 | >35 (Hashimoto's) |
| **Anti-TG** | <20 | <40 | >40 (Hashimoto's) |

**Key Rules:**
- TSH >2.5 in pregnancy = treat
- Anti-TPO positive = autoimmune thyroiditis (monitor)

## INFLAMMATION

### CRP (B-002-0001)
| Level | Interpretation |
|-------|----------------|
| **<1.0** | Optimal (minimum target) |
| **1.0-3.0** | Moderate risk |
| **>3.0** | High risk |

### Calprotectin (B-002-0002)
| Level | Interpretation |
|-------|----------------|
| **<50** | Normal (minimum target) |
| **50-120** | Borderline |
| **>120** | Gut inflammation (IBD) |

## MALE HORMONES (B-011-0001)

| Marker | Optimal | Notes |
|--------|---------|-------|
| **Free T** | 150-250+ | More is better |
| **Total T** | 800-1200 | Less important than free |
| **E2** | 10-30 | >40 = aromatization |
| **SHBG** | 20-40 | High = less free T |

**Key Rules:**
- Fix metabolic foundations first (sleep, exercise, nutrition, stress)
- Women: Don't mess with hormones — fix IR, thyroid, stress, they balance

## SUMMARY PRIORITIES

### First Priority (Fix These First)
1. Insulin resistance (glucose, insulin, HbA1c)
2. Free fatty acids (omega-6:3, DGLA, palmitoleic)
3. Inflammation (CRP, AA)

### Second Priority
4. Methylation (homocysteine, 5-MTHF, methylmalonic)
5. Vitamin D/PTH (with magnesium)
6. Iron (ferritin with methylation check)

### Third Priority
7. Lipid ratios (LDL:ApoB, TG:HDL)
8. Thyroid (if symptomatic)
9. CBC patterns

### Context/Monitoring
10. Kidney, liver (baseline)
11. Male hormones (if symptomatic)
