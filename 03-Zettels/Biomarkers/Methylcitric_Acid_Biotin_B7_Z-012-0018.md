# Methylcitric Acid — Biotin (B7) & Propionate Metabolism Marker

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**Methylcitric acid:** Citric acid with methyl group substitution

**Also called:**
- Methylcitrate
- 2-methylcitric acid

source-litnote: Synthesized from clinical practice
---

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**Propionate metabolism pathway:**
- Propionyl-CoA (from odd-chain fatty acids, amino acids) → Methylmalonyl-CoA
- Alternative: Propionyl-CoA + oxaloacetate → Methylcitric acid

**Biotin-dependent step:**
- Propionyl-CoA carboxylase requires biotin
- **Deficiency → shunting to methylcitric acid**

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### Methylcitric Acid Levels

| Level | Status | Interpretation |
|-------|--------|----------------|
| **Normal** | Low | Adequate biotin status |
| **Elevated** | High | Biotin deficiency or propionate overload |

### Causes of Elevation

**Biotin (B7) deficiency:**
- Impaired propionyl-CoA carboxylase
- Shunting to methylcitric pathway

**Propionate overload:**
- Odd-chain fatty acid metabolism
- Certain amino acids (methionine, threonine, valine)
- Gut bacterial production

**Genetic (propionic acidemia):**
- Propionyl-CoA carboxylase defect
- Severe elevation
- Neonatal metabolic emergency

## 4. COFACTORS

### Propionate Metabolism

| Nutrient | Role | Deficiency Effect |
|----------|------|-------------------|
| **Biotin (B7)** | Propionyl-CoA carboxylase | Methylcitric accumulation |
| **B12** | Methylmalonyl-CoA mutase | Combined deficiency pattern |

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### For Elevated Methylcitric Acid

**Biotin supplementation:**
- B7: 1-5 mg/day

**B12 support:**
- Often needed with biotin
- 1000 mcg/day

**Dietary:**
- Moderate protein intake
- Avoid excessive odd-chain fats

- Methylcitric: Elevated
- 3-HIA: Elevated
- **Both B7 markers**

**Combined B7/B12 Deficiency:**
- Methylcitric: Elevated
- Methylmalonic: Elevated
- **Both vitamins needed**
