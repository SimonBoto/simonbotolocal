# Monocytes — Innate Immune Phagocytes

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**Monocyte:** From Greek "monos" (single/one) + "kytos" (cell)

**Named for:** Large single-lobed nucleus

**Also called:**
- Mononuclear phagocytes
- Precursor to macrophages and dendritic cells

---

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**Circulating phagocytes:**
- 2-8% of WBCs
- Largest white blood cells
- Circulate 1-3 days before entering tissues

**Differentiation in tissues:**
- **Macrophages:** Tissue-resident phagocytes
- **Dendritic cells:** Antigen-presenting cells

**Functions:**
| Function | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Phagocytosis** | Engulf and destroy pathogens, debris |
| **Antigen presentation** | Present antigens to T-cells |
| **Cytokine production** | IL-1, IL-6, TNF-α (inflammation) |
| **Tissue repair** | Wound healing, remodeling |

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### Monocyte Levels

| Level (%) | Status | Interpretation |
|-----------|--------|----------------|
| **2-6** | Normal | Typical range |
| **>8** | Elevated | Monocytosis |
| **<2** | Low | Monocytopenia (rare) |

### Causes of Monocytosis

**Infections:**
- Tuberculosis
- Subacute bacterial endocarditis
- Malaria
- Recovery phase of acute infections

**Inflammatory/Autoimmune:**
- Systemic lupus erythematosus
- Rheumatoid arthritis
- Inflammatory bowel disease

**Hematologic:**
- Chronic myelomonocytic leukemia
- Myelodysplastic syndromes
- Lymphomas

**Other:**
- Sarcoidosis
- Recovery from neutropenia

### Clinical Significance

**Elevated monocytes suggest:**
- Chronic inflammation
- Chronic infection
- Autoimmune activity
- Hematologic disorder (if very high)

## 4. COFACTORS

### Monocyte Development

| Factor | Role |
|--------|------|
| **CSF-1 (M-CSF)** | Growth factor for monocytes/macrophages |
| **GM-CSF** | Stimulates monocyte production |
| **Vitamin D** | Modulates monocyte function |

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### For Elevated Monocytes

**Address underlying cause:**
- Treat infection
- Manage autoimmune disease
- Evaluate for hematologic disorder if persistent
