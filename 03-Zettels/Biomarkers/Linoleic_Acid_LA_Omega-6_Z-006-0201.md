# LA — Linoleic Acid, Essential Omega-6

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**Linoleic acid:** From "linum" (flax) + "oleic" (oil)

**Also called:**
- LA
- C18:2 n-6

---

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**Essential omega-6 fatty acid:**
- Must be obtained from diet
- Precursor to GLA, DGLA, AA
- Cell membrane component

**Balance needed:**
- Essential for health
- Excess = pro-inflammatory

---

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### LA Levels

| Level (% of total fatty acids) | Status | Interpretation |
|--------------------------------|--------|----------------|
| **10-15%** | Optimal | Adequate, not excessive |
| **>20%** | High | Excessive, pro-inflammatory |
| **>25%** | Very high | Significant inflammation risk |

### Clinical Significance

**High LA associated with:**
- High omega-6:3 ratio
- Inflammation
- Cardiovascular risk

**Sources:**
- Vegetable oils (corn, soybean, sunflower)
- Processed foods
- Grain-fed meat

---

## 4. COFACTORS

### LA Metabolism

- Delta-6-desaturase (B6, zinc, magnesium)
- Converts to GLA

---

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### For High LA

**Reduce sources:**
- Eliminate vegetable oils
- Reduce processed foods
- Choose grass-fed meat

**Increase omega-3:**
- EPA/DHA to balance ratio

**Target omega-6:3 ratio <4:1**

---

## 6. CONNECTED BIOMARKERS

| Biomarker | Relationship | Pattern |
|-----------|--------------|---------|
| **AA** | Product | High LA → high AA |
| **Omega-6:3 ratio** | Balance | High LA raises ratio |
| **EPA** | Competitor | Inverse relationship |

---
