# 3-Hydroxybutyric Acid — Ketone Body, Fat Oxidation Marker

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**3-Hydroxybutyric acid:** Hydroxy-substituted butyric acid at position 3

**Also called:**
- Beta-hydroxybutyrate (BHB)
- 3-HB

**Note:** Technically not a ketone (reduced form of acetoacetate)

source-litnote: Synthesized from clinical practice
---

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**Primary ketone body:**
- Acetoacetate → 3-HB (via beta-hydroxybutyrate dehydrogenase)
- 70% of circulating ketones
- Alternative fuel for brain, heart, muscle

**Ketogenesis triggers:**
| Condition | Mechanism |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Fasting** | Glycogen depletion |
| **Low-carbohydrate diet** | Insufficient glucose |
| **Prolonged exercise** | Glycogen depletion |
| **Diabetic ketoacidosis** | Insulin deficiency |

**Utilization:**
- Converted back to acetoacetate → acetyl-CoA
- Enters Krebs cycle
- Brain can use during starvation (spares glucose)

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### 3-HB Levels

| Level (mmol/L) | Status | Interpretation |
|----------------|--------|----------------|
| **<0.5** | Normal | Fed state |
| **0.5-3.0** | Nutritional ketosis | Low-carb diet, fasting |
| **3.0-5.0** | High ketosis | Extended fast, strict keto |
| **>5.0** | Very high | Monitor for acidosis |
| **>10.0 + acidosis** | DKA | Diabetic ketoacidosis |

### Clinical Significance

**Nutritional ketosis (0.5-3.0 mmol/L):**
- Normal physiological response
- Weight loss, mental clarity
- Not dangerous

**Starvation ketosis:**
- Adaptive response
- Brain fuel preservation

**Diabetic ketoacidosis (DKA):**
- Pathological
- Insulin deficiency
- pH <7.3, bicarbonate <18
- Medical emergency

**OAT (urinary) significance:**
- Fat oxidation capacity
- Mitochondrial beta-oxidation
- Ketogenic flexibility

## 4. COFACTORS

### Ketogenesis Requirements

| Nutrient | Role | Deficiency Effect |
|----------|------|-------------------|
| **Carnitine** | Fatty acid transport | Impaired beta-oxidation |
| **Riboflavin (B2)** | FAD synthesis | Impaired beta-oxidation |
| **Niacin (B3)** | NAD+ synthesis | Impaired ketogenesis |
| **Magnesium** | Cofactor | Impaired metabolism |
| **CoQ10** | Electron transport | Impaired oxidation |

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### For Low 3-HB (Poor ketogenesis)

**Support fat oxidation:**
- Carnitine: 1-3 g/day
- B2, B3, B5
- CoQ10
- Magnesium

**Dietary:**
- MCT oil (rapid ketone production)
- Coconut oil
- Intermittent fasting

### For High 3-HB (DKA)

**Medical emergency:**
- IV insulin
- IV fluids
- Electrolyte replacement
- Bicarbonate if severe acidosis

## 6. CONNECTED BIOMARKERS

| Biomarker | Relationship | Pattern |
|-----------|--------------|---------|
| **Acetoacetate** | Precursor | Interconvertible |
| **Glucose** | Counter-regulatory | Inverse relationship |
| **Insulin** | Suppresses ketogenesis | Low insulin → high ketones |
| **pH** | Acid-base | Low in DKA |
| **Bicarbonate** | Buffer | Low in DKA |

### Clinical Patterns

**Nutritional Ketosis:**
- 3-HB: 0.5-3.0 mmol/L
- Glucose: 70-90 mg/dL
- pH: Normal
- **Desired on ketogenic diet**

**DKA:**
- 3-HB: >5.0 mmol/L
- Glucose: >250 mg/dL
- pH: <7.3
- **Medical emergency**
