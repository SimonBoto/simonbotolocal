# Benzoic & Hippuric Acids — Preservative & Gut Markers

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**Benzoic acid:** From benzoin resin

**Hippuric acid:** From Greek "hippos" (horse) + "ouron" (urine) — first found in horse urine

---

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**Benzoic acid:**
- Food preservative
- Gut bacterial metabolite
- Detoxified to hippuric acid in liver

**Hippuric acid:**
- Glycine conjugate of benzoic acid
- Marker of gut permeability (leaky gut)
- Elevated with SIBO

---

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### Hippuric Acid Levels

| Level | Status | Interpretation |
|-------|--------|----------------|
| **Normal** | Low | Normal gut barrier |
| **Elevated** | High | Leaky gut or SIBO |

### Causes of Elevation

- SIBO
- Leaky gut
- High benzoate intake (preservatives)
- Gut dysbiosis

---

## 4. COFACTORS

### Detoxification Requirements

- Glycine (conjugation)
- Liver function
- Gut barrier integrity

---

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### For Elevated Hippuric Acid

**Gut healing:**
- L-glutamine
- Zinc carnosine
- Collagen
- Bone broth

**SIBO treatment:**
- Rifaximin or herbal antimicrobials

**Reduce benzoates:**
- Avoid processed foods
- Check labels
