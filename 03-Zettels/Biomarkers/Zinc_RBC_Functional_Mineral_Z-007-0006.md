# Zinc — RBC Zinc, Functional Mineral Status

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**Zinc:** German "Zink," origin uncertain

**Element symbol:** Zn (atomic number 30)

**RBC zinc:** Reflects long-term tissue zinc status (better than serum)

---

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**Essential trace mineral:**
| Function | Mechanism |
|----------|-----------|
| **Enzyme cofactor** | 300+ enzymes (carbonic anhydrase, SOD, etc.) |
| **Immune function** | T-cell development, cytokine production |
| **Wound healing** | Collagen synthesis, cell proliferation |
| **Protein synthesis** | DNA/RNA polymerases |
| **Hormone function** | Insulin, testosterone, growth hormone |
| **Antioxidant** | Copper-zinc SOD |
| **Taste/smell** | Gustin (taste bud enzyme) |

**RBC vs Serum Zinc:**
| Type | Reflects | Timeframe |
|------|----------|-----------|
| **Serum zinc** | Acute status, transport | Days |
| **RBC zinc** | Tissue stores, functional | Months |

**RBC zinc is better indicator of functional status**

---

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### RBC Zinc Levels

| Level (μg/g Hb) | Status | Interpretation |
|-----------------|--------|----------------|
| **40-50** | Optimal | Adequate functional status |
| **35-40** | Low-normal | Suboptimal |
| **<35** | Deficient | Functional deficiency |
| **<25** | Severe | Significant deficiency |

### Clinical Significance

**Deficiency symptoms:**
- Impaired immune function
- Poor wound healing
- Hair loss
- Taste/smell dysfunction
- Skin problems (acne, dermatitis)
- Growth retardation
- Delayed sexual maturation
- Depression, cognitive issues

**High-risk groups:**
- Vegetarians/vegans (phytates reduce absorption)
- Malabsorption (celiac, Crohn's)
- Alcoholics
- Elderly
- Pregnant/lactating women
- Athletes (sweat loss)

---

## 4. COFACTORS

### Zinc Metabolism

| Nutrient | Role | Interaction |
|----------|------|-------------|
| **Copper** | Antagonist | High zinc depletes copper |
| **Iron** | Competitor | High iron reduces zinc absorption |
| **Calcium** | Competitor | High calcium reduces absorption |
| **Phytates** | Inhibitor | In plant foods, reduces absorption |
| **Protein** | Enhancer | Amino acids improve absorption |

---

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### For Deficiency (<35 μg/g Hb)

**Supplementation:**
- Zinc picolinate or bisglycinate: 15-30 mg/day
- Higher doses if severe: 30-50 mg/day (short term)

**Food sources:**
- Oysters (highest)
- Beef, lamb
- Pumpkin seeds
- Cashews
- Chickpeas

**Absorption enhancement:**
- Take away from calcium/iron
- With protein-containing meal
- Soak/sprout legumes (reduce phytates)

**Caution:**
- >50 mg/day may cause copper deficiency
- Balance with copper if supplementing long-term

---

## 6. CONNECTED BIOMARKERS

| Biomarker | Relationship | Pattern |
|-----------|--------------|---------|
| **Copper** | Antagonist | High zinc → low copper |
| **Ceruloplasmin** | Copper transport | Low if copper depleted |
| **WBC** | Immune function | Low zinc → impaired immunity |
| **Testosterone** | Hormone synthesis | Low zinc → low T |
| **Vitamin A** | RBP synthesis | Low zinc → low A transport |

### Clinical Patterns

**Zinc Deficiency:**
- RBC zinc: Low
- Copper: Normal/high (relative)
- WBC: Impaired function
- **Functional deficiency**

**Zinc-Copper Imbalance:**
- Zinc: Very high (supplementation)
- Copper: Low
- Ceruloplasmin: Low
- **Copper deficiency from zinc excess**
