# Ketoacids — Thiamine (B1) Deficiency Markers

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**Ketoacids:** Organic acids with ketone functional group

**Branched-chain ketoacids (BCKA):**
- From leucine, isoleucine, valine

**Maple syrup urine disease (MSUD):**
- Genetic defect in BCKA dehydrogenase
- Named for sweet-smelling urine

source-litnote: Synthesized from clinical practice
---

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**Branched-chain amino acid (BCAA) metabolism:**
```
Leucine → α-ketoisocaproic acid (KIC)
Isoleucine → α-keto-β-methylvaleric acid (KMV)
Valine → α-ketoisovaleric acid (KIV)
```

**B1-dependent step:**
- BCKA dehydrogenase requires thiamine pyrophosphate (TPP)
- **Deficiency → ketoacid accumulation**

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### Ketoacids Levels

| Level | Status | Interpretation |
|-------|--------|----------------|
| **Normal** | Low | Adequate B1 status |
| **Elevated** | High | B1 deficiency or genetic defect |

### Causes of Elevation

**Thiamine (B1) deficiency:**
| Cause | Mechanism |
|-------|-----------|
| **Dietary insufficiency** | Poor intake |
| **Alcoholism** | Most common cause |
| **Malabsorption** | Bariatric surgery, celiac |
| **Hyperemesis gravidarum** | Pregnancy vomiting |
| **Dialysis** | Loss of water-soluble vitamins |
| **Chronic illness** | Increased demand |

**Genetic (MSUD):**
- BCKA dehydrogenase defect
- Severe elevation
- Neonatal screening
- Lifelong dietary management

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### For Elevated Ketoacids

**Thiamine supplementation:**
- B1: 100-300 mg/day
- IV thiamine if severe deficiency

**Dietary sources:**
- Pork
- Legumes
- Nuts
- Seeds
- Whole grains

**Genetic MSUD:**
- Specialized metabolic team
- Low-BCAA diet
- Lifelong management

### Clinical Patterns

**B1 Deficiency:**
- Ketoacids: Elevated
- Lactic acid: Elevated
- Pyruvic acid: Elevated
- **Wernicke's, beriberi**

**MSUD:**
- Ketoacids: Very high
- BCAAs: Very high
- **Maple syrup odor**
