# PTH — Parathyroid Hormone, Calcium Regulator

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**PTH:** Parathyroid Hormone

**Parathyroid:** Glands adjacent to thyroid ("para-" = beside)

**Also called:**
- Parathormone
- Parathyrin

**Discovery:** 1925 by James Collip

source-litnote: Synthesized from clinical practice
---

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**Primary calcium regulator:**
- Secreted by parathyroid glands (4 glands)
- Responds to ionized calcium levels
- **Low calcium → PTH release**

**Actions:**
| Target | Effect | Result |
|--------|--------|--------|
| **Bone** | Stimulates resorption | Releases calcium |
| **Kidney** | Increases calcium reabsorption | Retains calcium |
| **Kidney** | Decreases phosphate reabsorption | Phosphaturia |
| **Kidney** | Activates vitamin D | Increases calcium absorption |
| **Intestine** | Indirect (via vitamin D) | Increases absorption |

**Net effect:** Raises serum calcium, lowers phosphate

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### PTH Levels

| Level (pg/mL) | Status | Interpretation |
|---------------|--------|----------------|
| **10-55** | Normal | Adequate calcium regulation |
| **<10** | Low | Hypercalcemia, hyperparathyroidism suppressed |
| **55-100** | Borderline | Possible early hyperparathyroidism |
| **>100** | Elevated | Hyperparathyroidism or secondary causes |

### Clinical Significance

**Elevated PTH:**
| Cause | Mechanism |
|-------|-----------|
| **Primary hyperparathyroidism** | Adenoma/hyperplasia → autonomous secretion |
| **Secondary hyperparathyroidism** | Low calcium/vitamin D → compensatory |
| **Chronic kidney disease** | Phosphate retention, low calcium |
| **Vitamin D deficiency** | Low calcium absorption |
| **Calcium malabsorption** | Low serum calcium |

**Low PTH:**
| Cause | Mechanism |
|-------|-----------|
| **Hypercalcemia** | Suppresses PTH |
| **Hypoparathyroidism** | Gland destruction (surgery, autoimmune) |
| **Hypomagnesemia** | Required for PTH secretion |

## 4. COFACTORS

### PTH Regulation

| Factor | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| **Ionized calcium** | Primary regulator (inverse) |
| **Vitamin D** | Negative feedback |
| **Magnesium** | Required for secretion |
| **Phosphate** | Stimulates PTH |

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### For Elevated PTH

**Primary hyperparathyroidism:**
- Surgery (parathyroidectomy) if symptomatic
- Observation if mild/asymptomatic
- Cinacalcet (calcimimetic)

**Secondary hyperparathyroidism:**
- Vitamin D replacement
- Calcium supplementation
- Phosphate binders (CKD)
- Calcitriol (active vitamin D)

### For Low PTH

**Hypoparathyroidism:**
- Calcium supplementation
- Active vitamin D (calcitriol)
- Magnesium repletion

## 6. CONNECTED BIOMARKERS

| Biomarker | Relationship | Pattern |
|-----------|--------------|---------|
| **Calcium** | Primary regulator | Inverse relationship |
| **Vitamin D** | Feedback loop | Low D → high PTH |
| **Phosphate** | PTH effect | High PTH → low phosphate |
| **Magnesium** | Cofactor | Low Mg → low PTH |
| **ALP** | Bone marker | Elevated if bone turnover high |

### Clinical Patterns

**Primary Hyperparathyroidism:**
- PTH: Elevated or inappropriately normal
- Calcium: Elevated
- Phosphate: Low
- **Adenoma/hyperplasia**

**Secondary Hyperparathyroidism (Vitamin D deficiency):**
- PTH: Elevated
- Calcium: Low or normal
- Vitamin D: Low
- **Compensatory elevation**

**Secondary Hyperparathyroidism (CKD):**
- PTH: Elevated
- Calcium: Low
- Phosphate: High
- Vitamin D: Low
- **Renal osteodystrophy**

**Hypoparathyroidism:**
- PTH: Low
- Calcium: Low
- Phosphate: High
- **Gland failure**
