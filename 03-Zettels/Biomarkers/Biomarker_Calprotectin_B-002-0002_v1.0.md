# Calprotectin - B-002-0002

## 1. IDENTIFICATION

| Marker           | Full Name          | What It Measures            |
| ---------------- | ------------------ | --------------------------- |
| **Calprotectin** | Fecal Calprotectin | Neutrophil protein in stool |

**Specimen:** Stool
**Collection:** Random sample, keep cool

## 2. SIMOS MINIMUM TARGET

| Range | Interpretation | Action |
|-------|----------------|--------|
| **<50 mcg/g** | **MINIMUM - Normal** | Maintain |
| **50-120** | Borderline | Investigate |
| **>120** | Elevated | Active gut inflammation |
| **>200** | High | IBD likely |

**Simos Rule: Calprotectin should be <50 mcg/g (minimum target)**

## 3. WHAT CALPROTECTIN TELLS US

**Gut-Specific Inflammation:**
- Released from neutrophils in intestinal mucosa
- Stable in stool for days
- Not affected by diet (unlike lactoferrin)

**Causes of Elevation:**
- IBD (Crohn's, ulcerative colitis)
- Infectious colitis
- NSAID enteropathy
- Colorectal cancer
- Severe food intolerance

**Differentiation from IBS:**
- IBS: Calprotectin <50
- IBD: Calprotectin >120
- **Best test to distinguish IBS vs IBD**
