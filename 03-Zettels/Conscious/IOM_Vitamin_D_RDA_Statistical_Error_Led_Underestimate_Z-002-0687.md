# IOM Vitamin D RDA Statistical Error Led to Underestimate

## Core Claim
The **2011 Institute of Medicine (IOM) recommendation of 600 IU/day vitamin D** was based on a **statistical error** — using mean requirement instead of the 97.5th percentile — leading to a massive underestimate of actual population needs.

## The Error

### IOM 2011 Recommendation
| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **RDA** | 600 IU/day (ages 1-70) |
| **Goal** | 25(OH)D >20 ng/mL in 97.5% of population |
| **Method flaw** | Used mean requirement, not 97.5th percentile |

### Correct Statistical Approach
| Approach | Result |
|----------|--------|
| **Mean requirement** | Covers ~50% of population |
| **97.5th percentile** | Covers 97.5% of population |
| **IOM used** | Mean (wrong) |
| **Should have used** | 97.5th percentile |

## Consequences

### Inadequate Recommendations
| Population | Impact |
|------------|--------|
| **General public** | Following RDA, still deficient |
| **Healthcare providers** | Under-dosing patients |
| **Food fortification** | Insufficient levels |
| **Public health** | Widespread suboptimal vitamin D |

## Evidence

> "The IOM's statistical approach used the mean rather than the 97.5th percentile of the dose-response distribution, resulting in an RDA that meets the needs of only 50% of the population rather than the intended 97.5%."
> — Veugelers & Ekwaru, 2014
