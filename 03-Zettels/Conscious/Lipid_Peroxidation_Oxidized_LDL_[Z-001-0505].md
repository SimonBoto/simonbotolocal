# Lipid Peroxidation and Oxidized LDL

> Oxidative modification of LDL transforms it into a pro-inflammatory, pro-atherogenic particle that drives macrophage foam cell formation.

## 

**Initiation:**
- Free radicals attack PUFAs in LDL
- Vitamin E depletion allows oxidation
- Metal ions (Cu, Fe) catalyze

**Propagation:**
- Lipid peroxyl radicals form
- Chain reaction damages more lipids
- Lipid hydroperoxides accumulate

**Products:**
- **4-HNE:** Toxic aldehyde, protein modification
- **MDA:** Marker of peroxidation
- **Oxysterols:** Oxidized 

## Consequences

**Pro-inflammatory:**
- Scavenger receptors recognize oxLDL
- Unregulated macrophage uptake
- Foam cell formation

**Endothelial dysfunction:**
- Reduced NO bioavailability
- Increased adhesion molecules
- Monocyte recruitment

## Dietary Connection

**PUFA susceptibility:**
- High  intake → more substrate
- Heated/oxidized vegetable oils

**Antioxidant protection:**
- Vitamin E: chain-breaking
- Vitamin C: regenerates E
- Polyphenols: direct antioxidant
