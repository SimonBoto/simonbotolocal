# Iron Sulfur Clusters Ancient Cofactors

## Overview
Iron-sulfur (Fe-S) clusters are among the most ancient biological cofactors, essential for electron transfer, enzyme catalysis, and sensing cellular conditions.

## Structure Types

### [2Fe-2S] Cluster
- **Composition:** 2 iron atoms, 2 inorganic sulfides
- **Geometry:** Rhombic (diamond-shaped)
- **Examples:** Ferredoxins, aconitase

### [4Fe-4S] Cluster
- **Composition:** 4 iron atoms, 4 inorganic sulfides
- **Geometry:** Cubane structure
- **Examples:** Aconitase, succinate dehydrogenase, nitrogenase

### [3Fe-4S] Cluster
- **Formed from:** [4Fe-4S] losing one iron
- **Example:** Inactive aconitase (oxidative damage)

## Biological Functions

### 1. Electron Transfer
| Protein | Location | Function |
|---------|----------|----------|
| **Ferredoxins** | Chloroplasts,  | Photosynthesis, nitrogen fixation |
| **Succinate dehydrogenase** | Complex II | Krebs cycle, ETC |
| **NADH dehydrogenase** | Complex I | Electron transport |

### 2. Enzyme Catalysis
| Enzyme | Fe-S Role | Pathway |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| **Aconitase** | Catalytic | Krebs cycle (citrate → isocitrate) |
| **Nitrogenase** | Catalytic | N₂ fixation |
| **Endonuclease III** | DNA repair | Oxidative damage repair |

### 3. Sensing and Regulation
| Sensor | Detects | Response |
|--------|---------|----------|
| **IRP1** | Iron levels | Regulates iron  |
| **SoxR** | Superoxide | Activates antioxidant genes |
| **FNR** | Oxygen | Regulates anaerobic metabolism |

## Evolutionary Significance

### Ancient Origins
- Present in **all domains of life**
- Pre-date oxygenic photosynthesis
- May have formed **abiotically** on early Earth
- Hydrothermal vent chemistry produces similar structures

### Fe-S World Hypothesis
- Early metabolism may have been catalyzed by Fe-S minerals
- Transition to protein-bound Fe-S clusters
- Central to emergence of biochemistry

## Clinical Relevance

### Fe-S Cluster Biogenesis Defects
| Disorder | Gene | Consequence |
|----------|------|-------------|
| **Friedreich ataxia** | FXN (frataxin) |  Fe-S defect, neurodegeneration |
| **Multiple mitochondrial dysfunction syndromes** | Various | Combined OXPHOS deficiencies |

### Oxidative Damage
- **Superoxide** attacks Fe-S clusters
- Releases free iron (Fenton chemistry)
- Creates **oxidative stress amplification**
- Aconitase particularly vulnerable
