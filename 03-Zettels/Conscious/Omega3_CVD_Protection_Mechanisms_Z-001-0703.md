# Omega-3 CVD Protection Mechanisms

## Core Principle
**Omega-3 fatty acids protect against cardiovascular disease through four complementary mechanisms:** anti-inflammatory action, endothelial function improvement, triglyceride reduction, and antithrombotic effects.

## The Four Mechanisms

### 1. Anti-Inflammatory
- Reduces vascular inflammation
- Modulates inflammatory cytokine production
- Decreases systemic inflammatory markers

### 2. Endothelial Function
- Improves vascular endothelium (inner lining)
- Enhances vasodilation capacity
- Restores endothelial-dependent relaxation

### 3. Triglyceride Reduction
- ↓ Plasma triglycerides (dose-dependent)
- Improves lipid profile
- Reduces atherogenic particles

### 4. Antithrombotic
- Limits platelet aggregation
- Reduces thrombus formation
- Modulates coagulation cascade

## Multi-Target Advantage
Unlike single-mechanism drugs, omega-3 acts on multiple CVD pathways simultaneously — addressing the multifactorial nature of cardiovascular disease.
