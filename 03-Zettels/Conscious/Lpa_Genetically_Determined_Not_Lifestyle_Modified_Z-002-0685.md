# Lp(a) Genetically Determined Not Modified by Lifestyle

## Core Claim
**Lp(a) levels are 80-90% genetically determined** by the LPA gene and remain stable throughout life — they are **not significantly affected by diet, exercise, or statin therapy**, making Lp(a) a unique cardiovascular risk factor that requires different management approaches than modifiable risk factors.

## Genetic Determination

### LPA Gene
| Feature | Impact |
|---------|--------|
| **KIV-2 repeats** | Determines Apo(a) size |
| **Small Apo(a) isoforms** | Higher Lp(a) levels |
| **Large Apo(a) isoforms** | Lower Lp(a) levels |
| **Heritability** | 80-90% |

### Inheritance Pattern
| Parent with high Lp(a) | Child risk |
|------------------------|------------|
| **One parent** | ~50% chance elevated |
| **Both parents** | Higher probability |

## What Does NOT Lower Lp(a)

| Intervention | Effect on Lp(a) |
|--------------|-----------------|
| **Diet changes** | Minimal/none |
| **Exercise** | Minimal/none |
| **Weight loss** | Minimal/none |
| **Statins** | No effect or slight increase |
| **Ezetimibe** | No effect |
| **Fibrates** | Minimal effect |

## What CAN Lower Lp(a)

| Intervention | Magnitude |
|--------------|-----------|
| **Niacin** | ~20-30% (not outcome-proven) |
| **PCSK9 inhibitors** | ~20-30% |
| **Lipoprotein apheresis** | Dramatic (severe cases) |
| **Pelacarsen** | ~80% (investigational) |
| **Olpasiran** | ~95% (investigational) |

## Clinical Implications

### Testing Strategy
| Approach | Rationale |
|----------|-----------|
| **Once per lifetime** | Levels stable |
| **No need to repeat** | Unless treatment initiated |
| **Family screening** | Cascade testing |

### Management Focus
| Since Lp(a) fixed | Focus on |
|-------------------|----------|
| **Aggressive other risk factors** | LDL, BP, smoking |
| **Earlier intervention** | Lower thresholds for statins |
| **Novel therapies** | When available |

## Evidence

> "Lp(a) levels are genetically determined by the LPA gene and remain stable throughout life; diet, exercise, and statins do not significantly lower Lp(a), requiring alternative management strategies focused on aggressive control of other risk factors and emerging targeted therapies."
> — (Lp(a) genetics literature)
