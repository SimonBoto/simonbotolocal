# B12 Requirements and Supplementation

> B12 requirements increase with high protein intake, vegan diets, malabsorption, and certain medications; supplementation may be oral, sublingual, or parenteral depending on absorption capacity.

## Daily Requirements

| Population | Requirement |
|------------|-------------|
| Adults | 2.4 mcg/day |
| Pregnancy | 2.6 mcg/day |
| Lactation | 2.8 mcg/day |

## Increased Needs

- High protein intake
- Athletic training
- Pernicious anemia
- Gastric bypass surgery
- Elderly (reduced absorption)
- Vegan/vegetarian diets

## Forms of B12

### Oral
- **Cyanocobalamin:** Synthetic, stable, most common
- **Methylcobalamin:**  support
- **Adenosylcobalamin:** l support
- **Hydroxocobalamin:** Long-acting

### Parenteral
- **Indications:** Malabsorption, pernicious anemia
- **Route:** IM or SC injection
- **Schedule:** Weekly initially, monthly maintenance

### Alternative Routes
- **Sublingual:** Bypasses absorption issues
- **Intranasal:** Alternative to injections
- **Transdermal:** Limited evidence

## Dosing

| Purpose | Dose |
|---------|------|
| Prevention | 10-100 mcg/day |
| Deficiency treatment | 1000 mcg/day or weekly injections |
| High-dose support | 1000-5000 mcg |
