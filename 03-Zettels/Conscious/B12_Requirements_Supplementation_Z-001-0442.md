# B12 Requirements and Supplementation

## Core Claim
**B12 requirements increase with high protein intake, vegan diets, malabsorption, and certain medications; optimal supplementation requires both methylcobalamin (cytosolic/methylation) and adenosylcobalamin (mitochondrial/energy) forms for complete coverage** — high-dose oral supplementation can overcome most absorption issues.

## Evidence

### Daily Requirements
| Population | RDA | Notes |
|------------|-----|-------|
| **Adults** | 2.4 mcg/day | Baseline |
| **Pregnancy** | 2.6 mcg/day | Increased demand |
| **Lactation** | 2.8 mcg/day | Transfer to infant |
| **Athletes/high protein** | Higher | Increased metabolic turnover |

### Forms of B12
| Form | Target | Use Case |
|------|--------|----------|
| **Methylcobalamin** | Cytosol | Methylation support |
| **Adenosylcobalamin** | Mitochondria | Energy metabolism |
| **Hydroxocobalamin** | Both | Long-acting, conversion |
| **Cyanocobalamin** | Both | Synthetic, stable, common |

### Supplementation Routes
| Route | Dose | Indication |
|-------|------|------------|
| **Oral** | 10-100 mcg/day | Prevention |
| **Oral high-dose** | 1000-5000 mcg/day | Deficiency treatment |
| **Sublingual** | 1000 mcg/day | Absorption issues |
| **Parenteral (IM/SC)** | 1000 mcg weekly | Severe malabsorption |

## Clinical Implication
- **High-dose oral B12 (1000+ mcg)** overcomes most absorption issues via passive diffusion
- **Both forms needed:** Methylcobalamin for methylation, adenosylcobalamin for energy
- **METHAP protocol:** Uses 1000-5000 mcg B12 (both forms) for mitochondrial support
- **Monitor MMA** to confirm adequacy regardless of dose
