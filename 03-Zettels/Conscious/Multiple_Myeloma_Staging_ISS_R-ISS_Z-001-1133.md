## Core Claim
The International Staging System (ISS) and Revised-ISS (R-ISS) stratify multiple myeloma patients by risk using beta-2 microglobulin and albumin (ISS), with R-ISS additionally incorporating LDH and cytogenetics to better predict prognosis and guide treatment intensity.

## Evidence

### ISS (International Staging System)

| Stage | Criteria | Median Survival |
|-------|----------|-----------------|
| I | β2M <3.5 mg/L + albumin ≥3.5 g/dL | 62 months |
| II | Neither I nor III | 44 months |
| III | β2M ≥5.5 mg/L | 29 months |

### R-ISS (Revised ISS)

Adds:
- **LDH:** Elevated = high risk
- **Cytogenetics:** High-risk abnormalities (del(17p), t(4;14), t(14;16))

| Stage | Features | Median Survival |
|-------|----------|-----------------|
| I | ISS I + normal LDH + standard cytogenetics | Not reached (>100 mo) |
| II | Intermediate | 83 months |
| III | ISS III + high LDH or high-risk cytogenetics | 43 months |

### High-Risk Cytogenetics

| Abnormality | Frequency | Prognosis |
|-------------|-----------|-----------|
| **del(17p)** | 10% | Poor |
| **t(4;14)** | 15% | Poor |
| **t(14;16)** | 5% | Poor |
| **t(11;14)** | 15% | Standard risk |

## Clinical Implication

**Risk Stratification:**
- Guides treatment intensity
- Determines transplant eligibility
- Informs prognosis discussion
- Clinical trial selection
