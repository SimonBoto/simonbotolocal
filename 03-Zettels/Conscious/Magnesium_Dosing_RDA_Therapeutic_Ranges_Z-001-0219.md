## Core Principle

> While the RDA for magnesium prevents deficiency, therapeutic dosing for metabolic syndrome requires higher intakes to correct deficiency, optimize metabolic function, and achieve  benefits.

## Recommended Dietary Allowance (RDA)

**Prevention of Deficiency:**
| Population | RDA |
|------------|-----|
| Men (19-30 years) | 400 mg/day |
| Men (31+ years) | 420 mg/day |
| Women (19-30 years) | 310 mg/day |
| Women (31+ years) | 320 mg/day |
| Pregnancy | 350-360 mg/day |
| Lactation | 310-320 mg/day |

**Purpose:** Prevents overt deficiency symptoms (hypomagnesemia)
**Limitation:** Does not account for suboptimal status or therapeutic needs

## Core Principle

> While the RDA for magnesium prevents deficiency, therapeutic dosing for metabolic syndrome requires higher intakes to correct deficiency, optimize metabolic function, and achieve  benefits.

## Therapeutic Dosing for Metabolic Syndrome

**Clinical Trial Evidence:**
- Most trials use: **300-500 mg elemental Mg/day**
- Common effective dose: **365-400 mg/day**
- Higher doses (up to 600 mg) used in some studies

**Target Dosing Strategy:**
| Goal | Dose | Duration |
|------|------|----------|
| **Prevention/Maintenance** | 300-350 mg/day | Ongoing |
| **Deficiency correction** | 400-500 mg/day | 8-16 weeks |
| **Therapeutic optimization** | 400-500 mg/day | 12+ weeks |

## METHAP Protocol Specification

**Recommended Dose:**
- **400-500 mg elemental magnesium daily**
- **Rationale:** 
  - Above RDA to correct common deficiency
  - Within safe, well-tolerated range
  - Matches effective doses in clinical trials
  - Allows for compliance flexibility (can reduce if GI intolerance)

**Dosing Schedule:**
- **Divided doses:** 200-250 mg twice daily
- **Timing:** With meals (improves absorption, reduces GI effects)
- **Adjustments:** Can increase to 600 mg if tolerated and needed

## Dose-Response Relationships

**Blood Pressure:**
- Doses >370 mg/day show greater BP reduction
- Effect size increases with dose up to ~500 mg

** Sensitivity:**
- Doses ≥300 mg/day effective
- Greater effects with longer duration (>12 weeks)

**GI Tolerance:**
- Diarrhea risk increases with dose
- Individual tolerance varies
- Split dosing improves tolerance

## Upper Limits and Safety

**Tolerable Upper Intake Level (UL):**
- **350 mg/day** from supplements only (does not include food)
- Based on risk of diarrhea
- **Note:** Actual tolerance higher; many studies use 500-600 mg safely

**Signs of Excess:**
- Diarrhea (osmotic effect)
- Nausea
- Abdominal cramping
- Reduce dose or change form if occurs

## Core Principle

> While the RDA for magnesium prevents deficiency, therapeutic dosing for metabolic syndrome requires higher intakes to correct deficiency, optimize metabolic function, and achieve  benefits.

## Special Populations

**Renal Impairment:**
- Contraindicated in severe renal failure (GFR <30)
- Reduce dose in moderate impairment
- Monitor serum Mg closely

**Elderly:**
- Higher deficiency risk
- May benefit from higher end of dosing range
- Monitor for GI tolerance

**Athletes:**
- Increased losses through sweat
- May require 400-600 mg/day

## Core Principle

> While the RDA for magnesium prevents deficiency, therapeutic dosing for metabolic syndrome requires higher intakes to correct deficiency, optimize metabolic function, and achieve  benefits.

## Practical Dosing Tips

**Starting Dose:**
- Begin with 200-300 mg/day
- Increase by 100-150 mg every 1-2 weeks
- Target: 400-500 mg/day as tolerated

**Managing GI Effects:**
- Reduce dose if diarrhea occurs
- Switch to glycinate form (less laxative)
- Take with larger meals
- Split into 3-4 smaller doses

**Compliance Optimization:**
- Once or twice daily dosing
- Link to meals (habit stacking)
- Keep backup form for travel
