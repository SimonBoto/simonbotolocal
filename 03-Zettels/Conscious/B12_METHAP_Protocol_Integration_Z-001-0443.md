# B12 METHAP Protocol Integration

## Core Claim
**B12 is essential for METHAP's high-dose nutrient therapy, supporting amino acid oxidation, mitochondrial ATP production, and methylation capacity required for the protocol's metabolic demands** — both methylcobalamin (methylation) and adenosylcobalamin (energy) forms are needed for optimal outcomes.

## Evidence

### METHAP Metabolic Demands
| Demand | B12 Role |
|--------|----------|
| **High protein/nutrient processing** | Amino acid oxidation (Ile, Met, Thr, Val) |
| **Cellular proliferation** | DNA synthesis with folate |
| **High metabolic rate** | Mitochondrial ATP production |
| **Detoxification** | Glutathione synthesis support |

### B12 Functions in METHAP
| Form | Function | Requirement |
|------|----------|-------------|
| **Methylcobalamin** | Methionine regeneration, SAM synthesis | Methylation capacity |
| **Adenosylcobalamin** | Methylmalonyl-CoA mutase, energy | ATP production |

### Synergistic Nutrients
- **Folate** — Methionine synthase partner
- **B6** — Transsulfuration support
- **D/K2/Mg** — B12 enables their optimal function

## Clinical Implication
- **METHAP dosing:** 1000-5000 mcg B12 (both forms)
- **Monitoring:** MMA to confirm mitochondrial adequacy
- **Deficiency common:** Especially in metabolic syndrome, elderly, vegans
- **High-dose rationale:** Overcomes absorption issues, supports high metabolic demand
