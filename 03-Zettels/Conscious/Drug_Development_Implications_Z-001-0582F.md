# Drug Development Implications

> **Core Claim:** Enzyme selectivity is critical for reducing off-target effects and improving clinical safety.

## Key Principle

**Selective targeting** → Fewer side effects → Better compliance → Improved outcomes

## Safinamide as Example

| Feature | Design Choice | Clinical Benefit |
|---------|---------------|------------------|
| MAO-B selective | Avoids MAO-A | Preserves serotonin |
| No DAO effect | Different enzyme class | No histamine issues |
| Clean profile | Minimal off-targets | Fewer restrictions |

## Benefits of Selectivity

1. **Reduced side effects** — Only affects intended target
2. **Fewer drug interactions** — Less metabolic competition
3. **Better compliance** — Simpler dietary/medication rules
4. **Improved safety** — Predictable pharmacology

## Modern Drug Development

- Structure-based design for specificity
- High-throughput screening for selectivity
- Clinical validation of clean profiles

## Contrast with Non-Selective Agents

| Non-Selective | Selective |
|---------------|-----------|
| Tranylcypromine | Safinamide |
| Phenelzine | Rasagiline |
| More side effects | Cleaner profile |
