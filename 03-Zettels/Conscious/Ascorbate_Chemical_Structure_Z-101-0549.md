## Structural Features

### Molecular Composition
- **Ascorbic acid formula:** C₆H₈O₆
- **Ascorbate anion formula:** C₆H₇O₆⁻
- **Key structural elements:** Six-carbon lactone ring, enediol functional groups

### Ionization Behavior
| Property | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Acidic protons** | 1 primary acidic proton (pKa ~4.2) |
| **Ionization state** | pH-dependent equilibrium: Ascorbic acid ⇌ Ascorbate⁻ + H⁺ |
| **Physiological relevance** | >90% exists as ascorbate⁻ at blood pH 7.4 |

### Chemical Classification
- **Sugar acid:** Derived from glucose metabolism (L-gulonic acid pathway)
- **Redox active compound:** Strong reducing agent, electron donor
- **Antioxidant:** Neutralizes free radicals, reactive oxygen species
- **Enzyme cofactor:** Essential for prolyl and lysyl hydroxylases (collagen synthesis)

## Key Functional Groups

### Enediol Group (-C(OH)=C(OH)-)
- **Redox activity:** Enables electron donation/acceptance
- **Acidity:** Contributes to proton donation capability
- **Stability:** Susceptible to oxidation → dehydroascorbate

### Lactone Ring
- **Structure:** Five-membered ring with oxygen atom
- **Stability:** Acid-labile; opens at low pH
- **Biological significance:** Required for vitamin activity, enzymatic recognition

## Physical Properties

| Property | Value | Notes |
|----------|-------|-------|
| **Solubility** | Highly water soluble | 330 g/L at 20°C |
| **Melting point** | 190-192°C | Decomposes at melting |
| **Optical activity** | L-enantiomer active | D-isoascorbic acid has reduced activity |

## Structural Relationships

### Ascorbate vs. Ascorbic Acid
- **Ascorbic acid:** Protonated, neutral form
- **Ascorbate:** Deprotonated, anionic form
- **Biological predominance:** Ascorbate⁻ is primary circulating form

### Ascorbate vs. Oxalate
| Feature | Ascorbate | Oxalate |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| **Carbon count** | 6 | 2 |
| **Carboxyl groups** | 1 (lactone ring) | 2 (dicarboxylic) |
| **Redox state** | Reducing agent | Oxidized end product |
| **Metabolic role** | Vitamin, cofactor | Waste product, mineral chelator |
