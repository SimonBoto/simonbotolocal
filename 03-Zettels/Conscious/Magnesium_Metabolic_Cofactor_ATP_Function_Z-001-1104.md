## Core Claim
**Magnesium is essential for ATP function and serves as cofactor for over 300 enzymes — Mg-ATP (not free ATP) is the true substrate for kinases, and Mg²⁺ stabilizes the high-energy phosphate bonds while enabling their reactivity** — magnesium deficiency impairs virtually all energy metabolism.

## Evidence

### ATP-Magnesium Complex
| Property | Function |
|----------|----------|
| **Mg-ATP formation** | Mg²⁺ coordinates with phosphate oxygens |
| **Charge stabilization** | Neutralizes negative charges on ATP |
| **Reactivity** | Enables high-energy bond transfer |
| **True substrate** | Mg-ATP is what enzymes actually use |

### Why Mg²⁺?
| Property | Advantage |
|----------|-----------|
| **Charge density** | High charge/radius ratio optimal for phosphate |
| **Coordination** | Octahedral geometry fits ATP structure |
| **Abundance** | Most abundant divalent cation in cells |
| **Selectivity** | Outcompetes Ca²⁺ for ATP binding |

### Enzyme Classes Requiring Mg²⁺
| Class | Examples | Function |
|-------|----------|----------|
| **Kinases** | Hexokinase, PFK, pyruvate kinase | Phosphate transfer |
| **Phosphatases** | ATPases | Energy coupling |
| **DNA/RNA enzymes** | Polymerases, topoisomerases | Nucleic acid metabolism |

## Clinical Implication
- **Mg²⁺ deficiency is common** — up to 50% of population insufficient
- **Symptoms:** Fatigue, muscle cramps, arrhythmias, insulin resistance
- **High-dose vitamin D** increases Mg²⁺ demand (required for conversion to active form)
- **METHAP relevance:** Central to protocol; Mg²⁺ supports ATP, D/K2 activation, and 300+ enzymes
