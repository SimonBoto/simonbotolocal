# Amino Acids for Gut Healing Synergy

Glutamine and taurine work synergistically to support intestinal barrier repair, with glutamine fueling enterocyte regeneration and taurine supporting bile flow and antimicrobial defense — together addressing both structural and functional aspects of gut healing.

## Individual Roles

**Glutamine:**
- Primary fuel for enterocytes (intestinal epithelial cells)
- Supports mucosal barrier integrity
- Regulates tight junction proteins
- Anti-inflammatory effects on gut mucosa

**Taurine:**
- Bile acid conjugation (forms taurocholic acid)
- Antimicrobial effects via bile salts
- Antioxidant support (glutathione synthesis)
- Gut motility regulation

## Clinical Applications

**Leaky Gut Protocol:**
- Glutamine: 5–10 g/day (divided doses)
- Taurine: 1–3 g/day
- Best: Between meals, empty stomach
- Duration: 8–12 weeks minimum

**Post-Antimicrobial Support:**
- Glutamine: Barrier repair after die-off
- Taurine: Bile support for fat digestion
- Combine with zinc carnosine for enhanced repair

**SIBO Considerations:**
- Glutamine may fuel bacterial overgrowth if SIBO active
- Address SIBO first, then add glutamine
- Taurine generally well-tolerated
