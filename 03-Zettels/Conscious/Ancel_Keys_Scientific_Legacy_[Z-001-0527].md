# Ancel Keys — Scientific Legacy

> Ancel Keys (1904-2004) developed the diet-heart hypothesis that dietary saturated fat causes cardiovascular disease, shaping global [[KEYWORD-Nutrition|nutrition]] policy for decades through ambitious studies and charismatic advocacy.

## Biography

- PhD UC Berkeley
- Developed K-rations
- Studied high-altitude physiology

## The Lipid Hypothesis

**Core Claim:**
SFA → ↑ [[KEYWORD-Cholesterol|cholesterol]] → Atherosclerosis → CHD

## Key Studies

**Six Countries (1953):**
- Ecological correlation
- Post-hoc selection criticized

**Seven Countries (1958-1964):**
- 12,763 men, 7 countries
- 50-year follow-up
- Most influential nutrition study

## Advocacy

- AHA committee 1956
- Time cover 1961
- McGovern Committee 1977
- Lived to age 100

## Legacy

**Positive:**
- Attention to diet-CVD link
- Mediterranean diet promotion
- Long-term study design

**Controversial:**
- Methodological flaws
- Premature policy influence
- Difficult to correct
