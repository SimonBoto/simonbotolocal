# Health Philosophy Core Concepts

## Overview
14 foundational concepts defining health, disease, and biological function from a  perspective.

## The Terms (Category A)

| Term | Definition |  Relevance |
|------|------------|-------------------|
| **Υγεία (Health)** | Dynamic adaptive capacity, not absence of disease | Measure function, not just pathology |
| **Νόσος (Disease)** | Failure of adaptive s | Preceded by functional dysfunction |
| **Λειτουργική Δυσλειτουργία** | Suboptimal function before pathology | Where metabolomics intervenes |
| **Ομοιόσταση (Homeostasis)** | Dynamic equilibrium maintenance | Not fixed—adaptive range matters |
| **Προσαρμογή (Adaptation)** | Organism response to environmental load | Health = adaptation capacity |
| **Αυτορρύθμιση** | Self-regulating systems | Core biological intelligence |
| **Αυτοεπιδιόρθωση** | Self-repair mechanisms | Activated by proper support |
| **Περιβαλλοντικό Φορτίο** | Environmental stressors cumulative | Modern disease driver |
| **Ολική Βιολογική Ενέργεια** | Total biological energy available | Foundation of all function |
| **Φλεγμονή** | Acute vs. chronic distinction | Acute = protective; chronic = destructive |
| **Allostatic Load** | Cumulative wear from stress | Predicts burnout/disease |
| **Ενεργειακό Έλλειμμα** | Energy shortfall state | Core metabolomics target |
| **Οξειδωτικό Στρες** | ROS > antioxidant capacity | "Biological rusting" |
| **Κυτταρική Λειτουργία** | Cellular performance level | Metabolomics measures this |

## Core Principle
> Health is not the absence of disease—it is the capacity to adapt, self-regulate, and self-repair.
