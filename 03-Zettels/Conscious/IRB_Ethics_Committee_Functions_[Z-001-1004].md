# IRB and Ethics Committee Functions — Independent Review Requirements

## Overview

Institutional Review Boards (IRBs) in the U.S. and Research Ethics Committees (RECs) internationally provide independent review to ensure protection of human research participants.

## Purpose and Authority

### Core Functions

**Review Research Protocols:**
- Evaluate ethical acceptability
- Assess risk-benefit ratio
- Ensure informed consent adequacy
- Verify scientific validity

**Authority to:**
- Approve research
- Require modifications
- Disapprove research
- Conduct continuing review
- Suspend or terminate approval

### Legal Basis

**United States:**
- 45 CFR 46 (Common Rule)
- FDA regulations (21 CFR 50, 56)
- State laws

**International:**
- Declaration of Helsinki (ethics committee requirement)
- CIOMS Guidelines
- National regulations

## IRB Composition

### Required Diversity (U.S. 45 CFR 46)

**Minimum Members:**
- At least 5 members
- Varied backgrounds

**Required Expertise:**
1. **Scientist:** Experienced in research methods
2. **Non-scientist:** Non-research background
3. **Legal expert:** Familiar with institutional commitments
4. **Community member:** Unaffiliated with institution

**Additional Considerations:**
- Men and women
- Racial diversity
- Cultural sensitivity
- Vulnerable populations expertise (when relevant)

### Conflict of Interest

**Restrictions:**
- No member with conflicting interests
- Investigator cannot review own protocol
- Recusal when appropriate

## Review Levels

### Full Board Review

**Required For:**
- Greater than minimal risk
- Vulnerable populations
- Novel interventions
- Controversial research

**Process:**
- Complete protocol review
- Majority vote required
- Quorum required
- Detailed minutes

### Expedited Review

**Categories (Federal Register 1998):**
1. Minor changes to approved research
2. Research involving:
   - Collection of blood (limited volumes)
   - Prospective collection of biological specimens (noninvasive)
   - Noninvasive collection of data
   - Voice, video, digital, or image recordings
   - Individual or group characteristics/behavior
   - Continuing review of approved research

**Process:**
- Review by IRB chair or designee
- Not new research
- Cannot disapprove (must go to full board)

### Exempt Research

**Categories (45 CFR 46.101):**
1. Educational settings research
2. Educational tests, surveys, interviews (public officials excepted)
3. Benign behavioral interventions
4. Secondary research using identifiable data
5. Public benefit/service program evaluations
6. Taste/food quality evaluations

**Process:**
- Determination of exemption
- Not "IRB-approved" but exempt
- Minimal requirements

## Review Criteria

### 45 CFR 46.111 (Common Rule)

**Requirements for Approval:**

**1. Risks Minimized:**
- Sound research design
- Compatible with sound research
- Use procedures already performed for diagnosis/treatment (when appropriate)

**2. Risks Reasonable:**
- Relation to anticipated benefits
- Importance of knowledge

**3. Subject Selection Equitable:**
- Fair procedures
- Fair selection
- Vulnerable populations protected

**4. Informed Consent Adequate:**
- Requirements of 46.116 met
- Documented (46.117)

**5. Data Safety Monitoring:**
- When appropriate, data monitoring
- Stop rules if harm

**6. Privacy/Confidentiality Protected:**
- Adequate provisions
- Data security

**7. Additional Protections:**
- For vulnerable populations
- As required by subparts (B, C, D)

### Special Populations

**Subpart B: Pregnant Women, Fetuses, Neonates:**
- Additional protections
- Risk-benefit considerations
- Father's consent when possible

**Subpart C: Prisoners:**
- Additional protections
- Limited research categories
- Minimal risk preference

**Subpart D: Children:**
- Additional protections
- Parental permission
- Child assent
- Risk categories

## Continuing Review

### Requirements

**Frequency:**
- At least annually
- More frequent if higher risk
- Can be continuous (expedited)

**Process:**
- Progress report
- Safety data review
- Protocol compliance
- Continuing consent

**Suspension/Termination:**
- If non-compliance
- If unexpected serious harm
- If no longer justified

## Beyond Initial Approval

### Ongoing Oversight

**Monitoring:**
- Adverse event reporting
- Protocol deviations
- Data safety monitoring
- Complaint investigation

**Amendments:**
- Protocol changes require approval
- Some minor changes (expedited)
- Significant changes (full board)

### Unanticipated Problems

**Reporting Requirements:**
- Serious adverse events
- Protocol deviations
- Complaints
- Non-compliance

**Timeline:**
- Prompt reporting (often 5-10 days)
- Written report
- Follow-up actions

## International Variations

### Research Ethics Committees (RECs)

**Europe:**
- Ethics committees
- Often national/local
- May be research ethics boards

**Developing Countries:**
- National ethics committees
- Institutional committees
- International partnerships

**Harmonization:**
- CIOMS Guidelines
- ICH-GCP
- Varying standards

### Challenges in Global Research

**Ethical Review Capacity:**
- Limited resources
- Training needs
- Independence concerns

**Dual Review:**
- Host country
- Sponsor country
- Coordination needed

**Standards:**
- Universal vs contextual
- Minimum standards
- Capacity building

## Practical Considerations

### Application Process

**Protocol Submission:**
- Complete application
- Informed consent documents
- Investigator brochure
- Recruitment materials
- Data collection instruments

**Timeline:**
- Full board: 2-6 weeks
- Expedited: 1-2 weeks
- Exemption: Days

**Tips for Success:**
- Complete application
- Clear protocol
- Adequate consent
- Reasonable risk-benefit
- Responsive to questions

### Common Issues

**Inadequate Consent:**
- Too technical
- Missing elements
- Coercive language

**Risk-Benefit Problems:**
- Risks not minimized
- Benefits overstated
- Vulnerable subjects

**Scientific Issues:**
- Poor design
- Inadequate sample size
- Inappropriate methods

## Summary

**Key Functions:**
1. Independent review
2. Risk-benefit assessment
3. Consent adequacy
4. Ongoing monitoring
5. Protection of vulnerable

**Composition:**
- Diverse membership
- Scientific and non-scientific
- Community representation

**Review Levels:**
- Full board
- Expedited
- Exempt

**Core Principle:**
Independent review protects participants when investigator self-interest could conflict with participant welfare.
