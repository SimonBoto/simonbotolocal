## Telomere DNA Structure

**Sequence:** TTAGGG tandem repeats (humans)
- **Length:** 5-15 kb at birth, shortens with age
- **3' Overhang:** 150-300 nucleotides (G-rich strand)
- **Function:** Protects chromosome ends from degradation and fusion

**End Replication Problem:**
- DNA polymerase cannot fully replicate linear chromosome ends
- Each division loses ~50-200 bp
- Limits cellular lifespan (Hayflick limit)

---

## The Shelterin Complex

Six proteins that bind and protect telomeres:

| Protein | Binding | Function |
|---------|---------|----------|
| **TRF1** | Double-strand TTAGGG | Length regulation, replication |
| **TRF2** | Double-strand TTAGGG | T-loop formation, prevents ATM activation |
| **POT1** | Single-strand 3' overhang | Prevents ATR activation, recruits telomerase |
| **TIN2** | TRF1, TRF2, TPP1 | Structural core, stabilizes complex |
| **TPP1** | TIN2, POT1 | Links double/single strand, recruits telomerase |
| **RAP1** | TRF2 | Negative length regulation, gene silencing |

---

## The T-Loop Structure

**Formation:**
- 3' overhang invades double-stranded telomere DNA
- Forms D-loop displacement structure
- Hides chromosome end from DNA damage machinery

**Protection s:**
1. **Physical shielding:** End not recognized as DNA break
2. **ATM inhibition:** TRF2 prevents DNA damage response
3. **ATR inhibition:** POT1 blocks single-strand sensing

---

## Telomere Dysfunction Consequences

**When Shelterin Fails:**
- DNA damage response activation
- Cell cycle arrest (p53-dependent)
- Cellular senescence or apoptosis
- Chromosome end-to-end fusions
- Genomic instability

**Disease Relevance:**
- Dyskeratosis congenita (shelterin mutations)
- Idiopathic pulmonary fibrosis (telomere dysfunction)
- Autoimmune diseases (accelerated shortening)
