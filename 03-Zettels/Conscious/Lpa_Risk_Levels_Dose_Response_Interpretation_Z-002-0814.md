# Lp(a) Risk Levels and Dose-Response Interpretation

## Core Claim
**Lp(a) shows clear dose-response risk** — each 20 mg/dL increase raises CVD risk ~10%, with significant elevation (>50 mg/dL) increasing risk 50% or more.

## Risk Stratification

| Level | Risk Category | Interpretation |
|-------|---------------|----------------|
| **<30 mg/dL** | Normal | Not associated with increased risk |
| **30–50 mg/dL** | Borderline | Moderate risk increase |
| **>50 mg/dL** | High | Significant increase (50%+) |

## Dose-Response

| Lp(a) Increase | Risk Change |
|----------------|-------------|
| +20 mg/dL | ~10% higher |
| +40 mg/dL | ~20% higher |
| +60 mg/dL | ~30% higher |

## Multiplicative Effect

| Base Risk | Lp(a) Contribution |
|-----------|-------------------|
| **Low** | Small absolute impact |
| **High** | Large absolute impact |

> Lp(a) multiplies existing risk rather than adding a fixed amount.

## Testing Recommendation

| Population | Action |
|------------|--------|
| **Family history of early CVD** | Test once |
| **Personal CVD at young age** | Test once |
| **General population** | Consider testing |
