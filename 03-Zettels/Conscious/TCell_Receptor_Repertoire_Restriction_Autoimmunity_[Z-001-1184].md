# T-Cell Receptor Repertoire Restriction in Autoimmunity

## T-Cell Receptor (TCR) Diversity

### Normal Immune System
**Repertoire Size:**
- Estimated 10^6 to 10^8 different T-cell clones
- Generated by V(D)J recombination
- Ensures recognition of almost any pathogen

**Benefits:**
- Broad protection against infections
- Recognition of diverse antigens
- Robust immune responses

---

## Repertoire Restriction in Autoimmunity

### Oligoclonal Expansion
**Observation:**
- Few dominant clones expanded
- Loss of diversity
- Restricted TCR usage

**:**
```
Self-Antigen Recognition
      ↓
Specific T-Cell Activation
      ↓
Clonal Expansion (many divisions)
      ↓
Dominant Clones Take Over
      ↓
Reduced Repertoire Diversity
```

### Consequences

**Autoreactive Clones:**
- Expanded clones often autoreactive
- Persist despite regulation attempts
- Drive chronic 

**Infection Susceptibility:**
- Limited response to novel pathogens
- Reduced vaccine efficacy
- Impaired immune surveillance

---

## Measurement Techniques

### TCR Sequencing
**Methods:**
- Next-generation sequencing (NGS)
- Spectratyping (older method)
- Single-cell TCR analysis

### Diversity Metrics
**Clonality Index:**
- Measures distribution of clone frequencies
- High clonality = few dominant clones
- Low clonality = even distribution

**Richness:**
- Number of unique TCR sequences
- Reduced in autoimmunity

---

## Disease-Specific Patterns

### Rheumatoid Arthritis
- Shared TCR motifs in HLA-DR4+ patients
- Citrullinated peptide-specific clones
- Persistent over years

### Type 1 Diabetes
- -specific TCRs
- Proinsulin-reactive clones
- Early in disease process

### Multiple Sclerosis
- Myelin basic protein-specific TCRs
- Expanded in CNS and periphery
- Correlate with disease activity

---

## Connection to Telomeres

### Clonal Expansion Cost
**Each Division:**
- Telomere shortening
- Limited proliferative potential
- Eventually reach exhaustion

**Result:**
- Even dominant clones have shortened telomeres
- Functional impairment over time
- Contributes to immunosenescence

---

## Therapeutic Implications

### Targeted Therapy
**Opportunity:**
- Identify pathogenic clones
- Develop targeted interventions
- Preserve beneficial immunity

**Challenges:**
- Heterogeneity between patients
- Clones change over time
- Technical complexity

### Immune Reconstitution
**Approaches:**
- Hematopoietic stem cell transplantation (HSCT)
- Resets repertoire
- "Reboot" of immune system

**Risks:**
- High toxicity
- May not prevent recurrence
- Expensive and invasive
