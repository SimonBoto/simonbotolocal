## Core Claim
**Trioses (3-carbon sugars) are the simplest monosaccharides — glyceraldehyde (aldotriose) and dihydroxyacetone (ketotriose) are constitutional isomers that interconvert via triose phosphate isomerase, the fastest enzyme known** — these triose phosphates (G3P and DHAP) are the central hub connecting glycolysis, PPP, gluconeogenesis, and photosynthesis.

## Evidence

### The Two Trioses
| Feature | Glyceraldehyde | Dihydroxyacetone |
|---------|---------------|------------------|
| **Type** | Aldotriose (C1 aldehyde) | Ketotriose (C2 ketone) |
| **Formula** | C₃H₆O₃ | C₃H₆O₃ |
| **Metabolic form** | G3P | DHAP |
| **Chirality** | D- and L- (D-G3P used) | Optically inactive |

### Triose Phosphate Isomerase
| Property | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| **kcat** | ~10⁴ s⁻¹ (fastest enzyme) |
| **Equilibrium** | DHAP:G3P ~20:1 |
| **Function** | Rapidly equilibrates trioses |

### Metabolic Integration
| Pathway | Triose Role |
|---------|-------------|
| **Glycolysis** | G3P → pyruvate → energy |
| **PPP** | Non-oxidative branch interconversion |
| **Gluconeogenesis** | G3P ← glucose synthesis |
| **Calvin cycle** | Photosynthetic carbon fixation |

## Clinical Implication
- **Triose phosphate isomerase deficiency:** Rare, severe hemolytic anemia (G3P accumulation)
- **Evolutionary conservation:** Defects often lethal — fundamental to all life
- **Cancer metabolism:** Warburg effect alters triose flux toward biosynthesis
- **METHAP relevance:** Thiamine (B1) supports transketolase reactions with triose phosphates in PPP
