# OAT Clinical Action Mapping

## Core Claim
**Specific OAT markers map directly to targeted clinical interventions — with elevated pyruvate indicating B1 need, methylmalonate requiring B12, indican suggesting digestive support, and lactate/pyruvate ratios guiding mitochondrial support — enabling precise, mechanism-based treatment rather than empiric supplementation.**

## Evidence

### Direct Marker-to-Treatment Mapping
| Marker | Indicates | Primary Intervention | Dose |
|--------|-----------|---------------------|------|
| **Pyruvate ↑** | B1 (TPP) deficiency | Thiamine | 100-300 mg/day |
| **α-KG ↑** | B1/B2/B3/B5/Mg | B-complex + Mg | As per formula |
| **Succinate ↑** | B2 (FAD) deficiency | Riboflavin | 50-100 mg/day |
| **Methylmalonate ↑** | B12 deficiency | Methylcobalamin | 1000 mcg/day |
| **FIGLU ↑** | Folate deficiency | Methylfolate | 400-800 mcg/day |
| **Indican ↑** | Protein malabsorption | Digestive enzymes | With meals |
| **Adipate/Suberate ↑** | Carnitine need | L-carnitine | 1-3 g/day |
| **Citrate ↑** | Oxidative stress | Antioxidants | Mixed formula |

### Combination Patterns and Protocols
| Pattern | Markers | Protocol |
|---------|---------|----------|
| **Mitochondrial support** | Lactate, pyruvate, TCA markers | B-complex + Mg + CoQ10 + carnitine |
| **Methylation support** | Methylmalonate, FIGLU | Methylated B12 + folate + cofactors |
| **Gut restoration** | Indican, HPHPA, yeast | Enzymes + antimicrobials + probiotics |
| **Antioxidant** | Citrate, 8-OHdG | Vitamin C, E, selenium, NAC |

### Priority Matrix
| Priority | Marker/Pattern | Action Timeline |
|----------|---------------|-----------------|
| **Critical** | Lactate >5, ratio >50 | Immediate intervention |
| **High** | B12 deficiency, severe mitochondrial | Within 1 week |
| **Moderate** | Mild elevations, single markers | Within 1 month |
| **Optimization** | Borderline, prevention | Ongoing |

### Monitoring Strategy
| Marker | Recheck Timeline | Success Criteria |
|--------|-----------------|------------------|
| **B-vitamin markers** | 8-12 weeks | Normalization |
| **Mitochondrial markers** | 12-16 weeks | >50% improvement |
| **Gut markers** | 8-12 weeks | Significant reduction |
| **Oxidative stress** | 12-16 weeks | Trend improvement |

## Clinical Implication
- **Precision:** Target specific deficiencies
- **Efficiency:** Avoid unnecessary supplementation
- **Monitoring:** Objective markers track progress
- **Cost-effectiveness:** Right intervention, right patient
- **METHAP relevance:** Biomarker-guided interventions enable rigorous research methodology with objective outcomes
