# OAT Clinical Action Mapping

From biomarker to treatment: specific markers guide specific interventions. This mapping connects OAT patterns to evidence-based clinical actions.

## Evidence
> "The OAT is not just diagnostic—it's a roadmap for intervention. Each pattern points to specific treatments." (Functional medicine)

## Priority Matrix

### Immediate Action (High Priority)
| Condition | Markers |
|-----------|---------|
| Severe lactic acidosis | Lactate > 10, L/P > 100 |
| Severe B12 deficiency | MMA > 10× upper limit |
| Severe dysbiosis | Multiple markers > 10× |

### Short-term Action (2-4 weeks)
| Condition | Markers |
|-----------|---------|
| Moderate mitochondrial dysfunction | L/P 40-80 |
| Moderate gut dysbiosis | 3-5 markers 3-5× |
| Moderate oxidative stress | 8-OHdG 3-5× |
