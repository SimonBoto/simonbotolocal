# Aconitase Fe-S Cluster Dynamics

## Core Claim

**Aconitase activity depends on a [4Fe-4S] iron-sulfur cluster that is highly redox-sensitive** — ROS exposure destroys the cluster, converting aconitase from metabolic enzyme to iron regulatory protein (IRP1).

## Mechanism

### Fe-S Cluster States

| State | Iron Content | Activity | Function |
|-------|-------------|----------|----------|
| **Holo-aconitase** | [4Fe-4S] | ✅ Active | Citrate → isocitrate (TCA cycle) |
| **Apo-aconitase** | [3Fe-4S] or none | ❌ Inactive | Binds IRE (iron regulation) |

### Redox Sensitivity

**ROS → Cluster Destruction:**
- Superoxide (O₂⁻) attacks [4Fe-4S]
- Iron released → oxidative stress amplification
- Enzyme switches to IRP1 role

**Consequences:**
- Citrate accumulation (metabolic shift)
- Iron homeostasis dysregulation
- Potential therapeutic target

## Clinical Relevance

| Condition | Mechanism | Implication |
|-----------|-----------|-------------|
| **Cancer** | Warburg effect, iron addiction | Target Fe-S biogenesis |
| **Neurodegeneration** | Mitochondrial ROS, iron dyshomeostasis | Biomarker potential |
| **Ischemia-reperfusion** | ROS burst, cluster destruction | Protective strategies |
