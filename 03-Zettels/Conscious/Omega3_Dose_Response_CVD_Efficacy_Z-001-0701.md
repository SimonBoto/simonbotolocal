# Omega-3 Dose-Response Pattern in CVD Efficacy

## Core Principle
**Omega-3 cardiovascular efficacy follows a clear dose-response curve:** low doses (<1g/day) show no benefit, while high doses (1.8-4g/day) demonstrate significant CVD risk reduction.

## The Pattern

### Low-Dose Failures (<1g/day)
| Trial | Dose | Result |
|-------|------|--------|
| ASCEND | ~1g | No benefit |
| VITAL | ~1g | No benefit |
| ORIGIN | ~1g | No benefit |
| OMEGA | ~1g | No benefit |
| R&P | ~1g | No benefit |

### High-Dose Successes (1.8-4g/day)
| Trial | Dose | Result |
|-------|------|--------|
| REDUCE-IT | 4g EPA | 25% reduction |
| JELIS | 1.8g EPA | 19% reduction |
| PISCES | 3-4g | Significant benefit |
| Lok NEJM 2026 | 4g (EPA+DHA) | **43% reduction** |

## Clinical Implication
Minimum effective dose threshold likely exists between 1-2g/day. Subtherapeutic dosing explains prior negative trials.
