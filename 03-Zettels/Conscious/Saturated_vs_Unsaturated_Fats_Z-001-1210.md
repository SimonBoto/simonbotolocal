# Saturated vs Unsaturated Fatty Acids

## Core Claim
**Saturated fatty acids contain no double bonds (straight chains, solid at room temperature), while unsaturated fatty acids contain one (monounsaturated) or more (polyunsaturated) double bonds that create kinks in the hydrocarbon chain (liquid at room temperature) — with the degree of saturation determining physical properties, metabolic fate, and health effects, and the balance between saturated and unsaturated fats being a key factor in cardiovascular and metabolic health.**

## Evidence

### Structural Comparison
| Feature | Saturated | Monounsaturated | Polyunsaturated |
|---------|-----------|-----------------|-----------------|
| **Double bonds** | 0 | 1 | ≥2 |
| **Chain shape** | Straight | One kink | Multiple kinks |
| **Physical state** | Solid (fats) | Liquid (oils) | Liquid (oils) |
| **Stability** | High (stable) | Moderate | Low (easily oxidized) |
| **Formula** | CnH₂nO₂ | CnH₂n₋₂O₂ | CnH₂n₋₂ₓO₂ |

### Common Examples
| Type | Examples | Major Sources |
|------|----------|---------------|
| **Saturated** | Palmitic (C16:0), Stearic (C18:0) | Animal fats, coconut oil, palm oil |
| **Monounsaturated** | Oleic acid (C18:1ω9) | Olive oil, avocados, nuts |
| **Polyunsaturated ω-6** | Linoleic acid (C18:2) | Vegetable oils (corn, soybean, sunflower) |
| **Polyunsaturated ω-3** | ALA, EPA, DHA | Fish, flaxseed, walnuts |

### Health Effects
| Outcome | Saturated | Unsaturated |
|---------|-----------|-------------|
| **LDL cholesterol** | ↑ Increases | ↓ Decreases (MUFA/PUFA) |
| **HDL cholesterol** | Variable | ↑ Increases |
| **Cardiovascular risk** | ↑ Associated | ↓ Protective (when replacing SFA) |
| **Inflammation** | Neutral | ↓ Anti-inflammatory (PUFA) |
| **Oxidative stress** | Resistant | Susceptible (PUFA) |

### Metabolic Differences
| Aspect | Saturated | Unsaturated |
|--------|-----------|-------------|
| **β-oxidation** | Efficient | Efficient |
| **Membrane fluidity** | Decreases | Increases |
| **Gene expression** | SREBP-1c activation | PPAR-α activation |
| **Insulin sensitivity** | May impair | May improve |

## Clinical Implication
- **Replacement strategy:** Replace SFA with MUFA/PUFA, don't just add PUFA
- **Quality matters:** Whole food sources superior to isolated oils
- **Balance:** Some SFA necessary (cell membranes, hormones)
- **Oxidation risk:** PUFA require antioxidant protection
- **METHAP relevance:** Fatty acid composition affects metabolic flexibility and insulin sensitivity
