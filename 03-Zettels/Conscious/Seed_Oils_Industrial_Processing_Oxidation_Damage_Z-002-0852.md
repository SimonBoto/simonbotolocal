# Seed Oils: Industrial Processing Causes Oxidation and Lipid Damage

## Core Claim
**Industrial seed oils undergo extensive processing that damages lipids through oxidation** — damage worsens with air exposure and intensifies during cooking/frying.

## Industrial Processing Damage
| Issue | Result |
|-------|--------|
| **Extensive processing** | Lost nutrients and antioxidants |
| **High oxidation** | Damaged lipids |
| **Air exposure** | Worsens oxidation |
| **Cooking/frying** | Intensifies damage further |

## Historical Context
| Aspect | Reality |
|--------|---------|
| **Modern seed oils** | Industrial products (last 100 years) |
| **Human diet history** | Not part of traditional diets |
| **Not to be confused with** | Virgin olive oil, nut fats, traditional oils |

## Clinical Relevance
- Damaged lipids contribute to **inflammation**, **oxidative stress**, **cellular dysfunction**
- Cooking with seed oils generates **harmful oxidation products**

## Key Quote
> "The removal of processed foods and seed oils from the diet, combined with the use of traditional fats, is associated with substantial health improvement."

## Evidence Base
- Dozens of thousands of metabolomic analyses
- Published clinical studies in peer-reviewed journals
- International conference presentations
