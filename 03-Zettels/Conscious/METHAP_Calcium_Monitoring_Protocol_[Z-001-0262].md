# METHAP Calcium Monitoring Protocol

> High-dose vitamin D (8000 IU/day) in the METHAP trial requires systematic monitoring of calcium homeostasis to ensure participant safety, with scheduled assessments, clear intervention thresholds, and predefined stopping rules.

## Baseline Assessment (Pre-Study)

### Required Tests
- **Serum calcium (total)**
- **Ionized calcium** (if available)
- **25(OH)D level**
- **PTH** (if available)
- **Creatinine/eGFR** (renal function)
- **Albumin** (to correct total calcium)

### Exclusion Criteria
- Serum Ca >10.5 mg/dL
- History of kidney stones (relative)
- Severe renal impairment (eGFR <30)
- Current hyperparathyroidism
- Granulomatous disease (sarcoidosis, TB)

### Documentation
- Baseline values
- Risk factors
- Concomitant medications
- Informed consent for monitoring

## Monitoring Schedule

### 4 Weeks
**Tests:**
- Serum calcium
- 25(OH)D
- Symptom assessment

**Actions:**
| Result | Action |
|--------|--------|
| Ca <10.5 | Continue protocol |
| Ca 10.5-11.0 | Increase monitoring, increase K2 |
| Ca 11.0-11.5 | Reduce D to 4000 IU |
| Ca >11.5 | Stop D, investigate |

### 8 Weeks
**Tests:**
- Serum calcium
- 25(OH)D
- Symptom assessment

**Actions:**
| Result | Action |
|--------|--------|
| Ca normal | Continue protocol |
| Ca elevated | Follow 4-week protocol |
| Symptoms | Immediate evaluation |

### 12 Weeks (End of Study)
**Tests:**
- Serum calcium
- 25(OH)D
- PTH (if available)
- Creatinine
- Symptom assessment

**Actions:**
- Full safety assessment
- Compare to baseline
- Document outcomes
- Plan follow-up if needed

## Intervention Thresholds

### Serum Calcium
- **Normal:** <10.5 mg/dL
- **Mild elevation:** 10.5-11.0 (monitor)
- **Moderate elevation:** 11.0-11.5 (reduce D)
- **Severe elevation:** >11.5 (stop D)

### 25(OH)D Levels
- **Target:** 40-60 ng/mL
- **Optimal range:** 40-80 ng/mL
- **Toxicity risk:** >150 ng/mL

### Symptoms (Regardless of Lab Value)
- Nausea, vomiting
- Severe constipation
- Confusion
- Polyuria, polydipsia
- **Action:** Stop D, evaluate

## Safety Safeguards

### Built-In Protections
1. **K2 co-administration** (100-200 mcg)
   - Directs calcium to bone
   - Prevents vascular calcification

2. **Mg optimization** (400-500 mg)
   - Enables D activation
   - Prevents calcium overshoot

3. **No calcium supplements**
   - Dietary calcium only
   - Reduces hypercalcemia risk

4. **Participant education**
   - Recognize symptoms
   - Report promptly
   - Compliance with monitoring

### Emergency Contacts
- Study physician available
- Clear escalation pathway
- 24-hour contact for symptoms

## Documentation Requirements

### Study Records
- All lab values
- Adverse events
- Dose adjustments
- Interventions

### Data Safety Monitoring
- Regular review of safety data
- Independent oversight
- Predefined stopping rules
