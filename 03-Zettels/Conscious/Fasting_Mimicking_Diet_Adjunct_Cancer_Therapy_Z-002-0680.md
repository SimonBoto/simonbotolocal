# Fasting-Mimicking Diet as Adjunct Cancer Therapy

## Core Claim
**Fasting-mimicking diets (FMD)** — low-calorie, low-protein, plant-based protocols that mimic fasting's metabolic effects without complete food deprivation — represent a practical clinical intervention to enhance immunotherapy and potentially other cancer treatments with improved patient compliance.

## FMD Protocol

### Composition
| Component | Specification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Calories** | ~40% of normal (400-600 kcal/day) |
| **Protein** | <10% of calories (<15g/day) |
| **Fat** | Moderate, plant-based |
| **Carbohydrates** | Low glycemic, vegetable-based |

### Foods Allowed
| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| **Vegetables** | Soups, broths, low-starch |
| **Beverages** | Herbal teas, water |
| **Fats** | Olive oil (small amounts) |
| **Supplements** | Micronutrients, electrolytes |

## Advantages Over Water Fasting
| Aspect | FMD | Water Fast |
|--------|-----|------------|
| **Compliance** | Higher | Lower |
| **Safety** | Better monitored | More risks |
| **Acceptability** | Easier for patients | Difficult |
| **Metabolic effects** | Similar | Reference standard |

## Clinical Evidence

### Phase 2 Trials
| Finding | Result |
|---------|--------|
| **FMD + chemotherapy** | Reduced side effects |
| **FMD + immunotherapy** | Enhanced response |
| **Tumor sensitization** | Better treatment efficacy |
