# BCAA to Succinyl-CoA: The Methylmalonyl Pathway

## Four Amino Acids Converge on Propionyl-CoA

**Methionine, Isoleucine, Valine, and Threonine** all produce **propionyl-CoA** during catabolism, which is then converted to succinyl-CoA.

## The Complete Pathway

### Step 1: Amino Acid → Propionyl-CoA
```
Methionine ──┐
Isoleucine ──┼──→ Propionyl-CoA
Valine ──────┤
Threonine ───┘
```

### Step 2: Propionyl-CoA → Methylmalonyl-CoA
```
Propionyl-CoA + CO₂ + ATP → D-Methylmalonyl-CoA + ADP + Pi
```
- **Enzyme:** Propionyl-CoA carboxylase (PCC)
- **Cofactor:** Biotin (vitamin B7)

### Step 3: Methylmalonyl-CoA Epimerization
```
D-Methylmalonyl-CoA → L-Methylmalonyl-CoA
```
- **Enzyme:** Methylmalonyl-CoA epimerase

### Step 4: L-Methylmalonyl-CoA → Succinyl-CoA ⭐ CRITICAL STEP
```
L-Methylmalonyl-CoA → Succinyl-CoA
```
- **Enzyme:** **Methylmalonyl-CoA mutase (MMCoAM)**
- **Cofactor:** **Vitamin B12 (adenosylcobalamin)**
- **Location:** [[KEYWORD-Mitochondria|mitochondria]]l matrix

## Why This Pathway is Critical

### Vitamin B12 Dependence
The final step **requires B12** — this is the only human enzyme reaction that needs adenosylcobalamin.

**B12 deficiency consequences:**
- Methylmalonic acid (MMA) accumulation
- Impaired BCAA [[KEYWORD-Metabolism|metabolism]]
- Neurological symptoms (irreversible if prolonged)

### Clinical Disorders

| Disorder | Defect | Accumulation | Symptoms |
|----------|--------|--------------|----------|
| **Methylmalonic acidemia** | MMCoAM deficiency | Methylmalonic acid | Acidosis, developmental delay, lethargy |
| **B12 deficiency** | Cofactor lack | Methylmalonic acid | Neuropathy, megaloblastic anemia |
| **Propionic acidemia** | PCC deficiency | Propionic acid | Ketotic hyperglycinemia, seizures |

### Maple Syrup Urine Disease (MSUD)
While not directly in this pathway, **MSUD** affects the **earlier step** of BCAA catabolism:
- Defect in **branched-chain α-ketoacid dehydrogenase (BCKD)**
- Blocks leucine, isoleucine, valine → propionyl-CoA
- Accumulation of branched-chain ketoacids

## Research Relevance

### BCAA Metabolism in Disease
- **[[KEYWORD-Insulin|insulin]] resistance:** Elevated plasma BCAAs predict T2DM
- **Obesity:** Altered BCAA catabolism in adipose tissue
- **Cardiovascular:** BCAA metabolites associated with CVD risk

### Therapeutic Targets
- **BCKDK inhibitors:** Increase BCAA oxidation (potential for MSUD, metabolic disease)
