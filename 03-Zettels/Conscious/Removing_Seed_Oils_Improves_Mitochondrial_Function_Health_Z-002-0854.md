# Removing Seed Oils Improves Mitochondrial Function and Overall Health

## Core Claim
**Removing processed foods and seed oils while using traditional fats is associated with substantial health improvements** — 20 years of metabolomic analysis show benefits.

## Clinical Evidence (20 Years Metabolomic Analysis)
Removing seed oils + using traditional fats associated with:

| Improvement | Direction |
|-------------|-----------|
| **Mitochondrial energy production** | ↑ Better fuel quality |
| **Immune system function** | ↑ Reduced inflammation |
| **Insulin sensitivity** | ↑ Metabolic optimization |
| **Chronic inflammatory diseases** | ↓ Reduced disease activity |
| **Oxidation** | ↓ Reduced |
| **Chronic inflammation** | ↓ Reduced |
| **Fatigue** | ↓ Reduced |
| **Mood swings** | → Stabilized |

## Conditions Improved
- Autoimmune diseases
- Cardiovascular disease
- Diabetes
- Asthma

## Evidence Base
- Dozens of thousands of metabolomic analyses — consistent improvement
- Published clinical studies in peer-reviewed journals
- International conference presentations

## Mechanism
| Factor | Result |
|--------|--------|
| **Better fuel quality** | Improved mitochondrial function |
| **Reduced inflammation** | Restored immune regulation |
| **Metabolic optimization** | Enhanced insulin sensitivity |

## Clinical Implication
- Dietary fat quality is **foundational to metabolic health**
- Seed oil elimination should be **first-line intervention** for chronic inflammatory conditions
- Traditional fats support cellular function at **fundamental level**
