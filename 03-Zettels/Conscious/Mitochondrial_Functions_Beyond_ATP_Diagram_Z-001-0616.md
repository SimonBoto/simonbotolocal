## The Six Functions

### 1. 🔻 Cytochrome c → Apoptosis
- **Process:** Cytochrome c release from mitochondria triggers caspase cascade
- **Significance:** Programmed cell death initiation
- **Clinical relevance:** Cancer evasion, tissue homeostasis

### 2. 🔷 H₂O₂ → Redox + Protein Thiol Signaling
- **Process:** Mitochondrial ROS (reactive oxygen species) as signaling molecules
- **Significance:** Oxidative stress responses, protein modification
- **Clinical relevance:** Aging, metabolic disease

### 3. 🔴 NADH/NAD⁺ → Integrated Stress Response
- **Process:** Redox state communicates metabolic status to nucleus
- **Significance:** Cellular adaptation to nutrient availability
- **Clinical relevance:** Metabolic flexibility, fasting response

### 4. 🔺 AMPK → Metabolic/Catabolic Shift
- **Process:** Low energy signals activate AMP-activated protein kinase
- **Significance:** Switch from anabolic to catabolic metabolism
- **Clinical relevance:** Diabetes, exercise adaptation

### 5. 🧬 mtDNA/mtRNA → Innate Immune Activation
- **Process:** Mitochondrial nucleic acids trigger immune sensors (cGAS-STING)
- **Significance:** Inflammation, antiviral response
- **Clinical relevance:** Autoimmunity, sepsis

### 6. 💠 TCA Metabolites → Chromatin + Cell-Fate Programming
- **Process:** Citrate, α-ketoglutarate, acetyl-CoA as epigenetic regulators
- **Significance:** Histone modification, DNA methylation
- **Clinical relevance:** Stem cell differentiation, cancer metabolism

## Integrated View

These functions reveal mitochondria as **signaling hubs**, not just power plants:
- **Death signals** (apoptosis)
- **Stress signals** (ROS, NADH/NAD⁺)
- **Metabolic signals** (AMPK)
- **Immune signals** (mtDNA)
- **Epigenetic signals** (TCA metabolites)
