## Core Claim
**Weeks 7-8 form the "metabolomics brain" through energy-first reasoning, cellular detox, and membrane reconstruction — using daily case drills and treatment planning to transform trainees from students into competent clinicians who can independently manage complex metabolic cases.**

## Evidence

### Three Integration Algorithms
| Algorithm | Application | Key Questions |
|-----------|-------------|---------------|
| **Energy-first** | Fatigue, cognitive issues | Is this mitochondrial dysfunction? |
| **Detox/methylation** | Chemical sensitivity, hormonal issues | Are detox pathways overwhelmed? |
| **Membrane** | Neurological, immune issues | Is membrane integrity compromised? |

### Daily Practice Structure
| Component | Time | Purpose |
|-----------|------|---------|
| **Case presentation** | 30 min | Complex patient scenarios |
| **Interpretation** | 45 min | OAT + fatty acid analysis |
| **Treatment planning** | 45 min | Mechanism-based regimen design |
| **Communication** | 30 min | Patient explanation practice |

### Competency Demonstration
By graduation, trainee must:
- [ ] Interpret complex OAT without supervision
- [ ] Design personalized treatment protocols
- [ ] Explain findings in patient-friendly language
- [ ] Adjust plans based on follow-up data
- [ ] Integrate multiple test modalities
- [ ] Handle challenging clinical scenarios

### The "Metabolomics Brain"
| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Systems thinking** | See interconnections, not isolated markers |
| **Mechanism-based** | Understand why, not just what |
| **Individualized** | Tailor to patient, not protocol |
| **Adaptive** | Adjust based on response |

## Clinical Implication
- **Competence assurance:** Drills verify readiness for independent practice
- **Confidence building:** Repeated success in simulated cases builds real confidence
- **Error reduction:** Supervised practice catches errors before patient impact
- **Standardization:** Ensures consistent quality across trainees
- **METHAP relevance:** Research requires similar integration — combining biomarker data, clinical history, and mechanism to form hypotheses and design interventions
