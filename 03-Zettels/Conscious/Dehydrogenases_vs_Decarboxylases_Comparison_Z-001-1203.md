## Core Claim
**Dehydrogenases catalyze oxidation-reduction reactions by transferring hydride ions to cofactors (NAD+, FAD), while decarboxylases remove carboxyl groups as CO2 — with many enzymes combining both activities (oxidative decarboxylation) to extract maximum energy from substrates, particularly in the Krebs cycle where isocitrate dehydrogenase and α-ketoglutarate dehydrogenase exemplify this dual functionality.**

## Evidence

### Enzyme Class Comparison
| Feature | Dehydrogenases | Decarboxylases |
|---------|---------------|----------------|
| **Reaction type** | Oxidation-reduction | Carbon removal |
| **Cofactors** | NAD+, FAD | TPP, PLP, biotin |
| **Product** | Reduced cofactor (NADH, FADH2) | CO2 |
| **Energy yield** | ATP via ETC | None directly |
| **Examples** | LDH, MDH, PDH | Pyruvate decarboxylase, GAD |

### Combined Activity: Oxidative Decarboxylation
| Enzyme | Dehydrogenation | Decarboxylation | Net Reaction |
|--------|-----------------|-----------------|--------------|
| **Pyruvate DH** | Pyruvate → Acetyl-lipoamide | Acetyl-lipoamide → Acetyl-CoA | Pyruvate → Acetyl-CoA + CO2 + NADH |
| **Isocitrate DH** | Isocitrate → Oxalosuccinate | Oxalosuccinate → α-KG | Isocitrate → α-KG + CO2 + NADH |
| **α-KG DH** | α-KG → Succinyl-lipoamide | Succinyl-lipoamide → Succinyl-CoA | α-KG → Succinyl-CoA + CO2 + NADH |

### Cofactor Requirements
| Enzyme Type | Required Cofactors | Function |
|-------------|-------------------|----------|
| **Simple dehydrogenase** | NAD+ or FAD | Electron transfer |
| **Simple decarboxylase** | TPP or PLP | Carbanion stabilization |
| **Oxidative decarboxylase** | NAD+, TPP, lipoate, CoA, FAD | Both activities |

### Clinical Relevance
| Deficiency | Affected Enzyme | Clinical Manifestation |
|------------|-----------------|----------------------|
| **B1 (Thiamine)** | PDH, α-KG DH, BCKDH | Lactic acidosis, neurological |
| **B2 (Riboflavin)** | Multiple dehydrogenases | Fatigue, dermatitis |
| **B3 (Niacin)** | NAD+-dependent DHs | Pellagra |
| **B6 (Pyridoxine)** | Amino acid decarboxylases | Neuropathy, seizures |

## Clinical Implication
- **Dual-function enzymes:** Maximize energy extraction efficiency
- **Cofactor overlap:** B vitamins support both reaction types
- **Deficiency consequences:** Affect multiple pathways simultaneously
- **Diagnostic patterns:** Specific metabolites indicate enzyme blocks
- **METHAP relevance:** Understanding enzyme mechanisms enables rational metabolic support
