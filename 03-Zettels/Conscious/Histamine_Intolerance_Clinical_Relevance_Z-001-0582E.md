# Histamine Intolerance Clinical Relevance

> **Core Claim:** DAO deficiency impairs histamine degradation, causing symptoms unrelated to MAO inhibition.

## Pathophysiology

```
DAO deficiency → Impaired histamine breakdown → Accumulation → Symptoms
```

## Causes of DAO Deficiency

| Type | Examples |
|------|----------|
| **Genetic** | SNPs in AOC1 gene |
| **Acquired** | Gut inflammation, medications |

## Clinical Manifestations

| System | Symptoms |
|--------|----------|
| **GI** | Bloating, diarrhea, abdominal pain |
| **Neurological** | Headaches, migraines |
| **Dermatological** | Flushing, hives, itching |
| **Cardiovascular** | Palpitations, hypotension |

## Dietary Triggers

High-histamine foods:
- Fermented foods
- Aged cheeses
- Alcohol
- Processed meats

## Distinction from MAOI Effects

> Histamine intolerance is **NOT caused by MAO inhibitors** — separate mechanism entirely.

## Management

- DAO enzyme supplementation
- Low-histamine diet
- Address underlying gut issues
