# Carnitine Shuttle Fatty Acid Transport

## Overview
The carnitine shuttle system transports long-chain fatty acids across the l inner membrane for β-oxidation.

## The Three Components

### 1. CPT I (Carnitine Palmitoyltransferase I)
- **Location:** Outer  membrane
- **Function:** Rate-limiting step; conjugates fatty acyl-CoA with carnitine
- **Regulation:** Inhibited by malonyl-CoA (from fed state)
- **Isoforms:** CPT1A (liver), CPT1B (muscle), CPT1C (brain)

### 2. CACT (Carnitine-Acylcarnitine Translocase)
- **Location:** Inner mitochondrial membrane
- **Function:** Antiporter exchanging acylcarnitine (in) for free carnitine (out)
- **Mechanism:** 1:1 exchange

### 3. CPT II (Carnitine Palmitoyltransferase II)
- **Location:** Matrix side of inner membrane
- **Function:** Releases fatty acid from carnitine, regenerating acyl-CoA for β-oxidation

## Transport Sequence
```
Cytoplasm: Fatty acyl-CoA + Carnitine ──CPT I──> Acylcarnitine + CoA
              ↓
Intermembrane space
              ↓
Inner membrane: Acylcarnitine(in) + Carnitine(out) ←──CACT──→
              ↓
Matrix: Acylcarnitine + CoA ──CPT II──> Fatty acyl-CoA + Carnitine
              ↓
        → β-OXIDATION
```

## Clinical Relevance

### Primary Carnitine Deficiency
- **Cause:** OCTN2 transporter defect
- **Presentation:** Hypoketotic hypoglycemia, cardiomyopathy, muscle weakness
- **Treatment:** High-dose oral carnitine

### Secondary Carnitine Deficiency
| Cause | Mechanism |
|-------|-----------|
| **Valproate use** | Sequesters carnitine |
| **Renal loss** | Fanconi syndrome |
| **Dialysis** | Removal during treatment |
| **Malnutrition** | Insufficient intake (meat, dairy) |
| **Increased demand** | High-fat diet without adequate intake |

### OAT Markers
| Marker | Interpretation |
|--------|----------------|
| **Suberate ↑** | Medium-chain β-oxidation block |
| **Adipate ↑** | Carnitine insufficiency |
| **Low carnitine metabolites** | Functional carnitine deficiency |

## Acylcarnitine Profiles
Elevated acylcarnitines in blood spot testing indicate:
- **Specific enzyme blocks** in fatty acid oxidation
- **Carnitine insufficiency** (cannot transport)
- **Organic acidemias** (backup into acylcarnitine pool)

## Therapeutic Considerations
| Form | Use |
|------|-----|
| **L-carnitine** | General supplementation |
| **Acetyl-L-carnitine** | Brain/cognitive focus |
| **Propionyl-L-carnitine** | Cardiovascular focus |
