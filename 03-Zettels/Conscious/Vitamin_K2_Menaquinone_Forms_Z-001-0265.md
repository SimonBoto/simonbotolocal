# Vitamin K2 Menaquinone Forms

## Core Thesis
Vitamin K2 exists in multiple menaquinone (MK) forms with distinct pharmacokinetic properties, tissue distribution, and biological activity, with MK-7 showing superior bioavailability and sustained activity compared to MK-4.

## Key Concepts

### Menaquinone Forms Overview
- **MK-4:** Short-chain, synthesized from vitamin K1 in animal tissues, rapid clearance (t½ ~1-2 hours)
- **MK-7:** Long-chain, fermented food source (natto), slow clearance (t½ ~3 days), superior bioavailability
- **MK-8 & MK-9:** Longer-chain forms with similar properties to MK-7 but less studied
- **MK-10 & MK-11:** Even longer-chain forms, primarily in dairy products

### Pharmacokinetic Differences
| Parameter | MK-4 | MK-7 |
|-----------|------|------|
| Half-life | 1-2 hours | ~72 hours |
| Peak plasma | 1-2 hours | 6-8 hours |
| Bioavailability | Lower | Higher (up to 10x) |
| Tissue distribution | Liver-centric | Broader (bone, vasculature) |

### Biological Implications
- **MK-7's longer half-life** enables once-daily dosing and sustained activation of vitamin K-dependent proteins (MGP, osteocalcin)
- **MK-4's rapid clearance** requires multiple daily doses for sustained effect
- **Tissue specificity:** MK-7 preferentially accumulates in extrahepatic tissues critical for cardiovascular and bone health

## Evidence Base
- Knapen et al. (2013): MK-7 180 mcg/day showed significant reduction in arterial stiffness over 3 years
- Schurgers et al. (2007): MK-7 demonstrated superior carboxylation of MGP compared to MK-4
- Thijssen et al. (2006): MK-7 achieved higher plasma concentrations at lower doses than MK-4

## Clinical Significance
- MK-7 is the preferred form for cardiovascular protection due to sustained MGP activation
- MK-4 may be more relevant for rapid hepatic vitamin K-dependent clotting factor synthesis
- Dosing strategies must account for pharmacokinetic differences between forms

## METHAP Protocol
- Uses MK-7 (100-200 mcg/day) for optimal vascular protection
- Aligns with evidence showing MK-7's superior pharmacokinetics for long-term benefits
- Combined with high-dose vitamin D (8000 IU) and magnesium (400-500 mg) for synergistic effects
