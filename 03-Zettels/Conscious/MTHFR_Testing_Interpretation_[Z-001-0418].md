# MTHFR Testing and Interpretation

> MTHFR genotyping can guide  supplementation but should be interpreted cautiously as these are common variants, not pathogenic mutations, and dietary folate status remains the dominant factor.

## Testing Methods

**Available Tests:**
- PCR-based genotyping
- SNP arrays (23andMe, etc.)
- Direct sequencing
- Functional testing (rare)

## When to Test

**Indications:**
- Elevated homocysteine
- Recurrent pregnancy loss
- Neural tube defect history
- Family history CVD with high homocysteine
- Macrocytic anemia unexplained
- Treatment-resistant depression

## Interpretation Caution

**Common Variants:**
- 40-50% of population has at least one variant
- Normal genetic variation
- Not deterministic

**Environment Dominates:**
- Dietary folate status > genotype
- Supplemental folate overcomes variant effects
- Don't over-interpret

** Context:**
- One piece of puzzle
- Combine with homocysteine levels
- Consider other B vitamins
- Assess symptoms

## Practical Utility

**Guides:**
- Folate form selection (methylfolate)
- Dose (higher if variant)
- Explains homocysteine elevation
- Informs supplementation strategy

**Does Not:**
- Predict disease
- Require aggressive intervention
- Change everything
- Cause alarm
