# Vitamin D as Hormone, Not Just Vitamin

## Core Claim
Vitamin D functions as a **steroid hormone**, not merely a vitamin — regulating approximately **2,000 genes** (10% of the genome) and exerting endocrine, paracrine, and autocrine effects on multiple organ systems.

## Hormonal vs. Vitamin Classification

### Vitamin Definition
- Essential nutrient required in small amounts
- Prevents specific deficiency disease
- Example: Vitamin C prevents scurvy

### Hormone Definition
- Chemical messenger produced in one tissue
- Acts on distant target cells via receptors
- Regulates multiple physiological processes

### Vitamin D Fits Hormone Criteria
| Criterion | Vitamin D Evidence |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Synthesis in body | Skin synthesis from 7-dehydrocholesterol |
| Receptor-mediated action | VDR (vitamin D receptor) in most cells |
| Endocrine function | Renal activation → systemic circulation |
| Paracrine/autocrine function | Local activation in immune cells, etc. |
| Gene regulation | ~2,000 genes modulated |

## Mechanism of Action

### Synthesis Pathway
1. **Skin:** UVB → 7-dehydrocholesterol → cholecalciferol (D3)
2. **Liver:** 25-hydroxylation → 25(OH)D (calcidiol)
3. **Kidney/Other tissues:** 1α-hydroxylation → 1,25(OH)2D (calcitriol)

### Physiological Production
- **15-20 minutes midday sun:** 10,000-20,000 IU synthesized
- **Full-body exposure:** Up to 25,000 IU possible
- **Self-limiting:** Skin pigment prevents toxicity

## Gene Regulation

### VDR-Mediated Effects
- **Direct targets:** ~1,000 genes with VDR response elements
- **Indirect targets:** Additional ~1,000 genes via signaling cascades
- **Tissues affected:** Bone, immune, cardiovascular, neural, endocrine

### Key Regulated Functions
| System | Genes Regulated | Examples |
|--------|-----------------|----------|
| Immune | 500+ | Cytokines, antimicrobial peptides |
| Bone | 300+ | Osteocalcin, collagen |
| Metabolism | 400+ | Insulin receptor, lipogenesis |
| Cell cycle | 200+ | Apoptosis, differentiation |

## Clinical Implications

### Beyond Bone Health
- **Immune modulation:** Autoimmune prevention, infection resistance
- **Metabolic health:** Insulin sensitivity, weight regulation
- **Cancer prevention:** Cell differentiation, apoptosis induction
- **Cardiovascular:** Blood pressure, vascular function
- **Neurological:** Neuroprotection, mood regulation

### Dosing Implications
- Hormonal functions require **adequate levels**, not just "non-deficient"
- RDA concept (prevent deficiency) insufficient for hormonal optimization
- Target: 40-60 ng/mL for full genomic effects

## Evidence

> "Vitamin D regulates approximately 2,000 genes, affecting immune system, metabolism, and cellular differentiation."
> — Inbox synthesis from Sha et al. 2023, Hahn et al. 2022
