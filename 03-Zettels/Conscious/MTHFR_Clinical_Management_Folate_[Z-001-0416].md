# MTHFR [[KEYWORD-Clinical|clinical]] Management and [[KEYWORD-Folate|folate]]

> MTHFR polymorphisms increase folate requirements and necessitate using 5-methyltetrahydrofolate (methylfolate) rather than folic acid, as methylfolate bypasses the impaired MTHFR enzyme.

## Folate Requirements by Genotype

| Genotype | Folate Requirement | Form |
|----------|-------------------|------|
| CC/AA (wild) | Standard 400 mcg | Either |
| CT or AC | Moderate 600-800 mcg | Methylfolate preferred |
| TT or CC | High 800-1000+ mcg | Methylfolate required |
| Compound heterozygote | High 800-1000+ mcg | Methylfolate required |

## Methylfolate (5-MTHF) Advantage

**Bypasses MTHFR:**
- Already in active form
- No conversion needed
- Directly usable regardless of genotype
- No unmetabolized folic acid accumulation

**Folic Acid Limitation:**
- Must be converted by MTHFR
- Impaired in variants
- Unmetabolized folic acid may accumulate
- May mask B12 deficiency

## Homocysteine Management

**Target:** <10 μmol/L

**Supplementation:**
- Methylfolate (active)
- Vitamin B12 (methylcobalamin)
- Vitamin B6 (pyridoxine)
- Betaine (trimethylglycine) — alternative pathway

**Monitoring:**
- Check homocysteine levels
- Adjust dose to target
- Lifelong if genetic

## METHAP Relevance

**Protocol Design:**
- Use methylfolate not folic acid
- Higher dose if variants common
- Consider genotyping participants
- Optimize [[KEYWORD-Methylation|methylation]] for all genotypes
