## Core Claim
**Six-stage patient flow operationalization — Assessment → Sample → Analysis → Reporting → Treatment → Follow-up — with standard operating procedures at each stage ensures consistent, high-quality metabolomics care while maintaining quality metrics including <2% pre-analytical error rate and <5-day report turnaround.**

## Evidence

### Six-Stage Operational Framework
| Stage | SOP | Quality Check | Time Target |
|-------|-----|---------------|-------------|
| **1. Assessment** | Intake form completion | Metabolic story documented | 45 min |
| **2. Sample** | Collection protocol | Chain of custody maintained | 15 min |
| **3. Analysis** | OAT + fatty acid processing | QC within range | 3-5 days |
| **4. Reporting** | Standardized format | Patient-friendly language | 1 day |
| **5. Treatment** | Tiered prescribing protocol | Drug interactions checked | 30 min |
| **6. Follow-up** | Scheduled check-ins | Progress documented | Ongoing |

### Quality Metrics
| Metric | Target | Monitoring |
|--------|--------|------------|
| **Pre-analytical error rate** | < 2% | Monthly review |
| **Report turnaround time** | < 5 business days | Weekly tracking |
| **Patient satisfaction** | > 90% | Survey quarterly |
| **Clinical outcome tracking** | 100% at 12 weeks | Systematic |

## Clinical Implication
- **Consistency:** SOPs ensure uniform care quality
- **Efficiency:** Defined workflows reduce waste
- **Quality:** Metrics drive continuous improvement
- **Scalability:** Replicable system for multiple clinics
- **METHAP relevance:** Standardized protocols essential for research reproducibility
