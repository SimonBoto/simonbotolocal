# Glucose Chemistry — Emil Fischer and 19th Century Structure

> In the 19th century, the chemical formula of glucose (C6H12O6) was established, and by 1902 Emil Fischer's elucidation of glucose stereochemistry earned the Nobel Prize, creating the foundation for modern carbohydrate biochemistry.

## 19th Century Developments

**Chemical Formula (C6H12O6):**
- Established through elemental analysis
- Recognized as carbohydrate
- Isomerism discovered (same formula, different structure)
- Glucose, fructose, galactose identified as isomers

**Key Chemists:**
- Justus von Liebig (organic analysis)
- Jean Baptiste Dumas (molecular formulas)
- Multiple contributors to structure elucidation

## Emil Fischer (1852-1919)

**Nobel Prize 1902 (Chemistry):**
- "In recognition of the extraordinary services he has rendered by his work on sugar and purine syntheses"
- First organic chemist to receive Nobel

**Glucose Contributions:**
1. **Stereochemistry:** Determined spatial arrangement
2. **Fischer Projections:** 2D representation of 3D structure
3. **Synthesis:** First laboratory synthesis of glucose from glycerol
4. **Isomer Relationships:** Established glucose-fructose-galactose connections

**Fischer Projection:**
- Vertical line: Carbon chain
- Horizontal lines: Substituents (toward viewer)
- Still used in biochemistry today

## Stereochemistry and D-Glucose

**Chiral Centers:**
- 4 chiral carbons in glucose
- 16 possible stereoisomers
- D-glucose is the biologically relevant form

**Biological Specificity:**
- Enzymes recognize specific stereoisomers
- D-glucose metabolized; L-glucose not
- Lock-and-key specificity (later Fischer's work influenced this concept)

## Impact on Biochemistry

**Structural Biology Foundation:**
- 3D structure determines function
- Stereochemistry explains specificity
- Basis for enzyme-substrate interactions

** Understanding:**
- Glycolysis pathways mapped
- Structural basis for metabolic regulation
- Drug design applications

## Connection to Diabetes

**Precise Understanding:**
- Exact structure enabled   studies
- Glucose measurement methods developed
- Understanding of metabolic pathways

**Clinical Applications:**
- Blood glucose testing
- Synthetic insulin development
- Metabolic engineering
