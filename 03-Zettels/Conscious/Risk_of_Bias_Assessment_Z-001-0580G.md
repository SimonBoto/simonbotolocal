# Risk of Bias Assessment

> **Definition:** Risk of bias assessment is the systematic evaluation of methodological weaknesses in individual studies that could lead to systematic differences between compared groups or inaccurate estimates of effect, potentially compromising the validity of study findings.

## Key Assessment Tools

- **Cochrane RoB 2:** For randomized controlled trials (domain-based assessment)
- **ROBINS-I:** For non-randomized studies of interventions
- **STROBE:** For observational studies (cohort, case-control, cross-sectional)
- **CASP:** Critical Appraisal Skills Programme checklists
- **JBI:** Joanna Briggs Institute critical appraisal tools

## Core Domains (RoB 2)

1. **Randomization process:** Adequacy of random sequence generation and allocation concealment
2. **Deviations from intended interventions:** Impact of protocol deviations on outcomes
3. **Missing outcome data:** Impact of missing data on effect estimates
4. **Measurement of the outcome:** Risk of bias in outcome assessment
5. **Selection of the reported result:** Risk of bias from selective reporting

## Assessment Process

1. **Tool selection:** Choosing the appropriate tool based on study design
2. **Training:** Ensuring assessors are trained in the selected tool
3. **Dual assessment:** Independent assessment by two reviewers
4. **Consensus:** Resolving disagreements through discussion or third reviewer
5. **Documentation:** Recording detailed judgments and supporting evidence

## Reporting Standards

- Complete description of the assessment tool and process
- Domain-specific judgments for each included study
- Summary of risk of bias across studies
- Impact of risk of bias on results interpretation
- Use of ROB 2 summary plots or risk of bias tables
