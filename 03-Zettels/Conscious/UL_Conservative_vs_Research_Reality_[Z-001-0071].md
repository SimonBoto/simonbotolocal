# Upper Limits Conservative vs [[KEYWORD-Research|research]] Reality

Current IOM Upper Limits (UL) for [[KEYWORD-Vitamin_D|vitamin d]] are substantially lower than doses proven safe in [[KEYWORD-Clinical|clinical]] research. Pediatric studies demonstrate safety at doses 2-4x UL, creating evidence gap that affects clinical practice and regulatory policy.

**Comparison (infants):**
| Source | Dose | Safety |
|--------|------|--------|
| IOM UL (0-6mo) | 1,000 IU | Regulatory limit |
| IOM UL (6-12mo) | 1,500 IU | Regulatory limit |
| Saadi (2009) | 2,000 IU | Safe |
| Gallo (2013) | 1,600 IU | Safe |
| Stoss therapy | 50,000-600,000 IU | Safe with monitoring |

**Implication:** ULs appear arbitrary and not based on toxicity threshold. Clinical research consistently demonstrates safety at higher doses than regulatory limits allow.

**For protocols:** Research evidence supports doses 3-4x current ULs with appropriate monitoring.
