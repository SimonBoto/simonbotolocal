## Core Claim
**Methionine can be completely oxidized for energy, yielding both glucogenic (propionyl-CoA, pyruvate) and ketogenic potential, with full extraction requiring both B12 and B6** — this makes methionine a complete energy source when antioxidant demand (glutathione) is low.

## Evidence

### Degradation Pathway
| Step | Reaction | Cofactor | Product |
|------|----------|----------|---------|
| **1-3** | SAM → SAH → Homocysteine | — | Homocysteine |
| **4** | Transsulfuration | **B6** | Cysteine |
| **5** | Cysteine → Pyruvate | — | Pyruvate (glucogenic) |
| **6** | Methyl group → Propionyl-CoA | **B12** | Succinyl-CoA (glucogenic) |

### Energy Yield
| Component | Fate | ATP Contribution |
|-----------|------|------------------|
| **Pyruvate** | Krebs cycle | ~12 ATP |
| **Succinyl-CoA** | Krebs cycle | ~12 ATP |
| **Sulfur** | Excreted as sulfate | — |

### Net Result
- **Complete oxidation:** CO₂ + H₂O + ~24 ATP
- **Glucogenic:** Produces glucose precursors
- **Cofactor dependent:** Requires B6 and B12

## Clinical Implication
- **Methionine is glucogenic** — supports glucose production during fasting
- **B12/B6 deficiency blocks energy extraction** — methionine accumulates or diverts to homocysteine
- **High protein diets** increase methionine load — cofactor adequacy essential
- **METHAP relevance:** B12 and B6 support complete methionine oxidation; deficiency impairs energy from protein
