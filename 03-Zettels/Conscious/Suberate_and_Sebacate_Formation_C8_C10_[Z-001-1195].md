# Suberate and Sebacate Formation — C8 and C10 Dicarboxylic Acids

## Overview

While adipate (C6) is the most clinically studied dicarboxylic acid, suberate (C8) and sebacate (C10) provide additional information about fatty acid [[KEYWORD-Metabolism|metabolism]] patterns.

---

## Suberate (Octanedioic Acid) — C8

### Formation Pathway

**Precursors:**
- Octanoic acid (caprylic acid, C8:0)
- Partial β-oxidation of decanoic acid (C10:0)

**ω-Oxidation in ER:**
```
Octanoyl-CoA + O₂ + NADPH → ω-Hydroxyoctanoyl-CoA
          ↓
ω-Hydroxyoctanoyl-CoA → Octanedial → Suberate (HOOC-(CH₂)₆-COOH)
```

### Metabolism
Like adipate, suberate can undergo β-oxidation:
```
Suberate → Adipate → Succinyl-CoA + 2 Acetyl-CoA
```

### Clinical Significance
- Elevated with medium-chain fatty acid overload
- Seen in MCAD deficiency
- Less specific than adipate alone

---

## Sebacate (Decanedioic Acid) — C10

### Formation Pathway

**Precursors:**
- Decanoic acid (capric acid, C10:0)
- Partial β-oxidation of longer chains

**ω-Oxidation in ER:**
```
Decanoyl-CoA + O₂ + NADPH → ω-Hydroxydecanoyl-CoA
          ↓
ω-Hydroxydecanoyl-CoA → Decanedial → Sebacate (HOOC-(CH₂)₈-COOH)
```

### Metabolism
```
Sebacate → Suberate → Adipate → Succinyl-CoA + 3 Acetyl-CoA
```

### Clinical Significance
- Most sensitive to carnitine deficiency
- Elevated in medium-chain disorders
- Longer chain = slower metabolism

---

## Comparative Patterns

| Condition | Adipate (C6) | Suberate (C8) | Sebacate (C10) |
|-----------|--------------|---------------|----------------|
| **Normal** | Low | Low | Low |
| **Early IR** | ↑ Mild | Normal | Normal |
| **Advanced IR** | ↑↑ | ↑ Mild | ↑ Mild |
| **MCAD Deficiency** | ↑↑↑ | ↑↑ | ↑↑ |
| **Carnitine Deficiency** | ↑ | ↑ | ↑↑ |
| **Riboflavin Deficiency** | ↑ | ↑ | ↑ |

---

## Pattern Interpretation

### Adipate Dominant (High C6, Normal C8/C10)
- Indicates impairment of C6 fatty acid handling
- Early [[KEYWORD-Mitochondria|mitochondria]]l dysfunction
- Specific for [[KEYWORD-Insulin|insulin]] resistance pattern

### Elevated C6 + C8 + C10
- General β-oxidation impairment
- Genetic disorder likely (MCAD)
- Severe metabolic stress

### C10 > C8 > C6
- Carnitine transport limitation
- Longer chains accumulate preferentially
- Check acylcarnitine profile
