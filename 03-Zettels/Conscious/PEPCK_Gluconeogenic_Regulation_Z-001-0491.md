## Core Claim
**PEP carboxykinase (PEPCK) is rate-limiting for gluconeogenesis — its transcription is tightly controlled by hormonal signals: glucagon (via cAMP/CREB) and cortisol induce PEPCK, while insulin (via FOXO1 phosphorylation) represses it** — this hormonal control ensures glucose production during fasting while suppressing it after meals, with dysregulation contributing to diabetic hyperglycemia.

## Evidence

### PEPCK Function
| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Reaction** | Oxaloacetate + GTP → PEP + GDP + CO₂ |
| **Isozymes** | Cytosolic (PCK1) dominant; mitochondrial (PCK2) minor |
| **Location** | Liver, kidney, adipose (gluconeogenic tissues) |
| **Rate-limiting** | With pyruvate carboxylase |

### Glucagon Induction (Fasting)
```
Glucagon → cAMP ↑ → PKA activation
     ↓
CREB phosphorylation → binds PEPCK promoter
     ↓
PEPCK transcription ↑ (within hours)
```

### Cortisol Induction (Stress)
| Mechanism | Effect |
|-----------|--------|
| **Glucocorticoid receptor** | Binds PEPCK gene enhancer |
| **Synergy** | Amplifies glucagon effect |
| **Chronic elevation** | Contributes to insulin resistance |

### Insulin Repression (Fed State)
```
Insulin → FOXO1 phosphorylation → nuclear exclusion
     ↓
FOXO1 cannot activate PEPCK promoter
     ↓
PEPCK transcription ↓
```

### Additional Regulators
| Factor | Effect | Mechanism |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| **Thyroid hormone** | ↑ PEPCK | Transcriptional activation |
| **Retinoic acid** | ↑ PEPCK | RAR binding |
| **Glucose** | ↓ PEPCK | Repression via ChREBP |

## Clinical Implication
- **Type 2 diabetes:** Hepatic PEPCK overexpression → fasting hyperglycemia
- **Metformin:** Reduces hepatic gluconeogenesis (indirect PEPCK effect)
- **Cushing's syndrome:** Excess cortisol → PEPCK induction → hyperglycemia
- **METHAP relevance:** Controlling PEPCK expression is central to glucose management; lifestyle and medications target this pathway
