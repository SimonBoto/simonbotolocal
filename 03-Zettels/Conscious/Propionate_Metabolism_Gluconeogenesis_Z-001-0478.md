## Core Claim
**Propionate (CH₃-CH₂-COOH), a 3-carbon short-chain fatty acid produced by gut microbiome fermentation of dietary fiber, is gluconeogenic — it enters metabolism via propionyl-CoA and requires biotin (B7) for carboxylation and vitamin B12 for the methylmalonyl-CoA mutase reaction to form succinyl-CoA, a Krebs cycle intermediate** — deficiencies in either vitamin cause organic acidemias.

## Evidence

### Sources
| Source | Contribution |
|--------|--------------|
| **Gut microbiome** | Fermentation of dietary fiber (primary) |
| **Amino acid catabolism** | Methionine, valine, isoleucine, threonine |
| **Dairy products** | Natural content |

### Metabolic Pathway to Glucose
| Step | Conversion | Enzyme | Cofactor |
|------|------------|--------|----------|
| 1 | Propionate → Propionyl-CoA | Propionyl-CoA synthetase | ATP, CoA |
| 2 | Propionyl-CoA → Methylmalonyl-CoA | Propionyl-CoA carboxylase | **Biotin (B7)** |
| 3 | Methylmalonyl-CoA → Succinyl-CoA | Methylmalonyl-CoA mutase | **B12 (cobalamin)** |
| 4-6 | Succinyl-CoA → OAA → Glucose | Krebs + gluconeogenesis | — |

### Vitamin Dependencies
| Vitamin | Enzyme | Deficiency Consequence |
|---------|--------|------------------------|
| **B7 (Biotin)** | Propionyl-CoA carboxylase | Propionic acidemia |
| **B12 (Cobalamin)** | Methylmalonyl-CoA mutase | Methylmalonic acidemia |

## Clinical Implication
- **Propionic acidemia:** Autosomal recessive, presents with ketoacidosis, hyperammonemia
- **Methylmalonic acidemia:** B12-responsive or mutase-deficient forms
- **Gut health:** Fiber → propionate → host glucose/energy
- **METHAP relevance:** B12 and biotin are critical for propionate metabolism; deficiencies cause serious metabolic disorders
