# Insulin Receptor Autophosphorylation

## Core Principle
The insulin receptor undergoes autophosphorylation on tyrosine residues upon insulin binding, activating its intrinsic tyrosine kinase activity.

## Mechanism
1. Insulin binds to α-subunits of insulin receptor
2. Conformational change transmits to β-subunits
3. β-subunit tyrosine kinase domains activate
4. Autophosphorylation occurs on Tyr1158, Tyr1162, Tyr1163
5. Kinase activity fully activated

## Significance
- **Critical for:** Insulin signaling initiation
- **Downstream:** IRS1/2 phosphorylation, PI3K activation
- **Clinical:** Mutations impair glucose uptake
