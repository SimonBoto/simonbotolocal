## Core Claim
B-vitamins (folate, B12, B6, B2) are essential cofactors for methylation reactions that regulate gene expression through DNA and histone methylation; both deficiency and excess can alter the cancer epigenome, potentially promoting tumorigenesis through genome instability or tumor suppressor silencing.

## Evidence

### Methylation Pathways

**DNA Methylation:**
- DNMTs add methyl groups to cytosine
- Requires SAM (S-adenosylmethionine)
- SAM synthesis requires B-vitamins
- Patterns determine gene expression

**Histone Methylation:**
- Modifies chromatin structure
- Activates or represses transcription
- Requires SAM
- Dynamic and reversible

### Cancer Epigenetics

**Global Hypomethylation:**
- Genome instability
- Activation of oncogenes
- Associated with B-vitamin deficiency

**Promoter Hypermethylation:**
- Tumor suppressor silencing
- Common in cancer
- May be influenced by B-vitamin status

### The Dual Effect

**Deficiency:**
- Impaired methylation capacity
- Genome instability
- Increased cancer risk

**Excess (in existing cancer):**
- May fuel proliferation
- Provide methyl groups for tumor growth
- Context-dependent effects

## Clinical Implication

**Assessment:**
- Homocysteine (functional marker)
- RBC folate, B12 levels
- Consider methylation status

**Supplementation:**
- Prevent deficiency
- Caution with active cancer
- Individualized approach
