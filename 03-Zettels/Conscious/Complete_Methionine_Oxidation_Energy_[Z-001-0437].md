# Complete Methionine Oxidation — Energy Production

> Methionine can be completely oxidized for energy, producing propionyl-CoA (glucogenic), pyruvate (glucogenic), and sulfate, requiring B12 and B6 for complete extraction.

## Degradation Pathway

**Steps 1-3:** SAM → SAH → Homocysteine
- Standard methionine cycle

**Step 4:** Transsulfuration
- Homocysteine + Serine → Cystathionine → Cysteine
- B6-dependent

**Step 5:** Cysteine to Pyruvate
```
Cysteine → Pyruvate + NH₃ + H₂S
```
- Multiple enzymatic steps
- Sulfur released as H₂S (oxidized to sulfate)

**Step 6:** Propionate Pathway
- Methionine methyl group → Propionyl-CoA
- B12-dependent conversion to succinyl-CoA

## Energy Yield

**Pyruvate:**
- Enters [[KEYWORD-Krebs_Cycle|krebs cycle]] via acetyl-CoA
- Produces ATP, NADH, FADH₂

**Succinyl-CoA:**
- Krebs cycle intermediate
- Gluconeogenic precursor

## Net Result

- Complete oxidation → CO₂ + H₂O + energy
- Glucogenic (produces glucose)
- Sulfur excreted as sulfate
- Requires B6 and B12
