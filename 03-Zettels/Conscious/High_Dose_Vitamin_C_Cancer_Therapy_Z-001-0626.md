# High Dose Vitamin C Cancer Therapy Controversy

Pauling's most controversial claim — that high-dose vitamin C (10,000mg+) could prevent and treat cancer — triggered the strongest establishment backlash, yet recent research on IV vitamin C is validating aspects of his hypothesis.

## Pauling and Cameron's Work

**Initial studies (1970s):**
- Ewan Cameron (surgeon) + Linus Pauling
- Case series of terminal cancer patients
- 10,000mg/day oral vitamin C
- Reported improved survival, quality of life

**Claims:**
- Enhanced immune function
- Collagen barrier against metastasis
- Direct cytotoxicity to cancer cells
- Improved patient well-being

## The Mayo Clinic Rebuttal

**1985 study (Moertel et al.):**
- Randomized controlled trial
- Oral vitamin C vs. placebo
- No survival benefit found
- Widely cited as refutation

**Critique of Mayo study:**
- Oral vs. IV route (critical difference)
- Different patient population
- Prior chemotherapy (depleted patients)
- Discontinuation of vitamin C before death

## The Pharmacokinetic Key

**Critical insight (Levine et al., 1990s):**
- Oral vitamin C: limited absorption (~200mg max)
- IV vitamin C: achieves pharmacological concentrations
- Plasma levels 100x higher with IV
- Different mechanisms at high doses

**High-dose mechanisms:**
- Pro-oxidant effects (H2O2 generation)
- Epigenetic modulation
- Hypoxia-inducible factor inhibition
- Enhanced collagen synthesis

**Current status:**

## Pauling's Legacy

**He was partially right:**
- Route matters (IV vs. oral)
- Dose matters (pharmacological vs. nutritional)
- Mechanism is complex (not just antioxidant)
- Individual variation exists

**The tragedy:**
- Decades of research delay
- Patients denied access
- Mechanism not fully explored
- Stigma persists
