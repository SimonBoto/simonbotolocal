# OAT Category Interpretation Framework

Systematic approach to interpreting organic acids by metabolic category. Each category reveals specific aspects of cellular function, from energy production to detoxification capacity.

## Evidence
> "OAT interpretation is pattern recognition across metabolic categories—no single marker tells the full story." (Clinical metabolomics)

## The 7 OAT Categories

### 1. Glycolysis Markers
| Marker | Normal | Elevated Indicates |
|--------|--------|-------------------|
| **Lactate** | < 2.0 mmol/mol creat | Anaerobic shift, hypoxia, mitochondrial dysfunction |
| **Pyruvate** | < 2.5 mmol/mol creat | Glycolytic stress, B1 deficiency |
| **L/P Ratio** | < 20 | Mitochondrial dysfunction if elevated |

**Clinical focus:** Energy metabolism, oxygenation status

### 2. TCA Cycle (Krebs) Markers
| Marker | Normal | Elevated Indicates |
|--------|--------|-------------------|
| **Citrate** | 0.5-2.0 mmol/mol | Mitochondrial dysfunction, heavy metals |
| **Succinate** | < 2.0 mmol/mol | Complex II inhibition, hypoxia |
| **α-Ketoglutarate** | < 2.0 mmol/mol | B-vitamin cofactor need |

**Clinical focus:** Mitochondrial function, energy production

### 3. Fatty Acid Oxidation
| Marker | Normal | Elevated Indicates |
|--------|--------|-------------------|
| **Adipate** | < 2.0 mmol/mol | Carnitine deficiency, fasting stress |
| **Suberate** | < 2.0 mmol/mol | β-oxidation impairment |
| **Ethylmalonate** | < 2.0 mmol/mol | B2 (riboflavin) deficiency |

**Clinical focus:** Fat metabolism, carnitine status

### 4. B-Vitamin Dependent
| Marker | Normal | Elevated Indicates |
|--------|--------|-------------------|
| **Methylmalonate** | < 2.0 mmol/mol | B12 deficiency (specific) |
| **FIGLU** | < 2.0 mmol/mol | Folate deficiency (specific) |
| **α-KG** | < 2.0 mmol/mol | B-vitamin cofactors needed |

**Clinical focus:** Cofactor status, supplementation needs

### 5. Mitochondrial Markers
| Marker | Normal | Elevated Indicates |
|--------|--------|-------------------|
| **L/P ratio** | < 20 | Mitochondrial dysfunction |
| **Citrate** | 0.5-2.0 mmol/mol | Krebs cycle impairment |
| **Succinate** | < 2.0 mmol/mol | Complex II issues |

**Clinical focus:** Energy production capacity

### 6. Gut Microbiome Markers
| Marker | Normal | Elevated Indicates |
|--------|--------|-------------------|
| **Hippurate** | Variable | Polyphenol metabolism |
| **Indican** | < 2.0 mg/g | Protein putrefaction |
| **Phenylacetate** | < 2.0 mmol/mol | Dysbiosis |
| **HPHPA** | < 2.0 mmol/mol | Clostridia overgrowth |

**Clinical focus:** Microbiome health, dysbiosis

### 7. Oxidative Stress Markers
| Marker | Normal | Elevated Indicates |
|--------|--------|-------------------|
| **8-OHdG** | < 50 ng/mg creat | DNA oxidation, free radical burden |
| **Pyroglutamate** | < 50 mmol/mol | Glutathione depletion |

**Clinical focus:** Antioxidant status, inflammation

## Systematic Interpretation Protocol

### Step 1: Scan by Category
Review each category systematically—don't jump around

### Step 2: Identify Elevations
Mark all values outside reference range

### Step 3: Look for Patterns
| Pattern | Markers | Interpretation |
|---------|---------|----------------|
| **Energy crisis** | High lactate, pyruvate, L/P ratio | Mitochondrial dysfunction |
| **Cofactor deficiency** | High MMA, FIGLU, α-KG | B-vitamin needs |
| **Gut dysbiosis** | High indican, HPHPA, phenylacetate | Microbiome imbalance |
| **Oxidative stress** | High 8-OHdG, pyroglutamate | Antioxidant depletion |

### Step 4: Prioritize
1. **Life-threatening** — Severe lactic acidosis, extreme elevations
2. **Functional impairment** — Multiple category involvement
3. **Optimization** — Single marker elevations

### Step 5: Map to Intervention
Each pattern → specific treatment protocol
