## Core Claim
**Systematic OAT interpretation requires reviewing markers by functional category — glycolysis, TCA cycle, fatty acid oxidation, B-vitamin dependent enzymes, mitochondrial distress, gut metabolites, and oxidative stress — with each category telling a distinct metabolic story that must be integrated into a coherent clinical picture.**

## Evidence

### OAT Category Framework
| Category | Key Markers | Clinical Focus | Priority |
|----------|-------------|----------------|----------|
| **1. Glycolysis** | Lactate, pyruvate | Anaerobic metabolism | High |
| **2. TCA cycle** | Citrate, succinate, α-KG | Mitochondrial function | High |
| **3. Fatty acid oxidation** | Adipate, suberate, ethylmalonate | Beta-oxidation | High |
| **4. B-vitamin markers** | Methylmalonate, FIGLU | Cofactor status | High |
| **5. Mitochondrial** | Lactate/pyruvate ratio | Redox status | Critical |
| **6. Gut-derived** | Indican, hippurate, HPHPA | Microbiome | Moderate |
| **7. Oxidative stress** | 8-OHdG, lipid peroxides | Free radical damage | Moderate |

### Systematic Review Protocol
| Step | Action | Time |
|------|--------|------|
| 1 | Scan all values against reference ranges | 2 min |
| 2 | Identify elevated markers by category | 3 min |
| 3 | Look for patterns within categories | 3 min |
| 4 | Cross-reference between categories | 2 min |
| 5 | Prioritize life-threatening findings | 1 min |
| 6 | Formulate clinical hypothesis | 2 min |

### Critical Markers (Immediate Attention)
| Marker | Critical Value | Action |
|--------|---------------|--------|
| **Lactate** | >5 mmol/L | Rule out sepsis, ischemia |
| **Pyruvate** | >2 mmol/L | Mitochondrial emergency |
| **Lactate/pyruvate** | >20:1 | Severe mitochondrial dysfunction |

### Category Integration
| Pattern Combination | Interpretation |
|--------------------|----------------|
| **Glycolysis ↑ + TCA ↑** | Mitochondrial dysfunction |
| **B-vitamin ↑ + Mitochondrial ↑** | Cofactor deficiency |
| **Gut ↑ + Inflammation** | Dysbiosis driving systemic effects |
| **Oxidative stress ↑ + TCA ↑** | ROS-mediated damage |
