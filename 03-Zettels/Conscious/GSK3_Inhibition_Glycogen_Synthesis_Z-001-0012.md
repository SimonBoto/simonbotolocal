# GSK3 Inhibition and Glycogen Synthesis

 promotes hepatic glycogen synthesis by inhibiting GSK3, relieving its suppression of glycogen synthase.

**Default state (no insulin):**
- GSK3β active (constitutive kinase)
- GSK3 phosphorylates glycogen synthase (inactivates it)
- Glycogen synthesis blocked

**Insulin action:**
- Akt phosphorylates GSK3β at Ser9
- Ser9-P creates pseudosubstrate inhibition
- GSK3 activity suppressed
- Glycogen synthase dephosphorylated (activated)
- Glycogen synthesis proceeds

**Regulatory context:**
- Glucagon (via PKA) phosphorylates/activates GSK3 → inhibits glycogen synthesis
- Insulin and glucagon are opposing regulators
- GSK3 integrates these signals
