# DAO Enzyme Framework

> **Core Claim:** DAO (diamine oxidase/histaminase) primarily degrades histamine and plays a critical role in intestinal barrier function.

## Alternative Names

- Histaminase
- Amiloride-binding protein

## Primary Function

**Histamine degradation** — the main enzyme for metabolizing dietary and endogenous histamine.

## Tissue Distribution

| Tissue | Activity Level | Function |
|--------|---------------|----------|
| **Intestinal mucosa** | Highest | First-line histamine defense |
| **Kidney** | Moderate | Histamine clearance |
| **Placenta** | Moderate | Maternal-fetal barrier |

## Clinical Relevance

### DAO Deficiency

- Genetic or acquired impairment
- Impaired histamine degradation
- Leads to **histamine intolerance**

### Symptoms of DAO Deficiency

- Gastrointestinal symptoms
- Headaches
- Flushing
- Skin reactions

## Distinction from MAO

> DAO and MAO are **separate enzymes** with different substrates and functions.
