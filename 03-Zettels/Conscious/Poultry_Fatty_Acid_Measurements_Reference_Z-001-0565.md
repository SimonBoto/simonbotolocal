# Poultry Fatty Acid Measurements Reference

## Purpose

Quick reference table for  and omega-3 fatty acid content in chicken meat and egg yolks across different production systems. Data synthesized from peer-reviewed studies.

---

## Chicken Meat Measurements

### Conventional (Corn/Grain-Fed) Broilers
| Parameter | Value | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| **Total omega-6** | 35-40% of total  | Primarily linoleic acid |
| **Omega-6:3 ratio** | 15-20:1 | Highly pro-inflammatory |
| **Primary feed** | Corn, soybean meal | High linoleic acid content |
| **Tissue distribution** | Higher in skin and adipose | White meat lower than dark |

### Pasture-Raised Broilers
| Parameter | Value | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| **Total omega-6** | <35% (variable) | Depends on supplemental feed |
| **Omega-6:3 ratio** | 5-8:1 | Improved over conventional |
| **Primary feed** | Pasture + supplemental grain | Insects, plants diversify profile |
| **Variability** | High | Depends on pasture quality |

### Special Omega-3 Fed
| Parameter | Value | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| **Total omega-6** | 20-30% | Reduced with omega-3 feeding |
| **Omega-6:3 ratio** | 3-5:1 | Optimized fatty acid profile |
| **Feed intervention** | Flaxseed, fish oil | Direct omega-3 incorporation |

## Egg Yolk Measurements

### Conventional (Corn/Grain-Fed Hens)
| Parameter | Value | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| **Linoleic acid** | 18-20% of total FA | Predominant omega-6 fatty acid |
| **Total omega-6** | 25-30% of total FA | All omega-6 combined |
| **Omega-6:3 ratio** | 10-12:1 | Less favorable profile |
| **Yolk fat content** | ~5g per large egg | Significant omega-6 load |

### Omega-3 Enriched Eggs
| Parameter | Value | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| **Linoleic acid** | 15-18% of total FA | Reduced with omega-3 feeding |
| **Total omega-6** | 20-25% of total FA | Lower than conventional |
| **Omega-6:3 ratio** | 3-4:1 | Optimal anti-inflammatory ratio |
| **Enrichment source** | 2-3% flaxseed oil or fish oil | In hen diet |

### Pasture-Raised Eggs
| Parameter | Value | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| **Linoleic acid** | Variable | Depends on pasture composition |
| **Total omega-6** | Similar to conventional | But omega-3 often higher |
| **Omega-6:3 ratio** | ~5:1 | Intermediate improvement |
| **Variability** | High | Seasonal, geographic differences |

## Clinical Application Notes

### Calculating Patient Intake
**Example: Patient eating conventional chicken daily**
- 150g chicken breast: ~6g omega-6, 0.3g omega-3
- 2 conventional eggs: ~2.5g omega-6, 0.25g omega-3
- **Daily poultry omega-6:** ~8.5g
- **Daily poultry omega-3:** ~0.55g
- **Ratio from poultry alone:** ~15:1

### Balancing Strategy
To achieve 4:1 ratio with this poultry intake:
- Need additional ~1.8g omega-3
- Options:
  - 100g salmon (2-3g omega-3)
  - 3 omega-3 enriched eggs (1.5g omega-3)
  - Fish oil supplement (2g EPA+DHA)

## Usage Notes

**Caveats:**
- Values are approximate ranges from multiple studies
- Exact composition varies by feed formulation, season, genetics
- Measurements typically from yolk lipids or muscle/adipose tissue
- Laboratory methods (GC-MS, etc.) introduce minor variability

- This table should be updated as new research emerges
