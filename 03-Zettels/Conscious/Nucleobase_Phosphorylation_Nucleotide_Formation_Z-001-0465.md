## Core Claim
**Nucleotides are formed by progressive phosphorylation of nucleosides (base + ribose), with ATP as the phosphate donor — this energy investment creates the high-energy phosphoanhydride bonds that power cellular work and enable nucleic acid synthesis** — nucleoside triphosphates (NTPs) are required for DNA/RNA polymerization.

## Evidence

### Phosphorylation Cascade
```
Nucleobase → Nucleoside → NMP → NDP → NTP
```

### Steps
| Step | Reaction | Enzyme | Energy Cost |
|------|----------|--------|-------------|
| **Base → Nucleoside** | + Ribose | Nucleoside phosphorylase | — |
| **Nucleoside → NMP** | + Pi | Nucleoside kinase | 1 ATP |
| **NMP → NDP** | + Pi | NMP kinase | 1 ATP |
| **NDP → NTP** | + Pi | NDP kinase | 1 ATP |

### ATP Structure
```
Adenine — Ribose — Phosphate — Phosphate — Phosphate
                ↑
         (high-energy bonds)
```

### Signaling Molecules
| Molecule | Formation | Function |
|----------|-----------|----------|
| **cAMP** | ATP → cAMP + PPi | Second messenger (glucagon, epinephrine) |
| **cGMP** | GTP → cGMP + PPi | Nitric oxide signaling |

## Clinical Implication
- **Nucleotide synthesis:** Required for rapidly dividing cells (cancer, immune response)
- **Purine salvage disorders:** Lesch-Nyhan syndrome, SCID
- **Mitochondrial dysfunction:** Impairs NDP→NTP conversion
- **METHAP relevance:** High metabolic turnover requires robust nucleotide synthesis; ribose from PPP supports nucleotide production
