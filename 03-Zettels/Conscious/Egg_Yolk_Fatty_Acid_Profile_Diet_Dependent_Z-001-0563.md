## Core Concept

The fatty acid composition of egg yolks is **directly determined by the hen's diet**. Hens fed corn- and grain-based diets produce eggs with :3 ratios of **10-12:1**, while omega-3 enriched diets can improve this to **3-4:1**, and pasture-raised hens produce eggs with ratios around **5:1**.

---

## Egg Yolk Fatty Acid Composition by Hen Diet

### Conventional Eggs (Corn/Grain Fed)
| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Linoleic acid** | ~18-20% of total  |
| **Total omega-6** | ~25-30% of total fatty acid pool |
| **Omega-6:3 ratio** | ~10:1 to 12:1 |
| **Assessment** | Less favorable, pro-inflammatory tendency |

### Omega-3 Enriched Eggs
| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Dietary intervention** | 2-3% flaxseed oil or fish oil in feed |
| **Linoleic acid** | Reduced to ~15-18% |
| **Omega-6:3 ratio** | ~3:1 to 4:1 |
| **Assessment** | Optimal, anti-inflammatory balance |

### Pasture-Raised Eggs
| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Diet** | Diverse: insects, plants, green forage, some grain |
| **Omega-6:3 ratio** | ~5:1 |
| **Assessment** | Intermediate, improved over conventional |

---

## The Metabolic Mechanism

### 1. Dietary Fatty Acid Deposition
- Hens **directly deposit** dietary fatty acids into yolk
- Minimal modification of fatty acid profile
- Yolk composition **mirrors dietary intake**

### 2. Omega-6 Pathway
**Corn/grain diet → High linoleic acid → Egg yolk:**
1. Linoleic acid (omega-6) deposited in yolk
2. Consumer ingests high omega-6 egg
3. Contributes to systemic omega-6:3 imbalance
4. Potential pro-inflammatory effect

### 3. Omega-3 Enrichment Pathway
**Flaxseed/fish oil in feed → Omega-3 in yolk → Consumer:**
1. Alpha-linolenic acid (ALA) or EPA/DHA in feed
2. Deposited in egg yolk
3. Consumer receives beneficial omega-3s
4. Improved omega-6:3 ratio

## Clinical Applications

### For Inflammatory Conditions
- **Autoimmune diseases:** Omega-3 enriched eggs preferred
- **Metabolic syndrome:** Minimize conventional eggs or upgrade
- **Cardiovascular disease:** Target omega-6:3 ratio <5:1

### Practical Counseling
1. **Upgrade priority:** Eggs are consumed frequently — quality matters
2. **Omega-3 enriched:** Most cost-effective intervention for ratio improvement
3. **Pasture-raised:** Good middle ground if omega-3 enriched unavailable
4. **Conventional acceptable if:** Overall diet has excellent omega-6:3 balance

### Serving Size Context
- One large egg yolk: ~5g fat
- Conventional egg: ~1.25g omega-6 fatty acids
- Omega-3 enriched: ~0.6g omega-6, plus omega-3s
- Daily impact: 2-3 eggs can significantly affect omega-6:3 ratio

---

## Label Interpretation

### What "Omega-3 Enriched" Means
- **Requirement:** Minimum omega-3 content (varies by country)
- **Source:** Flaxseed, fish oil, or algae in hen feed
- **Verification:** Check omega-3 content on label (mg per egg)
- **Quality varies:** Some brands more enriched than others

### What "Pasture-Raised" Means
- **Housing:** Outdoor access, natural foraging
- **Diet:** Insects, plants, plus supplemental feed
- **Omega-3 content:** Variable, depends on pasture quality
- **Not standardized:** Less consistent than "omega-3 enriched"
