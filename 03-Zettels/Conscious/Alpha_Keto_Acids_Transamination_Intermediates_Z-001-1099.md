## Core Claim
**Alpha-keto acids are the deaminated carbon skeletons of amino acids, serving as the critical link between protein catabolism and central energy pathways via transamination** — this B6-dependent process enables nitrogen transfer while directing carbon skeletons to gluconeogenesis, ketogenesis, or the Krebs cycle.

## Evidence

### Transamination Reaction
```
Amino Acid + Alpha-ketoglutarate ↔ Alpha-keto acid + Glutamate
```
- **Enzyme:** Aminotransferase (transaminase)
- **Cofactor:** Pyridoxal phosphate (vitamin B6)
- **Reversible:** Direction depends on substrate concentrations

### Key Alpha-Keto Acids and Fates
| Amino Acid | Alpha-Keto Acid | Metabolic Fate | Pathway |
|------------|-----------------|----------------|---------|
| **Alanine** | Pyruvate | Glucose or Krebs | Glucogenic |
| **Aspartate** | Oxaloacetate | Krebs, glucose | Glucogenic |
| **Glutamate** | Alpha-ketoglutarate | Krebs cycle | Glucogenic |
| **Leucine** | Alpha-ketoisocaproate | Ketone bodies | Ketogenic |
| **Isoleucine** | Alpha-keto-beta-methylvalerate | Succinyl-CoA | Glucogenic |
| **Valine** | Alpha-ketoisovalerate | Succinyl-CoA | Glucogenic |
| **Phenylalanine** | Phenylpyruvate | Fumarate + acetoacetate | Both |

## Clinical Implication
- **B6 deficiency impairs all transamination** — limits amino acid utilization
- **Maple syrup urine disease:** Defect in branched-chain alpha-keto acid dehydrogenase — toxic accumulation
- **Liver function critical:** Most transamination occurs in liver
- **METHAP relevance:** B6 supports transamination; high protein intake increases B6 demand
