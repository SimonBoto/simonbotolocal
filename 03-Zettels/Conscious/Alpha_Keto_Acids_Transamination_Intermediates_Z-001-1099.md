# Alpha Keto Acids Transamination Intermediates

## Overview
Alpha-keto acids are the deaminated carbon skeletons of amino acids—key intermediates linking protein  to central energy pathways.

## Structure and Formation

### The Alpha Position
```
Amino Acid:     R-CH(NH₂)-COOH
                     |
                 Alpha carbon

Alpha-Keto Acid: R-C(=O)-COOH
                     |
                 Keto group (instead of amino)
```

### Formation via Transamination
```
Amino Acid + Alpha-ketoglutarate ↔ Alpha-keto acid + Glutamate
```

**Enzyme:** Aminotransferase (transaminase)
**Cofactor:** Pyridoxal phosphate (B6)

## Key Alpha-Keto Acids

| Amino Acid | Alpha-Keto Acid | Metabolic Fate |
|------------|-----------------|----------------|
| **Alanine** | Pyruvate | Gluconeogenesis, Krebs |
| **Aspartate** | Oxaloacetate | Krebs, gluconeogenesis |
| **Glutamate** | Alpha-ketoglutarate | Krebs cycle |
| **Leucine** | Alpha-ketoisocaproate | Ketogenesis |
| **Isoleucine** | Alpha-keto-beta-methylvalerate | Krebs (succinyl-CoA) |
| **Valine** | Alpha-ketoisovalerate | Krebs (succinyl-CoA) |
| **Phenylalanine** | Phenylpyruvate | Fumarate + acetoacetate |
| **Tyrosine** | p-Hydroxyphenylpyruvate | Fumarate + acetoacetate |

## The Major Alpha-Keto Acids in Krebs

### Pyruvate (from Alanine)
- **Entry:** Pyruvate dehydrogenase → Acetyl-CoA
- **Alternative:** Pyruvate carboxylase → Oxaloacetate (gluconeogenesis)
- **Clinical:** Elevated in B1 deficiency

### Oxaloacetate (from Aspartate)
- **Entry:** Direct Krebs cycle intermediate
- **Function:** Anaplerotic (refills Krebs)
- **Clinical:** Critical for gluconeogenesis

### Alpha-Ketoglutarate (from Glutamate)
- **Entry:** Direct Krebs cycle intermediate
- **Function:** Central hub of amino acid metabolism
- **Clinical:** Elevated in B-eficiencies

## Transamination: The Key Reaction

### Mechanism
1. **Amino acid** binds to enzyme-B6 complex
2. **Amino group** transfers to B6 cofactor
3. **Alpha-keto acid** released
4. **Glutamate** accepts amino group
5. **Alpha-ketoglutarate** regenerated

### Net Result
- Amino group moved to glutamate
- Carbon skeleton (alpha-keto acid) enters energy metabolism
- **Reversible:** Can make amino acids from keto acids

## Clinical Relevance

### Elevated Alpha-Keto Acids (OAT Markers)
| Marker | Indicates |
|--------|-----------|
| **Pyruvate ↑** | B1 deficiency, l dysfunction |
| **Alpha-ketoglutarate ↑** | B-vitamin cofactor insufficiency |
| **Phenylpyruvate ↑** | Phenylketonuria (PKU) |
| **Branched-chain keto acids ↑** | Maple syrup urine disease |

### Therapeutic Potential
- **Alpha-keto analogs:** Used in kidney disease to reduce nitrogen load
- **Leucine keto acid:** Preserves muscle protein
- **Ornithine alpha-ketoglutarate:** Wound healing, immune support
