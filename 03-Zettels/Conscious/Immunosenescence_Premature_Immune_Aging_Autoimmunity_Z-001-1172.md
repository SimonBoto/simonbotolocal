# Immunosenescence — Premature Immune Aging in Autoimmunity

## Definition

**Immunosenescence:** Age-related decline in immune function

**Key Features:**
- Reduced response to novel antigens
- Impaired vaccine efficacy
- Increased infection susceptibility
- Chronic low-grade  (inflammaging)

---

## Normal vs. Premature Immunosenescence

| Parameter | Normal Aging | Autoimmune Disease |
|-----------|--------------|-------------------|
| **Onset** | ~60 years | 20-40 years (disease onset) |
| **Rate** | Gradual | Accelerated |
| **Immune age** | = Chronological age | +10-20 years |
| **Reversibility** | None | Partial (with treatment) |

---

## Mechanisms of Premature Immunosenescence

### 1. Chronic Activation
- Persistent autoreactive T-cell stimulation
- Compensatory proliferation to maintain cell numbers
- Each division = telomere cost

### 2. Oxidative Stress
- Inflammation generates ROS
- Damages telomeres (G-rich, vulnerable)
- Accelerates shortening beyond replication loss

### 3. Inflammaging
- Chronic low-grade inflammation
- Elevated IL-6, TNF-α, CRP
- Self-sustaining cycle

### 4. l Dysfunction
- Reduced ATP production
- Increased ROS generation
- Further telomere damage

---

## Clinical Consequences

### Infection Risk
- **Normal:** Increased after age 65
- **Autoimmune:** Increased at younger ages
- **Mechanism:** Reduced naive T cells, impaired responses

### Vaccine Responses
- Reduced antibody titers
- Shorter protection duration
- Higher vaccine failure rates

###  Risk
- Immune surveillance impairment
- Increased lymphoma (SLE)
- Skin cancer (immunosuppression)

### Cardiovascular Disease
- Inflammation accelerates atherosclerosis
- SLE patients: CVD risk equivalent to +20 years age

---
