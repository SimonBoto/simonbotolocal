# Vitamin C Pro Oxidant  Selective Toxicity

## Overview
Pharmacological doses of vitamin C (ascorbate) act as a pro-oxidant, generating hydrogen peroxide (H₂O₂) that is selectively toxic to cancer cells due to their reduced catalase activity and antioxidant defenses.

## The 

### Step 1: High Plasma Ascorbate
| Route | Dose | Plasma Level |
|-------|------|--------------|
| **Oral** | 200 mg | ~80 μM (saturated) |
| **IV** | 10g | ~1,000 μM |
| **IV** | 50-100g | >5,000-10,000 μM |

### Step 2: Extracellular Conversion
```
Ascorbate + O₂ → Dehydroascorbate + H₂O₂
            (catalyzed by transition metals)
```

### Step 3: H₂O₂ Generation
- **Source:** Ascorbate oxidation
- **Location:** Extracellular fluid
- **Stability:** H₂O₂ crosses cell membranes easily

### Step 4: Selective Toxicity
| Cell Type | Catalase Activity | H₂O₂ Handling | Outcome |
|-----------|-------------------|---------------|---------|
| **Normal cells** | High | Detoxify H₂O₂ | Survive |
| **Cancer cells** | Low/impaired | Accumulate damage | Death |

## Why Cancer Cells Are Vulnerable

### Catalase Deficiency
| Factor | Cancer Cell Status |
|--------|-------------------|
| **Catalase expression** | Often reduced |
| **Glutathione peroxidase** | May be impaired |
| **Overall antioxidant capacity** | Lower than normal |

### Ferroptosis Connection
| Mechanism | Role |
|-----------|------|
| **Iron availability** | Cancer cells often iron-rich |
| **Lipid peroxidation** | H₂O₂ + iron → OH radicals |
| **Cell death** | Ferroptotic pathway |

## Evidence for Selectivity

### In Vitro Studies
| Finding | Significance |
|---------|--------------|
| **Cancer cells killed at 1-5 mM ascorbate** | Achievable with IV |
| **Normal cells survive** | Catalase protection |
| **Catalase inhibition sensitizes** | Confirms mechanism |
| **Iron chelation blocks effect** | Fenton chemistry involved |

### Animal Studies
| Model | Result |
|-------|--------|
| **Ovarian cancer xenografts** | Tumor growth inhibition |
| **Pancreatic cancer** | Enhanced gemcitabine effect |
| **Glioblastoma** | Prolonged survival |

## Synergy with Standard Therapy

| Combination | Mechanism |
|-------------|-----------|
| **Radiation** | H₂O₂ enhances oxidative damage |
| **Chemotherapy** | Multiple stress pathways |
| **Hyperbaric oxygen** | Increased ROS generation |
| **Ketogenic diet** | Metabolic stress + oxidative stress |

## Clinical Administration

### IV Protocol
| Parameter | Specification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Dose** | 1.5 g/kg or 50-100g |
| **Duration** | 2-3 hours |
| **Frequency** | 2-3x weekly |
| **Setting** | Outpatient infusion center |

### Monitoring
| Parameter | Purpose |
|-----------|---------|
| **Plasma ascorbate** | Confirm pharmacological levels |
| **Osmolarity** | Prevent osmotic issues |
| **Renal function** | Excess excreted in urine |
| **Iron status** | High iron may enhance effect |

## Safety Considerations

### Contraindications
| Condition | Risk |
|-----------|------|
| **G6PD deficiency** | Hemolysis risk |
| **Iron overload** | Enhanced pro-oxidant effect |
| **Renal failure** | Excretion impaired |
| **Oxalate kidney stones** | Metabolite concern |

### Side Effects
| Effect | Frequency | Management |
|--------|-----------|------------|
| **Nausea** | Common | Slow infusion |
| **Headache** | Occasional | Hydration |
| **Fatigue** | Rare | Rest |
