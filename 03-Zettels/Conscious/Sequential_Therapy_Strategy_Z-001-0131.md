# Sequential Therapy Strategy

## Goal Alignment Framework

| Treatment Goal | Preferred Therapy | Rationale |
|----------------|-------------------|-----------|
| **Rapid fracture reduction** | Anabolic (ROMO, TPTD) | Highest vertebral fracture reduction (70%+) |
| **Preserve bone mass** | Antiresorptive (BISPHOS, DENOS) | Slows loss, preserves existing mass |
| **Stabilize severe disease** | Anabolic first, then antiresorptive | Builds then preserves |
| **Maintenance therapy** | Antiresorptive | Long-term safety established |
| **Bridge after anabolic** | Antiresorptive | Prevents rapid bone loss |

## Clinical Strategy

- **Anabolic-first approach:** Initiate with anabolic therapy for 12–24 months in high-risk patients, then transition to antiresorptive therapy
- **Bridge therapy:** Essential after anabolic treatment to prevent rapid bone loss that occurs when anabolic effect wanes
- **Maintenance therapy:** Long-term antiresorptive therapy for sustained bone preservation
- **Stabilization:** Combining anabolic and antiresorptive therapies addresses both bone formation and resorption deficits

## Evidence Summary

- Sequential therapy (anabolic → antiresorptive) demonstrates superior long-term outcomes vs monotherapy
- Bridge therapy prevents the 5–10% bone loss that occurs in the first 6 months after stopping anabolic therapy
- Long-term maintenance with antiresorptives preserves 80–90% of gains achieved during anabolic therapy
- Sequential approaches are recommended in current NOF and ESCEO guidelines
