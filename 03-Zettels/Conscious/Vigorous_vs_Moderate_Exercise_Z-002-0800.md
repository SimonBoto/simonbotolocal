# Vigorous Activity More Efficient Than Moderate for Time-Limited Individuals

## Core Claim
**Vigorous physical activity is more time-efficient than moderate activity for mortality reduction** — small amounts of high-intensity exercise provide substantial benefits, making it practical for time-limited individuals to achieve health protection.

## Time Efficiency Comparison

### Vigorous Activity (Study Focus)
- **15-20 min/week** = significant protection
- High intensity = greater metabolic stimulus per minute
- Shorter time commitment

### Equivalent Moderate Activity
- Would require **2-4x more time**
- 30-80 min/week for similar benefits
- Less time-efficient

## Practical Implementation

### Example Weekly Routine
| Activity | Duration | Frequency |
|----------|----------|-----------|
| Walking (moderate) | 1 hour | 4x/week |
| Vigorous exercise | 15-30 min | 2x/week |

**Vigorous options:** weights, sprints, bodyweight exercises, HIIT

## Clinical Implications
- Time efficiency makes exercise adherence realistic
- High-intensity training (HIT) viable for busy populations
- Quality over quantity for time-limited individuals
- Can achieve optimal benefits with minimal time investment
