# Oxalate Mineral Binding and Bioavailability

## 

**Oxalic acid binds divalent cations:**
```
Oxalate²⁻ + Ca²⁺ → Calcium oxalate (insoluble)
Oxalate²⁻ + Fe²⁺ → Iron oxalate (poorly absorbed)
```

## Calcium Binding

**Formation:** Calcium oxalate (CaC₂O₄)
- **Solubility product (Ksp):** 2.32 × 10⁻⁹ (very insoluble)
- **Result:** Precipitates in gut, cannot be absorbed
- **Impact:** Reduces calcium bioavailability from food

** significance:**
- High-oxalate meals reduce calcium absorption
- Calcium supplements with meals can bind oxalate
- Ratio of calcium:oxalate important for stone risk

## Iron Binding

**Formation:** Iron oxalate complexes
- **Ferrous iron (Fe²⁺):** More soluble, still reduced absorption
- **Ferric iron (Fe³⁺):** Forms insoluble complexes
- **Impact:** Reduces non-heme iron absorption

**Compensation:**
- Vitamin C enhances iron absorption (counteracts oxalate effect)
- Heme iron (meat) absorption unaffected

##  and Zinc

Also bind oxalate, though less clinically significant than calcium.

## Dietary Strategy

**Reduce oxalate impact:**
1. Consume calcium-rich foods WITH high-oxalate meals (binds oxalate in gut)
2. Avoid tea/coffee with iron-rich meals
3. Vitamin C with plant iron sources
4. Cooking reduces oxalate in some vegetables
