# Platelet COX-1 Inhibition Leads to T-Cell Activation

## Molecular Pathway

```
Aspirin
   ↓
Acetylates COX-1 (Ser-529)
   ↓
Blocks arachidonic acid conversion
   ↓
↓ Thromboxane A2 (TXA2) synthesis
   ↓
↓ TP receptor signaling on T-cells
   ↓
↑ T-cell activation & proliferation
   ↓
Enhanced anti-tumor immunity
```

## Key Points

### Irreversible Inhibition
- Aspirin acetylates COX-1 permanently
- Platelets cannot synthesize new COX-1 (no nucleus)
- Effect lasts platelet lifespan (~7-10 days)

### T-Cell Specific Effects
- TXA2 suppresses T-cell receptor signaling
- Reduced TXA2 → enhanced T-cell cytotoxicity
- Particularly affects CD8+ cytotoxic T-cells

## HLA Class I Dependency
- Effect strongest in tumors with high HLA-I expression
- HLA-I presents tumor antigens to T-cells
- Suggests immune-mediated mechanism
