# PAI1_Senescence_Feedback_Loop

## Source
[[L-001-0654_Vaughan2017_PAI1_Aging_L-001-0654]]

## Core Claim
PAI-1 is produced by senescent cells and creates a vicious feedback loop where PAI-1 promotes further cellular senescence, contributing to systemic aging across multiple organ systems.

## Key Details
- Produced by senescent cells ("zombie cells")
- Senescent cells → PAI-1 → more senescent cells (vicious cycle)
- Cellular aging, stem cell depletion, metabolic disruption
- CVD aging, cognitive decline, cancer, sarcopenia

## Evidence
- [[Eren_2014_PNAS_R-001-0007]] — Foundational evidence for feedback loop
- [[Vaughan_2017_Arterioscler_Thromb_Vasc_Biol_R-001-0006]] — Vascular aging manifestation
- [[Jiang_2025_Ageing_Res_Rev_R-001-0008]] — Systemic aging manifestations

## Significance
The PAI-1 senescence feedback loop represents a key amplification mechanism in aging biology, making PAI-1 inhibition a potential strategy to break this cycle and slow aging progression.

## Clinical Significance
- Targeting the PAI-1 senescence feedback loop may delay multiple age-related pathologies
- Senolytic therapies combined with PAI-1 inhibition could have synergistic anti-aging effects
- Biomarker development for PAI-1 in senescence monitoring
