## Core Claim
**The vitamin D-PTH-calcium axis forms a tightly regulated hormonal system — where vitamin D (as calcitriol) enhances intestinal calcium absorption, PTH mobilizes bone calcium, and calcitonin provides counter-regulation — with dysfunction producing osteoporosis, secondary hyperparathyroidism, and extraskeletal calcification.**

## Evidence

### Hormonal Regulation
| Hormone | Source | Primary Action | Trigger |
|---------|--------|----------------|---------|
| **Vitamin D (calcitriol)** | Kidney (activated) | ↑ Ca²⁺ absorption, bone resorption | Low Ca²⁺, low PTH |
| **PTH** | Parathyroid glands | ↑ Ca²⁺ (bone, kidney), ↑ calcitriol synthesis | Low serum Ca²⁺ |
| **Calcitonin** | Thyroid C-cells | ↓ Ca²⁺ (bone protection) | High serum Ca²⁺ |

### Vitamin D Metabolism
| Step | Location | Enzyme | Product |
|------|----------|--------|---------|
| Skin synthesis | Skin | UVB exposure | Cholecalciferol (D3) |
| 25-hydroxylation | Liver | CYP2R1, CYP27A1 | 25(OH)D (storage form) |
| 1α-hydroxylation | Kidney | CYP27B1 | 1,25(OH)₂D (active form) |
| Catabolism | Multiple | CYP24A1 | 24,25(OH)₂D (inactive) |

### Calcium Homeostasis Targets
| Parameter | Target Range | Regulation |
|-----------|--------------|------------|
| **Serum calcium** | 8.5-10.5 mg/dL | Tightly controlled |
| **Ionized calcium** | 4.5-5.3 mg/dL | Physiologically active |
| **25(OH)D** | 30-50 ng/mL | Storage status |
| **1,25(OH)₂D** | 20-50 pg/mL | Active hormone |
| **PTH** | 10-65 pg/mL | Secondary indicator |

### Clinical States
| Condition | Mechanism | Presentation |
|-----------|-----------|--------------|
| **Vitamin D deficiency** | Inadequate intake/sun | Secondary hyperparathyroidism, bone loss |
| **Primary hyperparathyroidism** | PTH adenoma | Hypercalcemia, kidney stones |
| **Secondary hyperparathyroidism** | Low Ca²⁺/low D | Elevated PTH, normal/low Ca²⁺ |
| **Hypoparathyroidism** | PTH deficiency | Hypocalcemia, tetany |
| **Pseudohypoparathyroidism** | PTH resistance | Elevated PTH, hypocalcemia |

### Vitamin D Extraskeletal Effects
| System | Effect | Evidence |
|--------|--------|----------|
| **Immune** | Modulates innate/adaptive immunity | Strong |
| **Cardiovascular** | Vascular tone, cardiac function | Moderate |
| **Metabolic** | Insulin sensitivity, beta-cell function | Moderate |
| **Neurological** | Neuroprotection, mood | Emerging |

## Clinical Implication
- **Bone health:** Adequate D prevents osteoporosis and fractures
- **Cardiovascular:** D deficiency associated with CVD risk
- **Immune function:** D status affects infection susceptibility
- **Metabolic:** D supplementation may improve insulin sensitivity
- **METHAP relevance:** Vitamin D metabolism intersects with metabolic syndrome; VDR polymorphisms affect metabolic outcomes
