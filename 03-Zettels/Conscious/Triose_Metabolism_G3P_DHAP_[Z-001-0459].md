# Triose : G3P and DHAP

## The Simplest Sugars
**Trioses:** 3-carbon monosaccharides

| Triose | Type | Formula | Key Form |
|--------|------|---------|----------|
| **Glyceraldehyde** | Aldotriose | C₃H₆O₃ | G3P |
| **Dihydroxyacetone** | Ketotriose | C₃H₆O₃ | DHAP |

##  Connection
```
F1,6BP → DHAP + G3P (aldolase)
DHAP ↔ G3P (triose phosphate isomerase)
```
**Each glucose → 2 G3P → 2 pyruvate**

## Why Trioses?
**Evolutionary advantage:**
- Glycolysis ~3.5 billion years old
- Splitting 6C → 2×3C = more ATP/glucose
- Energy extraction efficiency
- Universal across all life domains

## Metabolic Integration
G3P connects:
- **Glycolysis** → pyruvate → energy
- **PPP** (non-oxidative) → ribose synthesis
- **Gluconeogenesis** ← glucose synthesis
- **Calvin cycle** (photosynthesis) ← carbon fixation

## Triose Phosphate Isomerase
- **Fastest enzyme:** kcat ~10⁴ s⁻¹
- Near-equilibrium: DHAP/G3P ratio ~20:1
- Essential for glycolytic flux

## 
**G3P as branch point:** Metabolic flexibility allows cells to switch between energy production and biosynthesis based on needs.
