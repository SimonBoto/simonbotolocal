# Respiratory Chain Complex Deficiencies — Clinical Phenotypes and Mechanisms

> **Deficiencies in respiratory chain complexes (I-IV) produce tissue-specific energy failure, with neurons and muscle most severely affected due to high ATP demand.**

---

## Complex I Deficiency (NDUFS4 Knockout)

### Gene
**NDUFS4** — NADH dehydrogenase [ubiquinone] iron-sulfur protein 4

### Phenotype
| Feature | Manifestation |
|---------|---------------|
| **Syndrome** | Leigh syndrome model |
| **Neurological** | Severe neurodegeneration, ataxia |
| **Survival** | Premature death (~7 weeks) |

### Mechanism
```
Complex I deficiency
    ↓
Reduced electron transport
    ↓
ATP depletion + ROS production
    ↓
Neuronal death (high energy demand)
```

---

## Complex IV Deficiency (COX10 Knockout)

### Gene
**COX10** — Cytochrome c oxidase assembly protein

### Tissue-Specific Phenotype
| Tissue | Manifestation |
|--------|---------------|
| **Brain** | Progressive neurodegeneration |
| **Muscle** | Muscle degeneration |
| **Systemic** | Severe OXPHOS deficiency, increased ROS |

### Clinical Relevance
- Models human cytochrome oxidase deficiency
- Prenatal lethality in severe cases
- Tissue-specific therapeutic targeting possible

---

## Key Insight

**Why neurons and muscle?**
- Highest ATP demand per cell volume
- Dependent on aerobic metabolism
- Limited glycolytic capacity (especially neurons)
- Mitochondrial density correlates with vulnerability
