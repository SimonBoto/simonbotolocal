# Isoprenoids and Terpenes Overview

## Core Concept

**Isoprenoids (terpenoids)** are a vast family of molecules built from **5-carbon isoprene units**. They are synthesized from isopentenyl pyrophosphate (IPP) and serve functions ranging from pigments and hormones to cell signaling and defense compounds. Humans primarily synthesize sterols and steroid hormones, while plants produce diverse terpenes for communication and defense.

## Functions by Class

### Monoterpenes (C₁₀)
**Plant Functions:**
- **Aromas:** Citrus (limonene), pine (pinene), mint (menthol)
- **Defense:** Repel herbivores, attract pollinators
- **Medicine:** Essential oils, aromatherapy

**Human Relevance:**
- Limonene: Potential anti-[[KEYWORD-Cancer|cancer]] properties
- Menthol: Cooling sensation, analgesic
- Pinene: Anti-inflammatory, bronchodilator

### Sesquiterpenes (C₁₅)
**Key Compounds:**
- **Farnesene:** Apple skin aroma, insect pheromone
- **Bisabolol:** Chamomile, skin healing
- **Caryophyllene:** Black pepper, CB2 receptor agonist
- **Artemisinin:** Antimalarial drug (from Artemisia annua)

### Diterpenes (C₂₀)
**[[KEYWORD-Clinical|clinical]]ly Important:**
- **Taxol (Paclitaxel):** Cancer chemotherapy
  - Stabilizes microtubules
  - From Pacific yew tree bark
- **Phylloquinone (Vitamin K1):** Blood clotting
- **Gibberellins:** Plant growth hormones

### Triterpenes (C₃₀)
**Human-Synthesized:**
- **Squalene:** Cholesterol precursor, skin moisturizer
- **Cholesterol:** Cell membranes, steroid hormones
- **Lanosterol:** First steroid in cholesterol synthesis

**Plant-Synthesized:**
- **Beta-sitosterol:** Plant sterol (cholesterol-lowering)
- **Lupeol:** Anti-inflammatory, anticancer properties
- **Oleanolic acid:** Liver protection, metabolic health
- **Ursolic acid:** Apple peels, muscle growth promoter

### Tetraterpenes (C₄₀)
**Carotenoids:**
- **β-carotene:** Vitamin A precursor (orange pigment)
- **Lycopene:** Tomato red, antioxidant
- **Lutein/Zeaxanthin:** Eye health (macula)
- **Astaxanthin:** Salmon pink, potent antioxidant

## Biosynthetic Pathways

### Mevalonate Pathway (Animals, Fungi)
```
Acetyl-CoA → Mevalonate → IPP → Terpenes
```
- Used by humans for cholesterol, CoQ10, steroids
- Statin target (HMG-CoA reductase)

### MEP Pathway (Plants, Bacteria, Plastids)
```
Pyruvate + G3P → MEP → IPP → Terpenes
```
- Alternative route to same IPP building blocks
- Target for antimicrobial drugs
- Not present in humans (selective toxicity)

---

## Fat Solubility

All terpenes/isoprenoids are **lipophilic (fat-soluble)** due to:
- Hydrocarbon structure (C-H bonds)
- Nonpolar character
- Ability to dissolve in membranes

**Clinical Implications:**
- Require dietary fat for absorption (carotenoids)
- Accumulate in adipose tissue
- Cross blood-brain barrier (small terpenes)
- Stored in lipid droplets
