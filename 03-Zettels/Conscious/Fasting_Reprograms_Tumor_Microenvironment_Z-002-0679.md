# Fasting Reprograms Tumor Microenvironment

## Core Claim
Fasting-induced metabolic changes — **low glucose, elevated ketones, reduced insulin/IGF-1** — reprogram the tumor microenvironment from immunosuppressive to immune-permissive, reducing immunosuppressive cell populations and enhancing anti-tumor immune cell function.

## Metabolic Changes

### Low Glucose
| Effect | Consequence |
|--------|-------------|
| **Tumor stress** | Cancer cells glucose-dependent |
| **Reduced proliferation** | Energy limitation |
| **Increased stress signals** | Immunogenic cell death |

### Ketone Elevation
| Effect | Consequence |
|--------|-------------|
| **Anti-inflammatory** | Reduces tumor-promoting inflammation |
| **Normal cell fuel** | Protects healthy tissue |
| **HDAC inhibition** | Epigenetic modulation |

### Reduced Insulin/IGF-1
| Effect | Consequence |
|--------|-------------|
| **Reduced cancer signaling** | PI3K/AKT/mTOR downregulation |
| **Slower growth** | Reduced proliferation |

## Immune Effects

| Population | Change | Result |
|------------|--------|--------|
| **MDSCs** | Decreased | Less immunosuppression |
| **Treg cells** | Decreased | Less suppression |
| **Cytotoxic T-cells** | Increased/enhanced | More killing |
| **NK cells** | Enhanced | More killing |
