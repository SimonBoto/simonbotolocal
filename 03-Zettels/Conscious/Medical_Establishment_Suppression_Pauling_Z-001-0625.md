# Medical Establishment Suppression of Pauling

The medical establishment's ridicule of Linus Pauling exemplifies a recurring pattern: suppressing paradigm-challenging ideas, especially from outsiders or those who challenge institutional dogma — with attacks on the person rather than engagement with the science.

## The Attacks on Pauling

**Rhetoric used:**
- "Quack"
- "Pseudoscience"
- "Dangerous"
- "Obsessed"
- Lost credibility
- Senile (in later years)

**Institutional response:**
- Dismissal without engagement
- Refusal to fund research
- Professional isolation
- Media mockery

## Why Pauling Was Targeted

**Threats he posed:**
1. **Outsider status** — chemist, not physician
2. **Self-treatment model** — patients could act independently
3. **Prevention focus** — threatened disease-care model
4. **Low-cost intervention** — vitamin C is cheap
5. **Paradigm challenge** — optimal vs. deficiency thinking

## Historical Pattern

**Other suppressed innovators:**
| Scientist | Idea | Initial Response | Later Status |
|-----------|------|-----------------|--------------|
| Semmelweis | Handwashing | Ridicule, asylum | Standard of care |
| Wegener | Continental drift | Pseudoscience | Plate tectonics |
| Marshall/Warren | H. pylori | Disbelief | Nobel Prize |
| Pauling | High-dose vitamin C | Quackery | Partial vindication |

## Mechanisms of Suppression

**Institutional:**
- Funding control
- Journal gatekeeping
- Professional licensing
- Credential requirements

**Social:**
- Peer pressure
- Reputation destruction
- Isolation
- Gaslighting

**Economic:**
- Threat to pharmaceutical model
- Prevention reduces treatment need
- Patient empowerment reduces dependency

## The Cost of Suppression

**To Pauling:**
- Reputation damage
- Research obstruction
- Personal suffering

**To medicine:**
- Delayed understanding
- Continued deficiency
- Missed therapeutic opportunities

**To patients:**
- Decades of suboptimal health
- Preventable suffering
- Unnecessary medicalization

## Modern Relevance

**Pattern continues:**
- Metabolic health
- Seed oils
- Sunlight/vitamin D
- Gut microbiome
- Any challenge to pharmaceutical model
