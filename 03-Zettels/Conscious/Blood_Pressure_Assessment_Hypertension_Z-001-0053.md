# Blood Pressure Assessment — Hypertension and Metabolic Connection

**Blood pressure** is a vital sign with profound metabolic implications. Hypertension is both a criterion for metabolic syndrome and a consequence of  resistance. The **optimal target is <120/80 mmHg**, not just the standard <140/90.

## 🎯 Blood Pressure Categories

| Category | Systolic | Diastolic | Metabolic Risk |
|----------|----------|-----------|----------------|
| **Optimal** | <120 | <80 | 🟢 Low |
| **Normal** | 120-129 | 80-84 | 🟡 Mild |
| **High-normal** | 130-139 | 85-89 | 🔶 Moderate |
| **Grade 1 HTN** | 140-159 | 90-99 | 🔴 High |
| **Grade 2 HTN** | 160-179 | 100-109 | 🔴 Very high |
| **Grade 3 HTN** | ≥180 | ≥110 | 🔴 Emergency |

**Metabolic syndrome threshold:** ≥130/85 mmHg (ATP III criterion)

## 🩺 Clinical Application

### Proper Measurement Technique

**Before measurement:**
- Rest 5 minutes seated
- No caffeine, exercise, smoking 30 minutes prior
- Empty bladder
- Arm at heart level
- Correct cuff size

**Multiple readings:**
- Take 2-3 readings, average them
- Check both arms initially (difference >10 mmHg suggests vascular disease)

### White Coat vs Masked Hypertension

| Pattern | Clinic BP | Home BP | Risk |
|---------|-----------|---------|------|
| **Normal** | <130/85 | <130/85 | Low |
| **White coat** | >140/90 | <130/85 | Moderate |
| **Masked** | <140/90 | >135/85 | High (missed diagnosis) |
| **Sustained** | >140/90 | >135/85 | High |

**Recommendation:** Home BP monitoring for all metabolic patients.

## 💊 Metabolic Hypertension Intervention

### Lifestyle (First-line)
| Intervention | SBP Reduction | Timeline |
|--------------|---------------|----------|
| **Weight loss (5-10%)** | 5-10 mmHg | 12 weeks |
| **DASH diet** | 8-14 mmHg | 4-8 weeks |
| **Sodium reduction (<2g/day)** | 2-8 mmHg | 2-4 weeks |
| **Exercise (150 min/week)** | 5-8 mmHg | 8-12 weeks |
| **Alcohol reduction** | 2-4 mmHg | 4 weeks |
| **Potassium increase** | 2-4 mmHg | 4 weeks |

### Pharmacologic
| Class | Best For | Caution |
|-------|----------|---------|
| **ACE-I/ARB** | Metabolic syndrome, diabetes | Monitor K+, creatinine |
| **CCB** | Isolated systolic HTN, elderly | Edema |
| **Thiazide** | Resistant HTN | Glucose, uric acid, K+ |
| **Spironolactone** | Resistant HTN | K+, gynecomastia |

**Note:** Thiazides can worsen glucose and uric acid — monitor in metabolic patients.

## 💡 Clinical Pearl

> "Blood pressure is the easiest vital sign to fix, but the hardest to get right. White coat hypertension is real — always confirm with home readings before starting lifelong medication."

> "In metabolic syndrome, fixing insulin resistance often fixes blood pressure. I've seen patients drop 15-20 mmHg systolic just from low-carb diet and 10% weight loss."

> "Check uric acid in all hypertensive patients. If >5.5, consider allopurinol — it can improve BP independent of other interventions."
