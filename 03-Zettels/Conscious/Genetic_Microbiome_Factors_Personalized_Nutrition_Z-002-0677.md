# Genetic and Microbiome Factors in Personalized Nutrition

## Core Claim
**Genetic polymorphisms** (APOE, FTO, MTHFR, etc.) and **gut microbiome composition** significantly influence individual responses to dietary interventions, enabling precision nutrition approaches that account for genetic predisposition and microbial metabolic capacity.

## Key Genetic Variants

### APOE
| Variant | Diet Response |
|---------|---------------|
| **APOE4** | Saturated fat sensitivity, benefit from Mediterranean diet |
| **APOE3** | Standard response |
| **APOE2** | Lower LDL, different fat handling |

### FTO
| Variant | Effect |
|---------|--------|
| **Risk allele** | Obesity susceptibility, responds to lifestyle |
| **Protective allele** | Lower obesity risk |

### MTHFR
| Variant | Implication |
|---------|-------------|
| **C677T variant** | Folate metabolism, B-vitamin needs |

## Microbiome Contributions

### Bacterial Species
| Species | Function |
|---------|----------|
| **Prevotella** | Carbohydrate metabolism |
| **Bacteroides** | Protein/fat metabolism |
| **Akkermansia** | Metabolic health, mucin degradation |

### Enterotypes
| Type | Characteristics |
|------|-----------------|
| **Prevotella-dominant** | Better carb utilizers |
| **Bacteroides-dominant** | Better protein/fat utilizers |

## Clinical Integration

### Testing
| Test | Information |
|------|-------------|
| **Nutrigenomics panel** | Key variants (APOE, FTO, MTHFR, etc.) |
| **Microbiome analysis** | Species composition, function |
