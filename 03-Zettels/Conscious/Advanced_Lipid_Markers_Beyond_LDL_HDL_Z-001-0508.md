# Advanced Lipid Markers — Beyond LDL and HDL

## Core Claim
ApoB, LDL particle number (LDL-P), and lipoprotein(a) [Lp(a)] provide superior cardiovascular risk prediction compared to standard lipid panels, particularly in insulin resistance, hypertriglyceridemia, and discordant risk scenarios where LDL-C underestimates true atherogenic burden.

## Evidence

### Advanced Markers Summary

| Marker | What It Measures | Optimal Level | Clinical Advantage |
|--------|------------------|---------------|-------------------|
| **ApoB** | Total atherogenic particle number (VLDL+IDL+LDL) | <80-90 mg/dL | Better predictor than LDL-C; one molecule per particle |
| **LDL-P** | LDL particle concentration | <1000 nmol/L | Captures discordant risk; size-independent |
| **Lp(a)** | LDL + Apo(a) complex | <30-50 mg/dL | Genetic risk; independent of lifestyle |
| **Remnant cholesterol** | VLDL remnants + IDL | <30 mg/dL | Triglyceride-rich particle risk |
| **sdLDL** | Small dense LDL fraction | Minimal | More atherogenic per particle |
| **TG/HDL ratio** | Insulin resistance proxy | <2.0 | Simple calculation; metabolic risk |

### Apolipoprotein B (ApoB)

**Structure:** One ApoB molecule per atherogenic particle
- VLDL, IDL, and LDL each contain one ApoB-100
- Direct measure of atherogenic particle number

**Advantages over LDL-C:**
- Not affected by cholesterol content variability per particle
- Accounts for VLDL and IDL remnants
- Better predictor in hypertriglyceridemia
- Mendelian randomization supports causality

### LDL Particle Number (LDL-P)

**Measurement Methods:**
- NMR spectroscopy (most common)
- Ion mobility
- Vertical auto profile (VAP)

**Discordance:**
- Occurs when LDL-C and LDL-P disagree (~20-30% of patients)
- High LDL-P with normal LDL-C = undertreated high risk
- Common in insulin resistance, metabolic syndrome

### Lipoprotein(a) — Lp(a)

**Structure:**
- LDL particle covalently bound to Apo(a)
- Apo(a) is homologous to plasminogen

**Characteristics:**
| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Genetics** | 80-90% heritable; determined by LPA gene |
| **Pro-atherogenic** | LDL component promotes plaque formation |
| **Pro-thrombotic** | Apo(a) inhibits fibrinolysis |
| **Not modifiable** | Resistant to lifestyle and most drugs |

**Management:**
- Statins do not lower Lp(a)
- Niacin reduces ~20% but no outcome benefit proven
- PCSK9 inhibitors reduce ~25-30%
- Apheresis for extreme elevations (>60 mg/dL) with CVD

### Remnant Cholesterol

**Calculation:** Total cholesterol − HDL-C − LDL-C
- Represents VLDL remnants and IDL
- Triglyceride-rich, cholesterol-enriched particles
- Independent risk factor in Mendelian randomization studies

### TG/HDL Ratio

| Ratio | Interpretation |
|-------|----------------|
| <2.0 | Optimal; insulin sensitive |
| 2.0-3.0 | Borderline; possible insulin resistance |
| >3.0 | High risk; significant insulin resistance |

## Clinical Implication

**When to Use Advanced Markers:**
- Discordant risk (LDL-C vs. clinical picture mismatch)
- Family history of premature CVD
- Metabolic syndrome, insulin resistance, diabetes
- Recurrent events despite "optimal" LDL-C
- Hypertriglyceridemia (TG >200 mg/dL)

**Therapeutic Implications:**
- ApoB-guided therapy may be superior to LDL-C targets
- Lp(a) elevation intensifies risk; lower other risk factors aggressively
- Remnant cholesterol responds to fibrates, omega-3s, lifestyle

## METHAP Relevance

**Comprehensive Risk Assessment:**
- Advanced markers for residual risk identification
- Integrated with metabolic markers (insulin, glucose, inflammatory markers)
- Personalized approach to lipid management beyond standard panels
