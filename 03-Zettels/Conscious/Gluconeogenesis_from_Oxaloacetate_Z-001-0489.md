# Gluconeogenesis from Oxaloacetate

## The Bypass Reaction
**Problem:** Pyruvate kinase reaction (PEP → pyruvate) is irreversible in cells.

**Solution:** Two-step bypass via oxaloacetate:
```
Pyruvate → Oxaloacetate (pyruvate carboxylase)
Oxaloacetate → PEP (PEPCK)
```

## Oxaloacetate to PEP Conversion

### Reaction
```
Oxaloacetate + GTP → Phosphoenolpyruvate + GDP + CO₂
```

**Enzyme:** PEP carboxykinase (PEPCK)
**Cofactor:** GTP (or ATP)
**Byproduct:** CO₂ (decarboxylation)

### Energetics
- **Energy investment:** 1 GTP per OAA
- **Reversible?** No (decarboxylation drives forward)

## Isozymes and Location

### Cytosolic PEPCK
- **Gene:** PCK1
- **Major gluconeogenic enzyme**
- **Regulated by:** Hormones, diet

### l PEPCK
- **Gene:** PCK2
- **Minor role in gluconeogenesis**
- **Anaplerotic function** more prominent

## Regulation of PEPCK

### Transcriptional Control
| Factor | Effect | Mechanism |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| **Glucagon** | ↑ PEPCK | cAMP → CREB → transcription |
| **Cortisol** | ↑ PEPCK | Glucocorticoid receptor |
| **** | ↓ PEPCK | FOXO1 inhibition |
| **Fasting** | ↑ PEPCK | Multiple hormones |

### Post-transcriptional
- **mRNA stability:** Regulated by nutrients
- **Protein degradation:** UPS pathway

```
Pyruvate → OAA → PEP → ... → Glucose
         (PC)   (PEPCK)

    shuttle required
```

## Energy Cost
**From pyruvate to glucose:**
- 2 Pyruvate → 1 Glucose
- **6 ATP total:**
  - 2 ATP (pyruvate carboxylase: 1 ATP each)
  - 2 GTP (PEPCK: 1 GTP each)
  - 2 ATP (phosphoglycerate kinase reverse)

## Clinical Relevance

### Type 2 Diabetes
- **Excessive gluconeogenesis:** Major contributor to fasting hyperglycemia
- **PEPCK overexpression:** In insulin-resistant liver
- **Metformin:** Inhibits gluconeogenesis (via AMPK)

### Fasting Hypoglycemia
- **PEPCK deficiency:** Rare, severe hypoglycemia
- **Cannot convert OAA to PEP:** Gluconeogenesis blocked
