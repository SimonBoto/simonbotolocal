## Core Claim
**The conversion of oxaloacetate to phosphoenolpyruvate (PEP) by PEP carboxykinase (PEPCK) is the critical bypass reaction that enables gluconeogenesis from pyruvate/lactate — since the pyruvate kinase reaction (PEP → pyruvate) is irreversible, cells use a two-step detour: pyruvate carboxylase (mitochondrial) makes OAA, PEPCK (cytosolic) converts OAA to PEP** — this energy-investing step (1 GTP) is rate-limiting and hormonally regulated.

## Evidence

### The Bypass Problem
| Reaction | Direction | Reversibility |
|----------|-----------|---------------|
| **Pyruvate kinase** | PEP → Pyruvate | Irreversible in cells |
| **Need** | Pyruvate → PEP | Requires detour |

### Two-Step Bypass
```
Pyruvate + CO₂ + ATP → Oxaloacetate (pyruvate carboxylase, mitochondrial)
Oxaloacetate + GTP → PEP + CO₂ (PEPCK, cytosolic)
```

### PEPCK Isozymes
| Feature | Cytosolic PEPCK (PCK1) | Mitochondrial PEPCK (PCK2) |
|---------|------------------------|---------------------------|
| **Location** | Cytosol | Mitochondria |
| **Role** | Major gluconeogenic enzyme | Minor, anaplerotic |
| **Regulation** | Hormonal (glucagon/insulin) | Constitutive |

### Energetics
| Cost | Per OAA | Per Glucose |
|------|---------|-------------|
| **GTP** | 1 | 2 (2 pyruvate) |
| **Total gluconeogenesis** | — | 6 ATP equivalent |

### Regulation
| Hormone | Effect | Mechanism |
|---------|--------|-----------|
| **Glucagon** | ↑ PEPCK | cAMP → CREB → transcription |
| **Cortisol** | ↑ PEPCK | Glucocorticoid receptor |
| **Insulin** | ↓ PEPCK | FOXO1 phosphorylation → nuclear exclusion |

## Clinical Implication
- **PEPCK overexpression:** Contributes to hyperglycemia in diabetes
- **Metformin:** Inhibits hepatic gluconeogenesis (indirectly affects PEPCK)
- **Gluconeogenic substrates:** Lactate, alanine, glycerol all enter via this pathway
- **METHAP relevance:** Controlling gluconeogenesis is central to blood glucose management; PEPCK is a key therapeutic target
