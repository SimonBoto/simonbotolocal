## Core Claim
**As little as 15-20 minutes of vigorous physical activity per week is associated with 16-40% lower risk of death from all causes** — demonstrating that substantial health benefits can be achieved with minimal time investment, making exercise accessible even to the busiest individuals.

## The Evidence

### Study Design
| Parameter | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Study** | Ahmadi et al., European Heart Journal 2022 |
| **Participants** | 72,000 people |
| **Follow-up** | ~6 years |
| **Activity measured** | Vigorous physical activity |

### Key Findings
- **15-20 min/week** → 16-40% mortality reduction
- **Benefits for:** All-cause, CVD, and cancer mortality
- **Below guidelines:** Far less than 150-300 min/week moderate or 75-150 min/week vigorous recommendations

## Clinical Implications
- Even minimal exercise provides substantial protection
- "I don't have time" is not a valid excuse
- Small investments yield large returns
- Accessibility for time-limited populations
