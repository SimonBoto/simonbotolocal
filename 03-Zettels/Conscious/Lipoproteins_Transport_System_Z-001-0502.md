# Lipoproteins — The Transport System

> Lipoproteins are macromolecular complexes that transport hydrophobic lipids through aqueous blood, with distinct classes carrying different lipid cargoes to specific tissues.

## Classes

| Class | Density | Origin | Main Lipid | Function |
|-------|---------|--------|------------|----------|
| **Chylomicrons** | Lowest | Intestine | Triglycerides | Dietary fat transport |
| **VLDL** | Very low | Liver | Triglycerides | Endogenous fat transport |
| **IDL** | Intermediate | VLDL | /TG | Transitional |
| **LDL** | Low | IDL | Cholesterol | Cell delivery |
| **HDL** | High | Liver | Phospholipids | Reverse transport |

## Structure

**Core:** Hydrophobic (TG, cholesteryl esters)
**Surface:** Amphipathic (phospholipids, free cholesterol, apolipoproteins)

## Apolipoproteins

| Apo | Lipoprotein | Function |
|-----|-------------|----------|
| **ApoB-48** | Chylomicrons | Intestinal absorption |
| **ApoB-100** | VLDL, LDL | Structural, receptor binding |
| **ApoA-I** | HDL | RCT, structural |
| **ApoE** | Remnants | Hepatic uptake |
| **ApoC-II** | VLDL, chylomicrons | LPL activation |

## Pathways

**Exogenous:**
Dietary fat → Chylomicrons → LPL releases TG → Remnants → Liver

**Endogenous:**
Liver VLDL → LPL → IDL → Hepatic lipase → LDL → Peripheral cells
