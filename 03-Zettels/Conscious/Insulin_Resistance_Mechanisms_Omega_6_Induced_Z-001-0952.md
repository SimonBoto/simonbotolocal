## Core Mechanism

High omega-6 intake induces insulin resistance through multiple convergent pathways rooted in chronic  and oxidative stress.

```
High Omega-6 Intake
    ↓
Pro-inflammatory eicosanoids + Oxidized lipids + Endotoxin/LPS
    ↓
Chronic inflammation + Oxidative stress
    ↓
Multiple signaling disruptions → INSULIN RESISTANCE
```

## Pathway 1: JNK Activation

**Mechanism:**
- Inflammatory cytokines (TNF-α, IL-6) activate **c-Jun N-terminal kinase (JNK)**
- JNK phosphorylates **IRS-1 at serine residues** (instead of tyrosine)
- Serine-phosphorylated IRS-1 cannot bind PI3K
- **Result:** Blocked insulin signaling downstream of insulin receptor

**Evidence:** JNK knockout mice are protected from obesity-induced insulin resistance (Hirosumi et al., 2002)

## Pathway 2: IKKβ/NF-κB

**Mechanism:**
- Inflammatory signaling activates **IKKβ (IκB kinase beta)**
- IKKβ phosphorylates IκB, releasing **NF-κB** to nucleus
- NF-κB induces transcription of inflammatory genes
- Creates **positive feedback loop:** more inflammation → more insulin resistance

**Key mediators:** TNF-α, IL-1β, IL-6, MCP-1

## Pathway 3: PKCθ Activation

**Mechanism:**
- Diacylglycerol (DAG) accumulates from altered lipid 
- DAG activates **protein kinase C theta (PKCθ)**
- PKCθ directly phosphorylates IRS-1 at serine sites
- **Result:** Impaired insulin receptor substrate function

**Tissue-specific:** Particularly important in skeletal muscle

## Pathway 4: SOCS Proteins

**Mechanism:**
- Inflammation induces **Suppressor of Cytokine Signaling (SOCS-1, SOCS-3)**
- SOCS proteins bind to IRS-1 and IRS-2
- Target IRS proteins for **ubiquitination and degradation**
- **Result:** Reduced insulin signaling capacity

## Lipid-Induced Mechanisms

**Ceramide Accumulation:**
- Saturated fatty acids (metabolically linked to omega-6 excess) increase ceramide synthesis
- Ceramide inhibits Akt/PKB phosphorylation
- Blocks glucose uptake via GLUT4 translocation

**Mitochondrial Dysfunction:**
- Oxidized cardiolipin (omega-6 enriched) disrupts ETC
- Reduced ATP → AMPK activation altered
- Impaired metabolic flexibility

## Clinical Correlation

| Marker | Effect of High Omega-6 |
|--------|------------------------|
| Fasting insulin | ↑ 15-30% |
| HOMA-IR | ↑ 20-40% |
| Adiponectin | ↓ (anti-inflammatory adipokine reduced) |
| TNF-α | ↑ (pro-inflammatory cytokine increased) |

## Integration with Other Factors

**Synergistic with:**
- Obesity (adipose inflammation amplifies effects)
- Gut dysbiosis (endotoxin entry)
- Sedentary lifestyle (reduced insulin sensitivity baseline)

**Protective against:**
- Omega-3 intake (competes for desaturase enzymes)
- Exercise (improves insulin sensitivity independent of diet)
- Caloric restriction (reduces overall metabolic load)

## Clinical Application

**Assessment:**
- Fasting insulin + glucose → HOMA-IR calculation
- Inflammatory markers: hsCRP, TNF-α, IL-6
- Omega-6:Omega-3 ratio in red blood cell membranes

**Intervention targets:**
1. Reduce omega-6 intake (<10g/day linoleic acid)
2. Increase omega-3 intake (EPA/DHA 2-4g/day)
3. Address gut permeability (reduce endotoxin)
4. Weight management (reduce adipose inflammation)
