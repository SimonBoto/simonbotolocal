# Redox Cofactors NAD FAD Biochemistry

## Core Claim
**NAD+/NADH and FAD/FADH₂ serve as the primary electron carriers in metabolism — derived from B-vitamins (B3 and B2 respectively) — with their redox status and ratio serving as critical indicators of cellular energy state, metabolic flexibility, and mitochondrial function.**

## Evidence

### NAD+/NADH (Vitamin B3)
| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Synthesis** | Tryptophan → niacin → NAD+ |
| **Forms** | NAD+ (oxidized), NADH (reduced) |
| **Function** | Electron transfer, signaling |
| **Reactions** | Dehydrogenases, sirtuins, PARPs |

### FAD/FADH₂ (Vitamin B2)
| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Synthesis** | Riboflavin → FMN → FAD |
| **Forms** | FAD (oxidized), FADH₂ (reduced) |
| **Function** | Electron transfer, covalent catalysis |
| **Reactions** | Succinate DH, fatty acyl-CoA DH |

### Redox Reactions
| Reaction | Enzyme | Cofactor | Clinical Marker |
|----------|--------|----------|-----------------|
| Pyruvate → Acetyl-CoA | PDH | NAD+ | Pyruvate ↑ |
| Lactate ↔ Pyruvate | LDH | NADH/NAD+ | Lactate/pyruvate ratio |
| α-KG → Succinyl-CoA | α-KGDH | NAD+ | α-KG ↑ |
| Succinate → Fumarate | SDH | FAD | Succinate ↑ |

### NAD+/NADH Ratio
| State | Ratio | Implication |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| **Oxidized** | High NAD+ | Catabolic, fasting state |
| **Reduced** | High NADH | Fed state, glycolysis active |
| **Imbalance** | Altered ratio | Metabolic dysfunction |

### Clinical Relevance
| Cofactor | Deficiency | Symptoms | OAT Marker |
|----------|------------|----------|------------|
| **B3 (NAD+)** | Pellagra | Dermatitis, dementia, diarrhea | Elevated pyruvate, α-KG |
| **B2 (FAD)** | Ariboflavinosis | Cheilitis, glossitis, photophobia | Elevated succinate |

### NAD+ and Aging
| Aspect | Mechanism | Intervention |
|--------|-----------|--------------|
| **Decline with age** | PARP activation, CD38 | NAD+ precursors |
| **Sirtuin function** | NAD+-dependent deacetylases | Caloric restriction |
| **Mitochondrial biogenesis** | SIRT1/PGC-1α pathway | Exercise, NR, NMN |

## Clinical Implication
- **Energy assessment:** NADH/NAD+ ratio indicates metabolic state
- **B-vitamin status:** OAT markers reveal cofactor adequacy
- **Aging interventions:** NAD+ precursors show promise
- **Mitochondrial support:** Redox balance essential for ETC function
- **METHAP relevance:** NAD+ metabolism is central to metabolic syndrome and aging research; sirtuins link energy status to longevity pathways
