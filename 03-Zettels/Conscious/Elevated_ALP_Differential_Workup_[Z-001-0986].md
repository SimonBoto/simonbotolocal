# Elevated ALP Differential — Systematic Workup for High Alkaline Phosphatase

## Clinical Approach

Elevated alkaline phosphatase (ALP) is a common laboratory finding requiring systematic evaluation to determine source (hepatic vs bone) and underlying etiology.

## Step 1: Confirm Elevation

### Pre-Analytical Considerations

**Fasting vs Non-Fasting:**
- Intestinal ALP rises after meals (fatty foods)
- Repeat fasting if mildly elevated
- 12-hour fast ideal

**Age-Specific Ranges:**
- Children: 2-3x adult upper limit (growth)
- Adolescents: Peak during growth spurt
- Pregnancy: Rises in third trimester (placental)
- Elderly: Slight rise acceptable

**Verify with Repeat:**
- Mild elevation (<1.5x upper limit): Repeat to confirm
- Moderate/marked: Proceed with workup

## Step 2: Determine Source

### Key Test: GGT or 5'-Nucleotidase

**Gamma-Glutamyl Transferase (GGT):**
- Most commonly available
- Elevated in hepatobiliary disease
- Normal in bone disease

| GGT Result | Interpretation | Next Step |
|------------|----------------|-----------|
| **Elevated** | Hepatic source | Liver workup |
| **Normal** | Bone source (or pregnancy) | Bone evaluation |

**5'-Nucleotidase:**
- More specific than GGT
- Not affected by alcohol or drugs
- Alternative if GGT unavailable or confounded

### Additional Clues

**Clinical Context:**
- Jaundice, pruritus, dark urine → Hepatic
- Bone pain, fractures, deformities → Bone
- Recent fracture, immobilization → Transient bone
- Pregnancy history → Placental

## Step 3: Hepatic Source Workup

### Pattern Recognition

**Cholestatic Pattern (ALP >> ALT/AST):**
- Bile duct obstruction (stone, stricture, tumor)
- Primary biliary cholangitis (PBC)
- Primary sclerosing cholangitis (PSC)
- Drug-induced cholestasis

**Hepatocellular Pattern (ALT/AST >> ALP):**
- Hepatitis (viral, autoimmune, alcoholic)
- Steatohepatitis
- Drug-induced liver injury

### Imaging

**Right Upper Quadrant Ultrasound:**
- First-line imaging
- Evaluate for ductal dilation
- Stones, masses
- Liver texture

**MRCP (if US inconclusive):**
- Better visualization of biliary tree
- Detect strictures, stones, tumors

**CT:**
- Mass evaluation
- Staging if malignancy suspected

### Additional Labs

**Autoimmune Workup:**
- ANA, SMA, AMA (PBC)
- ANCA (PSC)
- IgG4 (autoimmune pancreatitis/cholangitis)

**Viral Hepatitis:**
- Hepatitis B and C serologies

**Tumor Markers (if indicated):**
- CA 19-9 (cholangiocarcinoma)
- CEA, CA 19-9 (pancreatic)
- AFP (hepatocellular carcinoma)

## Step 4: Bone Source Workup

### Clinical Assessment

**History:**
- Bone pain location
- Fracture history
- Family history bone disorders
- Medications (bisphosphonates, steroids)

**Physical Exam:**
- Bone deformities
- Shortening of limbs
- Warmth over bones
- Tenderness

### Laboratory

**Bone Turnover Markers:**
- P1NP (formation)
- CTX or NTX (resorption)
- Elevated in high-turnover states

**Calcium Metabolism:**
- Calcium, phosphate
- PTH (hyperparathyroidism)
- 25-OH vitamin D

### Imaging

**X-Rays:**
- Suspected sites of involvement
- Fractures
- Characteristic Paget's changes

**Bone Scan:**
- Whole-body survey
- Identify all sites of increased turnover
- Most sensitive for Paget's, metastases

**DEXA:**
- If osteoporosis suspected
- Baseline if starting bone-active therapy

## Differential Diagnosis by ALP Level

### Mild Elevation (<2x Upper Limit)

| Category | Causes |
|----------|--------|
| **Physiologic** | Aging, pregnancy, recent meal |
| **Liver** | Early cholestasis, fatty liver, drug effect |
| **Bone** | Early osteoporosis treatment response |

**Approach:**
- Repeat fasting
- Check GGT
- Review medications
- Monitor if no symptoms

### Moderate Elevation (2-4x Upper Limit)

| Category | Causes |
|----------|--------|
| **Liver** | Cholestatic diseases, partial obstruction, cirrhosis |
| **Bone** | Healing fractures, osteoporosis treatment, hyperparathyroidism |
| **Other** | Healing bones, cardiac bypass |

**Approach:**
- Systematic workup based on GGT
- Imaging as indicated
- Consider bone density if bone source

### Marked Elevation (>4x Upper Limit)

| Category | Causes |
|----------|--------|
| **Liver** | Complete bile duct obstruction, severe PBC, infiltrative diseases |
| **Bone** | Paget's disease, extensive metastases, osteomalacia |

**Approach:**
- Urgent evaluation
- Comprehensive imaging
- Consider malignancy workup
- Bone scan if bone source suspected

## Special Situations

### Isolated ALP Elevation

**Definition:**
- ALP elevated
- Other LFTs normal
- GGT normal

**Differential:**
1. Bone source (most common)
2. Early or mild cholestasis
3. Placental (pregnancy)
4. Intestinal (postprandial)
5. Macro-ALP (complex with Ig)

**Workup:**
- Repeat fasting
- Confirm GGT normal
- If persistent → bone evaluation
- Isoenzyme electrophoresis if unclear

### Transient Elevation

**Causes:**
- Recent fracture
- Surgery
- Immobilization
- Certain medications

**Approach:**
- Repeat in 4-8 weeks
- Usually resolves

### Familial Elevated ALP

**Benign Familial Hyperphosphatasemia:**
- Asymptomatic
- Genetic
- No treatment needed
- Recognize to avoid unnecessary workup

## When to Refer

**Gastroenterology/Hepatology:**
- Cholestatic pattern
- Jaundice
- Suspected PBC/PSC
- Abnormal imaging

**Endocrinology/Rheumatology:**
- Paget's disease
- Complex metabolic bone disease
- Hyperparathyroidism

**Oncology:**
- Suspected metastatic bone disease
- Cholangiocarcinoma
- Hepatocellular carcinoma

**Orthopedics:**
- Pathologic fracture
- Severe Paget's complications

## Summary

**Key Steps:**
1. Confirm elevation (fasting)
2. Check GGT to determine source
3. Hepatic workup: Imaging + labs
4. Bone workup: X-rays, bone scan, turnover markers
5. Tailor workup to degree of elevation
6. Refer appropriately

**Clinical Pearl:** GGT is the key test. If elevated → liver. If normal → bone (or pregnancy). Always consider clinical context and pre-analytical factors.
