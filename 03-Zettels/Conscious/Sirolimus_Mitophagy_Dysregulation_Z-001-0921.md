## mTORC1 and Autophagy Regulation

**Normal State (Fed):**
- mTORC1 active → autophagy suppressed
- ULK1 phosphorylated (inactivated)
- Autophagosome formation blocked
- Cellular components preserved

**Starvation/Stress (Normal Response):**
- mTORC1 inhibited → autophagy activated
- ULK1 dephosphorylated (activated)
- Autophagosome formation
- Cellular components recycled

## Sirolimus Effect: Chronic Autophagy

**Acute Sirolimus (Beneficial):**
- Removes damaged mitochondria
- Quality control mechanism
- Improves mitochondrial health
- Cellular recycling

**Chronic Sirolimus (Harmful):**
- Continuous autophagy activation
- Excessive mitochondrial clearance
- **Without matching biogenesis**
- Net loss of healthy mitochondria

## The Imbalance Problem

**Mitochondrial Quality Equation:**
```
Mitochondrial Health = Biogenesis - (Damage + Clearance)
```

**Normal Balance:**
- Biogenesis ≈ Clearance + Damage
- Steady-state maintained

**Sirolimus Disruption:**
- Biogenesis ↓ (via PGC-1α suppression)
- Clearance ↑ (via autophagy activation)
- **Net: Progressive depletion**

## Mitophagy Mechanisms

**PINK1/Parkin Pathway:**
- Damaged mitochondria: membrane potential loss
- PINK1 accumulates on outer membrane
- Parkin recruitment
- Ubiquitination of outer membrane proteins
- Autophagosome recognition
- Lysosomal degradation

**Receptor-Mediated Mitophagy:**
- BNIP3, NIX, FUNDC1 as receptors
- Direct binding to LC3
- Less dependent on Parkin
