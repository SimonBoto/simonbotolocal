# NADPH Functions — Anabolism and Antioxidant Defense

## Core Claim
**NADPH provides the reducing power for ~60% of biosynthetic reactions and antioxidant defense — generated primarily via PPP, it maintains glutathione in reduced form (GSH) and fuels the respiratory burst in immune cells** — distinct from NADH which drives ATP production.

## Evidence

### Sources of NADPH
| Source | Contribution |
|--------|--------------|
| **PPP** | ~60% (primary) |
| **Malic enzyme** | Secondary |
| **Isocitrate dehydrogenase** | Secondary |

### Key Functions
| Function | Mechanism |
|----------|-----------|
| **Biosynthesis** | Fatty acid, cholesterol, nucleotide synthesis |
| **Antioxidant** | GSSG + NADPH → 2 GSH (glutathione reductase) |
| **Immunity** | NADPH oxidase → superoxide (respiratory burst) |

### NADPH vs NADH
| Feature | NADH | NADPH |
|---------|------|-------|
| **Primary role** | Energy (ETC) | Reduction (biosynthesis) |
| **Metabolic state** | Catabolism | Anabolism |
| **Pool ratio** | Oxidized favored | Reduced favored |

## Clinical Implication
- **Chronic Granulomatous Disease:** NADPH oxidase deficiency → impaired pathogen killing
- **G6PD deficiency:** Impaired NADPH production → hemolysis under oxidative stress
- **Cancer cells:** High NADPH demand for proliferation and redox balance
- **METHAP relevance:** NADPH maintains glutathione for antioxidant defense; high-dose nutrients increase metabolic demand for reducing power
