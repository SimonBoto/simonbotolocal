## Mechanism

**The methionine cycle activates methionine to SAM (universal methyl donor) for methylation reactions, then processes the byproduct SAH to homocysteine, which is either remethylated to methionine (B12-dependent) or enters transsulfuration (B6-dependent)** — this creates a metabolic switch between methylation and antioxidant synthesis.

## Mechanism

**The methionine cycle activates methionine to SAM (universal methyl donor) for methylation reactions, then processes the byproduct SAH to homocysteine, which is either remethylated to methionine (B12-dependent) or enters transsulfuration (B6-dependent)** — this creates a metabolic switch between methylation and antioxidant synthesis.

## Evidence

### Cycle Steps
| Step | Reaction | Enzyme | Cofactor |
|------|----------|--------|----------|
| **1. Activation** | Methionine + ATP → SAM | MAT | — |
| **2. Methylation** | SAM → SAH | Methyltransferases | — |
| **3. Hydrolysis** | SAH → Homocysteine + Adenosine | SAH hydrolase | — |
| **4a. Remethylation** | Homocysteine → Methionine | Methionine synthase | **B12, folate** |
| **4b. Transsulfuration** | Homocysteine → Cysteine | CBS/CGL | **B6** |

### Regulatory Switch
| Condition | Fate | Rationale |
|-----------|------|-----------|
| **High SAM** | Transsulfuration | Methylation capacity sufficient → make glutathione |
| **Low SAM** | Remethylation | Conserve methionine for methylation |

## Clinical Implication
- **B12 deficiency traps homocysteine** — cannot remethylate, pushes toward transsulfuration but may overwhelm
- **High SAM activates CBS** — diverts homocysteine to cysteine/glutathione when methylation adequate
- **METHAP relevance:** High-dose B12 and folate support remethylation; B6 supports transsulfuration → both needed for balance
