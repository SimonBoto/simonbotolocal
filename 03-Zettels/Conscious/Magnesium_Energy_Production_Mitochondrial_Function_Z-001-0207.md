## Core Principle

> Magnesium is indispensable for cellular energy , serving as a cofactor for key enzymes in glycolysis, the Krebs cycle, β-oxidation, and oxidative phosphorylation.

## Glycolysis

**Rate-Limiting Step:**
- **Phosphofructokinase-1 (PFK-1):** Committed step of glycolysis
- Mg-ATP required as substrate (not just cofactor)
- High Mg-ATP activates; high ATP/Mg ratio inhibits

**Other Mg-Dependent Glycolytic Enzymes:**
- Hexokinase/glucokinase
- Phosphoglycerate kinase
- Pyruvate kinase

## Krebs Cycle (Citric Acid Cycle)

**Key Mg-Dependent Enzymes:**
| Enzyme | Reaction | Significance |
|--------|----------|--------------|
| **Pyruvate dehydrogenase** | Pyruvate → Acetyl-CoA | Links glycolysis to Krebs cycle |
| **Isocitrate dehydrogenase** | Isocitrate → α-ketoglutarate | Rate-limiting; produces NADH |
| **α-ketoglutarate dehydrogenase** | α-ketoglutarate → Succinyl-CoA | Critical regulatory point |
| **Succinyl-CoA synthetase** | Succinyl-CoA → Succinate | Substrate-level phosphorylation (GTP) |

##  ATP Synthesis

**ATP Synthase (Complex V):**
- Mg²⁺ required for proton gradient coupling
- Mg-ADP + Pi → Mg-ATP + H₂O
- Structural Mg²⁺ in catalytic subunits

## β-Oxidation and Lipid Metabolism

**Fatty Acid Activation:**
- **Acyl-CoA synthetase:** Fatty acid + CoA + Mg-ATP → Acyl-CoA + Mg-AMP + PPi
- Required for all fatty acid metabolism (oxidation, synthesis)

## Energy Storage and Transfer

**Creatine Kinase System:**
- Mg-dependent enzyme (creatine + Mg-ATP ↔ phosphocreatine + Mg-ADP)
- Rapid ATP regeneration in muscle, brain
- "Energy shuttle" between mitochondria and cytosol

**Adenylate Kinase:**
- 2 ADP ↔ ATP + AMP (maintains energy charge)
- Mg²⁺ required for proper conformation

## Clinical Implications

**Magnesium Deficiency:**
- Impaired exercise tolerance (reduced ATP regeneration)
- Fatigue, muscle weakness
- Metabolic inflexibility (difficulty switching fuel sources)

**METHAP Context:**
- Energy metabolism optimization supports overall metabolic health
- Mg status affects physical activity capacity (lifestyle intervention component)
