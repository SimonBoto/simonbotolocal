# SHBG Physiology and Clinical Significance — Sex Hormone Binding Globulin

## Overview

Sex Hormone Binding Globulin (SHBG) is the primary transport protein for testosterone and estradiol in the circulation, regulating their bioavailability and metabolic clearance.

## Biology

### Structure and Production

**Production:**
- Synthesized in liver hepatocytes
- Released into circulation
- Glycoprotein structure (homodimer)

**Gene:**
- Located on chromosome 17
- Single gene encodes protein
- Polymorphisms affect levels (rs6259, rs727428)

### Binding Characteristics

**Ligands:**
| Hormone | Affinity | % Bound |
|---------|----------|---------|
| **DHT** | Highest | ~70% |
| **Testosterone** | High | ~45-65% |
| **Estradiol** | Moderate | ~35% |

**Binding Capacity:**
- Limited number of binding sites
- High affinity, low capacity
- Saturable at high hormone concentrations

### Circulation Dynamics

**Testosterone Distribution:**
```
Total Testosterone
    ↓
  ~2% Free (bioactive)
  ~40% SHBG-bound (inactive transport)
  ~58% Albumin-bound (weakly bound, partially active)
    ↓
Bioavailable Testosterone = Free + Albumin-bound (~40-50%)
```

**Key Point:** Only free testosterone readily enters cells

## Regulation of SHBG

### Factors That Increase SHBG

| Factor | Mechanism | Clinical Context |
|--------|-----------|------------------|
| **Estrogens** | Hepatic production ↑ | Pregnancy, oral contraceptives, estrogen therapy |
| **Thyroid hormone** | Hepatic synthesis ↑ | Hyperthyroidism |
| **Aging** | Unknown | Age-related increase |
| **Liver disease** | Reduced clearance? | Cirrhosis, hepatitis |
| **Drugs** | Various | Phenytoin, anticonvulsants |
| **Exercise** | Transient increase | Acute effect |

### Factors That Decrease SHBG

| Factor | Mechanism | Clinical Context |
|--------|-----------|------------------|
| **** | Inhibits hepatic synthesis | Insulin resistance, obesity, type 2 diabetes |
| **Androgens** | Suppress production | Testosterone therapy, anabolic steroids |
| **IGF-1** | Inhibits synthesis | Acromegaly, high growth hormone |
| **Hypothyroidism** | Reduced synthesis | Low thyroid function |
| **Obesity** | Insulin-mediated | Metabolic syndrome |
| **PCOS** | Insulin resistance | Women with PCOS |

### Metabolic Syndrome Pattern

**Typical Profile:**
- Low SHBG
- Normal/low total testosterone
- Normal/high free testosterone (initially)
- Eventually low bioavailable testosterone with chronic obesity

**Mechanism:**
```
Obesity → Insulin resistance → High insulin
    ↓
SHBG production suppressed
    ↓
Lower total testosterone (more free, but also more clearance)
    ↓
Paradox: Normal free, low total
```

## Clinical Significance

### Why SHBG Matters

**Total vs Free Testosterone:**
- **Total testosterone:** Includes bound (inactive) forms
- **Free testosterone:** Bioactive fraction
- **SHBG changes alter total without changing bioavailable**

**Clinical Example:**
| Scenario | SHBG | Total T | Free T | Interpretation |
|----------|------|---------|--------|----------------|
| Healthy man | Normal | Normal | Normal | Normal |
| Obese man | Low | Low | Normal | Normal bioavailable |
| Elderly man | High | Low | Low | True deficiency |
| Cirrhosis | High | Low | Low | True deficiency |
| Hypothyroid | High | Low | Low | True deficiency |

### When to Measure SHBG

**Indications:**
1. **Discordant total testosterone and symptoms**
   - Symptoms suggest hypogonadism but total T normal/high
   - Suspect altered SHBG

2. **Obesity/metabolic syndrome**
   - Low SHBG expected
   - Calculate free/bioavailable T

3. **Thyroid disease**
   - SHBG changes with thyroid status
   - Affects interpretation

4. **Liver disease**
   - Altered production

5. **Medication effects**
   - Anticonvulsants, androgens, estrogens

### Free Testosterone Calculation

**Methods:**
1. **Equilibrium dialysis:** Gold standard (research)
2. **Analog immunoassay:** Widely available, less accurate
3. **Calculated free T (Vermeulen formula):**
   - Uses total T, SHBG, albumin
   - Good correlation with equilibrium dialysis

**Formula (simplified):**
```
Free T = Total T / (1 + [SHBG binding constant] × [SHBG])
```

**Online calculators available**

### SHBG as 

**Independent of Testosterone:**
- Low SHBG predicts:
  - Type 2 diabetes risk
  - Metabolic syndrome
  - Cardiovascular disease
  - Non-alcoholic fatty liver disease

**Mechanism:**
- Marker of insulin resistance
- Hepatic manifestation of metabolic dysfunction

**Clinical Use:**
- Risk stratification
- Monitoring metabolic intervention
- Part of comprehensive metabolic assessment

## Clinical Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Obese Man with Hypogonadal Symptoms

**Labs:**
- Total T: 280 ng/dL (low-normal)
- SHBG: 12 nmol/L (low)
- Free T: 8 pg/mL (normal)
- Glucose: 110 mg/dL (elevated)
- Insulin: 25 μU/mL (elevated)

**Interpretation:**
- Low total T due to low SHBG (insulin effect)
- Free T normal → not truly hypogonadal
- Treat metabolic syndrome, not testosterone

### Scenario 2: Elderly Man with Symptoms

**Labs:**
- Total T: 260 ng/dL (low)
- SHBG: 55 nmol/L (high)
- Free T: 5 pg/mL (low)

**Interpretation:**
- High SHBG (aging)
- Low total AND free T
- True hypogonadism → consider treatment

### Scenario 3: Man on Anticonvulsants

**Labs:**
- Total T: 320 ng/dL (low-normal)
- SHBG: 60 nmol/L (high)
- Free T: 6 pg/mL (low-normal)

**Interpretation:**
- Phenytoin induces hepatic enzymes
- Increases SHBG
- May lower bioavailable T
- Consider medication review

## Therapeutic Considerations

### Testosterone Replacement

**Effect on SHBG:**
- Exogenous testosterone suppresses SHBG
- Lower SHBG with therapy
- Total T rises more than expected

**Monitoring:**
- Measure total T on therapy
- Target mid-normal range
- SHBG less relevant once on therapy

### Weight Loss

**Effect:**
- Improves insulin sensitivity
- Increases SHBG
- Raises total testosterone
- May improve symptoms without TRT

**Clinical Strategy:**
- Address obesity before considering TRT
- Lifestyle intervention first
- May normalize hormones

## Summary

**Key Points:**
1. SHBG transports testosterone and estradiol
2. Only free hormone is bioactive
3. SHBG regulated by insulin (inverse), estrogens (positive)
4. Low SHBG in obesity/metabolic syndrome
5. High SHBG in aging, liver disease, hyperthyroidism
6. Always consider SHBG when interpreting total testosterone
7. Calculate free testosterone in discordant cases

**Clinical Pearl:** In obese men with low total testosterone, check SHBG before diagnosing hypogonadism — free testosterone may be normal.
