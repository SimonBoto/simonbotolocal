# METHAP Informed Consent Framework

## Purpose
Ensure participants understand the study, their rights, and protections before enrollment.

## Required Elements (GDPR + Helsinki Declaration)

### 1. Study Information
| Element | Content |
|---------|---------|
| **Title** | Metabolomic Markers of Early Insulin Resistance (METHAP) |
| **Purpose** | Identify early s using urine organic acids and plasma free fatty acids |
| **Phases** | Observational study + optional 12-week intervention |
| **Duration** | 42 months total; participant involvement varies by phase |

### 2. Procedures
**Observational Phase:**
- Blood draw (fasting glucose, HbA1c, lipid panel)
- Urine collection (organic acid analysis)
- Questionnaires (diet, lifestyle, medical history)

**Intervention Phase (subset):**
- Low-carbohydrate diet counseling
- Supplementation (, Magnesium, Omega-3, Glutamine, Vitamin C, Probiotics)
- Follow-up visits at 4, 8, 12 weeks

### 3. Risks and Discomforts
| Procedure | Risk | Mitigation |
|-----------|------|------------|
| Blood draw | Bruising, dizziness, pain | Trained phlebotomist, seated position |
| Diet change | GI adjustment | Gradual transition, dietitian support |
| Supplements | GI upset, interactions | EOΦ-approved products, monitoring |

### 4. Benefits
- **Direct:** Free metabolic testing, personalized dietary guidance
- **Indirect:** Contribution to scientific knowledge, potential improved IR detection
- **No guarantee:** Individual health outcomes not assured

### 5. Confidentiality and Data Protection
| Measure | Implementation |
|---------|----------------|
| **Anonymization** | Coded identifiers, no names in datasets |
| **Storage** | Encrypted servers, restricted access |
| **Retention** | 5 years post-study, then secure deletion |
| **GDPR rights** | Access, correction, deletion, portability |
| **Third parties** | No data sharing without consent |

### 6. Voluntary Participation
- **Free to decline:** No penalty for refusal
- **Withdrawal anytime:** No explanation required
- **Data deletion:** Upon withdrawal request (email/letter)
- **No impact:** On future medical care

### 10. Complaints Procedure
Participants may contact:
- Principal Investigator for study-related concerns
- Ethics Committee for ethical concerns
- Data Protection Officer for privacy concerns

## Consent Form Checklist

- [ ] Study purpose explained in plain language
- [ ] Procedures described clearly
- [ ] Risks and benefits outlined
- [ ] Confidentiality measures detailed
- [ ] Voluntary nature emphasized
- [ ] Withdrawal process explained
- [ ] Supplements listed with approvals
- [ ] Funding sources disclosed
- [ ] Contact information provided
- [ ] Participant questions answered
- [ ] Signature and date obtained
- [ ] Copy provided to participant

## Enhancement Recommendations

### Add Lay Summary at Beginning
> *"This study explores whether specific substances in your urine and blood can detect early signs of insulin resistance, a condition that can lead to diabetes. We are also testing whether a specific diet and supplements can improve metabolic health."*

### Clarify Randomization
> *"If you are in the intervention group, you were randomly assigned using computer-generated random numbers. Neither you nor the researchers chose your group."*

### Incidental Findings Protocol
> *"If we discover unexpected health issues in your test results, we will contact you to discuss findings and recommend appropriate follow-up with your physician."*
