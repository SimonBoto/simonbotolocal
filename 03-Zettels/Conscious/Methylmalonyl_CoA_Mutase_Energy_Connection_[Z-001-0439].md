# Methylmalonyl-CoA Mutase — Energy Connection

> Methylmalonyl-CoA mutase produces succinyl-CoA, a Krebs cycle intermediate, linking propionate  to cellular energy production with a net yield of ~12 ATP per propionate molecule.

## Reaction

**Conversion:**
```
L-Methylmalonyl-CoA → Succinyl-CoA
```

- Intramolecular rearrangement
- No oxidation state change
- Enters Krebs cycle directly

## Energy Yield from Propionate

**Cost:**
- 1 ATP (propionyl-CoA carboxylation)

**Yield (succinyl-CoA → CO₂):**
- 1 GTP (substrate-level)
- 1 FADH₂ (~1.5 ATP)
- 1 NADH (~2.5 ATP)
- Plus continuation through Krebs

**Total:** ~12 ATP per propionate

## Anaplerotic Function

**Definition:**
- Fills Krebs cycle intermediates
- Maintains cycle flux
- Supports gluconeogenesis

**Sources feeding in:**
- Propionate (gut bacteria)
- Odd-chain 
- Amino acids (Ile, Met, Thr, Val)

## Efficiency

**Good yield for 3-carbon molecule:**
- Compare: Glucose (~30-32 ATP, 6 carbons)
- Propionate (~12 ATP, 3 carbons)
- Per carbon: Propionate efficient
