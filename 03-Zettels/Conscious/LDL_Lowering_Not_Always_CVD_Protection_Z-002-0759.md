# LDL Lowering Not Always CVD Protection — Context Matters

## Core Claim
**LDL lowering does not always translate to cardiovascular protection** — the benefit depends on metabolic context, inflammation status, and baseline risk. In healthy individuals with low CAC scores, aggressive LDL lowering provides minimal benefit.

## The Evidence

### When LDL Lowering Works
| Context | Benefit |
|---------|---------|
| **Established CVD** | High — prevents recurrent events |
| **High CAC (>100)** | Clear benefit |
| **FH (genetic high LDL)** | Essential |
| **Diabetes + risks** | Moderate-high |

### When LDL Lowering Less Effective
| Context | Limited Benefit |
|---------|-----------------|
| **CAC = 0** | Minimal — no plaque to stabilize |
| **Metabolic syndrome** | Residual risk remains |
| **High inflammation** | Need anti-inflammatory approach |
| **Primary prevention, low risk** | NNT very high |

## Why Context Matters

### LDL Is Necessary But Not Sufficient
- LDL necessary for atherosclerosis
- But other factors drive progression:
  - Inflammation
  - Insulin resistance
  - Endothelial dysfunction

### Residual Risk
Even with LDL <50 mg/dL:
- **30-40%** of events still occur
- Other risk factors active
- Need comprehensive approach

## Clinical Implication

### Stratify By Risk
| Risk | Approach |
|------|----------|
| **High** | Aggressive LDL lowering |
| **Moderate** | Moderate + lifestyle |
| **Low** | Lifestyle first |

### Don't Chase Numbers
- LDL 70 vs 90 in low-risk: minimal difference
- Focus on **absolute risk reduction**
- Consider **patient preferences**
