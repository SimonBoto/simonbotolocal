# MAO Enzyme Framework

> **Core Claim:** MAO-A and MAO-B isoforms have distinct substrate specificities and tissue distributions, providing the basis for selective inhibitor therapy.

## Isoform Specificity

| Isoform | Primary Substrates | Clinical Relevance |
|---------|-------------------|-------------------|
| **MAO-A** | Serotonin, norepinephrine, epinephrine | Depression treatment |
| **MAO-B** | Dopamine, phenylethylamine | Parkinson's disease |

## Tissue Distribution

- **Brain:** Both isoforms in neurons and glia
- **Liver:** Major site of peripheral metabolism
- **Intestinal mucosa:** First-pass metabolism of dietary amines

## Location

Mitochondrial outer membrane — requires membrane permeability for drug access.

## Clinical Applications

| Condition | Target | Drug Class |
|-----------|--------|------------|
| Depression | MAO-A | MAO-A inhibitors (moclobemide) |
| Parkinson's | MAO-B | MAO-B inhibitors (selegiline, rasagiline) |
