# Insulin More Predictive of CVD Than Glucose

## Core Claim
**Elevated insulin levels are stronger independent predictors of cardiovascular mortality than elevated glucose levels** — challenging the glucose-centric model of diabetes complications and highlighting insulin resistance/hyperinsulinemia as the primary driver of cardiovascular disease.

## The Evidence

### Zhao 2019 Findings
| Comparison | Result |
|------------|--------|
| **Insulin vs. glucose** | Insulin more predictive |
| **Fasting insulin** | Strong CVD mortality association |
| **C-peptide** | Confirms insulin-driven risk |
| **Independent effect** | Even when glucose normal |

### The Disconnect
| Traditional View | Evidence |
|------------------|----------|
| High glucose causes CVD | Weak independent association |
| Lower glucose = lower risk | Trials show limited CVD benefit |
| A1c target paramount | Insulin status matters more |

## Why Insulin Matters More

### Direct Vascular Effects
| Mechanism | Result |
|-----------|--------|
| **Endothelial dysfunction** | Impaired vasodilation |
| **Sodium retention** | Hypertension |
| **Sympathetic activation** | Increased cardiac workload |
| **Smooth muscle proliferation** | Atherosclerosis |

### Metabolic Effects
| Effect | Consequence |
|--------|-------------|
| **Dyslipidemia** | High TG, low HDL |
| **Inflammation** | Pro-inflammatory state |
| **Pro-thrombotic** | Clotting tendency |

## Clinical Implications

### Assessment Priority
| Test | Priority |
|------|----------|
| **Fasting insulin** | High — assess insulin resistance |
| **HOMA-IR** | High — quantify resistance |
| **Glucose** | Moderate — less predictive alone |
| **HbA1c** | Moderate — glycemic control |

### Treatment Focus
| Instead of | Focus on |
|------------|----------|
| Glucose lowering alone | Insulin reduction |
| A1c targets | Insulin sensitivity |
| Metformin only | Metformin + lifestyle |

## Evidence

> "Zhao 2019 analysis demonstrates that insulin levels predict CVD mortality independent of glucose levels; patients with normal glucose but elevated insulin (pre-diabetes/insulin resistance) have higher CVD risk than previously recognized."
> — Zhao et al., 2019
