# Review Process Framework

> **Core Claim:** Structured review within 2-4 weeks ensures consistency and professionalism across journals.

## Timeline

| Phase | Duration | Notes |
|-------|----------|-------|
| **Standard** | 2-4 weeks | Most common |
| **Rapid review** | 1-2 weeks | Expedited for urgent findings |
| **Extension** | Variable | Request if needed |

## Review Structure

1. **Summary** — Brief overview of manuscript
2. **Major concerns** — Issues requiring revision
3. **Minor concerns** — Clarifications needed
4. **Recommendation** — Final decision

## Recommendation Categories

| Category | Meaning | Typical Outcome |
|----------|---------|-----------------|
| **Accept** | Publish as-is | Rare on first review |
| **Minor revision** | Small changes needed | ~40% of reviews |
| **Major revision** | Significant rework required | ~40% of reviews |
| **Reject** | Not suitable for journal | ~20% of reviews |

## Best Practices

- Be constructive, not destructive
- Cite specific line numbers
- Distinguish major vs minor issues
- Maintain professional tone
