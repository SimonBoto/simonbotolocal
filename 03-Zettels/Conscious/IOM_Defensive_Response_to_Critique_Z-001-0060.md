Despite mathematical proof of statistical error from Veugelers & Ekwaru (2014), the IOM (now National Academy of Medicine) maintained their 2010 calculations were "appropriate and based on available evidence." They cited  concerns (hypercalcemia risk) rather than addressing the statistical validity.

**IOM position:**
- Defended methodology despite clear mathematical error
- Emphasized safety concerns over statistical accuracy
- Did not revise RDA in subsequent updates
- Safety argument: hypercalcemia risk at higher intakes

**Problem with defense:**
- Safety concerns don't invalidate mathematics
- Corrected RDA (8,000 IU) is physiological (body produces 10,000-20,000 IU naturally)
- Institutional inertia prevents correction of high-impact errors

**Pattern:** Large institutions resist admitting errors affecting global policy, even when mathematically proven.
