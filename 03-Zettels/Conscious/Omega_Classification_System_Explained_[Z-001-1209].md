# Omega Classification System Explained

## Overview

The omega (ω) classification system categorizes fatty acids by the position of the first double bond from the methyl (omega) end. This system is nutritionally relevant because it predicts metabolic fate and physiological effects.

---

## The Three Main Classes

### Omega-3 (ω-3)
**First double bond:** Carbon 3 from methyl end
**Structure:** CH₃-CH₂-CH=CH-...

**Key Fatty Acids:**
- **ALA (α-linolenic acid):** 18:3, plant source
- **EPA (eicosapentaenoic acid):** 20:5, marine source
- **DHA (docosahexaenoic acid):** 22:6, marine source

**Physiological Effects:**
- Anti-inflammatory (resolvins, protectins)
- Cardiovascular protection
- Brain development (DHA)
- Reduced triglycerides

**Sources:**
- ALA: Flaxseeds, chia, walnuts
- EPA/DHA: Fatty fish (salmon, mackerel, sardines)

### [[KEYWORD-Omega_6|omega-6]] (ω-6)
**First double bond:** Carbon 6 from methyl end
**Structure:** CH₃-(CH₂)₄-CH=CH-...

**Key Fatty Acids:**
- **LA (linoleic acid):** 18:2, predominant dietary form
- **AA (arachidonic acid):** 20:4, converted from LA

**Physiological Effects:**
- Pro-inflammatory eicosanoids (from AA)
- Cell membrane structure
- Essential for skin and hair
- Necessary but excess problematic

**Sources:**
- Vegetable oils (corn, soybean, safflower)
- Nuts and seeds
- Poultry and eggs

### Omega-9 (ω-9)
**First double bond:** Carbon 9 from methyl end
**Structure:** CH₃-(CH₂)₇-CH=CH-...

**Key Fatty Acids:**
- **Oleic acid:** 18:1, most abundant dietary fat

**Physiological Effects:**
- Cardiovascular protection (when replacing saturated)
- Improved [[KEYWORD-Insulin|insulin]] sensitivity
- Reduced [[KEYWORD-Inflammation|inflammation]]

**Sources:**
- Olive oil (75% oleic acid)
- Canola oil
- Avocado
- Almonds

**Note:** Omega-9 is non-essential (body can synthesize)

---

## The Omega-6:Omega-3 Ratio

### Historical Perspective
**Evolutionary ratio:** ~1:1 to 2:1

**Modern diet ratio:** 15:1 to 20:1

**Why the shift:**
- Industrial vegetable oils (high ω-6)
- Grain-fed livestock (higher ω-6)
- Decreased fish consumption

### Health Implications
**High ω-6:ω-3 ratio associated with:**
- Chronic inflammation
- Cardiovascular disease
- Autoimmune disorders
- Depression

**Target ratio:** 4:1 or lower

### Practical Recommendations

**Reduce ω-6:**
- Limit vegetable oils
- Choose grass-fed meat
- Avoid processed foods

**Increase ω-3:**
- Eat fatty fish 2-3x/week
- Consider fish oil supplement
- Add ground flaxseed

## Metabolic Interconversion

### Omega-3 Pathway
```
ALA (18:3) → EPA (20:5) → DHA (22:6)
     ↓
Limited conversion (~5-10%)
```

### Omega-6 Pathway
```
LA (18:2) → GLA (18:3) → DGLA (20:3) → AA (20:4)
     ↓
Better conversion than ω-3
```

**Competition:**
- Same enzymes (Δ6-desaturase, elongase)
- High LA can impair ALA conversion
- Another reason to balance ratio

---

## Clinical Applications

### Inflammation Management
- Increase ω-3 (anti-inflammatory)
- Decrease ω-6 (pro-inflammatory precursors)

### Cardiovascular Disease
- EPA/DHA supplementation
- Replace saturated with omega-9

### Neurological Development
- DHA critical in pregnancy/infancy
- Breast milk rich in DHA
