# PLP — Vitamin B6 Pyridoxal Phosphate Cofactor

## Overview

Pyridoxal phosphate (PLP) is the active form of vitamin B6. It's the most versatile cofactor in amino acid [[KEYWORD-Metabolism|metabolism]], involved in transamination, decarboxylation, and racemization reactions.

---

## PLP Structure

**Components:**
- Pyridine ring (aromatic)
- Aldehyde group (forms Schiff base)
- Phosphate group (enzyme binding)
- Hydroxymethyl group

**Reactive Site:**
- Aldehyde carbon (C4)
- Forms Schiff base with amino groups

---

## [[KEYWORD-Mechanism|mechanism]] of Action

### Schiff Base Formation
```
PLP (aldehyde) + Enzyme lysine → Aldimine (Schiff base)
```

### Substrate Binding
```
Amino acid substrate displaces enzyme lysine
Forms substrate-PLP aldimine
```

### Electronic Rearrangement
```
Electron withdrawal by pyridine nitrogen
Stabilizes carbanion intermediates
Facilitates bond cleavage
```

### Reaction Types
Depending on bond broken:
- **Transamination:** α-H transferred
- **Decarboxylation:** CO₂ released
- **Racemization:** α-H replaced
- **Side chain cleavage:** β-bond broken

---

## PLP-Dependent Enzymes

### 1. Amino Acid Decarboxylases

**Glutamate Decarboxylase (GAD):**
```
Glutamate → GABA + CO₂
```
- Major inhibitory neurotransmitter
- GAD antibodies in type 1 diabetes

**Aromatic Amino Acid Decarboxylase (AADC):**
```
L-DOPA → Dopamine
5-HTP → Serotonin
Tryptophan → Tryptamine
```
- Critical for catecholamine/serotonin synthesis

**Histidine Decarboxylase:**
```
Histidine → Histamine + CO₂
```
- Immune response, gastric acid

### 2. Transaminases (Aminotransferases)

**Aspartate Aminotransferase (AST):**
```
Aspartate + α-ketoglutarate ↔ Oxaloacetate + Glutamate
```

**Alanine Aminotransferase (ALT):**
```
Alanine + α-ketoglutarate ↔ Pyruvate + Glutamate
```

**Clinical:** AST/ALT markers of liver damage

### 3. Other Important Enzymes

**Glycogen Phosphorylase:**
- Glycogen breakdown
- Only non-amino acid PLP enzyme

**Cystathionine β-Synthase (CBS):**
```
Homocysteine + Serine → Cystathionine
```
- Transsulfuration pathway
- Deficiency → homocystinuria

**Serine Hydroxymethyltransferase (SHMT):**
```
Serine + THF → Glycine + 5,10-CH₂-THF
```
- One-carbon metabolism

## Vitamin B6 Deficiency

### Causes
- Malnutrition (rare in isolation)
- Alcoholism
- Isoniazid therapy (TB treatment)
- Oral contraceptives
- Malabsorption

### Clinical Features

**Dermatological:**
- Seborrheic dermatitis
- Glossitis (magenta tongue)
- Cheilosis

**Neurological:**
- Peripheral neuropathy
- Depression, confusion
- Seizures (infants)

**Hematological:**
- Sideroblastic anemia
- Iron overload in [[KEYWORD-Mitochondria|mitochondria]]

**Immunological:**
- Impaired immune function

### Isoniazid-Induced Deficiency
**Mechanism:** INH forms hydrazone with PLP → inactivation
**Prevention:** B6 supplementation with INH therapy

---

## Laboratory Assessment

### Direct Measurement
- Plasma PLP (best indicator)
- Urinary 4-pyridoxic acid (metabolite)
- Whole blood B6

### Functional Tests
- Erythrocyte transaminase activation
- Tryptophan load test

---

## Therapeutic Uses

### Deficiency Treatment
- Pyridoxine 50-100 mg/day
- Higher doses for INH-induced

### Specific Indications
- **Sideroblastic anemia:** High-dose B6
- **Homocystinuria:** B6-responsive (some cases)
- **Premenstrual syndrome:** Controversial
- **Morning sickness:** Doxylamine-pyridoxine

### Toxicity
- Sensory neuropathy at high doses (>200 mg/day long-term)
- Reversible on discontinuation
