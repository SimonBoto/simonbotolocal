# Rise of Seed Oils in US Diet — Government and Industry Role

> The transformation of American dietary fat from animal-based to vegetable oil-based resulted from a convergence of industry marketing, scientific claims about saturated fat, and government dietary guidelines that collectively shifted consumption patterns with potential long-term metabolic consequences.

## Timeline of Transformation

| Year | Event | Impact |
|------|-------|--------|
| **1911** | Crisco introduced | Seed oil enters mainstream |
| **1940s** | WWII rationing | Shortages of butter/lard |
| **1958-64** | Seven Countries Study | "Scientific" basis for anti-fat |
| **1977** | McGovern Committee | Dietary Goals for US (low-fat) |
| **1992** | USDA Food Pyramid | Visual low-fat message |
| **2000s** | Trans fat concerns | Seed oils questioned |
| **2010s+** | Reassessment | Fat quality > quantity |

## Major Industry Players

**Agricultural Commodity Companies:**
- **Archer Daniels Midland (ADM):** Soybean processing
- **Cargill:** Diverse seed oils
- **Bunge:** Soy, canola oils
- **Louis Dreyfus:** Global trading

**Food Manufacturers:**
- **Procter & Gamble:** Crisco
- **Unilever:** Margarines, spreads
- **Kraft:** Processed foods with seed oils
- **Nestle:** Global food products

**Economic Incentives:**
- Seed oils cheaper than animal fats
- Abundant raw materials (soy, corn, cotton)
- Long shelf life
- Mass production scalable

## Government Role

**USDA Dietary Guidelines:**
- **1977:** Dietary Goals for the United States
  - Reduce total fat to 30% calories
  - Reduce saturated fat to 10%
  - Increase carbohydrates to 55-60%

**Food Pyramid (1992):**
- Base: 6-11 servings grains (promotes carbs)
- Top: Fats "sparingly"
- Implicit: Vegetable oils acceptable
- Result: Increased grain/seed oil consumption

**Unintended Consequences:**
- Fat reduction → Carbohydrate increase
- Seed oils replaced saturated fats
- Processed food industry boomed
- Obesity/diabetes rates rose
