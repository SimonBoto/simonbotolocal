# MGP — Matrix Gla Protein Function

## Core Function
Matrix Gla Protein (MGP) is the strongest natural inhibitor of vascular calcification.

## Synthesis & Activation
- Produced by vascular smooth muscle cells
- Vitamin D upregulates gene expression
- Requires vitamin K-dependent carboxylation of 5 glutamic acid residues to form Gla domains
- Creates calcium-binding domains for inhibition

## Mechanism of Protection
1. **Crystal binding:** Binds nascent calcium crystals
2. **Growth inhibition:** Prevents crystal propagation
3. **BMP-2 antagonism:** Blocks osteogenic signaling
4. **Smooth muscle protection:** Maintains contractile phenotype

## When MGP Fails
- Vitamin K deficiency (insufficient carboxylation)
- Overwhelming calcium load
- Chronic inflammation
- Result: Unopposed calcification

## Clinical Significance
- MGP activation is critical for preventing pathological arterial calcification
- Explains the "calcium paradox" — calcium can strengthen bone or damage arteries depending on directional control
- METHAP's high-dose D protocol requires K2 to maximize MGP activation and provide vascular protection
