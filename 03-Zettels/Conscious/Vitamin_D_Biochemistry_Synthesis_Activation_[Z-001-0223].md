# [[KEYWORD-Vitamin_D|vitamin d]] Biochemistry — Synthesis, Activation, and VDR Mechanisms

> Vitamin D functions as a steroid hormone with complex synthesis, activation, and genomic signaling mechanisms that regulate metabolic health through widespread tissue expression of the vitamin D receptor.

## Synthesis and Activation Pathway

### Step 1: Cutaneous Synthesis
**Photochemical Reaction:**
- **Substrate:** 7-dehydrocholesterol in skin
- **Trigger:** UVB radiation (290-315 nm)
- **Product:** Pre-vitamin D3 → thermal isomerization → Cholecalciferol (D3)
- **Capacity:** 10,000-20,000 IU generated in 15-30 minutes (full body exposure)
- **Self-regulation:** Photodegradation prevents toxicity from sunlight

**Factors Affecting Synthesis:**
| Factor | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| **Latitude** | Reduced >35°N in winter |
| **Skin pigmentation** | Melanin reduces synthesis 50-99% |
| **Age** | 70% reduction by age 70 |
| **Sunscreen (SPF 30)** | Reduces synthesis 95% |
| **Time of day** | Peak 10am-3pm |

### Step 2: Hepatic 25-Hydroxylation
**Enzyme:** CYP2R1 (primary), CYP27A1
**Location:** Liver
**Product:** 25-hydroxyvitamin D [25(OH)D] — calcidiol
**Function:** Main circulating form, clinical status marker
**Half-life:** 2-3 weeks
**Cofactor:** Magnesium required

### Step 3: Renal 1α-Hydroxylation
**Enzyme:** CYP27B1
**Location:** Kidney (proximal tubule)
**Product:** 1,25-dihydroxyvitamin D [1,25(OH)₂D] — calcitriol
**Function:** Active hormonal form
**Half-life:** 4-6 hours
**Regulation:** Stimulated by PTH, inhibited by calcitriol (negative feedback)
**Cofactor:** Magnesium required

## Vitamin D Receptor (VDR)

### Structure and Function
- **Type:** Nuclear receptor superfamily (steroid hormone receptor)
- **Mechanism:** Ligand-activated transcription factor
- **Genomic effects:** Binds VDR response elements (VDREs) in target genes
- **Non-genomic effects:** Rapid membrane signaling via second messengers

### Tissue Distribution
**Metabolically Relevant Tissues:**
- **Pancreatic β-cells:** [[KEYWORD-Insulin|insulin]] synthesis and secretion
- **Skeletal muscle:** Glucose uptake, insulin sensitivity
- **Adipose tissue:** Adipogenesis, inflammation, adipokine production
- **Immune cells:** Macrophages, T-cells, dendritic cells
- **Liver:** Lipid [[KEYWORD-Metabolism|metabolism]], glucose production
- **Cardiovascular:** Endothelial cells, vascular smooth muscle

### Target Genes (>1000 regulated)
**Metabolic Pathways:**
- Insulin signaling components
- Glucose transporters (GLUT4)
- Lipid metabolism enzymes
- Inflammatory cytokines
- Renin (negative regulation)

## Regulation of Vitamin D Metabolism

### Stimulators of 1α-Hydroxylation
- **Parathyroid hormone (PTH):** Primary stimulator (low Ca²⁺)
- **Low serum calcium:** Via PTH
- **Low serum phosphate:** Direct stimulation
- **Estrogen:** Increases CYP27B1 activity
- **Prolactin:** During pregnancy/lactation

### Inhibitors
- **Calcitriol:** Negative feedback on CYP27B1
- **Fibroblast growth factor-23 (FGF-23):** From bone, suppresses CYP27B1
- **High calcium:** Suppresses PTH, reduces 1α-hydroxylation

### Catabolism
**Enzyme:** CYP24A1 (24-hydroxylase)
- Inactivates both 25(OH)D and 1,25(OH)₂D
- Upregulated by calcitriol (self-limiting)
- Creates 24,25(OH)₂D and calcitroic acid (excreted)

## METHAP Context

**High-Dose Cholecalciferol Strategy:**
- Bypasses cutaneous synthesis limitation
- Provides substrate for 25-hydroxylation
- Raises 25(OH)D to therapeutic levels (>40-50 ng/mL)
- Requires adequate Mg for activation

**Monitoring:**
- **25(OH)D:** Primary status marker (target >40 ng/mL)
- **1,25(OH)₂D:** Active hormone (variable, less useful)
- **Mg:** Cofactor for activation enzymes
