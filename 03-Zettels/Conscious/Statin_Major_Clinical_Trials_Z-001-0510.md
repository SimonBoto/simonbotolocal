# Statin Major  Trials

> Landmark trials established statin efficacy in secondary and primary prevention, demonstrating consistent cardiovascular event and mortality reductions.

## Secondary Prevention

**4S (1994)**
- 4,444 CHD patients, simvastatin 20-40 mg
- **Result:** Mortality ↓ 30%, CHD mortality ↓ 35%
- **Significance:** First mortality benefit

**CARE (1996)**
- Post-MI, average , pravastatin 40 mg
- **Result:** Fatal CHD/MI ↓ 24%
- **Significance:** Benefit at average cholesterol

**LIPID (1998)**
- 9,014 CHD patients, pravastatin 40 mg
- **Result:** Total mortality ↓ 22%, stroke ↓ 19%

**TNT (2005)**
- Stable CHD, atorvastatin 80 vs 10 mg
- **Result:** Events ↓ 22% with intensive therapy
- **Significance:** "Lower is better"

## Primary Prevention

**WOSCOPS (1995)**
- 6,595 men, no MI, pravastatin 40 mg
- **Result:** Nonfatal MI ↓ 31%, mortality ↓ 22%
- **Significance:** First primary prevention benefit

**JUPITER (2008)**
- 17,802 healthy, LDL <130, CRP ≥2, rosuvastatin 20 mg
- **Result:** Events ↓ 44%, stroke ↓ 48%
- **Significance:** -based indication

## Meta-Analysis

**CTT Collaboration**
- 170,000 participants, 26 trials
- Each 40 mg/dL LDL reduction = 20-25% event reduction
- Consistent across populations
