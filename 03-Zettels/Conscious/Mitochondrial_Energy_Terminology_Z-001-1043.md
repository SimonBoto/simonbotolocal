## Core Claim
**Thirteen essential terms — Mitochondria, ATP, Krebs Cycle, Glycolysis, OXPHOS, NAD+/NADH, Beta-Oxidation, and Ketosis — describe the cellular energy economy, where mitochondria function not merely as power plants but as regulatory hubs integrating metabolism, signaling, and cell fate decisions.**

## Evidence

### Core Definitions (13 Terms)
| Term | Definition | Clinical Relevance |
|------|------------|-------------------|
| **Mitochondria** | Energy-producing organelles | Decision centers, not just furnaces |
| **ATP** | Universal energy currency | Energy status indicator |
| **Krebs Cycle** | Central metabolic hub | Biosynthetic and catabolic crossroads |
| **Glycolysis** | Cytoplasmic glucose metabolism | Anaerobic energy, Warburg effect |
| **OXPHOS** | Oxidative phosphorylation | ATP synthesis via electron transport |
| **NAD+/NADH** | Redox electron carrier | Metabolic state indicator |
| **Beta-Oxidation** | Fatty acid breakdown | Ketone production, energy source |
| **Ketosis** | Ketone body metabolism | Alternative fuel, therapeutic state |

### Mitochondrial Functions Beyond ATP
| Function | Mechanism | Clinical Importance |
|----------|-----------|---------------------|
| **Calcium signaling** | Uptake/release regulation | Excitation-contraction, apoptosis |
| **ROS signaling** | Controlled free radical release | Redox signaling, immune function |
| **Apoptosis regulation** | Cytochrome c release | Cell death control |
| **Steroid synthesis** | Side chain cleavage | Hormone production |
| **Thermogenesis** | Uncoupling proteins | Brown fat, metabolic rate |

### Energy Production Summary
| Process | Location | ATP Yield | Oxygen Required |
|---------|----------|-----------|-----------------|
| **Glycolysis** | Cytoplasm | 2 ATP | No |
| **Krebs Cycle** | Mitochondria | 2 ATP (GTP) | Indirect |
| **OXPHOS** | Inner membrane | ~28 ATP | Yes |
| **Beta-oxidation** | Mitochondria | Generates acetyl-CoA | Yes |

### Clinical Markers
| Marker | Indicates | Deficiency |
|--------|-----------|------------|
| Elevated lactate | Anaerobic shift | OXPHOS impairment |
| Elevated pyruvate | PDH dysfunction | B1, B2, B3, B5, Mg |
| Low ketones | Beta-oxidation block | Carnitine, cofactors |
| Elevated citrate | Aconitase inhibition | Oxidative stress |

## Clinical Implication
- **Fatigue assessment:** Mitochondrial dysfunction often underlies unexplained fatigue
- **Cognitive function:** Brain highly dependent on mitochondrial ATP
- **Exercise capacity:** OXPHOS efficiency determines endurance
- **Aging:** Mitochondrial decline central to aging biology
- **METHAP relevance:** Mitochondrial function is core to metabolic syndrome, insulin resistance, and aging research
