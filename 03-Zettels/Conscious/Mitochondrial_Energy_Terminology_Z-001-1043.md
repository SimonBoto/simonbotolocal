# Mitochondrial Energy Terminology

Energy production vocabulary for metabolomics practice: Mitochondria, ATP, Krebs Cycle, Glycolysis, OXPHOS, NAD+/NADH, Beta-Oxidation, Ketosis. These 13 terms define cellular energy metabolism.

## Evidence
> "Mitochondria are not just power plants—they're signaling hubs, metabolic regulators, and determinants of health and disease." (Mitochondrial biology)

## Core Definitions

### Mitochondria
**Definition:** Organelles responsible for ATP production through oxidative metabolism.

**Key features:**
- Double membrane structure
- Own DNA (maternal inheritance)
- Dynamic (fission, fusion, mitophagy)
- Beyond energy: calcium buffering, apoptosis, signaling

**Clinical relevance:** Mitochondrial dysfunction underlies fatigue, neurodegeneration, metabolic disease.

### ATP (Adenosine Triphosphate)
**Definition:** Primary energy currency of the cell.

**Structure:** Adenine + ribose + 3 phosphate groups

**Energy release:** ATP → ADP + Pi releases ~7.3 kcal/mol

**Daily production:** ~50-75 kg ATP/day (recycled constantly)

**Clinical relevance:** ATP availability determines cellular function.

### Krebs Cycle (TCA Cycle, Citric Acid Cycle)
**Definition:** Central metabolic pathway oxidizing acetyl-CoA to CO₂, producing NADH and FADH₂.

**Location:** Mitochondrial matrix

**Inputs:** Acetyl-CoA (from carbs, fats, proteins)

**Outputs per acetyl-CoA:**
- 3 NADH
- 1 FADH₂
- 1 GTP (ATP)
- 2 CO₂

**Clinical relevance:** Krebs cycle integrity essential for energy production.

### Glycolysis
**Definition:** Cytoplasmic pathway converting glucose to pyruvate.

**Location:** Cytosol (no mitochondria needed)

**Yield:** 2 ATP, 2 NADH per glucose

**Fates of pyruvate:**
| Condition | Product | Location |
|-----------|---------|----------|
| **Aerobic** | Acetyl-CoA → Krebs | Mitochondria |
| **Anaerobic** | Lactate | Cytosol |

**Clinical relevance:** Glycolysis without mitochondria yields little ATP.

### OXPHOS (Oxidative Phosphorylation)
**Definition:** Process generating ATP using electron transport and proton gradient.

**Components:**
| Complex | Function |
|---------|----------|
| **I** | NADH oxidation |
| **II** | FADH₂ oxidation |
| **III** | Electron transfer |
| **IV** | Oxygen reduction |
| **V (ATP synthase)** | ATP production |

**Yield:** ~28-34 ATP per glucose (vs. 2 from glycolysis alone)

**Clinical relevance:** OXPHOS dysfunction → severe energy deficit.

### NAD+/NADH
**Definition:** Electron carrier pair (oxidized/reduced).

**Function:** Transfers electrons from metabolic pathways to ETC

**NAD+ roles:**
- Glycolysis
- Krebs cycle
- Oxidative metabolism
- Sirtuin activation (longevity)

**Clinical relevance:** NAD+ decline with age; supplementation may help.

### Beta-Oxidation
**Definition:** Mitochondrial breakdown of fatty acids to acetyl-CoA.

**Process:** Sequential removal of 2-carbon units

**Yield:** ~122 ATP per palmitate (16:0)

**Requirement:** Carnitine shuttle for transport into mitochondria

**Clinical relevance:** Primary fuel during fasting, endurance exercise.

### Ketosis
**Definition:** Elevated ketone bodies from fatty acid oxidation.

**Ketones:** Acetoacetate, β-hydroxybutyrate (BHB), acetone

**Inducers:** Fasting, low-carbohydrate diet, prolonged exercise

**Clinical relevance:** Alternative fuel for brain; indicates metabolic flexibility.

## Additional Key Terms

### Electron Transport Chain (ETC)
**Definition:** Series of protein complexes transferring electrons to oxygen, pumping protons.

### Proton Gradient
**Definition:** Electrochemical gradient driving ATP synthesis.

### Mitochondrial Membrane Potential
**Definition:** Electrical potential across inner membrane; indicator of function.

### Mitochondrial Biogenesis
**Definition:** Creation of new mitochondria (stimulated by exercise, cold, fasting).

### Mitophagy
**Definition:** Selective degradation of damaged mitochondria.

### Reactive Oxygen Species (ROS)
**Definition:** Byproducts of OXPHOS; signaling molecules at low levels, damaging at high.

### Uncoupling
**Definition:** Dissipation of proton gradient without ATP production (generates heat).

## Clinical Integration

### Energy Production Hierarchy
| Pathway | ATP Yield | Conditions |
|---------|-----------|------------|
| **OXPHOS** | High | Aerobic, mitochondria functional |
| **Glycolysis** | Low | Anaerobic, mitochondria impaired |
| **Ketosis** | Moderate | Fasting, low carbohydrate |

### Mitochondrial Dysfunction Patterns
| Marker | Elevation Indicates |
|--------|---------------------|
| **Lactate** | Anaerobic shift |
| **Pyruvate** | Glycolytic backup |
| **Citrate** | Krebs impairment |
| **8-OHdG** | Oxidative damage |
