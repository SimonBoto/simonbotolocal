# Kellogg Company Founding — Mass Production (1906)

> Will Keith Kellogg founded the Kellogg Company in 1906 to mass-produce cornflakes, transforming a Sanitarium health food into a global consumer product and rizing the American breakfast through innovative marketing and industrial-scale production.

## Founding (1906)

**Company Name:**
- Original: Battle Creek Toasted Corn Flake Company
- Later: Kellogg Company

**Founder:** Will Keith Kellogg (age 46)
**Investment:** Personal savings
**Vision:** Health food for the masses

**Motivation:**
- Saw commercial potential brother missed
- Believed in product
- Wanted independence from John Harvey
- Business opportunity

## Production Innovations

**Manufacturing:**
- First: Hand-operated equipment
- Scaled: Automated rollers
- Toasting: Conveyor ovens
- Packaging: Sealed boxes
- Quality: Consistent product

**Scale:**
- 1906: Local production
- 1910s: National distribution
- 1920s: International markets
- Continuous expansion

## Marketing Innovations

**"The Original Bears This Signature":**
- W.K. Kellogg's personal signature on boxes
- Authenticity guarantee
- Brand trust
- Still used today

**"Breakfast is the Most Important Meal":**
- Invented by Kellogg marketing (not science)
- Promoted cereal consumption
- Embedded in culture
- Persists today

**Other Innovations:**
- First  claims on packaging
- Cartoon mascots (later)
- Premiums (toys in boxes)
- Recipe booklets
- Radio advertising

## Breakfast Revolution

**Before Kellogg:**
- Heavy breakfast (eggs, meat, pancakes)
- Time-consuming preparation
- Regional variations
- Home-cooked

**After Kellogg:**
- Light, quick cereal
- Pour milk, eat immediately
- Standardized nationwide
- Industrial product

**Cultural Impact:**
- "Convenience" prioritized
- Industrial food acceptance
- Marketing-driven eating
- Breakfast category created

## Competition and Industry

**Rivals:**
- C.W. Post (Post Toasties)
- Copycats
- Patent battles
- Market share wars

**Industry Growth:**
- Dozens of cereal companies
- Battle Creek = "Cereal Capital"
- Innovation (puffed rice, wheat, etc.)
- Sugar-added varieties (later)

## Legacy

**Company Today:**
- $13+ billion annual revenue
- Global presence
- Multiple brands
- Breakfast, snacks, plant-based

**Philanthropy:**
- W.K. Kellogg Foundation (1930)
- $ billions in grants
- Education, health, food security
- Independent of company

**Cultural:**
- Breakfast cereal = American institution
- Global influence
- Convenience food paradigm
- Marketing model
