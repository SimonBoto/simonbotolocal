# Cholesterol Synthesis — Mevalonate Pathway

## Core Claim
HMG-CoA reductase is the rate-limiting enzyme in cholesterol synthesis, subject to multi-level regulation (feedback inhibition, SREBP-2 transcriptional control, and statin blockade), making it the primary therapeutic target for reducing endogenous cholesterol production.

## Evidence

### The Mevalonate Pathway

```
Acetyl-CoA (2C) → Acetoacetyl-CoA (4C) → HMG-CoA (6C)
    ↓ HMG-CoA reductase (RATE-LIMITING)
Mevalonate (6C) → IPP → DMAPP
    ↓
Geranyl-PP → Farnesyl-PP → Squalene (30C)
    ↓
Lanosterol → Cholesterol (27C)
```

### Key Enzymes and Regulation

| Enzyme | Function | Regulatory Mechanism |
|--------|----------|---------------------|
| **HMG-CoA reductase** | Converts HMG-CoA → mevalonate | Feedback inhibition by cholesterol; SREBP-2 activation; statin blockade |
| **Squalene synthase** | First committed step to sterols | Transcriptional regulation; alternative drug target |
| **LDL receptor** | Cholesterol uptake from circulation | SREBP-2 mediated; upregulated when synthesis inhibited |

### Quantitative Aspects

| Parameter | Value | Clinical Significance |
|-----------|-------|----------------------|
| Endogenous synthesis | ~50% of total cholesterol | Compensatory increase when dietary intake low |
| Statin LDL reduction | 30-60% | Dose-dependent; varies by statin potency |
| HMG-CoA reductase turnover | ~4 hours | Allows rapid response to cellular cholesterol |

## Clinical Implication

**Statin Therapy Optimization:**
- Statins competitively inhibit HMG-CoA reductase, reducing cholesterol synthesis
- Compensatory upregulation of hepatic LDL receptors increases LDL clearance
- Evening dosing may enhance efficacy (peak cholesterol synthesis occurs overnight)
- Combination with ezetimibe (blocks absorption) provides additive benefit

**Monitoring:**
- Check lipid panel 4-12 weeks after initiation or dose change
- ALT/CK only if symptomatic (myalgia, weakness)
- Consider CoQ10 supplementation (mevalonate pathway also produces CoQ10)

## METHAP Relevance

**Metabolic Health Application:**
- Cholesterol synthesis assessment via OAT markers (mevalonate, HMG metabolites)
- Statin intolerance: Alternative targets (squalene synthase inhibitors in development)
- Integrated approach: Address insulin resistance to reduce endogenous VLDL production
