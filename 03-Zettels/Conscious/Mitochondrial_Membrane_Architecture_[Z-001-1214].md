# [[KEYWORD-Mitochondria|mitochondria]]l Membrane Architecture — Double Membrane System

## Overview

Mitochondria are unique organelles enclosed by two distinct membranes — the outer and inner membranes. This double-membrane architecture creates distinct compartments essential for energy production and metabolic regulation.

---

## The Outer Membrane

### Structure
- **Appearance:** Smooth, continuous layer
- **Composition:** Phospholipid bilayer with embedded proteins
- **Permeability:** Permeable to small molecules (<5 kDa)

### Key Components: Porins
**What are porins?**
- Beta-barrel proteins forming channels
- Allow passive diffusion of ions and small molecules

**Function:**
```
Cytoplasm ↔ Intermembrane Space
- Ions (Na+, K+, Ca2+)
- Small metabolites
- ATP/ADP (regulated)
```

**Significance:**
- Maintains communication with cytoplasm
- Regulates metabolite exchange
- Does NOT require active transport for small molecules

---

## The Inner Membrane

### Structure
- **Appearance:** Extensively folded (cristae)
- **Composition:** Protein-rich (70-80% protein by weight)
- **Permeability:** Highly impermeable — requires specific transporters

### Key Features

**1. Cristae**
- Infoldings that increase surface area 5-10x
- House ETC complexes and ATP synthase
- Critical for oxidative phosphorylation capacity

**2. Protein Complexes Embedded:**
| Complex | Function |
|---------|----------|
| ETC Complex I | NADH dehydrogenase |
| ETC Complex II | Succinate dehydrogenase |
| ETC Complex III | Cytochrome bc1 |
| ETC Complex IV | Cytochrome c oxidase |
| ATP Synthase | ATP production |
| Transport proteins | Metabolite exchange |

---

## The Intermembrane Space (IMS)

### Location
Between outer and inner membranes

### Function
**1. Proton Reservoir**
```
ETC pumps H+ from matrix → IMS
Creates electrochemical gradient
Drives ATP synthesis
```

**2. Cytochrome c Storage**
- Located in IMS
- Critical for ETC electron transfer
- Apoptosis trigger when released

**3. Metabolite Exchange Hub**
- Pyruvate import
- ATP/ADP exchange
- Metabolite shuttling

---

## Membrane Comparison

| Feature | Outer Membrane | Inner Membrane |
|---------|----------------|----------------|
| **Permeability** | High (porins) | Low (selective) |
| **Surface Area** | Smooth, limited | Folded (cristae), extensive |
| **Protein Content** | ~50% | ~70-80% |
| **Function** | Permeability barrier | Energy production machinery |
| **Transport** | Passive diffusion | Active transport required |

---

## Clinical Relevance

### [[KEYWORD-Mitochondria|mitochondrial]] Disorders
**Outer membrane damage:**
- Release of cytochrome c → apoptosis
- Disrupted metabolite exchange

**Inner membrane dysfunction:**
- Impaired ETC function
- Reduced ATP production
- Metabolic disorders

### Therapeutic Targets
- **BCL-2 family:** Regulate outer membrane permeability
- **Uncouplers:** Target inner membrane proton gradient
- **Mitochondrial transplantation:** Replace damaged membranes

---

## Summary

**The double membrane is not redundant — it's functional specialization:**
- **Outer:** Permeable gatekeeper
- **Inner:** Impermeable energy factory
- **Space Between:** Proton reservoir and metabolite hub
