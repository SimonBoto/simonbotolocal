## Core Claim
**Suberate (C8, octanedioic acid) and sebacate (C10, decanedioic acid) are formed through ω-oxidation of medium-chain fatty acids when mitochondrial β-oxidation is impaired — with suberate indicating C8-C10 fatty acid metabolism issues and sebacate reflecting more severe impairment of C10-C12 fatty acid oxidation, providing complementary biomarker information to adipate in metabolic dysfunction assessment.**

## Evidence

### Dicarboxylic Acid Profiles
| Acid | Carbons | Source Fatty Acids | Clinical Significance |
|------|---------|-------------------|----------------------|
| **Adipate** | C6 | C12-C16 incomplete oxidation | Most common marker |
| **Suberate** | C8 | C8-C10 ω-oxidation | Medium-chain issues |
| **Sebacate** | C10 | C10-C12 ω-oxidation | Severe impairment |

### Formation Pathways
| Step | Suberate (C8) | Sebacate (C10) |
|------|---------------|----------------|
| **Precursor** | Caprylic acid (C8:0) | Capric acid (C10:0) |
| **ω-hydroxylation** | CYP4A11 | CYP4A11 |
| **Oxidation** | → Suberic acid | → Sebacic acid |
| **Alternative** | C10 incomplete β-ox | C12 incomplete β-ox |

### Clinical Interpretation
| Pattern | Interpretation | Common Causes |
|---------|---------------|---------------|
| **Adipate only ↑** | Mild β-oxidation impairment | Early insulin resistance |
| **Adipate + Suberate ↑** | Moderate impairment | Carnitine deficiency |
| **All three ↑↑** | Severe β-oxidation block | MCAD deficiency, fasting |
| **Suberate > Adipate** | Medium-chain specific issue | Dietary C8-C10 excess |

### Normal vs Elevated Levels
| Marker | Normal | Mild | Significant |
|--------|--------|------|-------------|
| **Suberate** | <1 mmol/mol creat | 1-3 mmol/mol | >3 mmol/mol |
| **Sebacate** | Undetectable | Trace | >1 mmol/mol |

## Clinical Implication
- **Complementary markers:** Suberate/sebacate add specificity to adipate
- **Chain-length specificity:** Different acids indicate different blocks
- **Severity indicator:** Sebacate elevation suggests significant impairment
- **Differential diagnosis:** Pattern helps identify specific defects
- **METHAP relevance:** Multi-marker approach improves insulin resistance detection sensitivity
