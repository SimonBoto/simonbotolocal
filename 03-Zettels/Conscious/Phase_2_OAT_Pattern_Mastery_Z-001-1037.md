## Core Claim
**Weeks 3-4 develop pattern recognition in organic acid testing by teaching category-based interpretation — markers come in flocks, never alone — enabling trainees to "feel" metabolomics as a language rather than decode it as a cipher.**

## Evidence

### Category-Based Approach
| Category | Markers | Clinical Meaning |
|----------|---------|------------------|
| **Mitochondrial** | Lactate, pyruvate, citrate, α-KG | Energy production status |
| **Detoxification** | 2-methylhippurate, orotate, glucarate | Phase I/II capacity |
| **B-vitamin** | Methylmalonate, FIGLU, pyruvate | Cofactor sufficiency |
| **Neurotransmitter** | HVA, VMA, 5-HIAA | Catecholamine/serotonin metabolism |
| **Gut microbial** | Hippurate, phenylacetate, indican | Dysbiosis signatures |
| **Fatty acid** | Adipate, suberate, ethylmalonate | Beta-oxidation status |

### Key Principle: Markers Come in Flocks
| Isolated Marker | Pattern | Interpretation |
|-----------------|---------|----------------|
| Elevated lactate | + pyruvate, ↓citrate | Mitochondrial dysfunction |
| Elevated methylmalonate | + FIGLU, ↑pyruvate | Multiple B-vitamin needs |
| Elevated hippurate | + phenylacetate, symptoms | Gut dysbiosis active |

### Training Methods
| Method | Purpose |
|--------|---------|
| **Case-based immersion** | 10 curated cases |
| **Pattern drills** | Category recognition speed |
| **Biochemical inference** | From markers to mechanisms |
| **Language fluency** | Thinking in metabolomics |

### Competency Milestone
> "Trainee can look at an OAT and see patterns, not just numbers"

## Clinical Implication
- **Interpretation speed:** Category recognition faster than marker-by-marker analysis
- **Clinical correlation:** Patterns connect to patient symptoms
- **Treatment guidance:** Patterns suggest intervention priorities
- **Error reduction:** Context prevents over-interpreting isolated abnormalities
- **METHAP relevance:** Pattern recognition applies directly to research data analysis — biomarker patterns reveal metabolic phenotypes
