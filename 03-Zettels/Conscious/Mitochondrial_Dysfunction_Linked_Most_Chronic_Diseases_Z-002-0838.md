# Mitochondrial Dysfunction Linked to Most Chronic Diseases

## Core Claim
**Mitochondrial dysfunction is linked to the development and progression of most chronic diseases** — autoimmune diseases, cardiovascular disease, diabetes, and cancer all show mitochondrial impairment.

## Diseases with Mitochondrial Dysfunction
| Disease | Mitochondrial Issue |
|---------|---------------------|
| **Autoimmune diseases** | Energy failure in immune regulation |
| **Cardiovascular disease** | Heart muscle energy deficit |
| **Diabetes** | Pancreatic beta-cell mitochondrial failure |
| **Cancer** | Warburg effect — altered metabolism |

## Factors Affecting Mitochondria
| Factor | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| **Vitamin deficiencies** | Impaired enzyme function |
| **Processed foods, smoking, alcohol** | Oxidative damage |
| **Seed oils, chemical additives** | Membrane damage |
| **Chronic inflammation** | Metabolic disruption |
| **Glucose/lipid disorders** | Fuel dysfunction |
| **Low sun exposure** | Vitamin D, circadian disruption |

## Stress Response Pattern
```
Initial: Body resists (stress hormones increase)
    ↓
Phase: Hyperactivity alternating with exhaustion
    ↓
Permanent: Fatigue (mitochondrial dysfunction established)
```

## Clinical Implication
Addressing mitochondrial health is **central to chronic disease prevention and management**.
