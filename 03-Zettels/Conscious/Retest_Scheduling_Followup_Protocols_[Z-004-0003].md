# Retest Scheduling — Clinical Follow-up Protocols

**Timing matters.** Retest too early, you waste money and patient patience. Retest too late, you miss opportunities to course-correct. These evidence-based intervals optimize monitoring for metabolic interventions.

---

## 🎯 Retest Intervals by Marker

###  Resistance Panel (Primary Outcomes)
| Marker | Baseline | Active Intervention | Stable/Optimal |
|--------|----------|---------------------|----------------|
| **HOMA-IR, TyG** | ✅ | 6-8 weeks | 3-6 months |
| **Fasting glucose, insulin** | ✅ | 6-8 weeks | 3-6 months |
| **HbA1c** | ✅ | 3 months | 6 months |
| **Lipids (full panel)** | ✅ | 8-12 weeks | 6 months |

**Rationale:** Insulin sensitivity changes gradually. 6-8 weeks minimum for meaningful shift.

### Foundational Nutrients
| Marker | Baseline | Intervention Start | Maintenance |
|--------|----------|-------------------|-------------|
| **** | ✅ | 8-12 weeks (dose change) | 6 months |
| **Homocysteine** | ✅ | 8 weeks (methylation support) | 6 months |
| **Ferritin + iron panel** | ✅ | 8 weeks (if treating) | 6 months |
| **B12/MMA** | ✅ | 8 weeks (if treating) | 6-12 months |

**Rationale:** Nutrient repletion shows in 8-12 weeks. RBC folate takes 4 months to reflect.

### Thyroid
| Marker | Baseline | Levothyroxine Start | Stable Dose |
|--------|----------|---------------------|-------------|
| **TSH** | ✅ | 6-8 weeks | 6 months |
| **fT3, fT4** | ✅ | 6-8 weeks | 6 months |
| **Anti-TPO/Anti-TG** | ✅ | 6 months (selenium trial) | 12 months |

**Rationale:** TSH takes 6 weeks to reflect steady-state hormone levels.

###  and Metabolic

## 🔄 Follow-up Schedules by Clinical Status

### New Patient — Comprehensive Baseline
**Visit 0 (Baseline):**
- IR panel: HOMA-IR, TyG, fasting glucose, insulin
- Lipids: Full panel + ApoB if indicated
- Foundationals: VitD, homocysteine, ferritin, TSH
- Inflammation: hs-CRP
- Safety: Liver enzymes, eGFR, CBC

**Visit 1 (6-8 weeks):**
- IR panel (primary outcome)
- hs-CRP (inflammation response)
- Liver enzymes (safety)

**Visit 2 (3 months):**
- Full lipid panel
- HbA1c
- hs-CRP
- Adjust interventions based on response

**Visit 3 (6 months):**
- Complete reassessment (all baseline markers)
- Determine maintenance vs intensification

### Active Intervention — Intensive Monitoring
**Frequency:** Every 6-8 weeks for first 6 months

**Minimum at each visit:**
- Weight, waist circumference, BP
- HOMA-IR or TyG (quick assessment)
- Medication/supplement tolerance
- Adherence check

**Full panel:** Every 3 months

### Stable/Optimal — Maintenance
**Frequency:** Every 6 months

**Full assessment:**
- Complete IR panel
- Lipids
- Foundationals (VitD, homocysteine, ferritin, TSH)
- Inflammation
- Safety markers

**Annual addition:**
- Advanced lipids if indicated (ApoB, Lp(a))
- Thyroid antibodies if Hashimoto's

## 💡 Clinical Pearl

> "The 6-8 week retest is sacred for IR markers. Patients want to see progress in 2 weeks — I explain that metabolic change is cellular remodeling, not a light switch. Six weeks minimum, or we're measuring noise."

> "Vitamin D is the exception — check it at 8-12 weeks after dose changes. Earlier, and you haven't reached steady state."

> "If a patient is stable and optimal, I stretch to 6 months. But metabolic syndrome has a way of creeping back — annual full reassessment is non-negotiable."
