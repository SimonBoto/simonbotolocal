## Core Concept

Environmental factors as key determinants of metabolic health, influencing mitochondrial function, metabolomic profiles, glycation processes, mineral status, and fatty acid balance.

## Key Principles

- **Toxin Exposure:** Environmental toxins can impair mitochondrial function and disrupt metabolic pathways
- **Nutrient Depletion:** Environmental factors can deplete essential minerals and cofactors
- **Metabolomic Disruption:** Toxins alter metabolic profiles and increase oxidative stress
- **Glycation Enhancement:** Environmental stressors can accelerate glycation processes

## Clinical Significance

Environmental optimization supports all five pillars of metabolic health by protecting mitochondrial function, maintaining metabolomic balance, reducing glycation stress, preserving mineral status, and supporting healthy fatty acid metabolism.
