# Satiety Restoration Enables Long-Term Dietary Control

## Core Claim
**The higher protein and quality fat content of low-carb diets restores satiety, enabling better long-term dietary control, higher adherence rates, and sustained motivation** — this satiety advantage makes low-carb more sustainable than low-fat.

## The Mechanism
```
Low-carb composition
    ↓
Higher quality fats + Higher protein
    ↓
Restored satiety
    ↓
Better long-term control
```

## Satiety Factors
| Factor | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| **Protein** | Most satiating macronutrient |
| **Fat** | Provides lasting fullness |
| **Reduced carbs** | Stable blood sugar → fewer cravings |

## Motivation Through Results
- Visible health improvements
- Weight loss, energy, mood
- High motivation to continue

## Metabolic Markers Improved
| Marker | Change |
|--------|--------|
| **HDL cholesterol** | ↑ Increased |
| **Triglycerides** | ↓ Decreased |
| **Blood glucose** | ↓ Decreased |
| **Insulin levels** | ↓ Decreased |

## No Long-Term Difference
| Marker | Both Diets |
|--------|------------|
| Total cholesterol | Similar |
| LDL cholesterol | Similar |
| Blood pressure | Both improved |

## Key Quote
> "A low-carbohydrate diet, richer in good fats and protein, is more effective in treating and preventing obesity and other chronic health problems."

## Clinical Implication
- Prioritize **satiety** when designing weight loss interventions
- Low-carb addresses **hunger**, the primary cause of diet failure
