## Core Claim
**Pyruvate sits at the metabolic crossroads connecting glycolysis to multiple pathways—its fate (acetyl-CoA, lactate, oxaloacetate, or alanine) is determined by oxygen availability, energy charge, and metabolic demand** — this branch point is regulated by pyruvate dehydrogenase complex (B-vitamin dependent) and lactate dehydrogenase.

## Evidence

### The Pyruvate Branch Point
```
Glycolysis → Pyruvate
                 |
    +------------+------------+------------+
    |            |            |            |
    v            v            v            v
Acetyl-CoA   Lactate    Oxaloacetate   Alanine
    |            |            |            |
    v            v            v            v
Krebs       Anaerobic   Gluconeogenesis  Protein
Cycle       glycolysis                synthesis
```

### Fate Determinants
| Condition | Primary Fate | Enzyme | Purpose |
|-----------|--------------|--------|---------|
| **Aerobic, fed** | Acetyl-CoA | PDH complex | Krebs cycle entry |
| **Anaerobic/hypoxia** | Lactate | LDH | NAD⁺ regeneration |
| **Gluconeogenesis** | Oxaloacetate | Pyruvate carboxylase | Glucose synthesis |
| **Protein synthesis** | Alanine | ALT | Amino acid production |

### PDH Complex (B-Vitamin Dependent)
| Component | Cofactor | Function |
|-----------|----------|----------|
| **E1** | TPP (B1) | Decarboxylation |
| **E2** | Lipoic acid, CoA (B5) | Transfer to CoA |
| **E3** | FAD (B2), NAD⁺ (B3) | Reoxidation |

## Clinical Implication
- **PDH deficiency** → lactic acidosis, neurological impairment (B-vitamin responsive)
- **Lactic acidosis** → pyruvate shunted to lactate when ETC impaired
- **Warburg effect** → cancer cells produce lactate even with oxygen
- **METHAP relevance:** High-dose B-vitamins (B1, B2, B3, B5) support PDH function for optimal pyruvate oxidation
