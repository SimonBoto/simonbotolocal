# Cholesterol and Weight Have Less Impact When Other Factors Are Controlled

## Core Claim
**Cholesterol and body weight have less independent impact on longevity when the other three risk factors are controlled** — cholesterol shows a U-shaped relationship, and weight effects are confounded by accompanying metabolic conditions.

## Cholesterol Findings
| Finding | Explanation |
|---------|-------------|
| **No clear difference** | Both very low and very high risky |
| **U-shaped curve** | Optimal range matters |
| **Context-dependent** | Less important when other factors controlled |

## Weight Findings
| Finding | Explanation |
|---------|-------------|
| **No significant difference alone** | Often with diabetes, hypertension |
| **Confounded** | Other factors affect risk more directly |

## Key Insight
> "When the other four factors are controlled, cholesterol does not appear to have particular significance on its own."

## Clinical Implication
- Prioritize **smoking, hypertension, diabetes** control first
- Address cholesterol and weight within overall metabolic context
- Don't overtreat cholesterol in isolation
