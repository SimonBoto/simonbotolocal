# Aspirin TXA2 Mechanism for Metastasis Prevention

## The Core Mechanism

**Aspirin → COX-1 inhibition → TXA2 reduction → T-cell activation → Metastasis prevention**

### Step-by-Step

| Step | Process | Location |
|------|---------|----------|
| 1 | Aspirin acetylates COX-1 | Platelets (irreversible) |
| 2 | TXA2 synthesis blocked | Platelet membrane |
| 3 | T-cell suppression released | Blood/circulation |
| 4 | Immune attack on cancer cells | Systemic |
| 5 | Micrometastases eliminated | Before establishment |

## Why Platelets?

- **Anucleate:** No DNA → cannot regenerate COX-1
- **Irreversible inhibition:** One aspirin dose affects platelets for their lifespan (7-10 days)
- **High TXA2 production:** Major source of immunosuppressive TXA2 in circulation

## Clinical Impact

- **Metastasis HR:** 0.64 (36% reduction)
- **Cancer death HR:** 0.49 (51% reduction)
- **Dose:** 75-300mg daily (low-dose sufficient)
