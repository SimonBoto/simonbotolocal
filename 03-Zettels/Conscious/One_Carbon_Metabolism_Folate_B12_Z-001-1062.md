## Core Claim
**The one-carbon metabolism pathway — integrating folate, vitamin B12, methionine, and homocysteine — serves dual critical functions of providing methyl groups for DNA methylation and epigenetic regulation, while also supplying precursors for DNA synthesis, with dysfunction producing elevated homocysteine, neural tube defects, and increased cardiovascular and neurodegenerative risk.**

## Evidence

### One-Carbon Pathway Overview
```
Dietary folate → Dihydrofolate → Tetrahydrofolate (THF)
                                    ↓
                              5,10-methylene-THF ──→ dTMP (DNA synthesis)
                                    ↓
                              5-methyl-THF + B12 ──→ Methionine
                                    ↓
                              SAM (universal methyl donor)
                                    ↓
                              DNA methylation, neurotransmitters, phospholipids
```

### Key Enzymes and Cofactors
| Enzyme | Cofactor | Function |
|--------|----------|----------|
| **MTHFR** | FAD, riboflavin | 5,10-methylene-THF → 5-methyl-THF |
| **Methionine synthase** | B12 (methylcobalamin) | Homocysteine → Methionine |
| **MTRR** | B12 | Regenerates methionine synthase |
| **THF reductase** | NADPH | Folate reduction |
| **DHFR** | NADPH | Dihydrofolate reduction |

### Homocysteine Metabolism
| Pathway | Requires | Product |
|---------|----------|---------|
| **Remethylation** | B12, folate, betaine | Methionine |
| **Transsulfuration** | B6 | Cysteine → Glutathione |

### Clinical Markers
| Marker | Normal Range | Interpretation |
|--------|--------------|----------------|
| **Homocysteine** | <10 μmol/L | Cardiovascular risk marker |
| **Methylmalonate** | <0.4 μmol/L | Functional B12 deficiency |
| **FIGLU** | <2.5 μmol/L | Functional folate deficiency |
| **RBC folate** | >305 nmol/L | Tissue folate status |

### MTHFR Polymorphisms
| Variant | Prevalence | Effect | Clinical Action |
|---------|------------|--------|-----------------|
| **C677T** | ~40% heterozygous | 30-40% reduced activity | Methylfolate preferred |
| **A1298C** | ~40-50% heterozygous | Reduced BH4 recycling | Consider combined |
| **Compound** | ~10% | Significant impairment | Active forms essential |

### Clinical Consequences of Dysfunction
| System | Consequence | Mechanism |
|--------|-------------|-----------|
| **Cardiovascular** | Atherosclerosis | Homocysteine toxicity |
| **Neurological** | Cognitive decline | Myelin damage, neurotransmitter deficit |
| **Reproductive** | Neural tube defects | DNA synthesis impairment |
| **Psychiatric** | Depression | SAM-dependent neurotransmitter synthesis |
| **Oncological** | Cancer risk | DNA hypomethylation |

## Clinical Implication
- **Cardiovascular risk:** Homocysteine >10 μmol/L indicates increased risk
- **Pregnancy:** Folate essential for neural tube closure
- **Mental health:** Methylation affects neurotransmitter synthesis
- **Aging:** B12 deficiency common in elderly, affects cognition
- **METHAP relevance:** One-carbon metabolism intersects with metabolic syndrome through homocysteine, inflammation, and epigenetic regulation
