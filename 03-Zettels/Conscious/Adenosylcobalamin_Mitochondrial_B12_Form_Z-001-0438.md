# Adenosylcobalamin — The l B12 Form

> Adenosylcobalamin (5'-deoxyadenosylcobalamin) is the  form of vitamin B12, characterized by a unique cobalt-carbon bond that enables radical  for methylmalonyl-CoA mutase.

## Structure

**Cobalamin Core:**
- Corrin ring (similar to heme but different)
- Central cobalt ion
- Axial ligands

**Unique Feature:**
- 5'-Deoxyadenosyl group
- Cobalt-carbon (Co-C) covalent bond
- Only B12 form with this bond

## Synthesis

**Transport:**
- Hydroxocobalamin enters mitochondria
- Converted to adenosylcobalamin
- Enzyme: ATP:corrinoid adenosyltransferase
- Requires ATP

## Function

**Cofactor for:**
- Methylmalonyl-CoA mutase
- Radical-based rearrangement
- Carbon skeleton isomerization

## Distinction

**Methylcobalamin:**
- Cytosolic
- Methionine synthase
- Methyl transfer

**Adenosylcobalamin:**
- Mitochondrial
- Methylmalonyl-CoA mutase
- Radical chemistry
