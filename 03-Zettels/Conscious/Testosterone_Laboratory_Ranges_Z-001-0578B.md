## Core Principle

This zettel documents the standard laboratory reference ranges for testosterone assessment in males, including total testosterone, free testosterone, and bioavailable testosterone.

## Reference Ranges

- Total testosterone: 270-1070 ng/dL (9.4-37.1 nmol/L)
- Free testosterone: 50-210 pg/mL (0.17-0.73 nmol/L)
- Bioavailable testosterone: 80-320 ng/dL (2.8-11.1 nmol/L)

## Clinical Interpretation

- Age-related decline: Testosterone levels decrease by ~1% per year after age 30
- Diurnal variation: Highest levels in morning (6-10 AM), lowest in evening
- Method variability: Different assays (LC-MS/MS vs immunoassay) may yield different results

## Key Considerations

- Always interpret testosterone levels in clinical context
- Consider SHBG levels when evaluating free testosterone
- Repeat testing recommended for borderline or equivocal results
