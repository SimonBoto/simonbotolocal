# Cofactor Deficiency Metabolite Patterns

## Overview
Specific metabolite accumulation patterns in blood or urine can reveal cofactor deficiencies—enabling targeted nutritional intervention.

## B-eficiency Signatures

### B1 (Thiamine) Deficiency
| Elevated Marker | Enzyme Affected | Clinical |
|-----------------|-----------------|----------|
| **Pyruvate** | Pyruvate dehydrogenase | Lactic acidosis |
| **Lactate** | PDH complex | Fatigue, neurological |
| **Alpha-ketoglutarate** | Alpha-KGDH | TCA impairment |

### B2 (Riboflavin) Deficiency
| Elevated Marker | Enzyme Affected | Clinical |
|-----------------|-----------------|----------|
| **Succinate** | Complex II | Mitochondrial dysfunction |
| **Glutaric acid** | ETF dehydrogenase | Fatty acid oxidation |
| **Branched-chain intermediates** | BCADH | Maple syrup urine disease |

### B3 (Niacin) Deficiency
| Marker | Finding | Clinical |
|--------|---------|----------|
| **NAD⁺/NADH ratio** | Decreased | Energy failure |
| **Lactate/pyruvate** | Elevated | Redox imbalance |

### B6 (Pyridoxine) Deficiency
| Elevated Marker | Pathway | Clinical |
|-----------------|---------|----------|
| **Kynurenate** | Kynureninase | Neurological |
| **Xanthurenate** | Kynureninase | B6 deficiency marker |
| **Glycine** | Glycine cleavage | Elevated glycine |
| **Homocysteine** | Cystathionine β-synthase | Cardiovascular risk |

### B12 (Cobalamin) Deficiency
| Elevated Marker | Enzyme | Clinical |
|-----------------|--------|----------|
| **Methylmalonate** | Methylmalonyl-CoA mutase | Neurological damage |
| **Homocysteine** | Methionine synthase | Cardiovascular risk |
| **FIGLU** | Formiminotransferase | Folate trap |

## Mineral Deficiency Patterns

### Magnesium Deficiency
| Finding | Mechanism |
|---------|-----------|
| **Hypokalemia** | Mg required for K channels |
| **Hypocalcemia** | Mg required for PTH function |
| **Elevated CRP** | Inflammation marker |

### Iron Deficiency
| Finding | Mechanism |
|---------|-----------|
| **Microcytic anemia** | Heme synthesis impaired |
| **Elevated ferritin** | Acute phase response |
| **Low transferrin saturation** | Transport deficiency |

## Organic Acids Test (OAT) Applications

### Comprehensive Cofactor Assessment
| OAT Marker | Indicates |
|------------|-----------|
| **Pyruvate + Lactate** | B1, B3, lipoic acid |
| **Succinate** | B2, CoQ10 |
| **Methylmalonate** | B12 |
| **FIGLU** | Folate |
| **Kynurenate/Xanthurenate** | B6 |
| **Citrate/Isocitrate ratio** | Iron-sulfur cluster function |

## Therapeutic Approach

### Interpretation Strategy
1. **Identify pattern** of elevated markers
2. **Match to cofactor** requirements
3. **Consider multiple deficiencies** (common)
4. **Supplement and retest** (4-8 weeks)

### Dosing for Deficiency Correction
| Cofactor | Deficiency Dose | Maintenance |
|----------|-----------------|-------------|
| **B1** | 100-300 mg/day | 50 mg/day |
| **B2** | 50-100 mg/day | 25 mg/day |
| **B6** | 50-100 mg/day | 25 mg/day |
| **B12** | 1000 mcg/day | 500 mcg/day |
| **Mg** | 400-800 mg/day | 200-400 mg/day |
