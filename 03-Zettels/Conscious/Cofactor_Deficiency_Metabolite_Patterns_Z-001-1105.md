# Cofactor Deficiency — Metabolite Patterns

## Core Claim
**Specific metabolite accumulation patterns in blood or urine reveal cofactor deficiencies — elevated substrates indicate blocked enzymatic reactions, enabling targeted nutritional intervention before clinical symptoms develop** — functional medicine uses these patterns for personalized supplementation.

## Evidence

### B-Vitamin Deficiency Signatures
| Deficiency | Elevated Marker | Enzyme Affected | Clinical |
|------------|-----------------|-----------------|----------|
| **B1 (Thiamine)** | Pyruvate, lactate | Pyruvate dehydrogenase | Lactic acidosis, fatigue |
| **B2 (Riboflavin)** | Succinate, glutaric acid | Complex II, ETF | Mitochondrial dysfunction |
| **B3 (Niacin)** | Low NAD⁺/NADH ratio | Multiple dehydrogenases | Energy failure |
| **B6 (Pyridoxine)** | Homocysteine, xanthurenate | CBS, kynureninase | Cardiovascular, neurological |
| **B12 (Cobalamin)** | MMA, homocysteine | Methylmalonyl-CoA mutase, methionine synthase | Neurological damage |

### Mineral Deficiency Patterns
| Deficiency | Marker | Effect |
|------------|--------|--------|
| **Magnesium** | Low RBC Mg, high urinary Ca | Muscle cramps, arrhythmia |
| **Zinc** | Low alkaline phosphatase | Immune dysfunction |
| **Iron** | Low ferritin, high TIBC | Anemia, fatigue |

## Clinical Implication
- **Metabolite testing reveals functional deficiencies** before overt disease
- **Organic acid testing** standard in functional medicine
- **MMA and homocysteine** most clinically validated B12 markers
- **METHAP relevance:** Baseline metabolite testing guides protocol personalization; repeat testing confirms nutrient adequacy
