## Principle
Oxalic acid occurs naturally in many plant foods. Content varies dramatically by species and preparation method.

## High-Oxalate Food Categories

### Leafy Greens
| Food | Oxalate Level | Notes |
|------|---------------|-------|
| Spinach | Very high | Highest common source |
| Swiss chard | Very high | Related to beet family |
| Beet greens | Very high | More concentrated than beet root |

### Vegetables
| Food | Oxalate Level | Notes |
|------|---------------|-------|
| Rhubarb | Very high | Stems consumed; leaves toxic |
| Beets (root) | Moderate | Less than greens |
| Okra | Moderate | Mucilaginous texture |
| Leeks | Moderate | Allium family |

### Nuts and Seeds
| Food | Oxalate Level | Notes |
|------|---------------|-------|
| Almonds | High | Popular snack, nut milk base |
| Cashews | High | Common in vegan diets |
| Sesame seeds | High | Tahini, Asian cuisine |

### Legumes
| Food | Oxalate Level | Notes |
|------|---------------|-------|
| Soybeans | High | Tofu, edamame, soy milk |
| Navy beans | Moderate | Baked beans |
| Black beans | Moderate | Latin American cuisine |

### Grains
| Food | Oxalate Level | Notes |
|------|---------------|-------|
| Buckwheat | High | Gluten-free alternative |
| Wheat bran | Moderate | Fiber source |

### Other Sources
| Food | Oxalate Level | Notes |
|------|---------------|-------|
| Dark chocolate | Moderate-High | Cocoa solids content dependent |
| Black tea | Moderate | Brewing time affects extraction |
| Cocoa powder | High | Baking, beverages |

## al Trade-offs
- **High-oxalate foods are nutritious:** Rich in vitamins, minerals, fiber, antioxidants
- **Complete exclusion risk:** May create nutritional deficiencies if not substituted
- **Targeted reduction:** More practical than elimination for stone formers

##  Context
- **Kidney stone patients:** Often advised to limit high-oxalate foods
- ** timing:** Dietary calcium binds oxalate in gut → reduces absorption
- **Cooking effects:** Boiling can reduce oxalate content (leaches into water)
