# Amino Acid Complete Classification Table

## Core Claim
**The 20 proteinogenic amino acids are classified by side chain polarity (hydrophobic vs polar), charge (acidic vs basic), essentiality (dietary requirement), and metabolic fate (glucogenic vs ketogenic)** — these classifications predict protein structure, solubility, and nutritional requirements.

## Evidence

### By Side Chain Polarity
| Category | Amino Acids | Characteristics |
|----------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Non-polar** | Gly, Ala, Val, Leu, Ile, Met, Phe, Trp, Pro | Hydrophobic, interior of proteins |
| **Polar uncharged** | Ser, Thr, Cys, Tyr, Asn, Gln | Hydrophilic, surface interactions |
| **Acidic (-)** | Asp, Glu | Negative at pH 7, active sites |
| **Basic (+)** | Lys, Arg, His | Positive at pH 7, salt bridges |

### By Essentiality
| Category | Amino Acids | Requirement |
|----------|-------------|-------------|
| **Essential (9)** | His, Ile, Leu, Lys, Met, Phe, Thr, Trp, Val | Must obtain from diet |
| **Conditionally essential** | Arg, Cys, Gln, Gly, Pro, Tyr | Required during growth/stress |
| **Non-essential** | Ala, Asp, Asn, Glu, Ser | Synthesized de novo |

### By Metabolic Fate
| Category | Amino Acids | End Products |
|----------|-------------|--------------|
| **Glucogenic** | Most amino acids | Pyruvate, Krebs intermediates → glucose |
| **Ketogenic** | Leu, Lys | Acetyl-CoA, acetoacetate → ketones |
| **Both** | Ile, Phe, Thr, Trp, Tyr | Both pathways |

## Clinical Implication
- **Essential amino acids** must be consumed in adequate amounts — deficiency limits protein synthesis
- **Leucine is ketogenic only** — cannot produce glucose
- **Complete proteins** contain all essential amino acids in adequate ratios (animal sources, quinoa, soy)
- **METHAP relevance:** High-dose nutrient protocols often include amino acid support; understanding classification guides supplementation
