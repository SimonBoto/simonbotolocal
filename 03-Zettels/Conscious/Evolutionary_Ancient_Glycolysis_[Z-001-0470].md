# Evolutionary Ancient Glycolysis

## Timeline
**~3.5 billion years old**
- Predates oxygenic photosynthesis
- Present in Last Universal Common Ancestor (LUCA)

## Evidence for Antiquity

### Universal Distribution
- **Archaea:** Complete glycolytic pathway
- **Bacteria:** All domains
- **Eukaryotes:** Conserved enzymes

### Anaerobic Function
- Works without oxygen
- Essential for early Earth (anoxic atmosphere)
- Fermentation products (lactate, ethanol) predate respiration

### Enzyme Conservation
Key enzymes ~30-50% identical across domains:
- Triose phosphate isomerase
- Glyceraldehyde-3-phosphate dehydrogenase
- Enolase

## Why Trioses?

### Efficiency Advantage
```
6C glucose → 2 × 3C trioses
```
- Each triose processed independently
- Double the ATP yield opportunity
- More efficient energy extraction

### Evolutionary Logic
- Simple 3C units easier to manage
- Trioses connect to multiple pathways
- Modular metabolism

## Prebiotic Plausibility

### Non-Enzymatic Glycolysis
- Some reactions proceed spontaneously
- Mineral surfaces can catalyze steps
- Iron-sulfur world hypothesis connection

### RNA World Context
- ATP from glycolysis powers ribozymes
- Early metabolism before protein enzymes
- Triose precursors from formose reaction?

## Modern Implications

### Cancer Metabolism (Warburg Effect)
- Aerobic glycolysis despite oxygen
- "Atavistic" return to ancient metabolism?
- Rapid ATP production for proliferation

### Extremophiles
- Glycolysis functions in:
  - Deep sea vents (high pressure, no light)
  - Acidic hot springs
  - Frozen environments
