# Patient Objection Response Scripts

25+ scripted responses to patient objections—cost, skepticism, complexity, compliance, previous failures. These scripts reframe objections into collaborative conversations.

## Evidence
> "Objections are not rejection—they're requests for more information. The right response turns doubt into commitment." (Clinical sales psychology)

## Cost/Value Objections

### "It's too expensive"
**Script:** "I understand cost is a concern. Let's look at what you're already spending on symptoms that aren't resolving—medications, supplements that don't work, time off work. This is an investment in not needing those anymore."

**Reframe:** Cost → Investment in resolution

### "Why not just take a multivitamin?"
**Script:** "Multivitamins are like using a fire hose to water a plant—you get everything, but not what you actually need. Your OAT shows exactly which pathways need support. We target those specifically."

**Reframe:** Generic → Targeted

### "My insurance doesn't cover this"
**Script:** "Insurance covers disease management. This is health optimization—getting ahead of problems before they become diagnoses that insurance does cover."

**Reframe:** Insurance limitation → Proactive health

## Skepticism Objections

### "My doctor says this is nonsense"
**Script:** "Your doctor is trained in disease management, which is important. This is functional medicine—we look at optimal function, not just absence of disease. Both have value. The question is: are you getting the results you want with your current approach?"

**Reframe:** Conflict → Complementary approaches

### "This isn't FDA approved"
**Script:** "FDA approval is for drugs treating diseases. We're using nutrients to optimize function—like recommending vitamin D for someone who's deficient. The OAT shows objective markers; we're addressing those with targeted nutrition."

**Reframe:** Drug approval → Nutritional optimization

### "Where's the evidence?"
**Script:** "There's extensive research on each marker and nutrient—over 50,000 studies on mitochondrial function alone. Would you like me to share some specific papers? Also, the evidence that matters most is your own results—we retest and track progress."

**Reframe:** Generic doubt → Specific evidence + personal proof

## Complexity Objections

### "This is too complicated"
**Script:** "It seems that way at first. But we break it into steps—Phase 1 is just these 3 supplements. Once that's routine, we add Phase 2. You don't do everything at once."

**Reframe:** Overwhelming → Phased approach

### "I don't understand all this"
**Script:** "You don't need to understand biochemistry—you need to understand the story. Your energy system needs support; these nutrients provide it. Simple as that."

**Reframe:** Complex science → Simple story

## Compliance Objections

### "That's too many supplements"
**Script:** "It can feel like a lot. Let's prioritize—if you could only take 3, which would address your worst symptom? We start there, build the habit, then add."

**Reframe:** All or nothing → Prioritized start

### "I can't change my diet"
**Script:** "You don't have to change everything. Start with one thing—maybe just adding more vegetables or cutting one trigger food. Small changes compound."

**Reframe:** Total overhaul → One small change

## Previous Failure Objections

### "I've tried everything, nothing works"
**Script:** "I hear that a lot. Usually 'everything' was guesswork—trying supplements without knowing what your body actually needs. The OAT shows us exactly where to target. Different approach, different results."

**Reframe:** Generic trying → Targeted approach

### "Supplements never helped me before"
**Script:** "Most people take supplements based on marketing—'this is good for energy.' But energy problems have different causes. Your OAT shows your specific cause. We match the supplement to your need, not to generic claims."

**Reframe:** Generic supplements → Targeted matching

## Science Challenge Objections

### "This seems like pseudoscience"
**Script:** "I understand the concern. Organic acids testing is used in mainstream medicine—newborn screening, inborn errors of metabolism. We're applying the same science to functional optimization. The markers are real; the approach is evidence-informed."

**Reframe:** Alternative → Mainstream science applied differently

### "How do you know this will work?"
**Script:** "We don't know for sure—that's why we test and track. But we do know: your markers show specific dysfunctions; we target those with nutrients that address them. The logic is sound. The proof is in your follow-up results."

**Reframe:** Guarantee → Logical approach + tracking

## The Reframing Principle

| Objection Type | Reframe To |
|----------------|------------|
| Cost | Investment in resolution |
| Skepticism | Complementary approach |
| Complexity | Phased, simple steps |
| Compliance | Prioritized, habit-building |
| Past failure | Targeted vs. guesswork |

## Using These Scripts

### Step 1: Listen Fully
Don't interrupt. Let them finish.

### Step 2: Validate
"I understand why you'd say that."

### Step 3: Reframe
Use the script to shift perspective.

### Step 4: Ask
"Does that make sense?" or "What questions do you have?"
