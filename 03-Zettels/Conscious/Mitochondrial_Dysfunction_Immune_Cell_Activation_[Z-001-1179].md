# Mitochondrial Dysfunction in Immune Cell Activation

## Metabolic Reprogramming in Activated T Cells

### The Warburg Effect in Immunity

**Resting T Cells:**
- Primarily oxidative phosphorylation (OXPHOS)
- Efficient ATP production
- Maintain membrane potential

**Activated T Cells:**
- Rapid shift to glycolysis (even with oxygen present)
- "Aerobic glycolysis" = Warburg effect
- OXPHOS maintained for signaling functions

**Why Glycolysis?**
- Faster ATP generation (vs. OXPHOS)
- Supports rapid proliferation
- Provides biosynthetic precursors
- But: less efficient, generates ROS

---

## Mitochondrial Role Changes

### Beyond ATP Production

**Resting State:**
- Primary function: ATP synthesis

**Activated State:**
- **Signaling hub:** ROS production, calcium signaling
- **Metabolic regulator:** Integration point for nutrient sensing
- **Apoptosis control:** Cytochrome c release regulation

### Mitochondrial Dynamics
**Activated T Cells:**
- Increased mitochondrial mass (biogenesis)
- Fragmented morphology (fission)
- Enhanced ROS production

---

## Chronic Activation Consequences

### Repeated Stimulation (Autoimmunity)

**Each Activation Cycle:**
1. Metabolic shift to glycolysis
2. Mitochondrial stress
3. ROS production
4. Telomere damage
5. Proliferation (telomere cost)

**Cumulative Effect:**
- Mitochondrial damage accumulates
- mtDNA mutations increase
- ETC function declines
- ROS production escalates

### Exhausted T Cells

**Phenotype:**
- Reduced mitochondrial function
- Persistent ROS production
- Impaired proliferative capacity
- Shortened telomeres

**Contrast with Effector Cells:**
- Effector: High function, eventually die
- Exhausted: Persist but dysfunctional

---

## ROS Production in Immune Cells

### Sources

**1. Mitochondrial ETC:**
- Complex I and III leak electrons
- Form superoxide
- Increases with ETC dysfunction

**2. NADPH Oxidase:**
- Expressed in phagocytes, B cells, T cells
- Produces superoxide for signaling/killing
- Rapid activation upon stimulation

**3. Other Sources:**
- Xanthine oxidase
- Lipoxygenase
- Uncoupled NOS

### Dual Role of ROS

**Beneficial:**
- T-cell receptor signaling
- Antimicrobial defense
- Redox signaling

**Harmful (in chronic inflammation):**
- Tissue damage
- Telomere shortening
- Cellular senescence

---

## Therapeutic Implications

### Mitochondrial Support
**Potential Interventions:**
- CoQ10: ETC function
- Alpha-lipoic acid: Antioxidant, mitochondrial metabolism
- L-carnitine: Fatty acid transport
- B-vitamins: Mitochondrial cofactors

**Evidence:** Limited in autoimmunity specifically

### Metabolic Modulation
**Ketogenic Diet:**
- Reduces glycolytic stress
- Promotes mitochondrial biogenesis
- Anti-inflammatory effects

**Fasting/Time-Restricted Eating:**
- Autophagy induction
- Mitochondrial quality control
- May clear damaged organelles
