## Core Claim
**Competency verification at each training stage — including biochemistry knowledge, OAT interpretation, patient communication, treatment planning, and quality standards — ensures new clinicians demonstrate proficiency before independent practice, maintaining clinical quality and patient safety.**

## Evidence

### Competency Domains
| Domain | Skills | Verification Method |
|--------|--------|---------------------|
| **Biochemistry** | Metabolic pathways, cofactors | Written exam |
| **OAT interpretation** | Pattern recognition, clinical correlation | Case review |
| **Patient communication** | Storytelling, objection handling | Role-play |
| **Treatment planning** | Tiered prescribing, safety | Case scenarios |
| **Quality standards** | SOPs, documentation | Observation |

### Training Stage Checkpoints
| Stage | Competency | Verification | Sign-off |
|-------|------------|--------------|----------|
| **1. Foundation** | Biochemistry, terminology | Exam | Chief Instructor |
| **2. Interpretation** | OAT pattern recognition | Case review | Senior clinician |
| **3. Communication** | Patient interaction | Observation | Supervisor |
| **4. Treatment** | Prescribing protocols | Supervised cases | Medical director |
| **5. Independence** | All competencies | Comprehensive assessment | Certification |

## Clinical Implication
- **Quality:** Competency verification ensures standards
- **Safety:** Graduated responsibility protects patients
- **Confidence:** Structured training builds competence
- **Accountability:** Documented verification maintains standards
- **METHAP relevance:** Training standardization enables reproducible research protocols across multiple sites
