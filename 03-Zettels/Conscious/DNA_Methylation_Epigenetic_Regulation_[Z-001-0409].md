# DNA  — Epigenetic Regulation

> DNA methylation at CpG dinucleotides is a stable epigenetic mark that regulates gene expression, with patterns established during development and modified by environmental factors including nutrient status.

## 

**Chemistry:**
- **Substrate:** Cytosine in CpG context
- **Donor:** SAM (methyl group)
- **Product:** 5-methylcytosine
- **Bond:** Covalent, stable

**Enzymes — DNA Methyltransferases (DNMTs):**

| Enzyme | Function | Timing |
|--------|----------|--------|
| **DNMT1** | Maintenance methylation | Replication (copies pattern to daughter strand) |
| **DNMT3A** | De novo methylation | Development, cell differentiation |
| **DNMT3B** | De novo methylation | Development, repeat silencing |

## CpG Islands

**Definition:**
- Regions with high CpG density (>50% GC, >200bp)
- ~60% of gene promoters associated with CpG islands
- Normally unmethylated (genes active)

**Methylation Patterns:**

| Location | Methylation State | Effect |
|----------|------------------|--------|
| **Promoter CpG island** | Methylated | Gene silencing |
| **Gene body** | Methylated | Enhanced transcription |
| **Intergenic** | Methylated | Repression of transposons |

## Biological Functions

**Development:**
- Genomic imprinting (parental-specific expression)
- X-chromosome inactivation (females)
- Cell differentiation

**Adult Tissues:**
- Gene expression regulation
- Genome stability (silencing repeats)
- Tissue-specific expression patterns

## Nutrient Connection

**One-Carbon  → DNA Methylation:**
```
B12/Folate → Methionine → SAM → DNMT activity → DNA methylation
```

**Deficiency Effects:**
- Reduced SAM
- Impaired DNMT function
- Altered methylation patterns
- Genomic instability
