## Core Claim
**Of the 20 proteinogenic amino acids, 14 are purely glucogenic (yield glucose precursors), 2 are purely ketogenic (yield ketone bodies), and 4 are both glucogenic and ketogenic** — this classification depends on catabolic fate: glucogenic amino acids produce pyruvate, oxaloacetate, α-ketoglutarate, succinyl-CoA, or fumarate; ketogenic amino acids produce acetyl-CoA or acetoacetyl-CoA.

### Ketogenic Only (2)
| Amino Acid | Products |
|------------|----------|
| **Leucine** | Acetoacetate + Acetyl-CoA |
| **Lysine** | Acetoacetyl-CoA |

### Both Glucogenic and Ketogenic (4)
| Amino Acid | Glucogenic | Ketogenic |
|------------|------------|-----------|
| **Isoleucine** | Succinyl-CoA | Acetyl-CoA |
| **Phenylalanine** | Fumarate | Acetoacetate |
| **Threonine** | Succinyl-CoA | Acetyl-CoA |
| **Tryptophan** | Pyruvate | Acetoacetyl-CoA |

## Clinical Implication
- **Starvation:** Glucogenic amino acids used for glucose synthesis
- **Ketogenic diets:** Ketogenic amino acids support ketosis
- **Protein quality:** Mix of glucogenic and ketogenic amino acids
- **METHAP relevance:** Understanding classification informs protein requirements and metabolic fate of dietary amino acids
