# Glucogenic vs Ketogenic Classification

## The Categories

### Glucogenic Only (14 )
Can be converted to glucose via gluconeogenesis.

**List:**
1. Alanine (→ pyruvate)
2. Arginine (→ glutamate → α-ketoglutarate)
3. Asparagine (→ aspartate → OAA)
4. Aspartate (→ OAA)
5. Cysteine (→ pyruvate)
6. Glutamate (→ α-ketoglutarate)
7. Glutamine (→ glutamate → α-ketoglutarate)
8. Glycine (→ serine → pyruvate)
9. Histidine (→ glutamate → α-ketoglutarate)
10. Methionine (→ succinyl-CoA)
11. Proline (→ glutamate → α-ketoglutarate)
12. Serine (→ pyruvate)
13. Threonine (→ succinyl-CoA or pyruvate)
14. Valine (→ succinyl-CoA)

### Ketogenic Only (2 amino acids)
Cannot be converted to glucose.

**List:**
1. **Leucine** (→ HMG-CoA → acetyl-CoA + acetoacetate)
2. **Lysine** (→ acetyl-CoA + acetoacetyl-CoA)

### Both Glucogenic and Ketogenic (4 amino acids)
Yield both glucogenic and ketogenic products.

| Amino Acid | Glucogenic Part | Ketogenic Part |
|------------|-----------------|----------------|
| **Isoleucine** | Succinyl-CoA | Acetyl-CoA |
| **Phenylalanine** | Fumarate | Acetoacetyl-CoA |
| **Tryptophan** | Alanine (→ pyruvate) | Acetyl-CoA |
| **Tyrosine** | Fumarate | Acetoacetyl-CoA |

##  Relevance

### Fasting 
**Glucogenic amino acids:**
- Can maintain blood glucose
- Spared initially during fasting
- Used for gluconeogenesis when needed

**Ketogenic amino acids:**
- Cannot spare blood glucose
- Add to ketone body pool
- Leucine is mTOR activator (anabolic)

### Ketogenic Diet
**Pure ketogenic amino acids (Leu, Lys):**
- Fit ketogenic diet paradigm
- Do not raise blood glucose
- May help maintain ketosis

### Protein Quality
**Mix of glucogenic and ketogenic amino acids:**
- All complete proteins have both
- No dietary concern unless specific metabolic disorder

## Memory Aid
**"Glucogenic = Can make glucose"**
- All except Leucine and Lysine can contribute to glucose
- Leucine and Lysine are the "pure fat-makers"
