# Dietary Guidelines on Saturated Fat Not Evidence-Based

## Core Claim
**Conventional dietary guidelines recommending restriction of animal and saturated fats are not evidence-based** — may have unintended negative consequences.

## Guidelines vs Evidence
| Old Advice | Evidence-Based Approach |
|------------|------------------------|
| Limit all saturated fat | Focus on overall diet quality |
| Avoid red meat, butter | Consider metabolic context |
| Use vegetable oils instead | Traditional fats may be superior |

## Problems with Current Guidelines
- Based on **weak observational evidence**
- Ignore **metabolic context**
- May cause harm (vegetable oils worse than saturated fat)
- Distract from real issues (UPF, sugar)

## What Actually Matters
| Factor | Why |
|--------|-----|
| **Food quality** | Whole vs processed |
| **Overall dietary pattern** | Not single nutrients |
| **Metabolic health status** | Context matters |
| **Individual genetic variation** | One size doesn't fit all |

## Clinical Implication
- Stop focusing on **single nutrients**
- Prioritize **whole food quality**
- Consider **individual metabolic context**
- Traditional animal fats can be part of healthy diet
