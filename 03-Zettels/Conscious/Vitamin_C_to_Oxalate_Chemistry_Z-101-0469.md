## Conversion Overview
Ascorbic acid (vitamin C) can be metabolically converted to oxalate through a two-step oxidative process.

## Step 1: Oxidation to Dehydroascorbic Acid (DHA)
```
Ascorbic Acid (C₆H₈O₆) → Dehydroascorbic Acid (C₆H₆O₆) + 2H⁺ + 2e⁻
```

**Structural change:**
- Two hydroxyl groups (-OH) oxidized to ketone groups (C=O)
- Loss of 2 hydrogen atoms
- Formation of double bond in ring structure

**Catalysts:**
| Factor |  |
|--------|-----------|
| Oxygen | Direct oxidation |
| Transition metals (Cu²⁺, Fe³⁺) | Redox cycling |
| Ascorbate oxidase | Enzymatic catalysis |
| Heat/light | Non-enzymatic acceleration |

## Step 2: Oxidation to Oxalic Acid
```
DHA (C₆H₆O₆) → Oxalic Acid (C₂H₂O₄) + 4 carbons (lost)
```

**Structural change:**
- Ring cleavage
- Loss of 4 carbon atoms
- Retention of 2 carbons as dicarboxylic acid

**Carbon fate:**
- Released as CO₂ through metabolic pathways
- Enters  for complete oxidation
- Exhaled via respiratory system

## Stoichiometry Summary
| Compound | Formula | Carbons | Hydrogens | Oxygens |
|----------|---------|---------|-----------|---------|
| Ascorbic acid | C₆H₈O₆ | 6 | 8 | 6 |
| DHA | C₆H₆O₆ | 6 | 6 | 6 |
| **Oxalic acid** | **C₂H₂O₄** | **2** | **2** | **4** |

**Maximum theoretical yield:** 1 mole ascorbate → 1 mole oxalate

## Conversion Efficiency
**Not all ascorbate becomes oxalate:**
- Fraction excreted as oxalate varies by individual
- Remainder: metabolized through other pathways, direct excretion, antioxidant consumption
- Influencing factors: oxidative stress, metabolic state, enzyme activity, genetics

## PhD Relevance
- **Dietary confounder:** High vitamin C intake increases endogenous oxalate production
- **Measurement challenge:** Urine oxalate reflects dietary + metabolic sources
- **Adipic acid advantage:** No known conversion from vitamin C; cleaner metabolic signal
