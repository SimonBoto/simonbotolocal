# B12 Absorption and Transport

> Vitamin B12 absorption is a complex, multi-step process requiring gastric acid, intrinsic factor, and specific ileal receptors, making it vulnerable to disruption at multiple points.

## Absorption Steps

### Step 1: Gastric Phase
**Food B12 Release:**
- Gastric acid and pepsin release B12 from food protein
- B12 binds to haptocorrin (R-protein, transcobalamin I)
- Haptocorrin protects B12 from gastric acid degradation

**Key Point:**
- Achlorhydria (low stomach acid) → Impaired B12 release
- Common in elderly (atrophic gastritis)
- Proton pump inhibitors → B12 malabsorption

### Step 2: Duodenal Phase
**Pancreatic Enzyme Action:**
- Trypsin releases B12 from haptocorrin
- B12 now free to bind intrinsic factor (IF)
- IF is glycoprotein secreted by gastric parietal cells

**IF-B12 Complex:**
- Resistant to proteolysis
- Transported to ileum
- Binds specific receptor

### Step 3: Ileal Absorption
**Cubilin Receptor:**
- Located on ileal enterocytes (terminal ileum)
- Binds IF-B12 complex
- Endocytosis and processing
- B12 released into portal circulation

**Conditions Affecting Ileal Absorption:**
- Crohn's disease (ileal involvement)
- Celiac disease
- Surgical resection
- Tropical sprue

## Transport Proteins

### Transcobalamin II (TC II)
- **Function:** Active delivery to cells
- **Synthesis:** Liver, endothelial cells
- **:** TC II-B12 complex binds TC II receptor on cells
- **Uptake:** Receptor-mediated endocytosis
- **Critical:** For cellular B12 delivery

### Haptocorrin (TC I)
- **Function:** Storage, inactive transport
- **Characteristics:** Glycosylated, electrophoretically distinct
- **Affinity:** High for B12 analogues
- **Role:** Unknown, possibly antimicrobial

### Transcobalamin III (TC III)
- **Function:** Minor role
- **Source:** Leukocytes (inflammatory states)

## Storage and Turnover

### Hepatic Storage
- **Amount:** 2-5 mg (primarily as adenosylcobalamin)
- **Duration:** 2-5 year reserve
- **Daily Loss:** 0.1% of stores (bile, urine)
- **Daily Requirement:** 2.4 mcg

### Enterohepatic Circulation
- B12 excreted in bile (5-10 mcg/day)
- Reabsorbed via IF mechanism
- Conserves B12

##  Correlates

| Condition | Mechanism | Result |
|-----------|-----------|--------|
| Pernicious anemia | Anti-IF antibodies | No IF, no absorption |
| Post-gastrectomy | No parietal cells | No IF production |
| Chronic PPI use | Achlorhydria | Poor B12 release |
| Ileal disease/resection | No cubilin | No absorption |
