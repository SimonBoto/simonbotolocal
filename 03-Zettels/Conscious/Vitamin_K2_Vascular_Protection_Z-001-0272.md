# Vitamin K2 — Vascular Protection

## Primary Role
Vitamin K2 (menaquinones MK-4, MK-7) is essential for activating Matrix Gla Protein (MGP), the body's strongest natural inhibitor of vascular calcification.

## Mechanism
- Carboxylates MGP's glutamic acid residues to form Gla domains
- Enables MGP to bind calcium crystals and prevent arterial calcification
- Works synergistically with vitamin D (D upregulates MGP synthesis, K2 activates it)

## Clinical Evidence
- Vitamin K2 supplementation (180-360 mcg/day MK-7) slows progression of vascular calcification by 40-50%
- Reduces arterial stiffness
- May partially reverse early calcification
- Particularly important in high-risk populations (diabetes, CKD, aging)

## METHAP Application
- High-dose vitamin D protocol requires concomitant K2 to prevent potential calcification acceleration
- K2 supplementation transforms the protocol from potentially harmful to demonstrably protective
- Provides mechanistic rationale for vascular safety in clinical trial

## Key Considerations
- MK-7 has longer half-life than MK-4
- Dosing: 180-360 mcg/day for vascular protection
- Works best with adequate magnesium
- Biomarker: cMGP/ucMGP ratio reflects functional vitamin K status
