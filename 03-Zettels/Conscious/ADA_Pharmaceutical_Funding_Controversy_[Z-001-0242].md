# ADA Pharmaceutical Funding — $53.6M+ Conflict of Interest

> The American  Association receives substantial funding from pharmaceutical companies, including over $53.6 million from  manufacturers, raising concerns about potential conflicts of interest and influence on diabetes care guidelines.

## Major Pharmaceutical Donors

**Insulin Manufacturers (Primary Concern):**
| Company | Product | Contribution |
|---------|---------|--------------|
| **Eli Lilly** | Humalog, Basaglar | Significant (undisclosed exact) |
| **Novo Nordisk** | Novolog, Levemir, Ozempic | Significant |
| **Sanofi** | Lantus, Toujeo | Significant |

**Other Major Sponsors:**
- Merck (Januvia)
- Abbott (Freestyle glucose monitors)
- Dexcom (CGM devices)
- Pfizer
- BD (injection devices)

## Pathway to Stop Diabetes Program

**Funding:**
- **Total contributions:** $53.6M+ (cumulative)
- **2024 allocation:** $4.8 million in research grants
- **Purpose:** Support early-career researchers
- **Return on investment:** $12.47 additional funding per $1 ADA investment

**Recipients:**
- Early-career scientists
- Innovative research projects
- Translational and clinical science
- Technology and cure-focused research

## Conflict of Interest Concerns

**Issues Raised:**
1. **Insulin pricing:** Donors charge high prices for insulin
2. **Guideline influence:** Potential to favor donor products
3. **Research priorities:** May align with commercial interests
4. **Independence:** Perceived and actual conflicts

**Examples:**
- Insulin prices increased dramatically while ADA received insulin-maker funding
- Guidelines may favor pharmaceutical over lifestyle interventions
- Conservative approach to alternative therapies

**Defense by ADA:**
- Firewalls between funding and guidelines
- Independent expert committees
- Disclosure of relationships
- Research independence maintained

## Alternative Perspectives
