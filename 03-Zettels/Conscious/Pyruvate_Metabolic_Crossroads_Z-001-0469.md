## Core Claim
**Pyruvate is the 3-carbon α-keto acid at the metabolic crossroads — its fate (acetyl-CoA, lactate, oxaloacetate, or alanine) is determined by oxygen availability, energy charge, and metabolic demand, with the pyruvate dehydrogenase complex requiring 5 B-vitamin cofactors** — this branch point connects glycolysis to aerobic energy, anaerobic survival, gluconeogenesis, and amino acid metabolism.

## Evidence

### Pyruvate Dehydrogenase Complex (Aerobic)
```
Pyruvate → Acetyl-CoA → Krebs cycle
```
| Component | Cofactor | Function |
|-----------|----------|----------|
| **E1** | TPP (B1) | Decarboxylation |
| **E2** | Lipoamide, CoA (B5) | Transfer to CoA |
| **E3** | FAD (B2), NAD⁺ (B3) | Reoxidation |

### Alternative Fates
| Condition | Fate | Enzyme | Purpose |
|-----------|------|--------|---------|
| **Anaerobic/hypoxia** | Lactate | LDH | NAD⁺ regeneration |
| **Gluconeogenesis** | Oxaloacetate | Pyruvate carboxylase (B7) | Glucose synthesis |
| **Transamination** | Alanine | ALT | Amino acid synthesis |

### Regulation
| Factor | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| **High acetyl-CoA/NADH** | Inhibits PDH (product inhibition) |
| **High insulin** | Activates PDH phosphatase |
| **High pyruvate** | Substrate activation |

## Clinical Implication
- **PDH deficiency:** Lactic acidosis, neurological impairment (B-vitamin responsive)
- **Thiamine deficiency (B1):** Impaired pyruvate oxidation → beriberi, Wernicke-Korsakoff
- **Lactic acidosis:** Pyruvate shunted to lactate when ETC impaired (sepsis, hypoxia)
- **METHAP relevance:** High-dose B-vitamins (B1, B2, B3, B5) support PDH function for optimal pyruvate oxidation and energy production
