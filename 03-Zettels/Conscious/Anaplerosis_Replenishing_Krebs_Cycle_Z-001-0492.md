# Anaplerosis — Replenishing Krebs Cycle Intermediates

## Core Claim
**Anaplerosis ("filling up") replenishes Krebs cycle intermediates that are diverted for other pathways — when oxaloacetate is removed for gluconeogenesis, pyruvate carboxylase (biotin-dependent) converts pyruvate + CO₂ + ATP to OAA, maintaining cycle flux** — this ensures the Krebs cycle continues to oxidize acetyl-CoA even when intermediates are siphoned off for biosynthesis.

## Evidence

### The Problem
| Scenario | Consequence |
|----------|-------------|
| **Gluconeogenesis** | OAA removed → Krebs cycle stalls |
| **Amino acid catabolism** | Intermediates removed for nitrogen disposal |
| **Fatty acid synthesis** | Citrate exported → OAA depleted |

### Major Anaplerotic Reactions
| Reaction | Enzyme | Cofactor | Significance |
|----------|--------|----------|--------------|
| **Pyruvate → OAA** | Pyruvate carboxylase | **Biotin (B7)** | Primary anaplerosis |
| **Aspartate → OAA** | Aspartate aminotransferase | B6 (PLP) | Protein catabolism |
| **Glutamate → α-KG** | Glutamate dehydrogenase | — | Amino acid entry |

### Pyruvate Carboxylase (Key Enzyme)
```
Pyruvate + CO₂ + ATP → Oxaloacetate + ADP + Pi
```
| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Cofactor** | Biotin (B7) — covalently bound |
| **Activator** | Acetyl-CoA (feed-forward activation) |
| **Location** | Mitochondrial matrix (liver, kidney) |
| **Regulation** | Coordinated with fatty acid oxidation |

### Acetyl-CoA Activation
| Condition | Acetyl-CoA | Pyruvate Carboxylase | Result |
|-----------|------------|---------------------|--------|
| **High fatty acid oxidation** | ↑ | Activated | Anaplerosis matches acetyl-CoA supply |
| **Fed state** | ↓ | Less active | Citrate export favored |

## Clinical Implication
- **Biotin deficiency:** Impaired pyruvate carboxylase → lactic acidosis
- **Pyruvate carboxylase deficiency:** Severe neurological disease, lactic acidosis
- **Gluconeogenesis failure:** Without anaplerosis, cannot make glucose from pyruvate
- **METHAP relevance:** Biotin (B7) is essential for anaplerosis; high-dose B-vitamins support this critical pathway
