## Overview
Hyperbaric oxygen therapy has established indications for radiation-related complications and emerging applications in  therapy, with a generally favorable safety profile when properly administered.

## Established Oncologic Indications

### Radiation-Related Tissue Injury
| Condition | HBOT Role |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Osteoradionecrosis (ORN)** | Standard of care |
| **Radiation cystitis** | Healing promotion |
| **Radiation proctitis** | Mucosal healing |
| **Soft tissue radionecrosis** | Wound healing |
| **Laryngeal radionecrosis** | Tissue preservation |

### Mechanism in Radiation Injury
| Effect | Benefit |
|--------|---------|
| **Neovascularization** | Restore blood flow |
| **Fibroblast activation** | Collagen synthesis |
| **Stem cell mobilization** | Tissue regeneration |
| **Reduced ** | Break hypoxia-inflammation cycle |

## Emerging Cancer Therapy Applications

### Direct Anti-Cancer Use
| Application | Status |
|-------------|--------|
| **Glioblastoma (Seyfried protocol)** | Case reports, trials |
| **Metastatic disease** | Investigational |
| **Radiosensitization** | Historical evidence, limited current use |

### Integrative Protocols
| Combination | Evidence |
|-------------|----------|
| **HBOT + Ketogenic diet** | Preclinical + case reports |
| **HBOT + Vitamin C** | Theoretical, limited data |
| **HBOT + Metformin** | Theoretical |

## Safety Profile

### Common Side Effects
| Effect | Frequency | Management |
|--------|-----------|------------|
| **Ear barotrauma** | Common | Equalization techniques, tubes |
| **Sinus squeeze** | Common | Decongestants, slow compression |
| **Fatigue** | Common | Rest, hydration |
| **Claustrophobia** | Occasional | Sedation, relaxation |

### Serious but Rare Complications
| Complication | Risk Factor | Prevention |
|--------------|-------------|------------|
| **Oxygen toxicity seizures** | High pressure/long duration | Air breaks, limit exposure |
| **Pneumothorax** | Lung disease | Screening, precautions |
| **Decompression sickness** | Rapid ascent | Proper protocols |
| **Fire hazard** | Ignition sources | Strict safety protocols |

## Absolute Contraindications

| Condition | Reason |
|-----------|--------|
| **Untreated pneumothorax** | Risk of tension pneumothorax |
| **Doxorubicin (recent)** | Enhanced cardiotoxicity |
| **Bleomycin (recent)** | Pulmonary toxicity |
| **Cisplatin (recent)** | Ototoxicity enhancement |
| **Disulfiram** | Blocks antioxidant enzymes |

## Relative Contraindications

| Condition | Precaution |
|-----------|------------|
| **COPD/emphysema** | Risk of pneumothorax |
| **History of ear surgery** | Barotrauma risk |
| **Fever/URI** | Difficulty equalizing |
| **Seizure disorder** | Lower seizure threshold |
| **Pregnancy** | Limited data |
| **Active malignancy (debated)** | Theoretical concern about promoting growth |

## HBOT in Active Cancer: Controversy

### Concerns
| Argument | Evidence |
|----------|----------|
| **May promote angiogenesis** | Theoretical, mixed data |
| **Could stimulate cancer growth** | Not supported by evidence |
| **Safety in active cancer** | Generally considered safe |

### Current Consensus
| Position | Rationale |
|----------|-----------|
| **HBOT safe in active cancer** | Extensive experience |
| **Does not promote growth** | Studies show neutral or beneficial |
| **Integrative use appropriate** | As adjunct, not replacement |

## Patient Selection

### Good Candidates
| Profile | Rationale |
|---------|-----------|
| **Radiation injury** | Established indication |
| **Motivated for integrative care** | Compliance |
| **Access to facility** | Logistics |
| **No contraindications** | Safety |

### Poor Candidates
| Profile | Concern |
|---------|---------|
| **Recent certain chemo** | Drug interactions |
| **Severe COPD** | Barotrauma risk |
| **Untreated pneumothorax** | Absolute contraindication |
| **Claustrophobia (severe)** | Tolerance |

## Treatment Logistics

### Finding HBOT
| Setting | Availability |
|---------|--------------|
| **Hospital-based** | Most common |
| **Private centers** | Increasing |
| **Cancer centers** | Selected locations |
| **Cost** | Variable, often out-of-pocket for cancer |

### Insurance Coverage
| Indication | Coverage |
|------------|----------|
| **Radiation injury** | Usually covered |
| **Cancer therapy** | Rarely covered |
| **Integrative use** | Self-pay |

## Monitoring During Treatment

### Safety Checks
| Parameter | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| **Ear clearing** | Before each session |
| **Blood glucose** | Diabetics ( may need adjustment) |
| **Seizure precautions** | High-risk patients |
| **Fire safety** | Strict enforcement |

### Response Assessment
| Metric | Timing |
|--------|--------|
| **Symptom improvement** | After 10-20 sessions |
| **Imaging (radiation injury)** | Post-treatment |
| **Tumor markers** | If cancer therapy |
| **Quality of life** | Ongoing |
