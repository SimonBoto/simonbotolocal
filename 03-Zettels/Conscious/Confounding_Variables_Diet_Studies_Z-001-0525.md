# Confounding Variables in Diet Studies

> Inadequate control of confounding variables (smoking, socioeconomic status, sugar, industrial fats) likely explains observed associations better than saturated fat intake.

## Major Confounders

**Smoking:**
- Crude measurement (current/former/never)
- No pack-years
- Higher smoking in high-SFA countries

**Socioeconomic Status:**
- Not measured
- Low SES → poor diet, high stress, limited care

**Sugar:**
- Inadequately assessed
- Linked to CHD independent of fat

**Industrial vs. Traditional Fats:**
- Not distinguished
- Critical difference in processing

**Physical Activity:**
- Occupation-only measurement
- Leisure activity ignored
- Dropped from models

**Micronutrients:**
- Polyphenols, antioxidants not captured
- Mediterranean advantage beyond macronutrients

## MUFA/SFA Ratio

Likely captures **Mediterranean pattern** (olive oil, vegetables, lifestyle) not mechanistic SFA vs. MUFA effect.
