## Core Claim
**The dietary component emphasizes eliminating ultra-processed foods, prioritizing whole nutrient-dense foods, using traditional fats, reducing carbohydrates, and ensuring adequate protein** — forming the foundation for metabolic health improvement.

## Diet Actions (5 of 12)
| Action | Benefit |
|--------|---------|
| **Eliminate UPF** | Removes inflammatory ingredients, additives, seed oils |
| **Prioritize whole foods** | Provides essential nutrients, fiber, phytochemicals |
| **Use traditional fats** | Stable fats, no oxidation damage |
| **Reduce carbs** | Improves insulin sensitivity, promotes fat oxidation |
| **Ensure adequate protein** | Preserves muscle, enhances satiety, supports metabolism |

## Rationale
- Diet is **foundational** to metabolic health
- Ultra-processed foods drive **inflammation** and **metabolic dysfunction**
- Whole foods provide **building blocks** for cellular function
- Traditional fats support **hormone production** and **cellular membranes**
- Lower carbs optimize **insulin** and **glucose metabolism**
- Adequate protein prevents **sarcopenia** and supports **metabolic rate**

## Clinical Implementation
1. Start with **UPF elimination** (highest impact)
2. Transition to **whole foods** over processed alternatives
3. Cook with **traditional fats exclusively**
4. Gradually **reduce carb intake** to individual tolerance
5. Ensure **protein at every meal**
