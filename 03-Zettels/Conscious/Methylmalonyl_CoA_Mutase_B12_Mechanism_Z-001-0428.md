## Mechanism

**Methylmalonyl-CoA mutase catalyzes the vitamin B12-dependent rearrangement of L-methylmalonyl-CoA to succinyl-CoA, enabling propionate-derived carbons to enter the Krebs cycle** — this adenosylcobalamin-dependent reaction uses a radical mechanism (Co-C bond homolysis) and is the definitive marker of functional B12 status via methylmalonic acid (MMA).

## Mechanism

**Methylmalonyl-CoA mutase catalyzes the vitamin B12-dependent rearrangement of L-methylmalonyl-CoA to succinyl-CoA, enabling propionate-derived carbons to enter the Krebs cycle** — this adenosylcobalamin-dependent reaction uses a radical mechanism (Co-C bond homolysis) and is the definitive marker of functional B12 status via methylmalonic acid (MMA).

## Evidence

### Reaction
| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Substrate** | L-Methylmalonyl-CoA |
| **Product** | Succinyl-CoA (Krebs cycle entry) |
| **Type** | Intramolecular carbon rearrangement |
| **Energy** | No oxidation state change |

### B12 Cofactor — Adenosylcobalamin
| Feature | Significance |
|---------|--------------|
| **Structure** | Cobalamin + 5'-deoxyadenosyl |
| **Unique bond** | Cobalt-carbon (Co-C) |
| **Activation** | Homolytic cleavage generates radical |

### Radical Mechanism
1. **Co-C bond homolysis** → 5'-deoxyadenosyl radical
2. **Hydrogen abstraction** from substrate
3. **Carbon skeleton rearrangement**
4. **Radical return** regenerates cofactor

### Clinical Marker — MMA
| Status | MMA Level | Interpretation |
|--------|-----------|----------------|
| **Normal B12** | Low | Efficient mutase activity |
| **B12 deficiency** | Elevated | Impaired mutase → accumulation |
| **Specificity** | High | MMA elevated only in B12 (not folate) deficiency |

## Clinical Implication
- **MMA is the most specific functional B12 marker** — reflects mitochondrial B12 (adenosylcobalamin) status
- **Elevated MMA** indicates impaired propionate metabolism and energy production
- **B12 supplementation** normalizes MMA within days (short half-life)
- **METHAP relevance:** B12 adequacy essential for energy extraction from amino acids and odd-chain fatty acids
