## Core Claim
**For the first time in human history, obese children outnumber undernourished children globally** — a historic reversal where obesity (9.4%) now exceeds underweight (9.2%) among school-age children and adolescents worldwide, driven by ultra-processed food environments.

## The Historic Shift

### Global Trends (2000 → 2025)
| Condition | 2000 | 2025 | Change |
|-----------|------|------|--------|
| **Underweight** | 13% | 9.2% | ↓ Decreasing |
| **Obesity** | 3% | 9.4% | ↑ Increasing rapidly |
| **Net result** | Underweight > Obese | **Obese > Underweight** | Historic reversal |

### Scale of the Problem
| Statistic | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Children with obesity** | 188 million (1 in 10 globally) |
| **Countries affected** | 190+ countries |
| **Regions spared** | Sub-Saharan Africa, South Asia only |
| **UNICEF classification** | "One of the most serious threats to children's health" |

## Why This Matters

### Paradigm Shift
| Old Problem | New Problem |
|-------------|-------------|
| **Food scarcity** | Food quality crisis |
| **Not enough calories** | Wrong kind of calories |
| **Undernutrition** | Malnutrition + obesity |
| **Hunger** | "Hidden hunger" (micronutrient deficiency with excess calories) |

### Health Consequences
| Risk | Effect |
|------|--------|
| **Diabetes T2** | Increasing in children |
| **Thyroid disorders** | Rising rates |
| **Autoimmune diseases** | Linked to obesity |
| **Long-term CVD** | Early onset |
| **Mental health** | Depression, stigma |

## The Role of Ultra-Processed Foods

### UNICEF Findings
| Factor | Mechanism |
|--------|-----------|
| **Cheap and available** | Everywhere children live, learn, play |
| **Aggressive marketing** | Targets children directly |
| **High calories, low nutrition** | Causes "hidden hunger" |
| **Addictive by design** | Hard to stop eating |
| **Industry tactics** | Undermine health policies |

### Quote
> "Unhealthy foods and beverages, including ultra-processed, are widely available, cheap, and aggressively promoted where children live, learn, and play."
> — UNICEF 2025 Child Nutrition Report
