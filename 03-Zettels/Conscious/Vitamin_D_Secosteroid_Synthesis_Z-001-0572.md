## Core Claim
**Vitamin D is a secosteroid hormone synthesized from 7-dehydrocholesterol in skin upon UV-B exposure — undergoing sequential hydroxylation in liver (25-hydroxylase) and kidney (1α-hydroxylase) to produce calcitriol — functioning as a transcriptional regulator of calcium homeostasis, bone metabolism, and immune function through the vitamin D receptor (VDR).**

## Evidence

### Synthesis Pathway
| Step | Location | Enzyme | Product | Key Factor |
|------|----------|--------|---------|------------|
| **1** | Skin | UV light (280-315 nm) | Pre-vitamin D3 | Sun exposure |
| **2** | Skin | Thermal isomerization | Vitamin D3 (cholecalciferol) | Temperature |
| **3** | Liver | CYP2R1 / CYP27A1 | 25(OH)D3 (calcidiol) | First hydroxylation |
| **4** | Kidney | CYP27B1 (1α-hydroxylase) | 1,25(OH)₂D3 (calcitriol) | PTH stimulated |

### Regulation of Synthesis
| Factor | Effect on Synthesis | Mechanism |
|--------|---------------------|-----------|
| **UV-B intensity** | ↑ Vitamin D3 | Substrate activation |
| **Skin pigmentation** | ↓ Synthesis | Melanin blocks UV |
| **Age** | ↓ Synthesis | ↓ 7-dehydrocholesterol |
| **PTH** | ↑ 1α-hydroxylase | Renal activation |
| **Calcium** | ↓ 1α-hydroxylase | Negative feedback |
| **Calcitriol** | ↓ 1α-hydroxylase | Negative feedback |

### Vitamin D Receptor (VDR) Actions
| Target Tissue | Primary Effect | Clinical Outcome |
|---------------|----------------|------------------|
| **Intestine** | ↑ Calcium absorption | Bone mineralization |
| **Bone** | ↑ Osteoclast activity | Calcium mobilization |
| **Kidney** | ↑ Calcium reabsorption | Conservation |
| **Immune cells** | Modulates cytokines | Anti-inflammatory |
| **Parathyroid** | ↓ PTH secretion | Feedback control |

### Non-Classical Actions
| System | Effect | Evidence Level |
|--------|--------|----------------|
| **Immune** | ↓ Autoimmunity | Strong (MS, T1DM) |
| **Cardiovascular** | ↓ Hypertension | Moderate |
| **Metabolic** | ↑ Insulin sensitivity | Emerging |
| **Cancer** | ↓ Colorectal risk | Moderate |
| **Neurological** | Neuroprotection | Preliminary |

### METHAP High-Dose Protocol
| Parameter | Conventional | METHAP Target |
|-----------|--------------|---------------|
| **Daily dose** | 600-800 IU | 5,000-10,000 IU |
| **Target 25(OH)D** | 20-50 ng/mL | 80-150 ng/mL |
| **Upper safety** | 4,000 IU/day | 10,000 IU/day (monitored) |
| **Monitoring** | Rare | Every 3-6 months |

### Statin Interaction
| Aspect | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| **Cholesterol substrate** | ↓ with statins |
| **7-dehydrocholesterol** | ↓ with statins |
| **Vitamin D synthesis** | Potentially impaired |
| **Clinical relevance** | Monitor D status on statins |

## Clinical Implication
- **Hormone, not vitamin:** Synthesized endogenously, acts via nuclear receptor
- **Deficiency common:** Especially at high latitudes, dark skin, aging
- **Broad effects:** Beyond bone — immune, metabolic, cardiovascular
- **Statin interaction:** May impair synthesis substrate
- **METHAP relevance:** High-dose vitamin D is core intervention for metabolic syndrome and inflammation
