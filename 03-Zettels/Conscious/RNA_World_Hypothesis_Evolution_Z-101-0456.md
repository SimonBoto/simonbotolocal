## Core Claim
**Early life relied on RNA as both genetic material and catalyst (ribozymes) before DNA and proteins became dominant — RNA can store information (like DNA), catalyze reactions (like proteins), and modern ribosomes remain ribozymes with catalytic RNA at their core** — this hypothesis explains the transition from prebiotic chemistry to cellular life through self-replicating RNA molecules.

## Evidence

### RNA Dual Function
| Function | Evidence |
|----------|----------|
| **Information storage** | Base pairing (A-U, G-C) |
| **Catalysis** | Ribozymes discovered (1980s) |
| **Ribosome structure** | 23S rRNA is peptidyl transferase |

### Supporting Evidence
| Finding | Significance |
|---------|--------------|
| **Ribosomes are ribozymes** | Catalytic RNA still central to biology |
| **tRNA/rRNA ancient** | Predates protein enzymes |
| **Prebiotic plausibility** | Nucleobases + ribose + phosphate can form |
| **Adenine central role** | ATP energy, A-U pairing, catalytic RNAs |

### Evolutionary Transition
| Stage | Genetic Material | Catalyst |
|-------|------------------|----------|
| **RNA World** | RNA | Ribozymes |
| **RNP World** | RNA | Protein enzymes |
| **DNA World** | DNA | Protein enzymes |

### Advantages of DNA
| Feature | Benefit |
|---------|---------|
| **Stability** | 2'-deoxy less reactive than ribose |
| **Error correction** | Better repair mechanisms |
| **Larger genomes** | Stable information storage |

- **Ribozyme therapeutics:** Synthetic ribozymes as drugs
- **Evolutionary medicine:** Understanding origins informs disease
