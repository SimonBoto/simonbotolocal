# Low-Carbohydrate Diets Increase Energy Expenditure — Metabolic Advantage

## Core Claim
**Low-carbohydrate diets increase total energy expenditure compared to higher-carbohydrate diets with identical calorie content** — the Framingham feeding study demonstrated a 278 kcal/day metabolic advantage for low-carb vs high-carb diets, suggesting that carbohydrate restriction enhances metabolic rate.

## The Evidence

### Ebbeling et al. BMJ 2018
| Study Element | Detail |
|---------------|--------|
| **Design** | Randomized controlled trial |
| **Participants** | 164 overweight adults |
| **Duration** | 20 weeks |
| **Control** | Isocaloric diets, different carb content |

### Results by Diet
| Diet Composition | Energy Expenditure Change |
|------------------|---------------------------|
| **High-carb (60%)** | Baseline (reference) |
| **Moderate-carb (40%)** | +91 kcal/day |
| **Low-carb (20%)** | +278 kcal/day |

**Statistical significance:** P<0.001

### What This Means
| Scenario | Effect |
|----------|--------|
| **Same calorie intake** | Low-carb burns more |
| **Same weight loss goal** | Low-carb easier to achieve |
| **Weight maintenance** | Low-carb more forgiving |

## Mechanisms

### Why Low-Carb Increases Expenditure
| Mechanism | Explanation |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Reduced insulin** | Less fat storage signaling |
| **Gluconeogenesis** | Energy-costly process |
| **Ketone metabolism** | Inefficient (wastes energy as heat) |
| **Preserved thyroid** | Better T3 levels |
| **Adaptive thermogenesis** | Less metabolic slowdown |

### Hormonal Changes
| Hormone | Low-Carb Effect | Result |
|---------|-----------------|--------|
| **Insulin** | Lower | Better fat access |
| **Leptin** | Better sensitivity | Appetite control |
| **Thyroid (T3)** | Preserved | Metabolic rate maintained |
| **Ghrelin** | Lower | Less hunger |

## Clinical Implications

### For Weight Loss
| Advantage | Magnitude |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Extra calories burned** | ~250-300/day |
| **Annual equivalent** | ~25-30 lbs fat |
| **Sustainability** | Easier maintenance |

### For Weight Maintenance
| Factor | Benefit |
|--------|---------|
| **Higher calorie tolerance** | Can eat more without gain |
| **Metabolic flexibility** | Better fuel switching |
| **Appetite control** | Natural reduction |

## Caveats

### Not Unlimited
| Limitation | Explanation |
|------------|-------------|
| **Calories still matter** | Can't eat unlimited fat |
| **Individual variation** | Not everyone responds equally |
| **Duration** | Effect may attenuate over time |
| **Study length** | 20 weeks; longer data needed |
