# Mitochondrial Dysfunction and  Resistance

Mitochondrial dysfunction increases reactive oxygen species (ROS) and lipid intermediates, contributing to .

**Mechanisms:**

**1. Incomplete fatty acid oxidation:**
- Reduced mitochondrial capacity (aging, sedentary behavior)
- FFA incompletely oxidized → DAG, ceramide accumulation
- DAG activates novel PKCs → IRS serine phosphorylation

**2. ROS production:**
- Electron transport chain dysfunction
- ROS activate stress kinases (JNK, p38, IKK)
- Stress kinases phosphorylate IRS at serine

**3. ATP depletion:**
- Reduced ATP → AMPK activation (acute)
- Chronic: metabolic inflexibility
- Cannot switch between glucose/fat oxidation

**Evidence:**
- Mitochondrial DNA mutations cause IR
- Exercise improves mitochondrial function and insulin sensitivity
