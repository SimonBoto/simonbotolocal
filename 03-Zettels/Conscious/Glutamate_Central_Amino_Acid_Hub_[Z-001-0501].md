# Glutamate: The Central Amino Acid Hub

## Central Position
**Glutamate is the metabolic crossroads of amino acid metabolism:**

### 1. Amino Group Receiver
- Receives amino groups from most amino acids (via transamination)
- Central collection point for nitrogen

### 2. Amino Group Donor
- Transfers amino groups to:
  - Urea cycle (for disposal as urea)
  - Other α-keto acids (for amino acid synthesis)

### 3. Energy Metabolism
- Enters Krebs cycle as α-ketoglutarate
- Yields ATP via oxidative phosphorylation

### 4. Neurotransmission
- Major excitatory neurotransmitter in brain
- Glutamate receptors (NMDA, AMPA, kainate)
- Precursor for GABA (inhibitory neurotransmitter)

## Glutamate ↔ α-Ketoglutarate

### Transamination (Reversible)
```
Glutamate + α-keto acid ↔ α-ketoglutarate + amino acid
```
- All transaminases use glutamate/α-ketoglutarate pair
- B6-dependent

### Oxidative Deamination
```
Glutamate + NAD⁺ + H₂O → α-ketoglutarate + NH₄⁺ + NADH
```
**Enzyme:** Glutamate dehydrogenase (GDH)

**Regulation:**
- Activated by: ADP, leucine
- Inhibited by: ATP, GTP, NADH
- **Energy sensor:** High energy (ATP/GTP) inhibits, low energy (ADP) activates

## Connected Amino Acids
All converge on glutamate:
- **Arginine** → Glutamate (via urea cycle intermediates)
- **Proline** → P5C → Glutamate
- **Histidine** → Urocanate → FIGLU → Glutamate

## Clinical Relevance

### Hyperammonemia
**Impaired glutamate → urea cycle:**
- Ammonia accumulation
- Neurotoxicity (brain swelling)
- Confusion, coma

### Glutamate Excitotoxicity
**Excess glutamate in brain:**
- Overactivation of NMDA receptors
- Calcium influx → neuronal death
- Implicated in stroke, ALS, Alzheimer's

## Summary
**Glutamate = Central hub of nitrogen and energy metabolism**
- Collects amino groups
- Disposes via urea cycle
- Enters energy metabolism
- Neurotransmitter function
