## Core Claim
**BHB functions as an endogenous histone deacetylase (HDAC) inhibitor — by inhibiting Class I and IIa HDACs, BHB increases histone acetylation, relaxes chromatin, and upregulates genes for oxidative stress resistance (FOXO3A, MT2) and metabolic efficiency** — this signaling role is distinct from its energy function and may explain longevity benefits of fasting/ketosis.

## Evidence

### Mechanism
```
BHB enters nucleus → Inhibits HDACs (Class I, IIa)
     ↓
Increased histone acetylation
     ↓
Chromatin relaxation → Gene expression changes
```

### Upregulated Genes
| Gene | Function | Benefit |
|------|----------|---------|
| **FOXO3A** | Stress resistance transcription factor | Longevity, stress tolerance |
| **MT2** | Metallothionein (metal detox) | Oxidative stress protection |
| **SOD2** | Mitochondrial superoxide dismutase | Antioxidant defense |
| **Catalase** | H₂O₂ decomposition | Redox balance |

### Metabolic Effects
| Pathway | Outcome |
|---------|---------|
| **Mitochondrial biogenesis** | ↑ PGC-1α, more mitochondria |
| **Fatty acid oxidation** | ↑ FAO enzymes |
| **Ketone utilization** | ↑ Ketone body enzymes |

### Comparison to Other HDAC Inhibitors
| Compound | Source | Clinical Use |
|----------|--------|--------------|
| **BHB** | Endogenous (ketosis) | Nutritional/therapeutic ketosis |
| **Butyrate** | Gut microbiome | Colon health, HDAC inhibition |
| **Vorinostat** | Synthetic | Cancer therapy |
| **Valproic acid** | Synthetic | Epilepsy, bipolar |

## Clinical Implication
- **Fasting benefits:** HDAC inhibition may mediate longevity effects
- **Metabolic switching:** Epigenetic reprogramming toward fat oxidation
- **Oxidative stress resistance:** Enhanced antioxidant gene expression
- **METHAP relevance:** Nutritional ketosis provides both fuel (BHB) and signaling (HDAC inhibition) benefits — dual mechanism for metabolic health
