# Vitamin Functions Overview

Comprehensive visual summary of essential vitamin functions and deficiency consequences (Wallace, 2025).

![[2025-12-13_X_DrWilliamWallac_Vitamin_Functions.jpg]]

## Core Principle
> "Every vitamin has a job. Every deficiency has a consequence. Every food choice moves the needle."

## Individual Vitamin Functions

### 🔵 Vitamin D
- **Function:** Bone strength and calcium absorption
- **Deficiency consequences:** Weak bones, fatigue, increased fracture risk
- **Clinical relevance:** Osteoporosis, immune function, mood regulation
- **Sources:** Sunlight, fatty fish, fortified foods

### 🟣 Vitamin B12
- **Function:** DNA synthesis, nerve protection, red blood cell formation
- **Deficiency consequences:** Anemia, peripheral neuropathy (tingling), brain fog
- **Clinical relevance:** Pernicious anemia, vegan diets, elderly, PPI users
- **Sources:** Animal products, fortified foods

### 🟡 Vitamin A
- **Function:** Vision, immunity, tissue growth and differentiation
- **Deficiency consequences:** Night blindness, impaired immunity, epithelial damage
- **Clinical relevance:** Eye health, skin conditions, infection resistance
- **Sources:** Liver, dairy, beta-carotene (plants)

### 🟢 Vitamin K
- **Function:** Blood clotting (coagulation cascade), bone metabolism
- **Deficiency consequences:** Bleeding risk, easy bruising, hemorrhage
- **Clinical relevance:** Warfarin therapy, newborn supplementation, bone health
- **Sources:** Leafy greens, fermented foods, gut bacteria (K2)

### 🟠 Vitamin C
- **Function:** Major antioxidant, collagen synthesis, immune support
- **Deficiency consequences:** Weak immunity, poor wound healing, scurvy
- **Clinical relevance:** Wound healing, iron absorption, skin health
- **Sources:** Citrus, berries, peppers, raw vegetables

### 🔴 B-Complex Vitamins (B6, B7, B9, B1, B2, B3)
- **Functions:**
  - **B6 (Pyridoxine):** Neurotransmitter synthesis, metabolism, hemoglobin
  - **B7 (Biotin):** Energy metabolism, hair/skin/nails
  - **B9 (Folate):** DNA synthesis, pregnancy (neural tube), methylation
  - **B1 (Thiamine):** Energy production, nerve function
  - **B2 (Riboflavin):** Energy metabolism, antioxidant (FAD)
  - **B3 (Niacin):** Energy metabolism, DNA repair, skin health
- **Deficiency consequences:** Fatigue, neurological issues, metabolic dysfunction
- **Clinical relevance:** Methylation support, pregnancy, energy disorders
- **Sources:** Whole grains, legumes, meats, leafy greens

## Clinical Insights

### Deficiency Patterns Mimic Common Complaints
| Deficiency | Mimics | Why Missed |
|------------|--------|------------|
| B12 | "Stress," "low energy," "brain fog" | Non-specific symptoms |
| D | "Winter blues," "aches" | Seasonal attribution |
| Iron + B vitamins | "Just tired" | Normalized in busy lifestyles |

### Food-First Approach
- **Vitamins aren't optional** — essential cofactors
- **You feel them before you see them** — subclinical deficiency
- **Small improvements matter** — cumulative effect
- **Nutrition is chemistry you control** — daily choices

### Supplementation Strategy
| Scenario | Approach |
|----------|----------|
| Suspected deficiency | Test first (25-OH-D, B12, folate, etc.) |
| Dietary gaps | Targeted supplementation |
| High-risk groups | Preventive (elderly, vegans, malabsorption) |
| Drug interactions | Monitor (warfarin + K, metformin + B12) |
