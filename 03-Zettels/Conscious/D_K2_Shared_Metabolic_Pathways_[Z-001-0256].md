# D-K2 Shared Metabolic Pathways

> Vitamins D and K2 converge on multiple metabolic pathways—calcium homeostasis, [[KEYWORD-Insulin|insulin]] signaling, inflammation, and lipid [[KEYWORD-Metabolism|metabolism]]—creating synergistic effects that exceed their individual contributions to metabolic health.

## 1. Calcium Homeostasis

### [[KEYWORD-Vitamin_D|vitamin d]] Actions
- Increases intestinal calcium absorption (calbindin upregulation)
- Mobilizes calcium from bone (when needed)
- Regulates PTH (negative feedback)

### Vitamin K2 Actions
- Directs calcium to bone (osteocalcin)
- Blocks arterial calcium (MGP)
- Optimizes calcium utilization

### Synergistic Outcome
```
D: Absorbs calcium from gut
    ↓
K2: Directs calcium to bone/away from arteries
    ↓
Result: Optimal bone mineralization + vascular safety
```

## 2. Insulin Sensitivity

### Vitamin D Actions
- Enhances insulin receptor expression
- Improves β-cell function
- Reduces inflammation-induced resistance

### Vitamin K2 Actions (via ucOC)
- Increases insulin secretion
- Enhances adiponectin (insulin sensitizer)
- Improves peripheral glucose uptake

### Synergistic Outcome
```
D: Improves insulin action at target tissues
    ↓
K2: Enhances insulin secretion + adipokine profile
    ↓
Result: Dual mechanism glucose control
```

## 3. Inflammation

### Vitamin D Actions
- Suppresses NF-κB pathway
- Reduces TNF-α, IL-6, CRP
- Promotes T-regulatory cells

### Vitamin K2 Actions
- Inhibits pro-inflammatory cytokines
- Reduces oxidative stress
- May modulate immune function

### Synergistic Outcome
```
D: Systemic anti-inflammatory
    ↓
K2: Additional anti-inflammatory layer
    ↓
Result: Greater reduction in chronic inflammation
```

## 4. Lipid Metabolism

### Vitamin D Actions
- Modulates hepatic lipid synthesis
- May improve HDL modestly
- Regulates adipogenesis

### Vitamin K2 Actions
- Potential triglyceride reduction
- May improve lipid profiles
- Vascular effects independent of lipids

### Synergistic Outcome
- Complementary lipid benefits
- Combined cardiovascular protection
