## Mechanism

**Thiamine pyrophosphate (TPP), the active form of vitamin B1, serves as an essential cofactor for three critical α-ketoacid dehydrogenase complexes — pyruvate dehydrogenase, α-ketoglutarate dehydrogenase, and branched-chain α-ketoacid dehydrogenase — through its reactive thiazole ring carbanion that stabilizes decarboxylation intermediates, with deficiency causing lactic acidosis and neurological dysfunction due to impaired energy metabolism.**

## Mechanism

**Thiamine pyrophosphate (TPP), the active form of vitamin B1, serves as an essential cofactor for three critical α-ketoacid dehydrogenase complexes — pyruvate dehydrogenase, α-ketoglutarate dehydrogenase, and branched-chain α-ketoacid dehydrogenase — through its reactive thiazole ring carbanion that stabilizes decarboxylation intermediates, with deficiency causing lactic acidosis and neurological dysfunction due to impaired energy metabolism.**

## Evidence

### TPP Structure and Reactivity
| Component | Function |
|-----------|----------|
| **Thiazole ring** | Contains reactive C2 carbon |
| **Pyrimidine ring** | Structural support |
| **Pyrophosphate group** | Enzyme binding |

**Reactive Site:**
- Carbon 2 of thiazole ring forms carbanion (C2⁻)
- Stabilized by adjacent nitrogen
- Nucleophilic attack on carbonyl carbons

### TPP-Dependent Enzymes
| Enzyme Complex | Substrate | Product | Clinical Relevance |
|----------------|-----------|---------|-------------------|
| **Pyruvate Dehydrogenase** | Pyruvate | Acetyl-CoA | Links glycolysis to Krebs |
| **α-Ketoglutarate Dehydrogenase** | α-Ketoglutarate | Succinyl-CoA | Krebs cycle |
| **Branched-chain α-Ketoacid DH** | BCKA | Acyl-CoA | Amino acid metabolism |
| **Transketolase** | Xylulose-5-P | Ribose-5-P | Pentose phosphate pathway |

### Mechanism of Action
| Step | Reaction | Intermediate |
|------|----------|--------------|
| **1** | Carbanion formation | Thiazole C2⁻ |
| **2** | Nucleophilic attack | Covalent substrate-TPP |
| **3** | Decarboxylation | CO₂ release |
| **4** | Electron transfer | Resonance stabilization |
| **5** | Product release | Aldehyde or acyl group |

### Deficiency States
| Condition | Mechanism | Clinical Features |
|-----------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Beriberi (wet)** | Cardiac PDH impairment | High-output cardiac failure |
| **Beriberi (dry)** | Neurological PDH impairment | Peripheral neuropathy |
| **Wernicke-Korsakoff** | CNS energy failure | Ataxia, ophthalmoplegia, amnesia |
| **Lactic acidosis** | Pyruvate → lactate shunt | Metabolic acidosis |

## Clinical Implication
- **Essential cofactor:** Required for glucose oxidation
- **High-demand tissues:** Brain, heart, muscle most vulnerable
- **Alcoholism risk:** Thiamine deficiency common in alcohol use disorder
- **Refeeding syndrome:** Glucose administration without thiamine can precipitate Wernicke's
- **METHAP relevance:** TPP-dependent enzymes are critical for metabolic flexibility; B1 status affects energy metabolism efficiency
