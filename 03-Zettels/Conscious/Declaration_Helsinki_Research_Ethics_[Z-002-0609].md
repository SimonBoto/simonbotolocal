# Declaration of Helsinki on  Ethics

World Medical Association's international ethical standard for medical research involving human subjects. First adopted 1964, revised multiple times (1975, 1983, 1989, 1996, 2000, 2008, 2013).

## Core Principles

| Principle | Application |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Patient welfare first** | Research subject's welfare takes precedence over science/society |
| **Informed consent** | Required for all research, with documentation |
| **Independent review** | Research must be approved by independent ethics committee |
| **Risk-benefit** | Risks must be justified by potential benefits |
| **Privacy/confidentiality** | Must be protected |
| **Vulnerable populations** | Special protections required |
| **Publication duty** | Negative and positive results must be published |

## Key Distinctions

### Therapeutic vs. Non-Therapeutic Research
- **Therapeutic:** Potential direct benefit to participant
- **Non-therapeutic:** Benefit to science/society only, not individual
- Higher scrutiny for non-therapeutic research

### Placebo Use
Controversial :
- 1964: Permitted if no proven therapy exists
- 2000: Placebo acceptable only if no proven effective alternative
- 2008: Placebo only when scientifically necessary and no additional risk

## Revisions Significance

| Year | Major Change |
|------|--------------|
| 1964 | Original — physician-centered |
| 1975 | Added independent review requirement |
| 2000 | Strengthened placebo restrictions |
| 2008 | Post-trial access to interventions |
| 2013 | Greater transparency, data sharing |

## Current Status

- **Not legally binding** but widely adopted by national regulations
- **Reference standard** for medical research ethics globally
- **Updated regularly** to address emerging issues

## Application to METHAP

METHAP must adhere to Helsinki principles:
- Participant welfare priority
- NKUA ethics committee approval
- Informed consent with documentation
- Risk minimization (blood draw only)
- Results publication regardless of outcome
