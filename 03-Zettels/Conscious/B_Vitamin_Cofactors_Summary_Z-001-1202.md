## Core Claim
**B vitamins serve as essential cofactors in energy metabolism — with B1 (TPP), B2 (FAD/FMN), B3 (NAD+), B5 (CoA), and B6 (PLP) each playing distinct roles in decarboxylation, redox reactions, and acyl group transfer — with deficiencies causing characteristic clinical syndromes that reflect the specific metabolic pathways affected.**

## Evidence

### B Vitamin Cofactor Functions
| Vitamin | Cofactor | Key Enzymes/Functions | Deficiency Syndrome |
|---------|----------|----------------------|---------------------|
| **B1 (Thiamine)** | TPP | Pyruvate DH, α-KG DH, Transketolase | Beriberi, Wernicke-Korsakoff |
| **B2 (Riboflavin)** | FAD/FMN | Succinate DH, Complex I/II, Fatty acid oxidation | Ariboflavinosis, Angular cheilitis |
| **B3 (Niacin)** | NAD+/NADP+ | Dehydrogenases (PDH, IDH, MDH), Redox reactions | Pellagra (3 Ds: Dermatitis, Diarrhea, Dementia) |
| **B5 (Pantothenic acid)** | CoA | Acyl transfer (PDH, α-KG DH, Fatty acid synthesis) | Burning feet syndrome, Fatigue |
| **B6 (Pyridoxine)** | PLP/PMP | Amino acid decarboxylases, Transaminases | Peripheral neuropathy, Seizures, Microcytic anemia |
| **B7 (Biotin)** | Biotin | Carboxylases (Pyruvate carboxylase, Acetyl-CoA carboxylase) | Dermatitis, Alopecia, Conjunctivitis |
| **B9 (Folate)** | THF | One-carbon transfer (DNA synthesis) | Megaloblastic anemia, Neural tube defects |
| **B12 (Cobalamin)** | AdoCbl, MeCbl | Methylmalonyl-CoA mutase, Methionine synthase | Megaloblastic anemia, Neurological degeneration |

### Cofactor Chemistry
| Cofactor | Chemical Role | Reaction Type |
|----------|---------------|---------------|
| **TPP** | Stabilizes carbanions | Decarboxylation |
| **FAD** | Accepts 2e- + 2H+ | Oxidation/reduction |
| **NAD+** | Accepts 2e- + 1H+ | Oxidation/reduction |
| **CoA** | Acyl group carrier | Acyl transfer |
| **PLP** | Schiff base formation | Transamination, Decarboxylation |
| **Biotin** | CO2 carrier | Carboxylation |
| **THF** | One-carbon carrier | Methyl transfer |
| **B12** | Radical chemistry | Rearrangement, Methyl transfer |

### Clinical Deficiency Patterns
| System | Affected by | Manifestations |
|--------|-------------|----------------|
| **Neurological** | B1, B3, B6, B12 | Neuropathy, cognitive decline, ataxia |
| **Hematological** | B9, B12, B6 | Anemia (megaloblastic or microcytic) |
| **Dermatological** | B2, B3, B7 | Dermatitis, cheilitis, alopecia |
| **Gastrointestinal** | B1, B3 | Diarrhea, malabsorption |
| **Cardiovascular** | B1 | High-output failure, edema |

## Clinical Implication
- **Interdependent function:** B vitamins work synergistically
- **High-demand tissues:** Brain, muscle, RBCs most vulnerable
- **Alcoholism risk:** Multiple B vitamin deficiencies common
- **Supplementation:** Often needed as B-complex rather than isolated
- **METHAP relevance:** B vitamin status is critical for metabolic flexibility and energy production
