# IRP1 — The Iron-Sensing Molecular Switch

## Overview

IRP1 (Iron Regulatory Protein 1) is a remarkable example of a protein with dual functions determined by cellular iron status. It can function as either a cytosolic enzyme (aconitase) or an RNA-binding regulatory protein.

---

## The Switch Mechanism

### Iron-Replete Conditions
```
[4Fe-4S] cluster assembled on IRP1
    ↓
Aconitase activity: Citrate ⇌ Isocitrate
    ↓
IRP1 cannot bind IREs
    ↓
Iron uptake ↓, storage ↑
```

### Iron-Deplete Conditions
```
[4Fe-4S] cluster disassembled or not synthesized
    ↓
IRP1 adopts open conformation
    ↓
IRE-binding activity
    ↓
Iron uptake ↑, storage ↓
```

---

## IRE-Binding Function (Iron-Depleted)

### Target: Iron-Responsive Elements (IREs)

**Structure:** Stem-loop sequences in mRNA UTRs

**IRP1 binding:**
- Stabilizes or blocks mRNA translation
- Sequence-specific recognition

### Effects on Target Genes

**Transferrin Receptor 1 (TfR1):**
- IRE location: 3' UTR
- IRP1 binding: **Stabilizes mRNA**
- Result: ↑ TfR1 protein → ↑ Iron uptake

**Ferritin:**
- IRE location: 5' UTR
- IRP1 binding: **Blocks translation**
- Result: ↓ Ferritin protein → ↓ Iron storage

**Ferroportin:**
- IRE location: 5' UTR
- IRP1 binding: Blocks translation
- Result: ↓ Iron export → Retain iron

---

## Physiological Significance

### Iron Homeostasis Control

**Low Iron Response:**
1. IRP1 binds IREs
2. ↑ Iron uptake (TfR1)
3. ↓ Iron storage (ferritin)
4. Maximize available iron
5. Prevent deficiency

**High Iron Response:**
1. IRP1 has Fe-S cluster (aconitase form)
2. ↓ Iron uptake
3. ↑ Iron storage
4. Prevent iron toxicity

---

## Comparison: IRP1 vs IRP2

| Feature | IRP1 | IRP2 |
|---------|------|------|
| **Dual function** | Yes (aconitase/IRP) | No (only IRP) |
| **Fe-S cluster** | Forms (becomes aconitase) | Does not form |
| **Regulation** | Iron-dependent switch | Degradation (FBXL5) |
| **Basal activity** | Low (iron-dependent) | Present |
| **Relative importance** | 30% of IRP activity | 70% of IRP activity |

**IRP2:** Dedicated iron regulator, degraded in iron-replete conditions

---

## Clinical Relevance

### Iron Deficiency Anemia
- IRP1/2 highly active
- High TfR1 expression
- Soluble TfR elevated (clinical marker)

### Hemochromatosis
- IRP1 in aconitase form
- High ferritin synthesis
- Tissue iron overload

### Aconitase/IRP1 Connection
- Metabolism and iron regulation linked
- Energy status affects iron homeostasis
- Oxidative stress affects both
