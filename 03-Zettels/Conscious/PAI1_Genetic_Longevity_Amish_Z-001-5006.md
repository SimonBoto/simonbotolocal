# PAI1_Genetic_Longevity_Amish

## Source
[[L-001-0654_Vaughan2017_PAI1_Aging_L-001-0654]]

## Core Claim
A loss-of-function mutation in the SERPINE1 gene (encoding PAI-1) found in the Amish community confers reduced PAI-1 activity, leading to longer telomeres, lower fasting insulin, diabetes protection, and extended lifespan by approximately 10 years.

## Key Details
- Amish mutation (50% PAI-1 function) → longer telomeres
- Amish mutation → lower fasting insulin, diabetes protection
- Amish mutation → +10 years lifespan
- Provides human genetic evidence that PAI-1 reduction extends healthspan and lifespan

## Evidence
- [[Khan_2017_Sci_Adv_R-001-0009]] — Key genetic evidence
- [[Vaughan_2017_Arterioscler_Thromb_Vasc_Biol_R-001-0006]] — Biological plausibility
- [[Jiang_2025_Ageing_Res_Rev_R-001-0008]] — Comprehensive aging context

## Significance
This natural human experiment provides compelling evidence that PAI-1 is a causal factor in human aging and longevity, validating PAI-1 as a therapeutic target for aging interventions.

## Clinical Significance
- Pharmacological PAI-1 inhibition may mimic the protective effects of the Amish mutation
- PAI-1 levels could serve as a predictive biomarker for longevity and age-related disease risk
- Genetic screening for PAI-1 variants may inform personalized aging prevention strategies
