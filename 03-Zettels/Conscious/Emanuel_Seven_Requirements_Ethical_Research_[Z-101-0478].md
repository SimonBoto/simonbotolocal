# Emanuel's Seven Requirements for Ethical 

## Source
Emanuel EJ, Wendler D, Grady C. What makes  research ethical? *JAMA*. 2000;283(20):2701-2711.

## Core Principle
**Informed consent is necessary but not sufficient.**

All seven requirements must be satisfied for research to be ethical. No single requirement alone ensures ethical integrity.

## The Seven Requirements

### 1. Social and Clinical Value
**Question:** Does this research answer an important question?

| Aspect | Implementation |
|--------|----------------|
| **Specific aim** | Well-defined research question |
| **Importance** | Addresses clinical or social need |
| **Knowledge contribution** | Advances health or understanding |
| **Publication obligation** | Both positive and negative results shared |

**Why it matters:** Prevents wasteful, exploitative, or redundant research.

---

### 2. Scientific Validity
**Question:** Is the research methodologically sound?

| Aspect | Implementation |
|--------|----------------|
| **Rigorous design** | Appropriate methods for question |
| **Competent execution** | Qualified researchers |
| **Adequate power** | Sufficient sample size |
| **Reliable outcomes** | Valid, reproducible results |

**Why it matters:** Invalid research exposes participants to risk without producing useful knowledge.

---

### 3. Fair Subject Selection
**Question:** Are participants selected fairly and equitably?

| Aspect | Implementation |
|--------|----------------|
| **Scientific criteria** | Selection based on study needs |
| **Vulnerable protection** | Not targeted due to ease of access |
| **Benefit distribution** | Fair sharing of research benefits |
| **Risk distribution** | Not disproportionately burdened |

**Why it matters:** Prevents exploitation of vulnerable populations.

---

### 4. Favorable Risk-Benefit Ratio
**Question:** Do benefits outweigh risks?

| Aspect | Implementation |
|--------|----------------|
| **Risk minimization** | Lowest possible risk to achieve aims |
| **Benefit maximization** | Enhanced potential benefits |
| **Systematic assessment** | Thorough, ongoing evaluation |
| **Favorable balance** | Benefits > risks for participants and society |

**Why it matters:** Protects participants from unjustified harm.

---

### 5. Independent Review
**Question:** Has neutral evaluation occurred?

| Aspect | Implementation |
|--------|----------------|
| **IRB/Ethics committee** | Independent body review |
| **Impartial judgment** | No conflicts of interest |
| **Ongoing oversight** | Continuing monitoring |
| **Transparency** | Clear documentation |

**Why it matters:** Ensures accountability and protects against researcher bias.

---

### 6. Informed Consent
**Question:** Do participants voluntarily agree with full understanding?

| Aspect | Implementation |
|--------|----------------|
| **Disclosure** | Complete information provided |
| **Comprehension** | Understandable presentation |
| **Voluntariness** | No coercion or undue influence |
| **Competence** | Capacity to decide |
| **Right to withdraw** | Can leave at any time |

**Why it matters:** Respects individual autonomy and self-determination.

---

### 7. Respect for Enrolled Subjects
**Question:** Are participants protected throughout and after?

| Aspect | Implementation |
|--------|----------------|
| **Privacy protection** | Confidentiality safeguards |
| **Data security** | Secure storage, limited access |
| **Ongoing monitoring** |  surveillance |
| **Post-study obligations** | Continued care if harmed |
| **Dignity preservation** | Respectful treatment always |

**Why it matters:** Ethical obligations do not end at enrollment.

## Application Framework

### Pre-Study Checklist
- [ ] Value: Important question identified
- [ ] Validity: Sound methodology designed
- [ ] Selection: Fair criteria established
- [ ] Risk-benefit: Favorable ratio documented
- [ ] Review: IRB approval obtained
- [ ] Consent: Comprehensive process developed
- [ ] Respect: Ongoing protections planned

### During Study
- [ ] Monitor risks continuously
- [ ] Maintain consent (ongoing process)
- [ ] Protect confidentiality
- [ ] Report to IRB as required

### Post-Study
- [ ] Publish all results (positive and negative)
- [ ] Maintain data securely
- [ ] Address any participant harm
- [ ] Share knowledge with community
