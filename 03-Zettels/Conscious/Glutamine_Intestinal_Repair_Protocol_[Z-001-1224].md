# Glutamine — Intestinal Repair Protocol

## Overview

Glutamine is the primary fuel source for enterocytes (intestinal epithelial cells) and plays a critical role in maintaining intestinal barrier integrity, supporting mucosal healing, and regulating immune function in the gut.

---

## Biological Functions

### 1. Enterocyte Fuel
- **Preferred substrate:** Provides 30-50% of enterocyte energy
- **Rapid turnover:** Supports high cellular replacement rate (every 3-5 days)
- **ATP production:** Direct energy source for intestinal cells

### 2. Intestinal Barrier Integrity
- **Tight junctions:** Supports occludin and claudin expression
- **Mucosal layer:** Fuel for mucus-producing goblet cells
- **Immune regulation:** Supports IgA production

### 3. Anti-Inflammatory Effects
- Reduces pro-inflammatory cytokines (TNF-α, IL-6)
- Supports regulatory T-cells
- Decreases intestinal permeability

---

##  Indications

### Primary Uses
| Condition | Rationale |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Leaky gut syndrome** | Repairs tight junctions |
| **IBD (Crohn's, UC)** | Mucosal healing, remission maintenance |
| **After antibiotics** | Restores barrier function |
| **Food sensitivities** | Reduces permeability |
| **Increased mucus** | Regulates mucosal production |
| **Post-surgery** | Accelerates intestinal healing |

---

## Dosing Protocol

### Standard Dosing
| Phase | Dose | Timing |
|-------|------|--------|
| **Start** | 2-3 g/day | Divided doses |
| **Maintenance** | 5 g/day | 2-3 divided doses |
| **Therapeutic** | 10 g/day | 2-3 divided doses |
| **Maximum** | 20-30 g/day | Under supervision |

### Administration
- **Best:** Between meals, on empty stomach
- **Form:** Powder mixed in water or broth
- **Absorption:** Rapid, complete

---

## Cautions and Contraindications

### Relative Contraindications
**Bacterial Overgrowth (SIBO):**
- Some bacteria can ferment glutamine
- May worsen bloating in active SIBO
- **Strategy:** Treat overgrowth first, then add glutamine

** Concerns:**
- Some cancers use glutamine for growth
- **Note:** Evidence mixed; consult oncology

### Monitoring
- Bloating
- Brain fog
- Changes in stool

**If symptoms worsen:** Reduce dose or discontinue
