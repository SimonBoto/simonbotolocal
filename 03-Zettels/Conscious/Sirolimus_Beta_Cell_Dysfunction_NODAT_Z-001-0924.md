# Sirolimus Induces Pancreatic Beta-Cell Dysfunction and NODAT

**Core Mechanism:** Pancreatic β-cells are exquisitely sensitive to sirolimus due to high metabolic demand for  secretion and dependence on mitochondrial ATP production.

## Why β-Cells Are Vulnerable

**High Metabolic Demand:**
- Continuous ATP production required
- Glucose sensing coupled to 
- Each insulin secretion event requires ATP

**Glucose-Stimulated Insulin Secretion (GSIS):**
```
Glucose enters → Glycolysis → Pyruvate → Mitochondria
                                              ↓
                                    TCA cycle, OXPHOS
                                              ↓
                                    ATP production ↑
                                              ↓
                                    ATP/ADP ratio rises
                                              ↓
                                    K_ATP channels close
                                              ↓
                                    Membrane depolarization
                                              ↓
                                    Ca²⁺ influx
                                              ↓
                                    Insulin granule exocytosis
```

**Mitochondrial Dependence:**
- 90% of ATP from oxidative phosphorylation
- Limited glycolytic capacity
- Mitochondria must rapidly respond to glucose

## Sirolimus Effects on β-Cells

### 1. Mitochondrial Dysfunction

**Mechanism:**
- PGC-1α suppression → reduced mitochondrial mass
- ETC impairment → reduced ATP production
- ROS accumulation → oxidative damage

**Consequence:**
- Impaired glucose-stimulated ATP production
- Reduced ATP/ADP ratio rise
- Defective K_ATP channel closure
- Impaired insulin secretion

### 2. Reduced β-Cell Mass

**Evidence:**
- Animal models show β-cell loss with chronic sirolimus
- Human autopsy studies (limited): reduced β-cell area
- Time-dependent: longer exposure = more loss

**Mechanisms:**
- Apoptosis from metabolic stress
- Autophagy-mediated cell death
- Reduced proliferation

### 3. Autophagy Dysregulation

**Acute:**
- Mitophagy removes damaged mitochondria
- Potentially protective

**Chronic:**
- Excessive autophagy
- Removal of functional components
- Autophagic cell death

## Clinical Pattern: NODAT

### New-Onset Diabetes After Transplantation

**Definition:**
- Diabetes diagnosed after transplant
- Not pre-existing
- Associated with immunosuppression

**Sirolimus Risk:**
- 15-30% incidence (vs. 5-10% with calcineurin inhibitors)
- Dose-dependent
- Reversible with discontinuation (partial)

### Diagnostic Criteria

**Timing:**
- Can occur weeks to years after starting
- Earlier onset with higher doses

**Glucose Pattern:**
- Fasting hyperglycemia
- Post-prandial hyperglycemia (often more severe)
- Progressive over time

**Insulin Secretion Pattern:**
- Reduced first-phase insulin response
- Delayed/inadequate insulin peak
- Relative insulin deficiency

## Distinction from Type 1 Diabetes

| Feature | Sirolimus-Induced | Type 1 Diabetes |
|---------|-------------------|-----------------|
| **Onset** | Weeks-months | Usually acute |
| **Ketosis** | Rare | Common at onset |
| **Autoantibodies** | Negative | Usually positive |
| **Insulin requirement** | Variable | Absolute |
| **Reversibility** | Partial (dose-dependent) | No |
| **C-peptide** | Low-normal | Undetectable |
