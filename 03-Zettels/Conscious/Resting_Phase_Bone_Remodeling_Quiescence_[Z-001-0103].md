# Resting Phase — Bone Remodeling Quiescence and Reactivation

## Core Principle
After mineralization completes, the bone surface enters a **quiescent state** maintained by bone-lining cells until reactivation signals trigger new remodeling.

## Quiescent Surface Characteristics

### Bone-Lining Cells
- **Origin:** Former osteoblasts that didn't become osteocytes or undergo apoptosis
- **Morphology:** Thin, flattened, spread across bone surface
- **Function:** 
  - Maintain bone-fluid barrier
  - Protect mineralized matrix
  - Sense mechanical and hormonal signals
  - Can dedifferentiate → proliferate → become active osteoblasts

### Surface State
- Covered by thin layer of non-mineralized material
- No active cellular resorption or formation
- Can persist for months to years
- Majority of bone surface at any given time

## Reactivation Triggers

### Mechanical Signals
| Signal |  | Effect |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| **Microdamage** | Crack formation in mineralized matrix | Osteocyte apoptosis → RANKL release |
| **Loading changes** | Increased/decreased mechanical strain | Osteocyte mechanotransduction |
| **Disuse** | Immobilization, spaceflight | Dramatically increased remodeling |

### Hormonal Signals
- **PTH (Parathyroid hormone):** Intermittent pulses favor formation; continuous favors resorption
- **Estrogen deficiency:** Increases activation frequency (postmenopausal)
- **Thyroid hormones:** Excess increases activation
- **Glucocorticoids:** Increase resorption, suppress formation

### Local Signals
- **Osteocyte apoptosis:** Release of RANKL, sclerostin suppression
- **Sclerostin reduction:** Wnt pathway activation → osteoblastogenesis
- **Inflammatory cytokines:** IL-1, IL-6, TNF-α stimulate resorption

## Sclerostin: Key Regulator

### Function
- Secreted by osteocytes
- Inhibits Wnt/β-catenin pathway
- Suppresses osteoblast proliferation and differentiation
- **High sclerostin:** Quiescence maintained
- **Low sclerostin:** Reactivation permitted

### Therapeutic Targeting
- **Romosozumab:** Anti-sclerostin antibody
- Mechanism: Blocks sclerostin → Wnt activation → increased formation + decreased resorption
- Unique dual action: Anabolic + antiresorptive

## Activation Frequency

### Definition
Number of new remodeling units initiated per unit of bone surface per unit time

### Variation by Site
| Location | Activation Frequency | Turnover Rate |
|----------|---------------------|---------------|
| **Trabecular bone** | High | 20–30% per year |
| **Cortical bone (endosteal)** | Moderate | 2–5% per year |
| **Cortical bone (periosteal)** | Low | <1% per year |
| **Haversian systems** | Variable | Depends on mechanical loading |

###  Variation
- **Young adult:** Balanced activation throughout skeleton
- **Postmenopausal female:** 3–5× increased activation frequency
- **Senile osteoporosis:** Decreased activation but prolonged resorption phase

## Duration and Statistics

### Temporal Characteristics
- **Duration:** Months to years (highly variable)
- **Intervention window:** Therapies target activation frequency
- **Bone age:** Average time since last remodeling varies by skeletal site

### Bone Surface Distribution
At any moment:
- **Quiescent:** 80–90% of bone surface
- **Resorbing:** 2–5%
- **Forming:** 5–10%
