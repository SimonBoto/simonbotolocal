## Core Claim
**Oxaloacetate cannot cross the inner mitochondrial membrane — two shuttle systems transfer OAA equivalents to the cytosol for gluconeogenesis: the malate-aspartate shuttle (transfers reducing equivalents as NADH, preferred for gluconeogenesis) and the aspartate shuttle (transfers nitrogen, used when NADH not needed)** — these shuttles coordinate mitochondrial and cytosolic metabolism.

## Evidence

### The Problem
| Issue | Constraint |
|-------|------------|
| **OAA impermeability** | Cannot cross inner mitochondrial membrane |
| **PEPCK location** | Cytosolic (needs OAA in cytosol) |
| **Solution** | Convert OAA to permeable intermediates |

### Malate-Aspartate Shuttle
| Step | Reaction | Location |
|------|----------|----------|
| **1** | OAA + NADH → Malate + NAD⁺ | Mitochondrial (malate dehydrogenase) |
| **2** | Malate transported to cytosol | Malate-α-ketoglutarate antiporter |
| **3** | Malate + NAD⁺ → OAA + NADH | Cytosolic (malate dehydrogenase) |

**Key feature:** Transfers 1 NADH per OAA

### Aspartate Shuttle
| Step | Reaction | Location |
|------|----------|----------|
| **1** | OAA + Glutamate → Aspartate + α-KG | Mitochondrial (AST) |
| **2** | Aspartate transported to cytosol | Aspartate-glutamate antiporter |
| **3** | Aspartate + α-KG → OAA + Glutamate | Cytosolic (AST) |

**Key feature:** No net NADH transfer

### Shuttle Comparison
| Feature | Malate Shuttle | Aspartate Shuttle |
|---------|---------------|-------------------|
| **Reducing equivalents** | Transfers NADH | No NADH transfer |
| **Use case** | Gluconeogenesis (needs NADH) | Urea cycle (nitrogen transfer) |
| **Energy cost** | None | None |

## Clinical Implication
- **Shuttle dysfunction:** Impairs gluconeogenesis
- **NADH requirement:** Gluconeogenesis needs cytosolic NADH (malate shuttle)
- **Tissue specificity:** Liver and kidney have robust shuttle capacity
- **METHAP relevance:** Efficient shuttles support gluconeogenesis; mitochondrial health ensures shuttle function
