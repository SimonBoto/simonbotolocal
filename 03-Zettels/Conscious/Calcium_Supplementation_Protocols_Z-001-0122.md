# Calcium Supplementation Protocols

## Clinical Context
Calcium supplementation is foundational for osteoporosis treatment and bone health. Adequate calcium status is mandatory for all osteoporosis therapies to be effective.

## Physiological Requirements
- **Daily requirement:** 1000–1200 mg/day (diet + supplement)
- **Primary absorption:** Duodenum and jejunum (vitamin D dependent)
- **Regulation:** PTH, calcitriol, calcitonin

## Dietary Sources
| Source | Calcium Content (mg) |
|--------|-----------------------|
| Milk (1 cup) | 300 |
| Yogurt (1 cup) | 400 |
| Cheese (1 oz) | 200 |
| Salmon (3 oz, canned with bones) | 180 |
| Kale (1 cup, cooked) | 100 |

## Supplementation Guidelines
- **Calcium carbonate:** 40% elemental calcium, requires acidic environment (take with meals)
- **Calcium citrate:** 21% elemental calcium, bioavailable without food
- **Maximum single dose:** 500–600 mg (absorption declines above this)

## Monitoring
- **Baseline:** Serum calcium, PTH
- **Annual:** Serum calcium, PTH

## Safety Thresholds
- **Calcium safe upper limit:** 2000–2500 mg/day
- **Risk of excess:** Constipation, renal stones, vascular calcification

## Integration Notes
- Assessment of calcium status is mandatory before starting any osteoporosis therapy
- Supplementation improves treatment efficacy and reduces fracture risk independently
- Individualized dosing based on dietary intake and response
