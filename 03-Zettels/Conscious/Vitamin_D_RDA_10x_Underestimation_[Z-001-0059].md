#  RDA 10x Underestimation

Corrected calculations using proper statistical methods (accounting for variability to cover 97.5% of population) show the RDA should be 7,000-8,000 IU/day, not the IOM's 600-800 IU. This represents a factor of 10 underestimation affecting global vitamin D status.

**Numbers:**
- **IOM RDA:** 600 IU (1-70 years), 800 IU (>70 years)
- **Corrected RDA:** 7,000-8,000 IU/day
- **Ratio:** ~10-13x underestimation
- **Population impact:** Billions receiving inadequate recommendations

**Why this matters:**
- Most people need 8,000+ IU to reach optimal levels (40-60 ng/mL)
- Current RDA maintains population-wide deficiency/suboptimal status
- High-dose protocols (like METHAP) are actually physiological, not pharmacological

**Clinical translation:** What appears "high dose" (4,000-8,000 IU) is actually physiological replacement.
