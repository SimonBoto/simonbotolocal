## Core Claim
**Bile acids — synthesized from cholesterol in the liver through 7α-hydroxylation (rate-limiting step) — serve as essential detergents for dietary fat emulsification and absorption, with 95% recycled via enterohepatic circulation, representing the primary pathway for cholesterol excretion and a therapeutic target for hypercholesterolemia through bile acid sequestrants.**

## Evidence

### Synthesis Pathway
| Step | Enzyme | Reaction | Regulation |
|------|--------|----------|------------|
| **1 (Rate-limiting)** | CYP7A1 | Cholesterol → 7α-hydroxycholesterol | Bile acid feedback |
| **2** | Multiple | → Cholic acid (3 OH) | Alternative pathway |
| **3** | Multiple | → Chenodeoxycholic acid (2 OH) | Classic pathway |
| **4** | Conjugation | + Glycine or Taurine | Bile salts formed |

### Primary vs Secondary Bile Acids
| Type | Examples | Source | Properties |
|------|----------|--------|------------|
| **Primary** | Cholic acid, Chenodeoxycholic acid | Liver synthesized | Hydrophilic |
| **Secondary** | Deoxycholic acid, Lithocholic acid | Gut bacteria modified | Hydrophobic, more potent |

### Enterohepatic Circulation
| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Efficiency** | 95% recycled |
| **Daily loss** | 0.5g (fecal excretion) |
| **Synthesis compensation** | ↑ when loss increases |
| **Regulation** | Bile acids inhibit CYP7A1 |

### Functions
| Function | Mechanism | Clinical Relevance |
|----------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Fat emulsification** | Detergent action | Essential for lipid digestion |
| **Micelle formation** | Solubilize lipids | Fat-soluble vitamin absorption |
| **Cholesterol excretion** | Biliary secretion | Major elimination route |
| **Signaling** | FXR, TGR5 receptors | Metabolic regulation |

### Therapeutic Manipulation
| Intervention | Mechanism | Effect on Cholesterol |
|--------------|-----------|----------------------|
| **Cholestyramine** | Bile acid sequestrant | ↑ Synthesis, ↓ LDL |
| **Colesevelam** | Bile acid sequestrant | ↓ LDL, improves glucose |
| **Ileal bypass** | Surgical interruption | Dramatic LDL reduction |

### Clinical Correlations
| Condition | Mechanism | Presentation |
|-----------|-----------|--------------|
| **Cholestasis** | Impaired bile flow | Jaundice, malabsorption |
| **Bile acid diarrhea** | Malabsorption | Watery diarrhea |
| **Gallstones** | Cholesterol supersaturation | Biliary colic |
| **CYP7A1 deficiency** | Blocked synthesis | Hypercholesterolemia |

### Statin Effects
| Aspect | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| **Bile acid composition** | May alter slightly |
| **Cholesterol → Bile acids** | ↑ Compensatory synthesis |
| **Enterohepatic efficiency** | Unchanged |
| **Clinical significance** | Minor |

## Clinical Implication
- **Major excretion route:** Only way to eliminate cholesterol from body
- **Efficient recycling:** 95% conservation minimizes synthesis burden
- **Therapeutic target:** Sequestrants lower LDL by 15-20%
- **Nutrient absorption:** Essential for fat-soluble vitamins (A, D, E, K)
- **METHAP relevance:** Bile acid metabolism connects to cholesterol homeostasis and metabolic syndrome; understanding enables therapeutic interventions
