# ROS-Mediated Telomere Damage Pathways

## Mechanisms of Oxidative Damage to Telomeres

### 1. Direct Base Oxidation

**8-Oxoguanine (8-oxoG) Formation:**
- Most common oxidative DNA lesion
- Guanine has lowest oxidation potential
- Telomeres are G-rich (TTAGGG) = vulnerable

**Consequences:**
- Mutagenic: 8-oxoG can pair with adenine (G→T transversions)
- Blocks replication: DNA polymerase stalls at 8-oxoG
- Repair required before cell division

---

### 2. Single-Strand Breaks (SSBs)

**Mechanism:**
- Sugar oxidation (abasic site formation)
- Direct strand cleavage at oxidized bases
- More common in single-strand regions (3' overhang)

**Telomere Impact:**
- SSBs in telomere = signal for DDR activation
- Shelterin must distinguish from true damage
- Excessive SSBs trigger senescence

---

### 3. Telomere Uncapping

**ROS Effects on Shelterin:**
- Direct oxidation of shelterin proteins
- Reduced DNA binding affinity
- Loss of T-loop structure

**Consequence:**
- Exposed chromosome end
- Recognized as DNA double-strand break
- ATM/ATR activation
- Cell cycle arrest or apoptosis


---

## Amplification in Autoimmune Diseases

### Inflammation Drives ROS
**Sources:**
- Neutrophil oxidative burst
- Macrophage respiratory burst
-  dysfunction from cytokines

**Cytokines:**
- TNF-α: Activates NADPH oxidase
- IL-1β: Mitochondrial ROS induction
- IFN-γ: Increases ROS production

### Chronic vs. Acute
**Acute Inflammation:**
- Short duration
- Repair mechanisms sufficient
- Limited telomere impact

**Chronic Inflammation (Autoimmunity):**
- Years to decades
- Overwhelms repair capacity
- Cumulative telomere damage
