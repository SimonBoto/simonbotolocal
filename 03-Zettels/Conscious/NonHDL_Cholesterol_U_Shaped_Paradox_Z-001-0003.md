# Non-HDL Cholesterol U-Shaped Paradox

## Source
[[Diabetes_CVD_Global_Burden_NEJM_2023_L-001-0001]]

## Core Claim
Non-HDL cholesterol exhibits a U-shaped association with mortality risk, where both very high levels and very low levels are associated with increased all-cause mortality risk.

## Key Details
- Very high non-HDL cholesterol levels → 1.6x increased CVD risk
- Very low non-HDL cholesterol levels → 1.6x increased all-cause death risk
- This creates an optimal range rather than a simple 'lower is better' paradigm
- The paradox suggests that non-HDL cholesterol serves essential biological functions at optimal levels
- This finding challenges conventional lipid management approaches

## Evidence
- Source-litnote extraction pending
- Evidence from NEJM 2023 study with 1.5 million participants across 34 countries

## Significance
This finding represents a fundamental shift in understanding lipid metabolism, indicating that cholesterol levels serve essential physiological functions that become detrimental when either too high or too low.

## Clinical Significance
- Supports personalized lipid management targets based on individual risk profiles
- Indicates need for caution with aggressive lipid-lowering therapies
- Highlights importance of monitoring for adverse effects of very low cholesterol levels
- Provides evidence for optimal cholesterol ranges in clinical guidelines
