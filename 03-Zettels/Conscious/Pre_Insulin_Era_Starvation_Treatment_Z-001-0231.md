# Pre- Era — The Starvation Treatment for Diabetes

> Before the discovery of insulin in 1921, Type 1 diabetes was a fatal diagnosis with no effective treatment; the only available therapy was severe caloric restriction (starvation diets) that could prolong life by months at the cost of severe mal.

## The Pre-Insulin Prognosis

**Type 1 Diabetes Before 1921:**
- **Life expectancy:** 1-3 years after diagnosis
- **Cause of death:** Diabetic ketoacidosis (DKA), infection, wasting
- **Age of onset:** Typically children and young adults ("juvenile diabetes")
- **No effective therapy:** Medicine was powerless

**Clinical Course:**
1. Polyuria (excessive urination)
2. Polydipsia (excessive thirst)
3. Weight loss despite increased appetite
4. Progressive weakness
5. Coma and death

## Frederick M. Allen's Starvation Diet

**Development:**
- **Pioneer:** Dr. Frederick M. Allen (physician)
- **Era:** 1910s-1920s (pre-insulin)
- **Rationale:** If diabetes is inability to metabolize carbohydrates, eliminate carbohydrates

**Dietary Prescription:**
- **Calories:** <1000 calories/day (often 400-800)
- **Carbohydrates:** Minimal (<10g/day)
- **Protein:** Severely restricted
- **Fat:** Primary caloric source
- **Frequency:** Multiple small meals

**Outcomes:**
- **Positive:** Could prolong life months to 1-2 years
- **Negative:** 
  - Severe malnutrition
  - Muscle wasting
  - Weakness and debility
  - Constant hunger
  - Poor quality of life

## Patient Experience

**Physical State:**
- Emaciated appearance
- Cachexia (wasting syndrome)
- Minimal body fat
- Muscle atrophy
- Cold intolerance

**Psychological Impact:**
- Constant hunger
- Depression
- Hopelessness
- Social isolation

## Historical Significance

**Comparison to Post-Insulin:**
- Starvation: Prolonged dying
- Insulin: Normal life possible
- The contrast illustrates insulin's miraculous impact

**Medical Ethics Context:**
- Only available option
- "Lesser of two evils"
- Foundation for dietary management even post-insulin

## Transition to Insulin Era

**Leonard Thompson (1922):**
- On starvation diet when treated
- Weighed 65 pounds at age 14
- Insulin transformed him from near-death to health
- Demonstrated starvation was no longer necessary

## Legacy

**Lessons Learned:**
- Importance of carbohydrate 
- Dietary management remains key (even with insulin)
- Desperation drives innovation
- Progress in medicine is not always linear

**Modern Perspective:**
- Gratitude for insulin availability
- Understanding of diabetes severity without treatment
- Appreciation for research progress
