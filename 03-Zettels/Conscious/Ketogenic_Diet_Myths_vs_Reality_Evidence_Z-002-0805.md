## Core Claim
Common concerns about ketogenic diets are **not supported by scientific evidence** — the largest study found higher carb intake, not lower, associated with increased mortality.

## Myth vs Reality

### Myth 1: Low-Carb Increases Heart Disease Risk
| Claim | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| "Low-carb harms heart" | **FALSE** |
| **Reality** | Largest study (135,335 people, 18 countries): **higher carb intake linked to increased mortality** |

### Myth 2: "Keto Flu" Is Unavoidable and Dangerous
| Claim | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| "Keto flu is inevitable" | **FALSE** |
| **Reality** | Symptoms (fatigue, dizziness, cramps, headache, brain fog) caused by **fluid/electrolyte loss** |
| **Prevention** | Salt, electrolytes, magnesium prevent symptoms |

### Myth 3: Low-Carb Is Unbalanced Long-Term
| Claim | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| "Not sustainable" | **FALSE** |
| **Reality** | Can be nutritionally complete when properly formulated |

## Evidence Base
| Study | Finding |
|-------|---------|
| **PURE study** | Higher carb = higher mortality |
| **Multiple RCTs** | Low-carb safe and effective |

## Significance

Common concerns about ketogenic diets are **not supported by scientific evidence** — the largest study found higher carb intake, not lower, associated with increased mortality.
