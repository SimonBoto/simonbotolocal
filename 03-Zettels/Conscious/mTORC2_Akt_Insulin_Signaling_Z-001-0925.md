# Chronic Sirolimus Inhibits mTORC2 Disrupting Akt  Signaling

**Core Mechanism:** While sirolimus acutely inhibits mTORC1, chronic or high-dose exposure also inhibits mTORC2, which is essential for Akt activation and insulin sensitivity.

## mTOR Complexes: Distinct Functions

### mTORC1 (Raptor-containing)
- **Acute sirolimus sensitivity:** High
- **Key functions:** Protein synthesis, autophagy, 
- **Substrates:** S6K1, 4E-BP1, ULK1

### mTORC2 (Rictor-containing)
- **Acute sirolimus sensitivity:** Low
- **Chronic sensitivity:** High (assembly disrupted)
- **Key functions:** Cell survival, metabolism, cytoskeleton
- **Substrates:** Akt, SGK, PKC

## mTORC2 → Akt Pathway

**Akt Activation Requires Two Phosphorylations:**
1. **Thr308** — Phosphorylated by PDK1 (PIP3-dependent)
2. **Ser473** — Phosphorylated by mTORC2

**Full Akt Activity:** Requires BOTH phosphorylations

### Downstream Akt Effects on Metabolism

| Target | Effect | Metabolic Outcome |
|--------|--------|-------------------|
| **AS160** | Phosphorylation | GLUT4 translocation |
| **GSK3β** | Inhibition | Glycogen synthesis |
| **FOXO1** | Exclusion from nucleus | Suppressed gluconeogenesis |
| **mTORC1** | Activation | Protein synthesis |
| **BAD** | Phosphorylation | Cell survival |

## Sirolimus Disruption

### Acute Exposure (mTORC1 only)
- mTORC1 inhibited
- mTORC2 intact → Akt Ser473 phosphorylation maintained
- Partial  (GLUT4 defect)
- Preserved cell survival signals

### Chronic/High-Dose Exposure (mTORC1 + mTORC2)
- **mTORC1 inhibited**
- **mTORC2 assembly disrupted → mTORC2 function lost**
- Akt Ser473 phosphorylation reduced
- **Partial Akt inactivation**

**Amplified Insulin Resistance:**
- mTORC1 effects (GLUT4) + mTORC2 effects (Akt)
- More severe metabolic dysfunction
- Broader impact on insulin signaling

## Experimental Evidence

**Cell Culture:**
- Acute rapamycin (hours): mTORC1 only
- Chronic rapamycin (days): mTORC2 also inhibited
- Rictor dissociates from mTOR over time

**Mouse Models:**
- Chronic rapamycin → glucose intolerance
- Liver-specific rictor knockout → insulin resistance
- Muscle-specific rictor knockout → impaired glucose uptake

**Human Studies:**
- Transplant patients on long-term sirolimus
- Correlation with duration/dose and diabetes risk
- Some evidence of mTORC2 disruption in PBMCs

## Tissue-Specific mTORC2 Functions

### Liver
- Akt-mediated suppression of gluconeogenesis
- mTORC2 loss → increased hepatic glucose output
- Contributes to fasting hyperglycemia

### Muscle
- Akt-mediated GLUT4 translocation
- mTORC2 loss → impaired glucose uptake
- Contributes to peripheral insulin resistance

### Adipose
- Akt-mediated glucose uptake and lipogenesis
- mTORC2 loss → altered lipid storage
- Contributes to lipotoxicity

## Clinical Implications

### Dose-Dependency
| Exposure | mTORC1 | mTORC2 | Metabolic Effect |
|----------|--------|--------|------------------|
| Acute/low | Inhibited | Spared | Moderate IR |
| Chronic/high | Inhibited | Inhibited | Severe IR |

### Therapeutic Considerations

**Intermittent Dosing:**
- Allows mTORC2 recovery between doses
- Preserves insulin sensitivity
- Potential longevity benefit with less metabolic risk

**Alternative mTOR Inhibitors:**
- ATP-competitive inhibitors (target both complexes equally)
- Different side effect profiles
- Less differential mTORC1 vs C2 selectivity

**Metformin Combination:**
- Metformin activates AMPK
- AMPK can partially compensate for mTOR pathway disruption
- Synergistic glucose-lowering
