# Keto Acids vs Carboxylic Acids

## Carboxylic Acids
**General formula:** R-COOH
- Carboxyl group at terminal position
- Acidic (donates H⁺)
- Examples: Acetate, propionate, lactate

## Keto Acids (α-Keto Acids)
**General formula:** R-CO-COOH
- Contains **both** ketone group AND carboxyl group
- Ketone at α-position (adjacent to carboxyl)
- Examples: Pyruvate, α-ketoglutarate, oxaloacetate

## Key Relationship
```
All keto acids ARE carboxylic acids
NOT all carboxylic acids are keto acids
```

## Common Examples

### Keto Acids
| Name | Structure | Role |
|------|-----------|------|
| **Pyruvate** | CH₃-CO-COOH | Glycolysis end product |
| **α-Ketoglutarate** | HOOC-CH₂-CH₂-CO-COOH | Krebs cycle |
| **Oxaloacetate** | HOOC-CH₂-CO-COOH | Krebs cycle, gluconeogenesis |

### Carboxylic Acids (Non-Keto)
| Name | Structure | Role |
|------|-----------|------|
| **Acetate** | CH₃-COOH | Acetyl-CoA precursor |
| **Propionate** | CH₃-CH₂-COOH | Gut metabolite, gluconeogenic |
| **Lactate** | CH₃-CHOH-COOH | Anaerobic glycolysis |

## Functional Implications

### Reactivity
- **Keto acids:** Can undergo transamination (amino group transfer)
- **Carboxylic acids:** Generally more stable

### Metabolism
- **Keto acids:** Often metabolic intermediates (reversible)
- **Carboxylic acids:** Often end products or substrates
