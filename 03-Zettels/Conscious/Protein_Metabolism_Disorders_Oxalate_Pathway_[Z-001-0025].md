# Protein Metabolism Disorders & Oxalate Pathway

## Core Claim
**Oxalate metabolism demonstrates the metabolite pathway analysis approach** — substrate accumulation, alternative pathway activation, and urinary excretion patterns applicable to adipic acid and other urine organic acid (OA) markers.

## The Oxalate Pathway

### Primary Hyperoxaluria
| Type | Enzyme Defect | Metabolic Consequence |
|------|---------------|----------------------|
| Type 1 | Alanine-glyoxylate aminotransferase (AGXT) | ↑ Glyoxylate → oxalate |
| Type 2 | Glyoxylate reductase/hydroxypyruvate reductase (GRHPR) | ↑ Hydroxypyruvate → oxalate |
| Type 3 | 4-hydroxy-2-oxoglutarate aldolase (HOGA1) | ↑ 4-hydroxy-2-oxoglutarate → oxalate |

### Diagnostic Pattern
```
Enzyme deficiency
       ↓
Substrate accumulation (glyoxylate/hydroxypyruvate)
       ↓
Conversion to oxalate (non-enzymatic or alternative pathway)
       ↓
Calcium oxalate precipitation (kidney stones)
       ↓
Elevated urinary oxalate (diagnostic marker)
```

## Dietary Contribution

> **Crivelli et al. (2021)** contribution of dietary oxalate and oxalate precursors to urinary oxalate excretion

### Sources
| Source | Examples | Contribution |
|--------|----------|--------------|
| **Dietary oxalate** | Spinach, rhubarb, beets | 10-50% of urinary oxalate |
| **Vitamin C** (ascorbic acid) | Supplements | Metabolized to oxalate |
| **Protein metabolism** | Glycine, hydroxyproline | Endogenous production |
| **Fructose metabolism** | High fructose intake | Increased endogenous synthesis |

## METHAP Relevance: Urine OA Class

### Oxalate-Adipic Acid Parallel
| Feature | Oxalate | Adipic Acid |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| **Class** | Urine organic acid | Urine organic acid |
| **Pathway** | Amino acid/glyoxylate | Lysine/tryptophan + fatty acid oxidation |
| **Accumulation cause** | Enzyme deficiency (primary) or diet (secondary) | Mitochondrial dysfunction (enzyme impairment) |
| **Diagnostic sample** | 24h urine | Urine organic acids panel |
| **Detection method** | LC-MS/MS | LC-MS/MS |

### Key Insight
Both oxalate and adipic acid use **identical analytical methodology** — the urine organic acid panel established for IMD diagnosis directly applies to IR biomarker detection.

## Clinical Application

### Urine OA Panel Interpretation
When analyzing urine organic acids, clinicians assess:
1. **Single metabolite elevation** — Specific enzyme block
2. **Pattern recognition** — Multiple pathway involvement
3. **Dietary vs. endogenous** — Origin determination
4. **Severity grading** — Quantitative thresholds

**Adipic acid application:** Same principles — single marker (adipic) + pattern (other OA) = mitochondrial dysfunction severity.
