# Coffee 2-3 Cups/Day Provides Optimal Dementia Protection

## Core Claim
**Moderate coffee consumption of 2-3 cups per day provides optimal protection against dementia** — the 37-year study found this dose range associated with the lowest dementia risk, with both lower and higher intakes showing less benefit.

## The Evidence

### Study Design
| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Participants** | 132,000 |
| **Follow-up** | 37 years |
| **Dementia cases** | 11,000 |

### Dose-Response Curve
| Intake | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| **None** | Baseline risk |
| **1 cup/day** | Some benefit |
| **2-3 cups/day** | ✅ Optimal protection |
| **>4 cups/day** | Benefit may plateau |

### Tea Also Protective
- **1-2 cups/day** = protective
- Similar caffeine content

## Clinical Implications
- Moderate coffee consumption can be recommended
- Part of dementia prevention strategy
- Consistent across 37 years = robust association
