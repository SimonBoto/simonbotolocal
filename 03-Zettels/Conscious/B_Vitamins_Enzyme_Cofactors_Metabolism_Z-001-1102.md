# B Vitamins as Enzyme Cofactors in Metabolism

## Core Claim
**B vitamins serve as essential cofactors for hundreds of metabolic enzymes, with each B vitamin having specific, non-overlapping roles: B1 (TPP) for decarboxylation, B2 (FAD) for redox, B3 (NAD) for electron transfer, B5 (CoA) for acyl transfer, and B6 (PLP) for amino acid metabolism** — deficiency in any impairs multiple pathways.

## Evidence

### B-Vitamin Functions
| Vitamin | Cofactor | Key Enzymes/Pathways | Deficiency |
|---------|----------|---------------------|------------|
| **B1 (Thiamine)** | TPP | Pyruvate DH, α-ketoglutarate DH, transketolase | Beriberi, Wernicke-Korsakoff |
| **B2 (Riboflavin)** | FAD/FMN | Complex I/II, acyl-CoA DHs, glutathione reductase | Mucocutaneous lesions |
| **B3 (Niacin)** | NAD⁺/NADP⁺ | Glycolysis, Krebs, fatty acid oxidation, sirtuins | Pellagra |
| **B5 (Pantothenate)** | CoA | Fatty acid oxidation/synthesis, Krebs cycle | Fatigue, paresthesia |
| **B6 (Pyridoxine)** | PLP | Transaminases, decarboxylases, glycogen phosphorylase | Microcytic anemia, neuropathy |
| **B7 (Biotin)** | Biotin | PCC, ACC (carboxylases) | Dermatitis, alopecia |
| **B9 (Folate)** | THF | One-carbon metabolism, DNA synthesis | Megaloblastic anemia, NTDs |
| **B12 (Cobalamin)** | AdoCbl/MeCbl | Methylmalonyl-CoA mutase, methionine synthase | Megaloblastic anemia, neuropathy |

## Clinical Implication
- **B-vitamins work as a team** — isolated deficiency rare, multiple deficiencies common
- **High metabolic demand** (athletes, stress, illness) increases B-vitamin needs
- **METHAP relevance:** High-dose B-complex supports all metabolic pathways; deficiency undermines protocol efficacy
