# Fructose as Glycolytic Intermediate

## The Fructose Step in 
```
Glucose-6-phosphate → Fructose-6-phosphate (phosphoglucose isomerase)
```

## Why Fructose as Intermediate?

### Structural Reason
- Fructose is a **ketose** (ketone at C2)
- Enables aldol cleavage by aldolase
- Aldolase splits C3-C4 bond of F1,6BP

### ary Insight
- **Fructose as intermediate ≠ dietary fructose**
- Glycolysis evolved to process glucose efficiently
- F6P/F1,6BP are metabolic intermediates, not "fructose "

## Key Intermediates

### Fructose-6-phosphate (F6P)
- Isomer of G6P
- Branch point: glycolysis vs PPP vs glycogen

### Fructose-1,6-bisphosphate (F1,6BP)
- **Committed step product**
- Split by aldolase → DHAP + G3P
- No regulatory bypass once formed

## Common Confusion
**Misconception:** Fructose in glycolysis means fructose was "introduced earlier" evolutionarily.

**Reality:**
- Fructose as free sugar is different from F6P intermediate
- Glucose is the primary energy source evolutionarily
- Fructose intermediate is structural requirement for cleavage
