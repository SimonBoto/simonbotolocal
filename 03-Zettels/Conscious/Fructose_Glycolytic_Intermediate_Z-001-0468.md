## Core Claim
**Fructose-6-phosphate is an essential intermediate in glycolysis — the isomerization from glucose-6-phosphate enables the aldol cleavage that splits the 6-carbon skeleton into two 3-carbon trioses** — this is distinct from dietary fructose metabolism, which bypasses regulation and drives lipogenesis.

## Evidence

### The Fructose Step
```
Glucose-6-phosphate → Fructose-6-phosphate (phosphoglucose isomerase)
```

### Why Fructose as Intermediate?
| Feature | Significance |
|---------|--------------|
| **Ketose structure** | Enables aldol cleavage by aldolase |
| **C3-C4 bond** | Split point for F1,6BP → DHAP + G3P |
| **Evolutionary optimization** | Most efficient glucose processing |

### Key Intermediates
| Intermediate | Role |
|--------------|------|
| **Fructose-6-phosphate (F6P)** | Branch point (glycolysis/PPP/glycogen) |
| **Fructose-1,6-bisphosphate (F1,6BP)** | Committed step product; no regulatory bypass |

### Critical Distinction
| Aspect | F6P Intermediate | Dietary Fructose |
|--------|------------------|------------------|
| **Source** | Glucose isomerization | Diet (fruit, HFCS) |
| **Regulation** | Controlled (PFK-1) | Bypasses PFK-1 |
| **Fate** | Energy/biosynthesis | Rapid lipogenesis |
| **Metabolic context** | Universal | Liver-centric |

## Clinical Implication
- **Common confusion:** Fructose in glycolysis ≠ dietary fructose benefits
- **Dietary fructose:** Unregulated hepatic uptake → NAFLD, metabolic syndrome
- **F6P intermediate:** Essential for all glucose metabolism
- **METHAP relevance:** Understanding the distinction informs dietary recommendations — glucose metabolism is regulated; excessive dietary fructose overwhelms regulation
