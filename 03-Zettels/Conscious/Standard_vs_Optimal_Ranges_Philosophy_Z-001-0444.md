# Standard vs Optimal Ranges —  Philosophy

> Standard laboratory ranges are derived from population averages and may miss functional deficiencies, while optimal ranges target peak physiological performance and prevention.

## Standard (Population) Ranges

**Basis:**
- 95% of population (2 standard deviations)
- Includes healthy and unhealthy individuals
- Statistically derived

**Limitations:**
- Wide ranges (e.g., B12 200-900)
- Lower cutoff may miss subclinical issues
- "Normal" doesn't mean "optimal"
- Reactive, not preventive

## Optimal (Functional) Ranges

**Basis:**
- Physiological function
- Disease prevention
- Peak performance

**Characteristics:**
- Narrower ranges
- More stringent cutoffs
- Goal: Optimal, not just adequate
- Proactive approach

## Clinical Implication

**Standard:** "Not sick"
**Optimal:** "Performing at best"

**METHAP Approach:**
- Target optimal ranges
- Prevent deficiency
- Maximize protocol benefits
- Monitor trends, not just thresholds
