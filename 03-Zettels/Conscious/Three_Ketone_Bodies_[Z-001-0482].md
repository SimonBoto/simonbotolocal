# The Three Ketone Bodies

## 1. Acetoacetate (AcAc)
- **First synthesized**
- **True ketone:** Carbonyl C=O at β-position
- **Formula:** CH₃-CO-CH₂-COOH
- **Fates:**
  - Converted to BHB (reversible)
  - Spontaneous decarboxylation → acetone
  - Exported to tissues for energy

## 2. Beta-Hydroxybutyrate (BHB)
- **Major circulating ketone** (70-80% during ketosis)
- **NOT a true ketone:** Hydroxy acid (β-hydroxybutyrate)
- **Formula:** CH₃-CHOH-CH₂-COOH
- **Most reduced form:** Highest energy content
- **Transported to tissues** for conversion back to AcAc

## 3. Acetone
- **Spontaneous breakdown** of acetoacetate (non-enzymatic)
- **Least abundant:** ~2% of ketone bodies
- **Volatile:** Exhaled ("fruity breath")
- **Not metabolized for energy**
- **Eliminated:** Via lungs, urine

## Interconversion
```
Acetoacetate ↔ BHB (β-OHBDH, NADH/NAD⁺ dependent)
     ↓
Acetone + CO₂ (spontaneous)
```

## Ratios in Different States
| State | AcAc : BHB | Acetone |
|-------|------------|---------|
| Normal (fed) | Minimal | Minimal |
| Fasting | 1:3 | Low |
| Ketoacidosis | 1:1 or higher | Elevated |
