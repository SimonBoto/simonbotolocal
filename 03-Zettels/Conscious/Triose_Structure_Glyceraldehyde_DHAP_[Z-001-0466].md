# Triose Structure: Glyceraldehyde and DHAP

## The Simplest Sugars
**Trioses:** 3-carbon monosaccharides — foundational to metabolism.

## Glyceraldehyde
- **Type:** Aldotriose (aldehyde group)
- **Formula:** C₃H₆O₃
- **Key form:** Glyceraldehyde-3-phosphate (G3P)
- **Chirality:** D- and L- enantiomers (D-G3P in metabolism)

## Dihydroxyacetone
- **Type:** Ketotriose (ketone group)
- **Formula:** C₃H₆O₃
- **Key form:** Dihydroxyacetone phosphate (DHAP)

## Isomeric Relationship
**Same formula, different structure:**
- Constitutional isomers (different connectivity)
- Interconvertible in metabolism

## Metabolic Significance
**G3P and DHAP:**
- Central to glycolysis
- PPP intermediates
- Lipid biosynthesis precursors
- Calvin cycle intermediates (photosynthesis)

## Triose Phosphate Isomerase
**Fastest enzyme:** kcat ~10⁴ s⁻¹
- Rapidly equilibrates DHAP ↔ G3P
- Near-equilibrium: ~20:1 DHAP:G3P ratio
- Essential for glycolytic flux
