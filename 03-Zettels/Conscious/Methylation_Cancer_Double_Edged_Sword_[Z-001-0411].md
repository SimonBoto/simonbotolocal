# Methylation and [[KEYWORD-Cancer|cancer]] — Double-Edged Sword

> Aberrant DNA methylation patterns contribute to carcinogenesis through hypermethylation (silencing tumor suppressors) and hypomethylation (activating oncogenes and genomic instability), creating complex therapeutic challenges.

## Hypermethylation — Gene Silencing

**[[KEYWORD-Mechanism|mechanism]]:**
- CpG island methylation at gene promoters
- DNMT overactivity or mislocalization
- Stable, heritable silencing

**Targets:**

| Gene Category | Examples | Function Lost |
|---------------|----------|---------------|
| **Cell cycle regulators** | p16INK4a, Rb | Cell cycle arrest |
| **DNA repair** | BRCA1, MLH1, MGMT | DNA repair capacity |
| **Apoptosis regulators** | DAPK1, TMS1 | Programmed cell death |
| **Adhesion molecules** | E-cadherin | Metastasis suppression |

**Consequence:** Uncontrolled proliferation, genomic instability

## Hypomethylation — Genome Activation

**Mechanism:**
- Global loss of methylation
- DNMT underactivity
- Passive or active demethylation

**Targets:**

| Element | Effect |
|---------|--------|
| **Repetitive elements** | LINEs, SINEs activated → genomic instability |
| **Oncogenes** | H-Ras, c-Myc overexpression |
| **Promoters of invasion** | MMPs, angiogenesis factors activated |

**Consequence:** Genomic chaos, activation of harmful genes

## The Therapeutic Paradox

**Folate and Cancer:**

| Context | Effect |
|---------|--------|
| **Pre-cancerous lesions** | Folate may prevent (corrects hypomethylation) |
| **Established tumors** | High folate may promote (fuels proliferation) |

**[[KEYWORD-Clinical|clinical]] Implication:**
- Timing matters
- Population vs. individual
- Prevention vs. treatment

**DNMT Inhibitors (Therapeutic):**
- Azacitidine (5-aza-CR)
- Decitabine (5-aza-CdR)
- Reverse hypermethylation of tumor suppressors
- Approved for myelodysplastic syndromes

## METHAP Consideration

**High-Dose Nutrients:**
- D may demethylate tumor suppressors (beneficial)
- B12/folate support normal methylation (beneficial)
- But context matters
- Monitoring essential
