# Methionine — Essential Sulfur Amino Acid

> Methionine is an essential sulfur-containing amino acid that serves as the precursor for SAM-dependent methylation reactions and, through transsulfuration, cysteine and glutathione synthesis.

## Structure

**Chemical Features:**
- Sulfur-containing (thioether bond)
- Methyl-thio group (-S-CH₃)
- Hydrophobic side chain

## Essentiality

**Cannot Be Synthesized:**
- No de novo synthesis in humans
- Must be obtained from diet
- Rate-limiting for protein synthesis

## Dietary Sources

**Animal Proteins:**
- Meat, poultry, fish
- Eggs
- Dairy products

**Plant Proteins:**
- Legumes (beans, lentils)
- Nuts and seeds
- Lower content than animal sources

## Metabolic Fates

1. **Protein synthesis:** Incorporation into proteins
2. **SAM synthesis:** Methylation reactions
3. **Cysteine synthesis:** Transsulfuration
4. **Energy:** Complete oxidation
