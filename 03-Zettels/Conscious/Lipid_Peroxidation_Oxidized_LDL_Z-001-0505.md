# Lipid Peroxidation and Oxidized LDL

## Core Claim
Oxidative modification of LDL transforms it into a pro-inflammatory, pro-atherogenic particle (oxLDL) that is recognized by scavenger receptors on macrophages, leading to unregulated uptake, foam cell formation, and atherosclerotic plaque development.

## Evidence

### Lipid Peroxidation Process

**Initiation:**
- Free radicals attack polyunsaturated fatty acids (PUFAs) in LDL
- Vitamin E depletion allows oxidation cascade
- Metal ions (Cu²⁺, Fe²⁺) catalyze radical formation

**Propagation:**
- Lipid peroxyl radicals form (chain reaction)
- Lipid hydroperoxides accumulate
- Oxidation spreads to adjacent lipids

**Termination Products:**

| Product | Source | Biological Effect |
|---------|--------|-------------------|
| **4-HNE** (4-hydroxynonenal) | Omega-6 PUFA oxidation | Toxic aldehyde, protein modification, signaling |
| **MDA** (malondialdehyde) | General lipid peroxidation | Marker of oxidative stress |
| **Oxysterols** | Cholesterol oxidation | LXR activation, cytotoxicity |

### OxLDL and Atherosclerosis

**Mechanism:**
```
Native LDL → Oxidative modification → oxLDL
    ↓
Scavenger receptors (SR-A, CD36) recognize oxLDL
    ↓
Unregulated macrophage uptake (no negative feedback)
    ↓
Foam cell formation → Fatty streak → Atherosclerotic plaque
```

**Pro-atherogenic Effects:**

| Effect | Mechanism | Consequence |
|--------|-----------|-------------|
| **Pro-inflammatory** | Activates endothelial cells, increases adhesion molecules | Monocyte recruitment |
| **Cytotoxic** | Direct toxicity to endothelial cells | Endothelial dysfunction |
| **Chemotactic** | Attracts monocytes to subendothelial space | Inflammatory cascade |
| **Immunogenic** | Generates antibodies, immune complexes | Autoimmune component |

### Endothelial Dysfunction

- Reduced NO bioavailability
- Increased adhesion molecules (VCAM-1, ICAM-1)
- Enhanced monocyte recruitment
- Pro-thrombotic environment

## Clinical Implication

**Risk Factors for LDL Oxidation:**
- High PUFA intake (especially oxidized/heated vegetable oils)
- Low antioxidant status (vitamins E, C)
- Smoking (oxidant exposure)
- Diabetes (hyperglycemia increases oxidation)

**Antioxidant Protection:**
- Vitamin E: Chain-breaking antioxidant in LDL
- Vitamin C: Regenerates vitamin E
- Polyphenols: Direct antioxidant effects
- **Caution:** Antioxidant supplements have not consistently reduced CVD in trials

**Therapeutic Implications:**
- Address root causes of oxidative stress (inflammation, smoking, poor diet)
- Statins have pleiotropic antioxidant effects independent of LDL lowering

## METHAP Relevance

**Oxidative Stress Assessment:**
- Oxidized LDL as marker of oxidative stress and inflammation
- Combined with hsCRP for comprehensive inflammatory risk
- Address through lifestyle (diet, exercise, smoking cessation) rather than supplements alone
