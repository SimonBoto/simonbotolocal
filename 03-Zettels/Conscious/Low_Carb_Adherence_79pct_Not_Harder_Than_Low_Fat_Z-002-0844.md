## Core Claim
**Contrary to common assumption, low-carb diets have a 79.51% completion rate compared to 77.72% for low-fat diets** — low-carb is actually slightly easier to adhere to long-term.

## The Common Assumption (FALSE)
> "Low-carb is harder to maintain than low-fat or calorie counting"

## The Evidence (23 studies)
| Diet Type | Completion Rate |
|-----------|-----------------|
| **Low-carb** | **79.51%** |
| **Low-fat** | 77.72% |

**Conclusion:** Low-carb is **NOT harder to maintain** — slightly easier.

## Why Better Adherence?
| Factor | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| **Restored satiety** | Better long-term dietary control |
| **Visible health improvements** | High motivation to continue |
| **Protein and fat** | More satisfying than low-fat |

## Clinical Implication
- Concerns about low-carb adherence are **unfounded**
- Patients can successfully maintain low-carb diets **long-term**
