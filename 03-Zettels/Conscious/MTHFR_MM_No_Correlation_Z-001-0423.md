# MTHFR and Multiple Myeloma — No Correlation

> Despite affecting folate  and bone marrow function, MTHFR polymorphisms are not directly correlated with multiple myeloma pathogenesis.

## Evidence

**No Strong Association:**
- No consistent genetic linkage studies
- MTHFR variants not enriched in MM patients
- MM primarily acquired, not inherited

**Population Genetics:**
- MTHFR variants common (10-40% have variants)
- If causal, MM would be more common
- Not observed

## Distinct s

**B12/Folate Deficiency:**
- Impaired DNA synthesis
- Megaloblastic changes
- Reversible with replacement

**Multiple Myeloma:**
- Clonal plasma cell proliferation
- Genetic mutations (acquired)
- Malignant transformation
- Progressive course

##  Implication

**Manage Separately:**
- Don't attribute MM to MTHFR
- Test and treat MTHFR variants independently
- Focus on evidence-based MM treatment
