# Oxidized Omega-6 Causes Metabolic Damage — Mechanism of Harm

## Core Claim
**Oxidized omega-6 fatty acids (linoleic acid) from heated seed oils create toxic aldehydes (4-HNE, MDA) that cause systemic inflammation, oxidative stress, and insulin resistance** — this is a primary mechanism linking seed oil frying to metabolic disease, independent of the calorie or macronutrient content of the food.

## The Oxidation Process

### Step 1: Heat Exposure
| Condition | Result |
|-----------|--------|
| **Frying temperature** | 160-190°C |
| **Polyunsaturated fats** | Double bonds vulnerable |
| **Oxygen present** | Oxidation accelerates |

### Step 2: Lipid Peroxidation
| Reaction | Product |
|----------|---------|
| **Free radical attack** | Lipid radicals |
| **Chain reaction** | Propagation |
| **Breakdown** | Aldehydes, ketones |

### Step 3: Toxic Aldehyde Formation
| Aldehyde | Toxicity |
|----------|----------|
| **4-HNE** | Highly reactive, genotoxic |
| **Malondialdehyde (MDA)** | Cross-links proteins |
| **Acrolein** | Respiratory toxin |

## Metabolic Consequences

### Inflammation Pathway
| Step | Effect |
|------|--------|
| **4-HNE activates** | NF-κB pathway |
| **Cytokine release** | TNF-α, IL-6, IL-1β |
| **Chronic inflammation** | Systemic |

### Insulin Resistance
| Mechanism | Result |
|-----------|--------|
| **Oxidative stress** | Beta-cell damage |
| **Inflammation** | Insulin signaling impaired |
| **Mitochondrial dysfunction** | Energy metabolism disrupted |

### Endothelial Dysfunction
| Effect | Consequence |
|--------|-------------|
| **Oxidized LDL** | Atherosclerosis |
| **Vascular inflammation** | Hypertension |
| **Impaired nitric oxide** | Reduced vasodilation |

## Sources of Oxidized Omega-6

### Dietary Sources
| Source | Risk Level |
|--------|------------|
| **Deep-fried foods** | Highest |
| **Commercial baked goods** | High |
| **Restaurant meals** | High |
| **Reused frying oil** | Very high |
| **Packaged snacks** | Moderate-high |

### Industrial Processing
| Step | Oxidation Risk |
|------|----------------|
| **High-heat extraction** | Initial oxidation |
| **Deodorization** | Masking rancidity |
| **Storage** | Continued oxidation |
| **Consumer heating** | Further damage |

## Protection Strategies

### Avoid Exposure
| Strategy | Effect |
|----------|--------|
| **Eliminate deep-fried foods** | Remove primary source |
| **Avoid reused oil** | Less oxidized products |
| **Check restaurant practices** | Often use repeatedly heated oil |

### Antioxidant Defense
| Nutrient | Function |
|----------|----------|
| **Vitamin E** | Protects PUFAs |
| **Polyphenols** | Scavenge radicals |
| **Glutathione** | Detoxifies aldehydes |

## Clinical Implication

### For Metabolic Health
| Recommendation | Rationale |
|----------------|-----------|
| **Minimize oxidized omega-6** | Reduce toxic aldehyde load |
| **Increase antioxidants** | Support detoxification |
| **Choose stable fats** | Prevent oxidation |

### Testing
| Marker | Indication |
|--------|------------|
| **Oxidized LDL** | Lipid peroxidation |
| **MDA (urine)** | Systemic oxidation |
| **8-OHdG** | DNA oxidative damage |
