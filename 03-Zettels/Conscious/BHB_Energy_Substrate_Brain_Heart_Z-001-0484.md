## Core Claim
**BHB crosses the blood-brain barrier via monocarboxylate transporters (MCTs) without insulin, providing an alternative fuel for the brain during glucose scarcity — after adaptation, ketones supply 60-70% of brain energy, sparing glucose for obligate users and reducing protein catabolism** — the heart also readily uses BHB, with more efficient ATP production per oxygen than fatty acids.

## Evidence

### Transport Mechanism
| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Transporters** | MCT1, MCT2, MCT4 (monocarboxylate transporters) |
| **Insulin requirement** | None (unlike GLUT4) |
| **Gradient-driven** | Proton-coupled symport |

### Brain Fuel Transition
| State | Brain Energy Source |
|-------|---------------------|
| **Fed** | 100% glucose |
| **Fasting 24-48h** | 25% ketones |
| **Fasting 72h+ / Keto-adapted** | 60-70% ketones |

### Neuroprotective Effects
| Mechanism | Benefit |
|-----------|---------|
| **Efficient fuel** | More ATP per O₂ than glucose |
| **Reduced ROS** | Decreased oxidative stress |
| **Anti-inflammatory** | NLRP3 inflammasome inhibition |
| **Epigenetic** | HDAC inhibition → stress resistance genes |

### Heart Metabolism
| Feature | Advantage |
|---------|-----------|
| **Preferred fuel** | Heart readily oxidizes BHB |
| **Efficiency** | Better coupling than fatty acids |
| **Oxygen sparing** | Less O₂ per ATP produced |

### Clinical Applications
| Condition | Evidence |
|-----------|----------|
| **Epilepsy** | Ketogenic diet reduces seizures (established) |
| **Alzheimer's** | "Type 3 diabetes" — impaired brain glucose use |
| **TBI** | Alternative fuel when glucose metabolism impaired |
| **Heart failure** | Improved cardiac function with ketone utilization |

## Clinical Implication
- **Brain energy crisis:** BHB bypasses glucose hypometabolism
- **Neurodegeneration:** Ketones support mitochondrial function
- **Cardiac efficiency:** BHB may benefit heart failure
- **METHAP relevance:** Nutritional ketosis provides brain fuel alternative; may support cognitive function in metabolic syndrome
