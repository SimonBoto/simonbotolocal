# Aconitase Publishability Criteria

## Core Claim

**Enzymes are not inherently publishable — angles are.** Aconitase becomes publishable when framed as a mechanism, biomarker, or therapeutic target with clinical relevance.

## The Publishability Matrix

| Angle | Novelty Required | Clinical Data Needed | Journal Tier |
|-------|-----------------|---------------------|--------------|
| **New mechanism** | PTMs, regulation, structure | In vitro + cell culture | JBC, Biochemistry |
| **Disease link** | Human cohort, animal model | Patient data, histology | Disease-specific (e.g., J Neurochem) |
| **Therapeutic target** | Drug screen, inhibitor design | Preclinical efficacy | Drug Discovery Today |
| **Clinical biomarker** | ROC analysis, validation cohort | Large patient cohort | Clinical Chemistry, Neurology |
| **Methods paper** | New assay, imaging technique | Proof-of-concept | Methods in Enzymology |

## What Makes Aconitase Specifically Publishable

### Strong Angles

1. **Iron-sulfur cluster as redox sensor**
   - Novel: Cluster as signaling molecule, not just cofactor
   - Clinical: ROS in disease, therapeutic targeting

2. **Dual function: enzyme + regulatory protein**
   - Novel: Metabolic-epigenetic crosstalk
   - Clinical: Iron dysregulation diseases

3. **Compartmentalization (ACO1 vs ACO2)**
   - Novel: Subcellular metabolic specialization
   - Clinical: Cancer metabolism, neurodegeneration

### Weak Angles (Avoid)

- Pure biochemistry without disease link
- Single crystal structure without functional insight
- Review without novel synthesis

## The Simos Test

> *"Would a clinician care? Would a patient benefit?"*

If yes → high publishability
If no → find the clinical angle or don't publish
