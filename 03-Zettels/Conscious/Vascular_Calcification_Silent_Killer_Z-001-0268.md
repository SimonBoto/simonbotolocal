# Vascular Calcification — The Silent Killer

> Vascular calcification—the deposition of  phosphate crystals in arterial walls—affects 90% of adults over 70, increases cardiovascular risk independent of traditional factors, and often remains asymptomatic until advanced stages.

## Definition and Types

**Intimal Calcification:**
- Associated with atherosclerosis
- Located in plaque
- Predicts plaque rupture risk

**Medial Calcification (Mönckeberg):**
- Independent of atherosclerosis
- Stiffens arteries
- Common in , CKD, aging

## Prevalence

**By Age:**
- 40-50 years: 20-30%
- 60-70 years: 60-70%
- >70 years: >90%

**High-Risk Groups:**
- Diabetes: 2-4x higher
- CKD: Nearly universal (dialysis)
- Metabolic syndrome: Accelerated
- Postmenopausal women: Increased

##  Consequences

**Arterial Stiffness:**
- Loss of Windkessel effect
- ↑ Systolic BP
- ↑ Pulse pressure
- LV hypertrophy

**Cardiovascular Events:**
- Myocardial infarction
- Stroke
- Heart failure
- Sudden death

**Organ Damage:**
- Reduced perfusion
- Ischemia
- Organ dysfunction

## Silent Nature

**Asymptomatic Until Advanced:**
- No warning signs early
- Detected incidentally
- CT for other reasons
- X-ray findings

**When Symptoms Occur:**
- Already advanced disease
- Reduced treatment options
- Poor prognosis

**Screening Rationale:**
- Identify early
- Risk stratification
- Guide prevention
