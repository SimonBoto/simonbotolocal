# Corrected Vitamin D RDA 8900 IU per Day

## Core Claim
Proper statistical analysis shows the **actual vitamin D RDA should be approximately 8,900 IU/day** — about **15 times higher** than the IOM recommendation — to achieve 25(OH)D >20 ng/mL in 97.5% of the population.

## Corrected Calculations

### Veugelers & Ekwaru 2014
| Finding | Value |
|---------|-------|
| **Corrected RDA** | ~8,900 IU/day |
| **Multiple of IOM** | 15× higher |
| **Statistical method** | Proper population coverage |

### Heaney 2015 Confirmation
| Target | Requirement |
|--------|-------------|
| **>30 ng/mL (optimal)** | 6,201 IU/day |
| **>20 ng/mL (adequate)** | Lower dose |

## Context

### Natural Production
| Source | Amount |
|--------|--------|
| **Summer sun exposure** | 15,000-20,000 IU/day possible |
| **Evolutionary context** | Humans adapted to higher intake |

### Current vs. Corrected
| Recommendation | Dose |
|----------------|------|
| **IOM (flawed)** | 600 IU/day |
| **Corrected (Veugelers)** | 8,900 IU/day |
| **Heaney for optimal** | 6,201 IU/day |

## Evidence

> "Corrected statistical analysis demonstrates that approximately 8,900 IU/day is required to achieve adequate vitamin D status in 97.5% of the population — far exceeding current recommendations."
> — Veugelers & Ekwaru, 2014
