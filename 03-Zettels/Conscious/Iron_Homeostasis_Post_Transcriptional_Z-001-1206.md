## Core Claim
**Iron homeostasis is primarily regulated post-transcriptionally through the IRP/IRE system — allowing rapid minute-by-minute adjustments to changing iron availability — with IRP1 and IRP2 binding to iron-responsive elements (IREs) on target mRNAs to modulate translation and stability, providing precise control of iron uptake, storage, and export without requiring transcriptional changes.**

## Evidence

### Why Post-Transcriptional Control?
| Regulation Level | Speed | Suitability for Iron |
|------------------|-------|---------------------|
| **Transcription** | Hours-days | Too slow for acute changes |
| **Translation** | Minutes-hours | Better, but delayed |
| **Post-transcriptional** | Minutes | **Ideal for iron fluctuations** |

**Rapid iron changes:**
- Meal ingestion (↑ iron)
- Bleeding (↓ iron)
- Inflammation (iron sequestration)

### IRP/IRE System Targets
| Target | IRE Location | Iron-Low Effect | Iron-High Effect |
|--------|--------------|-----------------|------------------|
| **Transferrin receptor** | 3' UTR | ↑ mRNA stability | ↓ mRNA stability |
| **Ferritin** | 5' UTR | ↓ Translation | ↑ Translation |
| **DMT1** | 5' UTR | ↓ Translation | ↑ Translation |
| **Ferroportin** | 5' UTR | ↓ Translation | ↑ Translation |
| **HIF-2α** | 5' UTR | ↓ Translation | ↑ Translation |

### System Architecture
| Component | Function |
|-----------|----------|
| **IRP1** | Primary sensor (aconitase/IRP1 switch) |
| **IRP2** | Secondary regulator (degraded when iron high) |
| **IRE** | RNA stem-loop structure (target recognition) |
| **FBXL5** | E3 ubiquitin ligase (degrades IRP2 when iron high) |

### Coordinated Response
| Iron Status | Uptake | Storage | Export |
|-------------|--------|---------|--------|
| **Low** | ↑ (TfR) | ↓ (Ferritin) | ↓ (Ferroportin) |
| **High** | ↓ (TfR) | ↑ (Ferritin) | ↑ (Ferroportin) |

## Clinical Implication
- **Precision control:** Minute-by-minute iron status adjustment
- **No transcription delay:** Immediate response to changes
- **Disease relevance:** Dysregulation in iron overload disorders
- **Therapeutic target:** IRE-IRP system modifiable
- **METHAP relevance:** Iron is essential for mitochondrial enzymes and energy metabolism
