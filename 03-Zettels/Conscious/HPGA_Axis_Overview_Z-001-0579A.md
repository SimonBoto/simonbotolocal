# HPGA Axis Overview

The hypothalamic-pituitary-gonadal (HPG) axis is the endocrine system responsible for regulating reproductive function, sexual development, and hormone production. It operates through a cascade of hormonal signals that maintain homeostasis and coordinate physiological responses.

## Core Components

- **Hypothalamus**: Produces gonadotropin-releasing hormone (GnRH) in pulsatile fashion
- **Pituitary**: Releases luteinizing hormone (LH) and follicle-stimulating hormone (FSH) in response to GnRH
- **Gonads**: Produce sex steroids (testosterone, estradiol) and gametes in response to LH/FSH

## Regulatory Principles

- **Pulsatile signaling**: GnRH must be released in pulses to maintain pituitary sensitivity
- **Feedback loops**: Sex steroids provide negative feedback to hypothalamus and pituitary
- **Integration**: The HPGA axis interacts with other endocrine systems (HPA, HPT)

## Clinical Significance

Understanding the HPGA axis is fundamental for diagnosing and managing disorders of sexual development, infertility, and hormone imbalances.
