# Primary Hypogonadism Causes

This zettel documents the causes of primary hypogonadism (testicular failure) in males.

## Genetic Causes

- Klinefelter syndrome (47,XXY)
- Y chromosome microdeletions
- Androgen insensitivity syndrome
- Congenital anorchia

## Acquired Causes

- Testicular trauma or surgery
- Orchitis (mumps, viral)
- Radiation or chemotherapy
- Autoimmune orchitis
- Chemotherapy-induced damage

## Endocrine Disorders

- Hemochromatosis
- Sertoli-cell only syndrome
- Germ cell aplasia
- Cryptorchidism (undescended testes)

## Clinical Features

- Elevated LH and FSH
- Low testosterone
- Small, firm testes
- Infertility
- Delayed puberty (if congenital)
