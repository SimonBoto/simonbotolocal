## Core Claim
**Mitochondrial dysfunction produces characteristic OAT signatures — elevated lactate and pyruvate indicating anaerobic shift, TCA intermediates revealing specific enzyme blocks, and the lactate/pyruvate ratio serving as a redox status indicator — enabling clinicians to distinguish between primary mitochondrial disease, secondary dysfunction, and simple cofactor deficiency.**

## Evidence

### Key Mitochondrial Markers
| Marker | Normal Range | Elevated Indicates |
|--------|--------------|-------------------|
| **Lactate** | <2.0 mmol/L | Anaerobic metabolism |
| **Pyruvate** | <0.15 mmol/L | PDH dysfunction, mitochondrial issue |
| **Lactate/Pyruvate ratio** | 10-20:1 | Redox status |
| **Citrate** | Variable | Aconitase inhibition (ROS) |
| **Succinate** | Variable | Complex II dysfunction |
| **α-Ketoglutarate** | Variable | α-KGDH block (B-vitamins) |

### Lactate/Pyruvate Ratio Interpretation
| Ratio | Interpretation | Clinical Context |
|-------|---------------|------------------|
| **<10:1** | Normal redox | Healthy mitochondria |
| **10-20:1** | Mild shift | Early dysfunction, exercise |
| **>20:1** | Severe dysfunction | Hypoxia, mitochondrial disease |
| **>50:1** | Critical | Lactic acidosis, emergency |

### Mitochondrial Patterns
| Pattern | Markers | Mechanism |
|---------|---------|-----------|
| **Glycolytic shift** | Lactate ↑, pyruvate ↑, ratio normal | Increased glycolysis |
| **PDH dysfunction** | Pyruvate ↑ > lactate ↑ | B1, B2, B3, B5, Mg deficiency |
| **ETC block** | Lactate ↑, ratio ↑ | Complex I-IV dysfunction |
| **TCA block** | Citrate ↑, succinate ↑, α-KG ↑ | Aconitase, SDH, α-KGDH issues |
| **Combined severe** | All elevated | Global mitochondrial failure |

### Primary vs Secondary Dysfunction
| Feature | Primary Mitochondrial Disease | Secondary Dysfunction |
|---------|------------------------------|----------------------|
| **Onset** | Childhood, progressive | Any age, acute/subacute |
| **Pattern** | Multiple markers, severe | Variable, often milder |
| **Response to cofactors** | Minimal | Often dramatic |
| **Other findings** | Neurological, multisystem | Related to cause |
| **Genetics** | Often positive | Negative |

### Clinical Causes of Secondary Dysfunction
| Cause | Mechanism | OAT Pattern |
|-------|-----------|-------------|
| **B-vitamin deficiency** | Cofactor lack | PDH/α-KGDH block |
| **Hypoxia** | Oxygen limitation | High lactate, high ratio |
| **Toxins** | ETC inhibition | Variable, often severe |
| **Medications** | Metformin, others | Complex I inhibition |
| **Sepsis** | Mitochondrial suppression | Severe lactic acidosis |

### Intervention Strategy
| Pattern | Priority Intervention |
|---------|----------------------|
| **Mild elevation** | B-complex, magnesium, antioxidants |
| **Moderate elevation** | Above + CoQ10, L-carnitine |
| **Severe elevation** | Above + specialist referral |
| **Critical** | Emergency care + mitochondrial cocktail |

## Clinical Implication
- **Fatigue diagnosis:** Mitochondrial dysfunction common cause
- **Exercise intolerance:** OAT reveals metabolic limitation
- **Neurological symptoms:** Brain highly dependent on mitochondria
- **Treatment guidance:** Pattern directs intervention
- **METHAP relevance:** Mitochondrial function is core to metabolic health; OAT patterns enable research stratification and outcome measurement
