# Iron-Sulfur Cluster Biochemistry

> Aconitase requires a [4Fe-4S]²⁺ iron-sulfur cluster for catalytic activity, with Feₐ coordinating substrate and mediating Lewis acid catalysis.

## [4Fe-4S] Structure

- **4 iron atoms** (Fe)
- **4 inorganic sulfides** (S²⁻)
- **4 cysteine residues** (protein coordination)
- **Cubane geometry**

## The Unique Iron (Feₐ)

- **3 irons:** Structural (cysteine-coordinated)
- **1 iron (Feₐ):** Catalytic (substrate-binding)
- **Function:** Lewis acid, activates substrate

## Catalytic Role

1. Citrate binds to Feₐ
2. Fe²⁺ polarizes C-OH bond
3. Acid-base catalysis
4. Stereospecific rearrangement

## Why Fe-S?

- **Redox active:** Fe²⁺/Fe³⁺
- **Lewis acid:** Accepts electrons
- **Flexible:** Binds various substrates
- **Ancient:** Pre-oxygen Earth evolution
