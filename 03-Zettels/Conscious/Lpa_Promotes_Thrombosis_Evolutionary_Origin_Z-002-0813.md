## Core Claim
**Lp(a) promotes clot formation** through its unique apolipoprotein(a) component — an evolutionary mechanism that aided survival through faster bleeding cessation, now a liability in modern times with longer lifespans.

## Structure
| Component | Effect |
|-----------|--------|
| **LDL-like base** | Cholesterol transport |
| **Apo(a) addition** | Pro-thrombotic activity |
| **Result** | Enhanced clotting |

## Evolutionary Context

| Era | Advantage |
|-----|-----------|
| **Prehistoric** | Faster wound healing |
| **Trauma common** | Reduced bleeding deaths |
| **Short lifespans** | No time for CVD manifestation |

## Modern Paradox

| Factor | Change |
|--------|--------|
| **Lifespan** | Much longer |
| **Trauma risk** | Lower |
| **CVD time** | Decades for development |
| **Same mechanism** | Now harmful |
