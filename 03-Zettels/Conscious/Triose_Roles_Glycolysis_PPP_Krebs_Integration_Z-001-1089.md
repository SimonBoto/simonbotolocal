# Triose Roles — Glycolysis, PPP, and Krebs Integration

## Core Claim
**Trioses (G3P and DHAP) serve as metabolic crossroads connecting glycolysis, the pentose phosphate pathway, and the Krebs cycle—this three-way integration enables cells to balance energy extraction, biosynthesis, and carbon flow based on metabolic demand** — the interconversion of DHAP to G3P is the critical junction.

## Evidence

### Three Pathway Connections
| Pathway | Triose Role | Direction |
|---------|-------------|-----------|
| **Glycolysis** | G3P → Pyruvate | Energy extraction |
| **PPP** | Ribose-5-P ↔ G3P (via non-oxidative) | Biosynthesis ↔ Energy |
| **Krebs** | Pyruvate (from G3P) → Acetyl-CoA | Complete oxidation |

### The Glycolytic Split (Critical Junction)
```
Fructose-1,6-bisphosphate (6C)
           ↓ Aldolase
DHAP + G3P (2 × 3C)
   ↓
[Triose phosphate isomerase] — DHAP ↔ G3P
   ↓
2 G3P → 2 Pyruvate → Energy/Acetyl-CoA
```

### PPP Non-Oxidative Phase
```
Ribose-5-P + Xylulose-5-P → [Transketolase/Transaldolase]
                                    ↓
                    G3P + Fructose-6-P + Sedoheptulose-7-P
```

### Metabolic Flexibility
| Need | PPP Direction | Output |
|------|---------------|--------|
| High NADPH/ribose | Oxidative | Biosynthetic precursors |
| Excess ribose | Non-oxidative | G3P → rejoin glycolysis |
| Energy priority | Bypass PPP | Full glycolytic flux |

## Clinical Implication
- **Triose phosphate isomerase deficiency** — severe hemolytic anemia (G3P accumulation)
- **Cancer metabolism** — high PPP flux for biosynthesis, trioses diverted to biomass
- **Metabolic syndrome** — impaired flexibility at triose junction
- **METHAP relevance:** B1 (transketolase), B3 (GAPDH) support triose metabolism
