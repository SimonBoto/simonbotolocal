## Mechanism

**The mevalonate pathway converts three acetyl-CoA molecules into isopentenyl pyrophosphate (IPP) through six enzymatic steps — with HMG-CoA reductase as the rate-limiting regulatory point — consuming 2 NADPH and 2 ATP to produce the central building block for cholesterol, CoQ10, and all isoprenoid biomolecules.**

## Mechanism

**The mevalonate pathway converts three acetyl-CoA molecules into isopentenyl pyrophosphate (IPP) through six enzymatic steps — with HMG-CoA reductase as the rate-limiting regulatory point — consuming 2 NADPH and 2 ATP to produce the central building block for cholesterol, CoQ10, and all isoprenoid biomolecules.**

## Evidence

### Six-Step Pathway Overview
| Step | Enzyme | Reaction | Energy Cost |
|------|--------|----------|-------------|
| **1** | Acetoacetyl-CoA thiolase | 2 Acetyl-CoA → Acetoacetyl-CoA | None |
| **2** | HMG-CoA synthase | + Acetyl-CoA → HMG-CoA | None |
| **3** | **HMG-CoA reductase** | + 2 NADPH → Mevalonate | **2 NADPH** |
| **4** | Mevalonate kinase | + ATP → Mevalonate-5-P | **1 ATP** |
| **5** | Phosphomevalonate kinase | + ATP → 5-PP-Mevalonate | **1 ATP** |
| **6** | Decarboxylase | → IPP + CO₂ | None |

### Key Enzymes and Functions
| Enzyme | Function | Clinical Relevance |
|--------|----------|-------------------|
| **Thiolase** | Condenses 2 acetyl-CoA | Ketogenesis overlap |
| **HMG-CoA synthase** | Adds third acetyl-CoA | Mitochondrial vs cytosolic isoforms |
| **HMG-CoA reductase** | Rate-limiting reduction | **Statin target** |
| **Mevalonate kinase** | First phosphorylation | Mevalonic aciduria (rare disease) |
| **Phosphomevalonate kinase** | Second phosphorylation | Essential for pathway flux |
| **Decarboxylase** | Releases CO₂, forms IPP | Commits to isoprenoid synthesis |

### Overall Stoichiometry
```
3 Acetyl-CoA + 2 NADPH + 2 ATP → IPP + 3 CoA-SH + 2 NADP⁺ + 2 ADP + CO₂
```

| Input | Molecules | Purpose |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| Acetyl-CoA | 3 | Carbon skeleton (15 carbons → 5 in IPP) |
| NADPH | 2 | Reducing power for HMG-CoA reductase |
| ATP | 2 | Phosphate activation for decarboxylation |

### Compartmentalization
| Location | Enzymes | Notes |
|----------|---------|-------|
| **Cytosol** | Steps 2-6 | Cholesterol synthesis |
| **Mitochondria** | Step 1 (thiolase) | Also ketogenesis |
| **Peroxisomes** | Partial pathway | Alternative route |

### Evolutionary Conservation
| Organism | Pathway | Products |
|----------|---------|----------|
| **Animals** | Mevalonate | Cholesterol, steroid hormones |
| **Plants** | Mevalonate + MEP | Carotenoids, terpenes |
| **Bacteria** | MEP primarily | Antibiotics targets |
| **Archaea** | Mevalonate | Unique lipids |

## Clinical Implication
- **Statin mechanism:** HMG-CoA reductase inhibition blocks step 3, reducing all downstream products
- **CoQ10 depletion:** Statins block IPP → CoQ10 synthesis, causing myopathy
- **Energy cost:** High metabolic investment explains tight regulation
- **Drug targets:** Each enzyme represents potential therapeutic intervention
- **METHAP relevance:** Understanding pathway enables rational CoQ10 supplementation and metabolic optimization
