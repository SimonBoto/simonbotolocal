# Omega-6 to Omega-3 Ratio — Evolutionary and Clinical Context

## Evolutionary Perspective

### The Natural State
> "The human diet evolved with an omega-6 to omega-3 ratio of approximately 1-4:1"

| Era / Population | Omega-6:Omega-3 | Primary Fat Sources |
|-----------------|-----------------|---------------------|
| **Paleolithic hunter-gatherers** | 1:1 to 2:1 | Wild game, fish, nuts, seeds |
| **Pre-agricultural humans** | 2:1 to 4:1 | Hunting, gathering, fishing |
| **Traditional Mediterranean** | 4:1 to 8:1 | Olive oil, fish, moderate meat |
| **Traditional Japanese** | 4:1 to 5:1 | Fish, seaweed, minimal seed oils |
| **Modern Western (2020)** | 15:1 to 25:1 | Seed oils, grain-fed meat, processed foods |

## The 20th Century Shift

### Timeline of Change
| Decade | Key Events | Omega-6:Omega-3 Trend |
|--------|-----------|----------------------|
| **1900-1920** | Animal fats dominant | ~4:1 |
| **1911** | Crisco introduced | ~5:1 |
| **1950s** | Ancel Keys Seven Countries Study | ~8:1 |
| **1970s** | Dietary guidelines demonize saturated fat | ~12:1 |
| **1980s** | Seed oil consumption accelerates | ~15:1 |
| **2000s** | Ultra-processed foods ubiquitous | ~20:1 |
| **2020** | Current standard | ~20:1+ |

### Sources of Increased Omega-6
1. **Cooking oils:** Safflower, sunflower, corn, soybean
2. **Salad dressings:** Mayonnaise, commercial dressings
3. **Fried foods:** Restaurant frying oils (soybean/corn blend)
4. **Grain-fed meat:** Cattle fed corn/soy (higher omega-6 in meat)
5. **Processed foods:** Crackers, chips, baked goods
6. **Nuts/seeds (excess):** Overconsumption of high omega-6 nuts

## Clinical Evidence by Ratio

### 4:1 Ratio — Optimal
**Simopoulos (2002):**
- 70% reduction in total mortality
- Reduced cardiovascular events
- Improved cognitive function

### 2-3:1 Ratio — Therapeutic
**Inflammatory conditions:**
- **Rheumatoid arthritis:** Reduced symptoms, less NSAID need
- **Asthma:** Improved lung function
- **Psoriasis:** Reduced severity

### 5:1 Ratio — Acceptable Upper Limit
**General population health:**
- Still associated with positive outcomes
- Manageable inflammatory tone
- Not associated with increased disease risk

### 10:1 Ratio — Warning Zone
- Inflammatory markers elevated
- Increased chronic disease risk
- [[KEYWORD-Insulin|insulin]] resistance begins

### 15-25:1 Ratio — Modern Western (High Risk)
- Chronic low-grade [[KEYWORD-Inflammation|inflammation]]
- Elevated IL-6, TNF-alpha, CRP
- Increased CVD, diabetes, cancer risk
- Accelerated cellular aging

## Mechanisms of Ratio Importance

### Eicosanoid Balance
```
Omega-6 (AA)              vs.           Omega-3 (EPA)
     ↓                                     ↓
PGE2 (pro-inflammatory)              PGE3 (less inflammatory)
TXA2 (pro-thrombotic)                TXA3 (less thrombotic)
LTB4 (chemotactic)                   LTB5 (less chemotactic)
     ↓                                     ↓
INFLAMMATION                          RESOLUTION
```

### Pro-Resolving Mediators
**Omega-3 derived (require adequate intake):**
- Resolvins (E-series from EPA, D-series from DHA)
- Protectins
- Maresins

**Function:** Active termination of inflammation
**Blocked by:** High omega-6 competing for enzymes

## Achieving Optimal Ratio

### Strategy 1: Reduce Omega-6
| Action | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| Eliminate safflower/sunflower/corn oil | -15g/day linoleic acid |
| Reduce processed food consumption | -10g/day linoleic acid |
| Choose grass-fed over grain-fed meat | -2g/day linoleic acid |
| Limit high omega-6 nuts (walnuts, sunflower seeds) | -5g/day linoleic acid |

### Strategy 2: Increase Omega-3
| Source | EPA+DHA Content |
|--------|----------------|
| Wild salmon (100g) | 2-3g |
| Sardines (100g) | 1-2g |
| Fish oil supplement (1g) | 300-500mg |
| Algae oil (vegan DHA) | 200-300mg |

### Target Daily Intake
| Fatty Acid | Target | Current Average |
|------------|--------|-----------------|
| **Linoleic acid (18:2 n-6)** | <10g | 20-30g |
| **ALA (18:3 n-3)** | 2-3g | 1-2g |
| **EPA+DHA** | 1-2g | 100-200mg |
