# METHAP Study Design Overview

## Study Title
**Metabolomic Markers of Early Insulin Resistance: Urine Organic Acids and Plasma Free Fatty Acids as Predictive Biomarkers**

## Primary Objective
Identify novel metabolomic signatures for **early detection of insulin resistance** before conventional markers (HbA1c, fasting glucose) become abnormal.

## Study Hypothesis
Specific patterns of urinary organic acids and plasma free fatty acids can discriminate insulin-resistant individuals from controls with greater sensitivity than current clinical tools.

## Design Structure

### Phase 1: Cross-Sectional Observational Study
| Element | Specification |
|---------|---------------|
| **Sample size** | 200 participants |
| **Group A (Control)** | 100 individuals without insulin resistance |
| **Group B (Cases)** | 100 individuals with insulin resistance |
| **Primary analysis** | Metabolomic profiling + statistical discrimination |

### Phase 2: Interventional Sub-study
| Element | Specification |
|---------|---------------|
| **Participants** | Subset of insulin-resistant group |
| **Duration** | 12 weeks |
| **Intervention** | Low-carbohydrate diet + targeted supplementation |
| **Primary outcome** | Change in metabolomic markers and insulin sensitivity |

## Analytical Platform
**Gas Chromatography-Mass Spectrometry (GC-MS)**
- **Urine:** Organic acid profiling
- **Plasma:** Free fatty acid analysis
- **Advantage:** High sensitivity, reproducible, established metabolomics platform

## Statistical Approach
| Method | Application |
|--------|-------------|
| **PCA** | Dimensionality reduction, pattern visualization |
| **Multivariate regression** | Association modeling |
| **Pathway analysis** | Biological interpretation of metabolite changes |
| **Sample size calculation** | MetSizeR package (R) |

## Intervention Protocol

### Dietary Component
- **Type:** Low-carbohydrate diet
- **Rationale:** Reduce glycemic load, improve insulin sensitivity
- **Monitoring:** Dietary logs, compliance checks

### Supplementation Protocol
| Supplement | Rationale |
|------------|-----------|
| **Omega-3 fatty acids** | Anti-inflammatory, metabolic improvement |
| **Vitamin D** | Insulin sensitivity, metabolic regulation |
| **Magnesium** | Glucose metabolism, insulin action |
| **Glutamine** | Gut barrier, metabolic fuel |
| **Vitamin C** | Antioxidant, cofactor |
| **Probiotics** | Gut microbiome modulation |

## Innovation
- **Integration:** Metabolomics + dietary intervention
- **Timing:** Early detection focus (pre-disease)
- **Translation:** Precision medicine approach to metabolic disorders
