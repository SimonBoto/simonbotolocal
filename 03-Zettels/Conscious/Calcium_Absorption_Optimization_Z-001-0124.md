# Calcium Absorption Optimization

## Clinical Context
Optimizing calcium absorption is critical for osteoporosis treatment efficacy. Absorption efficiency varies significantly based on form, timing, and co-factors.

## Key Determinants of Absorption
- **Vitamin D status:** Primary regulator of intestinal calcium transport
- **Gastric acidity:** Essential for calcium carbonate dissolution
- **Meal timing:** Calcium carbonate requires acidic environment (take with meals)
- **Co-factors:** Magnesium, vitamin K2, and strontium support calcium metabolism

## Absorption Efficiency by Form
| Form | Elemental Calcium | Absorption Efficiency | Optimal Timing |
|------|-------------------|------------------------|----------------|
| Calcium carbonate | 40% | 20–30% | With meals (requires gastric acid) |
| Calcium citrate | 21% | 25–35% | With or without food |
| Calcium lactate | 13% | 25–30% | With meals |
| Calcium gluconate | 9% | 20–25% | With meals |

## Optimization Strategies
- **Acid-dependent forms:** Calcium carbonate (40% elemental) - take with meals
- **Acid-independent forms:** Calcium citrate (21% elemental) - take any time
- **Divided dosing:** 500–600 mg per dose (absorption declines above this)
- **Vitamin D co-administration:** Essential for optimal absorption
- **Avoid inhibitors:** High fiber, phytates, oxalates, and iron supplements within 2 hours

## Clinical Monitoring
- **Biochemical markers:** Serum calcium, PTH, 25(OH)D, urinary calcium
- **Functional assessment:** Bone mineral density, fracture risk assessment
- **Symptom assessment:** GI tolerance (constipation, dyspepsia)

## Safety Considerations
- **Hypercalciuria risk:** Monitor urine calcium (>300 mg/24h indicates excess)
- **GI intolerance:** Most common side effect, managed by form and dosing adjustments
- **Cardiovascular concerns:** Balance calcium intake with vitamin K2 for vascular protection

## Integration Notes
- Calcium absorption optimization is foundational for all bone health interventions
- Requires individualized approach based on gastric function, diet, and comorbidities
- Essential component of comprehensive metabolic bone disease management
