# Aconitase Cancer Metabolism Node

## Core Claim

**Aconitase sits at a critical metabolic junction in cancer cells** — its dysfunction or redirection supports the Warburg effect, iron accumulation, and redox adaptation that enable tumor growth.

## Mechanism

### TCA Cycle Rewiring

```
Normal: Citrate → [Aconitase] → Isocitrate → α-KG → Oxidative phosphorylation
Cancer: Citrate ↑ (accumulates) → Exported for lipid synthesis
              ↓
         Aconitase inhibited or bypassed
```

### Iron Metabolism Link

| Process | Role in Cancer |
|---------|---------------|
| **Iron uptake** | ↑ Transferrin receptor (TfR1) |
| **Iron storage** | ↓ Ferritin (IRE binding) |
| **Iron utilization** | ↑ Ribonucleotide reductase (DNA synthesis) |
| **Labile iron pool** | ↑ Oxidative stress, proliferation signal |

**Aconitase as IRP1** regulates all these via iron-responsive elements (IREs).

## Clinical Implications

| Application | Rationale |
|-------------|-----------|
| **Therapeutic target** | Inhibit Fe-S biogenesis → starve cancer of iron |
| **Biomarker** | Aconitase/ICD (isocitrate dehydrogenase) ratio |
| **Drug resistance** | Mitochondrial aconitase upregulation in resistance |
