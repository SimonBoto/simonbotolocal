## Overview
Multi-instrument studies require specific ethical considerations beyond standard protocols.

## Pre-Application Preparation

### Questionnaire Selection
| Criterion | Assessment |
|-----------|------------|
| **Validation** | Published reliability and validity data |
| **Appropriateness** | Fit for research question and population |
| **Burden** | Time required; participant fatigue |
| **Sensitivity** | Content that may cause distress |
| **Copyright** | Permissions for use |

### Documentation to Prepare
- [ ] Copy of each questionnaire
- [ ] Validation references
- [ ] Scoring instructions
- [ ] Interpretation guidelines
- [ ] Copyright permissions (if proprietary)

## Ethics Application Elements

### 1. Informed Consent for Questionnaires

#### Options
| Approach | When to Use |
|----------|-------------|
| **Global consent** | All instruments at study start |
| **Per-questionnaire consent** | Different sensitivity levels; longitudinal |
| **Tiered consent** | Core instruments required; optional additional |

#### Sample Language
> "You will be asked to complete several questionnaires about [topics]. Each questionnaire takes approximately [time]. You may skip any questions that make you uncomfortable. You may also choose not to complete any particular questionnaire while remaining in the study."

### 2. Pilot Testing Plan

**Purpose:**
- Assess clarity and comprehension
- Estimate completion time
- Identify problematic items
- Refine administration procedures

**Pilot report elements:**
- Number of pilot participants
- Completion times
- Feedback received
- Changes made based on feedback

### 3. Data Quality Assurance

| Strategy | Implementation |
|----------|----------------|
| **Training** | Standardized administration protocols |
| **Monitoring** | Regular data quality checks |
| **Validation** | Logic checks; range checks |
| **Missing data** | Protocol for handling incomplete responses |

### 4. Risk Minimization

#### Potential Risks
| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| **Fatigue** | Breaks allowed; session limits |
| **Distress** | Skip options; debriefing resources |
| **Privacy** | Private setting; secure storage |
| **Coercion** | Voluntary emphasis; no penalties |

## Standardized Instruments — Common Examples

### Health-Related Quality of Life
| Instrument | Items | Time | Domain |
|------------|-------|------|--------|
| **SF-36** | 36 | 5-10 min | Physical/mental health |
| **EQ-5D** | 5 + VAS | 2-3 min | General health status |
| **WHOQOL** | 26 | 10-15 min | Quality of life |

### Dietary Assessment
| Instrument | Items | Time | Considerations |
|------------|-------|------|----------------|
| **FFQ** | 100-150 | 30-60 min | Food frequency; recall bias |
| **24-hour recall** | Variable | 20-40 min | Requires trained interviewer |
| **Food diary** | Open | Ongoing | High burden; detailed |

### Physical Activity
| Instrument | Items | Time | Considerations |
|------------|-------|------|----------------|
| **IPAQ** | 7 | 5-10 min | International standard |
| **Godin** | 3 | 2-3 min | Simple scoring |

## Administration Modes

### In-Person
| Advantage | Disadvantage |
|-----------|--------------|
| High completion rates | Resource intensive |
| Clarification available | Scheduling challenges |
| Immediate data entry | Travel burden |

### Online/Remote
| Advantage | Disadvantage |
|-----------|--------------|
| Convenience | Technical barriers |
| Automatic data capture | Lower response rates |
| Scalable | Cannot clarify |

### Hybrid Approach
- Core instruments in-person
- Follow-up online
- Sensitive items in private setting

## Special Populations

### Vulnerable Groups
| Population | Consideration |
|------------|---------------|
| **Elderly** | Larger font; extra time |
| **Low literacy** | Verbal administration; simplified language |
| **Cognitive impairment** | Proxy consent; simplified instruments |
| **Non-native speakers** | Translated versions; interpreters |

## Post-Collection Responsibilities

### Data Security
- Encryption at rest and in transit
- Access limited to research team
- Secure storage (physical and digital)
- Retention per protocol; secure disposal

### Feedback to Participants
- Summary of findings (if desired)
- Individual results (if ly relevant)
- Study completion acknowledgment
