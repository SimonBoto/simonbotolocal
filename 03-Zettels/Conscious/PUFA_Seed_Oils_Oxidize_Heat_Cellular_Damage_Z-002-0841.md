## Core Claim
**Polyunsaturated fatty acids (PUFAs) in seed oils contain multiple double bonds that make them highly susceptible to oxidation** — when heated, they generate harmful compounds that cause cellular damage.

## The Problem with PUFA Structure
- **Multiple double bonds** = high oxidation risk
- Oxygen attacks these bonds
- Creates harmful oxidized compounds

## High-PUFA Seed Oils
| Oil | Problem |
|-----|---------|
| **Sunflower oil** | High PUFA, oxidizes easily |
| **Corn oil** | High PUFA, oxidizes easily |
| **Soybean oil** | High PUFA, oxidizes easily |

## When Heated: Harmful Compounds Form
- **Aldehydes**
- **Lipid peroxides**
- Cause: cellular damage, tissue damage, inflammation, oxidative stress

## Risks
| Risk | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **Cooking** | Every heating episode increases oxidation |
| **Storage** | Oxidizes at room temperature over time |

## Comparison
| Fat Type | Stability |
|----------|-----------|
| **Saturated fats** | ✅ Stable |
| **MUFA (olive, avocado)** | Moderate (with antioxidants) |
| **PUFA seed oils** | ❌ Unstable, harmful when heated |

## Clinical Implication
- **Avoid seed oils for cooking**
- Use stable saturated fats (butter, coconut oil, tallow)
- Use MUFA (olive oil) for low-heat only
