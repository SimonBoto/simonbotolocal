# Kefir Grain Reactivation Protocol

Dormant kefir grains require gradual reactivation to restore full metabolic activity and microbial diversity before use in therapeutic fasting protocols or daily probiotic consumption.

## Core Principle

Dormant kefir grains require gradual reactivation to restore full metabolic activity and microbial diversity before use in therapeutic fasting protocols or daily probiotic consumption.

## Reactivation Procedure

**Step 1: Initial Rinse**
- Rinse dormant grains gently with filtered water
- Remove any visible debris or old milk residue
- Inspect for mold (discard if any mold present)

**Step 2: Small Volume Start**
- Ratio: 1:5 (grain:milk by volume)
- Milk type: Whole organic milk preferred
- Volume example: 1 tbsp grains → 5 tbsp milk

**Step 3: Short Initial Fermentation**
- Duration: 12–24 hours (first batch)
- Temperature: 20–24°C (room temperature)
- Cover loosely (cloth lid) — allows gas exchange

**Step 4: Gradual Strengthening**
| Batch | Duration | Milk Volume |
|-------|----------|-------------|
| 1 | 12–24h | Small (1:5) |
| 2 | 18–24h | Small (1:5) |
| 3 | 24h | Normal (1:10) |
| 4+ | 24h | Normal/full |

## Signs of Healthy Reactivation

**Positive Indicators:**
- Milk thickens within 12–24 hours
- Pleasant tangy aroma (not putrid)
- Grains become firm and plump
- Minimal whey separation

**Warning Signs:**
- Off-putting smell (sour ≠ putrid)
- Pink/orange discoloration (yeast overgrowth)
- Excessive whey separation (over-fermentation)
- Slimy texture (bacterial imbalance)

## Full-Strength Criteria

Grains are ready for therapeutic use when:
- Consistent 24-hour fermentation cycle
- Thick, pourable kefir consistency
- Pleasant tangy flavor
- Grains visibly growing/multiplying
