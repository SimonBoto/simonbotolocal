## Core Claim
**Effective micronutrient repletion requires regular blood testing and dose adjustment** — the TARGET-D protocol of measuring levels at baseline, adjusting dose every 3 months until target reached (40-80 ng/mL), then annual monitoring represents a model for personalized nutrition intervention.

## The TARGET-D Protocol

### Phase 1: Baseline Assessment
- Measure vitamin D levels at enrollment
- Assess individual starting point
- Identify deficiency severity

### Phase 2: Active Titration
| Parameter | Protocol |
|-----------|----------|
| **Frequency** | Every 3 months |
| **Target** | 40-80 ng/mL |
| **Adjustment** | Dose modified based on results |
| **Duration** | Until target achieved |

### Phase 3: Maintenance
- Annual monitoring once stable
- Dose adjustments as needed
- Long-term adherence support

## Results
- **52% risk reduction** vs no intervention
- **85% achieved target** with personalized approach
- **Contrast with standard care** — 85% remained suboptimal

## Clinical Model
The "test, treat, retest" approach:
1. **Test** — Baseline blood measurement
2. **Treat** — Individualized dosing
3. **Retest** — Regular monitoring and adjustment

This model outperforms empirical dosing and should be applied to other micronutrients.
