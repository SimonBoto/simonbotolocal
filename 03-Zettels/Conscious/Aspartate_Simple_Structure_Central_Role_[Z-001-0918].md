# Aspartate: Simple Structure, Central Role

## Structure
**Simplest acidic amino acid:**
- 4-carbon dicarboxylic structure
- Side chain: -CH₂-COO⁻ (additional carboxyl)
- Formula: HOOC-CH₂-CH(NH₂)-COOH

**Charge:** Negative at physiological pH (pKa of side chain ~3.9)

## Synthesis
```
Oxaloacetate + Glutamate ↔ Aspartate + α-ketoglutarate
```
**Enzyme:** Aspartate aminotransferase (AST)
**Cofactor:** Vitamin B6

## Central Roles

### 1. Urea Cycle
**Provides second nitrogen for urea:**
```
Citrulline + Aspartate → Argininosuccinate
```
- One nitrogen from ammonia (carbamoyl phosphate)
- One nitrogen from aspartate

### 2. Krebs Cycle
**Anaplerotic precursor:**
- Aspartate → Oxaloacetate (transamination)
- Replenishes Krebs cycle intermediates

### 3. Nucleotide Synthesis
**Purine/pyrimidine backbone:**
- Aspartate provides nitrogen for:
  - Inosine monophosphate (IMP) synthesis
  - Pyrimidine ring (C4, C5, N3)

### 4. Neurotransmission
**NMDA receptor:**
- Aspartate is co-agonist with glycine
- Required for NMDA receptor activation
- Less prominent than glutamate

## Evolutionary Significance

### Simos Insight
> "Aspartate is extremely simple and evolutionarily important"

**Why simple matters:**
- Early prebiotic synthesis plausible
- Minimal functional groups
- Core metabolic roles preserved across domains

**Evolutionary roles:**
- Urea cycle (nitrogen disposal)
- Krebs cycle (energy metabolism)
- Protein synthesis

| Feature | Aspartate | Complexity |
|---------|-----------|------------|
| Carbons | 4 | Simple |
| Functional groups | 2 carboxyl, 1 amino | Simple |
| Biosynthesis | From OAA (Krebs intermediate) | Direct |

## Clinical: AST
**Aspartate aminotransferase (AST/SGOT):**
- Clinical marker of tissue damage
- Elevated in: Myocardial infarction, liver disease, muscle injury
- Mitochondrial and cytosolic isozymes

## Summary
**Aspartate exemplifies "simple but essential":**
- Minimal structure
- Maximal metabolic impact
- Ancient and conserved
