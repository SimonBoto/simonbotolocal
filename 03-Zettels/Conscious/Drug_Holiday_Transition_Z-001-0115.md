## Core Concept

Strategic interruption of antiresorptive therapy to mitigate long-term risks while maintaining bone health benefits.

**Key Concept:** Balancing benefit-risk ratio for extended antiresorptive use, particularly with bisphosphonates.

**Clinical Imperative:** Prevent atypical femoral fractures and osteonecrosis of jaw while preserving treatment gains.

## Standard Protocol

- **Bisphosphonates:** Consider drug holiday after 3–5 years of oral therapy or 3 years of IV therapy
- **Denosumab:** Not appropriate for drug holiday due to rebound risk
- **Timing:** Assess individual fracture risk and bone density before initiating holiday

## Evidence

- FLEX study: Alendronate holiday after 5 years maintained BMD and reduced fracture risk
- HORIZON study: Zoledronic acid holiday after 3 years showed similar benefits

## Monitoring During Holiday

- Annual clinical assessment for fracture risk
- DEXA at 2 years to assess BMD stability
- Biochemical markers (CTX, P1NP) every 6 months

## Clinical Pitfalls

### Discontinuing Denosumab for Drug Holiday
- **Risk:** Rapid bone loss, rebound vertebral fractures
- **Solution:** Never discontinue denosumab without transition plan

### Inadequate Risk Assessment Before Holiday
- **Risk:** Premature holiday in high-risk patients
- **Solution:** Use FRAX and clinical judgment to determine holiday appropriateness
