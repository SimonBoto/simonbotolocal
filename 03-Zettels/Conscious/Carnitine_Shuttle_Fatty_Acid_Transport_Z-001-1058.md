# Carnitine Shuttle Fatty Acid Transport

L-carnitine essential for transporting long-chain fatty acids into mitochondria. Without carnitine, fats cannot be burned for energy—a critical bottleneck in fatty acid metabolism.

## Evidence
> "Carnitine is the ferry that carries fatty acids across the mitochondrial membrane. No carnitine, no fat burning." (Mitochondrial biochemistry)

## The Carnitine Shuttle Mechanism

### Step 1: Activation (Cytosol)
Fatty acid + CoA + ATP → Fatty acyl-CoA + AMP + PPi

**Enzyme:** Acyl-CoA synthetase

### Step 2: CPT1 Reaction (Outer Mitochondrial Membrane)
Fatty acyl-CoA + Carnitine → Acylcarnitine + CoA

**Enzyme:** Carnitine palmitoyltransferase 1 (CPT1)
**Rate-limiting step:** Regulated by malonyl-CoA

### Step 3: Translocation (Inner Mitochondrial Membrane)
Acylcarnitine transported via CACT (carnitine-acylcarnitine translocase)

### Step 4: CPT2 Reaction (Inner Mitochondrial Membrane)
Acylcarnitine + CoA → Fatty acyl-CoA + Carnitine

**Enzyme:** Carnitine palmitoyltransferase 2 (CPT2)

### Step 5: Beta-Oxidation (Mitochondrial Matrix)
Fatty acyl-CoA enters β-oxidation → Acetyl-CoA → Krebs cycle

### Step 6: Carnitine Recycling
Free carnitine transported back to cytosol via CACT

## Regulation of CPT1

### Inhibition by Malonyl-CoA
| Condition | Malonyl-CoA | CPT1 Activity | Effect |
|-----------|-------------|---------------|--------|
| **Fed state** | High | Inhibited | Fat burning blocked |
| **Fasting** | Low | Active | Fat burning enabled |
| **High carb** | High | Inhibited | Prefer glucose |
| **Ketogenic diet** | Low | Active | Prefer fat |

**Rationale:** When glucose is abundant (high malonyl-CoA), don't burn fat. When glucose is scarce (low malonyl-CoA), burn fat.

## Clinical Carnitine Deficiency

### Primary Deficiency (Genetic)
| Disorder | Enzyme | Presentation |
|----------|--------|--------------|
| **CPT1 deficiency** | CPT1 | Hepatomegaly, hypoketotic hypoglycemia |
| **CPT2 deficiency** | CPT2 | Muscle weakness, myoglobinuria |
| **CACT deficiency** | Translocase | Severe, often fatal in infancy |
| **Primary carnitine deficiency** | OCTN2 transporter | Cardiomyopathy, muscle weakness |

### Secondary Deficiency (Acquired)
| Cause | Mechanism |
|-------|-----------|
| **Vegan/vegetarian diet** | Low intake |
| **Aging** | Reduced synthesis |
| **Kidney disease** | Excess loss |
| **Valproic acid** | Depletes carnitine |
| **Chemotherapy** | Increased demand |

## Clinical Markers

### OAT Markers
| Marker | Elevation Indicates |
|--------|---------------------|
| **Adipate** | Impaired β-oxidation, possible carnitine deficiency |
| **Suberate** | Medium-chain fatty acid oxidation impairment |

### Plasma/Acylcarnitine Profile
| Pattern | Interpretation |
|---------|----------------|
| **Low free carnitine** | Deficiency |
| **High acylcarnitines** | Impaired β-oxidation |
| **Specific acylcarnitine elevations** | Enzyme-specific defects |

## Therapeutic Applications

### Carnitine Supplementation
| Form | Dose | Use |
|------|------|-----|
| **L-carnitine** | 1-3 g/day | General supplementation |
| **Acetyl-L-carnitine** | 500-1500 mg/day | Cognitive support |
| **Propionyl-L-carnitine** | 500-1500 mg/day | Cardiovascular |

### Indications for Supplementation
| Condition | Rationale |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Fatigue** | Improve fat burning |
| **Weight loss plateau** | Enhance fat oxidation |
| **Vegan/vegetarian** | Low dietary intake |
| **Elderly** | Reduced synthesis |
| **Statin myopathy** | Support muscle energy |
| **Heart failure** | Improve cardiac energy |
| **Cognitive decline** | Brain energy support |
