## Core Claim
**The carnitine shuttle system — comprising CPT-I, carnitine-acylcarnitine translocase, and CPT-II — is essential for transporting long-chain fatty acids across the impermeable inner mitochondrial membrane, with carnitine deficiency or translocase dysfunction producing severe metabolic derangements including hypoketotic hypoglycemia and cardiomyopathy.**

## Evidence

### Carnitine Shuttle Mechanism
| Step | Enzyme/Transporter | Location | Action |
|------|-------------------|----------|--------|
| 1 | **CPT-I** | Outer membrane | Fatty acyl-CoA + Carnitine → Acylcarnitine |
| 2 | **CACT** | Inner membrane | Acylcarnitine ↔ Carnitine exchange |
| 3 | **CPT-II** | Matrix side | Acylcarnitine → Fatty acyl-CoA + Carnitine |

### Carnitine Sources
| Source | Contribution | Factors |
|--------|--------------|---------|
| **Diet** | ~75% | Red meat, dairy primary sources |
| **Biosynthesis** | ~25% | Lysine + methionine (liver, kidney) |
| **Reabsorption** | Variable | Kidney conservation |

### Carnitine Forms
| Form | Function | Clinical Use |
|------|----------|--------------|
| **L-carnitine** | Transport | Primary supplement |
| **Acetyl-L-carnitine** | Brain, acetyl donor | Cognitive support |
| **Propionyl-L-carnitine** | Vascular | Peripheral vascular disease |
| **L-carnitine L-tartrate** | Absorption | Athletic performance |

### Clinical Deficiency States
| Cause | Mechanism | Presentation |
|-------|-----------|--------------|
| **Primary deficiency** | OCTN2 defect | Cardiomyopathy, hypoglycemia |
| **Secondary (renal)** | Dialysis losses | Fatigue, muscle weakness |
| **Secondary (liver)** | Decreased synthesis | Hepatic steatosis |
| **Secondary (diet)** | Vegan, elderly | Suboptimal fatty acid oxidation |
| **Valproate therapy** | Competition | Hepatotoxicity risk |

### CPT Deficiencies
| Deficiency | Enzyme | Presentation |
|------------|--------|--------------|
| **CPT-I** | Hepatic isoform | Hypoketotic hypoglycemia, hepatomegaly |
| **CPT-II** | Muscle isoform | Exercise intolerance, myoglobinuria, rhabdomyolysis |

### Supplementation Guidelines
| Indication | Dose | Monitoring |
|------------|------|------------|
| **Primary deficiency** | 50-100 mg/kg/day | Plasma free carnitine |
| **Secondary (dialysis)** | 1-2 g post-dialysis | Symptoms, plasma levels |
| **Cardiac support** | 1-3 g/day | Ejection fraction |
| **Cognitive** | 1-2 g ALCAR | Cognitive testing |

## Clinical Implication
- **Fatty acid oxidation:** Carnitine essential for long-chain FA entry
- **Cardiac function:** Heart highly dependent on fatty acid fuel
- **Exercise capacity:** CPT-II deficiency limits endurance
- **Drug interactions:** Valproate competes for carnitine
- **METHAP relevance:** Carnitine status affects metabolic flexibility; supplementation may improve glucose metabolism and fatty acid oxidation in metabolic syndrome
