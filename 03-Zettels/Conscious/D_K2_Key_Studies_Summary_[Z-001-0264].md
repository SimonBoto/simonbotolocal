# D-K2 Key Studies Summary

> Three landmark studies establish the evidence base for D-K2 co-supplementation: Knapen et al. (2013) on arterial stiffness, Shea et al. (2015) on coronary calcification, and Van Ballegooijen et al. (2017) on metabolic associations.

## Knapen et al. (2013)

**Study:** MK-7 and arterial stiffness in postmenopausal women

**Design:**
- Double-blind, placebo-controlled RCT
- n=244 healthy postmenopausal women
- Duration: 3 years
- Intervention: MK-7 180 mcg/day vs. placebo
- All participants: 400 IU D3 (standard)

**Results:**
- Reduced age-related increase in arterial stiffness
- Improved pulse wave velocity
- No benefit in placebo group
- **Significance:** Long-term vascular protection

**METHAP Relevance:**
- Supports K2 for vascular health
- 180 mcg MK-7 = Effective dose
- Long-term  demonstrated

## Shea et al. (2015)

**Study:** Vitamin K and coronary artery calcification

**Design:**
- RCT in older adults
- K1 and K2 supplementation
- Measured CAC progression

**Results:**
- Slowed CAC progression vs. placebo
- Most effective in compliant participants
- D status adequate but not primary focus

**Significance:**
- Direct calcification endpoint
- Supports anti-calcification effect
- Relevant to high-dose D context

## Van Ballegooijen et al. (2017)

**Study:** D and K status association with metabolic health

**Design:**
- Observational + intervention component
- Older adults at CVD risk

**Results:**
- Low D + Low K = Worst outcomes
- High D + High K = Best outcomes
- Synergistic association pattern

**Significance:**
- Population-level synergy evidence
- Supports combined approach
- Informs intervention design

## Synthesis

**Consistent Findings:**
- D+K2 together > either alone
- Vascular benefits clear
- Metabolic benefits emerging
- Safety established

**METHAP builds on these:**
- Higher D dose (8000 vs. 400 IU)
- MetS population (understudied)
- Systematic monitoring
- Triple nutrient (adds Mg)
