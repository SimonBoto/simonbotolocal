# Therapeutic Ketosis Clinical Applications

## Overview
Ketogenic diets and therapeutic ketosis have established benefits for epilepsy and emerging applications in neurodegenerative diseases, metabolic disorders, and cancer.

## Established Application: Epilepsy

### History
- **1920s:** Developed as treatment for epilepsy
- **Pre-anticonvulsant era:** Primary therapy
- **Modern use:** Drug-resistant epilepsy

### Mechanisms
| Mechanism | Effect |
|-----------|--------|
| **Enhanced GABA synthesis** | Increased inhibition |
| **Reduced glutamate excitotoxicity** | Neuroprotection |
| **Stabilized neuronal membranes** | Reduced excitability |
| **Improved mitochondrial function** | Energy efficiency |
| **Ketone anticonvulsant effects** | Direct neuroprotection |

### Efficacy
- **Drug-resistant epilepsy:** 50% reduction in seizures in ~50% of patients
- **Childhood epilepsy:** Particularly effective
- **Requires strict adherence:** Usually <20g carbs/day

## Emerging Applications

### Neurodegenerative Diseases
| Condition | Evidence | Mechanism |
|-----------|----------|-----------|
| **Alzheimer's disease** | Preliminary trials | Brain fuel alternative, reduced glucose hypo |
| **Parkinson's disease** | Early research | Mitochondrial support, reduced oxidative stress |
| **ALS** | Case reports | Energy support, reduced excitotoxicity |
| **Traumatic brain injury** | Animal models | Neuroprotection, reduced edema |

### Metabolic Disorders
| Condition | Rationale |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Type 2 diabetes** | Improved  sensitivity, weight loss |
| **Metabolic syndrome** | Reduced triglycerides, increased HDL |
| **PCOS** | Improved hormonal balance, weight loss |
| **GLUT1 deficiency** | Bypasses glucose transport defect |

### Cancer (Adjunctive)
| Rationale | Mechanism |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Warburg effect** | Cancer cells rely on glucose; ketones not used efficiently |
| **Anti-inflammatory** | Reduced tumor-promoting  |
| **IGF-1 reduction** | Lower growth factor signaling |
| **Enhanced treatment** | May improve chemotherapy/radiation efficacy |

**Note:** Not standalone cancer treatment; adjunctive only

## Therapeutic Ketosis Levels

| Level | BHB mmol/L | Application |
|-------|------------|-------------|
| **Mild** | 0.5–1.0 | General health, cognitive enhancement |
| **Nutritional** | 1.0–3.0 | Weight loss, metabolic health |
| **Therapeutic** | 3.0–5.0 | Epilepsy, neurodegeneration |
| **High** | >5.0 | Supervised medical protocols only |

## Implementation Strategies

### Classic Ketogenic Diet
- **Ratio:** 4:1 fat:carbs+protein (by weight)
- **Carbs:** <20g/day
- **Protein:** Moderate, not high
- **Fat:** 70-80% of calories

### Modified Approaches
| Variant | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **MCT oil diet** | Medium-chain triglycerides for easier ketosis |
| **Modified Atkins** | Less restrictive, no ratio calculation |
| **Low glycemic index** | Focus on low-GI carbs |
| **Intermittent fasting** | Periodic ketosis without chronic restriction |

## Safety and Monitoring

### Short-Term Side Effects ("Keto Flu")
- Fatigue, headache, nausea
- Usually resolves in 1-2 weeks
- Electrolyte management critical

### Long-Term Considerations
| Concern | Management |
|---------|------------|
| **Nutrient deficiencies** | Supplementation, varied whole foods |
| **Kidney stones** | Adequate hydration, citrate supplementation |
| **Lipid changes** | Monitor, adjust fat quality |
| **Growth in children** | Monitor closely, adjust as needed |

### Contraindications
- Pyruvate carboxylase deficiency
- Porphyria
- Fat metabolism disorders
- Pregnancy (relative)
