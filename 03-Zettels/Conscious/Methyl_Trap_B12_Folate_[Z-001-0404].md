# The Methyl Trap — B12-Folate Interdependence

> B12 deficiency causes functional folate deficiency through the 'methyl trap' mechanism, where 5-MTHF accumulates and cannot be converted back to THF for DNA synthesis, despite normal or elevated serum folate levels.

## The Mechanism

### Normal State
```
5-MTHF + B12 (methylcobalamin) → Methionine + THF
                        ↓
                  THF re-enters
                  folate cycle
```

### B12 Deficient State
```
5-MTHF cannot donate methyl group
        ↓
   5-MTHF accumulates (TRAPPED)
        ↓
    THF not regenerated
        ↓
   Folate cycle stalls
        ↓
   DNA synthesis impaired
        ↓
   Megaloblastic anemia
```

## Key Features

**Biochemical:**
- Elevated 5-MTHF (serum folate may appear normal/high)
- Low THF
- Impaired thymidylate synthesis
- Impaired purine synthesis

**Clinical:**
- Megaloblastic anemia identical to folate deficiency
- Hyperhomocysteinemia
- **But:** Neurological damage (B12-specific)

## Why Folate Supplementation Masks B12 Deficiency

**The Problem:**
- Folate corrects anemia (provides alternative 5-MTHF)
- Anemia resolves
- But B12 deficiency continues
- Neurological damage progresses (irreversible)

**Clinical Rule:**
> Always check B12 status before folate supplementation

## Diagnostic Implications

**B12 Deficiency with Normal Folate:**
- Serum folate: Normal or elevated
- RBC folate: Low (better indicator of tissue status)
- Serum B12: Low
- MMA: Elevated (specific)
- Homocysteine: Elevated

**Treatment Response:**
- B12 treatment → Resolves both B12 and folate trap
- Folate treatment alone → Worsens neurological status

## METHAP Relevance

**High-Dose Nutrient Context:**
- B12 essential if folate supplementation used
- Monitor both for optimal methylation
- Prevent functional deficiencies
- Ensure complete one-carbon metabolism support
