# Triglycerides and HDL More Predictive Than LDL Alone

## Core Claim
**Triglycerides and HDL cholesterol are often more predictive of cardiovascular risk than LDL alone** — especially in metabolic syndrome and insulin resistance, where the TG/HDL ratio captures atherogenic dyslipidemia better than isolated LDL levels.

## The Lipid Profile Hierarchy

### Traditional View (Outdated)
| Priority | Marker |
|----------|--------|
| **#1** | LDL only |
| **#2** | Total cholesterol |
| **Ignored** | TG, HDL |

### Evidence-Based View
| Priority | Marker |
|----------|--------|
| **#1** | Non-HDL or ApoB |
| **#2** | TG/HDL ratio |
| **#3** | LDL |
| **#4** | HDL functionality |

## TG/HDL Ratio

### Calculation
| Triglycerides | HDL | Ratio |
|---------------|-----|-------|
| 150 mg/dL | 50 mg/dL | 3.0 |
| 200 mg/dL | 40 mg/dL | 5.0 |

### Risk Stratification
| Ratio | Risk Level |
|-------|------------|
| **<2** | Low |
| **2-4** | Moderate |
| **>4** | High |
| **>6** | Very high |

## Why TG/HDL Matters

### Metabolic Context
| High TG + Low HDL | Indicates |
|-------------------|-----------|
| **Insulin resistance** | Prediabetes/T2D |
| **Small dense LDL** | More atherogenic |
| **Remnant particles** | Pro-inflammatory |

### Superior to LDL Alone
- Predicts CVD in metabolic syndrome
- Captures atherogenic dyslipidemia
- Responds to lifestyle (low-carb, exercise)

## Clinical Action

### Target Ratios
| Marker | Target |
|--------|--------|
| **Triglycerides** | <150 mg/dL |
| **HDL** | >40 (M), >50 (F) |
| **TG/HDL** | <2-3 |

### Interventions That Improve
| Approach | Effect on TG/HDL |
|----------|------------------|
| **Low-carb diet** | Dramatic improvement |
| **Exercise** | Improves both |
| **Weight loss** | Best intervention |
| **Omega-3s** | Lowers TG |
