# Low Carb Superior to Low Fat for Metabolic Syndrome

## Core Claim
Randomized controlled trials demonstrate that **low-carbohydrate diets are superior to low-fat diets** for reversing metabolic syndrome, with greater improvements in weight, glycemic control, and lipid profiles.

## RCT Evidence

### Metabolic Outcomes
| Outcome | Low-Carb | Low-Fat |
|---------|----------|---------|
| **Weight loss** | Greater | Less |
| **HbA1c reduction** | Greater | Less |
| **Triglycerides** | Lower | Higher |
| **HDL** | Higher | Lower |
| **Insulin sensitivity** | Improved | Minimal |

### Adherence
| Factor | Low-Carb | Low-Fat |
|--------|----------|---------|
| **Satiety** | Better | Worse |
| **Long-term adherence** | Better | Worse |
| **Hunger** | Less | More |

## Evidence

> "RCTs demonstrate low-carbohydrate diets superior to low-fat for metabolic syndrome reversal, with better weight loss, glycemic control, and adherence."
> — Diet comparison studies
