# Premature Cardiovascular Disease in Autoimmunity

## The Cardiovascular Risk in Autoimmune Diseases

### Striking Statistics
**Systemic Lupus Erythematosus (SLE):**
- CVD risk equivalent to **+20 years of age**
- Women with SLE (age 35-44): 50x increased MI risk
- Leading cause of mortality in SLE

**Rheumatoid Arthritis:**
- 2-3x increased CVD risk
- Comparable to diabetes mellitus
- Accelerated atherosclerosis

### Traditional vs. Non-Traditional Risk Factors

**Traditional:**
- Hypertension
- Dyslipidemia
- Smoking
- Diabetes
- Family history

**Non-Traditional (Autoimmune-Specific):**
- Disease activity
- Inflammation (CRP, IL-6)
- Corticosteroid use
- Telomere shortening (vascular aging)
- Autoantibodies (anti-oxLDL)

---

## Mechanisms of Accelerated Atherosclerosis

### 1. Chronic Inflammation
**Direct Effects:**
- Inflammatory cytokines damage endothelium
- Promote foam cell formation
- Activate macrophages in plaques

**s:**
- Elevated CRP
- High IL-6
- TNF-α activity

### 2. Autoantibodies
**Anti-oxLDL:**
- Oxidized LDL is atherogenic
- Autoantibodies form immune complexes
- Deposit in vessel walls

**Anti-phospholipid antibodies:**
- Pro-thrombotic
- Endothelial activation
- Accelerated plaque formation

### 3. Telomere Shortening
**Vascular Aging:**
- Endothelial cells have shortened telomeres
- Reduced regenerative capacity
- Premature senescence

**Mechanism:**
- Systemic inflammation → oxidative stress
- ROS damage telomeres
- Vascular aging accelerated

### 4. Corticosteroid Effects
**Metabolic Side Effects:**
- Hypertension
- Dyslipidemia
-  resistance
- Weight gain

**Net Effect:** Amplifies traditional risk factors

---

## Clinical Presentation

### Early Onset
- MI in 30s-40s (unusual in general population)
- Silent ischemia common
- Atypical symptoms (fatigue, dyspnea)

### Aggressive Disease
- Diffuse atherosclerosis
- Multiple vessel involvement
- Poor outcomes post-MI

---

## Prevention and Management

### Aggressive Traditional Risk Factor Control
**Targets (Lower than general population):**
- LDL < 70-100 mg/dL (vs < 100-130)
- BP < 130/80 (vs < 140/90)
- Smoking cessation mandatory

### Anti-Inflammatory Therapy
**Disease Control:**
- Effective DMARD/biologic therapy
- Reduces systemic inflammation
- May slow atherosclerosis progression

**Statins:**
- Lipid-lowering effect
- Additional anti-inflammatory benefits
- Recommended for most patients

### Novel Approaches
**Colchicine:**
- Anti-inflammatory
- Reduces CVD events in studies
- Under investigation in autoimmunity

**Canakinumab (anti-IL-1β):**
- Reduced CVD events in CANTOS trial
- Theoretical benefit in autoimmunity
