# Follow Up Assessment Rhythms

## Core Claim
**Structured follow-up assessment rhythms at 4-8-16-28 week intervals — with defined evaluation criteria at each checkpoint including symptom tracking, adherence monitoring, lab safety checks, and treatment adjustments — optimize clinical outcomes while maintaining patient engagement and ensuring safe, evidence-based care progression.**

## Evidence

### Follow-Up Schedule Framework
| Week | Focus | Assessment Type | Key Actions |
|------|-------|-----------------|-------------|
| **4** | Early response | Phone/email | Symptom check, adherence |
| **8** | Initial adjustment | In-person | Review progress, adjust doses |
| **16** | Mid-term evaluation | In-person | Safety labs, outcome measures |
| **28** | Long-term assessment | In-person | Repeat OAT, plan continuation |

### Safety Monitoring Schedule
| Parameter | Frequency | Threshold for Action |
|-----------|-----------|---------------------|
| **Symptom worsening** | Every contact | Immediate review |
| **Adverse events** | Every contact | Document, address |
| **Safety labs** | 16 weeks, then PRN | Per reference ranges |
| **Drug interactions** | Every visit | Update medication list |

## Clinical Implication
- **Optimization:** Structured follow-up maximizes outcomes
- **Safety:** Regular monitoring catches issues early
- **Engagement:** Scheduled contact maintains relationship
- **Data:** Systematic tracking enables outcome analysis
- **METHAP relevance:** Standardized follow-up schedules essential for research data collection
