# Fatty Acid Nomenclature and Numbering Systems

## Overview

Fatty acids are named using multiple systems. Understanding these is essential for interpreting biochemical literature and clinical reports.

---

## Basic Structure

**General Formula:**
```
CH₃-(CH₂)n-COOH
```

**Numbering from carboxyl end (Δ/delta system):**
```
1    2    3    ...   n-1   n   n+1
COOH-CH₂-CH₂-...-CH₂-CH₂-CH₃
 |                  |
C1                ω carbon
```

**Numbering from methyl end (ω/omega system):**
```
ω   ω-1  ω-2  ...   3    2    1
CH₃-CH₂-CH₂-...-CH₂-CH₂-COOH
```

---

## Shorthand Notation (C:D System)

**Format:** C:D or C:D:n-x

| Component | Meaning | Example |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| **C** | Number of carbons | 18 = 18 carbons |
| **D** | Number of double bonds | 1 = one double bond |
| **n-x** | Position of first double bond from methyl end | n-9 = omega-9 |

### Examples

**Oleic acid:**
- Systematic: cis-9-octadecenoic acid
- Shorthand: 18:1(n-9) or 18:1Δ9
- Common: Omega-9 fatty acid

**Linoleic acid:**
- Systematic: cis,cis-9,12-octadecadienoic acid
- Shorthand: 18:2(n-6) or 18:2Δ9,12
- Common: Omega-6 fatty acid

**EPA:**
- Systematic: 5,8,11,14,17-eicosapentaenoic acid
- Shorthand: 20:5(n-3) or 20:5Δ5,8,11,14,17
- Common: Omega-3 fatty acid

---

## Delta (Δ) vs Omega (ω) Systems

### Delta System (Carboxyl End)
**Use:** Biochemical reactions, enzyme specificity

**Example:** Δ9-desaturase
- Adds double bond at carbon 9
- Position counted from COOH

### Omega System (Methyl End)
**Use:** Nutritional classification, physiology

**Example:** Omega-3 fatty acids
- First double bond at carbon 3 from CH₃ end
- All omega-3s share metabolic fate

## Cis vs Trans Configuration

### Cis
- Hydrogens on same side
- Creates "kink" in chain
- Liquid at room temperature
- Natural form

### Trans
- Hydrogens on opposite sides
- Linear chain
- More solid
- Artificial (hydrogenation)
- Health concern
