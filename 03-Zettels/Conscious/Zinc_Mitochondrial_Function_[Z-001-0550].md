# Zinc Mitochondrial Function

> Zinc serves as structural and catalytic component of SOD2 and multiple mitochondrial enzymes while maintaining membrane integrity.

## SOD2 (MnSOD)

**Reaction:**
```
2 O₂⁻ + 2 H⁺ → H₂O₂ + O₂
```

- Converts superoxide to hydrogen peroxide
- First line ROS defense
- Protects mtDNA

## Additional Roles

**Enzyme Cofactor:**
- Carbonic anhydrase V
- Matrix metalloproteinases
- Multiple dehydrogenases

**Membrane Stabilization:**
- Maintains membrane potential
- Prevents permeability transition
- Stabilizes cardiolipin

**Protein Structure:**
- Zinc finger domains
- TFAM, POLG stability

## Status

- **RDA:** 8-11 mg/day
- **Upper limit:** 40 mg/day
- **Serum:** 70-120 μg/dL
