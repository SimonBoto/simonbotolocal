# Low-Fat Era Consequences

> The widespread adoption of low-fat dietary guidelines had unintended consequences: increased refined carbohydrate consumption, metabolic syndrome prevalence, and questionable cardiovascular benefit.

## Metabolic Epidemic

**Obesity (US Adults):**
- 1977: ~15%
- 2020: ~42%

**Type 2 Diabetes:**
- 1980: 5.5 million
- 2020: 34.2 million
- **6-fold increase**

**Metabolic Syndrome:**
- Now 1 in 3 adults
- Central obesity,  resistance

## Mechanism

**Carbohydrate-Insulin Model:**
1. High glycemic load
2. Glucose spikes
3. Insulin secretion
4. Fat storage
5. 

## Low-Fat Problems

**Increased Hunger:**
- Fat promotes satiety
- Carbs less satiating

**Displaced Nutrients:**
- Fat-soluble vitamins reduced
- Essential  reduced

**Processed Foods:**
- Fat removed, sugar added
- Ultra-processed increased

## Lipid Profile Worsening

**High-Carb Effects:**
- Triglycerides ↑
- HDL ↓
- Small dense LDL ↑

## Public Health Failure

Well-intentioned advice caused iatrogenic metabolic harm.
