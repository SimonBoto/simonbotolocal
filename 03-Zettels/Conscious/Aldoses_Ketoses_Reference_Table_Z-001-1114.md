# Aldoses and Ketoses — Reference Table

## Core Claim
**Aldoses (carbonyl at C1) and ketoses (carbonyl at C2) represent the two fundamental classes of monosaccharides, with biological roles determined by carbon number and stereochemistry** — glucose (aldohexose) and fructose (ketohexose) dominate metabolism, while ribose (aldopentose) is essential for nucleic acids.

## Evidence

### Aldoses (Aldehyde Sugars)
| Carbon # | Sugars | Biological Role |
|----------|--------|-----------------|
| **Trioses (3C)** | Glyceraldehyde | Glycolysis intermediate |
| **Tetroses (4C)** | Erythrose | PPP intermediate |
| **Pentoses (5C)** | Ribose, arabinose, xylose | RNA/ATP backbone, plant polysaccharides |
| **Hexoses (6C)** | Glucose, galactose, mannose | Primary fuel, lactose, glycoproteins |

### Ketoses (Ketone Sugars)
| Carbon # | Sugars | Biological Role |
|----------|--------|-----------------|
| **Trioses (3C)** | Dihydroxyacetone | Glycolysis intermediate |
| **Pentoses (5C)** | Ribulose | PPP intermediate |
| **Hexoses (6C)** | Fructose, sorbose | Fruit sugars, alternative sweeteners |

### Key Distinction
| Feature | Glucose (Aldose) | Fructose (Ketose) |
|---------|------------------|-------------------|
| **Carbonyl position** | C1 (aldehyde) | C2 (ketone) |
| **Glycemic response** | Moderate | Lower (different absorption) |
| **Metabolism** | Regulated (PFK-1) | Unregulated (bypasses PFK-1) |
| **Sweetness** | Less sweet | Sweeter |

## Clinical Implication
- **Fructose metabolism bypasses PFK-1 regulation** — enters glycolysis downstream, promoting lipogenesis
- **High fructose intake** associated with metabolic syndrome, NAFLD
- **Ribose from PPP** essential for nucleotide synthesis in rapidly dividing tissues
- **METHAP relevance:** Understanding sugar metabolism informs dietary recommendations; glucose monitoring central to metabolic syndrome reversal
