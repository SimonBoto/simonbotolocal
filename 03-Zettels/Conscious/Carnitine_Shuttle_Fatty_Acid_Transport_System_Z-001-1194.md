# Carnitine Shuttle — Fatty Acid Transport System

## Overview

Long-chain fatty acids cannot cross the inner l membrane directly. The carnitine shuttle transports activated fatty acids into the  matrix for β-oxidation.

---

## The Five Components

### 1. Carnitine
**Structure:** β-hydroxy-γ-trimethylammonium butyrate
**Function:** Carrier molecule for acyl groups
**Sources:**
- Diet (meat, dairy)
- Synthesis (lysine + methionine)

### 2. CPT-I (Carnitine Palmitoyltransferase I)
**Location:** Outer mitochondrial membrane
**Function:**
```
Fatty acyl-CoA + Carnitine → Acylcarnitine + CoA
```
**Regulation:**
- **Inhibited by:** Malonyl-CoA (high when fed)
- **Activated by:** Low malonyl-CoA (fasting)

**Isoforms:**
- CPT-1A (liver) — most studied
- CPT-1B (muscle)
- CPT-1C (brain)

### 3. CACT (Carnitine-Acylcarnitine Translocase)
**Location:** Inner mitochondrial membrane
**Function:**
```
Acylcarnitine (in) + Carnitine (out) → Exchange
```
- Antiport mechanism
- 1:1 exchange

### 4. CPT-II (Carnitine Palmitoyltransferase II)
**Location:** Matrix side of inner membrane
**Function:**
```
Acylcarnitine + CoA → Fatty acyl-CoA + Carnitine
```
- Regenerates fatty acyl-CoA for β-oxidation
- Releases carnitine for reuse

### 5. Carnitine Acetyltransferase (CrAT)
**Location:** Mitochondrial matrix
**Function:**
```
Acetyl-CoA + Carnitine → Acetylcarnitine + CoA
```
- Buffer for excess acetyl-CoA
- Export acetyl groups from mitochondria

---

## Regulatory Control

### Fed State (High )
- High malonyl-CoA (fatty acid synthesis active)
- **CPT-I inhibited**
- Fatty acids directed to storage (triglycerides)
- β-oxidation suppressed

### Fasting State (Low Insulin, High Glucagon)
- Low malonyl-CoA
- **CPT-I active**
- Fatty acids enter mitochondria
- β-oxidation and ketogenesis active

---

## Clinical Disorders

### Primary Carnitine Deficiency
**Cause:** OCTN2 defect (carnitine transporter)
**Features:**
- Severe hypoglycemia
- Cardiomyopathy
- Muscle weakness
- Elevated free fatty acids

### CPT-I Deficiency
**Cause:** Genetic defect
**Features:**
- Hypoketotic hypoglycemia
- Hepatomegaly
- Normal acylcarnitines (transport blocked)

### CPT-II Deficiency
**Most common fatty acid oxidation disorder**
**Features:**
- Muscle pain/cramps with exercise
- Rhabdomyolysis
- Myoglobinuria
- Cardiomyopathy (severe forms)

### CACT Deficiency
**Severe, often fatal in infancy**
**Features:**
- Hypoglycemia
- Cardiac arrest
- Elevated acylcarnitines

---

## Therapeutic Applications

### L-Carnitine Supplementation
**Indications:**
- Primary carnitine deficiency
- Secondary deficiency (dialysis, valproate)
- Cardiac support

**Dose:** 1-3 g/day

### MCT Oil (Medium-Chain Triglycerides)
**Advantage:** C8-C10 fatty acids enter mitochondria WITHOUT carnitine
**Use:** Carnitine disorders, malabsorption
