## Core Claim
**Hyperinsulinemia promotes cardiovascular disease through multiple mechanisms** — including endothelial dysfunction, sympathetic activation, sodium retention, and dyslipidemia — that operate **independently of blood glucose levels**.

## Mechanisms

### 1. Endothelial Dysfunction
| Effect | Result |
|--------|--------|
| **Reduced NO production** | Impaired vasodilation |
| **Increased ET-1** | Vasoconstriction |
| **Inflammation** | Endothelial activation |

### 2. Sympathetic Activation
| Effect | Result |
|--------|--------|
| **Increased heart rate** | Cardiac workload |
| **Vasoconstriction** | Hypertension |
| **Cardiac remodeling** | Hypertrophy |

### 3. Sodium Retention
| Effect | Result |
|--------|--------|
| **Kidney sodium reabsorption** | Volume expansion |
| **Hypertension** | CVD risk |

### 4. Dyslipidemia
| Effect | Result |
|--------|--------|
| **Increased VLDL** | High triglycerides |
| **Decreased HDL** | Reduced protection |
| **Small dense LDL** | More atherogenic |

## The Pre-Diabetes Problem

| Population | Glucose | Insulin | CVD Risk |
|------------|---------|---------|----------|
| **Normal** | Normal | Normal | Low |
| **Pre-diabetes (IR)** | Normal/elevated | **Elevated** | **Elevated** |
| **Diabetes** | Elevated | Elevated | High |

**Key insight:** Risk begins when insulin rises, not when glucose rises.
