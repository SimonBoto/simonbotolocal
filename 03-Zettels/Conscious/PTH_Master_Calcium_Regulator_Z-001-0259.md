## Parathyroid Glands

**Anatomy:**
- **Number:** 4 glands (usually)
- **Location:** Posterior thyroid
- **Size:** 5-7 mm each
- **Weight:** 30-50 mg total

**Cell Types:**
- **Chief cells:** Produce PTH (calcium-sensing)
- **Oxyphil cells:** Unknown function (increase with age)

## Calcium-Sensing Receptor (CaSR)

**Function:**
- G-protein coupled receptor
- Senses extracellular calcium
- Located on chief cell membrane
- **High Ca²⁺:** Suppresses PTH
- **Low Ca²⁺:** Stimulates PTH

**Sensitivity:**
- Responds to 1-2% changes in Ca²⁺
- Fast response (seconds)
- Exquisite sensitivity

## PTH Actions

### 1. Bone (Osteoclast Activation)
**:**
- PTH binds osteoblasts (not osteoclasts directly)
- Osteoblasts release RANKL
- RANKL activates osteoclasts
- Bone resorption releases Ca²⁺ and PO₄

**Effect:**
- Rapid calcium release (minutes)
- Short-term calcium homeostasis
- Chronic excess → Bone loss

### 2. Kidney (Calcium Conservation)
**Proximal Tubule:**
- Inhibits phosphate reabsorption
- Increases phosphate excretion

**Distal Tubule:**
- Increases calcium reabsorption
- Reduces urinary calcium loss

**Collecting Duct:**
- Further calcium reabsorption

### 3. Kidney ( Activation)
**1α-Hydroxylase:**
- PTH stimulates CYP27B1
- Converts 25(OH)D → 1,25(OH)₂D
- Active calcitriol

**Result:**
- Increases intestinal calcium absorption
- Hours to days effect

## PTH Feedback Loop

```
Low Serum Ca²⁺
      ↓
CaSR → ↑ PTH secretion
      ↓
┌─────────────────────────────────┐
│ • Bone: Ca²⁺ release            │
│ • Kidney: Ca²⁺ reabsorption     │
│ • Kidney: ↑ Calcitriol          │
│ • Intestine: ↑ Ca²⁺ absorption  │
└─────────────────────────────────┘
      ↓
↑ Serum Ca²⁺
      ↓
CaSR → ↓ PTH secretion
```

##  Relevance

### Primary Hyperparathyroidism
- Excess PTH (usually adenoma)
- Hypercalcemia
- Hypophosphatemia
- Bone loss, kidney stones

### Secondary Hyperparathyroidism
- Appropriate PTH response to low Ca²⁺
- Seen in: Vitamin D deficiency, CKD, malabsorption
- Treatment: Address underlying cause

### Tertiary Hyperparathyroidism
- Autonomous PTH secretion (long-standing secondary)
- Seen in: Long-term dialysis patients
- Requires parathyroidectomy

### METHAP Context
- Monitor PTH if available
- Should suppress with adequate D
- Persistent elevation suggests Mg deficiency or resistance
