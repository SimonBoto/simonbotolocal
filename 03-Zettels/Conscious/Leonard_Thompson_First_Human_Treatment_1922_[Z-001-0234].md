# Leonard Thompson — First Human Insulin Treatment (1922)

> On January 23, 1922, 14-year-old Leonard Thompson became the first human to receive effective insulin treatment, marking the moment when diabetes transformed from a fatal disease to a manageable condition.

## Leonard Thompson (1908-1935)

**Background:**
- 14 years old at treatment
- Severe Type 1 diabetes
- Weight: 65 pounds (severely emaciated)
- Near death at Toronto General Hospital
- Previous treatment: Frederick Allen's starvation diet

**Clinical State (January 1922):**
- Diabetic ketoacidosis
- Severe wasting/cachexia
- Little time remaining
- Parents desperate

## First Injection Attempt (January 11, 1922)

**Preparation:**
- Used Banting-Best extract (less purified)
- Injected subcutaneously
- Dose: Small (safety concern)

**Results:**
- Slight reduction in blood glucose
- Allergic reaction (abscess at injection site)
- Mild fever
- Insufficient purity

**Decision:**
- Treatment paused
- Need for better purification
- James Collip intensified efforts

## Successful Treatment (January 23, 1922)

**Preparation:**
- Used Collip's purified extract
- Sterile technique
- Careful dosing

**Results:**
- Blood glucose dropped from 520 mg/dL to 120 mg/dL
- Clinical improvement dramatic
- Increased strength and alertness
- Weight gain began

**Subsequent Treatment:**
- Continued daily injections
- Rapid recovery
- Discharged from hospital
- Lived 13 more years (died 1935, age 27, of pneumonia)

## Significance

**Medical:**
- Proof of insulin's efficacy in humans
- Demonstrated diabetes could be treated
- Transformed prognosis completely

**Historical:**
- "The birth of insulin therapy"
- Marked end of pre-insulin era
- Beginning of modern diabetes management

**Symbolic:**
- Represented millions who would benefit
- "Miracle cure" narrative
- Hope for previously hopeless condition

## Legacy

**Immediate Impact:**
- News spread rapidly
- Other patients urgently sought treatment
- Demand exceeded supply
- Eli Lilly collaboration began

**Long-term:**
- Leonard Thompson lived 13 years with insulin
- Demonstrated treatment durability
- Proved children could survive T1DM

**Memorial:**
- Historical significance recognized
- Represents all pre-insulin patients
- Symbol of medical progress

## Contrast with Pre-Insulin Era

**Without Insulin:**
- Death within months
- Starvation diet (misery)
- No hope
- Fatal diagnosis

**With Insulin:**
- Normal life possible
- Dietary flexibility
- Future restored
- Chronic but manageable

**Transformation:**
- Most dramatic in medical history
- From death sentence to life
- Foundation for all subsequent diabetes care
