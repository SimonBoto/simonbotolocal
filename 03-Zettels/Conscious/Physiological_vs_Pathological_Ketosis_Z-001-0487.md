## Core Claim
**Nutritional ketosis (1-3 mM BHB) is a safe, regulated metabolic state with normal blood pH, while diabetic ketoacidosis (DKA, >10-25 mM BHB) is a life-threatening emergency characterized by metabolic acidosis (pH <7.30), severe hyperglycemia, and absolute insulin deficiency** — the critical distinction is insulin presence: physiological ketosis maintains low but functional insulin; DKA has absent insulin with uncontrolled ketogenesis and impaired ketone utilization.

## Evidence

### Key Distinctions
| Feature | Nutritional Ketosis | Diabetic Ketoacidosis |
|---------|---------------------|----------------------|
| **BHB level** | 1-3 mM | >10-25 mM |
| **Blood pH** | Normal (7.35-7.45) | Low (<7.30) |
| **Bicarbonate** | Normal (>22 mEq/L) | Low (<18 mEq/L) |
| **Insulin** | Low but present | Absent/deficient |
| **Glucose** | Normal or low | Very high (>250 mg/dL) |
| **Ketone production** | Regulated | Uncontrolled |
| **Ketone utilization** | Normal | Impaired |

### Physiological Ketosis
| Trigger | Mechanism |
|---------|-----------|
| **Ketogenic diet** | 70-80% fat, <50g carbs |
| **Fasting** | >24-48 hours glycogen depletion |
| **Exercise** | Prolonged endurance |

**Characteristics:**
- pH maintained by buffering systems
- Brain adapts to use ketones
- Safe and potentially beneficial

### Pathological Ketosis (DKA)
| Feature | Pathophysiology |
|---------|-----------------|
| **Type 1 diabetes** | Absolute insulin deficiency |
| **Uncontrolled lipolysis** | Unrestrained ketogenesis |
| **Hyperglycemia** | Osmotic diuresis, dehydration |
| **Acidosis** | Accumulation of ketoacids |
| **Electrolyte imbalance** | K⁺ shifts, volume depletion |

## Clinical Implication
- **Ketogenic diet safety:** Nutritional ketosis ≠ DKA
- **DKA emergency:** Requires insulin, fluids, electrolytes
- **Type 1 diabetes risk:** Higher DKA risk with illness/missed insulin
- **METHAP relevance:** Nutritional ketosis can be part of metabolic syndrome reversal strategy; distinct from dangerous DKA
