## Core Claim
**Elevated LDL cholesterol ranks #10** in cardiovascular risk factor importance — below diabetes, insulin resistance, hypertension, smoking, HDL, triglycerides, sdLDL, body weight, and Lp(a).

## The Hierarchy

| Rank | Factor | Relative Importance |
|------|--------|---------------------|
| 1 | Diabetes | Highest |
| 2 | Insulin resistance | Core driver |
| 3 | Hypertension | Major |
| 4 | Smoking | Significant |
| 5-9 | Metabolic markers | Moderate-high |
| **10** | **LDL cholesterol** | **Lower than assumed** |
| 11 | Total cholesterol | Lowest |

## The Disproportionate Focus

| Reality | Perception |
|---------|------------|
| LDL is #10 | Treated as #1 |
| Guidelines emphasize drugs | Lifestyle vague |
| Nutritious foods restricted | Processed foods increased |

## Clinical Implication

| Shift From | Shift To |
|------------|----------|
| LDL-centric approach | Comprehensive metabolic assessment |
| Statin-first | Lifestyle-first for primary prevention |
| Cholesterol obsession | Insulin resistance focus |

## Evidence

> "LDL-cholesterol and pharmaceutical treatments play a documented role, mainly due to plaque stabilization. However, risk is determined not only by LDL levels, but by particle quality characteristics."
> — CVD risk analysis
