# NODAT Risk Factors  Monitoring and Management

**NODAT (New-Onset  After Transplantation):** Diabetes developing in previously non-diabetic transplant recipients, strongly associated with sirolimus use.

## Risk Stratification

### High Risk (≥3 Risk Factors)
| Risk Factor | Relative Risk |
|-------------|---------------|
| Obesity (BMI >30) | 2-3× |
| Family history T2DM | 2-3× |
| Hispanic/African American | 2-4× |
| Hepatitis C positive | 2-3× |
| Age >50 | 1.5-2× |
| Pre-transplant IFG | 3-5× |

### Moderate Risk (1-2 Risk Factors)
- Standard monitoring protocols
- Preventive lifestyle counseling

### Low Risk (0 Risk Factors)
- Routine monitoring
- Lower threshold for intervention

## Monitoring Protocol

### Baseline (Pre-Sirolimus)
**Required Tests:**
- Fasting plasma glucose
- HbA1c
- Fasting  (calculate HOMA-IR)
- Lipid panel (TC, LDL, HDL, TG)
- BMI, waist circumference

**Optional:**
- OGTT if high suspicion
- Fasting C-peptide

### Follow-Up Schedule

**First Year (Intensive):**
| Timepoint | Tests |
|-----------|-------|
| 2 weeks | Fasting glucose |
| 1 month | Fasting glucose, symptoms review |
| 3 months | Fasting glucose, HbA1c |
| 6 months | Full metabolic panel |
| 9 months | Fasting glucose, HbA1c |
| 12 months | Full metabolic panel |

**Subsequent Years:**
- Every 3-6 months: Fasting glucose or HbA1c
- Annually: Full metabolic assessment

### Red Flags Requiring Immediate Evaluation
- Fasting glucose >200 mg/dL
- Random glucose >300 mg/dL
- Symptoms: polyuria, polydipsia, weight loss
- Ketones in urine

## Diagnostic Criteria (ADA)

**Any ONE of the following:**
1. Fasting plasma glucose ≥126 mg/dL (7.0 mmol/L)
2. 2-hour OGTT ≥200 mg/dL (11.1 mmol/L)
3. HbA1c ≥6.5%
4. Random glucose ≥200 mg/dL with symptoms

**Confirm on separate day (unless unequivocal hyperglycemia)**

## Management Algorithm

### Stage 1: Pre-Diabetes (IFG 100-125 mg/dL)

**Non-Pharmacologic:**
- Lifestyle modification (diet, exercise)
- Weight loss if overweight (5-10% target)
- Carbohydrate awareness

**Pharmacologic (Consider):**
- Metformin (if eGFR >30)
- Consider TZD if high risk

**Sirolimus:**
- Optimize dose (therapeutic drug monitoring)
- Consider alternative immunosuppressant if possible

### Stage 2: Diabetes (FPG ≥126 mg/dL)

**Step 1: Metformin** (First-line)
- Start: 500 mg daily with meals
- Titrate: 500 mg weekly to max 2000 mg/day
- Monitor: eGFR, B12 annually

**Step 2: Add Agent if HbA1c >7%**
| Agent | Benefits | Considerations |
|-------|----------|----------------|
| **SGLT2 inhibitor** | Cardiorenal protection | UTI risk, volume depletion |
| **GLP-1 agonist** | Weight loss, CV benefit | GI side effects, injection |
| **DPP-4 inhibitor** | Well tolerated | Neutral weight, less potent |

**Avoid:** Sulfonylureas (hypoglycemia, weight gain)

**Step 3: Insulin if HbA1c >9% or symptomatic**
- Basal insulin (glargine, detemir, NPH)
- Target: Fasting glucose 100-130 mg/dL

### Stage 3: Sirolimus Modification

**Consider if:**
- Poor glycemic control despite 2-3 agents
- Severe hyperglycemia at diagnosis
- Patient preference

**Options:**
1. **Dose reduction** (if therapeutic margin)
2. **Switch to alternative:**
   - Belatacept (co-stimulation blocker)
   - Mycophenolate mofetil
   - Everolimus (different side effect profile)

**Caution:** Risk of rejection with changes

## Prevention Strategies

### Pre-Transplant (High-Risk Patients)
- Optimize weight
- Exercise program
- Metformin prophylaxis (evidence mixed)

### Post-Transplant
- **Lowest effective sirolimus dose**
- Therapeutic drug monitoring
- Early lifestyle intervention
- Metformin for pre-diabetes

## METHAP Integration

**Key Insight:** Sirolimus exemplifies the need for **metabolic monitoring** with immunosuppression.

**Protocol Elements:**
1. Pre-treatment risk stratification
2. Structured monitoring schedule
3. Early intervention thresholds
4. Multidisciplinary approach (nephrology + endocrinology)
