## Core Claim
**The thermic effect of food (TEF) varies dramatically by macronutrient** — protein requires 20-30% of its calories for digestion and processing, carbohydrates 5-10%, and fats 0-3%. This means 100 calories of protein provides only 70-80 net calories, while 100 calories of fat provides 97-100 net calories.

## Thermic Effect by Macronutrient

### Protein — Highest TEF
| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **TEF range** | 20-30% |
| **Net availability** | 70-80% |
| **Why so high** | Complex digestion, nitrogen handling |
| **Example** | 100 cal protein → 70-80 usable |

### Carbohydrates — Moderate TEF
| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **TEF range** | 5-10% |
| **Net availability** | 90-95% |
| **Varies by type** | Fiber higher, sugar lower |
| **Example** | 100 cal carbs → 90-95 usable |

### Fats — Lowest TEF
| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **TEF range** | 0-3% |
| **Net availability** | 97-100% |
| **Why so low** | Efficient storage process |
| **Example** | 100 cal fat → 97-100 usable |

## Why TEF Differs

### Protein Processing Costs
| Process | Energy Cost |
|---------|-------------|
| **Digestion** | Peptide bond breakdown |
| **Absorption** | Active transport |
| **Nitrogen removal** | Urea cycle (expensive) |
| **Amino acid synthesis** | Conversion to body proteins |

### Fat Processing Efficiency
| Feature | Efficiency |
|---------|------------|
| **Simple digestion** | Lipase action |
| **Passive absorption** | No active transport |
| **Direct storage** | Minimal conversion |
| **Body's preferred storage** | Evolutionary adaptation |

## Practical Implications

### For Weight Management
| Strategy | Effect |
|----------|--------|
| **Increase protein %** | Automatic calorie reduction via TEF |
| **Swap fat for protein** | Net calorie decrease |
| **Keep some fat** | Satiety, hormones |
| **Moderate carbs** | Balance energy and TEF |

### Example Calculation
| Diet | Calories | Effective Calories |
|------|----------|-------------------|
| **High-protein (30%)** | 2000 | ~1850 |
| **Moderate-protein (15%)** | 2000 | ~1950 |
| **Low-protein (10%)** | 2000 | ~1980 |

**150-calorie difference from TEF alone.**

## Beyond Weight Loss

### Metabolic Benefits of High TEF
| Benefit | Mechanism |
|---------|-----------|
| **Higher metabolic rate** | More energy burned at rest |
| **Better body composition** | Protein preserves muscle |
| **Improved satiety** | Slower digestion |
| **Post-meal thermogenesis** | Heat production |
