# Leaky Gut and Autoimmune Connection — Intestinal Permeability Mechanism

## Core Claim
**Intestinal permeability (leaky gut) is a prerequisite for autoimmune disease** — Dr. Alessio Fasano's research demonstrates that tight junction dysfunction allows antigen entry, triggering the immune cascade that leads to autoimmunity. Healing the gut barrier can prevent or halt autoimmune processes.

## The Mechanism

### Fasano's Triad
Autoimmunity requires three factors:
1. **Genetic susceptibility** — HLA genes
2. **Environmental trigger** — gluten, toxins, infection
3. **Intestinal permeability** — leaky gut

**Remove any one → prevent autoimmunity.**

### Step-by-Step Process
| Step | Event | Result |
|------|-------|--------|
| **1** | Tight junctions open | Barrier compromised |
| **2** | Undigested proteins enter | Antigen exposure |
| **3** | Immune activation | T-cell response |
| **4** | Molecular mimicry | Self-tissue attack |
| **5** | Chronic inflammation | Autoimmune disease |

## Zonulin — The Gatekeeper

### What Is Zonulin
| Feature | Function |
|---------|----------|
| **Protein** | Regulates tight junctions |
| **Produced by** | Gut, liver |
| **Triggered by** | Gluten, bacteria, inflammation |

### Zonulin and Disease
| Condition | Zonulin Level |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Celiac disease** | Very high |
| **T1D** | Elevated |
| **MS** | Elevated |
| **IBD** | Elevated |
| **Healthy** | Low |
