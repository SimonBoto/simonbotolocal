# BHB as Energy Substrate for Brain and Heart

## Transport
**BHB crosses membranes easily:**
- **Blood-brain barrier:** Monocarboxylate transporters (MCTs)
- **Cellular uptake:** MCT1, MCT2, MCT4
- **No [[KEYWORD-Insulin|insulin]] required:** Unlike glucose

## Brain [[KEYWORD-Metabolism|metabolism]]

### During Starvation
- **60-70% of brain energy** from ketones after adaptation
- **Spares glucose:** For obligate glucose users (RBCs)
- **Spares protein:** Reduces gluconeogenesis from amino acids

### Neuroprotective Effects
- **Efficient fuel:** More ATP per oxygen molecule than glucose
- **Reduces oxidative stress:** Decreases ROS production
- **Anti-inflammatory:** NLRP3 inhibition
- **Epigenetic benefits:** HDAC inhibition

### Clinical Applications
- **Epilepsy:** Ketogenic diet reduces seizures (established)
- **Alzheimer's:** "Type 3 diabetes" — [[KEYWORD-Insulin_Resistance|insulin resistance]] in brain
- **Traumatic brain injury:** Alternative fuel when glucose metabolism impaired
- **Parkinson's:** Mitochondrial dysfunction benefits

## Heart Metabolism

### Preferred Fuel
- **Heart readily uses BHB** as energy source
- **More efficient:** Better coupling than fatty acids
- **Less oxygen consumption:** Per ATP produced

### Cardiac Benefits
- **Improved function:** In heart failure models
- **Reduced oxidative stress:** Compared to fatty acid oxidation
- **Hypertrophy protection:** Prevents pathological remodeling

## Energy Yield

| Substrate | ATP per Molecule | ATP per O₂ |
|-----------|------------------|------------|
| **Glucose** | 30-32 | ~6.5 |
| **BHB** | ~4.7 | ~7.0 |
| **Fatty acids** | ~106 (palmitate) | ~6.0 |

**BHB advantage:** Better oxygen efficiency (critical in ischemia)

## Utilization Pathway
```
BHB → Acetoacetate (β-OHBDH)
AcAc → Acetoacetyl-CoA (SCOT enzyme)
Acetoacetyl-CoA → 2 Acetyl-CoA (thiolase)
Acetyl-CoA → Krebs cycle → ATP
```
