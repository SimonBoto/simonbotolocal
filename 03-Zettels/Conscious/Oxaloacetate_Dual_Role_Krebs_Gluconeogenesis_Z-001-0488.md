# Oxaloacetate Dual Role: Krebs and Gluconeogenesis

## Structure and Properties
- **4-carbon dicarboxylic acid**
- **α-keto acid** (like pyruvate)
- **Highly reactive:** Does not accumulate in free form
- **Central metabolite**

## Role 1: Krebs Cycle

### Condensation with Acetyl-CoA
```
Oxaloacetate + Acetyl-CoA → Citrate (citrate synthase)
```
- **Entry point** for acetyl-CoA into Krebs cycle
- **Rate-limiting step** of cycle
- **Inhibited by:** ATP, citrate, NADH

### Regeneration
- OAA regenerated at end of cycle (malate → OAA)
- **Turnover:** Each acetyl-CoA requires one OAA

## Role 2: Gluconeogenic Precursor

### Conversion to PEP
```
Oxaloacetate + GTP → Phosphoenolpyruvate + GDP + CO₂
```
**Enzyme:** PEP carboxykinase (PEPCK)

**Significance:**
- Bypasses irreversible pyruvate kinase step
- Allows glucose synthesis from pyruvate/lactate
- **Key regulatory point** in gluconeogenesis

## The Competition Problem

### During Fasting
**Liver faces competing demands:**

| Pathway | Need | OAA Consumption |
|---------|------|-----------------|
| **Krebs cycle** | Process acetyl-CoA | Condensation with acetyl-CoA |
| **Gluconeogenesis** | Make glucose | Conversion to PEP |

### Resolution
- **OAA preferentially diverted** to gluconeogenesis during fasting
- Less OAA available for Krebs cycle
- Excess acetyl-CoA → **shunted to ketogenesis**

### Result
**Starvation :**
- Glucose maintained for brain/RBCs
- Ketones produced as alternative fuel
- Fatty acids oxidized for ketogenesis

## Transport Problem: OAA Cannot Cross l Membrane

### Solution Required
**OAA must be converted to transportable form:**
- Malate shuttle
- Aspartate shuttle
