# Lp(a) Independent Risk Factor for Heart Disease and Stroke

## Core Claim
**Elevated lipoprotein(a) [Lp(a)]** is an independent, causal risk factor for atherosclerotic cardiovascular disease (ASCVD) and stroke that is **genetically determined, prevalent in 20-30% of the population**, and not captured by standard lipid panels — representing a major under-recognized cardiovascular risk.

## What is Lp(a)?

### Structure
| Component | Function |
|-----------|----------|
| **LDL-like particle** | Cholesterol delivery |
| **Apo(a) protein** | Unique to Lp(a), pro-thrombotic |
| **Plasminogen homology** | Interferes with clot lysis |

### Key Properties
| Property | Implication |
|----------|-------------|
| **Genetically determined** | 80-90% heritable |
| **Stable throughout life** | One measurement sufficient |
| **Not affected by lifestyle** | Diet/exercise don't lower |
| **Not on standard panels** | Must be specifically ordered |

## Risk Thresholds

| Level | Risk | Population |
|-------|------|------------|
| **<30 mg/dL (<75 nmol/L)** | Normal | ~70% |
| **30-50 mg/dL** | Borderline | ~15% |
| **>50 mg/dL (>125 nmol/L)** | High risk | ~20% |
| **>100 mg/dL** | Very high | ~5% |

## Evidence

> "Mendelian randomization studies establish Lp(a) as a causal risk factor for ASCVD and stroke independent of LDL cholesterol; elevated Lp(a) is present in 20-30% of the population and associated with 2-3x increased cardiovascular risk."
> — (Major Lp(a) studies, 2020s)

## Clinical Implications

### Who to Test
| Population | Recommendation |
|------------|----------------|
| **Premature ASCVD** | Test all |
| **Family history of early CVD** | Test all |
| **Recurrent CVD despite statins** | Test all |
| **Calcific aortic stenosis** | Test all |
| **General population** | Consider once (lifetime) |
