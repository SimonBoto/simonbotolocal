# Population Attributable Fraction Burden Quantification

## Source
[[Diabetes_CVD_Global_Burden_NEJM_2023_L-001-0001]]

## Core Claim
The five modifiable risk factors (BMI, systolic BP, non-HDL cholesterol, smoking, and diabetes) combined account for 53-57% of new cardiovascular disease cases and 19-22% of all-cause deaths globally.

## Key Details
- Combined population attributable fraction (PAF) for the five modifiable risk factors is 53-57% for new CVD cases
- Combined PAF for the five modifiable risk factors is 19-22% for all-cause deaths
- This quantifies the enormous potential for prevention through lifestyle modification
- The PAF demonstrates that a substantial portion of cardiovascular disease burden is preventable
- These metrics provide concrete evidence for public health prioritization

## Evidence
- Source-litnote extraction pending
- Evidence from NEJM 2023 study with 1.5 million participants across 34 countries

## Significance
This finding provides the quantitative foundation for public health policy, demonstrating that targeted interventions against these modifiable risk factors could prevent more than half of new CVD cases globally.

## Clinical Significance
- Supports evidence-based clinical guidelines for prevention
- Provides metrics for evaluating effectiveness of preventive interventions
- Highlights the importance of population-level approaches to cardiovascular health
- Demonstrates the clinical impact of lifestyle and dietary interventions
