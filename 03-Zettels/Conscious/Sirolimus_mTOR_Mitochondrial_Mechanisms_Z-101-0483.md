## Overview
Sirolimus (rapamycin) inhibits the mechanistic target of rapamycin (mTOR), a critical regulator of cell growth, , and mitochondrial function.

## mTOR Complexes
| Complex | Primary Function | Sirolimus Sensitivity |
|---------|------------------|----------------------|
| **mTORC1** | Growth, anabolism, autophagy suppression | **High** (primary target) |
| **mTORC2** | Cell survival, metabolism, cytoskeleton | Lower (chronic/high dose) |

## Mitochondrial Toxicity Mechanisms

### 1. Impaired Mitochondrial Biogenesis
```
Sirolimus → mTORC1 inhibition → ↓ PGC-1α → ↓ Mitochondrial biogenesis
```

| Step | Mechanism | Consequence |
|------|-----------|-------------|
| mTORC1 inhibition | Direct sirolimus target | Downstream signaling disrupted |
| ↓ PGC-1α expression | mTORC1 normally promotes PGC-1α | Master regulator of biogenesis reduced |
| ↓ mtDNA replication | PGC-1α drives mitochondrial DNA copy | Fewer mitochondrial genomes |
| ↓ Mitochondrial protein synthesis | Reduced transcription/translation | Decreased mitochondrial mass |

**Result:** Reduced mitochondrial capacity in high-energy tissues (heart, muscle, brain, β-cells)

---

### 2. Suppressed Oxidative Phosphorylation
```
mTORC1 inhibition → ↓ ETC protein translation → ↓ OXPHOS → ↓ ATP
```

| Component | Effect |
|-----------|--------|
| **Complex I** | Reduced expression |
| **Complex II** | Reduced expression |
| **Complex III** | Reduced expression |
| **Complex IV** | Reduced expression |
| **Complex V (ATP synthase)** | Reduced expression |

**Consequences:**
- Decreased ATP production
- Metabolic stress in energy-dependent tissues
- Potential cell death in extreme cases

---

### 3. Reactive Oxygen Species (ROS) Generation
```
ETC impairment → Electron leakage → ROS formation → Oxidative damage
```

| Source | Mechanism |
|--------|-----------|
| **Complex I** | Electron backflow to NADH |
| **Complex III** | Q-cycle disruption |

**Damage targets:**
- Mitochondrial DNA (mutations)
- Cardiolipin (membrane integrity)
- ETC proteins (vicious cycle)
- Nuclear DNA (if ROS escape)

**Mitochondrial permeability transition pore (mPTP):**
- ROS + Ca²⁺ overload → mPTP opening
- Mitochondrial depolarization
- Apoptosis or necrosis

---

### 4. Autophagy and Mitophagy Dysregulation

#### Normal mTOR Function
```
mTORC1 active → Autophagy suppressed → Growth/anabolism
```

#### Sirolimus Effect
```
mTORC1 inhibited → Autophagy induced → Cellular component clearance
```

| Process | Acute Effect | Chronic Effect |
|---------|--------------|----------------|
| **Autophagy** | Protective (removes damaged components) | Excessive if uncontrolled |
| **Mitophagy** | Removes damaged mitochondria | May clear functional mitochondria |
| **Net result** | Improved mitochondrial quality | Depletion if biogenesis < clearance |

**Critical balance:**
- Biogenesis vs. mitophagy determines mitochondrial mass
- Chronic sirolimus: biogenesis suppressed + mitophagy increased = net loss

---

### 5. Calcium Dysregulation
| Normal Function | Sirolimus Disruption |
|-----------------|----------------------|
| mTORC1 regulates Ca²⁺ handling | Disrupted calcium homeostasis |
| Mitochondrial Ca²⁺ buffering | Impaired uptake/release |
| Cytoplasmic Ca²⁺ signaling | Altered patterns |

**Consequences:**
- Mitochondrial Ca²⁺ overload
- mPTP opening
- Mitochondrial swelling
- Cell death

## Tissue-Specific Vulnerability
| Tissue | Mitochondrial Demand | Sirolimus Sensitivity |
|--------|---------------------|----------------------|
| **Heart** | Very high | High (cardiomyopathy risk) |
| **Skeletal muscle** | High | High (sarcopenia) |
| **Pancreatic β-cells** | Very high | High (diabetes risk) |
| **Brain** | Very high | High (neurotoxicity) |
| **Liver** | Moderate | Moderate |
| **Adipose** | Moderate | Moderate |

## Dose-Dependence and Context

### Low/Intermittent Dosing
- Primarily mTORC1 inhibition
- Beneficial autophagy induction
- Potential longevity benefits
- Lower mitochondrial toxicity

### High/Chronic Dosing
- mTORC2 inhibition (with prolonged use)
- Excessive autophagy
- Significant mitochondrial depletion
- Metabolic dysfunction

## Therapeutic Implications

### When Toxicity is Desired
| Context | Rationale |
|---------|-----------|
| **Cancer** | Cancer cells rely on mitochondria; sirolimus-induced dysfunction promotes death |
| **Autoimmunity** | Immune cell suppression |

### When Toxicity is Problematic
| Context | Concern |
|---------|---------|
| **Transplantation** | NODAT (new-onset diabetes); cardiovascular effects |
| **Longevity** | Metabolic side effects may limit long-term use |
| **Metabolic disease** | Worsens glucose homeostasis |
