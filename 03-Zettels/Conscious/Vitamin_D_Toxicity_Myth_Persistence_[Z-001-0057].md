# [[KEYWORD-Vitamin_D|vitamin d]] Toxicity Myth Persistence

Despite modern understanding that vitamin D toxicity is rare and requires massive supplemental intake (far exceeding physiological production), the toxicity myth persists in public consciousness, medical education, and [[KEYWORD-Clinical|clinical]] practice. This persistence stems from 1950s regulatory responses and failure to update curricula with genetic discoveries.

**Why the myth persists:**
- Medical education lag in updating safety information
- Conservative professional guidelines (IOM, medical societies)
- Fear of litigation drives defensive practice
- Patient/provider anxiety about "fat-soluble vitamin" toxicity

**Reality:**
- Toxicity requires sustained intake of 40,000+ IU daily for months
- Body produces 10,000-20,000 IU naturally in 15-30 minutes of sun
- True genetic susceptibility (IIH) is rare (<1:10,000)

**Clinical impact:** Patients remain deficient due to provider reluctance to prescribe adequate doses; population health suffers from unfounded fears.
