## Core Thesis
Menaquinone-7 (MK-7) exhibits superior pharmacokinetic properties compared to other vitamin K forms, characterized by prolonged half-life, enhanced bioavailability, and preferential distribution to extrahepatic tissues, making it the optimal form for cardiovascular and bone health applications.

## Key Concepts

### Pharmacokinetic Profile
- **Half-life:** ~72 hours (3 days) — significantly longer than MK-4 (1-2 hours)
- **Peak plasma concentration:** 6-8 hours after dosing
- **Bioavailability:** 10x higher than MK-4 at equivalent doses
- **Dose-response:** Linear relationship up to 450 mcg, then plateau
- **Accumulation:** Steady-state reached after ~3 weeks of daily dosing

### Tissue Distribution
- **Liver:** Moderate accumulation (primary site of clotting factor synthesis)
- **Bone:** High accumulation (osteocalcin activation)
- **Vasculature:** High accumulation (MGP activation for vascular protection)
- **Extrahepatic tissues:** Preferential distribution compared to MK-4

### Clinical Implications
- **Once-daily dosing:** Due to long half-life and sustained plasma levels
- **Sustained protein activation:** Continuous carboxylation of MGP and osteocalcin
- **Lower effective doses:** 100-200 mcg/day sufficient for therapeutic effect
- **Reduced variability:** More consistent plasma concentrations across individuals

## Evidence Base

### Thijssen et al. (2006)
- Compared MK-4 and MK-7 in healthy volunteers
- MK-7 achieved 7-8x higher plasma concentrations than MK-4
- MK-7 showed sustained plasma levels over 72 hours

### Schurgers et al. (2007)
- Demonstrated superior carboxylation of MGP with MK-7 vs MK-4
- MK-7 more effective at activating extrahepatic K-dependent proteins

### Knapen et al. (2013)
- 3-year RCT showing MK-7 180 mcg/day reduced arterial stiffness
- Long duration required to demonstrate clinical benefits
- Demonstrated safety and efficacy of long-term MK-7 use

## METHAP Protocol Integration
- Uses MK-7 (100-200 mcg/day) based on PK evidence
- Aligns with sustained activation needed for vascular protection
- Combined with high-dose vitamin D to maximize synthesis of K-dependent proteins
- Dosing protocol optimized for PK profile (once-daily, consistent timing)

## Significance

- **Lower effective doses:** 100-200 mcg/day sufficient for therapeutic effect

## Safety Considerations
- No adverse effects reported at doses up to 450 mcg/day
- No interactions with anticoagulants at therapeutic doses
- Long-term safety established in 3-year clinical trials
