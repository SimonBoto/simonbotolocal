## Core Claim
**All keto acids are carboxylic acids, but not all carboxylic acids are keto acids — keto acids (α-keto acids) contain both a ketone group and a carboxyl group (R-CO-COOH), while carboxylic acids contain only the carboxyl group (R-COOH)** — pyruvate, α-ketoglutarate, and oxaloacetate are essential metabolic keto acids, while acetate, propionate, and lactate are non-keto carboxylic acids.

## Evidence

### Structural Comparison
| Type | Formula | Functional Groups | Examples |
|------|---------|-------------------|----------|
| **Keto acid (α-keto)** | R-CO-COOH | Ketone + carboxyl | Pyruvate, α-KG, OAA |
| **Carboxylic acid** | R-COOH | Carboxyl only | Acetate, propionate, lactate |

### Key Keto Acids in Metabolism
| Name | Structure | Role |
|------|-----------|------|
| **Pyruvate** | CH₃-CO-COOH | Glycolysis end product |
| **α-Ketoglutarate** | HOOC-CH₂-CH₂-CO-COOH | Krebs cycle intermediate |
| **Oxaloacetate** | HOOC-CH₂-CO-COOH | Krebs cycle, gluconeogenesis |

### Non-Keto Carboxylic Acids
| Name | Structure | Role |
|------|-----------|------|
| **Acetate** | CH₃-COOH | Acetyl-CoA precursor |
| **Propionate** | CH₃-CH₂-COOH | Gut metabolite, gluconeogenic |
| **Lactate** | CH₃-CHOH-COOH | Anaerobic glycolysis |

### Key Relationship
```
All keto acids ⊂ Carboxylic acids
Keto acids = Carboxylic acids with additional ketone
```

## Clinical Implication
- **Keto acidosis:** Accumulation of keto acids (diabetic, starvation)
- **Organic acidemias:** Defects in carboxylic acid metabolism (propionic, methylmalonic)
- **B-vitamin dependence:** Both classes require B-vitamins for metabolism
- **METHAP relevance:** Understanding acid classes informs interpretation of metabolic panels and organic acid tests
