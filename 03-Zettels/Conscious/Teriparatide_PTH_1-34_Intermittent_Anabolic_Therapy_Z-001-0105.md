# Teriparatide — PTH 1-34 Intermittent Anabolic Therapy

## Core Principle
Recombinant human parathyroid hormone (1-34 fragment) given as **daily subcutaneous pulses** stimulates osteoblasts and builds new bone.

## The PTH Paradox

### Continuous vs. Intermittent
| Pattern | Effect |  Context |
|---------|--------|------------------|
| **Continuous elevation** | Bone resorption | Hyperparathyroidism causes bone loss |
| **Intermittent pulses** | Bone formation | Teriparatide builds bone |

**:** PTH receptor signaling kinetics determine response
- Continuous → cAMP sustained → RANKL upregulation → resorption
- Intermittent → cAMP transient → Wnt activation → formation

## Molecular Mechanism

### Osteoblast Stimulation
1. Binds PTH1 receptor on osteoblasts and osteocytes
2. Activates cAMP/PKA signaling pathway
3. Increases Wnt signaling (canonical pathway)
4. Promotes osteoblast proliferation and differentiation
5. Reduces osteoblast apoptosis

### Collateral Effects
- **Increases RANKL:** Also stimulates some resorption (coupling)
- **Net effect:** Formation exceeds resorption → bone gain

## Clinical Pharmacology

### Administration
- **Dose:** 20 μg daily
- **Route:** Subcutaneous injection (thigh, abdomen)
- **Timing:** Any time of day, with or without food
- **Duration:** Maximum 2 years lifetime

### Bone Mineral Density Effects
| Site | Increase at 18–24 months |
|------|--------------------------|
| **Lumbar spine** | 9–13% |
| **Femoral neck** | 3–6% |
| **Total hip** | 4–7% |

### Fracture Risk Reduction
- **Vertebral fractures:** 65% reduction
- **Non-vertebral fractures:** 53% reduction
- **Effect timeline:** Significant reduction by 12 months

## Duration Limit: The 2-Year Rule

### Rationale
- Rat studies: High-dose, long-term PTH → osteosarcoma
- Human relevance: Unknown, but regulatory precaution
- **Black box warning:** Limit to 2 years lifetime

### After 2 Years
- Must transition to antiresorptive (preserve gains)
- Can restart after antiresorptive period? No — lifetime limit
- Alternative anabolic: Abaloparatide (separate lifetime limit)

## Indications

### FDA-Approved
- Postmenopausal osteoporosis with high fracture risk
- Primary or hypogonadal osteoporosis in men
- Glucocorticoid-induced osteoporosis

### Clinical Scenarios Favoring Teriparatide
- Multiple vertebral fractures
- T-score < -3.5 with fractures
- Fracture while on bisphosphonate
- Severe osteoporosis requiring rapid BMD gain

## Adverse Effects

### Common
- **Hypercalcemia:** 3–11% (usually mild, transient)
- **Nausea:** 8%
- **Dizziness:** 8%
- **Leg cramps:** 3%
- **Injection site reactions:** Common but mild

### Serious
- **Osteosarcoma:** Theoretical concern (no confirmed cases in humans at therapeutic doses)
- **Hypercalcemia-related:** Kidney stones (rare)

## Contraindications
- **Paget's disease of bone:** Risk of osteosarcoma already elevated
- **Pediatric patients:** Open epiphyses
- **Prior radiation therapy:** Osteosarcoma risk
- **Bone metastases:** May stimulate tumor growth
- **Hypercalcemia:** Pre-existing

## Monitoring
| Time | Assessment |
|------|------------|
| **Baseline** | , creatinine, alkaline phosphatase |
| **1–6 months** | Serum calcium (peak effect) |
| **12–18 months** | DEXA to assess response |
| **Ongoing** | Adherence, injection technique |

## Comparison to Other Anabolics
- **vs. Abaloparatide:** Similar efficacy, TPTD has longer track record
- **vs. Romosozumab:** Slower onset, no CV risk signal, daily vs. monthly
