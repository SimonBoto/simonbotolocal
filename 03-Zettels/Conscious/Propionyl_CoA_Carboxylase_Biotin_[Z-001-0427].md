# Propionyl-CoA Carboxylase — Biotin-Dependent Step

> Propionyl-CoA carboxylase (PCC) catalyzes the ATP-dependent carboxylation of propionyl-CoA to D-methylmalonyl-CoA, requiring biotin as essential cofactor.

## Reaction

**Chemical Equation:**
```
Propionyl-CoA + HCO₃⁻ + ATP → D-Methylmalonyl-CoA + ADP + Pi
```

## Enzyme Characteristics

**Location:** [[KEYWORD-Mitochondria|mitochondria]]
**Structure:** Alpha and beta subunits
**Cofactor:** Biotin (vitamin B7)
**Type:** Biotin-dependent carboxylase family

## Biotin Role

**Carboxyl Carrier:**
- Biotin covalently bound to enzyme
- Transfers activated CO₂ to substrate
- ATP-dependent activation

## [[KEYWORD-Clinical|clinical]] Deficiency

**Propionic Acidemia:**
- Inherited PCC deficiency
- Organic acid accumulation
- Life-threatening metabolic crisis
- Treated with biotin (if responsive)

**Biotin Deficiency:**
- Impaired PCC activity
- Propionic acid accumulation
- Rare (biotin widely available)
