## Core Concept

The quality of nutrition as a primary determinant of metabolic health outcomes, influencing all five pillars through substrate availability and metabolic signaling.

## Key Principles

- **Whole Foods vs. Processed:** Whole, unprocessed foods provide optimal nutrient density and avoid metabolic disruptors
- **Macronutrient Quality:** Focus on high-quality fats, proteins, and carbohydrates that support metabolic flexibility
- **Micronutrient Synergy:** Nutrients work synergistically, making food-based nutrition superior to isolated supplementation
- **Glycation Control:** Food choices directly impact AGE formation and glycation stress

## Clinical Significance

Nutrition quality is foundational to all five pillars of metabolic health, providing the substrates and signaling molecules necessary for mitochondrial function, metabolomic balance, glycation control, mineral status, and fatty acid metabolism.
