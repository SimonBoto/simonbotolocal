## Core Concept

Switching from anabolic to antiresorptive therapy preserves bone mass gains after the anabolic phase concludes.

**Key Concept:** Anabolic agents build bone but have duration limits; antiresorptives preserve gains but don't create new bone.

**Clinical Imperative:** Without sequential therapy, rapid bone loss occurs after anabolic discontinuation.

## Standard Protocol

- **Timing:** Begin antiresorptive therapy immediately after final anabolic dose
- **Preferred agents:** Alendronate (oral) or Zoledronic acid (IV)
- **Rationale:** Prevents rapid bone loss that occurs in first 6–12 months post-anabolic therapy

## Evidence

- ALGOB study: Teriparatide → alendronate superior to either alone
- BONE study: Teriparatide → zoledronic acid maintains BMD gains

## Monitoring

- DEXA at 12 months to assess preservation of gains
- Biochemical markers (CTX, P1NP) at baseline and 6 months

## Clinical Pitfalls

### Delayed Transition After Anabolic Therapy
- **Risk:** Up to 5–7% BMD loss in first 6 months
- **Solution:** Schedule follow-up within 1 week of last anabolic dose

### Drug Holiday Considerations
- For some agents, brief drug holiday may be considered before transition
- Evidence suggests immediate transition is superior for BMD preservation
