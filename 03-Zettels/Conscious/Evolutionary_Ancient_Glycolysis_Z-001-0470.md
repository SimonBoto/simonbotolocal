# Evolutionary Ancient Glycolysis

## Core Claim
**Glycolysis is ~3.5 billion years old, present in the Last Universal Common Ancestor (LUCA), and universally conserved across archaea, bacteria, and eukaryotes — its anaerobic function, triose-based efficiency, and enzyme conservation make it among the most ancient metabolic pathways** — the splitting of glucose into two trioses doubled energy yield and enabled complex life.

## Evidence

### Timeline
| Era | Event |
|-----|-------|
| **~3.5 BYA** | Glycolysis evolves |
| **~3.0 BYA** | Photosynthesis emerges |
| **~2.4 BYA** | Great Oxygenation Event |
| **~1.8 BYA** | Eukaryotes appear |

### Universal Distribution
| Domain | Glycolysis |
|--------|------------|
| **Archaea** | Complete pathway |
| **Bacteria** | All phyla |
| **Eukaryotes** | Conserved enzymes |

### Evidence for Antiquity
| Feature | Significance |
|---------|--------------|
| **Anaerobic function** | Works without O₂ (early Earth was anoxic) |
| **Enzyme conservation** | Key enzymes 30-50% identical across domains |
| **Triose efficiency** | Splitting 6C → 2×3C doubles ATP yield |
| **Mineral catalysis** | Some steps proceed non-enzymatically on Fe-S surfaces |

### Enzyme Conservation Examples
- **Triose phosphate isomerase:** Ancient fold (TIM barrel)
- **Glyceraldehyde-3-phosphate dehydrogenase:** Conserved active site
- **Enolase:** Universal in energy metabolism

## Clinical Implication
- **Genetic defects often lethal:** Essential for all life
- **Cancer co-opts ancient pathway:** Warburg effect exploits glycolysis
- **Conservation implies optimality:** Hard to improve after 3.5 billion years
- **METHAP relevance:** Supporting glycolysis with thiamine (B1) ensures this ancient, essential pathway functions optimally
