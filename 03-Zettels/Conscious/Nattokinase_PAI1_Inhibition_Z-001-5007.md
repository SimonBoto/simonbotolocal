# Nattokinase_PAI1_Inhibition

## Source
[[L-001-0654_Vaughan2017_PAI1_Aging_L-001-0654]]

## Core Claim
Nattokinase, a fibrinolytic enzyme derived from fermented soy (natto), directly cleaves and inactivates plasminogen activator inhibitor-1 (PAI-1), thereby enhancing tissue plasminogen activator (tPA) activity and promoting arterial cleaning.

## Key Details
- Nattokinase (from fermented soy) cleaves/inactivates PAI-1 directly
- Enhances tPA action, promotes arterial cleaning
- Japanese natto consumption linked to longevity

## Evidence
- [[Urano_2001_J_Biol_Chem_R-001-0011]] — Foundational mechanism evidence
- [[Chen_2018_Biomarker_Insights_R-001-0012]] — Clinical efficacy evidence
- [[Vaughan_2017_Arterioscler_Thromb_Vasc_Biol_R-001-0006]] — Biological relevance

## Significance
Nattokinase represents a natural, food-derived therapeutic strategy to inhibit PAI-1 and potentially slow aging-related cardiovascular decline.

## Clinical Significance
- Nattokinase supplementation may benefit individuals with elevated PAI-1 levels
- Dietary intervention approach to PAI-1 inhibition with favorable safety profile
- Potential adjunct therapy for cardiovascular disease prevention
