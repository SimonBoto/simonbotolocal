# B12 and Exercise Performance

> B12 deficiency impairs energy metabolism, potentially affecting exercise performance through reduced ATP production from amino acids and odd-chain fatty acids.

## Mechanisms of Impairment

### Amino Acid Oxidation Block
- Isoleucine, methionine, threonine, valine not fully oxidized
- Reduced energy from protein catabolism
- Significant during exercise

### Odd-Chain Fatty Acid Block
- Cannot extract energy from dairy fats
- Minor contribution but significant

### Mitochondrial Dysfunction
- Methylmalonic acid accumulation (toxic)
- Impaired Krebs cycle anaplerosis
- Reduced oxidative capacity

## Clinical Observations

### Deficiency Symptoms
- Fatigue
- Weakness
- Reduced exercise tolerance
- Shortness of breath (anemia)

### Improvement with Repletion
- Increased energy
- Better exercise tolerance
- Resolution of fatigue

## Athletic Considerations

### Increased Needs
- High protein intake
- Increased metabolic turnover
- Potential marginal deficiency

### Supplementation
- Ensure adequacy
- Monitor if symptoms present
- B-complex often beneficial
