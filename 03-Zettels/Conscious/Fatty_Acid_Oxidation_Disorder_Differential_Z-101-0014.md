## Core Claim
**Fatty acid oxidation disorders (FAODs) present with distinct biochemical patterns that enable differential diagnosis — with carnitine deficiency showing elevated long-chain acylcarnitines and dicarboxylic acids, MCAD deficiency showing characteristic C8-C10 acylcarnitine elevation, and insulin resistance showing isolated dicarboxylic acid elevation without significant acylcarnitine abnormalities — allowing precise identification of the specific metabolic block through combined urine organic acid and plasma acylcarnitine profiling.**

## Evidence

### Disorder Comparison
| Disorder | Defect | Key Markers | Clinical Presentation |
|----------|--------|-------------|----------------------|
| **Carnitine deficiency** | Transport (CPT1/CPT2) | ↑ Long-chain acylcarnitines, ↑ dicarboxylic acids | Hypoketotic hypoglycemia, cardiomyopathy |
| **MCAD deficiency** | Medium-chain β-oxidation | ↑ C8-C10 acylcarnitines, ↑ dicarboxylic acids | Crisis with fasting, hypoglycemia |
| **VLCAD deficiency** | Very long-chain β-oxidation | ↑ C14-C18 acylcarnitines | Cardiomyopathy, hepatomegaly |
| **Insulin resistance** | Mitochondrial dysfunction | ↑ Dicarboxylic acids only | Gradual onset, metabolic syndrome |

### Diagnostic Markers
| Marker | Carnitine Def | MCAD Def | IR |
|--------|--------------|----------|-----|
| **Plasma free carnitine** | ↓↓ Low | Normal | Normal |
| **Long-chain acylcarnitines** | ↑↑ Elevated | Normal | Normal |
| **C8-C10 acylcarnitines** | Normal | ↑↑ Elevated | Normal |
| **Urinary adipate** | ↑↑ Elevated | ↑↑ Elevated | ↑ Elevated |
| **Ketones (fasting)** | ↓ Low | ↓ Low | Variable |

### Clinical Clues
| Feature | Suggests |
|---------|----------|
| **Hypoketotic hypoglycemia** | Carnitine deficiency, MCAD |
| **Cardiomyopathy** | Carnitine deficiency, VLCAD |
| **Metabolic crisis with fasting** | MCAD deficiency |
| **Gradual metabolic decline** | Insulin resistance |
| **Response to carnitine** | Carnitine deficiency |

## Clinical Implication
- **Pattern recognition:** Combined markers identify specific defect
- **Newborn screening:** Acylcarnitine profile detects many FAODs
- **Adult presentation:** May be milder, exercise-induced
- **Treatment varies:** By specific disorder
- **METHAP relevance:** Distinguishing IR from primary FAODs is critical for appropriate intervention
