# Cholesterol to Steroid Hormone Synthesis

## Core Concept

**Steroid hormones** are synthesized from cholesterol through a series of enzymatic modifications in the  and endoplasmic reticulum. The pathway branches to produce glucocorticoids (cortisol), mineralocorticoids (aldosterone), androgens (testosterone, DHEA), and estrogens (estradiol) — each serving distinct physiological functions.

---

## Common Starting Point

### Cholesterol → Pregnenolone (Rate-Limiting)
```
Cholesterol → Pregnenolone
```
| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Enzyme** | CYP11A1 (cholesterol side-chain cleavage enzyme) |
| **Location** | Mitochondria (inner membrane) |
| **Cofactors** | NADPH, oxygen |
| **Products** | Pregnenolone (C21) + Isocaproaldehyde |
| **Regulation** | Stimulated by ACTH (adrenals), LH (gonads) |

**Key Point:** This is the **committed step** — once pregnenolone formed, hormone synthesis proceeds.

---

## Glucocorticoids (Cortisol)

### Pathway: Cholesterol → Cortisol
```
Cholesterol → Pregnenolone → 17-OH-Pregnenolone → 17-OH-Progesterone →
11-Deoxycortisol → Cortisol
```

| Step | Enzyme | Location | Regulation |
|------|--------|----------|------------|
| Cholesterol → Pregnenolone | CYP11A1 | Mitochondria | ACTH |
| Pregnenolone → 17-OH-Pregnenolone | CYP17A1 | ER | ACTH |
| 17-OH-Pregnenolone → 17-OH-Progesterone | 3β-HSD | ER | ACTH |
| 17-OH-Progesterone → 11-Deoxycortisol | CYP21A2 | ER | ACTH |
| 11-Deoxycortisol → Cortisol | CYP11B1 | Mitochondria | ACTH |

### Functions of Cortisol
| System | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| **** | Gluconeogenesis, protein breakdown, lipolysis |
| **Immune** | Anti-inflammatory, immunosuppressive |
| **Cardiovascular** | Vascular tone, catecholamine sensitivity |
| **Stress** | "Fight or flight" preparation |

### Clinical: Cushing's vs Addison's
- **Cushing's:** Excess cortisol → Weight gain, , immunosuppression
- **Addison's:** Cortisol deficiency → Fatigue, hypotension, crisis

---

## Mineralocorticoids (Aldosterone)

### Pathway: Cholesterol → Aldosterone
```
Cholesterol → Pregnenolone → Progesterone → 
11-Deoxycorticosterone → Corticosterone → Aldosterone
```

**Key Features:**
- **Bypasses 17-hydroxylation** (no androgen intermediate)
- **Aldosterone synthase** (CYP11B2) — unique to zona glomerulosa

### Regulation
| Factor | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| **Angiotensin II** | Stimulates (primary) |
| **Potassium** | Stimulates |
| **ACTH** | Acute stimulation |
| **Sodium** | Inhibits (negative feedback) |

### Functions
- **Sodium reabsorption** (kidney distal tubule)
- **Potassium excretion**
- **Blood pressure maintenance**
- **Volume regulation**

---

## Androgens (Male Hormones)

### Pathway: Cholesterol → Testosterone
```
Cholesterol → Pregnenolone → 17-OH-Pregnenolone → DHEA → 
Androstenedione → Testosterone
```

**Sites of Synthesis:**
| Tissue | Primary Androgen | Function |
|--------|------------------|----------|
| **Testes (Leydig cells)** | Testosterone | Male reproduction, anabolism |
| **Adrenal cortex** | DHEA, DHEA-S | Weak androgens, precursor |
| **Ovaries** | Testosterone (small) | Female libido, precursor |

### Key Androgens
| Hormone | Potency | Source | Notes |
|---------|---------|--------|-------|
| **Testosterone** | High | Testes | Primary male hormone |
| **DHT** | Very high | Peripheral (5α-reductase) | Most potent |
| **DHEA** | Weak | Adrenals | Precursor, supplements popular |
| **Androstenedione** | Weak | Adrenals, gonads | Prohormone |

### Clinical: PCOS and Hypogonadism
- **PCOS:** Elevated androgens in women → Hirsutism, acne, anovulation
- **Hypogonadism:** Low testosterone → Fatigue, low libido, osteoporosis

---

## Estrogens (Female Hormones)

### Pathway: Androgens → Estrogens
```
Testosterone → Estradiol (via aromatase CYP19A1)
Androstenedione → Estrone (via aromatase)
```

**Aromatase (CYP19A1):**
- Converts androgens to estrogens
- Found in ovaries (primary), adipose, brain, bone
- **Adipose aromatization** increases with body fat

### Types of Estrogens
| Estrogen | Potency | Primary Source | Function |
|----------|---------|----------------|----------|
| **Estradiol (E2)** | Highest | Ovaries | Primary reproductive hormone |
| **Estrone (E1)** | Moderate | Adipose (post-menopause) | Menopausal estrogen |
| **Estriol (E3)** | Weak | Pregnancy | Fetal/placental |

### Functions
- **Reproductive:** Uterine lining, breast development
- **Skeletal:** Bone density maintenance
- **Cardiovascular:** Vasodilation, lipid profile
- **Neurological:** Cognitive function, mood

### Clinical: Menopause and HRT
- **Menopause:** Estrogen decline → Hot flashes, osteoporosis, CV risk
- **HRT:** Estradiol replacement (benefits vs risks debated)

---

## Regulation Summary

### By Stimulus
| Stimulus | Target | Hormone Class |
|----------|--------|---------------|
| **ACTH** | Adrenal cortex | Glucocorticoids, adrenal androgens |
| **Angiotensin II** | Adrenal (zona glomerulosa) | Mineralocorticoids |
| **LH** | Testes/Ovaries | Androgens, estrogens, progestogens |
| **FSH** | Ovaries | Estrogens, progestogens |

### Feedback Loops
- **Negative feedback:** Cortisol inhibits ACTH; estrogens inhibit GnRH
- **Positive feedback:** Estrogen surge triggers LH surge (ovulation)
