# Aldose vs Ketose 

## Core Distinction

### Aldose
- **Carbonyl:** Aldehyde group (-CHO)
- **Location:** Terminal carbon (C1)
- **Example:** Glucose

### Ketose
- **Carbonyl:** Ketone group (C=O)
- **Location:** Internal carbon (typically C2)
- **Example:** Fructose

## Structural Consequences

### Ring Formation
| Type | Ring Size | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| **Aldose** | Pyranose (6-membered) | Glucose |
| **Ketose** | Furanose (5-membered) | Fructose |

### Reactivity
- **Aldoses:** More easily oxidized (reducing sugars)
- **Ketoses:** Can isomerize to aldoses under basic conditions

## Classification by Carbon Number

### Aldoses
| Carbons | Name | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| 3 | Aldotriose | Glyceraldehyde |
| 4 | Aldotetrose | Erythrose |
| 5 | Aldopentose | Ribose |
| 6 | Aldohexose | Glucose |

### Ketoses
| Carbons | Name | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| 3 | Ketotriose | Dihydroxyacetone |
| 4 | Ketotetrose | Erythrulose |
| 5 | Ketopentose | Ribulose |
| 6 | Ketohexose | Fructose |

## Metabolic Implications

### Glucose (Aldose)
- Primary energy source
- Regulated uptake (-dependent)
- Universal cellular utilization

### Fructose (Ketose)
- Liver-centric 
- Unregulated uptake
- Lipogenic pathway
