# Primary vs Secondary Hypogonadism — Laboratory Differentiation

## Core Concept

Hypogonadism (testosterone deficiency) is classified by the level of dysfunction: **primary** (testicular failure) or **secondary** (pituitary/hypothalamic failure). The distinction is critical for diagnosis, treatment, and prognosis.

## Laboratory Patterns

### Primary Hypogonadism (Hypergonadotropic)

**Pathophysiology:**
- Testes fail to produce testosterone
- No negative feedback
- Pituitary compensates by increasing LH/FSH

**Laboratory Pattern:**
| Parameter | Finding |
|-----------|---------|
| **Testosterone** | Low (<300 ng/dL) |
| **LH** | Elevated |
| **FSH** | Elevated |
| **Ratio** | High LH:Testosterone |

**Causes:**
- **Genetic:** Klinefelter syndrome (47,XXY), Kallmann syndrome (anosmia + hypogonadism)
- **Acquired:** Chemotherapy/radiation, testicular trauma, torsion, orchitis (mumps)
- **Autoimmune:** Autoimmune orchitis
- **Drugs:** Ketoconazole, spironolactone, alcohol abuse
- **Systemic:** Hemochromatosis, cirrhosis, chronic kidney disease

### Secondary Hypogonadism (Hypogonadotropic)

**Pathophysiology:**
- Pituitary or hypothalamus fails to stimulate testes
- Low GnRH → low LH/FSH → low testosterone

**Laboratory Pattern:**
| Parameter | Finding |
|-----------|---------|
| **Testosterone** | Low (<300 ng/dL) |
| **LH** | Low or inappropriately normal |
| **FSH** | Low or inappropriately normal |
| **Prolactin** | May be elevated (prolactinoma) |

**Causes:**
- **Pituitary tumors:** Prolactinoma (most common), non-functioning adenomas
- **Hypothalamic:** Kallmann syndrome, idiopathic hypogonadotropic hypogonadism
- **Infiltrative:** Hemochromatosis, sarcoidosis, histiocytosis
- **Trauma/Surgery:** Pituitary apoplexy, surgery
- **Functional:** Obesity, sleep apnea, stress, mal, anabolic steroid use
- **Drugs:** Opioids, glucocorticoids, GnRH agonists

### Combined (Hypogonadotropic with Testicular Defect)

**Late-Onset Hypogonadism:**
- Age-related decline
- Both central and peripheral defects
- Gradual onset
- Often comorbid with obesity, 

##  Clues

### Suggesting Primary
- **History:** Cryptorchidism, testicular trauma, chemotherapy, mumps orchitis
- **Physical:** Small, firm testes; gynecomastia (Klinefelter); anosmia (Kallmann)
- **Lab:** High FSH/LH

### Suggesting Secondary
- **History:** Headaches, visual field defects (tumor); obesity; opioid use
- **Physical:** Normal or small testes; visual field defects; galactorrhea (hyperprolactinemia)
- **Lab:** Low/normal FSH/LH; elevated prolactin

## Diagnostic Algorithm

```
Low Testosterone (<300 ng/dL, morning)
    ↓
Measure LH and FSH
    ↓
┌──────────────────────┬──────────────────────┐
↓                      ↓                      ↓
LH/FSH Elevated      LH/FSH Low/Normal      LH/FSH Discordant
    ↓                      ↓                      ↓
PRIMARY              SECONDARY             Complex/Mixed
    ↓                      ↓                      ↓
- Karyotype           - Prolactin           - Aging
  (if young)          - MRI pituitary       - Chronic illness
- Ferritin            - Iron studies
- History             - Drug review
```

## Additional Testing

### For Primary
- **Karyotype:** If young, gynecomastia (rule out Klinefelter)
- **Ferritin:** Hemochromatosis screening
- **Testicular ultrasound:** Masses, atrophy
- **Semen analysis:** Fertility assessment

### For Secondary
- **Prolactin:** Rule out prolactinoma
- **MRI pituitary:** If elevated prolactin or symptoms of mass
- **Iron studies:** Hemochromatosis
- **Other pituitary axes:** TSH, cortisol, IGF-1

## Treatment Implications

### Primary
- **Testosterone replacement:** Primary treatment
- **Fertility:** May require assisted reproduction (testicular sperm extraction)
- **Monitoring:** Hematocrit, PSA, lipids

### Secondary
- **Address underlying cause:** Treat prolactinoma, stop offending drugs, weight loss
- **Testosterone replacement:** If persistent
- **Fertility:** Can often be restored with hCG or pulsatile GnRH
- **Pulsatile GnRH:** For hypothalamic causes (specialist)

## Special Considerations

### Fertility Desire
- **Primary:** Difficult to restore fertility, may need ART
- **Secondary:** Fertility often preserved with appropriate therapy
- **Testosterone replacement:** Suppresses spermatogenesis (contraceptive effect)

### Age Considerations
- **Young men:** Preserve fertility if possible
- **Older men:** Balance benefits vs risks
- **Late-onset:** Variable presentation, often milder

## Summary Table

| Feature | Primary | Secondary |
|---------|---------|-----------|
| **Site** | Testes | Pituitary/Hypothalamus |
| **LH/FSH** | High | Low/Normal |
| **Testosterone** | Low | Low |
| **Common causes** | Klinefelter, chemotherapy, trauma | Prolactinoma, obesity, opioids |
| **Fertility** | Often impaired | Often preserved |
| **Karyotype** | Sometimes indicated | Not indicated |
| **MRI** | Not indicated | If pituitary disease suspected |
