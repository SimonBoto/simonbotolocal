# Common Ketoses and Their Roles

## Core Claim
**Ketoses are ketone-containing sugars with the carbonyl at C2 — ketohexoses (fructose, psicose/allulose, sorbose, tagatose) serve as sweeteners and metabolic substrates, while ketopentoses (ribulose, xylulose) are critical intermediates in photosynthesis and the pentose phosphate pathway** — fructose dominates dietary intake while rare sugars like allulose offer low-calorie alternatives.

## Evidence

### Ketohexoses (6-Carbon)
| Ketose | Role | Source | Properties |
|--------|------|--------|------------|
| **Fructose** | Fruit sugar, sweetener, lipogenic | Fruits, honey, HFCS | 1.7× sweeter than glucose |
| **Psicose (Allulose)** | Low-calorie sweetener | Wheat, figs (trace) | ~70% sweetness, minimal calories |
| **Sorbose** | Vitamin C precursor | Industrial production | Ascorbic acid synthesis |
| **Tagatose** | Low glycemic sweetener | Dairy | ~90% sweetness, low calorie |

### Ketopentoses (5-Carbon)
| Ketose | Role | Source | Function |
|--------|------|--------|----------|
| **Ribulose** | Calvin cycle intermediate | Photosynthesis | CO₂ fixation |
| **Xylulose** | PPP intermediate | PPP non-oxidative | Pentose interconversion |

### Structural Features
- **All have ketone at C2** (internal carbonyl)
- **Form furanose rings** (5-membered)
- **More stable than aldoses** (less reactive)

## Clinical Implication
- **Fructose:** Linked to metabolic syndrome when consumed as added sugar
- **Allulose:** Emerging low-calorie alternative with minimal metabolic impact
- **Ribulose-1,5-bisphosphate:** Central to carbon fixation in all photosynthetic organisms
- **METHAP relevance:** Ketose chemistry enables alternative sweeteners; understanding metabolic differences informs dietary recommendations
