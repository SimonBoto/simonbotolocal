# Magnesium in Neuromuscular Function and Neurotransmission

> Magnesium modulates neurotransmission and neuromuscular excitability through NMDA receptor blockade, presynaptic acetylcholine regulation, and antagonism of calcium-dependent muscle contraction.

## Neurotransmission

### NMDA Receptor Modulation
**Physiological Blockade:**
- Mg²⁺ sits in NMDA receptor channel at resting potential
- Blocks ion flow unless depolarization occurs (voltage-dependent block)
- **Function:** Prevents excitotoxicity, regulates synaptic plasticity

**Pathological Relevance:**
- **Hypomagnesemia:** Reduced blockade → NMDA hyperactivation → seizures, excitotoxicity
- **Therapeutic potential:** Mg for neuroprotection (stroke, traumatic brain injury)

### Acetylcholine Release
- **Presynaptic:** Mg²⁺ regulates vesicle fusion and neurotransmitter release
- **Neuromuscular junction:** Mg affects acetylcholine quantal release
- **Clinical:** Hypomagnesemia → increased ACh release → hyperexcitability

### GABAergic Modulation
- Enhances GABA receptor function (inhibitory neurotransmission)
- Contributes to anti-anxiety, anti-convulsant effects of Mg

## Neuromuscular Function

### Excitation-Contraction Coupling
**Sequence:**
1. Action potential → voltage-gated Ca²⁺ channels open
2. Ca²⁺ influx triggers sarcoplasmic reticulum Ca²⁺ release
3. Muscle contraction
4. **Mg role:** Promotes Ca²⁺ reuptake by SERCA pumps (Mg-dependent)
5. Muscle relaxation

**Hypomagnesemia Consequence:**
- Impaired Ca²⁺ reuptake → sustained contraction → tetany
- Increased neuromuscular excitability → tremor, fasciculations

### Cardiac Muscle
**Rhythm Regulation:**
- **SA node:** Mg affects pacemaker activity
- **AV node:** Mg slows conduction (protective)
- **Ventricular:** Mg stabilizes membrane, prevents arrhythmias

**Anti-arrhythmic Mechanism:**
- Class IV effect (Ca²⁺ channel blockade)
- Stabilizes cardiac myocyte membrane
- Effective for torsades de pointes, digoxin toxicity

## Clinical Applications

### Therapeutic Uses of Magnesium
| Condition | Dosing | Mechanism |
|-----------|--------|-----------|
| **Eclampsia/preeclampsia** | 4-6g IV, then 1-2g/hr | CNS depression, vasodilation |
| **Preterm labor** | 4-6g IV loading | Uterine smooth muscle relaxation |
| **Torsades de pointes** | 1-2g IV over 15 min | Cardiac membrane stabilization |
| **Severe asthma** | 2g IV over 20 min | Bronchial smooth muscle relaxation |
| **Arrhythmias (digoxin toxicity)** | 1-2g IV | Competitive antagonism with Ca²⁺ |

### Deficiency Manifestations
- **Neurological:** Tremor, fasciculations, seizures, vertigo
- **Muscular:** Weakness, tetany, cramps
- **Psychiatric:** Anxiety, irritability, depression

## METHAP Context

**Patient Symptoms:**
- Mg deficiency common in MetS patients (poor diet, insulin resistance)
- Symptoms (fatigue, weakness) may be attributed to MetS itself
- Correction may improve exercise tolerance, compliance with lifestyle interventions

**Monitoring:**
- Serum Mg (poor marker of total body status)
- RBC Mg (better indicator)
- Clinical: Watch for tetany, arrhythmias in high-dose D protocols
