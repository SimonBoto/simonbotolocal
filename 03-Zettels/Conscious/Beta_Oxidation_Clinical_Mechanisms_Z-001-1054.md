# Beta Oxidation Clinical Mechanisms

## Core Claim
**Beta-oxidation is the mitochondrial process of breaking down fatty acids into acetyl-CoA units — generating NADH and FADH₂ for the electron transport chain — with clinical dysfunction manifesting as impaired energy production, hypoketotic hypoglycemia, and characteristic OAT markers (elevated adipate, suberate).**

## Evidence

### Beta-Oxidation Steps
| Step | Reaction | Cofactor | Product |
|------|----------|----------|---------|
| 1 | Activation (cytoplasm) | ATP, CoA | Fatty acyl-CoA |
| 2 | Carnitine shuttle | Carnitine | Transport into mitochondria |
| 3 | Dehydrogenation | FAD → FADH₂ | Trans-enoyl-CoA |
| 4 | Hydration | H₂O | L-3-hydroxyacyl-CoA |
| 5 | Dehydrogenation | NAD⁺ → NADH | 3-ketoacyl-CoA |
| 6 | Thiolysis | CoA | Acetyl-CoA + shortened fatty acid |

### Energy Yield (Palmitic Acid C16:0)
| Product | Quantity | ATP Equivalent |
|---------|----------|----------------|
| Acetyl-CoA | 8 | 80 ATP (via TCA) |
| NADH | 7 | 17.5 ATP |
| FADH₂ | 7 | 10.5 ATP |
| **Net** | — | **~106 ATP** |

### Clinical Disorders
| Disorder | Defect | Presentation |
|----------|--------|--------------|
| **MCAD deficiency** | Medium-chain acyl-CoA DH | Hypoketotic hypoglycemia |
| **VLCAD deficiency** | Very long-chain acyl-CoA DH | Cardiomyopathy, myopathy |
| **CPT-I deficiency** | Carnitine palmitoyltransferase | Hepatic encephalopathy |
| **CPT-II deficiency** | Carnitine palmitoyltransferase II | Muscle weakness, myoglobinuria |

### OAT Markers
| Marker | Indicates | Clinical Action |
|--------|-----------|-----------------|
| **Adipate (C6)** | Medium-chain oxidation block | Check MCAD |
| **Suberate (C8)** | Long-chain oxidation impairment | CPT assessment |
| **Ethylmalonate** | Short-chain oxidation issue | Multiple carboxylase deficiency |

### Beta-Oxidation vs. Ketogenesis
| State | Primary Fuel | Ketone Production |
|-------|--------------|-------------------|
| **Fed** | Glucose | Minimal |
| **Fasting** | Fatty acids | Moderate |
| **Ketogenic diet** | Fatty acids | High |
| **Diabetic ketoacidosis** | Fatty acids | Excessive |

## Clinical Implication
- **Fatigue etiology:** Beta-oxidation defects cause exercise intolerance
- **Hypoglycemia:** Inability to switch to fat burning during fasting
- **Cardiac function:** Heart highly dependent on fatty acid oxidation
- **OAT interpretation:** Adipate/suberate elevations indicate FAO dysfunction
- **METHAP relevance:** Fatty acid oxidation capacity is key to metabolic flexibility; impaired FAO contributes to insulin resistance and metabolic syndrome
