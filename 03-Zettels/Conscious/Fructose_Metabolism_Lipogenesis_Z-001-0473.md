# Fructose  and Lipogenesis

## The Liver-Centric Pathway
**~90% of dietary fructose** metabolized by liver.

## Bypasses Key Regulation
Unlike glucose, fructose:
- **Bypasses PFK-1** (no rate-limiting step)
- **No  regulation** of uptake
- **Rapid phosphorylation** → high flux

## Metabolic Pathway
```
Fructose → Fructose-1-phosphate (fructokinase)
        → DHAP + Glyceraldehyde (aldolase B)
        → Glycolysis/Gluconeogenesis/Lipogenesis
```

## Lipogenic Consequences

### High Flux → Fat Synthesis
1. Excess acetyl-CoA → fatty acids
2. DHAP → glycerol-3-phosphate (triglyceride backbone)
3. VLDL secretion → elevated blood lipids

### Metabolic Effects
| Parameter | Effect |
|-----------|--------|
| Triglycerides | ↑↑ |
| VLDL | ↑ |
| Uric acid | ↑ (AMP degradation) |
| Insulin resistance | ↑ |
| Satiety signals | ↓ (no GLP-1/GIP stimulation) |

## Dietary Sources
- **Sucrose:** 50% fructose
- **HFCS:** 42-55% fructose
- **Fruits:** Varying amounts (with fiber)
- **Honey:** ~40% fructose

## Clinical Relevance

### Non-Alcoholic Fatty Liver Disease (NAFLD)
- Strong association with high fructose intake
- First hit: Lipid accumulation
- Second hit:  (NASH)

### Metabolic Syndrome
Fructose consumption linked to:
- Central obesity
- Insulin resistance
- Hypertension (uric acid)
- Dyslipidemia
