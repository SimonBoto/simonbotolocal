# Vitamin D Supplementation Guidelines

## Clinical Context
Vitamin D supplementation is foundational for osteoporosis treatment and bone health. Adequate vitamin D status is mandatory for all osteoporosis therapies to be effective.

## Target Levels
| Status | 25(OH)D Level | Clinical Significance |
|--------|----------------|------------------------|
| **Deficient** | <20 ng/mL | Impaired calcium absorption, secondary hyperparathyroidism |
| **Insufficient** | 20–30 ng/mL | Suboptimal bone health, increased fracture risk |
| **Sufficient** | 30–50 ng/mL | Optimal calcium absorption, bone health |
| **Toxic** | >100 ng/mL | Hypercalcemia, renal calcification |

## Daily Requirements
- **General population:** 600–800 IU/day
- **Osteoporosis patients:** 800–2000 IU/day (titrate to target level)
- **Sun-deficient:** 2000–4000 IU/day (monitor levels)

## Monitoring
- **Baseline:** 25(OH)D level, serum calcium, PTH
- **After 3 months:** 25(OH)D level (if supplementing >2000 IU/day)
- **Annual:** All parameters

## Safety Thresholds
- **Vitamin D safe upper limit:** 4000 IU/day
- **Risk of excess:** Hypercalcemia, renal stones

## Integration Notes
- Assessment of vitamin D status is mandatory before starting any osteoporosis therapy
- Supplementation improves treatment efficacy and reduces fracture risk independently
- Individualized dosing based on baseline status and response
