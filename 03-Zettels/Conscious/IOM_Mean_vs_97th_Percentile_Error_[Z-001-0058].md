# IOM Mean vs 97.5th Percentile Statistical Error

The IOM (Institute of Medicine, now National Academy of Medicine) made a fundamental statistical error in calculating vitamin D RDA: they used the mean (average) intake required to achieve target serum levels instead of the 97.5th percentile. This meant the RDA was designed for 50% of population coverage, not the stated 97.5%.

**The error:**
- **Target:** Ensure 97.5% of population reaches 20 ng/mL serum 25(OH)D
- **IOM method:** Used mean requirement + 2 SD
- **Correct method:** Should account for variability in individual response
- **Result:** Mean-based RDA covers ~50%, not 97.5%

**Discovered by:** Veugelers & Ekwaru (University of Alberta), 2014

**Implication:** Basic statistical error propagated into global public health policy affecting billions.
