## Central Mechanism: Insulin Resistance

**Definition:** Impaired cellular response to insulin, requiring compensatory hyperinsulinemia to maintain euglycemia.

**Tissue-Specific Manifestations:**
- **Muscle:** Reduced glucose uptake (GLUT4 translocation impaired)
- **Adipose:** Increased lipolysis, elevated FFA → ectopic fat deposition
- **Liver:** Increased gluconeogenesis, VLDL overproduction

## Contributing Pathways

### 1. Adipose Tissue Dysfunction
**Visceral adiposity as driver:**
- Pro-inflammatory adipokine secretion (↑TNF-α, IL-6, resistin)
- Reduced protective adiponectin
- Macrophage infiltration and chronic inflammation
- Impaired adipogenesis, adipocyte hypertrophy

### 2. Chronic Low-Grade Inflammation
- **Elevated CRP:** Marker and mediator of CV risk
- **Cytokine cascade:** TNF-α → insulin receptor substrate inhibition
- **JNK pathway activation:** Serine phosphorylation of IRS-1
- **IκB kinase β (IKKβ):** NF-κB activation, inflammatory gene expression

### 3. Endothelial Dysfunction
- Reduced nitric oxide bioavailability
- Increased endothelin-1, vasoconstriction
- Increased vascular permeability
- Pro-thrombotic state

### 4. Oxidative Stress
- Mitochondrial dysfunction
- Increased reactive oxygen species (ROS)
- Lipid peroxidation
- DNA damage, cellular dysfunction

### 5. Dyslipidemia
- **Overproduction:** VLDL from liver (insulin fails to suppress lipolysis)
- **Small dense LDL:** More atherogenic particles
- **HDL reduction:** Impaired reverse cholesterol transport

## Vicious Cycles

```
Insulin Resistance → Hyperinsulinemia → Weight Gain → Adipose Dysfunction → Inflammation → Worsening IR
```

## Magnesium Connection

Magnesium deficiency contributes to:
- Impaired insulin receptor tyrosine kinase activity
- Reduced GLUT4 translocation
- Increased inflammation
- Endothelial dysfunction

**This explains why magnesium supplementation may benefit MetS.**
