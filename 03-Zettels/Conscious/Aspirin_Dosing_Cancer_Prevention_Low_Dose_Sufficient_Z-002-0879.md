## The Evidence

**Low-dose aspirin (75-300mg daily) is sufficient for metastasis prevention.**

### Why Low Dose Works

| Factor | Explanation |
|--------|-------------|
| **Irreversible inhibition** | Single dose permanently inhibits COX-1 in platelets |
| **Platelet lifespan** | Effect lasts 7-10 days (platelet turnover) |
| **High sensitivity** | Platelet COX-1 highly sensitive to aspirin |
| **Specific mechanism** | Only need to block TXA2, not systemic prostaglandins |

## Dosing Comparison

| Dose | Use | Cancer Prevention? |
|------|-----|-------------------|
| 75-100mg/day | Cardiovascular | ✅ Yes, sufficient |
| 300mg/day | Pain/Inflammation | ✅ Yes, sufficient |
| 325mg/day | Cardiovascular (US) | ✅ Yes, sufficient |
| >500mg/day | Pain/Inflammation | ⚠️ Unnecessary, higher bleeding risk |

## Risk-Benefit

### Benefits (Low Dose)
- 36% reduction in metastasis
- 51% reduction in cancer death
- Low cost, high availability

### Risks (All Doses)
- GI bleeding (dose-dependent)
- Hemorrhagic stroke (rare)
- **Lower doses = lower bleeding risk**

## Clinical Recommendation

> **75-100mg daily** appears optimal for cancer prevention based on current evidence.
