# Aconitase Mechanism and Iron-Sulfur Cluster

## Mechanism

**Aconitase catalyzes the isomerization of citrate to isocitrate via cis-aconitate intermediate in the Krebs cycle — utilizing a catalytic [4Fe-4S] iron-sulfur cluster that binds substrate and facilitates dehydration-rehydration — with the enzyme serving dual functions as both a metabolic catalyst and, when the Fe-S cluster is disassembled, as the iron regulatory protein IRP1.**

## Evidence

### Reaction Mechanism
| Step | Reaction | Intermediate |
|------|----------|--------------|
| **1** | Citrate binding | Substrate-Fe-S complex |
| **2** | Dehydration | cis-Aconitate + H₂O |
| **3** | Rehydration | Isocitrate formation |
| **4** | Product release | Isocitrate + enzyme |

**Net Reaction:**
```
Citrate ⇌ cis-Aconitate ⇌ Isocitrate
```

### Iron-Sulfur Cluster [4Fe-4S]
| Feature | Structure/Function |
|---------|-------------------|
| **Composition** | 4 iron + 4 sulfur atoms |
| **Coordination** | Cysteine residues (3 Cys in aconitase) |
| **Location** | Active site cavity |
| **Function** | Substrate binding, catalysis, electron transfer |
| **Vulnerability** | Sensitive to ROS, oxidative damage |

### Dual Function: Aconitase ↔ IRP1
| State | Fe-S Cluster | Function | Activity |
|-------|--------------|----------|----------|
| **Aconitase** | [4Fe-4S] intact | Citrate → Isocitrate | Enzymatic |
| **IRP1** | [4Fe-4S] disassembled | mRNA binding (IRE) | Regulatory |

### Clinical Significance
| Condition | Effect on Aconitase | Marker Value |
|-----------|---------------------|--------------|
| **Oxidative stress** | Fe-S cluster damage | Early indicator |
| **Iron overload** | Favors aconitase form | ↑ Catalytic activity |
| **Iron deficiency** | Favors IRP1 form | ↑ Iron uptake |
| **Mitochondrial dysfunction** | ↓ Activity | Metabolic impairment |

## Clinical Implication
- **Metabolic hub:** Critical Krebs cycle enzyme
- **Oxidative stress sensor:** Fe-S cluster damage indicates ROS
- **Iron-energy link:** Connects iron status to ATP production
- **Dual functionality:** Same protein, different roles based on Fe-S
- **METHAP relevance:** Aconitase activity reflects mitochondrial health and oxidative stress status
