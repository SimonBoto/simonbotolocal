## Core Claim
**Three ketone bodies are produced during ketogenesis: acetoacetate (the first synthesized, a true β-keto acid), beta-hydroxybutyrate (BHB, the major circulating form at 70-80%, actually a hydroxy acid), and acetone (a spontaneous decarboxylation product, ~2%)** — AcAc and BHB are energy-yielding; acetone is volatile and eliminated via breath/urine.

## Evidence

### 1. Acetoacetate (AcAc)
| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Structure** | CH₃-CO-CH₂-COOH (true ketone) |
| **Synthesis** | First ketone body formed |
| **Fates** | Converted to BHB, or → acetone + CO₂ |
| **Energy yield** | ~4 ATP (converted to 2 acetyl-CoA) |

### 2. Beta-Hydroxybutyrate (BHB)
| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Structure** | CH₃-CHOH-CH₂-COOH (hydroxy acid, NOT ketone) |
| **Circulation** | 70-80% of ketones during ketosis |
| **Formation** | AcAc + NADH → BHB (β-OHBDH) |
| **Energy yield** | ~4.5 ATP (most reduced form) |

### 3. Acetone
| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Structure** | CH₃-CO-CH₃ (volatile ketone) |
| **Formation** | Spontaneous decarboxylation of AcAc |
| **Abundance** | ~2% of ketone bodies |
| **Elimination** | Exhaled (fruity breath), urine |
| **Energy yield** | None (not metabolized) |

### Interconversion
```
Acetoacetate ↔ BHB (β-OHBDH, reversible, NADH/NAD⁺ dependent)
     ↓
Acetone + CO₂ (spontaneous, irreversible)
```

### Ratios in Different States
| State | AcAc : BHB | Total Ketones |
|-------|------------|---------------|
| Fed | Minimal | <0.2 mM |
| Fasting 24h | 1:3 | 1-3 mM |
| Keto diet | 1:4 to 1:6 | 1-5 mM |
| DKA | 1:1 to 1:2 | >10 mM |

## Clinical Implication
- **BHB preferred fuel:** More reduced, higher energy yield
- **AcAc → acetone:** Explains "fruity breath" in ketosis
- **Ketone measurements:** BHB most stable; AcAc variable (urine strips)
- **METHAP relevance:** Understanding ketone types informs monitoring strategies; BHB is the primary therapeutic target
