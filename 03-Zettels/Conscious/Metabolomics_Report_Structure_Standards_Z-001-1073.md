#  Report Structure Standards

## Overview
Standardized formats for live and email metabolomics reports ensuring clarity and actionability.

## Live Report Format (In-Person/Video)

### 1. Opening (2 minutes)
- **Greeting** — Personal connection
- **Summary** — "Your  is showing us [key insight]"
- **Preview** — "I'll walk you through what we found and what to do"

### 2. The Story (5 minutes)
- **Visual** — Show the metabolic map
- **Key findings** — 3-5 most important markers
- **** — Why this is happening
- **Connection** — Link to their symptoms

### 3. The Plan (5 minutes)
- **Priority actions** — Top 3-5 interventions
- **Timeline** — When to expect changes
- **Next steps** — Follow-up schedule

### 4. Closing (3 minutes)
- **Recap** — Key takeaways
- **Questions** — Invite clarification
- **Support** — How to reach you

## Email Report Format

### Subject Line
> "Your Metabolomics Results + Personalized Action Plan"

### Structure

**1. Executive Summary**
> "Your test reveals [primary finding]. This explains [symptoms]."

**2. Key Findings**
- Bullet list of 5-7 most significant markers
- Highlighted ranges (normal vs. elevated)
- Brief explanation of each

**3. What This Means**
> "These patterns suggest [interpretation]. This is why you're experiencing [symptoms]."

**4. Your Personalized Protocol**
| Priority | Action | Timeline |
|----------|--------|----------|
| 1 | [Specific supplement/diet change] | Start immediately |
| 2 | [Next intervention] | Week 2 |
| 3 | [Third intervention] | Week 4 |

**5. Expected Outcomes**
> "Most patients notice [specific improvement] within [timeframe]."

**6. Follow-Up Plan**
- Next appointment date
- When to repeat testing
- How to contact with questions

## Visual Standards

### Color Coding
| Color | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **Green** | Optimal |
| **Yellow** | Mild elevation (watch) |
| **Orange** | Moderate (intervene) |
| **Red** | Significant (priority) |

### Typography
- **Headers:** Clear, bold
- **Numbers:** Large, easy to read
- **Whitespace:** Generous, not cramped

## Quality Checklist
- [ ] Patient's name included
- [ ] Date of test and report
- [ ] Summary on first page
- [ ] Clear action items
- [ ] Timeline specified
- [ ] Contact information
- [ ] Follow-up scheduled
