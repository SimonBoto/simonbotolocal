# IRB Escalation Pathways

When standard follow-up fails, structured escalation protects your interests while preserving professional relationships. Escalation should be proportional and documented.

## Escalation Ladder

### Level 1: Administrative Contact
**When:** 2x normal response time  
**Who:** Committee administrator or coordinator  
**Method:** Email  
**Goal:** Confirm receipt, identify bottleneck

### Level 2: Committee Chair
**When:** 3x normal response time  
**Who:** Ethics committee chair  
**Method:** Email + phone  
**Goal:** Direct attention to pending application

### Level 3: Supervisor Involvement
**When:** 4x normal response time  
**Who:** Supervisor emails chair  
**Method:** Formal email with you copied  
**Goal:** Institutional weight behind request

### Level 4: Department Head
**When:** 5x normal response time  
**Who:** Department head or PhD coordinator  
**Method:** Formal inquiry  
**Goal:** Administrative pressure

### Level 5: Institutional Ombudsman
**When:** 6x+ normal response time  
**Who:** University ombudsman or equivalent  
**Method:** Formal complaint  
**Goal:** Independent investigation

## Escalation Risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| **Alienate committee** | Maintain respectful tone at all levels |
| **Damage supervisor relationship** | Consult supervisor before each escalation |
| **Appear impatient** | Document timeline, show reasonableness |
| **Burn bridges** | Focus on process, not personalities |

## Documentation for Escalation

Before escalating, ensure you have:
- [ ] Submission confirmation
- [ ] All follow-up dates and methods
- [ ] Reference numbers
- [ ] Committee meeting schedules (if public)
- [ ] Institutional policies on review timelines

## When NOT to Escalate

Avoid escalation if:
- Normal response time hasn't elapsed
- You missed requested revisions
- Committee is in quorum call (no meetings scheduled)
- You have unaddressed conflicts of interest

## Application to METHAP

If NKUA delays:
1. Document everything from submission
2. Follow up at 8 weeks (normal ~6 weeks)
3. Escalate to PhD coordinator at 12 weeks
4. Consider ombudsman only if >6 months
