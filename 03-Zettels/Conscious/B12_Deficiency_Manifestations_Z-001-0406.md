# B12 Deficiency Manifestations and Mimics

> Severe vitamin B12 deficiency causes megaloblastic anemia, irreversible neurological damage, and bone marrow changes that can mimic hematological malignancies, requiring careful diagnostic differentiation.

## Hematological Manifestations

### Megaloblastic Anemia
**:** Impaired DNA synthesis in rapidly dividing cells

**Peripheral Blood:**
- **Macrocytic anemia:** MCV >100 fL
- **Hypersegmented neutrophils:** >5 lobes (>5% of cells)
- **Pancytopenia:** In severe cases (anemia, leukopenia, thrombocytopenia)

**Bone Marrow:**
- Hypercellular
- Megaloblastic erythroid precursors
- Giant metamyelocytes
- No clonal plasma cells (vs. multiple myeloma)

## Neurological Manifestations

### Subacute Combined Degeneration
**Location:** Posterior columns, lateral corticospinal tracts

**Symptoms:**
- **Early:** Numbness, paresthesias (glove and stocking)
- **Progressive:** Ataxia, loss of proprioception
- **Advanced:** Spasticity, weakness, paraplegia
- **Cognitive:** Dementia, depression, psychosis

**Mechanism:** Impaired myelin synthesis ( defect)

**Critical:**
- **Irreversible** if untreated >6-12 months
- Folate supplementation can mask anemia but not prevent neuro damage

## Mimics of Multiple Myeloma

### Similarities
- Anemia
- Bone pain
- Bone marrow hypercellularity
- Elevated LDH

### Key Differences
| Feature | B12 Deficiency | Multiple Myeloma |
|---------|---------------|------------------|
| **Plasma cells** | Polyclonal, reactive | Monoclonal, malignant |
| **M-protein** | Absent | Present |
| **Bone lesions** | Diffuse demineralization | Focal lytic lesions |
| **Hypercalcemia** | Rare | Common |
| **Renal failure** | Absent | Common |

## Diagnosis

### Laboratory Markers

**Serum B12:**
- **Low:** <200 pg/mL (deficient)
- **Borderline:** 200-300 pg/mL (insufficient)

**Methylmalonic Acid (MMA):**
- **Elevated:** Specific for B12 deficiency
- More sensitive than serum B12

**Homocysteine:**
- **Elevated:** Non-specific (also folate deficiency)

**Anti-Intrinsic Factor Antibodies:**
- **Present:** Pernicious anemia

### Bone Marrow Biopsy
- Differentiates from MM (no clonal plasma cells)
- Megaloblastic changes

## Treatment

**Parenteral B12:**
- 1000 mcg IM daily × 7 days
- Then weekly × 4 weeks
- Then monthly for life (pernicious anemia)

**Response:**
- Reticulocytosis in 5-7 days
- Hemoglobin rises in 2 months
- Neurological improvement (if treated early)
