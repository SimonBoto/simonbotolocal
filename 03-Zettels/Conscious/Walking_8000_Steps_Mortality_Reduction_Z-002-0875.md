## Core Claim
**8,000 steps 1-2 times per week reduces all-cause mortality risk by over 50% over 10 years** — one of the most powerful health interventions available.

## Key Finding
| Metric | Result |
|--------|--------|
| **Frequency** | 1-2 times per week |
| **Steps** | 8,000 |
| **Mortality reduction** | >50% over 10 years |
| **Men** | 70% reduction |
| **Women** | 50% reduction |

## Dose-Response
Increasing to **3-7 days/week** provides additional risk reduction.

## Clinical Significance
Benefit of **enormous magnitude** — few interventions demonstrate effect sizes of this order.

## Practical Recommendations
- Take stairs whenever possible
- Take 5-minute walking breaks throughout the day
- Walk around the block
- Park farther from home or work
- Gradually increase daily walking

## Key Insight
> "The human body functions better when it moves."

## Clinical Implication
- Walking is one of the **highest-impact health interventions**
- Minimal frequency (1-2x/week) still provides dramatic benefit
- Accessible to virtually everyone regardless of fitness level
