## Core Claim
**Ultra-processed foods and sugar-sweetened beverages are the primary drivers of childhood obesity** — combined with sedentary lifestyles, screen time, sleep disruption, and environmental toxins.

## Driving Factors
| Factor | Contribution |
|--------|--------------|
| **Ultra-processed foods** | Primary driver |
| **Sugar-sweetened beverages** | Major contributor |
| **Sedentary lifestyle** | Reduced energy expenditure |
| **Screen time** | Displaces physical activity |
| **Sleep disruption** | Metabolic dysfunction |
| **Environmental toxins** | Endocrine disruption |

## Mechanism
| Factor | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| **UPF** | Hyper-palatability → overconsumption |
| **Sugar beverages** | Rapid glucose spikes → insulin resistance |
| **Screen time** | Reduced outdoor play → lower activity |
| **Poor sleep** | Hunger hormone dysregulation → overeating |

## The Perfect Storm
- Multiple factors **compound each other**
- Modern environment **obesogenic by design**
- Children especially **vulnerable to marketing and addiction**

## Clinical Implication
- **UPF elimination** and **sugar reduction** = highest impact
- Cannot exercise way out of poor diet
- Need **environmental redesign**, not just individual willpower
