## Core Claim
**The omega (ω) classification system categorizes polyunsaturated fatty acids based on the position of the first double bond from the methyl (omega) end — with omega-3 and omega-6 being essential fatty acids that humans cannot synthesize, serving as precursors to distinct eicosanoid families with opposing inflammatory properties, and the omega-6:omega-3 ratio being a critical determinant of inflammatory status and cardiovascular risk.**

## Evidence

### Omega Classification by Position
| Class | First Double Bond | Key Fatty Acids | Dietary Sources |
|-------|-------------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| **Omega-3** | C3-C4 | ALA, EPA, DHA | Fish, flaxseed, walnuts |
| **Omega-6** | C6-C7 | Linoleic acid, Arachidonic acid | Vegetable oils, nuts, seeds |
| **Omega-7** | C7-C8 | Palmitoleic acid | Macadamia nuts, sea buckthorn |
| **Omega-9** | C9-C10 | Oleic acid | Olive oil, avocados |

### Essential Fatty Acids
| Fatty Acid | Omega Class | Can Synthesize? | Daily Requirement |
|------------|-------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| **Alpha-linolenic acid (ALA)** | ω-3 | No (essential) | ~1-2g |
| **Linoleic acid (LA)** | ω-6 | No (essential) | ~10-15g |
| **EPA/DHA** | ω-3 | From ALA (limited) | ~250-500mg |
| **Arachidonic acid (AA)** | ω-6 | From LA | Not required |

### Omega-6:Omega-3 Ratio
| Era/Diet | Ratio | Health Implications |
|----------|-------|---------------------|
| **Evolutionary/hunter-gatherer** | 1:1 to 2:1 | Optimal inflammatory balance |
| **Traditional Mediterranean** | 4:1 | Cardiovascular protective |
| **Modern Western** | 15:1 to 20:1 | Pro-inflammatory, disease risk |
| **Target for health** | < 4:1 | Reduced inflammation |

### Eicosanoid Production
| Precursor | Pathway | Primary Eicosanoids | Effect |
|-----------|---------|---------------------|--------|
| **EPA (ω-3)** | LOX, COX | Resolvins, protectins, 3-series PG | Anti-inflammatory |
| **Arachidonic acid (ω-6)** | LOX, COX | 2-series PG, 4-series LT | Pro-inflammatory |

## Clinical Implication
- **Essential nutrients:** Must obtain from diet
- **Ratio matters:** More important than absolute amounts
- **Modern imbalance:** Excess ω-6 drives inflammation
- **Therapeutic potential:** ω-3 supplementation reduces inflammation
- **METHAP relevance:** ω-3 status and ω-6:ω-3 ratio are metabolic health indicators
