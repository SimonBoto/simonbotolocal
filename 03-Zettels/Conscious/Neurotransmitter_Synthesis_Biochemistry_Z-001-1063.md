## Core Claim
**Monoamine neurotransmitters — dopamine, serotonin, norepinephrine, and epinephrine — are synthesized from dietary amino acid precursors (tyrosine and tryptophan) through enzymatic pathways requiring specific cofactors (iron, BH4, B6), while GABA derives from glutamate, with synthesis rates dependent on precursor availability, enzyme activity, and cofactor status.**

## Evidence

### Catecholamine Synthesis Pathway
```
Phenylalanine ──PAH──→ Tyrosine ──TH──→ L-DOPA ──AADC──→ Dopamine
                                                            ↓
                                                      ──DBH──→ Norepinephrine
                                                            ↓
                                                      ──PNMT──→ Epinephrine
```

### Serotonin Synthesis Pathway
```
Tryptophan ──TPH──→ 5-HTP ──AADC──→ Serotonin ──MAO──→ 5-HIAA (metabolite)
                          ↓
                   Melatonin (in pineal)
```

### GABA Synthesis
```
Glutamate ──GAD──→ GABA ──GABA-T──→ Succinic semialdehyde → Krebs cycle
```

### Cofactor Requirements
| Enzyme | Cofactor | Function |
|--------|----------|----------|
| **PAH** | BH4, Fe²⁺ | Phenylalanine → Tyrosine |
| **TH** | BH4, Fe²⁺ | Tyrosine → L-DOPA |
| **AADC** | B6 (PLP) | Decarboxylation (DOPA, 5-HTP) |
| **DBH** | Cu²⁺, Vitamin C | Dopamine → Norepinephrine |
| **TPH** | BH4, Fe²⁺ | Tryptophan → 5-HTP |
| **GAD** | B6 (PLP) | Glutamate → GABA |

### Precursor Competition
| Mechanism | Effect | Clinical Relevance |
|-----------|--------|-------------------|
| **LNAA transport** | Tyrosine and tryptophan compete | High protein → favors tyrosine |
| **Tryptophan hydroxylase** | Rate-limiting | Serotonin synthesis bottleneck |
| **Tyrosine hydroxylase** | Rate-limiting | Dopamine synthesis control |

### Clinical Markers (Urine)
| Marker | Indicates | Interpretation |
|--------|-----------|----------------|
| **HVA** | Dopamine metabolism | Elevated = high turnover |
| **VMA** | Norepinephrine/epinephrine | Pheochromocytoma marker |
| **5-HIAA** | Serotonin metabolism | Carcinoid tumor marker |
| **Vanilmandelate** | Catecholamine balance | Neurotransmitter status |

### Nutritional Influences
| Factor | Effect | Intervention |
|--------|--------|--------------|
| **Low tyrosine** | Reduced dopamine | Protein intake |
| **Low tryptophan** | Reduced serotonin | Carbohydrate timing |
| **BH4 deficiency** | Impaired synthesis | Methylfolate, cofactors |
| **B6 deficiency** | Reduced conversion | Pyridoxal-5-phosphate |
| **Iron deficiency** | Impaired hydroxylases | Iron repletion |

## Clinical Implication
- **Mood disorders:** Precursor availability affects neurotransmitter synthesis
- **ADHD:** Dopamine pathway dysfunction implicated
- **Depression:** Serotonin and catecholamine theories
- **Anxiety:** GABA/glutamate balance central
- **METHAP relevance:** Neurotransmitter metabolism links to metabolic health through BH4 recycling, methylation status, and amino acid availability
