# Homocysteine Ranges and Cardiovascular Risk

> Homocysteine >10-12 μmol/L is associated with increased cardiovascular risk, while optimal levels are <8-10 μmol/L.

## Standard vs Optimal

| Level | Standard | Risk |
|-------|----------|------|
| <6 | Low | Optimal |
| 6-10 | Normal | Acceptable |
| 10-15 | Borderline | Mild elevation |
| >15 | High | CV risk |

## Risk Stratification

**Low risk:** <8 μmol/L
**Optimal:** <10 μmol/L
**Mild elevation:** 10-15
**Moderate:** 15-30
**Severe:** >30

## Clinical Significance

- Cardiovascular disease
- Stroke
- Cognitive decline
- Bone fractures

## Causes of Elevation

- B12 deficiency
- Folate deficiency
- B6 deficiency
- MTHFR variants
- Renal failure
- Hypothyroidism
