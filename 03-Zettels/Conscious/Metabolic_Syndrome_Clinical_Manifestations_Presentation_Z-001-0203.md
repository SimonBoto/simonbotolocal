## Core Clinical Features

### 1. Central Obesity (Visceral Adiposity)
- **Android distribution:** Apple-shaped body (waist > hips)
- **Waist circumference:** Key diagnostic measure
- **Metabolic activity:** Visceral fat more metabolically active, pro-inflammatory

### 2. Hypertension
- Often asymptomatic ("silent killer")
- Discovered on routine screening
- May cause headache, dizziness in severe cases

### 3. Dyslipidemia
- **Physical signs (uncommon):** Xanthomas, xanthelasma
- **Lab findings:** Elevated triglycerides, low HDL

### 4. Hyperglycemia
- Early: Often asymptomatic
- Progressive: Polyuria, polydipsia, fatigue, blurred vision
- Advanced: Signs of diabetes complications

### Other Signs
- **Hepatomegaly:** Fatty liver (often non-tender)
- **Accelerated atherosclerosis:** Bruits, diminished pulses
- **PCOS features** (women): Hirsutism, acne, menstrual irregularities

## Associated Conditions

### Non-Alcoholic Fatty Liver Disease (NAFLD)
- Present in 70-80% of MetS patients
- Spectrum: Steatosis → NASH → Fibrosis → Cirrhosis
- Often asymptomatic; elevated transaminases

### Obstructive Sleep Apnea
- Strong bidirectional association with MetS
- Contributes to hypertension, 

### Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS)
- MetS equivalent in women of reproductive age
- Shared insulin resistance mechanism

## Clinical Pearl

> **Metabolic syndrome is typically silent.** Most patients are diagnosed through routine screening or incidentally during evaluation for other conditions. Physical signs (acanthosis nigricans) are often the only visible clues.

## METHAP Screening Implications

- **Baseline assessment:** Document all MetS components
- **Physical exam:** Look for acanthosis nigricans as IR marker
- **Monitoring:** Track changes in MetS criteria as outcome measure
