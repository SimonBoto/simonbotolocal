## Core Claim
**Hong Kong residents consume ~145 kg of meat per person annually — the highest globally — while having the highest life expectancy in the world** — high meat intake does not necessarily reduce lifespan.

## The Hong Kong Metrics
| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Life expectancy** | **Highest globally** |
| **Meat consumption** | **Highest globally** (per capita) |
| **Annual meat per person** | ~145 kg |

## Impact Assessment
| Observation | Conclusion |
|-------------|------------|
| **High meat intake** | **No negative impact** on health or longevity |
| **Coexistence** | High meat + longest life, not mutually exclusive |

## Key Quote
> "Hong Kong has world's longest life expectancy. Hong Kong also has highest meat consumption per capita globally. Each resident consumes approximately 145 kg of meat annually — and this does not appear to have negative impact on health or life expectancy."

## Data Sources
- Health & Protection, February 2025
- AHDB Asia meat consumption data
- Our World in Data

## Clinical Implication
Population-level data contradicts simple "meat is harmful" narratives — **quantity of meat alone is not determinant of health outcomes**.
