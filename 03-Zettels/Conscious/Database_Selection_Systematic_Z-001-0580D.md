> **Definition:** Database selection is the strategic process of choosing appropriate electronic databases to ensure comprehensive identification of relevant literature for a systematic review.

## Source

[[Meta-Analysis_Methodology_-_From_Search_Strategy_L-001-0580_v4.0]]

## Core Principles

- **Complementarity:** Selecting databases that cover different subject areas, publication types, and geographic regions
- **Relevance:** Prioritizing databases that align with the research question and study population
- **Coverage:** Ensuring adequate representation of the literature domain and study types
- **Accessibility:** Considering database availability, cost, and search interface capabilities

## Key Databases and Their Strengths

- **PubMed/MEDLINE:** Comprehensive biomedical literature coverage with MeSH indexing
- **Embase:** Strong coverage of European journals, pharmacology, and drug-related topics
- **Cochrane Central Register of Controlled Trials (CENTRAL):** High-quality randomized controlled trials
- **Web of Science:** Citation tracking and interdisciplinary coverage
- **Scopus:** Broad interdisciplinary coverage with advanced citation analysis
- **PsycINFO:** Comprehensive coverage of psychology and behavioral sciences
- **CINAHL:** Nursing and allied health literature

## Selection Criteria

1. **Subject coverage:** Relevance to the research question and population
2. **Study type coverage:** Ability to identify the required study designs
3. **Geographic coverage:** Representation of different regions and publication sources
4. **Search functionality:** Advanced search capabilities and controlled vocabulary
5. **Cost and access:** Institutional subscriptions and open access availability
6. **Reporting requirements:** Ability to document search strategies and results

## Best Practices

- Use at least 3-4 databases for comprehensive coverage
- Include one major biomedical database (PubMed/Embase)
- Include one trial registry (CENTRAL/ClinicalTrials.gov)
- Consider discipline-specific databases when appropriate
- Document rationale for database selection
