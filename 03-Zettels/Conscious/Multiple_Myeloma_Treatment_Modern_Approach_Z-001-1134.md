# Multiple Myeloma Treatment Modern Approach

## Overview
Modern multiple myeloma treatment utilizes novel agents (immunomodulatory drugs, proteasome inhibitors, monoclonal antibodies) combined with stem cell transplantation and maintenance therapy to achieve deep responses and prolonged survival.

## Treatment Phases

### 1. Induction Therapy
| Goal | Achieve rapid response |
|------|------------------------|
| **Duration** | 4-6 cycles (3-4 months) |
| **Setting** | Outpatient |

#### Standard Regimens
| Regimen | Drugs | Use |
|---------|-------|-----|
| **VRd** | Bortezomib, Lenalidomide, Dex | Standard of care |
| **DRd** | Daratumumab, Lenalidomide, Dex | High efficacy |
| **KRd** | Carfilzomib, Lenalidomide, Dex | High risk |
| **VTd** | Bortezomib, Thalidomide, Dex | Pre-ASCT |

### 2. Consolidation
| Approach | Candidates |
|----------|------------|
| **Autologous stem cell transplant (ASCT)** | Eligible patients |
| **High-dose melphalan** | Conditioning regimen |
| **Tandem ASCT** | High-risk patients |

### 3. Maintenance
| Drug | Schedule | Benefit |
|------|----------|---------|
| **Lenalidomide** | Daily until progression | Improved PFS |
| **Bortezomib** | Weekly/biweekly | High risk |

## Drug Classes

### Immunomodulatory Drugs (IMiDs)
| Drug | Generation |  |
|------|------------|-----------|
| **Thalidomide** | First | Cereblon binding, IL-6 inhibition |
| **Lenalidomide** | Second | Enhanced IMiD, NK cell activation |
| **Pomalidomide** | Third | Active in resistant disease |

**Key toxicities:**
- Thrombosis (requires prophylaxis)
- Neutropenia
- Neuropathy (thalidomide)

### Proteasome Inhibitors
| Drug | Route | Reversibility |
|------|-------|---------------|
| **Bortezomib** | IV/SC | Reversible |
| **Carfilzomib** | IV | Irreversible |
| **Ixazomib** | Oral | Reversible |

**Key toxicities:**
- Peripheral neuropathy (bortezomib)
- Cardiotoxicity (carfilzomib)
- GI toxicity

### Monoclonal Antibodies
| Antibody | Target | Mechanism |
|----------|--------|-----------|
| **Daratumumab** | CD38 | Direct cytotoxicity, ADCC, CDC |
| **Elotuzumab** | SLAMF7 | NK cell activation |
| **Isatuximab** | CD38 | Similar to daratumumab |

**Key toxicities:**
- Infusion reactions
- Neutropenia
- Infections

### Steroids
| Drug | Role |
|------|------|
| **Dexamethasone** | Backbone of all regimens |
| **Mechanism** | Anti-inflammatory, anti-myeloma |

**Key toxicities:**
- Hyperglycemia
- Insomnia/mood changes
- Osteoporosis
- Infection risk

## Treatment by Risk Category

### Standard Risk
| Phase | Treatment |
|-------|-----------|
| **Induction** | VRd or DRd (4 cycles) |
| **ASCT** | High-dose melphalan + ASCT |
| **Consolidation** | 2 cycles VRd/DRd |
| **Maintenance** | Lenalidomide until progression |

### High Risk
| Phase | Treatment |
|-------|-----------|
| **Induction** | Quadruplet (Dara-KRd or Dara-VRd) |
| **ASCT** | Consider tandem |
| **Consolidation** | Intensive |
| **Maintenance** | Proteasome inhibitor + lenalidomide |

### Transplant-Ineligible
| Approach | Regimen |
|----------|---------|
| **Continuous therapy** | DRd until progression |
| **Fixed duration** | VRd → Rd maintenance |

## Relapsed/Refractory Disease

### At First Relapse
| Consideration | Approach |
|---------------|----------|
| **Prior response duration** | >2 years → reuse class |
| **Cytogenetics** | High risk → different class |
| **Toxicities** | Avoid prior toxicities |

### Options
| Class | Examples |
|-------|----------|
| **New anti-CD38** | Isatuximab if daratumumab exposed |
| **CAR-T cells** | Cilta-cel, Idecabtagene |
| **Bispecific antibodies** | Teclistamab, Elranatamab |
| **Selinexor** | XPO1 inhibitor |
| **Belaumab** | Anti-BCMA ADC |

## Supportive Care

### 
| Intervention | Rationale |
|--------------|-----------|
| **Bisphosphonates** | Reduce skeletal events |
| **Denosumab** | Alternative to bisphosphonates |
| **Kyphoplasty/vertebroplasty** | Painful compression fractures |

### Infection Prevention
| Intervention | Indication |
|--------------|------------|
| **Antiviral prophylaxis** | Bortezomib (HSV/VZV risk) |
| **PJP prophylaxis** | High-dose steroids |
| **IVIG** | Recurrent infections |

### Thrombosis Prophylaxis
| Risk Level | Prophylaxis |
|------------|-------------|
| **All IMiD patients** | Aspirin or anticoagulation |
| **High risk** | LMWH or DOAC |

## Future Directions

### Emerging Therapies
| Approach | Agents |
|----------|--------|
| **CAR-T cells** | BCMA-targeted |
| **Bispecific antibodies** | BCMA, GPRC5D, FcRH5 |
| **ADCs** | Belantamab mafodotin |
| **Novel targets** | CELMoDs, Kinesin inhibitors |
