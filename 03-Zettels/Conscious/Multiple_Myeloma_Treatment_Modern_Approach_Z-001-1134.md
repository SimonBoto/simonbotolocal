## Core Claim
Modern multiple myeloma treatment combines immunomodulatory drugs (IMiDs like lenalidomide), proteasome inhibitors (bortezomib, carfilzomib), monoclonal antibodies (daratumumab), and corticosteroids in various combinations, with autologous stem cell transplant for eligible patients, achieving unprecedented response rates and improved survival compared to historical chemotherapy approaches.

## Evidence

### Drug Classes

| Class | Examples | Mechanism |
|-------|----------|-----------|
| **IMiDs** | Lenalidomide, pomalidomide | Immune modulation, anti-angiogenic |
| **Proteasome inhibitors** | Bortezomib, carfilzomib | Protein degradation block |
| **Monoclonal antibodies** | Daratumumab (anti-CD38) | Immune-mediated killing |
| **Steroids** | Dexamethasone | Anti-inflammatory, anti-myeloma |

### Standard Regimens

**Initial Therapy (Transplant-Eligible):**
- VRd (bortezomib, lenalidomide, dexamethasone)
- Daratumumab-based quadruplets

**Initial Therapy (Transplant-Ineligible):**
- Rd (lenalidomide, dexamethasone)
- VRd-lite

**Relapsed/Refractory:**
- Carfilzomib-based
- Pomalidomide-based
- Daratumumab combinations

### Stem Cell Transplant

**Autologous SCT:**
- Standard for eligible patients
- Consolidation after induction
- Improves depth of response
- Not curative but prolongs remission

## Clinical Implication

**Response Assessment:**
- M-protein reduction
- Free light chain ratio
- Bone marrow response
- MRD (minimal residual disease)

**Supportive Care:**
- Bisphosphonates (bone protection)
- Antiviral prophylaxis
- Growth factors
- Infection prevention
