## Core Claim
**Propionyl-CoA carboxylase (PCC) catalyzes the ATP-dependent carboxylation of propionyl-CoA to D-methylmalonyl-CoA, requiring biotin (vitamin B7) as essential cofactor** — this is the first committed step in propionate metabolism, linking biotin status to the ability to extract energy from fiber, amino acids, and odd-chain fatty acids.

## Evidence

### Reaction
```
Propionyl-CoA + HCO₃⁻ + ATP → D-Methylmalonyl-CoA + ADP + Pi
```

### Enzyme Characteristics
| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Structure** | Alpha and beta subunits |
| **Cofactor** | Biotin (vitamin B7) — covalently bound |
| **Family** | Biotin-dependent carboxylases |
| **Energy** | ATP-dependent CO₂ activation |

### Biotin Role
- **Carboxyl carrier:** Transfers activated CO₂ to substrate
- **Covalently bound** to enzyme lysine residue
- **Recycled** after each reaction

## Clinical Implication
- **Inherited PCC deficiency → propionic acidemia** — life-threatening organic acid accumulation
- **Biotin deficiency rare** but impairs PCC (biotin widely available in foods)
- **Biotin-responsive PCC deficiency** — some mutations respond to high-dose biotin
- **METHAP relevance:** Biotin adequacy supports propionate metabolism; rarely limiting unless inherited defect
