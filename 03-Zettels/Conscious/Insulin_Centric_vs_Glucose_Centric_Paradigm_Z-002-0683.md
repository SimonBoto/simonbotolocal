# Insulin-Centric vs Glucose-Centric Diabetes Paradigm

## Core Claim
The **insulin-centric paradigm** — focusing on insulin resistance and hyperinsulinemia as primary pathogenic drivers — better explains cardiovascular risk patterns than the glucose-centric paradigm, suggesting interventions targeting insulin may be more cardioprotective than glucose-focused approaches alone.

## The Glucose-Centric Failure

### ACCORD, ADVANCE, VADT Trials
| Trial | Finding |
|-------|---------|
| **Intensive glucose lowering** | Limited CVD benefit |
| **Aggressive A1c targets** | Some harm (ACCORD) |
| **Hypoglycemia** | Increased with intensive treatment |

### The Disconnect
| Assumption | Reality |
|------------|---------|
| Lower glucose = lower CVD | Modest effect |
| A1c is best target | Insulin status matters more |
| Medication is primary | Lifestyle more effective |

## The Insulin-Centric Alternative

### Focus Areas
| Target | Intervention |
|--------|--------------|
| **Insulin resistance** | Carbohydrate restriction |
| **Hyperinsulinemia** | Fasting, time-restricted eating |
| **Weight loss** | Caloric deficit, low-carb |
| **Exercise** | Improves insulin sensitivity |

### Evidence Base
| Study | Finding |
|-------|---------|
| **Low-carb diets** | Reduce insulin, improve CVD risk |
| **Weight loss** | Reverses diabetes, reduces insulin |
| **Metformin** | Improves insulin sensitivity |

## Clinical Shift

### From
| Old Approach | Limitation |
|--------------|------------|
| A1c targets | Misses insulin-driven risk |
| Glucose-lowering drugs | Limited CVD benefit |
| Generic dietary advice | Doesn't address insulin |

### To
| New Approach | Advantage |
|--------------|-----------|
| Insulin assessment | Identifies true risk |
| Insulin-lowering strategies | Addresses root cause |
| Personalized metabolic approach | Better outcomes |

## Evidence

> "The disconnect between glucose control and CVD outcomes in diabetes trials (ACCORD, ADVANCE, VADT) suggests glucose-lowering alone is insufficient; interventions that reduce insulin levels improve CVD risk factors beyond glucose reduction."
> — Zhao et al., 2019
