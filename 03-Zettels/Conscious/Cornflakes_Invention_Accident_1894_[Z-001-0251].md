# Cornflakes Invention — The Accident (1894)

> In 1894, a kitchen accident at the Battle Creek Sanitarium led to the invention of flaked cereal when boiled wheat was left sitting, softened, and then rolled into flakes—creating the foundation of the multi-billion dollar breakfast cereal industry.

## The Accident

**Date:** 1894
**Location:** Battle Creek Sanitarium kitchen
**Personnel:** John Harvey and Will Keith Kellogg

**What Happened:**
1. **Intended:** Cook wheat for Sanitarium patients
2. **Distraction:** Interrupted, pot forgotten
3. **Result:** Wheat sat, continued to soften
4. **Discovery:** Could be rolled into thin flakes
5. **Experiment:** Baked the flakes
6. **Outcome:** Crispy, toasted, palatable

## Initial Development

**First Flakes:**
- Wheat flakes (original)
- Served to Sanitarium patients
- Well-received
- Seen as "health food"
- Fit vegetarian diet

**Transition to Corn:**
- Wheat flakes successful
- Experimented with corn (maize)
- Cornflakes even more popular
- Better flavor, texture
- Became signature product

**Rationale:**
- Easy to digest
- Vegetarian (religious requirement)
- "Pure" food
- Long shelf life
- Novel texture

## Technical Innovation

**Process:**
1. Boil grain (wheat/corn)
2. Soften sufficiently
3. Roll into thin flakes
4. Toast/bake until crispy
5. Package immediately

**Improvements:**
- Mechanical rollers developed
- Consistent thickness
- Uniform toasting
- Scalable production

## Significance

**Immediate:**
- Popular Sanitarium food
- "Healthful" alternative
- Satisfied vegetarian patients
- Innovation in food texture

**Long-term:**
- Foundation of cereal industry
- Breakfast revolution
- Mass-produced convenience food
- Global food category

**Irony:**
- Health food origins
- Eventually criticized (processed, sugary)
- "Healthy" to "junk food" trajectory
- Lost original mission
