## Leonard Thompson (1908-1935)

**Background:**
- 14 years old at treatment
- Severe Type 1 diabetes
- Weight: 65 pounds (severely emaciated)
- Near death at Toronto General Hospital
- Previous treatment: Frederick Allen's starvation diet

** State (January 1922):**
- Diabetic ketoacidosis
- Severe wasting/cachexia
- Little time remaining
- Parents desperate

## First Injection Attempt (January 11, 1922)

**Preparation:**
- Used Banting-Best extract (less purified)
- Injected subcutaneously
- Dose: Small (safety concern)

**Results:**
- Slight reduction in blood glucose
- Allergic reaction (abscess at injection site)
- Mild fever
- Insufficient purity

**Decision:**
- Treatment paused
- Need for better purification
- James Collip intensified efforts

## Core Principle

> On January 23, 1922, 14-year-old Leonard Thompson became the first human to receive effective insulin treatment, marking the moment when  transformed from a fatal disease to a manageable condition.

## Successful Treatment (January 23, 1922)

**Preparation:**
- Used Collip's purified extract
- Sterile technique
- Careful dosing

**Results:**
- Blood glucose dropped from 520 mg/dL to 120 mg/dL
- Clinical improvement dramatic
- Increased strength and alertness
- Weight gain began

**Subsequent Treatment:**
- Continued daily injections
- Rapid recovery
- Discharged from hospital
- Lived 13 more years (died 1935, age 27, of pneumonia)

- Demonstrated diabetes could be treated

- "The birth of insulin therapy"
- Marked end of pre-insulin era
- Beginning of modern diabetes management

**Symbolic:**
- Represented millions who would benefit
