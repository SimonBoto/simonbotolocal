## Mechanism

**The purine salvage pathway recovers free purine bases (hypoxanthine, guanine, adenine) from nucleotide degradation and converts them back to nucleotides using PRPP, saving ~4-5 ATP per nucleotide compared to de novo synthesis** — brain and bone marrow are highly dependent on salvage due to limited de novo capacity.

## Mechanism

**The purine salvage pathway recovers free purine bases (hypoxanthine, guanine, adenine) from nucleotide degradation and converts them back to nucleotides using PRPP, saving ~4-5 ATP per nucleotide compared to de novo synthesis** — brain and bone marrow are highly dependent on salvage due to limited de novo capacity.

## Evidence

### Energy Economics
| Pathway | ATP Cost | Efficiency |
|---------|----------|------------|
| **De novo synthesis** | ~6 ATP | Low |
| **Salvage pathway** | ~1-2 ATP | High |
| **Net savings** | 4-5 ATP | Essential for high-demand tissues |

### Key Enzymes
| Enzyme | Substrates | Products | Tissue |
|--------|------------|----------|--------|
| **HGPRT** | Hypoxanthine, Guanine + PRPP | IMP, GMP | Brain (high), all tissues |
| **APRT** | Adenine + PRPP | AMP | All tissues |

### Tissue Dependence
| Tissue | Salvage Dependence | Rationale |
|--------|-------------------|-----------|
| **Brain** | High | Limited de novo capacity |
| **Bone marrow** | High | Rapid proliferation |
| **Erythrocytes** | Total | No de novo synthesis |

## Clinical Implication
- **Lesch-Nyhan syndrome:** HGPRT deficiency → hyperuricemia, neurological symptoms, self-injury
- **Gout:** Purine overproduction or underexcretion → uric acid crystals
- **Allopurinol** treats gout by inhibiting xanthine oxidase (degradation pathway)
- **METHAP relevance:** High metabolic turnover increases nucleotide demand; salvage pathway efficiency supports cellular energy economy
