## Mechanism

**Adipic acid (C6 dicarboxylic acid) is formed through ω-oxidation in the endoplasmic reticulum when mitochondrial β-oxidation is impaired — serving as a critical urinary biomarker for mitochondrial dysfunction and early insulin resistance, with formation requiring cytochrome P450-mediated terminal hydroxylation of hexanoyl-CoA derived from incomplete palmitate oxidation.**

## Mechanism

**Adipic acid (C6 dicarboxylic acid) is formed through ω-oxidation in the endoplasmic reticulum when mitochondrial β-oxidation is impaired — serving as a critical urinary biomarker for mitochondrial dysfunction and early insulin resistance, with formation requiring cytochrome P450-mediated terminal hydroxylation of hexanoyl-CoA derived from incomplete palmitate oxidation.**

## Evidence

### Pathway Overview
| Step | Location | Process | Key Enzyme/Cofactor |
|------|----------|---------|---------------------|
| **1** | Cytosol | Palmitate activation | Acyl-CoA synthetase, CoA (B5) |
| **2** | Mitochondria | Partial β-oxidation | β-oxidation enzymes |
| **3** | ER membrane | ω-Hydroxylation | CYP4A11, NADPH |
| **4** | ER | Terminal oxidation | Alcohol/Aldehyde dehydrogenase |

### Step 1: Activation
```
Palmitate (C16:0) + CoA + ATP → Palmitoyl-CoA + AMP + PPi
```
| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Enzyme | Acyl-CoA synthetase |
| Cofactor | Coenzyme A (vitamin B5-dependent) |
| Purpose | Activates fatty acid for metabolism |

### Step 2: Partial β-Oxidation
```
Palmitoyl-CoA → [5 cycles] → Hexanoyl-CoA (C6)
```
| Condition | Result |
|-----------|--------|
| **Normal** | Completes 7 cycles → 8 Acetyl-CoA |
| **Impaired** | Stops at C6 → Hexanoyl-CoA accumulation |
| **Causes** | Carnitine deficiency, mitochondrial dysfunction, genetic defects |

### Step 3: ω-Oxidation (ER)
**ω-Hydroxylation:**
```
Hexanoyl-CoA + O₂ + NADPH → ω-Hydroxyhexanoyl-CoA + NADP⁺ + H₂O
```
| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Enzyme | CYP4A11 (cytochrome P450) |
| Location | ER membrane |
| Action | Adds -OH to terminal (omega) carbon |

**Terminal Oxidation:**
```
ω-Hydroxyhexanoyl-CoA + O₂ + NAD⁺ → Adipate + CoA + NADH + H⁺
```
| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Enzymes | Alcohol dehydrogenase → Aldehyde dehydrogenase |
| Action | Oxidizes -OH to -COOH |
| Product | Adipic acid (hexanedioic acid) |

### Why ω-Oxidation Occurs
| Factor | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| **Mitochondrial overload** | β-oxidation capacity exceeded |
| **Carnitine deficiency** | Transport block |
| **Genetic defects** | MCAD, VLCAD deficiencies |
| **Insulin resistance** | Impaired mitochondrial function |

## Clinical Implication
- **Biomarker significance:** Urinary adipate indicates β-oxidation impairment
- **Early detection:** Elevates before clinical symptoms of insulin resistance
- **PhD relevance:** Core biomarker for METHAP insulin resistance detection
- **Compensatory pathway:** ω-oxidation provides alternative when β-oxidation fails
- **METHAP relevance:** Adipate elevation guides intervention timing and metabolic support strategies
