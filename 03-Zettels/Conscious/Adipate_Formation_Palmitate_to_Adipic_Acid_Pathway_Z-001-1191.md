# Adipate Formation — Palmitate to Adipic Acid Pathway

## Overview

Adipic acid (hexanedioic acid) is a C6 dicarboxylic acid formed when β-oxidation is impaired and fatty acids undergo ω-oxidation. It serves as a critical  for mitochondrial dysfunction and early insulin resistance.

---

## Complete Pathway: Palmitate → Adipate

### Step 1: Activation
```
Palmitate (C16:0) + CoA + ATP → Palmitoyl-CoA + AMP + PPi
```
- Enzyme: Acyl-CoA synthetase
- Requires: Coenzyme A (vitamin B5-dependent)

### Step 2: Partial β-Oxidation (Mitochondria)
```
Palmitoyl-CoA → [5 cycles β-oxidation] → Hexanoyl-CoA (C6)
```
- Normal: Would complete all 7 cycles to acetyl-CoA
- Impaired: Stops at C6 (hexanoyl-CoA)
- Why impaired: Carnitine deficiency, mitochondrial dysfunction, genetic defects

### Step 3: Transport to ER
```
Hexanoyl-CoA exits mitochondria
Enters endoplasmic reticulum
```

### Step 4: ω-Oxidation (ER)

**Step 4a: ω-Hydroxylation**
```
Hexanoyl-CoA + O₂ + NADPH → ω-Hydroxyhexanoyl-CoA + NADP⁺ + H₂O
```
- Enzyme: CYP4A11 (cytochrome P450)
- Location: ER membrane
- Adds -OH to terminal (omega) carbon

**Step 4b: Terminal Oxidation**
```
ω-Hydroxyhexanoyl-CoA + O₂ + NAD⁺ → Adipate + CoA + NADH + H⁺
```
- Enzyme: Alcohol dehydrogenase → Aldehyde dehydrogenase
- Oxidizes -OH to -COOH

**Result:** Adipic acid (hexanedioic acid, HOOC-(CH₂)₄-COOH)

---

## Why This Pathway Activates

### Normal Conditions
- β-oxidation handles all fatty acid catabolism
- ω-oxidation minimal (5-10% of fatty acid )
- Adipate production: Low/normal

### Pathological Conditions
1. **β-Oxidation Impairment**
   - Carnitine deficiency
   - Mitochondrial dysfunction
   - MCAD (genetic)
   - Insulin resistance

2. **Fatty Acid Overload**
   - High-fat diet + impaired oxidation
   - Fasting (when carnitine-limited)

3. **Compensatory Response**
   - ω-oxidation acts as "overflow" pathway
   - Produces water-soluble dicarboxylic acids
   - Allows urinary excretion

---

## Clinical Detection

### Urine Organic Acids Test (OAT)
**Normal:** < 20 mg/g creatinine
**Elevated:** Indicates β-oxidation stress

### Pattern Recognition
| Condition | Adipate | Suberate | Sebacate |
