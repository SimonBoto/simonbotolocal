# Amino Acid to Oxalate 

## Overview
Oxalic acid is produced endogenously through amino acid metabolism. Two amino acids are primary precursors.

## Primary Precursors

### 1. Glycine Metabolism
```
Glycine → Glyoxylate → Oxalate
```
- **Location:** Primarily l
- **Enzymes:** D-amino acid oxidase, glyoxylate reductase (reverse)
- **Regulation:** Glyoxylate partitioned between oxalate and other fates

### 2. Hydroxyproline Metabolism
```
Hydroxyproline → Glyoxylate → Oxalate
```
- **Source:** Collagen turnover (major hydroxyproline reservoir)
- **Clinical relevance:** High collagen turnover states → increased oxalate
- **Pathway:** 4-hydroxy-L-proline → glyoxylate via enzymatic cascade

## Key Intermediate: Glyoxylate
| Fate | Enzyme | Product |
|------|--------|---------|
| Oxidation | Lactate dehydrogenase | **Oxalate** |
| Reduction | Glyoxylate reductase | Glycolate |
| Transamination | Alanine-glyoxylate aminotransferase | Glycine |
| Decarboxylation | — | Formate + CO₂ |

## Primary Hyperoxaluria
**Genetic disorder → excessive oxalate production**

| Type | Defect | Inheritance |
|------|--------|-------------|
| PH1 | Alanine-glyoxylate aminotransferase (AGXT) | Autosomal recessive |
| PH2 | Glyoxylate reductase/hydroxypyruvate reductase (GRHPR) | Autosomal recessive |
| PH3 | 4-hydroxy-2-oxoglutarate aldolase (HOGA1) | Autosomal recessive |

**Clinical features:**
- Nephrocalcinosis (kidney calcium deposition)
- Recurrent calcium oxalate stones
- Systemic oxalosis (deposits in heart, bones, retina)
- Progressive renal failure

## Physiological Factors Affecting Oxalate Production
| Factor | Effect |  |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| High collagen turnover | ↑ Oxalate | Hydroxyproline load |
| High protein intake | ↑ Oxalate | Glycine load |
| Vitamin B6 deficiency | ↑ Oxalate | AGXT cofactor impaired |
| Ethylene glycol poisoning | ↑↑ Oxalate | Metabolized to glyoxylate |

## PhD Relevance
- **Endogenous vs. exogenous:** Urine oxalate reflects both dietary intake AND metabolic production
- **Marker challenge:** Hard to distinguish sources in clinical samples
- **Adipic acid advantage:** Primarily exogenous/metabolic intermediate; may offer cleaner signal
