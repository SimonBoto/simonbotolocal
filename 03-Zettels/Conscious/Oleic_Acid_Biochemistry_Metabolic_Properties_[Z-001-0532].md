# Oleic Acid —  and Metabolic Properties

> Oleic acid (18:1, omega-9) offers unique metabolic stability through its single double bond, optimal membrane incorporation, and direct genomic signaling effects.

## Structure

**Chemical:**
- 18 carbons, 1 double bond (position 9)
- Monounsaturated (MUFA)
- Cis configuration

**Properties:**
- Melting point: 13-14°C
- Fluid at room temperature
- Smoke point: ~200°C

## Metabolic Stability

**Oxidation Resistance:**
- Single double bond less reactive
- Lower free radical generation
- Reduced lipid peroxidation

**Heat Stability:**
- Suitable for cooking
- Less toxic oxidation products

## Membrane Effects

**Phospholipid Bilayer:**
- Optimal fluidity
- Preserves receptor function
- Supports signaling

**Comparison:**
- SFA: Too rigid
- PUFA: Too fluid, oxidizes
- MUFA: Optimal balance

## Cellular Signaling

- PPAR activation
- SREBP regulation
- Anti-inflammatory gene programs
-  signaling enhancement
