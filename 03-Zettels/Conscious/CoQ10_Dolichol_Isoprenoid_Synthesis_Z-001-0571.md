## Core Claim
**CoQ10 (ubiquinone) and dolichol — both synthesized from isopentenyl pyrophosphate via the mevalonate pathway — serve critical cellular functions: CoQ10 as an essential electron carrier in mitochondrial oxidative phosphorylation and membrane antioxidant, while dolichol enables N-linked protein glycosylation in the endoplasmic reticulum, with statin-induced mevalonate inhibition depleting both and causing mitochondrial dysfunction and protein processing defects.**

## Evidence

### Coenzyme Q10 (Ubiquinone)

#### Structure and Synthesis
| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Structure** | Benzoquinone ring + isoprenoid tail (10 units in humans) |
| **Synthesis** | Tyrosine + 10 IPP units → CoQ10 |
| **Location** | Inner mitochondrial membrane |
| **Tissue concentration** | Highest in heart, liver, kidney |

#### Functions
| Function | Mechanism | Clinical Relevance |
|----------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Electron transport** | Complex I → III shuttle | Essential for ATP production |
| **Antioxidant** | Membrane lipid protection | Prevents oxidative damage |
| **Protein stabilization** | Membrane protein function | Maintains ETC complexes |
| **pH buffering** | Mitochondrial matrix | Protects enzymes |

#### Statin-Induced Depletion
```
Statin → HMG-CoA reductase inhibition
    ↓
↓ Mevalonate → ↓ IPP
    ↓
↓ CoQ10 synthesis
    ↓
Mitochondrial dysfunction → Myopathy, fatigue
```

| Parameter | Effect |
|-----------|--------|
| **Plasma CoQ10** | ↓ 40-50% with statins |
| **Muscle CoQ10** | ↓ 50-75% |
| **Symptom threshold** | Variable (genetic factors) |
| **Reversal** | Supplementation effective |

### Dolichol

#### Structure and Function
| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Structure** | Long isoprenoid chain (16-23 units) + terminal alcohol |
| **Location** | Endoplasmic reticulum membrane |
| **Function** | N-linked glycosylation carrier |

#### Protein Glycosylation Role
| Step | Action | Importance |
|------|--------|------------|
| **1** | Dolichol-P + sugar → Dolichol-PP-sugar | Sugar activation |
| **2** | Transfer to nascent proteins | Quality control |
| **3** | Protein folding assistance | ER quality control |
| **4** | Trafficking signals | Cellular localization |

#### Clinical Significance
| Condition | Mechanism | Outcome |
|-----------|-----------|---------|
| **CDG (congenital disorders)** | Dolichol defects | Multi-system disease |
| **Statin effects** | ↓ Dolichol | Impaired glycosylation |
| **Aging** | ↓ Dolichol synthesis | Cellular dysfunction |

### Prenylated Proteins
| Protein Family | Prenyl Group | Function |
|----------------|--------------|----------|
| **Ras, Rab, Rho** | Farnesyl or geranylgeranyl | Signal transduction |
| **Nuclear lamins** | Farnesyl | Nuclear structure |
| **Rhodopsin** | Farnesyl | Vision |
| **G-protein γ subunits** | Farnesyl | Membrane anchoring |

### Statin Side Effect Mechanisms
| Target | Effect | Clinical Manifestation |
|--------|--------|----------------------|
| **CoQ10** | ↓ Mitochondrial function | Myopathy, fatigue |
| **Dolichol** | ↓ Protein glycosylation | Cellular stress |
| **Prenylated proteins** | ↓ Signal transduction | Variable effects |
| **Isoprenoids** | ↓ Cell signaling | Pleiotropic effects |

### Supplementation Strategies
| Compound | Dose | Indication |
|----------|------|------------|
| **CoQ10 (ubiquinone)** | 100-300 mg/day | Statin therapy |
| **CoQ10 (ubiquinol)** | 100-200 mg/day | Better absorption |
| **Timing** | With statin | Concurrent depletion |

## Clinical Implication
- **Universal statin effect:** All patients on statins have ↓ CoQ10
- **Symptom variability:** Genetic differences in CoQ10 metabolism
- **Supplementation rationale:** Evidence supports CoQ10 with statins
- **Dolichol understudied:** Potential unrecognized statin effect
- **METHAP relevance:** CoQ10 is essential for mitochondrial function; metabolic syndrome patients on statins require monitoring and supplementation
