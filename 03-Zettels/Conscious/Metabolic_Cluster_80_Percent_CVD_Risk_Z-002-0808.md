# Metabolic Cluster Covers 80 Percent of CVD Risk

## Core Claim
**Hypertension + high triglycerides + low HDL + sdLDL + obesity** are not separate risk factors but **manifestations of insulin resistance** — together covering ~80% of cardiovascular risk.

## The Cluster Components

| Marker | Mechanism | Root Cause |
|--------|-----------|------------|
| **Hypertension** | Insulin-mediated sodium retention | Insulin resistance |
| **High triglycerides** | VLDL overproduction | Insulin resistance |
| **Low HDL** | Impaired reverse transport | Insulin resistance |
| **sdLDL** | Pattern B shift | Insulin resistance |
| **Obesity** | Adipose dysfunction | Insulin resistance |

## Unified vs. Fragmented Approach

| Fragmented View | Unified View |
|-----------------|--------------|
| Treat each separately | Address root insulin resistance |
| Multiple medications | Lifestyle intervention |
| Symptom management | Cause resolution |

## The 80% Figure

| Risk Category | Coverage |
|---------------|----------|
| **Metabolic/lifestyle factors** | ~80% of CVD risk |
| **LDL alone** | Small fraction |
| **Genetic factors** | Variable |

## Clinical Strategy

| Priority | Action |
|----------|--------|
| **First** | Assess insulin resistance (HOMA-IR, fasting insulin) |
| **Second** | Lifestyle intervention (diet, exercise, sleep) |
| **Third** | Address individual risk factors |

## Evidence

> "Hypertension, elevated triglycerides, low HDL, small dense LDL (sdLDL), and increased body weight are closely linked to insulin resistance and constitute basic manifestations of accompanying metabolic dysfunction."
> — Risk factor analysis
