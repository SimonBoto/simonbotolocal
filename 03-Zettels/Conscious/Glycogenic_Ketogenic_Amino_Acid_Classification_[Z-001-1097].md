# Glycogenic Ketogenic Amino Acid Classification

## Overview
Amino acids are classified by their metabolic fate: glycogenic (can be converted to glucose) or ketogenic (can be converted to ketones/).

## The Two Categories

### Glycogenic Amino Acids
**Definition:** Can be converted to pyruvate or Krebs cycle intermediates → glucose via gluconeogenesis

**The 18 Glycogenic Amino Acids:**
| Alanine | Arginine | Asparagine | Aspartate |
|---------|----------|------------|-----------|
| Cysteine | Glutamate | Glutamine | Glycine |
| Histidine | Isoleucine* | Methionine | Phenylalanine* |
| Proline | Serine | Threonine | Tryptophan* |
| Tyrosine* | Valine | | |

*Also has ketogenic component

### Ketogenic Amino Acids
**Definition:** Can be converted to acetyl-CoA or acetoacetyl-CoA → ketones or fatty acids

**The Purely Ketogenic Amino Acids:**
- **Leucine** (exclusively ketogenic)
- **Lysine** (exclusively ketogenic)

**Mixed (Both Glycogenic and Ketogenic):**
- Isoleucine
- Phenylalanine
- Tryptophan
- Tyrosine

## Classification Table

| AA | Glycogenic | Ketogenic | Entry Point |
|----|------------|-----------|-------------|
| **Leucine** | No | Yes | Acetoacetyl-CoA |
| **Lysine** | No | Yes | Acetoacetyl-CoA |
| **Isoleucine** | Yes | Yes | Succinyl-CoA + Acetyl-CoA |
| **Phenylalanine** | Yes | Yes | Fumarate + Acetoacetyl-CoA |
| **Tryptophan** | Yes | Yes | Pyruvate + Acetoacetyl-CoA |
| **Tyrosine** | Yes | Yes | Fumarate + Acetoacetyl-CoA |
| **All others** | Yes | No | Various Krebs intermediates |

## Clinical Significance

### Fasting and Starvation
- **Glycogenic AAs:** Maintain blood glucose via gluconeogenesis
- **Ketogenic AAs:** Support ketogenesis when fat stores low
- **Protein catabolism:** Muscle breakdown provides both

### Ketogenic Diet Considerations
- High leucine/lysine intake → ketogenic amino acids support ketosis
- However, most dietary protein contains mixed AAs
- Gluconeogenesis from glycogenic AAs can reduce ketosis

### Maple Syrup Urine Disease
- **Defect:** Branched-chain alpha-keto acid dehydrogenase
- **Affected AAs:** Leucine, isoleucine, valine
- **Consequence:** Toxic accumulation of branched-chain AAs and their ketoacids
