# Electrolytes and Minerals — Magnesium, Zinc, Selenium

**Magnesium, zinc, and selenium** are essential cofactors for metabolic and thyroid function. Unlike sodium and potassium (tightly regulated), these minerals often show suboptimal levels in metabolic patients and require targeted assessment.

---

## 🎯 Magnesium (Mg)

### Reference Ranges
| Form | Range | Best Test |
|------|-------|-----------|
| **Serum Mg** | 1.7-2.2 mg/dL | Poor (only 1% of body Mg) |
| **RBC Mg** | 4.2-6.8 mg/dL | Better (intracellular) |
| **Ionized Mg** | 1.8-2.4 mg/dL | Best (active form) |

### Functions
- **Cofactor for 300+ enzymes**
-  activation (hydroxylases require Mg)
- Insulin signaling and glucose transport
- Blood pressure regulation
- Muscle and nerve function

### Supplementation
| Form | Dose | Notes |
|------|------|-------|
| **Magnesium glycinate** | 200-400 mg elemental | Best absorbed, least GI effects |
| **Magnesium citrate** | 200-400 mg elemental | Good absorption, mild laxative |
| **Magnesium oxide** | 400 mg | Poor absorption, strong laxative |

**Target:** RBC Mg >5.0 mg/dL

---

## 🎯 Zinc (Zn)

### Reference Ranges
| Marker | Range | Notes |
|--------|-------|-------|
| **Serum zinc** | 70-120 μg/dL | Acute phase reactant (↓ in inflammation) |
| **RBC zinc** | 1,000-1,500 μg/dL | Better status marker |

### Functions
- **Cofactor for 100+ enzymes**
- Thyroid hormone receptor function
- Insulin synthesis and secretion
- Immune function
- Wound healing

### Clinical Relevance
- [[Thyroid_Optimal_Ranges_Z-005-0001.md]] — Thyroid function (zinc required for T3 receptor)
- [[HOMA_IR_Calculation_Interpretation_Z-001-0046.md]] — Insulin resistance (zinc in β-cell insulin granules)

### Supplementation
- **Zinc picolinate or bisglycinate:** 15-30 mg elemental zinc
- **With food** (nausea if empty stomach)
- **Balance with copper:** Long-term high-dose zinc → copper deficiency

---

## 🎯 Selenium (Se)

### Reference Ranges
| Marker | Range |
|--------|-------|
| **Serum selenium** | 70-150 μg/L |
| **RBC selenium** | 150-250 μg/L |

### Functions
- **Deiodinase cofactor** (T4 → T3 conversion)
- Glutathione peroxidase (antioxidant defense)
- Thyroid peroxidase modulation (may reduce antibodies)
- Immune function

### Clinical Relevance
- [[Thyroid_Optimal_Ranges_Z-005-0001.md]] — Thyroid function (selenium required for T3 production)
- [[Hashimoto_Pattern_Anti_TPO_Anti_TG_Z-005-0002.md]] — Hashimoto's (200 mcg Se may reduce Anti-TPO)

### Supplementation
| Population | Dose |
|------------|------|
| **General** | 50-100 mcg |
| **Thyroid disease** | 200 mcg |
| **Toxicity threshold** | >400 mcg/day (selenosis) |

**Caution:** Brazil nuts are selenium-dense (50-100 mcg per nut) — easy to overdose.

## 🔗 Connected Systems

**Thyroid:**
- [[Thyroid_Optimal_Ranges_Z-005-0001.md]] — T4→T3 conversion requires selenium and zinc
- Deficiency → impaired thyroid function despite adequate hormone

**Vitamin D:**
- [[Vitamin_D_Optimal_Range_80_150_Z-002-0004.md]] — VitD activation requires magnesium
- Low Mg → low 25-OH D despite supplementation

**Methylation:**
- [[Folate_5MTHF_vs_Folic_Acid_Z-004-0002.md]] — 5-MTHF synthesis requires zinc
- Methionine synthase requires magnesium

## 🩺 Clinical Patterns

### Pattern: Refractory Vitamin D Deficiency
| Finding | Interpretation |
|---------|----------------|
| 25-OH D remains low despite 10,000 IU | Check RBC Mg — likely deficient |
| Magnesium repleted | Vitamin D rises with same dose |

### Pattern: Suboptimal Thyroid Response
| Finding | Interpretation |
|---------|----------------|
| TSH improves but fT3 stays low | Check selenium and zinc |
| Supplementation | Improved T4→T3 conversion |

### Pattern: Insulin Resistance with Muscle Cramps
| Finding | Interpretation |
|---------|----------------|
| HOMA-IR elevated + muscle cramps/arrhythmias | Check magnesium |
| Low Mg | Impaired glucose transport, insulin signaling |

---

## 🔬 Testing Strategy

| Mineral | Best Test | When to Check |
|---------|-----------|---------------|
| **Magnesium** | RBC Mg | Refractory VitD, muscle cramps, arrhythmias |
| **Zinc** | RBC Zn | Poor wound healing, hypogeusia, thyroid issues |
| **Selenium** | Serum Se | Thyroid disease, geographic deficiency risk |

**Note:** Serum levels are affected by inflammation and acute phase response.

---

## 💡 Clinical Pearl

> "I check RBC magnesium in every patient with 'refractory' Vitamin D deficiency. 70% of the time, they're magnesium depleted. Fix the magnesium, Vitamin D rises without increasing dose."

> "Selenium is thyroid medicine. I give 200 mcg to every Hashimoto's patient. It doesn't always lower antibodies, but when it does, it's dramatic."

> "Zinc is underrated for insulin resistance. β-cells need zinc for insulin crystallization. Low zinc → impaired insulin secretion on top of resistance."
