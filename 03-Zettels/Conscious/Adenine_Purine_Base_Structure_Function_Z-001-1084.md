# Adenine — Purine Base Structure and Function

## Core Claim
**Adenine is a purine nucleobase that serves dual essential roles: pairing with thymine/uracil in DNA/RNA for genetic information storage, and forming adenosine triphosphate (ATP) for cellular energy currency** — its two-ring structure enables both base-pairing specificity and high-energy phosphate bonding.

## Evidence

### Chemical Structure
| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Class** | Purine (fused pyrimidine + imidazole rings) |
| **Formula** | C₅H₅N₅ |
| **Base pairing** | 2 hydrogen bonds with T (DNA) or U (RNA) |
| **Helix width** | Purine-pyrimidine pairing maintains constant width |

### Metabolic Pathways
| Pathway | Input | Energy Cost | Tissue |
|---------|-------|-------------|--------|
| **De novo synthesis** | Glycine, Aspartate, Glutamine, CO₂, Formate | 6 ATP | Liver |
| **Salvage pathway** | Free adenine + PRPP | 1 ATP | All tissues |

### Energy Role
- **ATP:** Adenosine triphosphate — universal energy carrier
- **ADP/AMP:** Energy depletion signals
- **cAMP:** Second messenger for hormone signaling

## Clinical Implication
- **Lesch-Nyhan syndrome:** HGPRT deficiency → purine overproduction → hyperuricemia, neurological dysfunction
- **Adenine salvage** is more energy-efficient than de novo synthesis — preferred when possible
- **METHAP relevance:** High metabolic turnover increases nucleotide demand; B-vitamins (folate, B12) support purine synthesis
