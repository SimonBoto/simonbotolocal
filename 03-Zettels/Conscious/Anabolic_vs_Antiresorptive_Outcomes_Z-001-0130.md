## Outcome Comparison

| Outcome | Anabolic Therapy | Antiresorptive Therapy | Notes |
|---------|------------------|------------------------|-------|
| **12-month spine BMD gain** | +7–15% | +2–6% | Anabolic superior |
| **12-month hip BMD gain** | +2–4% | +1–3% | Anabolic superior |
| **Vertebral fracture reduction** | 60–73% | 40–50% | Anabolic superior |
| **Non-vertebral fracture reduction** | 35–45% | 20–35% | Anabolic superior |
| **Time to effect** | 3–6 months | 6–12 months | Anabolic faster |

## Clinical Implications

- Anabolic agents provide rapid, substantial bone formation and superior fracture reduction
- Antiresorptive agents effectively slow bone loss and provide good fracture reduction with longer-term safety
- Anabolic therapy is preferred when rapid fracture prevention is critical
- Antiresorptive therapy is preferred for long-term maintenance and preservation

## Evidence Summary

- Anabolic therapy demonstrates higher efficacy across all measured outcomes
- The magnitude of benefit is greatest for vertebral fractures and spine BMD
- Time to clinical effect is significantly faster with anabolic agents
- Both classes have established safety profiles with specific contraindications
