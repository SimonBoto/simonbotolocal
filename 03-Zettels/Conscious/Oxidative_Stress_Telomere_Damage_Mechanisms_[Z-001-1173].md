# Oxidative Stress and Telomere Damage Mechanisms

## Why Telomeres Are Vulnerable to Oxidative Damage

### 1. G-Rich Sequence
- Telomere DNA: TTAGGG repeats (G-rich strand)
- Guanine has lowest oxidation potential of all bases
- 8-oxoguanine formation preferentially at G residues

### 2. T-Loop Structure
- Hidden 3' overhang limits access for repair machinery
- Single-strand region vulnerable to breakage
- Double-strand breaks in telomeres = chromosome fusions

### 3. Reduced Repair Capacity
- Shelterin complex blocks DNA repair factors
- Necessary to prevent "repair" that would cause fusion
- Side effect: Slow damage removal

---

## ROS Sources in Autoimmunity

### Mitochondrial Dysfunction
- Impaired ETC → electron leak → superoxide
- Chronic inflammation damages mitochondria
- Vicious cycle: Damage → more ROS → more damage

### NADPH Oxidase
- Expressed in immune cells (phagocytes, B cells, T cells)
- Produces superoxide for microbial killing
- Also damages host tissues

### Inflammatory Cytokines
- TNF-α, IL-1β, IFN-γ increase ROS production
- Activate NADPH oxidase
- Induce mitochondrial dysfunction

---

## Types of Oxidative Damage

| Damage Type | Mechanism | Consequence |
|-------------|-----------|-------------|
| **8-oxoguanine** | Base oxidation | Mutagenic, blocks replication |
| **Single-strand breaks** | Sugar oxidation | Telomere uncapping |
| **Double-strand breaks** | Clustered damage | Chromosome fusion |
| **Telomere uncapping** | Shelterin disruption | DDR activation |

---

## Magnitude of Effect

**Telomere Damage Rate:**
- 4-5x higher than other genomic regions
- Due to G-richness and poor repair

**Autoimmune Amplification:**
- Chronic inflammation = chronic ROS exposure
- Accelerates shortening beyond replication loss
- May account for 10-20 year immune age acceleration

---

## Antioxidant Defense

### Enzymatic
- **Superoxide dismutase (SOD):** Converts superoxide → H₂O₂
- **Catalase:** Converts H₂O₂ → H₂O
- **Glutathione peroxidase:** Uses GSH to reduce peroxides

### Non-Enzymatic
- **Glutathione:** Primary intracellular antioxidant
- **Vitamin C, E:** Scavenge free radicals
- **Polyphenols:** Plant-derived antioxidants

**In Autoimmunity:**
- Often overwhelmed by ROS production
- Antioxidant supplementation may help
