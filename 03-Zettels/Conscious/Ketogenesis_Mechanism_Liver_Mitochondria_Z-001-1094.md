# Ketogenesis Mechanism — Liver Mitochondria

## Core Claim
**Ketogenesis is the liver-specific process that converts fatty acid-derived acetyl-CoA into ketone bodies (BHB, acetoacetate, acetone) during low-insulin states, providing an alternative fuel for brain and muscle when glucose is scarce** — this HMG-CoA-dependent pathway is essential for survival during fasting.

## Evidence

### Triggers (Low Insulin States)
| Condition | Insulin Status | Ketogenesis |
|-----------|----------------|-------------|
| **Fasting** | Low | Activated |
| **Low-carb diet** | Low | Activated |
| **Prolonged exercise** | Low | Activated |
| **Fed state** | High | Inhibited |

### Biochemical Pathway
| Step | Reaction | Enzyme | Location |
|------|----------|--------|----------|
| **1** | Fatty acid → Acetyl-CoA | β-oxidation | Mitochondria |
| **2** | 2 Acetyl-CoA → Acetoacetyl-CoA | Thiolase | Mitochondria |
| **3** | + Acetyl-CoA → HMG-CoA | HMG-CoA synthase | Mitochondria |
| **4** | HMG-CoA → Acetoacetate | HMG-CoA lyase | Mitochondria |
| **5** | Acetoacetate → BHB | BHB dehydrogenase | Mitochondria |

### Ketone Body Fates
| Ketone Body | Fate | Tissue |
|-------------|------|--------|
| **BHB** | Oxidation for energy | Brain, muscle, heart |
| **Acetoacetate** | Decarboxylation or oxidation | Various |
| **Acetone** | Exhaled/urinary waste | — |

## Clinical Implication
- **Ketogenesis is liver-specific** — other tissues cannot produce ketones, only use them
- **BHB is the predominant circulating ketone** during prolonged fasting
- **Therapeutic ketosis** (epilepsy, metabolic syndrome) mimics fasting metabolism
- **METHAP relevance:** Low-carb approaches may induce mild ketosis; BHB has signaling roles beyond energy
