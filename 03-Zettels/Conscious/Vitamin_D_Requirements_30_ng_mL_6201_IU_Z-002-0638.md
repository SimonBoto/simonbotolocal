# Vitamin D Requirements for 30 ng/mL Target

## Core Claim
To achieve the higher serum 25(OH)D target of **30 ng/mL** (associated with optimal health outcomes), approximately **6,201 IU/day** is required for 97.5% of adults — still 10× the current RDA.

## Why 30 ng/mL?

### Evidence-Based Thresholds
| Level (ng/mL) | Status | Health Implications |
|---------------|--------|---------------------|
| <20 | Deficient | Rickets/osteomalacia risk |
| 20-30 | Insufficient | Suboptimal for many functions |
| 30-50 | Sufficient | Optimal for most outcomes |
| 50-80 | High normal | May benefit autoimmune |
| >100 | Excessive | Potential toxicity risk |

### Outcomes at ≥30 ng/mL
- Reduced all-cause mortality
- Lower cancer-specific mortality
- Decreased autoimmune disease incidence
- Better metabolic health (insulin sensitivity)
- Improved immune function

## The 6,201 IU Calculation

### Heaney et al. 2015 Analysis
- **Study design:** Dose-response meta-analysis
- **Target:** 30 ng/mL for 97.5% of population
- **Result:** 6,201 IU/day required
- **Confirmation:** Independent validation of Veugelers & Ekwaru methodology

### Practical Implications
| Current RDA | For 20 ng/mL | For 30 ng/mL |
|-------------|--------------|--------------|
| 600 IU/day | 8,900 IU/day | 6,201 IU/day |

*Note: Higher target (30 ng/mL) requires less than 20 ng/mL because starting from replete status requires less supplementation than correcting deficiency.*

## Individual Variation

### Factors Affecting Requirements
- **Baseline status:** Deficient individuals need more
- **BMI:** Higher body fat → sequestration → higher needs
- **Genetics:** VDR, DBP, CYP2R1 polymorphisms
- **Age:** Reduced skin synthesis with aging
- **Skin pigmentation:** Melanin reduces UV penetration

### Clinical Approach
- Test 25(OH)D baseline
- Supplement based on target and current level
- Re-test in 3 months
- Adjust dose individually

## Evidence

> "6,201 IU/day is needed for 97.5% of adults to achieve serum 25(OH)D >30 ng/mL."
> — Heaney et al., 2015

## Significance

To achieve the higher serum 25(OH)D target of **30 ng/mL** (associated with optimal health outcomes), approximately **6,201 IU/day** is required for 97.5% of adults — still 10× the current RDA.
