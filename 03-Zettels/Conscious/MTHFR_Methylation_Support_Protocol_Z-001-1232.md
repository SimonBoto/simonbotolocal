# MTHFR — Methylation Support Protocol

## Overview

MTHFR (methylenetetrahydrofolate reductase) is a critical enzyme in the folate cycle and methylation pathway. Homozygous mutations (C677T or A1298C) reduce enzyme activity 60-70%, impairing methylation and increasing homocysteine. Targeted nutrient support can bypass these genetic limitations.

---

## MTHFR Polymorphisms

### C677T Variant
| Genotype | Enzyme Activity | Population Frequency |
|----------|-----------------|---------------------|
| **CC (normal)** | 100% | 40-50% |
| **CT (heterozygous)** | 60-70% | 35-45% |
| **TT (homozygous)** | 30-40% | 10-15% |

** Impact:**
- Elevated homocysteine
- Increased cardiovascular risk
- Neural tube defect risk
- Mood disorders (depression, anxiety)
- Recurrent miscarriage

### A1298C Variant
| Genotype | Primary Impact |
|----------|----------------|
| **CC** | BH4 production impaired (neurotransmitters) |
| **CT** | Moderate reduction |
| **AA** | Normal |

**Clinical Impact:**
- Neurotransmitter issues
- Mood disorders
- Cognitive concerns

---

## Methylation Pathway Support

### 1. Methylfolate (5-MTHF)
**Why:** Bypasses MTHFR enzyme directly

| Form | Dose | Notes |
|------|------|-------|
| **5-MTHF ( salt)** | 400-800 mcg | Start low if sensitive |
| **Quatrefolic®** | 400-800 mcg | Active glucosamine salt |

**Caution:** Start low (200 mcg) — overmethylation can cause anxiety, insomnia

### 2. Methylcobalamin (B12)
**Why:** Essential methyl group acceptor

| Form | Dose |
|------|------|
| **Methylcobalamin** | 1,000-5,000 mcg/day |
| **Sublingual** | Preferred for absorption |

**Avoid:** Cyanocobalamin (requires conversion)

### 3. Riboflavin (B2)
**Why:** MTHFR cofactor

| Dose | Timing |
|------|--------|
| 10-25 mg/day | With other B vitamins |

**Function:** FAD cofactor for MTHFR enzyme

### 4. Supporting Nutrients
| Nutrient | Dose | Function |
|----------|------|----------|
| **** | 400-600 mg | Methylation cofactor |
| **Zinc** | 15-30 mg | Enzyme function |
| **B6 (P5P)** | 10-25 mg | Transsulfuration |
| **Betaine (TMG)** | 500-1,000 mg | Alternative methyl donor |
| **Choline** | 400-600 mg | Methylation backup |

## Homocysteine Management

### Target Levels
| Status | Homocysteine (μmol/L) |
|--------|----------------------|
| **Optimal** | <8 |
| **Mild elevation** | 8-12 |
| **Moderate** | 12-15 |
| **High risk** | >15 |

### Lowering Protocol
**For homocysteine >12:**
1. 5-MTHF: 800-1,000 mcg
2. Methylcobalamin: 2,000-5,000 mcg
3. B6 (P5P): 25-50 mg
4. Riboflavin: 10-25 mg
5. Magnesium: 400-600 mg

**Retest:** 8-12 weeks

---

## Avoid for MTHFR

### Synthetic Folic Acid
**Problem:**
- Requires MTHFR conversion (impaired)
- Can accumulate as unmetabolized folic acid (UMFA)
- May block folate receptors

**Check labels:** "Folic acid" = avoid
**Look for:** "Methylfolate," "5-MTHF," "Quatrefolic"

### Cyanocobalamin (B12)
**Problem:**
- Requires conversion to methylcobalamin
- Releases cyanide (minimal but unnecessary)

**Use instead:** Methylcobalamin or hydroxocobalamin

## My Health Supplement Assessment

### MTHFR-Friendly Features ✅
- **Folate:** Methylfolate (not folic acid)
- **B12:** Methylcobalamin (1,200% DV)
- **B2:** High dose (343% DV) — MTHFR cofactor
- **B6:** Pyridoxine (643% DV)

### Considerations
- **B3:Niacin:** Very high (7,300% DV relative to B2)
- May need additional **P5P** (active B6)
- Consider **betaine** for additional methylation support
