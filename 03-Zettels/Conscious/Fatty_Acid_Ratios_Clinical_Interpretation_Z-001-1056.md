## Core Claim
**The AA/EPA ratio and omega-6:3 ratio serve as quantitative inflammation indices — with AA/EPA <3 representing optimal anti-inflammatory balance, and omega-6:3 ratios >10:1 indicating pro-inflammatory substrate dominance that drives eicosanoid-mediated pathology.**

## Evidence

### AA/EPA Ratio (Arachidonic to Eicosapentaenoic Acid)
| Ratio Range | Status | Clinical Interpretation |
|-------------|--------|------------------------|
| **< 1.5** | Optimal | Strong anti-inflammatory |
| **1.5 - 3** | Good | Adequate balance |
| **3 - 10** | Elevated | Mild pro-inflammatory |
| **10 - 30** | High | Significant inflammation |
| **> 30** | Very high | High cardiovascular risk |

### Omega-6:3 Ratio
| Ratio Range | Status | Dietary Pattern |
|-------------|--------|-----------------|
| **< 2:1** | Optimal | Traditional/hunter-gatherer |
| **2-4:1** | Good | Mediterranean-style |
| **4-10:1** | Moderate | Typical Western diet |
| **10-20:1** | Poor | High seed oil consumption |
| **> 20:1** | Very poor | Extreme Western diet |

### Mechanism: Competitive Eicosanoid Synthesis
| Precursor | Enzyme | Product Class | Effect |
|-----------|--------|---------------|--------|
| **AA** | COX-2 | Prostaglandin E2 | Pro-inflammatory |
| **AA** | COX-2 | Thromboxane A2 | Pro-thrombotic |
| **EPA** | COX-2 | Prostaglandin E3 | Less inflammatory |
| **EPA** | COX-2 | Thromboxane A3 | Less thrombotic |
| **EPA** | LOX | Resolvins | Anti-inflammatory, resolving |

### Clinical Applications
| Condition | Target AA/EPA | Intervention |
|-----------|---------------|--------------|
| **Cardiovascular prevention** | < 3 | EPA 1-2g/day |
| **Active inflammation** | < 1.5 | EPA 2-4g/day |
| **Depression** | < 3 | EPA 1-2g/day |
| **ADHD** | < 3 | EPA/DHA combined |
| **Pregnancy** | < 3 | DHA 200-300mg/day |

### Omega-3 Index (RBC EPA+DHA)
| Level | Status | Cardiovascular Risk |
|-------|--------|---------------------|
| **> 8%** | Optimal | Low |
| **4-8%** | Intermediate | Moderate |
| **< 4%** | Low | High |

## Clinical Implication
- **Inflammation assessment:** Ratios more predictive than absolute levels
- **Cardiovascular risk:** AA/EPA correlates with events
- **Dose guidance:** Severe elevation requires higher EPA doses
- **Monitoring:** Recheck in 3-4 months after intervention
- **METHAP relevance:** Fatty acid ratios are objective biomarkers of inflammatory status; omega-3 index is validated CVD risk marker
