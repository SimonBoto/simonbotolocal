# Fatty Acid Ratios Clinical Interpretation

AA/EPA ratio as inflammation index; omega-6:3 ratio as dietary balance marker. These ratios predict cardiovascular risk, inflammatory status, and guide therapeutic interventions.

## Evidence
> "The AA/EPA ratio is one of the most powerful predictors of cardiovascular risk—more predictive than cholesterol in some studies." (Lipid research)

## Key Fatty Acid Ratios

### 1. AA/EPA Ratio (Inflammation Index)

| Range | Interpretation |
|-------|----------------|
| **< 3** | Optimal, low inflammation |
| **3-6** | Mild elevation, moderate risk |
| **6-15** | Elevated, increased CV risk |
| **> 15** | High, significant inflammation |

**Clinical significance:**
- AA (arachidonic acid) → pro-inflammatory eicosanoids
- EPA (eicosapentaenoic acid) → anti-inflammatory eicosanoids
- Ratio reflects balance of inflammatory mediators

**Evidence:**
- AA/EPA > 6 associated with 3× CV risk
- Ratio improves before absolute levels with supplementation

### 2. Omega-6:3 Ratio

| Range | Interpretation |
|-------|----------------|
| **1-2:1** | Optimal (evolutionary baseline) |
| **2-4:1** | Good, modern achievable target |
| **4-10:1** | Elevated, common in Western diet |
| **> 10:1** | High, pro-inflammatory |

**Historical context:**
- Hunter-gatherers: ~1-2:1
- Modern Western diet: 15-20:1
- Target: < 4:1

**Clinical significance:**
- Reflects dietary balance
- Higher ratios associated with:
  - Cardiovascular disease
  - Inflammatory conditions
  - Mood disorders
  - Autoimmune disease

### 3. Omega-3 Index

| Range | Interpretation |
|-------|----------------|
| **> 8%** | Optimal cardioprotection |
| **4-8%** | Intermediate risk |
| **< 4%** | High risk (90% of population) |

**Definition:** EPA + DHA as % of total RBC fatty acids

**Clinical significance:**
- Strong inverse correlation with sudden cardiac death
- Each 1% increase → ~10% CV risk reduction
- Target: > 8% for high-risk patients

## Clinical Applications

### Cardiovascular Risk Assessment
| Marker | Risk Stratification |
|--------|---------------------|
| **AA/EPA > 15** | High inflammatory risk |
| **Omega-3 index < 4%** | High arrhythmia risk |
| **Omega-6:3 > 10:1** | High atherosclerotic risk |

### Inflammatory Conditions
| Condition | Target AA/EPA |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Rheumatoid arthritis** | < 3 |
| **Inflammatory bowel** | < 3 |
| **Psoriasis** | < 5 |
| **General inflammation** | < 5 |

### Mood Disorders
| Condition | Target EPA |
|-----------|------------|
| **Depression** | High EPA formula (EPA > DHA) |
| **Bipolar** | High EPA, monitor closely |
| **ADHD** | Balanced EPA/DHA |

## Interpreting Ratios in Context

### When AA/EPA is High
| Cause | Solution |
|-------|----------|
| **Low EPA/DHA** | Increase fish oil |
| **High AA** | Reduce meat/eggs, increase EPA |
| **Poor conversion** | Use preformed EPA/DHA |

### When Omega-6:3 is High
| Cause | Solution |
|-------|----------|
| **High omega-6 intake** | Eliminate vegetable oils |
| **Low omega-3 intake** | Increase fatty fish |
| **Both** | Address both simultaneously |

### When Omega-3 Index is Low
| Cause | Solution |
|-------|----------|
| **No fish intake** | Start supplementation |
| **Poor absorption** | Check digestive function |
| **High oxidative stress** | Add antioxidants |

## Therapeutic Targets

### For High AA/EPA Ratio
| Intervention | Dose | Expected Change |
|--------------|------|-----------------|
| **EPA/DHA supplement** | 2-4 g/day | Ratio ↓ 50% in 3 months |
| **Fatty fish** | 3×/week | Gradual improvement |
| **Reduce AA sources** | Limit meat/eggs | Modest effect |

### For High Omega-6:3 Ratio
| Intervention | Target |
|--------------|--------|
| **Eliminate vegetable oils** | Soybean, corn, sunflower, safflower |
| **Use olive oil** | Primary cooking fat |
| **Increase omega-3** | 2-3 g EPA/DHA daily |
| **Eat whole foods** | Minimize processed foods |

### For Low Omega-3 Index
| Intervention | Dose | Timeline |
|--------------|------|----------|
| **EPA/DHA** | 2-4 g/day | Retest in 4 months |
| **Fatty fish** | 3-4×/week | Retest in 6 months |
| **Algae oil** | 2-3 g/day (vegan option) | Retest in 4 months |

## Monitoring Response

### Retest Timeline
| Parameter | Retest |
|-----------|--------|
| **Plasma fatty acids** | 8-12 weeks |
| **RBC (omega-3 index)** | 4-6 months |
| **AA/EPA ratio** | 3-4 months |

### Expected Improvements
| Intervention | AA/EPA Change | Timeline |
|--------------|---------------|----------|
| **2 g EPA/DHA** | 30-50% reduction | 3 months |
| **4 g EPA/DHA** | 50-70% reduction | 3 months |
| **Dietary changes only** | 20-30% reduction | 6 months |
