# Omega-3 Meta-Analysis CVD Benefit Confirmation

## Core Principle
**Two recent large-scale meta-analyses confirm omega-3 cardiovascular benefit** — establishing consistent efficacy across diverse populations and study designs, even in patients already receiving statin therapy.

## Meta-Analysis 1: Dong et al., 2024

### Study Details
- **Journal:** Nutrition Journal
- **Trials:** 12 RCTs
- **Participants:** ~100,000

### Findings
- Significant reduction in myocardial infarction
- Reduced cardiac death
- Reduced need for coronary revascularization

## Meta-Analysis 2: Dinu et al., 2024

### Study Details
- **Journal:** European Journal of Preventive Cardiology
- **Trials:** 18 RCTs
- **Participants:** 134,000+

### Key Finding
- Cardiovascular benefit **even in patients already receiving statins**
- Consistent across high-risk populations

## Evidence Strength
| Level | Support |
|-------|---------|
| RCT (Lok NEJM 2026) | 43% reduction, high-risk population |
| Meta-analysis 1 | ~100k participants, multiple outcomes |
| Meta-analysis 2 | 134k+ participants, statin-compatible |

## Clinical Implication
Omega-3 benefit is robust, reproducible, and additive to standard care (statins).
