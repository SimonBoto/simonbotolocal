# Therapeutic Ketosis — Clinical Applications

## Core Claim
**Ketogenic diets and therapeutic ketosis provide established benefits for drug-resistant epilepsy (50% seizure reduction in ~50% of patients) and emerging applications in neurodegenerative diseases, metabolic syndrome, and as cancer adjunctive therapy** — mechanisms include enhanced GABA synthesis, improved mitochondrial function, and alternative brain fuel.

## Evidence

### Established: Epilepsy
| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **History** | Since 1920s, pre-anticonvulsant era |
| **Efficacy** | 50% seizure reduction in ~50% of drug-resistant patients |
| **Mechanisms** | Enhanced GABA, reduced glutamate excitotoxicity, stabilized neuronal membranes |
| **Requirement** | Strict adherence, usually <20g carbs/day |

### Emerging Applications
| Condition | Evidence Level | Mechanism |
|-----------|---------------|-----------|
| **Alzheimer's** | Preliminary trials | Brain fuel alternative, reduced glucose hypometabolism |
| **Parkinson's** | Early research | Mitochondrial support, reduced oxidative stress |
| **Type 2 diabetes** | Strong evidence | Improved insulin sensitivity, weight loss |
| **Metabolic syndrome** | Established | Reduced triglycerides, increased HDL |
| **Cancer (adjunctive)** | Experimental | Warburg effect exploitation, anti-inflammatory |

### Therapeutic Ketosis Levels
| Level | BHB (mmol/L) | Application |
|-------|--------------|-------------|
| **Mild** | 0.5–1.0 | General health, cognitive enhancement |
| **Nutritional** | 1.0–3.0 | Weight loss, metabolic health |
| **Therapeutic** | 3.0–5.0 | Epilepsy, neurodegeneration |
| **High** | >5.0 | Supervised medical protocols only |

## Clinical Implication
- **Drug-resistant epilepsy** — ketogenic diet is evidence-based first-line alternative
- **Neurodegenerative diseases** — BHB provides alternative fuel when glucose metabolism impaired
- **Metabolic syndrome** — carbohydrate restriction induces ketosis, improves all markers
- **METHAP relevance:** Mild ketosis (BHB 1-3 mmol/L) may enhance metabolic flexibility; not required but potentially beneficial
