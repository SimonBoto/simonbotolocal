# Phase 3 Lipidomics Interpretation

## Core Claim
**Weeks 5-6 teach ratio-based fatty acid interpretation and lipid signaling — understanding that ratios matter more than absolutes, and that seed oil consumption represents a clinical insult with measurable biochemical consequences.**

## Evidence

### Ratio Gospel
| Ratio | Optimal | Clinical Significance |
|-------|---------|----------------------|
| **Omega-6:3** | 2-4:1 | Inflammation status |
| **AA:EPA** | 1-3:1 | Pro/anti-inflammatory balance |
| **Saturated:Unsaturated** | Context-dependent | Membrane fluidity |
| **Trans fat index** | <1% | Cardiovascular risk |

### Key Concepts
| Concept | Understanding |
|---------|--------------|
| **Ratios > absolutes** | Balance matters more than single values |
| **Membrane function** | Fatty acids determine membrane properties |
| **Inflammation signaling** | Eicosanoid production from fatty acid precursors |
| **Seed oil collapse** | Excess linoleic acid → metabolic dysfunction |

### Clinical Decision-Making
| Pattern | Interpretation | Intervention |
|---------|---------------|--------------|
| High omega-6:3 (>10:1) | Pro-inflammatory state | Omega-3 supplementation, reduce seed oils |
| High AA:EPA (>10:1) | Thrombotic/inflammatory risk | EPA/DHA, reduce arachidonic acid sources |
| Low DHA | Cognitive/cardiovascular risk | Algae/fish oil supplementation |
| Elevated trans fats | Processed food consumption | Dietary elimination |

### Membrane Repair Protocol
1. **Remove:** Seed oils, processed foods
2. **Add:** Omega-3s (EPA/DHA), monounsaturated fats
3. **Support:** Antioxidants (vitamin E, polyphenols)
4. **Monitor:** Repeat fatty acid panel

## Clinical Implication
- **Dietary assessment:** Fatty acid profile reveals true dietary intake
- **Inflammation management:** Ratios predict inflammatory state
- **Cardiovascular risk:** AA:EPA ratio correlates with CV events
- **Cognitive health:** DHA status linked to brain function
- **METHAP relevance:** Fatty acid ratios are key biomarkers in metabolic syndrome research; omega-6/3 balance central to inflammatory phenotype
