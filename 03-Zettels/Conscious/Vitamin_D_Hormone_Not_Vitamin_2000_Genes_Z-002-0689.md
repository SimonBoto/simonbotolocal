# Vitamin D Functions as Hormone Regulating 2000 Genes

## Core Claim
Vitamin D functions as a **steroid hormone** (not merely a vitamin) that regulates **over 2,000 genes** throughout the body, affecting immune function, metabolism, cellular differentiation, and numerous physiological processes.

## Hormone Functions

### Gene Regulation
| Scope | Impact |
|-------|--------|
| **2,000+ genes** | Directly or indirectly regulated |
| **~10% of genome** | Affected by vitamin D status |
| **Tissue distribution** | Receptors in most cell types |

### Physiological Systems
| System | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| **Immune system** | Innate and adaptive regulation |
| **Metabolism** | Insulin sensitivity, energy |
| **Cellular differentiation** | Cancer prevention |
| **Bone health** | Calcium homeostasis |
| **Cardiovascular** | Vascular function |

## Evidence

> "Vitamin D functions as a hormone regulating over 2,000 genes, with receptors present in most tissues; adequate levels support immune function, metabolic health, and cellular differentiation."
> — Vitamin D mechanistic literature
