# FRAX Output Thresholds — Treatment Decision Guidelines

## Core Principle
FRAX provides **10-year probability estimates** that guide pharmacologic intervention decisions through established risk thresholds.

## FRAX Output Metrics

### 10-Year Probabilities Calculated
1. **Major osteoporotic fracture**
   - Composite endpoint: hip,  spine, forearm, proximal humerus
   - Most commonly used for treatment decisions
   
2. **Hip fracture specifically**
   - Higher clinical consequence (mortality, disability)
   - Often drives treatment even when major fracture risk lower

## US National Osteoporosis Foundation (NOF) Thresholds

### Treatment Recommendation Guidelines
| Risk Category | Major Fracture | Hip Fracture | Action |
|--------------|----------------|--------------|---------|
| **Low** | < 10% | < 1% | Lifestyle counseling, reassess in 5–10 years |
| **Moderate** | 10–20% | 1–3% | Consider treatment if additional risk factors |
| **High** | > 20% OR | > 3% OR | Pharmacologic therapy indicated |
| | Prior fragility fracture | T-score ≤ -2.5 | |

### Automatic Treatment Indications (Regardless of FRAX)
- **T-score ≤ -2.5** at femoral neck or spine (osteoporosis)
- **Prior hip or vertebral fracture** (clinical or morphometric)
- **T-score -1.0 to -2.5** (osteopenia) with:
 - FRAX ≥ 3% hip fracture probability
 - FRAX ≥ 20% major fracture probability

## International Threshold Variations

### United Kingdom (NICE Guidelines)
| Age Group | Intervention Threshold (Major Fracture) |
|-----------|----------------------------------------|
| 50–70 years | > 10% |
| 70–80 years | > 15% |
| > 80 years | > 20% |

**Approach:** Age-dependent thresholds account for competing mortality.

### European Union
- **Variable:** Each country sets own thresholds
- **Range:** Generally 10–20% major fracture
- **Tendency:** Lower thresholds in Northern Europe, higher in Southern

### Japan
- **Major fracture:** ≥ 15%
- **Hip fracture:** ≥ 3%
- **Population-adjusted:** Lower average BMD than Western populations

## Applying Thresholds in Practice

### Clinical Scenario Decision Tree

#### Scenario 1: Low FRAX, No Other Risks
```
FRAX: 8% major, 0.5% hip
T-score: -1.8 (osteopenia)
Action: Lifestyle measures, recalculate FRAX in 5 years
```

#### Scenario 2: Borderline FRAX, Additional Risks
```
FRAX: 15% major, 2% hip (moderate)
Additional: Frequent falls, TBS low, recent steroid use
Action: Treat (additional factors push to high risk)
```

#### Scenario 3: High FRAX
```
FRAX: 25% major, 5% hip
T-score: -2.2 (osteopenia)
Action: Treat (exceeds thresholds despite osteopenia)
```

### Special Populations

#### Glucocorticoid Users
- **Lower threshold:** Treat if ≥ 10% major fracture
- **Duration:** >3 months prednisone ≥ 5mg/day
- **BMD:** Treat if T-score < -1.0 (not -2.5)

#### Postmenopausal Women (Very Low BMD)
- **T-score < -3.0:** Treat regardless of FRAX
- **Rationale:** FRAX may underestimate at extremes

#### Men
- **Same thresholds** as women apply
- **Less evidence** but same risk levels justify treatment

## Limitations in Threshold Application

### When to Override FRAX Thresholds

| Situation | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| **Recent fracture** (< 2 years) | Treat even if FRAX below threshold |
| **Multiple fractures** | Treat regardless of FRAX |
| **High fall risk** | Consider treatment at lower thresholds |
| **Very low TBS** (< 1.2) | Increase effective FRAX by ~30% |
| **Patient preference** | Shared decision-making for borderline |

### When FRAX Overestimates
- **High BMD** with multiple clinical risk factors
- **Recent smoking cessation** (still counted as current)
- **BMI > 30** with normal waist circumference

## Monitoring After Treatment Decision

### If Treated
- **Baseline DEXA:** Before starting therapy
- **Follow-up DEXA:** Every 1–2 years
- **Biochemical markers:** Optional (CTX, PINP) at 3–6 months

### If Not Treated
- **Reassess FRAX:** Every 2–5 years
- **Reassess sooner if:** New fracture, new risk factor, significant height loss

## Cost-Effectiveness Considerations

### Treatment Cost-Effective When
- **Hip fracture cost:** ~$40,000 USD acute + long-term care
- **Medication cost:** $500–2,000/year
- **Thresholds:** Generally cost-effective at 20% major fracture in US
- **Generic options:** Alendronate generic makes lower thresholds cost-effective

### Shared Decision-Making
For borderline cases (15–25% major fracture):
- Discuss patient values (fear of fracture vs. medication)
- Review medication risks (rare but serious)
- Consider 1–2 year trial with reassessment
