## Core Claim
**Teaching through guided discovery — letting trainees make errors, modeling correction, and explaining reasoning patterns — develops clinical reasoning rather than mere knowledge memorization, creating clinicians who can think independently rather than follow algorithms.**

## Evidence

### The Socratic Cycle
| Step | Action | Purpose |
|------|--------|---------|
| **1. Elicit Error** | Present case, let trainee reason to conclusion | Reveal thinking process |
| **2. Explore Reasoning** | Ask "How did you get there?" | Identify faulty assumptions |
| **3. Model Correction** | Demonstrate correct reasoning explicitly | Show proper approach |
| **4. Explain Pattern** | Connect error to pattern of thinking | Prevent future similar errors |

### Example Application
| Stage | Dialogue |
|-------|----------|
| **Elicit** | "OAT shows elevated lactate and pyruvate. What's happening?" |
| **Trainee** | "B12 deficiency?" |
| **Explore** | "Walk me through your thinking. What markers led you there?" |
| **Reveal** | Trainee confused lactate (mitochondrial) with methylmalonate (B12) |
| **Model** | "Lactate/pyruvate indicate mitochondrial dysfunction — think CoQ10, B1, oxygen" |
| **Pattern** | "You reached for a B-vitamin — correct instinct, wrong marker. Let's review the map." |

### Why Socratic vs. Didactic
| Method | Outcome | Limitation |
|--------|---------|------------|
| **Didactic** | Knowledge transfer | Cannot apply to novel situations |
| **Socratic** | Reasoning development | Transferable to any case |

### When to Use Direct Teaching
| Situation | Approach |
|-----------|----------|
| **Safety-critical** | Direct instruction first |
| **Foundational facts** | Didactic, then Socratic application |
| **Reasoning development** | Socratic primarily |
| **Error patterns** | Socratic with explicit pattern naming |

## Clinical Implication
- **Long-term competence:** Socratic-trained clinicians adapt better
- **Error prevention:** Understanding why prevents repetition
- **Confidence building:** Discovery creates ownership
- **Time investment:** Slower initially, faster long-term
- **METHAP relevance:** Research training benefits equally — guiding RAs to discover data patterns builds analytical competence vs. just following protocols
