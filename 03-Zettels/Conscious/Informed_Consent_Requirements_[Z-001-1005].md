# Informed Consent Requirements — Elements, Documentation, and Exceptions

## Overview

Informed consent is the process by which potential participants learn about a  study and voluntarily decide whether to enroll. It is both an ethical and legal requirement.

## Belmont Report Foundation

### Three Elements

**1. Information:**
- Disclosure of relevant information
- Research nature, procedures, risks, benefits

**2. Comprehension:**
- Subject understands disclosed information
- Appropriate for subject population

**3. Voluntariness:**
- Free from coercion and undue influence
- Can withdraw without penalty

## Required Elements (45 CFR 46.116)

### Basic Elements

**1. Statement of Purpose:**
- That study is research
- Purpose of research
- Expected duration
- Procedures to be followed
- Experimental procedures

**2. Risks or Discomforts:**
- Reasonably foreseeable risks
- Both physical and psychological
- Social and economic risks
- Probability of occurrence

**3. Benefits:**
- To subject (if any)
- To others/society
- No guarantee of benefit

**4. Alternative Procedures:**
- Non-research alternatives
- Standard care options
- Risks and benefits of alternatives

**5. Confidentiality:**
- Record confidentiality
- Who will have access
- Limits of confidentiality
- Possible regulatory inspections

**6. Compensation and Medical Treatment:**
- Whether compensation available
- Whether medical treatments available
- For research-related injury
- What they consist of

**7. Contact Information:**
- For questions about research
- For questions about rights
- Who to contact in emergency

**8. Voluntary Participation:**
- Participation is voluntary
- Refusal to participate no penalty
- Can discontinue at any time
- No penalty for withdrawal

### Additional Elements (When Appropriate)

**9. Unforeseeable Risks:**
- To subject or fetus/embryo
- If subject becomes pregnant

**10. Termination by Investigator:**
- Circumstances under which subject's participation may be terminated
- Without subject's consent

**11. Additional Costs:**
- Additional costs to subject
- From participation

**12. Consequences of Withdrawal:**
- Consequences of subject's decision to withdraw
- Procedures for orderly withdrawal

**13. New Findings:**
- Significant new findings
- That might affect willingness to continue
- Will be provided

**14. Number of Subjects:**
- Approximate number of subjects
- Involved in study

## Documentation of Consent

### Written Consent Form (45 CFR 46.117)

**Requirements:**
- Signed by subject or legally authorized representative
- Date
- Copy given to subject

**Form Contents:**
- Written statement of required elements
- Statement that study involves research
- Signatures
- Date

### Short Form (When Appropriate)

**Requirements:**
- Oral presentation of required elements
- Short form stating elements were presented orally
- Witness signature
- Subject signature
- Person obtaining consent signature

**Use When:**
- Subject unable to read long form
- Language barrier
- Cognitive limitations

### Electronic Consent

**Acceptable If:**
- Meets all regulatory requirements
- Properly authenticated
- Secure storage
- Audit trail

## Waiver or Alteration of Consent

### Criteria (45 CFR 46.116(d))

**IRB May Waive/Alter If:**

**1. Minimal Risk:**
- Research involves no more than minimal risk
- Could not be carried out practicably without waiver

**2. Waiver Does Not Adversely Affect Rights:**
- Subjects' rights/welfare not adversely affected

**3. Research Could Not Be PractICABLY Carried Out:**
- Without the waiver
- Essential for research validity

**4. Subjects Provided with Additional Information:**
- When appropriate
- After participation

### Common Waiver Scenarios

**Chart Reviews:**
- Retrospective
- De-identified data
- Minimal risk

**Observational Research:**
- Public settings
- No interaction
- Minimal risk

**Emergencies:**
- Exception from informed consent (21 CFR 50.24)
- Life-threatening situations
- No time to obtain consent
- Community consultation required

## Vulnerable Populations

### Children

**Parental Permission:**
- One or both parents (depending on risk)
- Informed consent standard
- For child's participation

**Child Assent:**
- Child's affirmative agreement
- Age-appropriate
- Not merely lack of objection
- Consider age, maturity, psychological state

**Age Guidelines:**
- 7-17: Generally assent required
- <7: Assent not required
- Emergencies: May waive assent

### Cognitively Impaired

**Legally Authorized Representative:**
- Surrogate decision-maker
- Advance directive
- Court-appointed guardian

**Assessment:**
- Capacity to consent
- May have fluctuating capacity
- Respect for remaining autonomy

### Prisoners

**Additional Protections:**
- Limited research categories
- Minimal risk preference
- No undue influence
- Independent advocacy

### Pregnant Women

**Additional Protections:**
- Risk to fetus considerations
- Father's consent when possible
- No inducements to terminate

## Process of Consent

### Timing

**Before:**
- Any research procedures
- Adequate time to consider
- Opportunity to ask questions

### Ongoing Process

**Not One-Time Event:**
- Continue throughout study
- New information disclosed
- Re-consent if significant changes

### Comprehension Assessment

**Methods:**
- Teach-back method
- Question and answer
- Quizzes (research)
- Observation of understanding

**Indicators of Understanding:**
- Asks appropriate questions
- Can explain key elements
- Decision consistent with values

## Cultural Considerations

### Language

**Requirement:**
- In language subject understands
- Professional translation
- Back-translation for accuracy

### Cultural Norms

**Community Consent:**
- Some cultures require community leader consent
- Supplement to individual consent
- Not substitute

**Gender Considerations:**
- Male head-of-household issues
- Female autonomy
- Cultural sensitivity

## Electronic and Digital Consent

### E-Consent Platforms

**Features:**
- Interactive multimedia
- Embedded comprehension checks
- Digital signatures
- Remote consent capability

**Validation:**
- FDA guidance
- 21 CFR Part 11 (if applicable)
- Data integrity

### Benefits

- Accessibility
- Multimedia enhancements
- Remote enrollment
- Audit trail

### Challenges

- Digital divide
- Technology literacy
- Security concerns
- Regulatory acceptance

## Documentation and Record Keeping

### Required Records

- Signed consent forms
- Version dates
- Any amendments
- Withdrawal documentation
- Adverse event reports

### Retention

**Duration:**
- Minimum 3 years post-study
- Often longer (institutional policy)
- Subject to audit

## Summary

**Core Requirements:**
- Full disclosure of information
- Subject comprehension
- Voluntary participation
- Documentation

**Special Populations:**
- Additional protections
- Surrogate consent when needed
- Assent from children

**Flexibility:**
- Waivers possible in limited circumstances
- Electronic consent acceptable
- Cultural adaptations appropriate

** Pearl:** Informed consent is a process, not just a form. Genuine understanding and voluntary participation are the goals, not just signatures on paper.
