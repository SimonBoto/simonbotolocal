## Core Claim
**APOE4 is the evolutionarily oldest APOE variant** — adapted to meat-rich, low-carbohydrate Paleolithic environments. Modern plant-based diets create an **evolutionary mismatch** that disadvantages APOE4 carriers, increasing their dementia risk.

## Evolutionary Context

### APOE Variants Timeline
| Variant | Emergence | Dietary Environment |
|---------|-----------|---------------------|
| **APOE4** | Oldest (~200,000+ years) | Hunter-gatherer, meat-rich |
| **APOE3** | Middle (~80,000 years) | Mixed diet |
| **APOE2** | Newest (~40,000 years) | Agricultural, plant-rich |

### APOE4 Adaptations
| Feature | Advantage in Paleolithic |
|---------|--------------------------|
| **Cholesterol metabolism** | Efficient with high meat intake |
| **Fat utilization** | Optimized for animal fats |
| **Inflammation response** | Strong for pathogen defense |

## The Mismatch

### Modern Environment
| Factor | Effect on APOE4 |
|--------|-----------------|
| **Low meat intake** | Nutrient deficiency |
| **High carbohydrate** | Impaired lipid metabolism |
| **Plant-based diets** | B12, iron, zinc deficiency |
| **Sedentary lifestyle** | Compounded metabolic stress |

### Result
- APOE4 carriers **thrived** in ancestral environment
- APOE4 carriers **suffer** in modern plant-rich environment
- **Dementia risk** increases with dietary mismatch

## Clinical Implication

### For APOE4 Carriers
- **Emphasize meat consumption** — matches evolutionary adaptation
- **Monitor B12, iron, zinc** — critical nutrients
- **Consider genotype** in dietary recommendations
