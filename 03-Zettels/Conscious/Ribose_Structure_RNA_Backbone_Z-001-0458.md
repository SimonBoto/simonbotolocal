## Core Claim
**Ribose is a 5-carbon pentose sugar (C₅H₁₀O₅) that forms the structural backbone of RNA and ATP, with its 2'-OH group distinguishing RNA from DNA and enabling catalytic functions** — all nucleotide coenzymes (NAD⁺, FAD, CoA) contain ribose, making it essential for metabolism.

## Evidence

### Structure
| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Formula** | C₅H₁₀O₅ |
| **Ring form** | Furanose (5-membered) |
| **2'-OH group** | Critical for RNA catalysis |

### Ribose vs Deoxyribose
| Feature | Ribose (RNA) | Deoxyribose (DNA) |
|---------|--------------|-------------------|
| 2' carbon | -OH | -H |
| Stability | Less stable | More stable |
| Function | Catalytic flexibility | Long-term storage |

### Biological Roles
| Role | Example |
|------|---------|
| **RNA backbone** | Phosphodiester bonds |
| **ATP** | Adenine + ribose + 3 phosphates |
| **Coenzymes** | NAD⁺/NADP⁺, FAD, CoA |
| **Second messengers** | cAMP, cGMP |

## Clinical Implication
- **Ribose synthesis:** Primarily via PPP (not directly from glucose)
- **Rapidly dividing tissues:** High ribose demand for nucleotide synthesis
- **Ribose supplements:** Mixed evidence for cardiac energetics/fibromyalgia
- **METHAP relevance:** High metabolic turnover increases nucleotide demand; B-vitamins support PPP enzymes for ribose synthesis
