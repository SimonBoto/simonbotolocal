## Core Claim
**Overall metabolic health** (insulin sensitivity, inflammation status, body composition) is **more predictive** of cardiovascular and all-cause mortality than isolated LDL cholesterol values in primary prevention populations.

## The Metabolic Health Advantage

### Why Metabolic Health Matters More
| Factor | Mechanism | CVD Impact |
|--------|-----------|------------|
| **Insulin sensitivity** | Determines how body handles nutrients | Root of dyslipidemia, inflammation |
| **Low inflammation** | Prevents endothelial damage | Directly reduces atherogenesis |
| **Healthy body composition** | Less visceral adiposity | Reduced inflammatory cytokines |
| **Good mitochondrial function** | Efficient energy metabolism | Less oxidative stress |

### Metabolic Health vs. LDL: The Evidence Hierarchy
| Predictor | Primary Prevention | Secondary Prevention |
|-----------|-------------------|----------------------|
| **Metabolic syndrome** | Strong predictor | Strong predictor |
| **Insulin resistance** | Strong predictor | Moderate predictor |
| **hs-CRP** | Strong predictor | Moderate predictor |
| **LDL cholesterol** | **Weak/no predictor** | Moderate predictor |

## Markers of Metabolic Health

### Clinical Assessment
| Marker | Optimal Range | What It Indicates |
|--------|---------------|-------------------|
| **Fasting insulin** | <10 μIU/mL | Insulin sensitivity |
| **HOMA-IR** | <2.0 | Insulin resistance index |
| **hs-CRP** | <1.0 mg/L | Systemic inflammation |
| **Waist circumference** | <94 cm (M), <80 cm (F) | Visceral adiposity |
| **Triglycerides/HDL ratio** | <2.0 | Insulin resistance proxy |
| **Fasting glucose** | <100 mg/dL | Glycemic control |

### The Metabolic Health Score
A person with:
- High LDL + **good metabolic health** = Lower risk
- Normal LDL + **poor metabolic health** = Higher risk

## Mechanism: Why Metabolic Health Dominates

### The Causal Chain
```
Poor metabolic health (insulin resistance, inflammation)
              ↓
    Endothelial dysfunction
              ↓
    LDL penetration and oxidation (in damaged endothelium)
              ↓
    Atherosclerotic plaque formation
              ↓
    Cardiovascular events
```

### Key Insight
- **Healthy endothelium** resists LDL penetration
- **Damaged endothelium** (from inflammation/IR) allows LDL entry
- **LDL oxidation** (in inflamed environment) creates pathology

## Evidence

> "The disconnect between LDL and mortality implies that metabolic dysfunction markers (elevated insulin, CRP, visceral adiposity) drive cardiovascular risk more than cholesterol transport alone."
> — Kip et al., 2024

## Clinical Application

### Risk Stratification Priority
1. **First:** Assess metabolic health (insulin, glucose, waist, TG/HDL)
2. **Second:** Measure inflammation (hs-CRP)
3. **Third:** Consider LDL in context of above
4. **Fourth:** Add Lp(a) for genetic risk

### Treatment Hierarchy
| Priority | Intervention | Rationale |
|----------|--------------|-----------|
| 1 | Improve insulin sensitivity (diet, exercise) | Root cause |
| 2 | Reduce inflammation (omega-3, weight loss) | Direct benefit |
| 3 | Optimize body composition | Metabolic improvement |
| 4 | Consider LDL modification | If still elevated after metabolic optimization |
