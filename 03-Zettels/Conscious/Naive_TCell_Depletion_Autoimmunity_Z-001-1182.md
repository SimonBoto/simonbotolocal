# Naive T-Cell Depletion in 

## The Shift in T-Cell Compartments

### Normal Distribution (Young Adult)
| Population | Percentage | Function |
|------------|------------|----------|
| **Naive T cells** | 40-50% | Respond to new antigens |
| **Memory T cells** | 30-40% | Rapid response to known pathogens |
| **Effector T cells** | 10-20% | Active immune response |

### Autoimmune Disease Pattern
| Population | Percentage | Change |
|------------|------------|--------|
| **Naive T cells** | 20-30% | **Reduced** |
| **Memory T cells** | 50-60% | **Expanded** |
| **Effector T cells** | 20-30% | **Increased** |

---

## s of Naive Cell Loss

### 1. Chronic Activation
- Persistent antigen stimulation (self-antigens)
- Naive cells differentiate to memory/effector
- Pool of naive cells consumed

### 2. Compensatory Proliferation
- Cell loss drives proliferation
- Thymic output insufficient to replenish
- Gradual depletion over years

### 3. Telomere Exhaustion
- Each division shortens telomeres
- Eventually reach senescence/apoptosis
- No longer capable of proliferation

### 4. Thymic Involution
- Thymus shrinks with age
- Reduced naive T-cell production
- Compounded by chronic activation

---

##  Consequences

### Impaired Response to New Antigens
**Vaccines:**
- Reduced antibody titers
- Shorter protection duration
- Higher failure rates

**Infections:**
- Slowed response to novel pathogens
- Prolonged illness
- Increased severity

### Expanded Memory/Effector Pool
**Autoreactive Clones:**
- Memory cells include autoreactive specificities
- Persist and reactivate
- Drive disease flares

**Limited Repertoire:**
- Reduced diversity
- Dominated by few clones
- Vulnerability to infections

---

## The Double Hit

**Autoimmune patients face:**
1. **Disease-related:** Chronic activation depletes naive cells
2. **Age-related:** Normal thymic involution

**Result:** Premature immune aging (10-20 years advanced)

---

## Assessment

### Flow Cytometry Markers
**Naive T cells:**
- CD45RA+, CCR7+, CD62L+
- CD28+ (co-stimulatory molecule)

**Memory T cells:**
- CD45RO+, variable CCR7
- May be CD28- (senescent)

### Clinical Correlation
- Lower naive percentages = worse outcomes
- Predicts infection risk
- May guide vaccine strategies

---

## Therapeutic Implications

### Immune Reconstitution
**Current Limitations:**
- No proven way to expand naive pool
- IL-7 therapy (experimental)
- Thymic regeneration (investigational)

### Vaccination Strategy
- **Timing:** Vaccinate when naive cells highest
- **Boosters:** More frequent may be needed
- **High-dose:** May improve responses

### Treatment Goals
- Control disease to reduce activation
- Preserve remaining naive cells
- Prevent further telomere loss
