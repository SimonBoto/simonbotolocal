> Alkaline phosphatase (ALP) is a family of zinc metalloenzymes with tissue-specific isoenzymes distributed across liver, bone, intestine, placenta, and tumors.

## Core Concept

**Key Process:**
```
ALP isoenzymes: Liver (hepatocytes) → Bone (osteoblasts) → Intestinal → Placental → Regan (tumor)
```

## Mechanism

1. **Zinc metalloenzyme** with tissue-specific post-translational modifications
2. **Catalytic function:** Removes phosphate groups at alkaline pH
3. **Tissue-specific forms** enable differential diagnosis of elevated ALP

## Isoenzyme Distribution

| Source | Cell Type | Clinical Relevance |
|--------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Liver** | Hepatocytes | Hepatobiliary disease marker |
| **Bone** | Osteoblasts | Bone turnover indicator |
| **Intestine** | Enterocytes | Postprandial elevation |
| **Placenta** | Syncytiotrophoblasts | Pregnancy elevation |
| **Regan** | Tumor cells | Malignancy marker (rare) |

## Clinical Significance

Tissue-specific distribution enables **differential diagnosis** of elevated serum ALP:
- Hepatic vs. bone source differentiation
- Guides appropriate diagnostic workup
