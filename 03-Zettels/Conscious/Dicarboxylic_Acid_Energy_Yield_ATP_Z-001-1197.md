# Dicarboxylic Acid Energy Yield — ATP Production Comparison

## Overview

Dicarboxylic acids (adipate, suberate, sebacate) are not just metabolic waste—they can be converted to energy. Understanding their ATP yield helps interpret their clinical significance.

---

## ATP Yield Calculations

### Adipate (C6) Complete Oxidation

**Pathway:** Adipate → 2 cycles β-oxidation → Succinyl-CoA + 2 Acetyl-CoA

| Step | Products | ATP Equivalent |
|------|----------|----------------|
| 2 FADH₂ | ETC | 3 ATP |
| 2 NADH | ETC | 5 ATP |
| 2 Acetyl-CoA | Krebs | 20 ATP |
| Succinyl-CoA | Krebs completion | 12 ATP |
| **Total** | | **~40 ATP** |

**Comparison:**
- Adipate (C6): ~40 ATP
- Glucose (C6): ~30-32 ATP
- **Adipate is 25-30% more efficient!**

---

### Suberate (C8) Complete Oxidation

**Pathway:** Suberate → 3 cycles β-oxidation → Succinyl-CoA + 3 Acetyl-CoA

| Step | ATP |
|------|-----|
| 3 FADH₂ | 4.5 ATP |
| 3 NADH | 7.5 ATP |
| 3 Acetyl-CoA | 30 ATP |
| Succinyl-CoA | 12 ATP |
| **Total** | **~54 ATP** |

---

### Sebacate (C10) Complete Oxidation

**Pathway:** Sebacate → 4 cycles β-oxidation → Succinyl-CoA + 4 Acetyl-CoA

| Step | ATP |
|------|-----|
| 4 FADH₂ | 6 ATP |
| 4 NADH | 10 ATP |
| 4 Acetyl-CoA | 40 ATP |
| Succinyl-CoA | 12 ATP |
| **Total** | **~68 ATP** |

---

## Efficiency Comparison

### Per Carbon

| Substrate | Carbons | ATP | ATP/Carbon |
|-----------|---------|-----|------------|
| **Adipate** | 6 | 40 | **6.7** |
| **Suberate** | 8 | 54 | **6.75** |
| **Sebacate** | 10 | 68 | **6.8** |
| **Glucose** | 6 | 31 | **5.2** |
| **Palmitate** | 16 | 106 | **6.6** |

**Key Finding:** Dicarboxylic acids are comparable to or better than glucose for energy!

---

## Why Urinary Excretion Then?

**Question:** If dicarboxylic acids yield so much energy, why are they excreted?

**Answer:** Rate limitation

```
Production Rate >> Metabolic Capacity
          ↓
Overflow to urine
```

**Factors Limiting :**
1. Dicarboxylic acid pathway enzyme capacity
2. CoA availability (shared with other pathways)
3. l capacity overwhelmed
4. Competing demands (glucose, other fatty acids)

---

## Clinical Interpretation

### Elevated Urinary Dicarboxylic Acids =

**Not:** "Wasting energy"

**But:** "Production exceeds metabolic capacity"

### Therapeutic Implications

**Goal:** Reduce production OR increase metabolism

**Reduce Production:**
- Improve β-oxidation (carnitine, riboflavin)
- Reduce fatty acid load (diet modification)
- Improve  sensitivity

**Increase Metabolism:**
- Support dicarboxylic pathway (B-vitamins)
- Optimize mitochondrial function
- Consider MCT oil ( bypasses some limitations)
