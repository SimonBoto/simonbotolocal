## Core Claim
**Methylmalonyl-CoA mutase produces succinyl-CoA, linking propionate metabolism to the Krebs cycle with a net energy yield of ~12 ATP per propionate molecule** — this B12-dependent reaction enables energy extraction from fiber, amino acids, and odd-chain fatty acids that would otherwise be lost.

## Evidence

### Reaction
```
L-Methylmalonyl-CoA → Succinyl-CoA
```
- **Type:** Intramolecular carbon rearrangement
- **Energy:** No oxidation state change
- **Product:** Direct Krebs cycle entry

### Energy Yield from Propionate
| Step | Input/Output | ATP Equivalent |
|------|--------------|----------------|
| **Investment** | 1 ATP (carboxylation) | -1 |
| **Return** | 1 GTP | +1 |
| **Return** | 1 FADH₂ | +1.5 |
| **Return** | 1 NADH | +2.5 |
| **Return** | Continuation through Krebs | +8 |
| **Net** | | **~12 ATP** |

### Efficiency
- **Propionate (3C):** ~12 ATP = 4 ATP/carbon
- **Glucose (6C):** ~30 ATP = 5 ATP/carbon
- **Propionate is efficient** for a 3-carbon molecule

## Clinical Implication
- **B12 deficiency blocks propionate energy extraction** — MMA accumulates instead
- **Anaplerotic function:** Replenishes Krebs cycle intermediates during fasting
- **Gut microbiome contribution:** Fiber → propionate → energy (B12-dependent)
- **METHAP relevance:** B12 status determines efficiency of energy extraction from fiber and protein
