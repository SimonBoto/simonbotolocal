## Core Claim
Initiating a low-carbohydrate or ketogenic diet produces **temporary withdrawal symptoms** including fatigue, irritability, mood changes, and carbohydrate cravings that typically **peak in the first week and resolve within 1-2 weeks** as the body adapts to fat metabolism.

## The Withdrawal Syndrome

### Common Symptoms
| Symptom | Peak Timing | Duration |
|---------|-------------|----------|
| **Fatigue/lethargy** | Days 3-7 | 1-2 weeks |
| **Irritability** | Days 3-7 | 1-2 weeks |
| **Mood changes** | Days 3-7 | 1-2 weeks |
| **Carbohydrate cravings** | Days 3-7 | 1-2 weeks |
| **Impaired concentration** | Days 3-7 | 1-2 weeks |
| **Headache** | Days 1-5 | 3-7 days |
| **Muscle cramps** | Days 3-10 | 1-2 weeks |

### Harvey 2019 RCT Findings
| Finding | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Onset** | 24-48 hours after carb restriction |
| **Peak severity** | Days 3-7 |
| **Resolution** | By day 14 for most symptoms |
| **Not hypoglycemia** | Blood glucose typically normal |

## Mechanism: The Metabolic Shift

### From Glucose to Fat
```
Days 1-2:
    ↓ Carbohydrate intake
    ↓ Liver glycogen depletion
    ↓ Blood glucose maintained (gluconeogenesis)
    ↓ Fat oxidation not yet efficient
    ↓ Energy gap → fatigue, cravings

Days 3-7 (Peak symptoms):
    ↓ Ketone production begins
    ↓ Brain still preferring glucose
    ↓ Mitochondrial fat oxidation ramping up
    ↓ Electrolyte shifts (natriuresis)
    ↓ Withdrawal symptoms peak

Days 7-14:
    ↓ Ketone levels rise
    ↓ Brain adapts to ketones
    ↓ Fat oxidation efficient
    ↓ Symptoms resolve
```

## Distinct from Hypoglycemia

| Feature | Carb Withdrawal | True Hypoglycemia |
|---------|-----------------|-------------------|
| **Blood glucose** | Normal (70-100 mg/dL) | Low (<70 mg/dL) |
| **Timing** | Days after starting diet | Hours after last meal |
| **Response to food** | Cravings, not relief | Immediate relief |
| **Mechanism** | Metabolic adaptation | Insulin/glucose mismatch |

## Clinical Implications

### Patient Counseling
| Message | Rationale |
|---------|-----------|
| "First 1-2 weeks are hardest" | Sets realistic expectations |
| "Symptoms are temporary" | Prevents early discontinuation |
| "Not dangerous, just uncomfortable" | Reduces anxiety |
| "Energy improves after week 2" | Motivates persistence |

### Management Strategies
| Symptom | Intervention |
|---------|--------------|
| **Fatigue** | Adequate salt, water, rest |
| **Cravings** | High-fat snacks, patience |
| **Headache** | Hydration, electrolytes |
| **Muscle cramps** | Magnesium, potassium |
| **Irritability** | Warn family, temporary |

## Evidence

> "Randomized clinical trial documents transient carbohydrate withdrawal syndrome with symptoms peaking days 3-7 and resolving by week 2; symptoms include decreased energy, impaired concentration, irritability, and intense carbohydrate cravings — distinct from true hypoglycemia."
> — Harvey et al., 2019
