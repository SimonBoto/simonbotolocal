## Core Principle

U.S. National Commission for the Protection of Human Subjects landmark report establishing three core ethical principles: Respect for Persons, Beneficence, and Justice. Foundation for U.S.  regulations (45 CFR 46, Common Rule).

## Three Core Principles

### 1. Respect for Persons
**Definition:** Individuals should be treated as autonomous agents, and persons with diminished autonomy are entitled to protection.

**Applications:**
- Informed consent (autonomous choice)
- Protection of vulnerable populations
- Privacy and confidentiality
- Right to withdraw

### 2. Beneficence
**Definition:** Obligation to maximize possible benefits and minimize possible harms.

**Applications:**
- Risk-benefit assessment
- Research design optimization
- Monitoring for adverse events
- Competent investigators

### 3. Justice
**Definition:** Fair distribution of research burdens and benefits.

**Applications:**
- Fair subject selection
- No exploitation of vulnerable groups
- Equitable access to research benefits
- Community representation in review

## Tuskegee Legacy

The Belmont Report was directly triggered by revelations about the Tuskegee Study:
- 600 Black men denied syphilis treatment
- Research continued after penicillin available (1940s)
- No informed consent
- Led to IRB requirement and informed consent regulations
