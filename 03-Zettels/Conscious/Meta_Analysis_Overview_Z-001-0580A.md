> **Definition:** A meta-analysis is a statistical technique for combining the results of multiple independent studies to produce a single, more precise estimate of the effect of an intervention or association.

## Source

[[Meta-Analysis_Methodology_-_From_Search_Strategy_L-001-0580_v4.0]]

## Core Principles

- **Systematic approach:** Comprehensive, reproducible literature search and selection criteria
- **Statistical synthesis:** Quantitative integration of effect sizes from multiple studies
- **Heterogeneity assessment:** Evaluation of variability in study results beyond chance
- **Bias evaluation:** Assessment of risk of bias in individual studies and across the body of evidence

## Key Components

1. **Protocol development:** Pre-specification of research questions, search strategy, inclusion criteria, and analysis plan
2. **Literature search:** Comprehensive search across multiple databases with appropriate filters
3. **Study selection:** Application of predefined inclusion/exclusion criteria
4. **Data extraction:** Standardized collection of study characteristics and outcomes
5. **Quality assessment:** Evaluation of methodological quality and risk of bias
6. **Statistical analysis:** Combination of effect sizes using appropriate models (fixed or random effects)
7. **Interpretation and reporting:** Consideration of heterogeneity, bias, and clinical relevance

## Purpose in Research

- Increases statistical power and precision of effect estimates
- Resolves uncertainty when individual studies provide conflicting results
- Identifies patterns and relationships across studies
- Provides evidence for clinical practice guidelines and health policy
- Guides future research by identifying gaps and inconsistencies
