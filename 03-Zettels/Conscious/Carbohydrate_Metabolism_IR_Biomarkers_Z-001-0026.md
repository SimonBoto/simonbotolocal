# Carbohydrate  & Insulin Resistance s

## Core Claim
**Carbohydrate metabolism disorders generate specific metabolite ** — glucose intermediates, lactate, and organic acids that quantify insulin resistance severity and metabolic dysfunction.

## Glycolysis-Gluconeogenesis Axis

### Key Intermediates as Biomarkers
| Metabolite | Normal Function | IR-Associated Dysfunction | Clinical Marker Value |
|------------|----------------|--------------------------|----------------------|
| **Glucose** | Primary fuel | Elevated fasting/post-prandial | Standard (but late indicator) |
| **Pyruvate** | Glycolysis endpoint | ↑ (glycolytic stress) | Early metabolic stress |
| **Lactate** | Anaerobic glycolysis | ↑ (mitochondrial dysfunction) | Tissue hypoxia marker |
| **Citrate** | Krebs cycle entry | Altered (mitochondrial impairment) | Energy metabolism |

## Lactate as Metabolic Stress Indicator

> **Andersen et al. (2013)** — Etiology and therapeutic approach to elevated lactate levels

### Lactate Elevation Mechanisms
| Mechanism | Cause | Clinical Context |
|-----------|-------|-----------------|
| **Tissue hypoxia** | O₂ delivery inadequate | Shock, sepsis, severe IR |
| **Mitochondrial dysfunction** | Impaired oxidative phosphorylation | Metabolic syndrome, IR |
| **Accelerated glycolysis** | Pyruvate production exceeds oxidation | High glucose, insulin resistance |
| **Impaired gluconeogenesis** | Lactate not recycled | Liver dysfunction |

### METHAP Relevance
**Elevated lactate** indicates:
- Metabolic stress preceding hyperglycemia
- Mitochondrial dysfunction (shared mechanism with adipic acid)
- Severity of insulin resistance

## Insulin Resistance Mechanisms

> **Savage et al. (2005)** — Mechanisms of insulin resistance in humans and possible links with inflammation

### Key Mechanisms (From Source)
1. **Lipid-induced insulin resistance** (lipotoxicity)
2. **Inflammatory cytokine interference** (TNF-α, IL-6)
3. **Mitochondrial dysfunction** (reduced oxidative capacity)
4. **Oxidative stress** (ROS impairment of insulin signaling)
5. **ER stress** (unfolded protein response)

### Metabolite Manifestations
| Mechanism | Biomarker Signature |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Lipotoxicity | ↑ Free fatty acids, ceramides, acylcarnitines |
| Inflammation | ↑ Kynurenine, quinolinic acid |
| Mitochondrial dysfunction | ↑ Adipic acid, lactate, succinate |
| Oxidative stress | ↑ 8-OHdG, 2-hydroxybutyrate |
| ER stress | ↑ Pyroglutamate |

## METHAP Trial Biomarker Panel

### Primary Endpoints
| Biomarker | Rationale | Measurement |
|-----------|-----------|-------------|
| **Adipic acid** | Mitochondrial dysfunction | Urine OA (primary) |
| **Lactate** | Metabolic stress | Plasma/serum |
| **Glucose** | Standard glycemic control | Fasting, OGTT |
| **Insulin** | Direct IR measure | Fasting, HOMA-IR |

### Secondary/Exploratory
- Pyruvate, citrate (glycolytic intermediates)
- 2-hydroxybutyrate (oxidative stress)
- Acylcarnitines (fatty acid oxidation)

## Clinical Application

### IR Severity Grading via Metabolites
| Grade | Glucose | Lactate | Adipic Acid | Interpretation |
|-------|---------|---------|-------------|----------------|
| Normal | <100 mg/dL | <1.0 mmol/L | Normal | No dysfunction |
| Mild IR | 100-125 mg/dL | 1.0-1.5 mmol/L | Slight ↑ | Early metabolic stress |
| Moderate IR | 126-140 mg/dL | 1.5-2.0 mmol/L | Elevated | Established dysfunction |
| Severe IR | >140 mg/dL | >2.0 mmol/L | Markedly ↑ | Advanced disease |
