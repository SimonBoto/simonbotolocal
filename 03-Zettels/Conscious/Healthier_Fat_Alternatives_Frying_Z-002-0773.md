## Core Claim
**Traditional animal fats (tallow, lard) and saturated plant fats (coconut oil) are superior to seed oils for high-heat cooking** — their chemical stability prevents oxidation and toxic aldehyde formation, while air frying and oven roasting eliminate the need for deep frying entirely.

## Fat Stability Hierarchy

### Best for High Heat (Frying)
| Fat | Smoke Point | Stability | Best Use |
|-----|-------------|-----------|----------|
| **Tallow (beef)** | 250°C | Excellent | Deep frying, searing |
| **Lard (pork)** | 190°C | Very good | Pan frying, roasting |
| **Duck fat** | 190°C | Very good | Pan frying, roasting |
| **Coconut oil** | 177°C | Excellent | Medium-heat frying |
| **Ghee/butter** | 250°C | Excellent | Sautéing, pan frying |

### Moderate Heat (Sautéing)
| Fat | Smoke Point | Notes |
|-----|-------------|-------|
| **Olive oil (extra virgin)** | 160-190°C | Good for light sauté |
| **Avocado oil** | 270°C | Neutral flavor, expensive |
| **Macadamia oil** | 210°C | Monounsaturated |

### Avoid for High Heat
| Fat | Why Avoid |
|-----|-----------|
| **Soybean oil** | High PUFA, oxidizes easily |
| **Corn oil** | High PUFA |
| **Canola oil** | Moderate PUFA, processing issues |
| **Sunflower oil** | Very high PUFA |
| **Safflower oil** | Very high PUFA |

## Cooking Method Alternatives

### Air Frying
| Advantage | Explanation |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Minimal oil** | 1-2 tsp vs cups |
| **No submersion** | Less oil absorption |
| **Crispy result** | Similar texture |
| **Lower AGEs** | Less browning |

### Oven Roasting
| Advantage | Explanation |
|-----------|-------------|
| **No frying** | Eliminates deep fry risk |
| **Even cooking** | Consistent results |
| **Less oil** | Coating vs submersion |
| **Batch cooking** | Efficient |

### Pan Frying (Shallow)
| Advantage | Explanation |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Less oil** | Than deep frying |
| **Control temperature** | Prevent overheating |
| **Stable fats work** | Tallow, lard, ghee |

## Practical Swaps

### For French Fries
| Instead Of | Use |
|------------|-----|
| **Deep fried in vegetable oil** | Air fried with 1 tsp olive oil |
| **Fast food fries** | Homemade baked wedges |
| **Frozen fries** | Fresh cut, oven roasted |

### For Hash Browns
| Instead Of | Use |
|------------|-----|
| **Pan fried in canola oil** | Pan fried in butter/ghee |
| **Restaurant hash browns** | Homemade with tallow |

### For Fried Chicken
| Instead Of | Use |
|------------|-----|
| **Deep fried in soybean oil** | Air fried or oven fried |
| **Fast food chicken** | Homemade with tallow/lard |

## Storage and Reuse

### Safe Practices
| Practice | Rationale |
|----------|-----------|
| **Use fresh oil** | Avoid accumulated oxidation |
| **Don't overheat** | Stay below smoke point |
| **Strain after use** | Remove food particles |
| **Store properly** | Cool, dark, sealed |
| **Limit reuse** | 2-3 times maximum |

### Danger Signs
| Indicator | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| **Dark color** | Discard |
| **Thick/sticky** | Discard |
| **Foaming** | Discard |
| **Off smell** | Discard |
| **Smoke at lower temp** | Discard |

## Clinical Recommendations

### For Patients
| Priority | Action |
|----------|--------|
| **1** | Eliminate deep-fried restaurant foods |
| **2** | Use stable fats at home |
| **3** | Prefer air frying or baking |
| **4** | Never reuse oil multiple times |

### Shopping Guide
| Buy | Avoid |
|-----|-------|
| **Tallow, lard** | Vegetable oil blends |
| **Coconut oil** | Soybean, corn oil |
| **Olive oil (EVOO)** | Canola oil |
| **Butter, ghee** | Margarine, spreads |
