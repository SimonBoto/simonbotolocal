## Mechanism

**The mevalonate pathway is metabolically expensive — requiring 2 NADPH and 2 ATP per IPP molecule (42 ATP equivalents total opportunity cost) — with cholesterol synthesis consuming approximately 250+ ATP equivalents per molecule, explaining the tight regulatory control and evolutionary pressure to maintain efficient cholesterol recycling through enterohepatic circulation.**

## Mechanism

**The mevalonate pathway is metabolically expensive — requiring 2 NADPH and 2 ATP per IPP molecule (42 ATP equivalents total opportunity cost) — with cholesterol synthesis consuming approximately 250+ ATP equivalents per molecule, explaining the tight regulatory control and evolutionary pressure to maintain efficient cholesterol recycling through enterohepatic circulation.**

## Evidence

### Energy Requirements per IPP
| Input | Quantity | ATP Equivalent | Purpose |
|-------|----------|----------------|---------|
| **Acetyl-CoA** | 3 | ~30 ATP (if oxidized) | Carbon skeleton |
| **NADPH** | 2 | ~10 ATP | Reduction (HMG-CoA reductase) |
| **ATP** | 2 | 2 ATP | Phosphorylations |
| **Total** | — | **~42 ATP equivalents** | Per IPP molecule |

### Cholesterol Synthesis Cost
| Component | Requirement | ATP Equivalent |
|-----------|-------------|----------------|
| **6 IPP molecules** | 6 × 42 | ~252 ATP |
| **Condensation steps** | Enzymatic | Minimal |
| **Demethylation** | NADPH required | ~10 ATP |
| **Total per cholesterol** | — | **~250+ ATP equivalents** |

### Comparative Metabolic Costs
| Product | ATP Investment | Function |
|---------|----------------|----------|
| **1 Cholesterol** | ~250 ATP | Membranes, hormones |
| **1 Palmitate (C16)** | ~100 ATP | Energy storage |
| **1 Glucose (gluconeogenesis)** | ~6 ATP | Energy/fuel |
| **1 Urea** | ~4 ATP | Nitrogen excretion |

### Why So Expensive?
| Factor | Explanation |
|--------|-------------|
| **Irreversible steps** | Committed resources |
| **Reductive synthesis** | NADPH-intensive |
| **Multiple enzymes** | Maintenance costs |
| **Regulatory overhead** | Control mechanisms |

### Energy Recovery via Recycling
| Pathway | Recovery | Efficiency |
|---------|----------|------------|
| **Enterohepatic circulation** | 95% bile acid recycling | High |
| **Cholesterol esterification** | Storage for later use | Moderate |
| **Steroid hormone synthesis** | Functional conversion | Variable |

### Clinical Implications of High Cost
| Observation | Explanation |
|-------------|-------------|
| **Tight regulation** | Energy investment requires control |
| **Dietary preference** | Absorb cholesterol when available |
| **Statin efficacy** | Small inhibition → large effect |
| **Circadian rhythm** | Synthesis peaks at night (energy surplus) |

### Fasting vs Fed State
| State | Cholesterol Synthesis | Mechanism |
|-------|----------------------|-----------|
| **Fed (high insulin)** | ↑ Active | SREBP-2 activated |
| **Fasted (low insulin)** | ↓ Inhibited | AMPK activated |
| **Energy surplus** | ↑ Increased | mTOR → SREBP-2 |
| **Energy deficit** | ↓ Conserved | AMPK inhibition |

## Clinical Implication
- **Evolutionary pressure:** High cost explains efficient recycling systems
- **Dietary cholesterol:** Preferred over synthesis when available
- **Statin timing:** Night dosing matches peak synthesis
- **Metabolic syndrome:** Dysregulated energy sensing alters pathway
- **METHAP relevance:** Understanding energy cost informs metabolic optimization strategies and cholesterol management approaches
