# Fatty Acid Ratios Clinical Interpretation

## Overview
Fatty acid ratios provide integrated s of dietary intake, inflammatory status, and metabolic health.

## The AA/EPA Ratio (Inflammation Index)

| Range | Interpretation | Clinical Action |
|-------|----------------|-----------------|
| **< 3** | Optimal | Maintain current status |
| **3-6** | Mild inflammation | Increase omega-3, reduce omega-6 |
| **6-10** | Moderate inflammation | Targeted EPA/DHA supplementation |
| **> 10** | High inflammation | High-dose EPA, address sources |

**Clinical significance:** Arachidonic acid (pro-inflammatory) vs. eicosapentaenoic acid (anti-inflammatory)

## Omega-6/Omega-3 Ratio

| Range | Interpretation |
|-------|----------------|
| **1-2:1** | Evolutionary baseline |
| **2-4:1** | Target range |
| **5-10:1** | Modern Western diet |
| **> 15:1** | Pro-inflammatory risk |

**Current population average:** ~20:1

## Omega-3 Index

| Level | Status | Risk |
|-------|--------|------|
| **> 8%** | Optimal | Lowest cardiovascular risk |
| **4-8%** | Intermediate | Moderate risk |
| **< 4%** | Deficient | High risk |

**Composition:** (EPA + DHA) / total erythrocyte fatty acids × 100

## Additional Ratios

| Ratio | Optimal | Clinical Meaning |
|-------|---------|------------------|
| **Chol/HDL** | < 4 | Cardiometabolic risk |
| **HDL/TG** | > 1 | Insulin sensitivity proxy |
| **LDL/ApoB** | > 1.2 | Particle size quality |

## Interpretation Strategy
1. **Assess AA/EPA first** — Inflammation status
2. **Check omega-6:3** — Dietary balance
3. **Verify omega-3 index** — Tissue status
4. **Triangulate with symptoms** — Clinical correlation

## Clinical Pearl
> "Ratios matter, not absolutes. A patient can have 'normal' absolute levels but dangerous ratios."
