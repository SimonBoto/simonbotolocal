# ADA-AHA Collaboration — Know  by Heart™ Initiative

> The American Diabetes Association and American Heart Association jointly launched the "Know Diabetes by Heart™" initiative to address the leading cause of death in people with Type 2 diabetes—cardiovascular disease—through education, research, and community outreach.

## Collaboration Overview

**Partners:**
- **American Diabetes Association (ADA)** — Diabetes expertise
- **American Heart Association (AHA)** — Cardiovascular expertise

**Launch:**
- Joint initiative combining strengths
- Addresses overlapping patient populations
- Shared goal: Reduce CVD in diabetes

## Know Diabetes by Heart™ Program

**Mission:**
- Reduce cardiovascular deaths in T2DM
- Increase awareness of CVD-diabetes link
- Improve management of both conditions
- Address health disparities

**Activities:**
1. **Community Grants:** Fund local education programs
2. **Healthcare Provider Education:** CVD risk management
3. **Patient Resources:** Educational materials
4. **Awareness Campaigns:** Public messaging

**Target Populations:**
- High-risk communities
- Black and Latino populations (higher T2DM/CVD rates)
- Underserved areas

## Rationale for Collaboration

** Connection:**
- T2DM increases CVD risk 2-4 fold
- 70% of T2DM patients die from CVD
- Shared risk factors (obesity, hypertension, dyslipidemia)
- Integrated management needed

**Synergies:**
- ADA: Glucose management expertise
- AHA: Blood pressure, lipid management
- Combined: Comprehensive risk reduction

## METHAP Relevance

** Connection:**
- MetS combines diabetes + CVD risk factors
- METHAP targets both simultaneously
- Mg + D + K2 address both aspects
- Comprehensive approach aligned with initiative

**Research Implications:**
- Validates multi-system approach
- Supports metabolic intervention
- Early detection/prevention focus
- Beyond glucose-only management
