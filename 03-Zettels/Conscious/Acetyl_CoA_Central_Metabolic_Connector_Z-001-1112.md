## Core Claim
**Acetyl-CoA is the central metabolic hub connecting carbohydrate, fat, and protein catabolism to the Krebs cycle, with its fate determined by metabolic state: energy production (fed state), ketogenesis (fasting), or biosynthesis (anabolic conditions)** — compartmentalization (mitochondrial vs cytosolic) regulates these divergent pathways.

## Evidence

### Sources of Acetyl-CoA
| Macronutrient | Pathway | Key Enzyme |
|---------------|---------|------------|
| **Carbohydrate** | Glycolysis → Pyruvate → Acetyl-CoA | Pyruvate dehydrogenase |
| **Fat** | Beta-oxidation | Acyl-CoA dehydrogenases |
| **Protein** | Amino acid catabolism | Various dehydrogenases |

### Fates of Acetyl-CoA
| Condition | Fate | Pathway |
|-----------|------|---------|
| **Fed state** | Krebs cycle → ATP | Energy production |
| **Fasting (liver)** | Ketogenesis | BHB, acetoacetate production |
| **Excess calories** | Lipogenesis | Fatty acid synthesis |
| **Anabolic needs** | Biosynthesis | Cholesterol, acetylcholine |

### Compartmentalization
| Location | Primary Function |
|----------|------------------|
| **Mitochondrial** | Krebs cycle entry, ketogenesis |
| **Cytosolic** | Lipogenesis, cholesterol synthesis |

## Clinical Implication
- **Pyruvate dehydrogenase deficiency** blocks glucose oxidation → lactic acidosis
- **Fatty acid oxidation defects** impair acetyl-CoA from fats → hypoketotic hypoglycemia
- **Metabolic flexibility** requires efficient acetyl-CoA routing between pathways
- **METHAP relevance:** High-dose nutrients support PDH complex (B1, B2, B3, B5, lipoic acid) for optimal acetyl-CoA production
