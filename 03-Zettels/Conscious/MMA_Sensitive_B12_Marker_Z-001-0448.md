# MMA — The Sensitive B12 Marker

> Methylmalonic acid (MMA) is the most sensitive and specific marker of functional B12 status, elevating before serum B12 drops and reflecting tissue/cellular B12 adequacy.

## Reference Range

| Level | Status |
|-------|--------|
| <0.27 | Optimal |
| 0.27-0.40 | Borderline |
| >0.40 | Deficient |

## Advantages

**Most Sensitive:**
- Elevates before serum B12 drops
- Early indicator of deficiency

**Specific for B12:**
- Not affected by  status
- Differentiates from folate deficiency

**Functional Marker:**
- Reflects tissue status
- Cellular/l B12

## When to Use

- Borderline serum B12 (200-400)
- Symptoms with normal B12
- Confirming deficiency
- Monitoring treatment

##  Pearl

**Elevated MMA + Normal B12 =** Functional deficiency, tissue deficiency
