## Core Claim
**Higher meat consumption eliminates the increased genetic dementia risk in APOE4 carriers** — reducing dementia risk by 55% (HR 0.45) in people with the APOE4 allele who consume relatively high amounts of meat.

## The Evidence

### Norgren 2026 Study
| Finding | Result |
|---------|--------|
| **Population** | APOE4 carriers (3/4 and 4/4 genotypes) |
| **Meat intake** | Higher consumption |
| **Dementia risk** | 55% reduction (HR 0.45, P=0.04) |
| **Cognitive decline** | Reduced (P=0.01) |

### Key Insight
- APOE4 carriers have **~3x higher baseline dementia risk**
- High meat intake **normalizes this risk**
- Effect is **genotype-specific**

## Why This Matters

### Precision Nutrition
| Genotype | Dietary Implication |
|----------|---------------------|
| **APOE4 carriers** | May require meat for brain health |
| **General population** | More dietary flexibility |

### Challenge to Dogma
- **Plant-based diets** may harm APOE4 carriers
- **One-size-fits-all** nutrition fails
- **Genetics** determines optimal diet
