## Core Claim
**Mitochondrial β-oxidation sequentially cleaves 2-carbon acetyl-CoA units from fatty acids through four enzymatic steps (oxidation, hydration, oxidation, thiolysis) — generating FADH₂ and NADH for ATP production — with carnitine-dependent transport required for long-chain fatty acids, and impairment leading to dicarboxylic acid formation via compensatory ω-oxidation.**

## Evidence

### The Four Steps of β-Oxidation
| Step | Reaction | Enzyme | Product | ATP Yield |
|------|----------|--------|---------|-----------|
| **1** | Acyl-CoA + FAD → trans-Δ²-Enoyl-CoA | Acyl-CoA dehydrogenase | FADH₂ | ~1.5 ATP |
| **2** | trans-Δ²-Enoyl-CoA + H₂O → L-3-Hydroxyacyl-CoA | Enoyl-CoA hydratase | — | — |
| **3** | L-3-Hydroxyacyl-CoA + NAD⁺ → 3-Ketoacyl-CoA | Hydroxyacyl-CoA dehydrogenase | NADH | ~2.5 ATP |
| **4** | 3-Ketoacyl-CoA + CoA → Acyl-CoA(n-2) + Acetyl-CoA | Thiolase | Acetyl-CoA | ~10 ATP |

### Energy Yield Example (Palmitate C16:0)
| Calculation | Result |
|-------------|--------|
| Cycles needed | 7 cycles |
| Acetyl-CoA produced | 8 molecules |
| FADH₂ produced | 7 molecules (~10.5 ATP) |
| NADH produced | 7 molecules (~17.5 ATP) |
| Acetyl-CoA oxidation | 8 × 10 ATP = 80 ATP |
| **Total gross** | **~108 ATP** |
| Activation cost | -2 ATP |
| **Net yield** | **~106 ATP** |

### Compartmentalization
| Fatty Acid Chain | Primary Location | Transport Required |
|------------------|------------------|-------------------|
| **Short-chain (<C8)** | Mitochondria | Direct diffusion |
| **Medium-chain (C8-C12)** | Mitochondria | Some carnitine-dependent |
| **Long-chain (>C12)** | Mitochondria | Carnitine shuttle essential |
| **Very long-chain (>C22)** | Peroxisomes | Carnitine for shortened products |

### Clinical Correlations
| Condition | Effect on β-Oxidation | Biomarker |
|-----------|----------------------|-----------|
| **Carnitine deficiency** | Transport block | Elevated dicarboxylic acids |
| **MCAD deficiency** | Medium-chain block | C8-C10 acylcarnitines ↑ |
| **Insulin resistance** | Impaired mitochondrial function | Adipate ↑ |
| **Fasting/ketosis** | Increased flux | Normal adaptation |

## Clinical Implication
- **Primary energy source:** Fatty acids provide most ATP during fasting/exercise
- **Carnitine dependence:** Long-chain fatty acids require functional shuttle
- **Impairment markers:** Dicarboxylic acids indicate backup pathway activation
- **Insulin resistance link:** Mitochondrial dysfunction impairs β-oxidation
- **METHAP relevance:** β-oxidation efficiency is core to metabolic flexibility assessment
