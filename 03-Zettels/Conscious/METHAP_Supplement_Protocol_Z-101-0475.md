# METHAP Supplement Protocol

## Overview
12-week targeted supplementation as part of interventional sub-study in insulin-resistant participants.

## Rationale
Address micronutrient deficiencies and metabolic dysregulation common in insulin resistance through evidence-based supplementation.

## Supplement Stack

### 1. 3 (Cholecalciferol)
| Attribute | Specification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Rationale** | Insulin sensitivity, beta-cell function, anti-inflammatory |
| **Typical dose** | 2000-4000 IU/day (individualized based on baseline 25-OH-D) |
| **EOΦ approval** | [To be specified] |
| **Timing** | Morning, with fat-containing meal |

**Safety considerations:**
- Monitor 25-OH-D levels at baseline and 12 weeks
- Upper limit: 4000 IU/day without monitoring
- Toxicity rare but possible at very high doses

### 2. Magnesium (as glycinate or citrate)
| Attribute | Specification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Rationale** | Glucose , insulin receptor signaling, ATP production |
| **Typical dose** | 200-400 mg elemental Mg/day |
| **EOΦ approval** | [To be specified] |
| **Timing** | Evening (may improve sleep) |

**Safety considerations:**
- Can cause diarrhea (dose-dependent)
- Separate from antibiotics by 2-4 hours
- Caution with renal impairment

### 3. Omega-3 Fatty Acids (EPA/DHA)
| Attribute | Specification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Rationale** | Anti-inflammatory, improve lipid profile, cell membrane fluidity |
| **Typical dose** | 1-2 g combined EPA+DHA/day |
| **EOΦ approval** | [To be specified] |
| **Timing** | With meals (reduces fishy aftertaste) |

**Safety considerations:**
- Potential bleeding risk at high doses (caution with anticoagulants)
- Choose molecularly distilled (mercury-free) products
- May lower blood pressure

### 4. Glutamine
| Attribute | Specification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Rationale** | Gut barrier integrity, metabolic fuel for enterocytes |
| **Typical dose** | 5-10 g/day |
| **EOΦ approval** | [To be specified] |
| **Timing** | Between meals or before bed |

**Safety considerations:**
- Generally well-tolerated
- Caution with anticonvulsant medications
- May affect ammonia metabolism in severe liver disease

### 5. Vitamin C (Ascorbic Acid)
| Attribute | Specification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Rationale** | Antioxidant, collagen synthesis, carnitine synthesis |
| **Typical dose** | 500-1000 mg/day |
| **EOΦ approval** | [To be specified] |
| **Timing** | Divided doses (water-soluble) |

**Safety considerations:**
- High doses may cause GI upset
- Increases oxalate excretion (relevant to study s)
- May interfere with certain lab tests

See: [[Ascorbate_Oxalate_Measurement_Interference_Z-101-0470]

### 6. Probiotics (Multi-strain)
| Attribute | Specification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Rationale** | Gut microbiome modulation, metabolic endotoxemia reduction |
| **Typical dose** | 10-50 billion CFU/day |
| **EOΦ approval** | [To be specified] |
| **Timing** | Morning, 30 min before breakfast |

**Safety considerations:**
- Generally safe
- Caution in immunocompromised individuals
- Refrigeration may be required

## Drug-Supplement Interactions

| Supplement | Interacting Drug | Mechanism | Management |
|------------|------------------|-----------|------------|
| Vitamin D | Thiazide diuretics | ↑ Hypercalcemia risk | Monitor calcium |
| Magnesium | Fluoroquinolones | ↓ Antibiotic absorption | Separate by 2-4h |
| Magnesium | Bisphosphonates | ↓ Drug absorption | Separate by 2h |
| Omega-3 | Warfarin | ↑ Bleeding risk | Monitor INR |
| Omega-3 | Aspirin | ↑ Bleeding risk | Caution at high doses |
| Glutamine | Anticonvulsants | ↓ Drug efficacy | Monitor levels |
