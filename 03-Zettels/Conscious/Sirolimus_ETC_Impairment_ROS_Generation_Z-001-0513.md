# Sirolimus Impairs ETC Function and Increases ROS Generation

**Core :** Sirolimus reduces expression of electron transport chain components, leading to inefficient electron transfer and reactive oxygen species (ROS) production.

## mTORC1 and ETC Maintenance

**Normal Regulation:**
- mTORC1 promotes translation of ETC subunits
- Coordinates Complex I-V protein levels
- Maintains optimal ETC stoichiometry

**ETC Complexes Affected:**
- Complex I (NADH dehydrogenase)
- Complex II (Succinate dehydrogenase)
- Complex III (Cytochrome bc1)
- Complex IV (Cytochrome c oxidase)
- Complex V (ATP synthase)

## Sirolimus-Induced ETC Impairment

**Reduced Protein Synthesis:**
- Decreased nuclear-encoded ETC subunits
- Impaired assembly of functional complexes
- Reduced ETC efficiency

**Consequences:**
1. **Decreased ATP production**
2. **Electron leakage** from impaired complexes
3. **Superoxide generation** (O₂⁻)
4. **Oxidative damage cascade**

## ROS Generation Mechanism

**Electron Leakage Sites:**
- **Complex I:** FMN and Q sites
- **Complex III:** Qo site (especially)

**Superoxide Formation:**
```
Leaked electrons + O₂ → O₂⁻ (superoxide)
        ↓
Superoxide dismutase (SOD)
        ↓
H₂O₂ (hydrogen peroxide)
        ↓
Hydroxyl radical (•OH) via Fenton reaction
```

## Targets of Oxidative Damage

| Target | Damage Type | Consequence |
|--------|-------------|-------------|
| **l membranes** | Lipid peroxidation | Loss of membrane integrity |
| **ETC proteins** | Oxidation | Further ETC impairment |
| **mtDNA** | Mutations | Heritable  defects |
| **Cardiolipin** | Oxidation | Cytochrome c release, apoptosis |

## Amplifying Cycle

```
ETC Impairment → ROS Generation
        ↑                ↓
        └──── Mitochondrial ────┘
              Damage
                ↓
        More ETC Impairment
```

**Result:** Progressive mitochondrial dysfunction

## mPTP Opening

**ROS + Ca²⁺ → mPTP Opening:**
- Mitochondrial permeability transition pore
- Inner membrane depolarization
- Matrix swelling
- Outer membrane rupture
- Cytochrome c release
- Apoptosis activation

## Clinical Manifestations

- **Fatigue** (reduced ATP)
- **Muscle weakness** (oxidative fiber damage)
- **Cardiac dysfunction** (cardiomyocyte vulnerability)
- **Accelerated aging phenotypes** (oxidative stress)
