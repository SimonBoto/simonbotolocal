# Isoprenoids and Terpenes Overview

## Core Claim
**Isoprenoids (terpenoids) constitute a vast family of biomolecules built from five-carbon isoprene units — ranging from small volatile monoterpenes to large tetraterpenes like carotenoids — serving essential functions as hormones, pigments, vitamins, defense compounds, and pharmaceutical precursors in all living organisms.**

## Evidence

### Classification by Size
| Class | Isoprene Units | Carbons | Examples | Functions |
|-------|----------------|---------|----------|-----------|
| **Hemiterpenes** | 1 | C5 | Isoprene | Plant signaling |
| **Monoterpenes** | 2 | C10 | Limonene, pinene, menthol | Aromas, defense |
| **Sesquiterpenes** | 3 | C15 | Farnesene, bisabolol, caryophyllene | Scents, anti-inflammatory |
| **Diterpenes** | 4 | C20 | Taxol, gibberellins | Cancer drugs, plant hormones |
| **Triterpenes** | 6 | C30 | Squalene, cholesterol | Membranes, precursors |
| **Tetraterpenes** | 8 | C40 | β-carotene, lycopene | Pigments, antioxidants |

### Biological Functions
| Category | Examples | Role |
|----------|----------|------|
| **Hormones** | Gibberellins, brassinosteroids | Plant growth regulation |
| **Pigments** | Carotenoids, chlorophyll | Photosynthesis, coloration |
| **Vitamins** | A, K, E | Vision, clotting, antioxidant |
| **Defense** | Pyrethrins, nicotine | Anti-herbivore compounds |
| **Medicine** | Taxol, artemisinin | Cancer, antimalarial |
| **Signaling** | Farnesyl-PP, geranylgeranyl-PP | Protein prenylation |

### Biosynthetic Pathways
| Pathway | Organisms | Products |
|---------|-----------|----------|
| **Mevalonate** | Animals, fungi, some bacteria | Cholesterol, steroid hormones |
| **MEP (non-mevalonate)** | Plants, bacteria, apicomplexa | Carotenoids, terpenes |
| **Both** | Plants | Diverse isoprenoid portfolio |

### Human-Relevant Isoprenoids
| Compound | Class | Source | Function |
|----------|-------|--------|----------|
| **Cholesterol** | Triterpene | Synthesized | Membranes, hormones |
| **CoQ10** | Benzoquinone | Synthesized | Mitochondrial ETC |
| **Vitamin K2** | Diterpene | Bacteria/synthesized | Clotting, bone |
| **Vitamin A** | Tetraterpene | Diet (β-carotene) | Vision, immune |
| **Dolichol** | Polyprenol | Synthesized | Protein glycosylation |

### Pharmaceutical Applications
| Drug/Compound | Source Isoprenoid | Therapeutic Use |
|---------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| **Taxol (paclitaxel)** | Diterpene | Cancer chemotherapy |
| **Artemisinin** | Sesquiterpene lactone | Malaria treatment |
| **Ginkgolides** | Diterpene | Cognitive enhancement |
| **Cannabinoids** | Meroterpenes | Pain, inflammation |

## Clinical Implication
- **Diversity:** Single pathway (mevalonate) produces thousands of distinct molecules
- **Therapeutic potential:** Plant terpenes are major drug discovery source
- **Statin side effects:** Blocking mevalonate affects all isoprenoid synthesis
- **Nutrition:** Carotenoids require dietary intake (cannot synthesize)
- **METHAP relevance:** Understanding isoprenoid diversity explains broad statin effects and highlights CoQ10 supplementation necessity
