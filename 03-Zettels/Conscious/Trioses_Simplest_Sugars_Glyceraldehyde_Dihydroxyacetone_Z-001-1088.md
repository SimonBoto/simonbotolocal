# Trioses Simplest Sugars Glyceraldehyde Dihydroxyacetone

## Overview
Trioses are the simplest monosaccharides—three-carbon sugars that serve as fundamental building blocks and metabolic intermediates.

## The Two Primary Trioses

### 1. Glyceraldehyde
- **Type:** Aldotriose (contains aldehyde group)
- **Formula:** C₃H₆O₃
- **Structure:** 3-carbon chain with aldehyde at C1
- **Role:** Key intermediate in  and photosynthesis

### 2. Dihydroxyacetone
- **Type:** Ketotriose (contains ketone group)
- **Formula:** C₃H₆O₃
- **Structure:** 3-carbon chain with ketone at C2
- **Role:** Isomer of glyceraldehyde; glycolytic intermediate

## Stereochemistry
- **D-glyceraldehyde:** Reference molecule for D-sugar configuration
- **Optical activity:** Glyceraldehyde has enantiomers (D- and L-)
- **Dihydroxyacetone:** Optically inactive (symmetrical molecule)

## Biological Significance

### In Glycolysis
| Triose | Form | Fate |
|--------|------|------|
| **Glyceraldehyde-3-phosphate** | Phosphorylated | Continues in glycolysis |
| **Dihydroxyacetone phosphate** | Phosphorylated | Converted to G3P by triose phosphate isomerase |

### In Photosynthesis
- **Glyceraldehyde-3-phosphate:** Product of Calvin cycle
- Key intermediate in carbon fixation

### In 
- Building block for larger sugars
- Precursor for glycerol (lipid backbone)
- Entry point for gluconeogenesis

## Evolutionary Perspective
- Likely among the **first sugars** to evolve
- Simple synthesis from prebiotic precursors
- Foundation for more complex carbohydrate metabolism
