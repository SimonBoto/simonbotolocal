# Trioses — Simplest Sugars (Glyceraldehyde & Dihydroxyacetone)

## Core Claim
**Trioses (three-carbon sugars) are the simplest monosaccharides—glyceraldehyde (aldotriose) and dihydroxyacetone (ketotriose) serve as fundamental metabolic intermediates and likely represent the first sugars to evolve, forming the foundation for all carbohydrate metabolism** — their interconversion in glycolysis represents a conserved ancient mechanism.

## Evidence

### The Two Primary Trioses
| Feature | Glyceraldehyde | Dihydroxyacetone |
|---------|---------------|------------------|
| **Type** | Aldotriose (C1 aldehyde) | Ketotriose (C2 ketone) |
| **Formula** | C₃H₆O₃ | C₃H₆O₃ |
| **Optical activity** | Active (D- and L-) | Inactive (symmetrical) |
| **Reference standard** | D-glyceraldehyde defines D-sugars | — |

### Biological Roles
| Role | Form | Function |
|------|------|----------|
| **Glycolysis** | G3P & DHAP | Energy extraction |
| **Photosynthesis** | G3P | Calvin cycle product |
| **Lipid synthesis** | DHAP → Glycerol-3-P | Triglyceride backbone |
| **Gluconeogenesis** | G3P | Entry point |

### Glycolytic Split
```
Fructose-1,6-bisphosphate → [Aldolase] → G3P + DHAP
                                    ↓
                           [Triose phosphate isomerase]
                                    ↓
                              2 G3P (continues)
```

## Clinical Implication
- **G3P and DHAP are metabolic crossroads** — simplest intersection of multiple pathways
- **Triose phosphate isomerase deficiency** — rare, severe hemolytic anemia
- **Evolutionary conservation** — defects often lethal due to fundamental importance
- **METHAP relevance:** Thiamine (B1) supports transketolase reactions involving triose phosphates in PPP
