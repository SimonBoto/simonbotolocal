## Core Claim
**Glycolysis is a 10-step pathway that converts glucose to 2 pyruvate + 2 ATP + 2 NADH — phosphofructokinase-1 (PFK-1) is the committed step and primary control point, regulated by ATP/AMP ratio and hormonal signals** — this ancient pathway (~3.5 billion years) requires no oxygen and is universal across all life.

## Evidence

### Net Reaction
```
Glucose + 2 NAD⁺ + 2 ADP + 2 Pi → 2 Pyruvate + 2 NADH + 2 ATP + 2 H⁺ + 2 H₂O
```

### Investment Phase (Steps 1-5)
| Step | Reaction | Enzyme | Cost/Key |
|------|----------|--------|----------|
| 1 | Glucose → G6P | **Hexokinase** | -1 ATP, traps glucose |
| 2 | G6P → F6P | PGI | Isomerization |
| 3 | F6P → F1,6BP | **PFK-1** | -1 ATP, **committed step** |
| 4 | F1,6BP → DHAP + G3P | Aldolase | Cleavage to trioses |
| 5 | DHAP ↔ G3P | TPI | Equilibration |

### Payoff Phase (Steps 6-10)
| Step | Reaction | Yield |
|------|----------|-------|
| 6 | G3P → 1,3-BPG | + NADH |
| 7 | 1,3-BPG → 3-PG | + ATP |
| 8 | 3-PG → 2-PG | Isomerization |
| 9 | 2-PG → PEP | Dehydration |
| 10 | PEP → Pyruvate | + ATP (pyruvate kinase) |

### Key Regulation
| Enzyme | Inhibited By | Activated By |
|--------|--------------|--------------|
| **Hexokinase** | G6P | — |
| **PFK-1** | ATP, citrate | AMP, F2,6BP, insulin |
| **Pyruvate kinase** | ATP, acetyl-CoA | F1,6BP (feed-forward) |

## Clinical Implication
- **Pyruvate kinase deficiency:** Most common glycolytic enzyme defect → hemolytic anemia
- **PFK-1 regulation:** Key target for metabolic control in diabetes/cancer
- **Hypoxia:** Glycolysis becomes primary ATP source (Warburg effect)
- **METHAP relevance:** Thiamine (B1) required for pyruvate dehydrogenase; mitochondrial nutrients support pyruvate oxidation
