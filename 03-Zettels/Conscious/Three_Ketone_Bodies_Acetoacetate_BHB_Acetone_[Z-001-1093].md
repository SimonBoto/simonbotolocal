# Three Ketone Bodies Acetoacetate BHB Acetone

## Overview
The liver produces three ketone bodies from fatty acid oxidation: acetoacetate, beta-hydroxybutyrate (BHB), and acetone—each with distinct properties and fates.

## 1. Acetoacetate (AcAc)

### Properties
- **Structure:** True ketone (4-carbon with keto group)
- **Formula:** C₄H₆O₃
- **Production:** First ketone body synthesized

### 
- Converted to BHB (reversible) or acetone (irreversible)
- Transported to tissues for energy
- Decarboxylates spontaneously to acetone

## 2. Beta-Hydroxybutyrate (BHB)

### Properties
- **Structure:** Hydroxyacid (not technically a ketone)
- **Formula:** C₄H₈O₃
- **Production:** Reduced form of acetoacetate

### Significance
- **Major circulating ketone** (70-80% of total)
- Most stable form
- Preferred fuel for brain and heart

## 3. Acetone

### Properties
- **Structure:** Simplest ketone (3-carbon)
- **Formula:** C₃H₆O
- **Production:** Spontaneous breakdown of acetoacetate

### Characteristics
- Cannot be used for energy (waste product)
- Exhaled through lungs ("fruity breath")
- Excreted in urine
- Non-enzymatic formation

## Comparative Summary

| Feature | Acetoacetate | BHB | Acetone |
|---------|--------------|-----|---------|
| **Chemical class** | Ketone | Hydroxyacid | Ketone |
| **Energy yield** | Yes | Yes | No |
| **Circulating %** | ~20% | ~78% | ~2% |
| **Stability** | Moderate | High | Volatile |
| **Excretion** | Minimal | Minimal | Breath/urine |

## Interconversion

```
Acetoacetate ←→ BHB
      ↓
   Acetone + CO₂ (spontaneous)
```

**Enzyme:** Beta-hydroxybutyrate dehydrogenase (reversible)
- Location: 
- Cofactor: NAD⁺/NADH
- Equilibrium favors BHB under reducing conditions

## Measurement Methods

| Method | Detects | Clinical Use |
|--------|---------|--------------|
| **Blood BHB** | Primary ketone | Most accurate, therapeutic monitoring |
| **Urine ketones** | Acetoacetate | Screening, less precise |
| **Breath acetone** | Acetone proxy | Non-invasive, research use |
