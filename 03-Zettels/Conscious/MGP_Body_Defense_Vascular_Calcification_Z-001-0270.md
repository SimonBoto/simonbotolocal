## Core Principle

> Matrix Gla Protein (MGP) is the body's most potent natural inhibitor of vascular calcification, requiring both -stimulated synthesis and -mediated activation to provide arterial protection.

## MGP Basics

**Structure:**
- 84 amino acids
- 5 glutamic acid residues
- Vitamin K-dependent protein

**Synthesis:**
- Vascular smooth muscle cells
- Chondrocytes (cartilage)
- Vitamin D upregulates gene expression

**Activation:**
- Requires carboxylation (Glu → Gla)
- Vitamin K2 as cofactor
- Creates calcium-binding sites

## Protective s

### 1. Crystal Binding
- Binds nascent calcium phosphate crystals
- Prevents nucleation
- Inhibits growth

### 2. BMP-2 Inhibition
- Blocks bone morphogenetic protein-2
- Prevents osteogenic signaling
- Maintains VSMC phenotype

### 3. Collagen Binding
- Localizes to elastic fibers
- Protects arterial elasticity
- Prevents mineralization

## When MGP Fails

**Vitamin K Deficiency:**
- Uncarboxylated MGP (ucMGP)
- Inactive
- No protection
- Calcification proceeds

**Measurement:**
- **cMGP:** Carboxylated (active)
- **ucMGP:** Uncarboxylated (inactive)
- **Ratio:** Indicates vascular K status

## D-K2 Synergy for MGP

**Vitamin D:**
- ↑ MGP gene expression
- ↑ Protein synthesis
- Provides substrate

**Vitamin K2:**
- Carboxylates MGP
- Activates protein
- Enables function

**Together:**
- High active MGP
- Maximum protection
- Prevents calcification
