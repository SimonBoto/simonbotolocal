## Core Claim
**Cis and trans fatty acids are geometric isomers that differ in the spatial arrangement of hydrogen atoms around double bonds — with cis configuration (hydrogens on same side) being the natural form that creates chain kinks, while trans configuration (hydrogens on opposite sides) produces straight chains similar to saturated fats — with industrial trans fats from partial hydrogenation being strongly associated with cardiovascular disease, leading to regulatory bans in many countries.**

## Evidence

### Structural Differences
| Feature | Cis Configuration | Trans Configuration |
|---------|-------------------|---------------------|
| **Hydrogen position** | Same side of double bond | Opposite sides |
| **Chain shape** | Kinked/bent | Straight |
| **Physical state** | Liquid at room temp | Semi-solid/solid |
| **Natural occurrence** | Yes (virtually all natural) | Rare (ruminant fats) |
| **Industrial source** | No | Partial hydrogenation |

### Visual Representation

### Health Effects Comparison
| Outcome | Cis Fats | Industrial Trans Fats |
|---------|----------|----------------------|
| **LDL cholesterol** | Variable | ↑↑ Strong increase |
| **HDL cholesterol** | Variable | ↓ Decreases |
| **LDL:HDL ratio** | Neutral | ↑↑ Worsens significantly |
| **Inflammation** | Variable | ↑ Pro-inflammatory |
| **Endothelial function** | Neutral | ↑↑ Impairs |
| **Cardiovascular risk** | Context-dependent | ↑↑↑ Strong increase |

### Sources
| Type | Sources |
|------|---------|
| **Natural trans** | Ruminant meats, dairy (conjugated linoleic acid) |
| **Industrial trans** | Partially hydrogenated oils, baked goods, fried foods |
| **Cis fats** | All natural vegetable oils, fish, nuts |

### Regulatory Status
| Region/Policy | Trans Fat Regulation |
|---------------|---------------------|
| **FDA (USA)** | Banned (2015, phased out 2018-2020) |
| **EU** | <2% of total fat in products |
| **WHO** | <1% of total energy intake |
| **Denmark** | First country to ban (2003) |

## Clinical Implication
- **Zero tolerance:** No safe level for industrial trans fats
- **Natural vs industrial:** Ruminant trans fats less harmful (but still caution)
- **Label reading:** "Partially hydrogenated
