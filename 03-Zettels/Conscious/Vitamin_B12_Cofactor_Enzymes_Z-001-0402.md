## Enzyme 1: Methionine Synthase

**Reaction:**
```
Homocysteine + 5-MTHF → Methionine + THF
```

**Cofactor:** Methylcobalamin
**Location:** Cytosol
**EC Number:** 2.1.1.13

**:**
1. Methylcobalamin accepts methyl from 5-MTHF
2. Methylcobalamin becomes temporarily demethylated
3. Methyl transferred to homocysteine
4. Regenerates methionine

**Function:**
- Regenerates methionine from homocysteine
- Maintains SAM (S-adenosylmethionine) synthesis
- Prevents homocysteine accumulation
- Links folate cycle to methylation reactions

**Clinical Marker:**
- Elevated homocysteine → Suggests B12 or folate deficiency

## Enzyme 2: Methylmalonyl-CoA Mutase

**Reaction:**
```
L-Methylmalonyl-CoA → Succinyl-CoA
```

**Cofactor:** Adenosylcobalamin (5'-deoxyadenosylcobalamin)
**Location:** Mitochondria
**EC Number:** 5.4.99.2

**Mechanism:**
- Radical mechanism involving adenosyl group
- Rearrangement of carbon skeleton
- No change in oxidation state

**Function:**
- Odd-chain fatty acid catabolism
- Amino acid degradation (valine, isoleucine, methionine, threonine)
- Propionate metabolism
- Entry into Krebs cycle (succinyl-CoA)

**Clinical Marker:**
- Elevated methylmalonic acid (MMA) → Specific for B12 deficiency

## Clinical Implications

**B12 Deficiency → Both Enzymes Impaired:**
- ↑ Homocysteine (methionine synthase)
- ↑ Methylmalonic acid (methylmalonyl-CoA mutase)
- **MMA more specific** for B12 deficiency
