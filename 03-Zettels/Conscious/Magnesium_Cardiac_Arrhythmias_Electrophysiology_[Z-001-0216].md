# Magnesium and Cardiac Arrhythmias — Electrophysiological Stability

> Magnesium is critical for cardiac electrophysiology, serving as a first-line therapy for life-threatening ventricular arrhythmias and stabilizing myocardial electrical activity through multiple ion channel effects.

## Electrophysiological Mechanisms

### Ion Channel Effects
**Na⁺/K⁺-ATPase:**
- Maintains resting membrane potential (-90 mV in cardiac cells)
- Mg required for pump function
- Deficiency → depolarization → increased automaticity

**Calcium Channels (L-type):**
- Mg blocks Ca²⁺ entry (natural calcium channel blocker)
- Prolongs effective refractory period
- Reduces triggered activity

**Potassium Channels:**
- Inward rectifier K⁺ channels (IK1) require Mg
- Maintains phase 3 repolarization
- Deficiency → prolonged QT, arrhythmia risk

### Anti-Arrhythmic Classification
**Class IV (Calcium Channel Blocker-like):**
- Similar mechanism to verapamil, diltiazem
- Slows conduction, prolongs refractoriness
- Different receptor site than Class I-III agents

**Unique Properties:**
- Multiple ion channel effects
- No significant negative inotropy at therapeutic doses
- Safe in heart failure (unlike some anti-arrhythmics)

## Clinical Applications

### Torsades de Pointes (First-Line Therapy)
**Indication:**
- Polymorphic VT with QT prolongation
- Drug-induced (antiarrhythmics, antibiotics, antipsychotics)
- Congenital long QT syndrome

**Dosing:**
- **1-2 grams IV over 15 minutes**
- Followed by infusion (0.5-1 g/hr)
- Often terminates arrhythmia within minutes

**Mechanism:**
- Shortens QT interval
- Stabilizes ventricular repolarization
- Reduces early afterdepolarizations

### Digoxin Toxicity
**Competitive Antagonism:**
- Mg competes with digoxin for Na⁺/K⁺-ATPase binding
- Reverses digoxin-induced arrhythmias
- Adjunctive to digoxin-specific antibody (Fab)

**Dosing:**
- 2 grams IV over 15-30 minutes
- Corrects hypomagnesemia (often present)

### Post-Myocardial Infarction
**Ventricular Ectopy:**
- Reduces PVCs, nonsustained VT
- May reduce sudden cardiac death (controversial)
- Adjunctive to standard therapy

**Magnesium in MI Trials:**
- LIMIT-2, ISIS-4: Mixed results on mortality
- Subgroup: Benefit in Mg-deficient patients, those not receiving thrombolytics

### Atrial Fibrillation (Adjunctive)
**Acute:**
- May facilitate cardioversion
- Reduces ventricular response rate
- Evidence weaker than for ventricular arrhythmias

**Chronic:**
- Limited role in maintenance of sinus rhythm
- Some studies show reduced recurrence post-cardioversion

## Electrocardiographic Effects

**QT Interval:**
- Shortens QT (beneficial in long QT)
- Reduces QT dispersion (homogenizes repolarization)

**QRS Duration:**
- Minimal effect at therapeutic levels

**PR Interval:**
- May prolong slightly (AV nodal effect)

**ST Segment:**
- May normalize ST changes in ischemia (controversial)

## Clinical Considerations

**Safety:**
- Excellent safety profile
- Main risk: Hypotension, bradycardia (rare)
- Contraindication: Severe renal failure (accumulation)

**Monitoring:**
- Continuous ECG during IV administration
- Blood pressure
- Deep tendon reflexes (if high-dose/repeated dosing)

**Drug Interactions:**
- **Potentiates:** Calcium channel blockers (caution with combination)
- **Antagonizes:** Digoxin (therapeutic benefit)
- **No significant interaction:** With most antiarrhythmics

## METHAP Context

**Metabolic Syndrome Risk:**
- Sudden cardiac death risk increased in MetS
- Mg provides antiarrhythmic protection
- Part of comprehensive CV risk reduction

**High-Dose Vitamin D Consideration:**
- Hypomagnesemia can cause arrhythmias
- Repletion prevents D-related cardiac complications
- Monitoring: ECG at baseline if high CV risk
