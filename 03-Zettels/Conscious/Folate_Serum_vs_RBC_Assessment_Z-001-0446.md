#  Serum vs RBC Assessment

> Serum folate reflects recent intake (fluctuates with meals), while RBC folate reflects 2-3 month tissue stores and is more reliable for assessing functional status.

## Core Principle

> Serum folate reflects recent intake (fluctuates with meals), while RBC folate reflects 2-3 month tissue stores and is more reliable for assessing functional status.

## Serum Folate

**Range:**
- Standard: >3-4 ng/mL
- Optimal: >10-15 ng/mL

**Limitations:**
- Recent intake only
- Fluctuates with meals
- Short-term marker

## Core Principle

> Serum folate reflects recent intake (fluctuates with meals), while RBC folate reflects 2-3 month tissue stores and is more reliable for assessing functional status.

## RBC Folate

**Range:**
- Standard: >140-160 ng/mL
- Optimal: >400-600 ng/mL

**Advantages:**
- 2-3 month stores
- Stable
- Better functional indicator

## Core Principle

> Serum folate reflects recent intake (fluctuates with meals), while RBC folate reflects 2-3 month tissue stores and is more reliable for assessing functional status.

## When to Use

**Serum:**
- Screening
- Acute assessment
- Quick check

**RBC:**
- Definitive assessment
- Monitoring
- METHAP baseline
