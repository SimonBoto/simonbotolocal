# Endocrine Society vs IOM  Guidelines

The Endocrine Society (clinical endocrinologists) recommends 1,500-2,000 IU/day for adults—significantly higher than IOM's 600-800 IU. This divergence reflects different interpretations of evidence and clinical vs. population health perspectives.

**Comparison:**
| Organization | Adult RDA | Target Level | Perspective |
|--------------|-----------|--------------|-------------|
| IOM | 600-800 IU | 20 ng/mL | Population average |
| Endocrine Society | 1,500-2,000 IU | 30-40 ng/mL | Clinical optimization |
| Corrected (Veugelers) | 7,000-8,000 IU | 20 ng/mL | Statistical accuracy |

**Clinical reality:** Most practitioners following Endocrine Society guidelines, not IOM, because IOM RDA fails to achieve adequate levels in clinical practice.

**Implication:** Professional medical organizations have effectively rejected IOM guidelines through higher clinical recommendations.
