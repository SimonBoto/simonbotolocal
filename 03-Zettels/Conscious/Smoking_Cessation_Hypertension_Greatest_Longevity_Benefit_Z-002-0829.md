# Smoking Cessation and Hypertension Control Provide Greatest Longevity Benefits

## Core Claim
**Among the five modifiable risk factors, smoking cessation provides the greatest longevity benefit, followed by hypertension control** — these two factors have the largest individual impact on life extension.

## Hierarchy of Impact
| Rank | Factor | Impact |
|------|--------|--------|
| **1** | **Smoking cessation** | Greatest benefit |
| **2** | **Hypertension control** | Second greatest |
| **3-5** | Diabetes, weight, cholesterol | Significant but less |

## Why Smoking is #1
- Single most important modifiable risk factor
- Affects multiple organ systems
- Cessation benefits begin within days

## Why Hypertension is #2
- "Silent killer" — often asymptomatic
- Major vascular stress
- Affects heart, brain, kidneys, eyes

## Modification Pathways
| Factor | Intervention |
|--------|--------------|
| **Hypertension** | Lifestyle, diet, micronutrients |
| **Diabetes** | Carb restriction, weight loss |
| **Weight** | Dietary changes, metabolic optimization |
| **Smoking** | Cessation programs, support |
| **Cholesterol** | Address when other factors uncontrolled |

## Clinical Implication
Prioritize **smoking cessation** and **blood pressure control** for maximum longevity gain.
