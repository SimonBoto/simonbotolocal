# Adenosine — Metabolic Roles in Energy and Signaling

## Mechanism

**Adenosine (adenine + ribose) serves as the universal metabolic hub connecting energy status (ATP/ADP/AMP), neural signaling (sleep promotion), and genetic information (RNA/DNA)** — its phosphorylation state signals cellular energy charge, while free adenosine regulates sleep-wake cycles.

## Evidence

### Energy Transfer
| Molecule | Phosphates | Energy Status | Role |
|----------|------------|---------------|------|
| **ATP** | 3 | High-energy, charged | Powers all cellular work |
| **ADP** | 2 | Intermediate | Accepts phosphate to regenerate ATP |
| **AMP** | 1 | Low-energy, discharged | Signals energy depletion |

**ATP hydrolysis:** Releases ~7.3 kcal/mol — drives muscle contraction, nerve impulses, biosynthesis

### Signal Transduction
| Receptor | Effect | Clinical Relevance |
|----------|--------|-------------------|
| **A1** | Inhibitory, slows heart | Cardioprotective |
| **A2A** | Inhibitory, promotes sleep | Caffeine antagonizes |
| **A2B, A3** | Various tissues | Inflammatory regulation |

**Sleep promotion:** Adenosine accumulates during wakefulness → promotes sleepiness

### Genetic Material
- **RNA:** Adenosine (ribonucleoside)
- **DNA:** Deoxyadenosine (deoxyribonucleoside)
- **Evolution:** Central to RNA world hypothesis

## Clinical Implication
- **Caffeine blocks adenosine receptors** → prevents sleep signaling
- **ADA deficiency** → SCID (severe combined immunodeficiency)
- **ATP depletion** → cellular dysfunction, apoptosis
- **METHAP relevance:** High metabolic demand requires robust ATP production; mitochondrial nutrients (B12, CoQ10, magnesium) support ATP synthesis
