# Diabetes as Strongest Mortality Risk Factor

## Source
[[Diabetes_CVD_Global_Burden_NEJM_2023_L-001-0001]]

## Core Claim
Diabetes is the strongest modifiable risk factor for all-cause mortality among the five major modifiable risk factors studied, conferring approximately a 3-fold increased risk at younger ages.

## Key Details
- Diabetes confers ~3x increased risk of all-cause death at younger ages
- This exceeds the mortality risk from smoking (~2.5x), hypertension (~1.5x), and obesity (~1.5x)
- The association is strongest in younger populations, indicating diabetes has a particularly severe impact on life expectancy when developing early in life
- This finding establishes diabetes as the most critical target for mortality reduction in global health policy

## Evidence
- Source-litnote extraction pending
- Evidence from NEJM 2023 study with 1.5 million participants across 34 countries

## Significance
This finding shifts the clinical perspective on diabetes from being primarily a metabolic disorder to being the most critical determinant of mortality risk, requiring prioritized intervention strategies.

## Clinical Significance
- Indicates need for early and aggressive diabetes prevention and management
- Supports screening and intervention programs targeting prediabetes and early diabetes
- Highlights importance of lifestyle interventions to prevent diabetes development
- Provides evidence for prioritizing diabetes management in resource-constrained settings
