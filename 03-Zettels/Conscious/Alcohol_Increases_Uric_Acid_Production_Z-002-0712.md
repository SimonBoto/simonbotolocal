## Core Claim
**Alcohol consumption increases uric acid** through multiple mechanisms: increased purine metabolism, lactate competition for excretion, and dehydration.

## Mechanisms

| Factor | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| **Purine content** | Beer especially high |
| **Lactate production** | Competes with urate excretion |
| **Dehydration** | Reduced renal clearance |

## Evidence

> "Alcohol increases uric acid production and decreases excretion, with beer particularly problematic due to high purine content."
> — Gout management guidelines
