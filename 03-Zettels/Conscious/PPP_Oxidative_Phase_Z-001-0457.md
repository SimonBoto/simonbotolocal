# Pentose Phosphate Pathway: Oxidative Phase

## Overview
Generates **ribose-5-phosphate** (nucleotide synthesis) and **NADPH** (reducing power) from glucose-6-phosphate.

## Reaction Sequence

| Step | Reaction | Enzyme | Product |
|------|----------|--------|---------|
| 1 | G6P → 6-phosphoglucono-δ-lactone | **G6P dehydrogenase** | **NADPH** |
| 2 | Lactone → 6-phosphogluconate | Lactonase | - |
| 3 | 6PG → Ribulose-5-P + CO₂ | **6PG dehydrogenase** | **NADPH** |
| 4 | Ribulose-5-P → Ribose-5-P | Phosphopentose isomerase | R5P |

## Net Reaction
```
G6P + 2 NADP⁺ + H₂O → R5P + CO₂ + 2 NADPH + 2 H⁺
```

## Regulation
- **NADP⁺/NADPH ratio:** Primary controller
- High NADPH → inhibits G6P dehydrogenase (feedback inhibition)
- **:** Upregulates PPP enzymes (anabolic signal)

## : G6PD Deficiency
- Most common enzyme deficiency (400M+ people)
- X-linked recessive
- RBCs vulnerable → hemolysis under oxidative stress
- **Evolutionary advantage:** Protection against malaria
