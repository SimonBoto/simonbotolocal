# PPP Oxidative Phase — Ribose and NADPH Generation

## Core Claim
**The oxidative phase of the pentose phosphate pathway converts glucose-6-phosphate to ribose-5-phosphate, generating 2 NADPH per glucose — this is the primary source of NADPH (~60%) for biosynthesis and antioxidant defense in most cells** — G6PD deficiency is the most common enzyme deficiency worldwide (400M+ people).

## Evidence

### Reaction Sequence
| Step | Reaction | Enzyme | Product |
|------|----------|--------|---------|
| 1 | G6P → 6-phosphoglucono-δ-lactone | **G6P dehydrogenase** | **NADPH** |
| 2 | Lactone → 6-phosphogluconate | Lactonase | — |
| 3 | 6PG → Ribulose-5-P + CO₂ | **6PG dehydrogenase** | **NADPH** |
| 4 | Ribulose-5-P → Ribose-5-P | Phosphopentose isomerase | R5P |

### Net Reaction
```
G6P + 2 NADP⁺ + H₂O → R5P + CO₂ + 2 NADPH + 2 H⁺
```

### Regulation
| Factor | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| **High NADPH** | Inhibits G6P dehydrogenase (feedback) |
| **Insulin** | Upregulates PPP enzymes (anabolic) |
| **High NADP⁺** | Stimulates PPP (oxidized signal) |

## Clinical Implication
- **G6PD deficiency:** Most common enzyme deficiency; X-linked; RBCs vulnerable → hemolysis under oxidative stress (fava beans, sulfa drugs)
- **Evolutionary advantage:** Heterozygote protection against malaria
- **Rapidly dividing tissues:** High PPP activity for ribose (bone marrow, gut, skin)
- **METHAP relevance:** B-vitamins support PPP; high metabolic demand requires robust NADPH generation for antioxidant defense
