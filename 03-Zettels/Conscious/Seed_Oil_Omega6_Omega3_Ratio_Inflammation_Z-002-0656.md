# Seed Oil Omega-6:Omega-3 Ratio Promotes Inflammation

## Core Claim
The **high omega-6:omega-3 fatty acid ratio** in seed oils promotes systemic inflammation when consumed regularly, contributing to insulin resistance and type 2 diabetes risk beyond the effects of oxidation products from frying.

## The Ratio Problem

### Evolutionary vs. Modern Ratios
| Era | Omega-6:Omega-3 Ratio |
|-----|----------------------|
| **Paleolithic** | 1:1 to 4:1 |
| **Pre-industrial** | 2:1 to 5:1 |
| **Modern (seed oil era)** | 10:1 to 20:1 |
| **High seed oil consumers** | 25:1+ |

**The shift:** 10-20× increase in omega-6 relative to omega-3 in 100 years.

### Seed Oil Contributions
| Oil | Omega-6 % | Omega-3 % | Ratio |
|-----|-----------|-----------|-------|
| **Soybean** | 55% | 7% | ~8:1 |
| **Corn** | 58% | 1% | ~58:1 |
| **Sunflower** | 69% | 0% | ∞ |
| **Safflower** | 75% | 0% | ∞ |

## Mechanism: From Ratio to Inflammation

### The Eicosanoid Pathway
```
Omega-6 (Linoleic acid)
         ↓
    Arachidonic acid (AA)
         ↓
    Pro-inflammatory eicosanoids:
    • PGE2 (inflammation)
    • LTB4 (leukocyte chemotaxis)
    • TXA2 (platelet aggregation)

Omega-3 (ALA/EPA/DHA)
         ↓
    Resolvins, protectins
         ↓
    Anti-inflammatory, pro-resolving
```

### Competitive Inhibition
| Factor | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| **Shared enzymes** | COX-2, LOX process both omega-6 and omega-3 |
| **High omega-6** | Outcompetes omega-3 for enzyme access |
| **Result** | Pro-inflammatory products dominate |
| **Low omega-3** | Insufficient anti-inflammatory counterbalance |

## Metabolic Consequences

### Inflammation → Insulin Resistance
| Inflammatory Mediator | Metabolic Effect |
|----------------------|------------------|
| **TNF-α** | Serine phosphorylates IRS-1 (insulin resistance) |
| **IL-6** | Impairs GLUT4 translocation |
| **PGE2** | Promotes adipose tissue inflammation |
| **CRP** | Marker and contributor to metabolic dysfunction |

### The Vicious Cycle
```
High omega-6 intake
         ↓
    Pro-inflammatory eicosanoids
         ↓
    Systemic inflammation
         ↓
    Insulin resistance
         ↓
    Metabolic syndrome
         ↓
    Type 2 diabetes
         ↓
    [Amplifies inflammation further]
```

## Evidence

> "Modern seed oils contain omega-6:omega-3 ratios of 10-20:1 (vs. evolutionary 1-4:1); this imbalance drives arachidonic acid-derived pro-inflammatory eicosanoids that contribute to metabolic dysfunction and diabetes pathogenesis."
> — Mousavi et al., 2025

## Clinical Implications

### Assessment
| Marker | Target | Interpretation |
|--------|--------|----------------|
| **Omega-6:Omega-3 ratio** | <4:1 | Lower inflammation |
| **AA:EPA ratio** | <3:1 | Better balance |
| **hs-CRP** | <1.0 mg/L | Lower inflammation |

### Intervention
| Strategy | Mechanism |
|----------|-----------|
| **Reduce seed oils** | Lower omega-6 intake |
| **Increase fatty fish** | EPA/DHA (omega-3) |
| **Add flax/chia** | ALA (omega-3 precursor) |
| **Use olive oil** | Monounsaturated (neutral) |

### Practical Targets
| Current | Target |
|---------|--------|
| Seed oil-based dressings | Olive oil + vinegar |
| Fried foods | Baked/grilled |
| Processed snacks | Whole foods |
| Corn/soybean oil | Butter, coconut, olive |
