# ependence in Organic Acid 

## Critical Cofactors

### Thiamine (B1)
**Role:** Pyruvate dehydrogenase, α-ketoglutarate dehydrogenase, transketolase
**Deficiency:**
- Beriberi (wet/dry)
- Wernicke-Korsakoff syndrome
- Lactic acidosis (PDH impaired)

### Riboflavin (B2)
**Role:** FAD synthesis (oxidation-reduction)
**Enzymes:** PDH complex, succinate dehydrogenase
**Deficiency:** Fatigue, angular cheilitis, neurological symptoms

### Niacin (B3)
**Role:** NAD⁺ synthesis
**Enzymes:** Dehydrogenases throughout metabolism
**Deficiency:** Pellagra (3 Ds: dermatitis, diarrhea, dementia)

### Pantothenate (B5)
**Role:** CoA synthesis
**Function:** Acyl group transfer
**Deficiency:** Fatigue, malaise (rare)

### Biotin (B7)
**Role:** Carboxylase reactions
**Key enzyme:** Propionyl-CoA carboxylase, pyruvate carboxylase
**Deficiency:** Propionic acidemia symptoms, dermatitis, hair loss

### Cobalamin (B12)
**Role:** Methylmalonyl-CoA mutase, methionine synthase
**Deficiency:**
- Methylmalonic acidemia (MMA accumulation)
- Pernicious anemia
- Neurological damage (irreversible)

##  Testing
**Organic acidemia workup:**
- Methylmalonic acid (B12 deficiency marker)
- Propionic acid (biotin-related)
- Lactate/pyruvate ratio (thiamine)
