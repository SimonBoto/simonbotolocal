## Core Claim
**Six B-vitamins are essential cofactors for organic acid metabolism — thiamine (B1), riboflavin (B2), niacin (B3), pantothenate (B5), biotin (B7), and cobalamin (B12) each serve distinct roles in pyruvate oxidation, Krebs cycle function, and carboxylation reactions** — deficiencies cause characteristic organic acidemias and neurological syndromes.

## Evidence

### B-Vitamin Roles
| Vitamin | Active Form | Key Enzymes | Deficiency Syndrome |
|---------|-------------|-------------|---------------------|
| **B1 (Thiamine)** | TPP | PDH, α-KGDH, transketolase | Beriberi, Wernicke-Korsakoff |
| **B2 (Riboflavin)** | FAD/FMN | PDH complex, succinate dehydrogenase | Fatigue, angular cheilitis |
| **B3 (Niacin)** | NAD⁺/NADP⁺ | Dehydrogenases throughout | Pellagra (3 Ds) |
| **B5 (Pantothenate)** | CoA | PDH complex, acyl transfer | Fatigue, malaise (rare) |
| **B7 (Biotin)** | Biotin-lysine | Pyruvate carboxylase, propionyl-CoA carboxylase | Dermatitis, alopecia, acidemia |
| **B12 (Cobalamin)** | Adenosyl-B12, Me-B12 | Methylmalonyl-CoA mutase, methionine synthase | Pernicious anemia, MMA, neuropathy |

### Critical Pathways
| Pathway | Vitamins Required |
|---------|-------------------|
| **Pyruvate dehydrogenase** | B1, B2, B3, B5, lipoate |
| **α-Ketoglutarate dehydrogenase** | B1, B2, B3, B5, lipoate |
| **Propionate metabolism** | B7, B12 |
| **Gluconeogenesis** | B7 (pyruvate carboxylase) |

## Clinical Implication
- **Multiple deficiencies common:** Malnutrition, alcoholism, malabsorption
- **Organic acid testing:** Elevated methylmalonic acid (B12), propionic acid (B7), lactate/pyruvate (B1)
- **High-dose therapy:** Some disorders respond to pharmacologic B-vitamin doses
- **METHAP relevance:** High-dose B-complex supports multiple organic acid pathways simultaneously; testing organic acids guides targeted supplementation
