# Clinical Assessment of B12 Status — MMA

> Methylmalonic acid (MMA) is the most sensitive and specific marker of B12 deficiency, reflecting functional l B12 status, while homocysteine is elevated in both B12 and folate deficiency.

## Serum B12

**Reference Range:**
- Normal: 200-900 pg/mL
- Deficient: <200 pg/mL

**Limitations:**
- Poor sensitivity
- Functional deficiency possible with normal levels
- Late marker

## Methylmalonic Acid (MMA)

**Reference Range:**
- Normal: <0.4 μmol/L (serum)
- Elevated: >0.4 μmol/L

**Advantages:**
- Most sensitive marker
- Specific for B12 (not folate)
- Reflects functional status
- Early indicator

## Homocysteine

**Reference Range:**
- Normal: <10 μmol/L
- Elevated: >10 μmol/L

**Limitations:**
- Non-specific
- Elevated in B12 OR folate deficiency
- Also affected by renal function, genetics

## Interpretation

| Pattern | Interpretation |
|---------|----------------|
| **Low B12, High MMA** | B12 deficiency |
| **Normal B12, High MMA** | Functional/tissue deficiency |
| **High Homocysteine only** | Likely folate deficiency |
| **Both elevated** | B12 deficiency |

## Clinical Pearls

- Check MMA if B12 borderline
- Elevated MMA confirms deficiency
- Homocysteine alone insufficient
- MMA more sensitive than B12
