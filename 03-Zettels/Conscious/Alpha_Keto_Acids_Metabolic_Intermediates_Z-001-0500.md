# α-Keto Acids as Metabolic Intermediates

## Definition
**α-Keto acids = Amino acids without the amino group**
- Also called "2-oxo acids"
- The carbon skeletons remaining after deamination/transamination
- Entry points to central 

## Key α-Keto Acids

| α-Keto Acid | From Amino Acid | Krebs Entry | Classification |
|-------------|-----------------|-------------|----------------|
| **Pyruvate** | Alanine | Pyruvate (→ OAA or acetyl-CoA) | Glucogenic |
| **Oxaloacetate** | Aspartate |  | Glucogenic |
| **α-Ketoglutarate** | Glutamate | Krebs cycle | Glucogenic |
| **α-Ketoisocaproate** | Leucine | → HMG-CoA | Ketogenic |
| **α-Ketoisovalerate** | Valine | → Succinyl-CoA | Glucogenic |
| **α-Keto-β-methylvalerate** | Isoleucine | → Succinyl-CoA + acetyl-CoA | Both |
| **Phenylpyruvate** | Phenylalanine | → Fumarate + acetoacetate | Both |
| **p-Hydroxyphenylpyruvate** | Tyrosine | → Fumarate + acetoacetate | Both |
| **α-Ketobutyrate** | Threonine | → Propionyl-CoA | Glucogenic |

## Transamination: The Exchange

### Reversible Reaction
```
Amino Acid₁ + α-Keto Acid₂ ↔ α-Keto Acid₁ + Amino Acid₂
```

### Example
```
Alanine + α-ketoglutarate ↔ Pyruvate + Glutamate
```
- Enzyme: Alanine aminotransferase (ALT)
- Cofactor: B6 (pyridoxal phosphate)

## Metabolic Fates

### Glucogenic α-Keto Acids
Enter gluconeogenesis:
- Pyruvate → OAA → Glucose
- Oxaloacetate → PEP → Glucose
- α-Ketoglutarate → Succinyl-CoA → OAA → Glucose

### Ketogenic α-Keto Acids
Yield acetyl-CoA or acetoacetyl-CoA:
- Cannot make glucose
- Enter ketogenesis or fatty acid synthesis

## : Maple Syrup Urine Disease (MSUD)

### Defect
**Branched-chain α-keto acid dehydrogenase (BCKDH) deficiency**
- Cannot degrade branched-chain α-keto acids
- Accumulation of:
  - α-ketoisocaproate (from leucine)
  - α-ketoisovalerate (from valine)
  - α-keto-β-methylvalerate (from isoleucine)

### Detection
**Newborn screening:**
- Elevated BCAAs in blood
- Characteristic "maple syrup" odor
- Confirmatory testing required

## Summary
**α-Keto acids are the bridge between amino acid metabolism and central metabolism.**
- Remove amino group → carbon skeleton enters Krebs/gluconeogenesis
- Transamination allows amino group exchange
- B6-dependent enzymes critical
