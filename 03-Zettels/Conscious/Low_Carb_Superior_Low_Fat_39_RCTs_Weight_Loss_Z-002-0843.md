# Low-Carb Superior to Low-Fat in 39 RCTs for Weight Loss

## Core Claim
**In all 39 randomized controlled trials analyzed, individuals who restricted carbohydrates lost more weight than those who restricted fats** — low-carb demonstrated consistent statistical superiority.

## The Evidence
| Metric | Result |
|--------|--------|
| **39 RCTs analyzed** | Low-carb consistently superior |
| **Statistical significance** | Demonstrated in all 39 studies |
| **Weight loss** | More weight lost on low-carb |

> "In all 39 studies, individuals who restricted carbohydrates lost more weight than those who restricted fats."

## Additional Health Improvements
| Marker | Improvement |
|--------|-------------|
| **Cardiovascular risk** | Improved |
| **Insulin resistance** | Reduced |
| **Diabetes** | Better control |
| **Gallstone risk** | Reduced |
| **GI function** | Improved |
| **GERD** | Reduced |
| **Chronic inflammation** | Reduced |

## Diet Composition Advantage
| Shift | Effect |
|-------|--------|
| **Higher quality fats** | Satiety, nutrient density |
| **Higher protein** | Satiety, muscle preservation |
| **Lower carbs** | Insulin reduction, fat oxidation |

## Clinical Implication
Low-carb preferred for **obesity treatment** and **chronic disease prevention**.
