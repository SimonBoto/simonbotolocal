#   Terminology

## Overview
9 core terms defining metabolism as methodology and metabolomics as clinical practice.

## The Terms (Category B)

| Term | Definition | Clinical Application |
|------|------------|---------------------|
| **Μεταβολισμός** | Chemical transformations producing energy and building blocks | Information flow, not just chemistry |
| **Μεταβολίτες** | Small molecules of metabolic pathways | Diagnostic windows into function |
| **Μεταβολομική** | Study of metabolites in biological systems | Clinical metabolomics = functional diagnosis |
| **Οργανικά Οξέα** | Acidic metabolic intermediates (OAT markers) | Urine-based functional assessment |
| **Λιπαρά Οξέα** | Fatty acid metabolites and profiles | Membrane and signaling status |
| **Νόμος Ελάχιστης Απαίτησης (Liebig)** | Growth limited by scarcest resource | Identifying limiting nutrients |
| **Ενεργειακή Ροή** | Movement of energy through pathways | Efficiency vs. blockage assessment |
| **Βιοχημικά Μονοπάτια** | Sequential enzymatic reactions | Pathway mapping for intervention |
| **Ισορροπία Redox** | Oxidant-antioxidant balance | NAD+/NADH, glutathione status |
| **Γλουταθειόνη** | Master antioxidant and detoxifier | Key metabolomics marker |
| **Detox Phase I/II** | Biotransformation pathways | Phase I = modification; Phase II = conjugation |

## Key Distinction
> Metabolism as *information flow* vs. metabolism as *chemistry*

Metabolomics sees dysfunction before pathology by reading metabolic information.
