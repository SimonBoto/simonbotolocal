# Mitochondrial Supplementation Protocol

## Core Claim
Targeted supplementation based on organic acid patterns—prioritizing magnesium, CoQ10, B-vitamins, and carnitine according to individual deficiency patterns—combined with low-carbohydrate dietary modification, restores mitochondrial function and normalizes metabolic markers within 6-8 weeks.

## Evidence

### Tier 1 — Critical Cofactors

| Nutrient | Dose | Form | Timing | Rationale |
|----------|------|------|--------|-----------|
| **Magnesium** | 200-400 mg elemental | Glycinate, citrate, malate | Divided doses | Cofactor for 6+ TCA enzymes |
| **CoQ10** | 100-200 mg | Ubiquinol (reduced) | With fat | ETC electron shuttle |
| **B-Complex** | High-quality | Activated forms | Morning | Multiple dehydrogenase cofactors |
| **L-Carnitine** | 500-2000 mg | L-carnitine or ALCAR | Divided doses | Fatty acid transport |

**B-Complex Specifics:**
- B1: Thiamine pyrophosphate (TPP) or benfotiamine
- B2: Riboflavin-5-phosphate
- B3: Niacinamide or NAD+ precursors (NMN/NR)
- B5: Pantethine (active form)

### Tier 2 — Supporting Nutrients

| Nutrient | Dose | Indication |
|----------|------|------------|
| **Iron** | Per deficiency | If ferritin low, Fe-S cluster support |
| **Zinc** | 15-30 mg | Antioxidant defense, enzyme function |
| **Selenium** | 100-200 mcg | Glutathione peroxidase, thyroid |
| **Alpha-lipoic acid** | 300-600 mg | Mitochondrial antioxidant, glucose uptake |

### Dietary Interventions

**Low-Carbohydrate, High-Fat Approach:**
- **Rationale:** Reduces glycolytic burden on impaired mitochondria
- **Mechanism:** Shifts metabolism to ketones, bypassing some TCA blocks
- **Implementation:** <50g net carbs daily, moderate protein, high healthy fats

**Nutrient-Dense Foods:**
- **Organ meats:** Liver (B12, iron, CoQ10), heart (CoQ10)
- **Fatty fish:** Omega-3s, B-vitamins, selenium
- **Eggs:** Choline, B-vitamins, complete protein
- **Leafy greens:** Magnesium, folate, antioxidants

**Avoid:**
- Processed foods (depleted nutrients, additives)
- Refined carbohydrates (glycemic stress)
- Industrial seed oils (pro-inflammatory)
- Alcohol (mitochondrial toxin)

### Monitoring Protocol

**Timeline:**
| Timepoint | Assessment |
|-----------|------------|
| **Baseline** | OAT, symptoms, exercise tolerance |
| **4 weeks** | Symptom check, adherence review |
| **6-8 weeks** | Repeat OAT, clinical correlation |
| **3 months** | Maintenance dosing established |

**Expected Improvements:**
- Reduction in elevated organic acid markers
- Increased energy levels
n- Improved exercise tolerance
- Better sleep quality
- Enhanced cognitive function

## Clinical Implication

**Individualization:**
- Adjust doses based on severity of OAT elevations
- Prioritize based on primary pattern (citrate/aconitate → Mg; 3-HMGA → carnitine)
- Consider comorbidities (kidney disease limits Mg dosing)

**Common Issues:**
- **GI upset with Mg:** Switch to glycinate or reduce dose
- **Insomnia with B-vitamins:** Take earlier in day
- **Cost:** Prioritize Tier 1 nutrients if budget limited

**Drug Interactions:**
- Statins: Increase CoQ10 requirement
- Metformin: Increase B12 requirement
- PPIs: Impair B12, Mg, iron absorption

## METHAP Relevance

**Integration with METHAP:**
- OAT-guided supplementation personalizes protocol
- Builds on existing Mg, B-vitamin, D/K2 foundation
- CoQ10 complements cardiovascular protection
- Carnitine enhances insulin sensitivity
- Objective monitoring validates intervention
