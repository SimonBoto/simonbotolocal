# Lp(a) Management: Aggressive Risk Factor Control Plus Emerging Therapies

## Core Claim
Management of elevated Lp(a) focuses on **aggressive control of modifiable cardiovascular risk factors** (LDL, BP, smoking) since Lp(a) itself is not modifiable by lifestyle, with **emerging RNA-targeted therapies** (pelacarsen, olpasiran) showing promise for direct Lp(a) lowering.

## Current Management: Aggressive Risk Factor Control

### LDL Cholesterol
| Strategy | Target |
|----------|--------|
| **High-intensity statins** | LDL <70 if ASCVD |
| **Add ezetimibe** | Further LDL reduction |
| **PCSK9 inhibitors** | For very high risk |
| **Lower thresholds** | Treat at lower LDL if high Lp(a) |

### Blood Pressure
| Target | Rationale |
|--------|-----------|
| **<130/80** | Aggressive control |
| **Earlier treatment** | Don't wait for complications |

### Other Factors
| Factor | Action |
|--------|--------|
| **Smoking** | Absolute cessation |
| **Diabetes** | Optimal glycemic control |
| **Inflammation** | Address if elevated |

## Emerging Therapies

### Pelacarsen (Antisense Oligonucleotide)
| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Mechanism** | Reduces hepatic Apo(a) production |
| **Effect** | ~80% Lp(a) reduction |
| **Dosing** | Monthly injection |
| **Status** | Phase 3 trials (HORIZON) |
| **Outcome data** | Pending |

### Olpasiran (siRNA)
| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Mechanism** | Silences LPA gene expression |
| **Effect** | ~95% Lp(a) reduction |
| **Dosing** | Quarterly injection |
| **Status** | Phase 2/3 trials |
| **Outcome data** | Pending |

## Clinical Decision Framework

| Lp(a) Level | Action |
|-------------|--------|
| **<30 mg/dL** | Routine care |
| **30-50 mg/dL** | Consider testing family |
| **50-100 mg/dL** | Aggressive risk factor control |
| **>100 mg/dL** | Aggressive control + consider apheresis/referral |
| **>180 mg/dL** | Apheresis consideration |

## Evidence

> "Current management focuses on aggressive control of modifiable risk factors (LDL, BP, smoking) since Lp(a) is not lifestyle-modifiable; emerging RNA-targeted therapies (pelacarsen, olpasiran) demonstrate dramatic Lp(a) lowering (~80-95%) with outcome trials pending."
> — (Lp(a) therapeutic literature)
