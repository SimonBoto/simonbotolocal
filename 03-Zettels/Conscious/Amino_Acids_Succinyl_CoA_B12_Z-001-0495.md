# Amino Acids to Succinyl-CoA — B12 Criticality

## Core Claim
**Four amino acids (isoleucine, valine, methionine, threonine) enter the Krebs cycle at succinyl-CoA via a common propionyl-CoA intermediate — the critical final step requires vitamin B12 (adenosylcobalamin) as cofactor for methylmalonyl-CoA mutase, making B12 essential for glucogenic metabolism of these amino acids** — B12 deficiency causes methylmalonic acid accumulation.

## Evidence

### Amino Acids to Propionyl-CoA
| Amino Acid | Pathway | Notes |
|------------|---------|-------|
| **Isoleucine** | → Propionyl-CoA | Branched-chain (also ketogenic) |
| **Valine** | → Propionyl-CoA | Branched-chain |
| **Methionine** | → SAM → Homocysteine → Propionyl-CoA | Methyl donor first |
| **Threonine** | → α-ketobutyrate → Propionyl-CoA | Threonine dehydrogenase |

### Common Pathway: Propionate Metabolism
```
Propionyl-CoA → Methylmalonyl-CoA (biotin-dependent carboxylase)
Methylmalonyl-CoA → Succinyl-CoA (B12-dependent mutase)
```

### Critical B12-Dependent Step
| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Enzyme** | Methylmalonyl-CoA mutase (MMCM) |
| **Cofactor** | Adenosylcobalamin (B12) |
| **Reaction** | Methylmalonyl-CoA → Succinyl-CoA (intramolecular rearrangement) |

### B12 Deficiency Consequences
- **Methylmalonic acid (MMA) accumulation**
- **Metabolic acidosis**
- **Neurological impairment**

## Clinical Implication
- **B12 essential:** For metabolism of 4 amino acids
- **MMA test:** Diagnostic for B12 deficiency
- **Propionic/methylmalonic acidemia:** Inborn errors of these pathways
- **METHAP relevance:** B12 critical for amino acid catabolism; high protein intake increases B12 demand
