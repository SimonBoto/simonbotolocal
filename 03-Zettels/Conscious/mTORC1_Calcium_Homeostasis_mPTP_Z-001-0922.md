# mTORC1 Inhibition Disrupts Calcium Homeostasis and mPTP Opening

**Core Mechanism:** Sirolimus disrupts l calcium buffering, leading to calcium overload, mPTP opening, and cell death.

## Calcium Homeostasis Overview

**Cytosolic Calcium ([Ca²⁺]i):**
- Resting: ~100 nM
- Signaling: rises to 1-10 μM
- Must be rapidly cleared after signals

** Calcium ([Ca²⁺]m):**
- Important buffer for cytosolic Ca²⁺
- Activates TCA cycle dehydrogenases
- Stimulates ATP production
- Excess triggers pathology

## mTORC1 and Calcium Handling

**Normal mTORC1 Function:**
- Indirectly supports calcium homeostasis
- Maintains ER-mitochondria contact sites
- Supports MCU (mitochondrial calcium uniporter) function
- Regulates calcium efflux mechanisms

**mTORC1 Effects:**
- Promotes mitochondrial calcium uptake capacity
- Supports calcium-induced metabolic activation
- Protects against calcium overload

## Sirolimus Disruption

**Impaired Calcium Buffering:**
1. Reduced mitochondrial Ca²⁺ uptake capacity
2. Cytosolic Ca²⁺ accumulation
3. Sustained elevation of [Ca²⁺]i
4. Continued mitochondrial Ca²⁺ loading

**Calcium Overload:**
- [Ca²⁺]m exceeds buffering capacity
- Matrix Ca²⁺ activates dehydrogenases (initially)
- Prolonged elevation → pathological

## mPTP (Mitochondrial Permeability Transition Pore)

**Structure:**
- VDAC (outer membrane)
- ANT (adenine nucleotide translocase, inner membrane)
- Cyclophilin D (regulatory component)
- Other associated proteins debated

**Opening Triggers:**
- High matrix Ca²⁺ (>100 μM)
- ROS (oxidative stress)
- Depolarization
- **Sirolimus produces all three**

## mPTP Opening Consequences

**Immediate:**
- Inner membrane permeabilization
- Proton gradient collapse (ΔΨm loss)
- Matrix swelling (osmotic)

**Progressive:**
- Outer membrane rupture
- Release of intermembrane space proteins:
  - Cytochrome c
  - AIF (apoptosis-inducing factor)
  - SMAC/Diablo
  - Omi/HtrA2

**Cell Death Pathways:**
- **Cytochrome c + Apaf-1 + caspase-9** → Caspase cascade → Apoptosis
- **AIF** → Nuclear translocation → Caspase-independent cell death

## The Calcium-ROS Synergy

```
Sirolimus → Mitochondrial dysfunction
                ↓
        ┌───────┴───────┐
        ↓               ↓
    Calcium         ROS
    dysregulation   generation
        ↓               ↓
        └───────┬───────┘
                ↓
        Calcium + ROS
                ↓
            mPTP Opening
                ↓
        Cell Death
```

**Why synergistic:**
- ROS sensitizes mPTP to Ca²⁺
- Ca²⁺ amplifies ROS production
- Together, lower thresholds trigger opening

## Tissue Vulnerability

**High Vulnerability:**
- **Cardiomyocytes:** Ca²⁺ cycling essential for contraction
- **Neurons:** Excitotoxicity risk, Ca²⁺ signaling central
- **β-cells:** Glucose-stimulated  secretion Ca²⁺-dependent

**Clinical Correlations:**
- Cardiotoxicity with sirolimus
- Neurotoxicity (rare but reported)
- β-cell dysfunction
