# Pro-Inflammatory Eicosanoids — Arachidonic Acid Derived PGE2 and LTB4

## Eicosanoid Synthesis Pathway

### From Arachidonic Acid to Bioactive Mediators
```
Arachidonic Acid (AA, 20:4 n-6)
        ↓
    Phospholipase A2 (PLA2)
        ↓
Free AA released from membrane
        ↓
    ↙ COX pathway ↘    ↙ LOX pathway ↘
   Prostaglandins     Leukotrienes
   Thromboxanes       Lipoxins
```

## COX Pathway Products

### Cyclooxygenase Enzymes
| Enzyme | Expression | Function |
|--------|-----------|----------|
| **COX-1** | Constitutive | Homeostatic prostaglandins (gut, kidney, platelets) |
| **COX-2** | Inducible | Inflammatory prostaglandins (immune cells) |

### Key Pro-Inflammatory Products

#### PGE2 (Prostaglandin E2)
**Synthesis:** AA → PGH2 → PGE2 (via PGE synthase)

**Actions:**
- **Fever:** Hypothalamus thermoregulation
- **Pain:** Sensitizes nociceptors
- **Vasodilation:** Increases blood flow (redness, heat)
- **Edema:** Increases vascular permeability
- **Immune modulation:** Th17 promotion, antibody production

**Clinical Relevance:**
- NSAIDs (ibuprofen, aspirin) work by inhibiting COX → ↓PGE2
- Chronic elevation: arthritis, chronic pain syndromes

#### TXA2 (Thromboxane A2)
**Primary source:** Platelets

**Actions:**
- **Platelet aggregation:** Promotes clot formation
- **Vasoconstriction:** Narrows blood vessels
- **Pro-thrombotic state:** Increased CVD risk

**Balance with PGI2 (prostacyclin):**
- PGI2: Anti-thrombotic, vasodilatory (from endothelial cells)
- TXA2 dominance → Cardiovascular events

## LOX Pathway Products

### Lipoxygenase Enzymes
| Enzyme | Position of Oxygenation | Primary Products |
|--------|------------------------|------------------|
| **5-LOX** | C5 | LTB4, cysteinyl-LTs |
| **12-LOX** | C12 | 12-HETE |
| **15-LOX** | C15 | 15-HETE, lipoxins |

### LTB4 (Leukotriene B4)
**Synthesis:** AA → 5-HPETE → LTA4 → LTB4

**Actions:**
- **Neutrophil chemotaxis:** Recruits immune cells to site
- **Adhesion molecule expression:** ICAM-1, VCAM-1 upregulation
- **Superoxide generation:** Respiratory burst
- **Degranulation:** Releases inflammatory enzymes

**Clinical Relevance:**
- Psoriasis, asthma, COPD, inflammatory bowel disease
- Targeted by 5-LOX inhibitors (zileuton)

### Cysteinyl Leukotrienes (LTC4, LTD4, LTE4)
**Actions:**
- **Bronchoconstriction:** Powerful smooth muscle contraction
- **Vascular permeability:** Plasma extravasation
- **Mucus secretion:** Airway hypersecretion

**Clinical:** Targeted by leukotriene receptor antagonists (montelukast)

## The Omega-6 Excess Problem

### Eicosanoid Overproduction
| Dietary State | Tissue AA | Eicosanoid Output | Clinical Result |
|--------------|-----------|-------------------|-----------------|
| **Low omega-6 (ancestral)** | Low | Minimal | Basal  |
| **High omega-6 (modern)** | High | Excessive | Chronic inflammation |

### COX-2 Induction
- High AA substrate → COX-2 upregulation
- Positive feedback loop
- Chronic COX-2 expression → Cancer risk (colorectal)

## Anti-Inflammatory Counterparts (Omega-3 Derived)

### EPA-Derived Eicosanoids
| Omega-6 Product | Omega-3 Counterpart | Effect |
|-----------------|---------------------|--------|
| PGE2 | PGE3 | Less inflammatory |
| TXA2 | TXA3 | Less thrombotic |
| LTB4 | LTB5 | Less chemotactic |

### Resolvins (Resolution Phase)
- **RvE1, RvE2:** From EPA
- **RvD1-RvD5:** From DHA
- **Function:** Active termination of inflammation

**Blocked by:** High omega-6 (competes for enzymes)

## Therapeutic Implications

### Reducing Pro-Inflammatory Eicosanoids
1. **Reduce AA substrate:** Lower omega-6 intake
2. **Increase EPA/DHA:** Compete with AA
3. **COX-2 inhibitors:** NSAIDs (acute), caution with chronic use
4. **LOX inhibitors:** Zileuton (asthma)
5. **Corticosteroids:** Block PLA2 (prevents AA release)

### s
- **Urinary PGE-M:** PGE2 metabolite
- **Serum LTB4:** Leukotriene levels
- **AA:EPA ratio:** Predicts eicosanoid balance
