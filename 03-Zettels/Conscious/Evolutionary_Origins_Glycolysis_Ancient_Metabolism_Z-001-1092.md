# Evolutionary Origins of Glycolysis — Ancient Metabolism

## Core Claim
**Glycolysis is one of the most ancient metabolic pathways, universally conserved across all domains of life—its anaerobic nature, iron-sulfur mineral catalysis, and role in the RNA world suggest it emerged before oxygenic photosynthesis, possibly at hydrothermal vents** — modern glycolysis retains the same 10 steps found in bacteria and humans.

## Evidence

### Universal Conservation
- **All organisms:** Bacteria, archaea, eukaryotes share same core reactions
- **Enzyme structures:** Highly conserved folds and active sites
- **No oxygen required:** Functions in anoxic environments

### Prebiotic Plausibility
| Scenario | Evidence |
|----------|----------|
| **Iron-sulfur world** | Fe-S minerals catalyze reductive reactions similar to glycolysis |
| **Hydrothermal vents** | Chemical gradients provide energy for organic synthesis |
| **Zinc sulfide** | Photosynthetic-like reactions without biology |

### RNA World Connection
1. **Mineral-catalyzed reactions** create simple organics
2. **RNA emerges** as catalyst and information storage
3. **Glycolysis-like pathways** provide energy for RNA replication
4. **Proteins evolve** to improve efficiency
5. **Modern glycolysis** established

## Clinical Implication
- **Conservation implies optimality** — glycolysis is robust and hard to improve
- **Genetic defects often lethal** — essential for all life
- **Cancer cells rely heavily on glycolysis** (Warburg effect) — ancient pathway co-opted
- **METHAP relevance:** Supporting glycolysis with thiamine (B1) ensures fundamental energy production
