# Six Stage Patient Flow Framework

## Core Claim
**The complete metabolomics patient journey follows six stages — Assessment → Sample Logistics → Analysis → Reporting → Treatment → Follow-up — with each stage teaching distinct clinical skills and together forming the curriculum for training metabolomics clinicians.**

## Evidence

### The Six Stages
| Stage | Focus | Learning Objective | Key Skill |
|-------|-------|-------------------|-----------|
| **1. Assessment** | Intake ritual | Understand patient as metabolic story | History taking, symptom clustering |
| **2. Sample Logistics** | Pre-analytical discipline | Ensure diagnostic accuracy | Collection protocols, handling |
| **3. Analysis** | OAT + fatty acid interpretation | Create coherent metabolic narrative | Pattern recognition, correlation |
| **4. Reporting** | Metabolic storytelling | Explain findings in human language | Communication, visualization |
| **5. Treatment** | Therapeutic algorithm | Mechanism-based prescribing | Protocol selection, dosing |
| **6. Follow-up** | Longitudinal care | Evaluate trends and compliance | Monitoring, adjustment |

### Socratic Challenges by Stage
| Stage | Challenge Question |
|-------|-------------------|
| Assessment | "What do you notice about when the fatigue occurs?" |
| Analysis | "How do these OAT markers connect to the symptoms?" |
| Treatment | "Why this supplement and not that one?" |
| Follow-up | "What has changed? What hasn't? Why?" |

### Training Progression
| Phase | Stages | Competency |
|-------|--------|------------|
| **Foundation** | 1-2 | Patient interaction, sample integrity |
| **Application** | 3-4 | Interpretation, communication |
| **Integration** | 5-6 | Treatment, longitudinal care |

## Clinical Implication
- **Curriculum design:** Each stage is a teaching module with specific competencies
- **Quality assurance:** Errors at early stages (logistics) compromise later stages
- **Patient experience:** Smooth flow between stages builds trust and compliance
- **METHAP relevance:** Research protocols follow similar stages — assessment, sampling, analysis, reporting — making this framework transferable to study design
