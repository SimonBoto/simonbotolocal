## Core Claim
**The RNA world hypothesis proposes that early life was based on RNA as both genetic material and catalyst (ribozymes), before DNA and proteins evolved their modern roles** — this resolves the chicken-egg paradox of which came first, and is supported by molecular fossils (rRNA, tRNA) and laboratory evidence of self-replicating RNAs.

## Evidence

### RNA's Dual Capacity
| Function | Evidence | Significance |
|----------|----------|--------------|
| **Information storage** | Base pairing (A-U, G-C) | Like DNA, but less stable |
| **Catalytic activity** | Ribosomal RNA catalyzes peptide bonds | Pre-protein catalyst |
| **Self-replication** | Created in vitro | Evolutionary potential |

### Molecular Fossils in Modern Cells
| Feature | Ancient Origin | Modern Function |
|---------|---------------|-----------------|
| **Ribosomes** | rRNA catalytic | Protein synthesis |
| **tRNA** | Adapter molecule | Codon-amino acid link |
| **ATP, NAD⁺, FAD, CoA** | All contain adenosine | Energy/coenzymes |

### Transition to Modern Biology
```
RNA World → RNA + Proteins → DNA/RNA/Protein World
   ↓              ↓                    ↓
RNA stores    Proteins better    DNA more stable
RNA catalyzes  catalysts         information storage
```

## Clinical Implication
- **Ribosomal RNA is ancient** — antibiotics target bacterial rRNA (evolutionary conservation)
- **RNA-based therapeutics** — mRNA vaccines, RNAi exploit RNA versatility
- **METHAP relevance:** Understanding evolutionary origins informs appreciation of metabolic conservation; core pathways (glycolysis, PPP) are ancient and robust
