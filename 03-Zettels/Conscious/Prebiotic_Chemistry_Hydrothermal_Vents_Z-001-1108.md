# Prebiotic Chemistry — Hydrothermal Vents

## Core Claim
**Hydrothermal vents at the ocean floor provide conditions that may have facilitated prebiotic chemistry—temperature gradients, mineral catalysis (iron-sulfur), and chemical disequilibrium that could drive organic synthesis from simple precursors** — the iron-sulfur world hypothesis suggests mineral surfaces catalyzed first metabolic reactions before enzymes evolved.

## Evidence

### Hydrothermal Vent Environment
| Parameter | Value | Significance |
|-----------|-------|--------------|
| **Temperature** | 60-400°C | Thermal energy for reactions |
| **Pressure** | 200-400 atm | Stabilizes organic molecules |
| **pH gradient** | Vent (alkaline) vs seawater (acidic) | Energy source |
| **Minerals** | Iron, nickel, sulfide | Catalytic surfaces |

### Iron-Sulfur Mineral Catalysis
| Mineral | Formula | Catalytic Activity |
|---------|---------|-------------------|
| **Pyrite** | FeS₂ | Electron transfer, surface catalysis |
| **Mackinawite** | FeS | Reducing agent, Fe-S cluster precursor |
| **Greigite** | Fe₃S₄ | Magnetic, redox chemistry |

### Reactions Catalyzed
- **CO₂ reduction** → formate, acetate
- **N₂ reduction** → ammonia (Haber-Bosch-like)
- **Organic synthesis** → amino acids, bases from simple precursors

## Clinical Implication
- **Fe-S clusters universal in metabolism** — evolutionary legacy of mineral catalysis
- **Mitochondrial Complex I/II** — contain Fe-S clusters from ancient origins
- **METHAP relevance:** Iron (if deficient) and antioxidants protect Fe-S clusters in modern metabolism
