## Core Claim
**Ribose is a five-carbon aldopentose sugar that forms the structural backbone of RNA and ATP, connecting carbohydrate metabolism (via PPP) to genetic information storage and energy transfer** — its 2'-OH group distinguishes RNA from DNA and enables catalytic functions.

## Evidence

### Chemical Structure
| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Formula** | C₅H₁₀O₅ |
| **Class** | Aldopentose |
| **Ring form** | Furanose (5-membered) |
| **Stereoisomer** | D-ribose (natural) |

### Biological Roles
| Role | Function | Significance |
|------|----------|--------------|
| **RNA backbone** | Phosphodiester bonds | Genetic information |
| **ATP structure** | Links adenine to phosphates | Energy currency |
| **Coenzymes** | NAD⁺, FAD, CoA components | Redox reactions |
| **2'-OH group** | RNA catalysis (ribozymes) | Distinction from DNA |

### Metabolic Origin (PPP)
```
Glucose-6-P → [PPP] → Ribose-5-P + NADPH
```

| PPP Phase | Products | When Active |
|-----------|----------|-------------|
| **Oxidative** | Ribose-5-P, NADPH | High biosynthetic demand |
| **Non-oxidative** | Sugar interconversions | Excess ribose |

## Clinical Implication
- **Rapidly dividing tissues** (bone marrow, gut) have high PPP activity for ribose
- **Ribose supplements** marketed for energy — limited evidence
- **Deoxyribose** (DNA) synthesized from ribose by ribonucleotide reductase
- **METHAP relevance:** High metabolic turnover requires robust nucleotide synthesis; B-vitamins support PPP enzymes
