# , Cholesterol, and Metabolic Syndrome

> Metabolic syndrome represents a pro-inflammatory, pro-atherogenic lipid profile characterized by elevated TG, low HDL, and small dense LDL driven by  resistance.

## Metabolic Syndrome Lipid Profile

**Dyslipidemia Pattern:**
- **TG:** >150 mg/dL
- **HDL:** Men <40, Women <50 mg/dL
- **Small dense LDL:** Predominant subtype
- **Elevated ApoB:** Reflects particle number

## Mechanisms

**Insulin Resistance:**
- Increases VLDL production
- Delays VLDL clearance
- Increases CETP activity
- Promotes sdLDL

**Inflammation:**
- Cytokines alter lipid 
- Reduced LPL activity
- Acute phase increases VLDL
- HDL becomes dysfunctional

**Adipose Tissue:**
- Releases free fatty acids
- Promotes hepatic VLDL
- Adipokine imbalance

**Oxidative Stress:**
- Increases ROS
- Promotes LDL oxidation
- Impairs HDL function

## Clinical Implications

- LDL lowering crucial
- Address insulin resistance (root cause)
- TG reduction (fibrates, omega-3)
- Quality of HDL matters
