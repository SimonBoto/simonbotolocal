# Insulin Discovery: Banting and Best

## The 1921 Breakthrough
> "The discovery of insulin in 1921 by Banting and Best helped to clarify the crucial role of glucose in the body's metabolic processes."

### Frederick Banting (1881-1941)
- Canadian physician and scientist
- Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine (1923)
- Conceived the idea for isolating pancreatic extract
- Collaborated with Charles Best at University of Toronto

### Charles Best (1899-1978)
- Canadian physiologist and biochemist
- Graduate student working with Banting
- Performed key experiments isolating insulin
- Co-developed the first effective insulin treatment

## Key Scientific Contributions
- **Pancreatic extract isolation:** Developed method to extract insulin from pancreatic tissue
- **Diabetes treatment:** First successful treatment of human diabetes with insulin
- **Metabolic integration:** Clarified the crucial role of glucose in the body's metabolic processes
- **Clinical revolution:** Transformed diabetes from fatal disease to manageable condition

## Historical Context
- Pre-1921: Diabetes was uniformly fatal
- 1921: Banting and Best's experiments with dogs demonstrated insulin's effect
- 1922: First human patient (Leonard Thompson) treated successfully
- 1923: Nobel Prize awarded to Banting and Macleod (controversially excluding Best)

## Relationship to Glucose Metabolism
- Insulin regulates glucose uptake by cells
- Glucose is essential for cellular energy production
- Diabetes represents failure of glucose regulation
- The discovery completed the understanding of glucose homeostasis

## Significance
The insulin discovery represents one of the most important medical breakthroughs of the 20th century. It not only saved countless lives but also established the foundation for modern endocrinology and metabolic medicine. The discovery directly connected glucose chemistry with clinical physiology, completing the century-long journey from Marggraf's isolation to clinical application.
