## Overview

Two primary methods for extracting oil from seeds: **mechanical pressing** (traditional/modern) and **solvent extraction** (industrial). The method dramatically affects oil quality, yield, and potential contamination.

## Method 1: Expeller Press (Mechanical)

**Process:**
1. Seeds cleaned and dehulled
2. Heated slightly (to improve flow)
3. Fed into continuous screw press
4. Pressure (50-100 MPa) squeezes oil through perforated barrel
5. Oil collected, filtered
6. Residual cake (5-10% oil remaining) used for animal feed

**Types:**
| Type | Temperature | Characteristics |
|------|-------------|-----------------|
| Cold-press | <40°C | Minimal heat, better nutrient retention |
| Warm-press | 60-90°C | Higher yield, some nutrient loss |

**Advantages:**
- No chemical solvents
- Lower environmental impact
- Retains more natural antioxidants (tocopherols)
- No solvent residues

**Disadvantages:**
- Lower yield (leaves 5-10% oil in cake)
- Higher cost per unit oil
- Requires more energy for pressing
- Shorter shelf life (more natural compounds)

## Method 2: Hexane Solvent Extraction

**Process:**
1. Seeds flaked to increase surface area
2. Pre-pressed to remove ~60% oil
3. Flakes mixed with **hexane** (boiling point: 69°C)
4. Hexane dissolves oil (forms miscella)
5. Miscella filtered from solid residue (marc)
6. **Distillation** recovers hexane for reuse
7. Desolventizer-toaster (DT) removes final hexane from marc
8. Oil refined further

**Yield:**
- Extracts **>99%** of oil from seeds
- Significantly more efficient than pressing alone

**Advantages:**
- Maximum oil recovery
- Lower production costs
- Hexane recyclable (closed-loop systems)
- Industry standard for commodity oils

**Disadvantages:**
- **Hexane residues** (trace amounts remain)
- Petroleum-derived solvent
- Higher energy requirements (distillation)
- Environmental concerns (VOC emissions)
- Destroys heat-sensitive nutrients

## Hexane Residue Concerns

**Regulatory Limits:**
- FDA: <25 ppm hexane in food oils
- EU: Similar limits for hexane residues

**Health Considerations:**
- Hexane classified as **neurotoxin** (chronic exposure)
- Industrial safety concern for workers
- Food-grade hexane (high purity) required for extraction
- Modern processes achieve near-complete removal

**Detection:**
- Headspace GC-MS analysis
- Sensory thresholds much lower than safety limits

## Comparison Matrix

| Factor | Expeller Press | Hexane Extraction |
|--------|----------------|-------------------|
| Oil yield | 90-95% | >99% |
| Cost | Higher | Lower |
| Solvent residues | None | Trace (<25 ppm) |
| Heat exposure | Moderate | High (distillation) |
| Nutrient retention | Better | Reduced |
| Environmental impact | Lower | Higher (VOCs) |
| Scale | Small-medium | Industrial |
| Shelf life | Shorter | Longer (more refined) |

## Hybrid Approach

**Pre-press + Solvent:**
1. Mechanical press extracts ~60% oil
2. Pressed cake goes to solvent extraction
3. Maximizes efficiency while reducing solvent load
4. Most common industrial setup

## Emerging Alternatives

**Supercritical CO₂ Extraction:**
- CO₂ under pressure acts as solvent
- No toxic residues
- Preserves heat-sensitive compounds
- High equipment costs (limited adoption)

**Enzyme-Assisted Extraction:**
- Cell-wall degrading enzymes increase yield
- Lower temperatures possible
- Higher costs, slower process

**Aqueous Extraction:**
- Water-based methods
- Very low yields currently
- Research stage for most applications

## Implications for Consumers

**Cold-pressed/Cold-extracted:**
- Marketing term — not legally defined
- Generally implies <50°C processing
- Better omega-3 preservation
- More natural antioxidants retained

**"Expeller-pressed" on label:**
- Indicates mechanical extraction
- Often combined with heat
- Doesn't guarantee "cold" processing

##  Relevance

**al Impact:**
- Cold-pressed: Higher vitamin E, phytosterols
- Solvent-extracted: Stripped of many micronutrients during refining
-  content similar regardless of method

**Toxin Formation:**
- Heat in both methods can initiate oxidation
- Hexane extraction followed by high-heat refining
- Cold-press minimizes initial oxidation
