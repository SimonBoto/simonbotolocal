# Glucose Ribose Interconversion PPP Link

## Overview
Glucose-6-phosphate enters the pentose phosphate pathway to become ribose-5-phosphate, creating a metabolic bridge between carbohydrate and nucleotide metabolism.

## The Conversion Pathway

```
Glucose (dietary or from glycogen)
    ↓ Hexokinase/Glucokinase
Glucose-6-phosphate
    ↓ PPP oxidative phase
Ribulose-5-phosphate + 2 NADPH + CO₂
    ↓ Isomerization
Ribose-5-phosphate
    ↓ PRPP synthetase
PRPP (phosphoribosyl pyrophosphate)
    ↓ Base attachment
Nucleotides (AMP, GMP, etc.)
```

## Why This Link Matters

### Metabolic Context
| Situation | Flow Direction | Purpose |
|-----------|----------------|---------|
| **High glucose, need nucleotides** | Glucose → Ribose | Support DNA replication |
| **High glucose, excess energy** | Glucose → Fat storage | Storage, not ribose |
| **Fasting, need nucleotides** | Protein → Ribose (indirect) | Maintain synthesis |
| **Rapid growth** | High PPP flux | Meet ribose demand |

### Tissue-Specific Needs
| Tissue | Ribose Demand | PPP Activity |
|--------|---------------|--------------|
| **Bone marrow** | Very high (blood cell production) | High |
| **Intestinal mucosa** | High (rapid turnover) | High |
| **Skin** | Moderate (renewal) | Moderate |
| **Muscle** | Low | Low |
| **Brain** | Moderate (membrane turnover) | Moderate |

## Alternative Sources of Ribose

### De Novo (from glucose via PPP)
- Primary source under normal conditions
- Requires functional PPP enzymes
- Generates NADPH as bonus

### Salvage Pathway
- Reuses ribose from degraded nucleotides
- More energy efficient
- Important in tissues with limited de novo capacity (brain)

### Dietary Ribose
- Limited absorption
- Most dietary nucleic acids degraded
- Minimal contribution to body ribose pool

## Clinical Implications

### Cancer Metabolism
- Tumors often show high PPP activity
- Need ribose for rapid proliferation
- NADPH supports antioxidant defenses against ROS
- **Therapeutic target:** G6PD inhibitors

### Ribose Supplementation
- **D-ribose** marketed for energy enhancement
- **Claims:** Improved exercise recovery, heart function
- **Evidence:** Mixed; some benefit in heart failure
- **Mechanism:** Bypasses slow PPP, directly provides ribose

### Metabolic Disorders
- **Transketolase deficiency:** Impairs non-oxidative PPP
- **Ribose-5-phosphate isomerase deficiency:** Rare, neurological symptoms
- Both affect glucose-to-ribose flux

## Evolutionary Significance
- Link between energy metabolism (glycolysis) and information storage (nucleic acids)
- Suggests evolutionary pressure for coordinated regulation
- PPP likely evolved as expansion of glycolytic capacity
