# Conflicts of Interest Framework

> **Core Claim:** Disclosure of financial, intellectual, and personal conflicts maintains scientific integrity and reader trust.

## Types of Conflicts

| Type | Examples | Severity |
|------|----------|----------|
| **Financial** | Grants, stocks, consulting fees | High |
| **Intellectual** | Competing research, prior publications | Moderate |
| **Personal** | Professional rivalries, personal relationships | Variable |

## Disclosure Requirements

- Required by most journals
- Must be comprehensive (not selective)
- Updated if new conflicts arise
- Includes past 3-5 years typically

## Management Strategies

| Approach | When Used |
|----------|-----------|
| **Disclosure** | Standard for all COI |
| **Editor awareness** | Editor informed of conflict |
| **Recusal** | Reviewer steps aside |
| **Transparency to readers** | Published with article |

## Serious Violation

> **Undisclosed COI is research misconduct** — undermines trust in science.

## Reviewer Responsibility

- Declare your own COI before accepting review
- Note undisclosed COI in manuscript
- Maintain objectivity despite potential bias
