# Palmitoleic Acid — Omega-7, MUFA Marker

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**Palmitoleic acid:** From "palmitic" (palm) + "oleic" (oil)

**Omega-7:** First double bond at 7th carbon from methyl end

**Also called:**
- C16:1 (16 carbons, 1 double bond)
- Palmitoleate

source-litnote: Synthesized from clinical practice
---

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**Monounsaturated fatty acid (MUFA):**
- 16-carbon omega-7
- Produced from palmitic acid (desaturation)

**Metabolic significance:**
- Lipokine (fat-derived signaling molecule)
- Improves insulin sensitivity
- Reduces inflammation
- **Inversely associated with metabolic syndrome**

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### Palmitoleic Acid Levels

| Level (% of total fatty acids) | Status | Interpretation |
|--------------------------------|--------|----------------|
| **<1.5%** | Low | Lower protective effect |
| **1.5-3.0%** | Optimal | Cardiometabolic benefit |
| **>3.0%** | High | May indicate high carbohydrate intake |

### Clinical Significance

**Low palmitoleic associated with:**
- Metabolic syndrome
- Type 2 diabetes
- Cardiovascular disease

**High palmitoleic:**
- Carbohydrate conversion to fat (de novo lipogenesis)
- May indicate high sugar/refined carb intake

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### For Low Palmitoleic

**Dietary sources:**
- Macadamia nuts (richest source)
- Sea buckthorn oil
- Avocado
- Oily fish

**Reduce refined carbohydrates:**
- If high palmitoleic from carb conversion
