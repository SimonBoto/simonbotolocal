# Linoleic Acid (LA) — Essential Omega-6

## Biochemistry

**Structure:** C18:2 ω6
- 18 carbon skeleton
- 2 double bonds
- First double bond at carbon 6

**Classification:** Essential fatty acid (cannot be synthesized)
- **Only essential ω6 fatty acid**

## Sources

### High LA Sources (Problematic in Excess)
| Source | LA Content |
|--------|------------|
| **Corn oil** | Very high |
| **Soybean oil** | Very high |
| **Sunflower oil** | Very high |
| **Safflower oil** | Very high |
| **Cottonseed oil** | Very high |
| **Canola oil** | Moderate-high |
| **Most nuts** | Moderate |
| **Grain-fed meat** | Moderate-high |

### Historical vs. Modern Intake
| Era | Primary Fat Source | LA Intake |
|-----|-------------------|-----------|
| **Pre-industrial** | Animal fats, olive oil | Low |
| **Modern** | Vegetable oils | Very high |

## The LA Problem

### Excessive LA Intake
**Modern Western diet:** LA consumption has increased 10-20x

**Consequences:**
- Pro-inflammatory AA production
- Oxidative stress (LA easily oxidized)
- LDL oxidation
- Atherogenesis
- Displaces ω3 from membranes

### The Dual Nature

**Adequate LA (balanced):**
- Reduces total cholesterol
- Improves all-cause mortality
- Essential for skin health
- Wound healing

**Excessive LA:**
- Promotes inflammation
- Oxidative damage
- Cardiovascular risk
- Metabolic dysfunction

## Metabolism

**Conversion pathway:**
LA → (Δ6-desaturase) → GLA → (elongase) → DGLA → (Δ5-desaturase) → AA

**Critical enzyme:** Δ6-desaturase
- Same enzyme used for ALA → EPA conversion
- **Competition with ω3**
- Rate-limiting step

## Clinical Interpretation

### Elevated LA Indicates
1. **High vegetable oil consumption**
   - Processed foods
   - Restaurant meals
   - Home cooking with seed oils

2. **Conjugated LA supplementation**

3. **Reduced cofactors**
   - Inability to metabolize LA
   - Accumulation

### Clinical Associations with High LA
| Condition | Mechanism |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Inflammatory diseases** | AA precursor |
| **Malignancy** | Pro-inflammatory environment |
| **Cardiovascular disease** | Oxidized LDL |
| **Neural healing** | Context-dependent |

### Reduced LA Indicates
- Rarely from low intake (ubiquitous in modern diet)
- **Skin lesions**
- **Impaired wound healing**

## The ω6:ω3 Ratio

### Historical Balance
**Evolutionary ratio:** 1:1 to 4:1

### Modern Imbalance
**Current Western diet:** 20:1 (ω6:ω3)

**Health implications:**
- Chronic inflammation
- Insulin resistance
- Obesity
- Cardiovascular disease

### Optimal Target
**ω6:ω3 ratio = 2-4:1**

## Interventions

### Reduce LA Intake
1. **Eliminate vegetable oils:**
   - Corn, soybean, sunflower, safflower
   - Use instead: olive oil, butter, coconut oil, avocado oil

2. **Reduce processed foods:**
   - Most contain vegetable oils
   - Read labels

3. **Choose meat wisely:**
   - Grass-fed > grain-fed
   - Lower LA content

4. **Limit nuts moderately:**
   - Not eliminate, but don't overconsume

### Support Metabolism
- **Zinc** — Δ6-desaturase cofactor
- **Magnesium** — enzyme function
- **B3** — desaturase activity
- **Reduce alcohol** — inhibits conversion
