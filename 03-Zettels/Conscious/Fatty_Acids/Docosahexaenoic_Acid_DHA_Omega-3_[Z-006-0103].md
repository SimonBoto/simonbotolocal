# Docosahexaenoic Acid (DHA) — Brain & Retina Omega-3

## Biochemistry

**Structure:** C22:6 ω3
- 22 carbon skeleton
- 6 double bonds
- Most unsaturated common fatty acid

**Formation:**
- Dietary intake (primary)
- Conversion from EPA (limited)

## Sources

| Source | DHA Content |
|--------|-------------|
| **Fatty fish** | High |
| **Algae oil** | Vegetarian source |
| **Fish oil supplements** | Concentrated |
| **Breast milk** | Infant nutrition |
| **Fortified foods** | Variable |

## Unique Functions

### Brain
- **40% of brain's polyunsaturated fats**
- **50% of neuronal membrane weight**
- Synaptic plasticity
- Neurotransmission
- Neuroprotection

### Retina
- **60% of retinal photoreceptor membranes**
- Visual acuity
- Phototransduction
- Protection from light damage

### Cell Membranes
- **Membrane fluidity** — maintains function
- **Receptor function** — signal transduction
- **Ion channel regulation**

## Clinical Applications

### Pregnancy & Infancy
| Stage | Importance |
|-------|------------|
| **Pregnancy** | Fetal brain development |
| **Third trimester** | Peak DHA accumulation |
| **Breastfeeding** | Infant brain growth |
| **Early childhood** | Cognitive development |

### Cognitive Function
- Memory
- Learning
- Processing speed
- Attention

### Mental Health
- Depression (adjunctive)
- Bipolar disorder
- Cognitive decline prevention

### Visual Health
- Macular degeneration prevention
- Dry eye syndrome
- Visual development in infants

## DHA vs. EPA

| Feature | DHA | EPA |
|---------|-----|-----|
| **Primary role** | Structural (brain, retina) | Anti-inflammatory signaling |
| **Brain uptake** | High | Limited |
| **Cardiovascular** | Moderate | Strong |
| **Inflammation** | Moderate | Strong |

## Optimal Intake

### General Population
- **250-500mg/day** combined EPA+DHA
- Minimum for general health

### Pregnancy/Lactation
- **300-600mg DHA/day**
- Critical for fetal/infant development

### Cognitive Support
- **1g DHA/day**
- Higher doses for therapeutic effects

### Children
- **100-200mg/day**
- Age-dependent

## Testing

### RBC DHA Levels
- **Desirable:** >4% of total fatty acids
- **Optimal:** 6-8%

### Omega-3 Index
- EPA + DHA in RBC membranes
- **Target:** >8%

## Deficiency Signs

- Poor memory
- Difficulty concentrating
- Visual disturbances
- Dry eyes
- Mood changes

## Special Considerations

### Vegetarians/Vegans
- Algae-derived DHA available
- More expensive than fish oil
- Comparable efficacy

### Pregnancy
- Critical third trimester
- Supplementation recommended
- Safe at therapeutic doses

### Aging
- Brain DHA decreases with age
- Supplementation may slow cognitive decline
- Part of dementia prevention strategy
