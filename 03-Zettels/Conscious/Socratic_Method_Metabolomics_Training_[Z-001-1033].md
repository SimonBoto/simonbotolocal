# Socratic Method  Training

## Overview
Teaching metabolomics through guided inquiry rather than information delivery. Trainees learn by confronting their own reasoning errors.

## The Three-Step Socratic Cycle

### Step 1: Elicit the Error
> "Let the trainee make a common error."

**Process:**
- Present clinical scenario
- Ask for interpretation or plan
- Allow trainee to commit to reasoning
- Do not interrupt or correct prematurely

**Purpose:** Surface actual thinking patterns, not theoretical knowledge

### Step 2: Model the Correction
> "Model the correction."

**Process:**
- Demonstrate correct reasoning
- Show the analytical path
- Reference the specific error made
- Connect to broader principles

**Purpose:** Provide concrete example of expert thinking

### Step 3: Explain Why the Pattern Fails
> "Explain why their pattern leads to mistakes."

**Process:**
- Identify the cognitive trap
- Explain downstream consequences
- Provide alternative mental models
- Offer practice scenarios

**Purpose:** Prevent recurrence by addressing root cause

## Example Socratic Prompts

| Stage | Prompt | Target Error |
|-------|--------|--------------|
| Analysis | "Lactate + BHB elevated. l blockage or ketogenic physiology?" | Pattern confusion |
| Treatment | "Impaired β-oxidation. Carnitine, diet, or cofactors first?" | Prescribing sequence |
| Reporting | "Explain  redox imbalance to a 78-year-old farmer." | Translation failure |
| Follow-up | "4-week check: what should you evaluate?" | Premature lab focus |

## Key Principle
> "Teach your doctors how to *reason* like metabolomics clinicians."
