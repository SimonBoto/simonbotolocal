#  and  Absorption — Dose-Response Relationship

> Vitamin D increases intestinal calcium absorption from a baseline of 10-15% to a maximum of 30-40% through active transport s, with the degree of enhancement proportional to vitamin D status and 25(OH)D levels.

## Mechanism of Absorption

### Active Transport (D-Dependent)
**Calbindin-D9k:**
- Vitamin D-induced calcium-binding protein
- Facilitates calcium movement across enterocyte
- Concentrates calcium for export

**TRPV6 Channel:**
- Epithelial calcium channel
- Located on brush border
- Vitamin D upregulates expression
- Rate-limiting step for entry

**PMCA1b (Plasma Membrane Ca²⁺-ATPase):**
- Basolateral export pump
- Active transport against gradient
- Vitamin D regulated

### Passive Diffusion (D-Independent)
- Paracellular pathway
- Concentration-dependent
- Accounts for ~10% of absorption
- Not vitamin D dependent

## Dose-Response Relationship

| 25(OH)D Level | Calcium Absorption | Status |
|---------------|-------------------|--------|
| <10 ng/mL | 10-15% | Severe deficiency |
| 10-20 ng/mL | 15-20% | Deficiency |
| 20-30 ng/mL | 25-30% | Insufficiency |
| 30-50 ng/mL | 30-35% | Sufficiency |
| >50 ng/mL | 35-40% | Optimal |

**Plateau:**
- Maximum absorption ~40%
- Achieved at 25(OH)D >50 ng/mL
- Further D increases do not enhance absorption

## METHAP High-Dose Context

**Protocol:**
- Dose: 8000 IU/day
- Target 25(OH)D: >40-50 ng/mL
- Expected absorption: 35-40%

**Comparison to Standard:**
- Standard intake (400-800 IU): ~20-25% absorption
- METHAP (8000 IU): ~35-40% absorption
- **~50% increase in calcium absorption**

**Safety Implications:**
- Higher absorption = higher calcium load
- Requires K2 for proper direction
- Requires monitoring for hypercalcemia
- Mg optimization prevents overshoot

## Regulation and Saturation

**Upregulation:**
- Low calcium intake → ↑ VDR expression
- ↑ D sensitivity
- Adaptive increase

**Downregulation:**
- High calcium → ↓ VDR expression
- Protective mechanism
- Prevents hyperabsorption

**METHAP Consideration:**
- High-dose D maximizes absorption capacity
- Dietary calcium intake matters
- Supplemental calcium may be unnecessary
