## Core Claim
**The ACCORD study definitively showed that intensive glucose lowering to near-normal levels (HbA1c <6.0%) in type 2 diabetes increased mortality by 22% and did not significantly reduce cardiovascular events** — challenging the "lower is better" glucose paradigm and suggesting harm from aggressive treatment.

## Study Design

| Parameter | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Participants** | 10,251 high-risk T2D patients |
| **Intervention** | Intensive (HbA1c <6.0%) vs standard (7.0-7.9%) |
| **Duration** | Stopped early at 3.5 years |
| **Primary outcome** | CVD events |

## Key Findings

### Mortality
- **22% higher death rate** in intensive group
- Study stopped early due to safety concerns

### Cardiovascular Events
- **No significant reduction** in primary CVD endpoint
- Aggressive glucose lowering did not protect hearts

### Adverse Effects
- **Hypoglycemia** more common in intensive group
- **Weight gain** more common in intensive group

## Impact on Guidelines
- Revised guidelines abandoned tight glucose targets
- HbA1c <7% became acceptable (not <6%)
- Shift toward individualized targets
- Recognition of treatment harm potential

## Clinical Implications
- "Lower is better" is WRONG for glucose in T2D
- Aggressive treatment can kill
- Balance glucose control with treatment safety
- Consider insulin-promoting effects of therapy
