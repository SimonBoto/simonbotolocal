## Core Claim
**Beta-hydroxybutyrate (BHB) is chemically a β-hydroxy acid (CH₃-CHOH-CH₂-COOH), not a true ketone — the name "ketone body" is functional (part of the ketone body system), not structural; BHB lacks a carbonyl group and instead has a hydroxyl at the β-carbon** — this distinction matters for chemistry, interconversion, and nomenclature.

## Evidence

### Structural Comparison
| Feature | Acetoacetate (True Ketone) | BHB (Hydroxy Acid) |
|---------|---------------------------|-------------------|
| **Formula** | CH₃-CO-CH₂-COOH | CH₃-CHOH-CH₂-COOH |
| **Functional group** | Ketone (C=O) at β-carbon | Hydroxyl (-OH) at β-carbon |
| **Chemical class** | β-keto acid | β-hydroxy acid |
| **Reduction state** | Less reduced | More reduced (higher energy) |

### Why "Ketone Body" Then?
| Reason | Explanation |
|--------|-------------|
| **Interconvertible** | BHB ↔ AcAc via β-OHBDH (rapid equilibrium) |
| **Metabolic class** | Both yield acetyl-CoA for energy |
| **Functional grouping** | All three share metabolic fate |
| **Historical** | Discovered as part of ketosis metabolism |

### Interconversion Chemistry
```
Acetoacetate + NADH + H⁺ ↔ BHB + NAD⁺
     (ketone)              (hydroxy acid)
```
- **Enzyme:** β-hydroxybutyrate dehydrogenase (β-OHBDH)
- **Direction:** Driven by NADH/NAD⁺ ratio
- **Mitochondrial:** Tightly coupled to redox state

## Clinical Implication
- **Nomenclature confusion:** "Ketone" in ketone body is functional, not chemical
- **BHB as fuel:** More reduced = higher ATP yield per molecule
- **Redox indicator:** BHB/AcAc ratio reflects mitochondrial NADH/NAD⁺
- **METHAP relevance:** Understanding BHB chemistry explains why it's the preferred circulating form — more stable, more reduced, better fuel
