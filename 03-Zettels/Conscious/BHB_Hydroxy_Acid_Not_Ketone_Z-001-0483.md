# BHB is a Hydroxy Acid, Not a Ketone

## Chemical Distinction

### True Ketone (Acetoacetate)
```
CH₃-CO-CH₂-COOH
     ↑
   Carbonyl C=O
```
- Carbonyl carbon bonded to two carbons
- Class: **Ketone**

### BHB (Beta-Hydroxybutyrate)
```
CH₃-CHOH-CH₂-COOH
      ↑
    Hydroxyl -OH
```
- Hydroxyl group at β-carbon (3-position)
- Class: **Hydroxy acid** (β-hydroxy acid)

## Why the Confusion?

### Historical/Functional Nomenclature
1. **Interconvertible:** BHB ↔ AcAc via β-OHBDH
2. **Ketone :** Part of ketone body system
3. **Major circulating form:** Most abundant during ketosis
4. **Energy equivalent:** Both yield acetyl-CoA

### Why "Ketone Body" is Functional, Not Chemical
- Describes **metabolic class**, not chemical structure
- All three share: 4-carbon skeleton, energy-yielding
- AcAc and BHB rapidly interconvert in vivo

## Reduction 
```
Acetoacetate + NADH + H⁺ → BHB + NAD⁺
```
- **Reduction** of ketone to alcohol
- Reversible (β-OHBDH enzyme)
- Direction depends on NADH/NAD⁺ ratio

##  Implication
**Ketone tests:**
- **Urine strips:** Detect AcAc (nitroprusside reaction)
- **Blood meters:** Detect BHB (specific enzymatic)
- **Different readings possible** due to AcAc:BHB ratio variations
