## Core Concepts

Glucocorticoid-induced osteoporosis (GIOP) is the most common secondary cause of osteoporosis, with unique pathophysiology and management requirements.

## Pathophysiological Mechanisms

### Direct Effects on Bone Cells
- **Osteoblasts:** Apoptosis, reduced differentiation, inhibited collagen synthesis
- **Osteocytes:** Apoptosis, loss of mechanosensing, reduced sclerostin regulation
- **Osteoclasts:** Initial stimulation (early phase), then suppression (late phase)

### Indirect Effects on Calcium Homeostasis
- **Intestinal calcium absorption:** Reduced (vitamin D metabolism impaired)
- **Renal calcium excretion:** Increased
- **Parathyroid hormone:** Secondary hyperparathyroidism develops

### Temporal Pattern
| Time | Bone Loss Pattern | Dominant Mechanism |
|------|-------------------|--------------------|
| **0–6 months** | Rapid loss (10–15%) | Osteoblast apoptosis, osteocyte death |
| **6–12 months** | Slowing of loss | Continued osteoblast suppression |
| **>12 months** | Plateau, then continued loss | Secondary hyperparathyroidism, reduced bone formation |
