# Lactic Acid — Anaerobic Glycolysis Marker

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**Lactic acid:** From Latin "lac" (milk)

**Named for:** First isolated from sour milk

**Also called:**
- Lactate
- 2-hydroxypropanoic acid

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**End product of anaerobic glycolysis:**
- Glucose → pyruvate → lactate (when oxygen limited)
- Regenerates NAD+ for continued glycolysis

**Normal production:**
- RBCs (no mitochondria)
- Muscle during intense exercise
- Gut bacteria

**Clearance:**
- Liver (Cori cycle: lactate → glucose)
- Kidneys

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### Lactic Acid Levels

| Level (mmol/L) | Status | Interpretation |
|----------------|--------|----------------|
| **<1.0** | Normal | Adequate oxygenation |
| **1.0-2.0** | Borderline | Mild elevation |
| **2.0-4.0** | Elevated | Lactic acidosis (mild) |
| **>4.0** | High | Lactic acidosis (severe) |
| **>10** | Critical | Life-threatening |

### Causes of Elevation

**Type A (Tissue hypoxia):**
| Cause | Mechanism |
|-------|-----------|
| **Shock** | Poor perfusion |
| **Severe hypoxia** | Low oxygen |
| **Severe anemia** | Reduced oxygen carrying |
| **Carbon monoxide poisoning** | Impaired oxygen delivery |

**Type B (Metabolic):**
| Cause | Mechanism |
|-------|-----------|
| **Thiamine (B1) deficiency** | Impaired pyruvate dehydrogenase |
| **Mitochondrial dysfunction** | Impaired oxidative metabolism |
| **Liver disease** | Impaired clearance |
| **Sepsis** | Mitochondrial dysfunction |
| **Metformin** | Inhibits complex I |
| **Malignancy** | Warburg effect |
| **Seizures** | Muscle overactivity |

**OAT-specific (urinary):**
- Mitochondrial dysfunction
- B1 deficiency
- Gut dysbiosis

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### For Elevated Lactic Acid

**Emergency (severe lactic acidosis):**
- Treat underlying cause
- IV fluids
- Bicarbonate (if pH <7.1)
- Thiamine 100-200 mg IV

**Chronic/OAT elevation:**
- Thiamine supplementation (B1)
- B-complex support
- Mitochondrial support (CoQ10, carnitine)
- Address gut dysbiosis

## 6. CONNECTED BIOMARKERS

| Biomarker | Relationship | Pattern |
|-----------|--------------|---------|
| **Pyruvate** | Precursor | Lactate:Pyruvate ratio (normal ~10:1) |
| **pH** | Acid-base | Low pH with high lactate |
| **Bicarbonate** | Buffer | Low in lactic acidosis |
| **Anion gap** | Calculation | Elevated in lactic acidosis |

### Clinical Patterns

**Thiamine Deficiency:**
- Lactate: Elevated
- Pyruvate: Elevated
- B1: Low
- **Wernicke-Korsakoff if severe**

**Mitochondrial Dysfunction:**
- Lactate: Elevated
- Pyruvate: Elevated
- Other TCA markers: Abnormal
- **Multiple deficiencies**
