# Benzoic & Hippuric Acid — Preservative & Polyphenol Markers

## Biochemistry

**Source:**
1. **Polyphenol metabolism** — Dietary sources
2. **Preservative consumption** — Sodium benzoate

**Conversion:**
Benzoic Acid → (glycine conjugation in liver) → Hippuric Acid

## Clinical Interpretation

### Elevated Benzoic/Hippuric Indicates

#### 1. High Polyphenol Intake
- Natural dietary sources
- Generally beneficial (antioxidants)

#### 2. Preservative Exposure (More Concerning)
- **Sodium benzoate** in processed foods
- **Low-quality vitamins/supplements** (critical source)
- Soft drinks, sauces, pickles

## The Supplement Trap

**Critical clinical insight:**
Many patients taking "healthy" supplements are unknowingly consuming preservatives:

| Product Type | Risk |
|--------------|------|
| Cheap multivitamins | High sodium benzoate |
| Liquid supplements | Preservation needed |
| Imported supplements | Variable quality |
| Long-shelf-life products | More preservatives |

## Pattern Recognition

### Benzoic Elevated + Hippuric Normal
- Recent preservative intake
- Liver conjugation working
- Check supplement quality

### Both Elevated
- High total load (dietary + preservatives)
- Prioritize reducing preservatives
- Assess liver glycine status

### With Other Dysbiosis Markers
- Gut barrier compromise may increase absorption
- Address dysbiosis first

## Interventions

### Immediate Actions
1. **Audit all supplements** — check labels for sodium benzoate
2. **Switch to high-quality brands** — preservative-free
3. **Reduce processed foods** — especially sauces, dressings

### Support Detoxification
- **Glycine** — cofactor for hippuric acid formation
- **Magnesium** — supports conjugation pathways
- **Glutathione** — general detox support

### Retest
- After 4-6 weeks of supplement change
- Should see significant reduction if preservative source identified
