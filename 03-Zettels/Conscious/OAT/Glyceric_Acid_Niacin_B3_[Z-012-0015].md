# Glyceric Acid — Niacin (B3) & Serine Metabolism Marker

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**Glyceric acid:** Oxidized form of glycerol

**Relation to:**
- Glycerol (3-carbon alcohol)
- Serine metabolism

source-litnote: Synthesized from clinical practice
---

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**Serine degradation pathway:**
- Serine → glycine + 5,10-methylene-THF
- Alternative: Serine → glycerate → glyceric acid

**Niacin (B3) connection:**
- NAD+ synthesis from tryptophan
- Requires B3-dependent enzymes
- **Elevated glyceric may indicate B3 issues**

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### Glyceric Acid Levels

| Level | Status | Interpretation |
|-------|--------|----------------|
| **Normal** | Low | Normal metabolism |
| **Elevated** | High | Serine metabolism issue or B3 deficiency |

### Causes of Elevation

**Niacin (B3) deficiency:**
- Impaired NAD+ synthesis
- Altered serine metabolism

**Serine metabolism disorders:**
- Rare genetic defects
- Enzyme deficiencies

**Mitochondrial dysfunction:**
- Impaired oxidation pathways

## 4. COFACTORS

### B3-Dependent Enzymes

| Enzyme | Function | B3 Form |
|--------|----------|---------|
| **NAD+ dependent dehydrogenases** | Multiple pathways | NAD+ |
| **Serine hydroxymethyltransferase** | Serine-glycine interconversion | NAD+ dependent |

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### For Elevated Glyceric Acid

**Niacin supplementation:**
- B3: 25-50 mg/day
- Niacinamide or nicotinic acid

**Serine support:**
- Ensure adequate protein intake
- B6, folate, B12 (serine-glycine metabolism)
