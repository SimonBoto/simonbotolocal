# 5-HIAA — 5-Hydroxyindoleacetic Acid, Serotonin Metabolite

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**5-HIAA:** 5-Hydroxyindoleacetic acid

**Indole:** Heterocyclic aromatic organic compound

**Acetic acid:** Simple carboxylic acid

source-litnote: Synthesized from clinical practice
---

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**Serotonin (5-HT) metabolite:**
- Serotonin → 5-HIAA (via MAO-A and aldehyde dehydrogenase)
- Primary urinary metabolite
- Reflects whole-body serotonin turnover

**Serotonin functions:**
| System | Function |
|--------|----------|
| **Brain** | Mood, sleep, appetite |
| **Gut** | Motility, secretion (90% of body's serotonin) |
| **Cardiovascular** | Platelet function |

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### 5-HIAA Levels

| Level | Status | Interpretation |
|-------|--------|----------------|
| **Low** | Reduced | Low serotonin turnover |
| **Normal** | Balanced | Normal serotonin |
| **High** | Elevated | High serotonin turnover |

### Causes of Abnormal Levels

**Low 5-HIAA:**
| Cause | Mechanism |
|-------|-----------|
| **Tryptophan deficiency** | Precursor shortage |
| **Malabsorption** | Poor nutrient absorption |
| **Depression** | Reduced serotonin |
| **MAO inhibitors** | Block breakdown (paradoxically low 5-HIAA) |

**High 5-HIAA:**
| Cause | Mechanism |
|-------|-----------|
| **Carcinoid syndrome** | Serotonin-secreting tumor |
| **Serotonin-rich foods** | Bananas, walnuts, avocados |
| **5-HTP supplementation** | Precursor loading |
| **MAO-A activity** | Increased breakdown |

## 4. COFACTORS

### Serotonin Synthesis

| Nutrient | Role | Deficiency Effect |
|----------|------|-------------------|
| **Tryptophan** | Precursor | Reduced serotonin |
| **B6** | Aromatic L-amino acid decarboxylase | Reduced conversion |
| **Iron** | Tryptophan hydroxylase cofactor | Reduced synthesis |
| **Magnesium** | Serotonin receptor function | Altered signaling |

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### For Low 5-HIAA

**Tryptophan support:**
- Tryptophan: 500-2000 mg/day
- 5-HTP: 50-200 mg/day (more direct)

**Cofactors:**
- B6: 50-100 mg/day
- Magnesium: 400-600 mg/day

**Dietary:**
- Turkey, chicken, eggs
- Dairy
- Nuts, seeds

### For High 5-HIAA

**If carcinoid suspected:**
- Medical evaluation
- Imaging
- Specialized care

## 6. CONNECTED BIOMARKERS

| Biomarker | Relationship | Pattern |
|-----------|--------------|---------|
| **HVA** | Dopamine metabolite | HVA:5-HIAA ratio important |
| **Tryptophan** | Precursor | May be low |
| **Chromogranin A** | Neuroendocrine marker | Elevated in carcinoid |

### Clinical Patterns

**Depression:**
- 5-HIAA: Often low
- HVA: May be low
- **Low monoamine metabolites**

**Carcinoid Syndrome:**
- 5-HIAA: Very high
- Flushing, diarrhea, wheezing
- **Serotonin-secreting tumor**

**HVA:5-HIAA Ratio:**
- Normal: ~2:1
- High ratio: Dopamine dominance
- Low ratio: Serotonin dominance
