# 3-Hydroxyisovaleric Acid — Biotin (B7) Deficiency Marker

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**3-Hydroxyisovaleric acid:** Hydroxy-substituted isovaleric acid

**Also called:**
- 3-HIA
- Hydroxyisovalerate

source-litnote: Synthesized from clinical practice
---

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**Leucine metabolism intermediate:**
- Leucine → HMG-CoA → 3-methylcrotonyl-CoA → 3-hydroxyisovaleryl-CoA
- **Biotin-dependent carboxylation step**

**B7-dependent enzyme:**
- 3-methylcrotonyl-CoA carboxylase (MCC)
- Requires biotin as cofactor
- **Deficiency → 3-HIA accumulation**

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### 3-HIA Levels

| Level | Status | Interpretation |
|-------|--------|----------------|
| **Normal** | Low | Adequate biotin status |
| **Elevated** | High | Biotin deficiency or dysfunction |

### Causes of Elevation

**Biotin (B7) deficiency:**
| Cause | Mechanism |
|-------|-----------|
| **Dietary insufficiency** | Rare (biotin in many foods) |
| **Raw egg whites** | Avidin binds biotin |
| **Long-term TPN** | Without biotin supplementation |
| **Certain drugs** | Anticonvulsants, antibiotics |
| **Chronic illness** | Increased demand |
| **Genetic (MCC deficiency)** | Rare inborn error |

**Gut dysbiosis:**
- Probiotic bacteria produce biotin
- Dysbiosis may reduce biotin availability

## 4. COFACTORS

### Biotin-Dependent Enzymes

| Enzyme | Function |
|--------|----------|
| **Acetyl-CoA carboxylase** | Fatty acid synthesis |
| **Pyruvate carboxylase** | Gluconeogenesis |
| **Propionyl-CoA carboxylase** | Odd-chain fatty acid metabolism |
| **3-methylcrotonyl-CoA carboxylase (MCC)** | Leucine metabolism |

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### For Elevated 3-HIA

**Biotin supplementation:**
- B7: 1-5 mg/day (high dose safe)
- Higher doses if deficiency severe

**Dietary sources:**
- Egg yolks
- Liver
- Nuts
- Seeds
- Salmon
- Avocado

**Avoid raw egg whites:**
- Cook eggs (denatures avidin)

**Probiotics:**
- Support biotin-producing gut bacteria
