## Core Claim
**Succinyl-CoA serves as the critical entry point for propionate-derived carbons into the Krebs cycle, providing anaplerotic substrate that maintains cycle flux and enables gluconeogenesis** — this B12-dependent pathway converts "non-glucose" carbons (fiber, amino acids, odd-chain fats) into glucose via the Krebs cycle.

## Evidence

### Anaplerotic Function
| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Definition** | "Filling up" the Krebs cycle |
| **Purpose** | Replenish intermediates removed for biosynthesis |
| **Result** | Maintains cycle flux for energy production |

### Sources of Succinyl-CoA
| Source | Pathway |
|--------|---------|
| **Propionate** | Via methylmalonyl-CoA mutase (B12-dependent) |
| **Odd-chain fats** | 15:0 and 17:0 oxidation |
| **Amino acids** | Isoleucine, methionine, threonine, valine |

### Energy Yield from Propionate
| Step | Output |
|------|--------|
| **Investment** | 1 ATP (carboxylation) |
| **Return** | GTP + FADH₂ + NADH + oxaloacetate |
| **Net** | Positive energy balance (~12 ATP) |

## Clinical Implication
- **Succinyl-CoA connects fiber → glucose** — gut microbiome-dependent gluconeogenesis
- **B12 deficiency blocks this pathway** → impaired energy from fiber and protein
- **Critical for fasting metabolism** — maintains glucose when dietary carbs absent
- **METHAP relevance:** B12 adequacy essential for metabolic flexibility; succinyl-CoA is a metabolic hub
