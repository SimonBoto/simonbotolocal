# Core Metabolic s Panel — , Omega-3, hsCRP, Homocysteine, HbA1c

## Overview

A focused panel of metabolic biomarkers provides insight into nutritional status, inflammation, glycation, and cardiovascular risk. These five markers together offer a comprehensive metabolic health snapshot.

## The Five Core Biomarkers

### 1. Vitamin D (25-Hydroxyvitamin D)

**Why It Matters:**
- Steroid hormone with pleiotropic effects
- 300+ gene regulation targets
- Bone health, immunity, cardiovascular protection
- Cancer prevention (epidemiological)
- Mood and cognitive function

**Optimal Range:**
| Level | Status |
|-------|--------|
| <20 ng/mL (<50 nmol/L) | Deficient |
| 20-30 ng/mL (50-75 nmol/L) | Insufficient |
| 30-40 ng/mL (75-100 nmol/L) | Adequate |
| **40-60 ng/mL (100-150 nmol/L)** | **Optimal** |
| 60-100 ng/mL (150-250 nmol/L) | High normal |
| >100 ng/mL (>250 nmol/L) | Potential toxicity |

**Clinical Actions:**
- <30 ng/mL: Supplement 2000-5000 IU D3 daily
- 30-40 ng/mL: Supplement 1000-2000 IU
- >40 ng/mL: Maintenance 1000-2000 IU
- Retest in 3 months
- Consider vitamin K2 (menaquinone) cofactor for bone/cardiovascular health

**Seasonal Variation:**
- Test in late winter (lowest point)
- Levels vary 20-40% seasonally
- Consistent supplementation advised

### 2. Omega-3 Index

**Why It Matters:**
- RBC membrane EPA+DHA percentage
- Direct measure of tissue omega-3 status
- Cardiovascular protection
- Anti-inflammatory
- Brain health
- Cell membrane fluidity

**Measurement:**
- Red blood cell fatty acid analysis
- % of total fatty acids
- Reflects 3-4 month status

**Optimal Range:**
| Level | Status |
|-------|--------|
| <4% | High risk |
| 4-6% | Intermediate risk |
| 6-8% | Adequate |
| **>8%** | **Optimal** |

**Cardiovascular Protection:**
- Anti-arrhythmic (prevents sudden cardiac death)
- Anti-thrombotic (reduces platelet aggregation)
- Triglyceride lowering (20-30% reduction)
- Endothelial function improvement
- Blood pressure reduction (modest)

**Clinical Actions:**
- <4%: EPA+DHA 3-4g daily
- 4-8%: EPA+DHA 2-3g daily
- >8%: Maintenance 1-2g daily
- Recheck in 4 months (RBC turnover)
- Fatty fish 2-3x/week or supplements

### 3. hsCRP (High-Sensitivity C-Reactive Protein)

**Why It Matters:**
- Acute phase reactant
- Systemic inflammation marker
- Cardiovascular risk predictor (independent of lipids)
- Metabolic syndrome indicator
- Response to lifestyle interventions

**Optimal Range:**
| Level | Cardiovascular Risk |
|-------|---------------------|
| **<1.0 mg/L** | **Low** |
| 1.0-3.0 mg/L | Moderate |
| 3.0-10 mg/L | High |
| >10 mg/L | Very high (acute inflammation likely) |

**Interpretation Notes:**
- **Trend more important than single value**
- Acute illness, infection elevates (check when healthy)
- Exercise, minor trauma can elevate transiently
- Not specific to cause of inflammation

**Clinical Actions:**
- >3 mg/L: Investigate cause (infection, autoimmune, metabolic syndrome)
- Address root causes:
  - Weight loss if overweight
  - Exercise
  - Omega-3 supplementation
  - Anti-inflammatory diet
  - Sleep optimization
  - Stress reduction

### 4. Homocysteine

**Why It Matters:**
- Methylation cycle intermediate
- Cardiovascular risk factor (independent)
- Cognitive decline association
- Pregnancy outcomes (neural tube defects)
- Osteoporosis risk

**Optimal Range:**
| Level | Status |
|-------|--------|
| **<10 μmol/L** | **Optimal** |
| 10-15 μmol/L | Elevated |
| 15-30 μmol/L | Moderately high |
| >30 μmol/L | Very high |

**Causes of Elevation:**

**Nutritional:**
- MTHFR C677T polymorphism (reduced enzyme activity)
- Vitamin B12 deficiency
- Folate deficiency
- Vitamin B6 deficiency
- Riboflavin (B2) deficiency

**Medical:**
- Hypothyroidism
- Chronic kidney disease
- Psoriasis
- Certain medications (methotrexate, phenytoin, carbamazepine)

**Clinical Actions:**

**Supplementation:**
- **Methylfolate** (5-MTHF): 400-800 mcg daily (bypasses MTHFR defect)
- **Methylcobalamin** (B12): 1000 mcg daily
- **P5P** (B6): 25-50 mg daily
- **Betaine** (trimethylglycine): 500-1000 mg daily (alternative methylation pathway)

**Testing:**
- Check MTHFR status if elevated
- B12, folate, B6 levels
- Thyroid function
- Kidney function

### 5. HbA1c (Glycated Hemoglobin)

**Why It Matters:**
- 2-3 month average blood glucose
- Glycation damage marker
- Diabetes diagnosis and monitoring
- Cardiovascular risk predictor
- Brain health (cognitive decline association)

**Optimal Range:**
| Level | Status |
|-------|--------|
| **<5.5%** | **Optimal** |
| 5.5-5.7% | Excellent |
| 5.7-6.4% | Prediabetes |
| 6.0-6.4% | High prediabetes |
| ≥6.5% | Diabetes |

**Limitations:**
- Affected by red blood cell lifespan
- Falsely low: Hemolytic anemia, recent transfusion, chronic kidney disease
- Falsely high: Iron deficiency, B12 deficiency (macrocytosis), asplenia
- Not for gestational diabetes

**Clinical Actions:**
- >5.7%: Lifestyle intervention
  - Carbohydrate reduction
  - Time-restricted eating
  - Exercise
  - Weight loss if overweight
- >6.5%: Diabetes management protocol
- Verify with fasting glucose if discrepancies

## The Complete Metabolic Panel

### Core Five Plus:

**Lipid Profile:**
- Total cholesterol
- LDL-C
- HDL-C
- Triglycerides
- Non-HDL-C
- ApoB (preferred for risk)

**Glucose :**
- Fasting glucose
- Fasting insulin
- HOMA-IR calculation

**Liver Function:**
- AST, ALT
- GGT
- Alkaline phosphatase
- Bilirubin
- Albumin

**Kidney Function:**
- Creatinine
- eGFR
- BUN
- Electrolytes

**Complete Blood Count:**
- Hemoglobin
- Hematocrit
- MCV, MCH
- Platelets
- WBC with differential

## Clinical Application

### Annual Screening

**Healthy Adults:**
1. Comprehensive metabolic panel
2. Lipid profile
3. Vitamin D
4. HbA1c or fasting glucose

**High-Risk/Metabolic Issues:**
- Add: Omega-3 index, hsCRP, homocysteine
- More frequent monitoring

### Interpretation Framework

**Pattern Recognition:**

**Metabolic Syndrome:**
- Elevated HbA1c
- Low omega-3 index
- Elevated hsCRP
- Dyslipidemia

**Methylation Issues:**
- Elevated homocysteine
- Low B12/folate
- MTHFR polymorphism
- Fatigue, cognitive issues

**Inflammatory State:**
- Elevated hsCRP
- Low omega-3 index
- Elevated homocysteine
- Poor vitamin D status

## Summary

**The Five Core Metabolic Biomarkers:**

| Biomarker | Optimal | Primary Action |
|-----------|---------|----------------|
| **Vitamin D** | 40-60 ng/mL | Supplement D3 + K2 |
| **Omega-3 Index** | >8% | EPA+DHA 2-4g |
| **hsCRP** | <1.0 mg/L | Anti-inflammatory lifestyle |
| **Homocysteine** | <10 μmol/L | B vitamins, methylation support |
| **HbA1c** | <5.5% | Glycemic control, metabolic optimization |

**Clinical Pearl:** These five biomarkers together provide a comprehensive metabolic health assessment. Address in order of abnormality—glycation (HbA1c) and inflammation (hsCRP) often respond first to lifestyle interventions, while omega-3 and vitamin D require 3-4 months to optimize due to tissue turnover.
