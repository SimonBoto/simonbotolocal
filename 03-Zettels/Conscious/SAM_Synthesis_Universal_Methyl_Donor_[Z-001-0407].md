# SAM Synthesis — Universal Methyl Donor

> S-Adenosylmethionine (SAM) is synthesized from methionine and ATP, serving as the primary methyl donor for over 100  reactions including DNA, histones, neurotransmitters, and phospholipids.

## Synthesis

**Reaction:**
```
Methionine + ATP → SAM + PPi + Pi
```

**Enzyme:** Methionine adenosyltransferase (MAT)
- **MAT I/III:** Liver (high capacity)
- **MAT II:** All tissues (ubiquitous)

**Location:** Primarily liver, but all nucleated cells

## Structure and Chemistry

**Key Features:**
- **Sulfonium ion:** Positively charged sulfur
- **Activated methyl group:** High-energy, easily transferred
- **Adenosine moiety:** From ATP

**Why Universal Donor:**
- Methyl group is "activated" (unstable)
- Readily transfers to acceptors
- Spontaneous or enzyme-catalyzed

## Methylation Reactions

### DNA Methylation
- **Enzymes:** DNMTs
- **Target:** Cytosine in CpG
- **Function:** Gene silencing, imprinting

### Histone Methylation
- **Enzymes:** HMTs
- **Targets:** Lysine, arginine residues
- **Function:** Chromatin structure, gene expression

### Neurotransmitter Synthesis
- **Norepinephrine:** From dopamine
- **Melatonin:** From serotonin
- **Epinephrine:** From norepinephrine

### Other Critical Reactions
- Phosphatidylcholine synthesis
- Creatine synthesis
- RNA methylation
- Protein methylation

## Regulation

**Substrate Availability:**
- Methionine from diet or remethylation
- B12/ status affects methionine regeneration
- One-carbon  fuels the cycle

**Product Inhibition:**
- SAH inhibits methyltransferases
- SAM/SAH ratio critical
