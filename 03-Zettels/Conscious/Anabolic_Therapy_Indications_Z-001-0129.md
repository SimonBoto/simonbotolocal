# Anabolic Therapy Indications

## High-Risk Indicators for Anabolic Therapy

Anabolic therapy is indicated for patients with high fracture risk based on the following criteria:

| Indicator | Definition | Evidence Strength |
|-----------|------------|-------------------|
| **Multiple fractures** | ≥2 fragility fractures | High |
| **T-score < -3.5** | Hip or spine DXA | High |
| **Severe osteoporosis** | T-score < -3.0 + fracture | High |
| **Rapid bone loss** | >10% spine in 1 year | Moderate |
| **Glucocorticoid use** | ≥7.5 mg/day prednisone | High |

## Clinical Rationale

- Anabolic agents (teriparatide, abaloparatide, romosozumab) demonstrate superior fracture reduction in high-risk populations
- Early intervention with anabolic therapy prevents further bone loss and structural damage
- High-risk patients benefit most from the rapid bone formation effect of anabolic agents

## Evidence Summary

- Vertebrae: 60–73% fracture reduction with anabolic therapy vs 40–50% with antiresorptives
- Non-vertebral: 35–45% fracture reduction with anabolic therapy vs 20–35% with antiresorptives
- 12-month spine BMD gain: +7–15% with anabolic therapy vs +2–6% with antiresorptives
