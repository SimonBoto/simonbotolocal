## Core Claim
**Social relationships and community connections are critical determinants of health outcomes** — strong social ties predict longevity as powerfully as smoking predicts mortality, while loneliness and social isolation increase risk of death, cardiovascular disease, depression, and cognitive decline comparable to major biomedical risk factors.

## The Evidence

### Mortality Prediction
| Factor | Risk Increase |
|--------|---------------|
| **Social isolation** | +26-45% mortality |
| **Loneliness** | +26% mortality |
| **Living alone** | +32% mortality |
| **Comparison** | Comparable to smoking 15 cigarettes/day |

### Health Outcomes
| Outcome | Social Connection Effect |
|---------|--------------------------|
| **Cardiovascular disease** | 29% lower risk with strong ties |
| **Stroke** | 32% lower risk |
| **Dementia** | 50% lower risk |
| **Depression** | 2x higher risk if isolated |
| **Immune function** | Stronger with support |
| **Wound healing** | Faster with social support |

## Mechanisms

### Biological Pathways
| Mechanism | Effect |
|-----------|--------|
| **Stress buffering** | Social support reduces cortisol |
| **Immune modulation** | Better immune response |
| **Cardiovascular** | Lower blood pressure, heart rate |
| **Inflammation** | Reduced inflammatory markers |
| **Health behaviors** | Social norms influence choices |

### Psychological Pathways
| Factor | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| **Purpose** | Meaning through relationships |
| **Self-esteem** | Validation from others |
| **Coping** | Shared problem-solving |
| **Belonging** | Fundamental human need |
