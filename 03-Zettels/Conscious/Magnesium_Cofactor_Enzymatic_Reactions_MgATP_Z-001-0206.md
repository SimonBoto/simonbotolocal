# Magnesium as Cofactor — Enzymatic Reactions and Mg-ATP Complex

> Magnesium is an essential cofactor for over 300 enzymatic reactions, primarily through its role in stabilizing ATP as the Mg-ATP complex, the true substrate for most ATP-dependent enzymes.

## Biochemical Mechanism

**Mg-ATP Complex:**
- ATP exists in biological systems primarily as Mg-ATP²⁻ complex
- Mg²⁺ stabilizes the triphosphate chain through chelation
- This complex is the actual substrate for ATP-dependent enzymes
- Without Mg²⁺, ATP cannot effectively participate in phosphoryl transfer reactions

## Key Enzyme Classes

### 1. Kinases (Phosphoryl Transfer)
- **Hexokinase:** Glucose → Glucose-6-phosphate (glycolysis entry)
- **Phosphofructokinase:** Rate-limiting step in glycolysis
- **Protein kinases:** Signal transduction cascades
- **Creatine kinase:** Rapid ATP regeneration in muscle

### 2. ATPases (Energy Coupling)
- **Na⁺/K⁺-ATPase:** Maintains electrochemical gradients
- **Ca²⁺-ATPase:** Muscle relaxation, signal termination
- **H⁺-ATPases:** Proton pumping (lysosomes, gastric acid)

### 3. Nucleic Acid Enzymes
- **DNA polymerases:** DNA replication and repair
- **RNA polymerases:** Transcription
- **Topoisomerases:** DNA supercoiling management

### 4. Cyclases (Second Messenger Production)
- **Adenylate cyclase:** cAMP production (G-protein signaling)
- **Guanylate cyclase:** cGMP production (NO signaling)

## Magnesium Deficiency Consequences

**Systemic Effects:**
- Impaired energy  (glycolysis, Krebs cycle)
- Reduced protein synthesis
- Impaired nucleic acid synthesis
- Altered signal transduction

**Clinical Correlates:**
- Fatigue, weakness (energy failure)
- Impaired glucose tolerance (reduced  sensitivity)
- Muscle dysfunction (impaired contraction/relaxation)

## METHAP Relevance

**Critical Protocol Point:**
- High-dose  requires Mg-dependent enzymes (CYP hydroxylases)
- Mg deficiency → impaired 25(OH)D and 1,25(OH)₂D synthesis
- Optimizing Mg status essential for METHAP success
