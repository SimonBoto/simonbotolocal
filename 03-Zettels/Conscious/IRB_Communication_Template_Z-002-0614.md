# IRB Communication Template

Professional, effective communication with ethics committees requires clarity, respect, and appropriate tone. Templates ensure consistency and completeness.

## Standard Follow-Up Message

**Subject:** Follow-Up Regarding Ethics Application [Reference Number]

Dear [Committee Chair/Dr. Name],

I hope this message finds you well. My name is [Your Name], and I am a PhD candidate under the supervision of [Supervisor Name] at [Institution].

I am writing regarding my ethics application submitted on [Date] (Reference: [Number]). Despite following up through official channels, I have not received an update on the status of my application.

Given that [Time Period] has elapsed, I am concerned about the impact on my  timeline. I would greatly appreciate:
- An update on the current status
- Information on any additional requirements
- An estimated timeline for completion

Please let me know if you require any further information or if a meeting would be helpful.

Thank you for your time and assistance. I look forward to hearing from you.

Kind regards,  
[Your Name]  
[Contact Information]  
[Program/Department]

---

## Escalation Message

**Subject:** Urgent: Ethics Application [Reference Number] — [Time Period] Pending

Dear [Committee Chair],

I am writing to follow up on my ethics application ([Reference Number]) submitted [Date]. Despite [Number] previous inquiries, I have not received a response.

As this delay now extends to [Time Period], my research timeline is significantly impacted. I have copied [Supervisor/Department Head] on this message to seek guidance on appropriate next steps.

I remain committed to addressing any concerns with my application and am available for discussion at your earliest convenience.

Respectfully,  
[Your Name]

---

## Key Principles

| Principle | Application |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Identify yourself** | Name, supervisor, institution, program |
| **Reference specifics** | Submission date, reference number |
| **State concern neutrally** | Facts, not accusations |
| **Request specific action** | Status, requirements, timeline |
| **Offer cooperation** | Additional information, meeting |
| **Maintain respect** | Professional tone regardless of frustration |

## Greek Context

For Greek ethics committees:
- Use formal address (Αξιότιμε/η)
- Include full academic titles
- Reference institutional hierarchy
- Expect longer response times than US/UK
