## Mechanism

**Oxidative phosphorylation — comprising electron transport Complexes I-IV and ATP synthase — generates ~90% of cellular ATP through chemiosmotic coupling, with dysfunction producing characteristic clinical syndromes ranging from fatigue to neurodegeneration and requiring oxygen, membrane potential, and intact mitochondrial DNA.**

## Evidence

### Electron Transport Chain
| Complex | Input | Output | Inhibitors | Clinical Association |
|---------|-------|--------|------------|---------------------|
| **I** (NADH DH) | NADH | Ubiquinone | Rotenone, metformin | Parkinson's, diabetes |
| **II** (SDH) | FADH₂ | Ubiquinone | Malonate, TTFA | Paraganglioma, pheochromocytoma |
| **III** (Cyt bc1) | Ubiquinol | Cytochrome c | Antimycin A | Exercise intolerance |
| **IV** (Cyt c oxidase) | Cytochrome c | O₂ → H₂O | Cyanide, CO | Severe hypoxia |
| **V** (ATP synthase) | Proton gradient | ATP | Oligomycin | ATP production failure |

### Chemiosmotic Coupling
| Component | Function | Clinical Relevance |
|-----------|----------|-------------------|
| **Proton gradient** | Energy storage | Uncoupling → heat production |
| **Membrane potential** | ΔΨm drives ATP synthesis | Apoptosis trigger if lost |
| **ATP synthase** | Proton-driven phosphorylation | Inhibition → energy crisis |

### ATP Yield
| Substrate | Entry Point | ATP Produced |
|-----------|-------------|--------------|
| NADH | Complex I | ~2.5 ATP |
| FADH₂ | Complex II | ~1.5 ATP |
| Glucose (complete oxidation) | Glycolysis + TCA | ~30-32 ATP |
| Fatty acid (palmitate) | Beta-oxidation | ~106 ATP |

### Clinical OXPHOS Dysfunction
| Condition | Mechanism | Presentation |
|-----------|-----------|--------------|
| **Mitochondrial disease** | mtDNA mutations | Multisystem, maternal inheritance |
| **Complex I deficiency** | NADH oxidation impaired | Leigh syndrome, PD |
| **Complex IV deficiency** | Cytochrome c oxidase defect | Severe infantile myopathy |
| **MELAS** | tRNA mutation | Stroke-like episodes, lactic acidosis |
| **MERRF** | tRNA mutation | Myoclonus, epilepsy, ragged red fibers |

### OAT Markers
| Marker | Indicates | Complex |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| Elevated lactate | Impaired OXPHOS | Any |
| Elevated pyruvate | PDH or Complex I issue | I |
| Elevated succinate | Complex II dysfunction | II |

## Clinical Implication
- **Fatigue workup:** OXPHOS dysfunction common in unexplained fatigue
- **Exercise intolerance:** ETC defects limit aerobic capacity
- **Neurodegeneration:** Complex I linked to Parkinson's
- **Toxin susceptibility:** ETC inhibitors produce severe symptoms
- **METHAP relevance:** OXPHOS efficiency is key metabolic health indicator; Complex I inhibition by metformin is diabetes therapeutic mechanism
