## Core Claim
**A coronary artery calcium (CAC) score of zero indicates no benefit from statin therapy** — individuals without detectable atherosclerotic plaque do not derive cardiovascular risk reduction from statins.

## The Evidence

### No Benefit Zones
| CAC Score | Statin Benefit |
|-----------|----------------|
| **0** | ❌ No benefit |
| **1-100** | ❌ No benefit |

### Why No Benefit?
- Statins work by **stabilizing existing plaque**
- If **no plaque exists**, there is no target for statin action
- CAC = 0 predicts very low 10-year CVD risk regardless of LDL

### Overtreatment Common
- Many low-risk individuals prescribed statins based on LDL alone
- Risk calculators estimate population risk, not individual plaque burden

## CAC Scan Practicalities
| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Radiation dose** | ~1 mSv (less than annual background) |
| **Cost** | Cost-effective for risk stratification |
| **Predictive value** | High |

## Clinical Implication
CAC = 0 can **reassure patients** and **avoid unnecessary medication**.
