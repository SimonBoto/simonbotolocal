# Nucleoside Formation Phosphorylation Pathways

## Overview
The synthesis of nucleotides from bases involves two key steps: nucleoside formation (base + sugar) and phosphorylation to create energy-rich nucleotides.

## Step 1: Nucleoside Formation

### The Reaction
```
Base + Sugar → Nucleoside

Example:
Adenine + Ribose → Adenosine
```

### Enzymatic Catalysis
| Enzyme | Reaction | Notes |
|--------|----------|-------|
| **Adenosine phosphorylase** | Adenine + Ribose-1-P → Adenosine + Pi | Salvage pathway |
| **Adenosine kinase** | Adenosine + ATP → AMP + ADP | Phosphorylates existing nucleoside |

### Alternative Pathway: Direct Phosphorylation
- Direct phosphorylation of **free bases** is rare
- Bases must typically be attached to sugar first
- Exception: Some synthetic/prebiotic chemistry scenarios

## Step 2: Nucleoside Phosphorylation

### Progressive Phosphorylation
```
Nucleoside → Nucleotide (mono) → Di → Tri

Adenosine → AMP → ADP → ATP
```

### Key Enzymes
| Step | Enzyme | Reaction |
|------|--------|----------|
| **Nucleoside → NMP** | Nucleoside kinase | Adds first phosphate |
| **NMP → NDP** | Nucleoside monophosphate kinase | Adds second phosphate |
| **NDP → NTP** | Nucleoside diphosphate kinase | Adds third phosphate |

### ATP as Phosphate Donor
- Each phosphorylation step uses ATP as phosphate donor
- **Net investment:** 2 ATP to go from nucleoside to NTP
- Economic trade-off: Energy investment for future energy storage

## The Nucleotide Synthesis Hierarchy

```
Level 0: Base (adenine) - nitrogenous, no energy capacity
    ↓
Level 1: Nucleoside (adenosine) - base + sugar
    ↓
Level 2: NMP (AMP) - monophosphate, low energy
    ↓
Level 3: NDP (ADP) - diphosphate, intermediate
    ↓
Level 4: NTP (ATP) - triphosphate, high energy, fully functional
```

## Metabolic Integration

### Connection to PPP
- Ribose-5-phosphate from PPP feeds into nucleotide synthesis
- Direct precursor for nucleoside formation
- Links carbohydrate [[KEYWORD-Metabolism|metabolism]] to nucleic acid synthesis

### Salvage vs. De Novo
| Pathway | Starting Point | Energy Cost | When Active |
|---------|----------------|-------------|-------------|
| **De novo** | Simple precursors | High (6 ATP per purine) | Abundant resources |
| **Salvage** | Pre-formed bases | Low (1-2 ATP) | Scarcity, recycling |

## Prebiotic Chemistry Perspective
- How did nucleotides form before enzymes?
- Phosphorylation of nucleosides may have occurred on mineral surfaces
- Alternative pathways: Direct base phosphorylation (rare in biology, possible prebiotically)

## Clinical Relevance

### Purine Nucleoside Phosphorylase Deficiency
- **Enzyme defect:** PNP deficiency
- **Consequence:** T-cell immunodeficiency
- **[[KEYWORD-Mechanism|mechanism]]:** Toxic metabolite accumulation
- **Treatment:** Supportive, bone marrow transplant

### Nucleoside Analog Drugs
| Drug | Mechanism | Use |
|------|-----------|-----|
| **AZT** | Thymidine analog, inhibits HIV reverse transcriptase | HIV/AIDS |
| **Acyclovir** | Guanosine analog, inhibits viral DNA polymerase | Herpes |
| **5-Fluorouracil** | Pyrimidine analog, disrupts DNA synthesis | [[KEYWORD-Cancer|cancer]] |
