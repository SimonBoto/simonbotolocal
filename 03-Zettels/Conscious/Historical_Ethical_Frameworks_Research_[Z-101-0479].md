# Historical Ethical Frameworks for Research

## Overview
Modern research ethics emerged from responses to abuses, particularly Nazi medical experiments. Each framework built upon predecessors to create comprehensive protections.

## Foundational Documents

### Nuremberg Code (1947)
**Context:** Nuremberg Military Tribunal (U.S. v. Brandt) — response to Nazi atrocities

| Principle | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Voluntary consent** | Absolute, without coercion |
| **Scientific necessity** | Results unobtainable by other means |
| **Animal research first** | Prior experimentation on animals |
| **Avoid unnecessary harm** | No suffering if avoidable |
| **No expectation of death/disability** | Prohibited as outcome |
| **Risk proportional to importance** | Degree of risk justified by problem significance |
| **Protection against injury** | Adequate preparations must exist |
| **Qualified researchers only** | Conducted by scientifically qualified persons |
| **Subject withdrawal** | Right to withdraw at any time |
| **Termination by researcher** | Must stop if continuation likely to result in injury/disability/death |

**Significance:** First international code; established voluntary consent as foundational.

---

### Declaration of Helsinki (1964, revised 1975, 1983, 1989, 1996, 2000, 2008, 2013)
**Source:** World Medical Association

| Principle | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Physician duty** | Patient health paramount over society/science |
| **Informed consent** | Written consent after adequate information |
| **Vulnerable protection** | Special protections for those who cannot consent |
| **Risk-benefit** | Potential benefits justify risks |
| **Publication duty** | Results must be reported |
| **Placebo use** | Only when no proven effective alternative exists |
| **Post-trial access** | Provisions for participants to access beneficial interventions |

**Key Distinction:** Therapeutic vs. non-therapeutic research
- **Therapeutic:** Potential benefit to participant
- **Non-therapeutic:** Benefit to others/society only (higher scrutiny)

---

### Belmont Report (1979)
**Source:** US National Commission for Protection of Human Subjects

**Three Core Principles:**

| Principle | Definition | Application |
|-----------|------------|-------------|
| **Respect for Persons** | Individuals as autonomous agents; protection for those with diminished autonomy | Informed consent; special protections for vulnerable |
| **Beneficence** | Do no harm; maximize benefits, minimize harms | Risk-benefit assessment; study design |
| **Justice** | Fair distribution of benefits and burdens | Fair subject selection; equitable access |

**Significance:** Foundation for US research regulations; basis for IRB system.

---

### CIOMS International Ethical Guidelines (1982, revised 1993, 2002, 2016)
**Source:** Council for International Organizations of Medical Sciences + WHO

| Focus | Key Elements |
|-------|--------------|
| **Global application** | Standards for international research |
| **Vulnerable populations** | Special protections for disadvantaged |
| **Community engagement** | Host community consultation |
| **Post-trial obligations** | Reasonable availability of beneficial interventions |
| **Research in low-resource settings** | Avoid exploitation; capacity building |

## Regulatory Implementation

### United States: 45 CFR 46 (Common Rule)
| Element | Requirement |
|---------|-------------|
| **Established** | 1981; revised 1991, 2017, 2018 |
| **Scope** | Federally funded research |
| **IRB requirement** | Review by Institutional Review Board |
| **Informed consent** | Documented, comprehensive |
| **Subparts** | Additional protections for specific populations (pregnant women, prisoners, children) |

### International: ICH Good Clinical Practice (E6)
| Element | Requirement |
|---------|-------------|
| **Established** | 1996; harmonized EU/US/Japan |
| **Scope** | Pharmaceutical clinical trials |
| **Quality standards** | Data integrity, documentation |
| **Ethical standards** | Participant protection |
| **Regulatory compliance** | Meeting FDA/EMA requirements |

## Evolution Timeline
```
1947 — Nuremberg Code (response to Nazi crimes)
   ↓
1964 — Declaration of Helsinki (WMA international standard)
   ↓
1979 — Belmont Report (US regulatory foundation)
   ↓
1981 — 45 CFR 46 Common Rule (US federal implementation)
   ↓
1982 — CIOMS Guidelines (global research ethics)
   ↓
1996 — ICH E6 GCP (pharmaceutical harmonization)
   ↓
2000 — Emanuel's Seven Requirements (comprehensive framework)
   ↓
Ongoing — Revisions adapting to new research contexts
```

## Contemporary Challenges
| Challenge | Framework Response |
|-----------|-------------------|
| **Big data/AI research** | Evolving consent models; privacy protections |
| **Global health research** | CIOMS post-trial obligations; capacity building |
| **Gene editing** | Enhanced oversight; heritable vs. somatic distinction |
| **Pandemic research** | Accelerated review; maintained protections |
