# Calorie Source Affects Metabolism — Not Just Quantity

## Core Claim
**The source of calories affects metabolism, not just quantity** — when carbohydrates are reduced, the body burns more calories, demonstrating that "a calorie is a calorie" is an oversimplification.

## Old Thinking vs New Evidence
| Old Thinking | New Evidence |
|--------------|--------------|
| "Calories are calories" | Source affects metabolism |
| "Only quantity matters" | Quality changes energy burn |
| "Weight loss = calories in/out" | Metabolic adaptation varies |

## Study Design
| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Participants** | 164 adults (post-weight loss) |
| **Duration** | 20 weeks |
| **Groups** | 3 (high/moderate/low carb) |
| **Measurement** | Total energy expenditure |

## Key Finding
When carbohydrates are reduced, the body **burns more calories**.

## Key Quote
> "It is not only how many calories we eat that matters, but also where they come from. Diets with fewer carbohydrates help the body burn more energy, while diets with many carbohydrates make metabolism slower."

## Clinical Implication
- Weight loss strategies must consider **macronutrient composition**
- Not just calorie counting
- Metabolic adaptation varies by diet type
