## Core Claim
**Glucose-6-phosphate enters the pentose phosphate pathway to become ribose-5-phosphate, creating a metabolic bridge between dietary carbohydrate and nucleotide synthesis** — this connection is essential for rapidly dividing tissues (bone marrow, gut, skin) that require continuous nucleotide production.

## Evidence

### Conversion Pathway
```
Glucose → Glucose-6-P → [PPP oxidative] → Ribulose-5-P → Ribose-5-P → PRPP → Nucleotides
```

### Tissue-Specific PPP Activity
| Tissue | Ribose Demand | PPP Activity | Rationale |
|--------|---------------|--------------|-----------|
| **Bone marrow** | Very high | High | Blood cell production |
| **Intestinal mucosa** | High | High | Rapid epithelial turnover |
| **Skin** | Moderate | Moderate | Continuous renewal |
| **Muscle** | Low | Low | Stable cell population |

### Metabolic Context
| Situation | Flow Direction | Purpose |
|-----------|----------------|---------|
| **High glucose + growth** | Glucose → Ribose | DNA replication support |
| **Fasting + need nucleotides** | Protein → Ribose (indirect) | Maintain synthesis |

## Clinical Implication
- **Rapidly dividing tissues most vulnerable** to PPP impairment
- **G6PD deficiency** → hemolytic anemia (RBCs cannot make ribose for membrane maintenance)
- **Cancer cells upregulate PPP** → support proliferation
- **METHAP relevance:** B-vitamins support PPP enzymes; high metabolic demand requires robust nucleotide synthesis
