## Core Claim
**Pyruvate (CH₃-CO-COOH) is the simplest α-keto acid and the endpoint of glycolysis — it sits at the metabolic crossroads with four major fates determined by oxygen availability and energy needs: mitochondrial oxidation (via PDH requiring 5 B-vitamins), lactate fermentation, gluconeogenic carboxylation, or transamination to alanine** — this 3-carbon molecule connects glycolysis to aerobic energy, anaerobic survival, and amino acid metabolism.

## Evidence

### Structure
- **Formula:** CH₃-CO-COOH (3 carbons)
- **Class:** α-keto acid (ketone at α-carbon)
- **Glycolysis yield:** 2 pyruvate per glucose

### Metabolic Fates
| Condition | Fate | Enzyme | Cofactors |
|-----------|------|--------|-----------|
| **Aerobic** | Acetyl-CoA → Krebs | PDH complex | B1, B2, B3, B5, lipoate |
| **Anaerobic** | Lactate | Lactate dehydrogenase | NADH |
| **Gluconeogenesis** | Oxaloacetate | Pyruvate carboxylase | Biotin (B7), ATP |
| **Transamination** | Alanine | ALT (GPT) | B6 (PLP) |

### PDH Complex (5 Cofactors)
| Component | Cofactor | Function |
|-----------|----------|----------|
| **E1** | TPP (B1) | Decarboxylation |
| **E2** | Lipoamide, CoA (B5) | Transfer to CoA |
| **E3** | FAD (B2), NAD⁺ (B3) | Reoxidation |

## Clinical Implication
- **PDH deficiency:** Lactic acidosis, neurological impairment (B-vitamin responsive)
- **Thiamine deficiency:** Impaired pyruvate oxidation → beriberi, Wernicke-Korsakoff
- **Lactic acidosis:** Pyruvate → lactate when ETC impaired
- **METHAP relevance:** High-dose B-vitamins (B1, B2, B3, B5) support PDH for optimal pyruvate oxidation and energy production
