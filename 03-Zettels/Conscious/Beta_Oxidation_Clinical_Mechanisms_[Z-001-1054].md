# Beta Oxidation Clinical Mechanisms

## Overview
l process of breaking down fatty acids into acetyl-CoA units, generating NADH and FADH₂ for ATP production.

## Four-Step Cycle (per 2-carbon removal)

| Step | Reaction | Cofactor | Product |
|------|----------|----------|---------|
| 1 | Oxidation | FAD → FADH₂ | Trans-Δ²-enoyl-CoA |
| 2 | Hydration | H₂O | L-3-hydroxyacyl-CoA |
| 3 | Oxidation | NAD⁺ → NADH | 3-ketoacyl-CoA |
| 4 | Thiolysis | CoA | Acetyl-CoA + shortened fatty acid |

## Energy Yield

| Fatty Acid | Acetyl-CoA | NADH | FADH₂ | Total ATP |
|------------|------------|------|-------|-----------|
| Palmitic (16:0) | 8 | 7 | 7 | ~106 ATP |
| Stearic (18:0) | 9 | 8 | 8 | ~120 ATP |

*Net after activation cost (-2 ATP)*

## Carnitine Shuttle Requirement
Long-chain fatty acids (>12 carbons) require:
1. **CPT I** (outer membrane) — Rate-limiting step
2. **Carnitine-acylcarnitine translocase**
3. **CPT II** (inner membrane)

## Clinical Markers of β-Oxidation Status
| OAT Marker | Interpretation |
|------------|----------------|
| **Suberate ↑** | Medium-chain β-oxidation impairment |
| **Adipate ↑** | General fatty acid oxidation stress |
| **Ethylmalonate ↑** | Multiple acyl-CoA dehydrogenase deficiency |
| **Low odd-chain fats** | Reduced dairy/fermented food intake |

## Deficiency States
| Cofactor | Effect | Marker |
|----------|--------|--------|
| **Carnitine** | Impaired transport | Elevated suberate/adipate |
| **Riboflavin (B2)** | FAD deficiency | Elevated fatty acid intermediates |
| **Niacin (B3)** | NAD⁺ deficiency | General energy failure |

## Clinical Applications
- **Fatigue:** Assess β-oxidation capacity
- **Weight loss:** Optimize fat burning
- ** resistance:** Improve metabolic flexibility
