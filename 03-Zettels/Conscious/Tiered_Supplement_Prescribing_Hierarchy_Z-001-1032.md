## Core Claim
**Systematic supplement prescribing follows a six-tier hierarchy — Foundational → Targeted mitochondrial → Detox/methylation → Gut rebalancing → Lipid repair → Lifestyle — ensuring logical sequencing, avoiding patient overwhelm, and enabling clear attribution of benefits to specific interventions.**

## Evidence

### The Six Tiers
| Tier | Focus | Timing | Key Supplements |
|------|-------|--------|-----------------|
| **1. Foundational** | Common deficiencies | Weeks 1-4 | Vit D, Mg, Omega-3, B-complex, Probiotic |
| **2. Mitochondrial** | Energy production | Weeks 5-8 | CoQ10, L-Carnitine, ALA, NAC |
| **3. Detox/Methylation** | Pathway support | Weeks 9-12 | Methylated B vitamins, glutathione support |
| **4. Gut Rebalancing** | Microbiome | Weeks 13-16 | Targeted antimicrobials, prebiotics |
| **5. Lipid Repair** | Membrane support | Weeks 17-20 | Phosphatidylcholine, omega-3 optimization |
| **6. Lifestyle** | Behavioral | Ongoing | Sleep, exercise, stress protocols |

### Tier 1: Foundational (All Patients)
| Supplement | Dose | Rationale |
|------------|------|-----------|
| **Vitamin D3** | 2000-5000 IU | Universal deficiency, immune support |
| **Magnesium** | 400 mg | Cofactor for 300+ enzymes |
| **Omega-3 (EPA/DHA)** | 2-3 g | Anti-inflammatory, membrane support |
| **B-complex** | 1-2 capsules | Energy metabolism, methylation |
| **Probiotic** | 50 billion CFU | Gut foundation |

### Entry Criteria by Tier
| Tier | OAT/Clinical Indicators |
|------|------------------------|
| **Tier 2** | Elevated lactate, pyruvate, adipate, suberate |
| **Tier 3** | Elevated methylmalonate, FIGLU, detox markers |
| **Tier 4** | Dysbiosis markers, digestive symptoms |
| **Tier 5** | Fatty acid imbalances, membrane issues |

## Clinical Implication
- **Compliance:** Gradual introduction prevents overwhelm
- **Attribution:** Clear effects from each tier
- **Safety:** Foundation first prevents adverse reactions
- **Individualization:** Tiers adjusted based on OAT markers
- **METHAP relevance:** Tiered approach applies to research protocols — foundational nutrients first, then targeted interventions based on biomarkers
