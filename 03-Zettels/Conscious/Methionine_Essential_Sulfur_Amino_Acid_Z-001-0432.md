## Core Claim
**Methionine is an essential sulfur-containing amino acid that serves as the universal methyl donor (via SAM) and gateway to both transsulfuration (cysteine/glutathione synthesis) and complete energy oxidation** — making it a critical hub connecting protein metabolism, methylation capacity, antioxidant defense, and energy production.

## Evidence

### Structural Features
| Feature | Significance |
|---------|--------------|
| **Thioether bond** | Sulfur enables unique chemistry |
| **Methyl-thio group (-S-CH₃)** | Source of methyl groups for SAM |
| **Hydrophobic side chain** | Determines protein structure |

### Essentiality
- **Cannot be synthesized de novo** in humans
- **Must be obtained from diet**
- **Rate-limiting for protein synthesis**

### Dietary Sources
| Source | Methionine Content |
|--------|-------------------|
| **Animal proteins** | High (meat, fish, eggs, dairy) |
| **Plant proteins** | Lower (legumes, nuts, seeds) |

### Metabolic Fates
1. **Protein synthesis** — Incorporation into proteins
2. **SAM synthesis** — Universal methyl donor
3. **Transsulfuration** — Cysteine and glutathione synthesis
4. **Energy oxidation** — Complete oxidation via propionyl-CoA

## Clinical Implication
- **Methionine adequacy is essential for:** methylation capacity, glutathione synthesis, and energy metabolism
- **Deficiency leads to:** impaired detoxification, elevated homocysteine, reduced antioxidant capacity
- **High-dose methionine without B12/folate** can trap homocysteine — requires cofactors for recycling
- **METHAP relevance:** B12 and folate support methionine cycle function; magnesium required for SAM synthesis
