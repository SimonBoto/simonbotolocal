## Procter & Gamble Background

**Company:**
- Founded 1837 (soap, candles)
- Expanded to consumer goods
- Major food product innovations
- Marketing pioneers

**Economic Opportunity:**
- Cottonseed oil (byproduct of cotton industry)
- Previously used for soap
- Surplus availability
- Potential food application

## Crisco Development (1911)

**Technical Innovation:**
- **Hydrogenation:** Process to solidify liquid oil
- Hydrogen added to unsaturated fats
- Created semi-solid consistency
- Extended shelf life

**Product Name:**
- "Crystallized cottonseed oil"
- Shortened to "Crisco"
- Sounded scientific, modern

**Advantages Over Lard:**
- Longer shelf stability
- Neutral flavor
- Lower cost
- Consistent quality
- Kosher (appealed to Jewish market)

## Marketing Strategy

**Positioning:**
- "Modern" vs. "old-fashioned" lard
- "Scientific" production
- "Pure" and "digestible"
- "Clean" (no meat associations)

**Advertising Campaigns:**
- Magazine advertisements
- Recipe booklets (free distribution)
- Cooking demonstrations
- Celebrity endorsements
- Home economics partnerships

**Target Markets:**
- Housewives (primary cooks)
- Jewish households (kosher)
- Urban populations
- "Modern" consumers

## Impact on American Diet

**Market Success:**
- Rapid adoption
- Became kitchen staple
- Competition emerged (Spry, others)
- Transformed cooking practices

**Dietary Shift:**
- Animal fat consumption declined
- Vegetable oil consumption rose
-  intake increased dramatically
- Traditional fats marginalized
