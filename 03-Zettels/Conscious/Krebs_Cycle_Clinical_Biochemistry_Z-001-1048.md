# Krebs Cycle Clinical Biochemistry

Central mitochondrial pathway oxidizing acetyl-CoA to CO₂ while generating reducing equivalents (NADH, FADH₂) and biosynthetic intermediates.

## Evidence
> "The Krebs cycle is not just energy production — it is the metabolic crossroads of the cell." (Biochemistry textbooks)

## The 8 Steps

| Step | Enzyme | Input | Output | Cofactors | Clinical Marker |
|------|--------|-------|--------|-----------|-----------------|
| 1 | Citrate synthase | Acetyl-CoA + OAA | Citrate | None | — |
| 2 | **Aconitase** | Citrate | Isocitrate | Fe-S cluster | Citrate ↑ = oxidative stress |
| 3 | Isocitrate DH | Isocitrate | α-KG | NAD⁺ → NADH | — |
| 4 | **α-KG dehydrogenase** | α-KG | Succinyl-CoA | TPP, FAD, NAD⁺, CoA, Mg²⁺ | α-KG ↑ = B1/B2/B3/B5/Mg deficiency |
| 5 | Succinyl-CoA synthetase | Succinyl-CoA | Succinate | GTP produced | — |
| 6 | **Succinate dehydrogenase** | Succinate | Fumarate | FAD → FADH₂ | Succinate ↑ = Complex II dysfunction |
| 7 | Fumarase | Fumarate | Malate | None | — |
| 8 | Malate dehydrogenase | Malate | OAA | NAD⁺ → NADH | — |

## Energy Yield
- 3 NADH → 7.5 ATP
- 1 FADH₂ → 1.5 ATP
- 1 GTP → 1 ATP
- **Total: ~10 ATP per acetyl-CoA**

## Clinical Interpretation

### OAT Patterns
| Marker | Interpretation | Cofactor Needed |
|--------|----------------|-----------------|
| Citrate ↑ | Aconitase inhibition (oxidative stress, Fe-S damage) | Antioxidants, Fe |
| α-Ketoglutarate ↑ | α-KGDH block | B1, B2, B3, B5, Mg |
| Succinate ↑ | Complex II dysfunction, hypoxia | CoQ10, B2 |

### Critical Cofactor: Pyruvate Dehydrogenase
Requires **6 cofactors**: TPP (B1), FAD (B2), NAD⁺ (B3), CoA (B5), Mg²⁺, Lipoic acid
→ Deficiency in ANY → pyruvate accumulation → lactic acidosis

## Context
The Krebs cycle is the **central hub** of metabolism:
- Entry point for carbs (via pyruvate)
- Entry point for fats (via β-oxidation → acetyl-CoA)
- Entry point for proteins (via amino acid deamination)
- Source of biosynthetic precursors (anaplerosis/cataplerosis)
