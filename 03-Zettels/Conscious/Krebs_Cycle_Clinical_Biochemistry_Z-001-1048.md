## Core Claim
**The Krebs cycle (TCA cycle) serves as the central metabolic crossroads of the cell — oxidizing acetyl-CoA to CO₂ while generating reducing equivalents (NADH, FADH₂) and biosynthetic intermediates, with each step requiring specific cofactors whose deficiencies produce characteristic clinical and OAT marker patterns.**

## Evidence

### The 8 Steps of the Krebs Cycle
| Step | Enzyme | Input | Output | Cofactors | Clinical Marker |
|------|--------|-------|--------|-----------|-----------------|
| 1 | Citrate synthase | Acety-CoA + OAA | Citrate | None | — |
| 2 | **Aconitase** | Citrate | Isocitrate | Fe-S cluster | Citrate ↑ = oxidative stress |
| 3 | Isocitrate DH | Isocitrate | α-KG | NAD⁺ → NADH | — |
| 4 | **α-KG dehydrogenase** | α-KG | Succinyl-CoA | TPP, FAD, NAD⁺, CoA, Mg²⁺ | α-KG ↑ = B-vitamin deficiency |
| 5 | Succinyl-CoA synthetase | Succinyl-CoA | Succinate | GTP produced | — |
| 6 | **Succinate dehydrogenase** | Succinate | Fumarate | FAD → FADH₂ | Succinate ↑ = Complex II dysfunction |
| 7 | Fumarase | Fumarate | Malate | None | — |
| 8 | Malate dehydrogenase | Malate | OAA | NAD⁺ → NADH | — |

### Energy Yield per Acetyl-CoA
| Product | Quantity | ATP Equivalent |
|---------|----------|----------------|
| NADH | 3 | 7.5 ATP |
| FADH₂ | 1 | 1.5 ATP |
| GTP | 1 | 1 ATP |
| **Total** | — | **~10 ATP** |

### Clinical Significance
| Feature | Implication |
|---------|-------------|
| **Amphibolic** | Both catabolic and anabolic functions |
| **Anaplerosis** | OAA replenishment critical for cycle continuity |
| **Cataplerosis** | Intermediates leave for biosynthesis |
| **Cofactor dependence** | B-vitamins essential at multiple steps |

### OAT Markers Related to TCA
| Marker | Indicates | Cofactor Need |
|--------|-----------|---------------|
| Citrate ↑ | Aconitase inhibition (ROS) | Antioxidants |
| α-KG ↑ | α-KGDH dysfunction | B1, B2, B3, B5, Mg |
| Succinate ↑ | Complex II inhibition | B2, CoQ10 |

## Clinical Implication
- **Fatigue etiology:** TCA block produces profound energy deficit
- **Cofactor optimization:** B-vitamin status directly impacts cycle flux
- **Oxidative stress:** ROS inhibits aconitase, creating vicious cycle
- **Biosynthetic support:** TCA intermediates feed amino acid synthesis
- **METHAP relevance:** TCA cycle function is central to metabolic health; intermediates are research biomarkers for mitochondrial dysfunction
