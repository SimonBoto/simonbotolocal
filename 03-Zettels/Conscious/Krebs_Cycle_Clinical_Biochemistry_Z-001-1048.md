# Krebs Cycle Clinical 

## Overview
Central l pathway oxidizing acetyl-CoA to CO₂ while generating reducing equivalents (NADH, FADH₂) and biosynthetic intermediates.

## Cycle Reactions (8 Steps)

| Step | Enzyme | Input | Output | Cofactors |
|------|--------|-------|--------|-----------|
| 1 | Citrate synthase | Acetyl-CoA + Oxaloacetate | Citrate | None |
| 2 | Aconitase | Citrate | Isocitrate | Fe-S cluster |
| 3 | Isocitrate dehydrogenase | Isocitrate | α-Ketoglutarate | NAD⁺ → NADH |
| 4 | α-Ketoglutarate dehydrogenase | α-KG | Succinyl-CoA | TPP, FAD, NAD⁺, CoA, Mg²⁺ |
| 5 | Succinyl-CoA synthetase | Succinyl-CoA | Succinate | GTP produced |
| 6 | Succinate dehydrogenase | Succinate | Fumarate | FAD → FADH₂ |
| 7 | Fumarase | Fumarate | Malate | None |
| 8 | Malate dehydrogenase | Malate | Oxaloacetate | NAD⁺ → NADH |

## Energy Yield (per acetyl-CoA)
- 3 NADH → 7.5 ATP (via ETC)
- 1 FADH₂ → 1.5 ATP (via ETC)
- 1 GTP → 1 ATP (substrate-level)
- **Total: ~10 ATP**

## Anaplerotic Inputs
- Pyruvate → oxaloacetate (pyruvate carboxylase)
- Glutamate → α-ketoglutarate
- Aspartate → oxaloacetate

## Cataplerotic Outputs
- Citrate → fatty acid synthesis
- α-KG → glutamate, glutamine
- Succinyl-CoA → heme synthesis
- Oxaloacetate → gluconeogenesis

## Clinical Relevance
OAT markers reveal cycle dysfunction:
- Elevated citrate → aconitase inhibition (oxidative stress)
- Elevated succinate → Complex II dysfunction
- Low intermediates → substrate limitation
