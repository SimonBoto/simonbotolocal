# Drug Holiday Recommendations

## Indications for Holiday

- **Low fracture risk:** FRAX score < 10% 10-year major osteoporotic fracture risk
- **Improved BMD:** Stable or improved bone mineral density on treatment
- **No new fractures:** Absence of clinical or vertebral fractures during therapy
- **Patient preference:** When risk-benefit analysis favors temporary discontinuation

## Monitoring During Holiday

- **DXA scans:** Every 2 years to assess BMD changes
- **Clinical assessment:** Annually to evaluate new risk factors
- **Biochemical markers:** CTX or P1NP every 6-12 months to monitor bone turnover

## Restart Criteria

- **BMD decline:** >5% loss from baseline or nadir
- **New fracture:** Clinical or morphometric vertebral fracture
- **FRAX increase:** >20% increase in 10-year major osteoporotic fracture risk
- **Worsening risk factors:** New glucocorticoid therapy, significant weight loss, or other clinical changes

## Special Considerations

- **Bisphosphonates:** Most common candidates for drug holidays due to long skeletal half-life
- **Denosumab:** Not recommended for drug holidays due to rapid bone loss rebound
- **Anabolic agents:** Not typically used with drug holidays due to limited duration of therapy
