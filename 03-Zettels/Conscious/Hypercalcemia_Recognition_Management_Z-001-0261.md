## Core Principle

> Hypercalcemia, defined as serum calcium >10.5 mg/dL, is the primary safety concern with high-dose  protocols, requiring systematic recognition, grading, and management to prevent serious complications including cardiac arrhythmias and renal damage.

## Causes of Hypercalcemia

### Common Causes
| Rank | Cause |  |
|------|-------|-----------|
| 1 | Primary hyperparathyroidism | Excess PTH |
| 2 | Malignancy | PTHrP, bone metastases |
| 3 | Vitamin D toxicity | Excessive absorption |
| 4 | Thiazide diuretics | Reduced renal excretion |
| 5 | Immobilization | Bone resorption |

### Vitamin D-Related
- Excessive supplementation (>10,000 IU without monitoring)
- Granulomatous diseases (sarcoidosis, TB)
- Idiopathic hypercalcemia of infancy

## Clinical Manifestations

### Gastrointestinal
- Nausea, vomiting
- Constipation (classic)
- Abdominal pain
- Pancreatitis (rare)

### Renal
- Polyuria, polydipsia
- Nephrogenic  insipidus
- Kidney stones (calcium oxalate)
- Nephrocalcinosis
- Renal failure (chronic)

### Neurological
- Fatigue, weakness
- Depression, confusion
- Cognitive impairment
- Coma (severe)

### Cardiac
- Shortened QT interval
- Arrhythmias (severe)
- Hypertension

### Musculoskeletal
- Bone pain (paradoxical)
- Pathologic fractures (chronic)

## Grading and Management

### Mild (10.5-11.5 mg/dL)
**Usually asymptomatic**
- Monitor
- Increase hydration
- Reduce calcium intake

### Moderate (11.5-13 mg/dL)
**May have symptoms**
- IV hydration (NS)
- Loop diuretics (after hydration)
- Treat underlying cause
- For D toxicity: Stop D, consider glucocorticoids

### Severe (>13 mg/dL or symptomatic)
**Medical emergency**
- Aggressive IV hydration
- Loop diuretics
- Calcitonin (rapid, short-lived effect)
- Bisphosphonates (longer effect)
- Dialysis (if renal failure)

## METHAP-Specific Management

### Prevention
- Baseline calcium measurement
- Exclude hypercalcemia
- Ensure K2 co-administration
- Mg optimization

### Monitoring
- Check calcium at 4, 8, 12 weeks
- Patient education on symptoms
- Clear stopping rules

### Intervention Thresholds
| Serum Ca | Action |
|----------|--------|
| 10.5-11.0 | Monitor closely |
| 11.0-11.5 | Reduce D 50% |
| >11.5 | Stop D, investigate |
| Symptoms | Immediate action |

### If Hypercalcemia Occurs
1. Stop vitamin D immediately
2. Check 25(OH)D level
3. Assess for other causes (PTH, malignancy)
4. Hydration
5. Consider need for other treatments
