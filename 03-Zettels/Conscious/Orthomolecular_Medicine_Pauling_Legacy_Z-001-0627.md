# Orthomolecular Medicine Pauling Legacy

Pauling founded orthomolecular medicine — the practice of preventing and treating disease by providing the body with optimal amounts of natural substances it requires — a paradigm that challenges deficiency-based medicine and is gaining scientific validation decades later.

## Definition

**Orthomolecular medicine:**
> "The preservation of good health and the treatment of disease by varying the concentrations in the human body of substances that are normally present in the body and required for health."
> — Linus Pauling, 1968

**Key principles:**
1. Substances must be natural to the body
2. Concentrations must be optimal, not just adequate
3. Individual variation is recognized
4. Prevention is prioritized

## Contrast with Conventional Medicine

| Aspect | Conventional | Orthomolecular |
|--------|-------------|----------------|
| Focus | Disease treatment | Health optimization |
| Standard | Prevent deficiency | Achieve optimal function |
| Tools | Pharmaceuticals | Natural substances |
| Model | Reactive | Preventive |
| Dose | Minimum effective | Optimal for individual |

## Pauling's Specific Contributions

**Vitamin C advocacy:**
- 2,000-10,000mg daily recommendations
- Based on evolutionary biology
- Challenged RDA adequacy
- Explored therapeutic potential

**Other orthomolecular interests:**
- Vitamin B3 (niacin) for schizophrenia
- Micronutrient optimization
- Mental health applications
- Cardiovascular disease prevention

## The Scientific Foundation

**Why it made sense to Pauling:**
- Molecular biology background
- Understanding of enzyme kinetics
- Recognition of biochemical individuality
- Evolutionary perspective

**Mechanisms:**
- Cofactor saturation
- Metabolic optimization
- Individual biochemical variation
- Prevention of subclinical deficiency

## Modern Validation

**Areas gaining acceptance:**
- **Nutrigenomics** — gene-nutrient interactions
- **Personalized medicine** — individual variation
- **Functional medicine** — optimal vs. normal
- **Preventive cardiology** — lifestyle interventions

**Research supporting orthomolecular concepts:**
- High-dose B vitamins for homocysteine
- Vitamin D optimization
- Omega-3 fatty acids
- Magnesium supplementation

## Remaining Controversial

**Still debated:**
- High-dose vitamin C for cancer
- Megavitamin therapy for mental illness
- Orthomolecular psychiatry
- Claims beyond evidence base

## The Legacy

**Pauling's impact:**
- Coined "orthomolecular"
- Established theoretical framework
- Inspired research (despite opposition)
- Created patient empowerment model

**Current status:**
- Orthomolecular Medicine News Service (OMNS)
- Journal of Orthomolecular Medicine
- Practitioners worldwide
- Growing scientific interest
