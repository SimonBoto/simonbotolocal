# Ketogenic Amino Acids: Leucine and Lysine

## The Only Purely Ketogenic Amino Acids

### Definition
**Ketogenic amino acids** cannot be converted to glucose.
- They yield acetyl-CoA or acetoacetyl-CoA
- No net production of oxaloacetate or other glucogenic intermediates

## Leucine 

### Pathway
```
Leucine → α-ketoisocaproate → Isovaleryl-CoA → β-methylcrotonyl-CoA →
Methylglutaconyl-CoA → HMG-CoA → Acetoacetate + Acetyl-CoA
```

### Products
- **Acetoacetate:** Ketone body (ketogenic)
- **Acetyl-CoA:** Enters Krebs or ketogenesis (ketogenic)

### Regulation Note
**Leucine allosterically activates:**
- Glutamate dehydrogenase (amino acid catabolism)
- mTOR pathway (protein synthesis, anabolic signal)

## Lysine Metabolism

### Complex Pathway
**Bifurcated:**
- One branch → Acetoacetyl-CoA
- Another branch → Acetyl-CoA

**Key enzyme:** Bifunctional enzyme (lysine-ketoglutarate reductase/saccharopine dehydrogenase)

### Location
- **Liver:** Primarily catabolized
- **Periphery:** Limited metabolism

## Both Also Yield Acetyl-CoA

### Acetyl-CoA Fates
| Condition | Fate |
|-----------|------|
| **Fed** | Fatty acid synthesis,  synthesis |
| **Fasting** | Ketogenesis (liver) |
| **Aerobic** | Krebs cycle (if OAA available) |

## : Maple Syrup Urine Disease (MSUD)

### Cause
**Branched-chain α-keto acid dehydrogenase (BCKDH) deficiency**
- Affects leucine, isoleucine, valine
- Leucine most toxic

### Consequences
- Accumulation of BCAAs and their α-keto acids
- Severe neurological damage
- "Maple syrup" odor in urine/earwax
- Neonatal crisis if untreated

### Treatment
- **BCAA-restricted diet** (lifelong)
- **Liver transplant:** Curative (provides functional BCKDH)
- **Gene therapy:** Investigational

## Comparison: Leucine vs Lysine

| Feature | Leucine | Lysine |
|---------|---------|--------|
| **Side chain** | Branched | Basic (ε-amino group) |
| **Pathway complexity** | Straightforward | Complex, bifurcated |
| **mTOR activation** | Strong | None |
| **Inborn error** | MSUD | No common deficiency |
