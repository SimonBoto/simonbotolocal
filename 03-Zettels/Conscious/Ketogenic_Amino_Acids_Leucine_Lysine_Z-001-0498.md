## Core Claim
**Leucine and lysine are the only purely ketogenic amino acids — they cannot be converted to glucose because they yield only acetyl-CoA or acetoacetyl-CoA without producing oxaloacetate or other glucogenic intermediates** — leucine also allosterically activates mTOR, signaling protein synthesis and anabolism.

## Evidence

### Definition
| Feature | Ketogenic Amino Acids |
|---------|----------------------|
| **Products** | Acetyl-CoA or acetoacetyl-CoA |
| **Glucose synthesis** | Impossible (no net OAA production) |
| **Number** | Only 2: leucine and lysine |

### Leucine Catabolism
```
Leucine → α-ketoisocaproate → Isovaleryl-CoA → β-methylcrotonyl-CoA →
Methylglutaconyl-CoA → HMG-CoA → Acetoacetate + Acetyl-CoA
```
| Product | Fate |
|---------|------|
| **Acetoacetate** | Ketone body |
| **Acetyl-CoA** | Krebs or ketogenesis |

### Leucine → mTOR Activation
| Feature | Effect |
|---------|--------|
| **Signal** | Nutrient sufficiency |
| **Outcome** | Protein synthesis ↑, autophagy ↓ |
| **Clinical** | Important for muscle protein synthesis |

### Lysine Catabolism
```
Lysine → Saccharopine → α-aminoadipate → ... → Acetoacetyl-CoA
```
- **Complex pathway:** Multiple steps
- **Final product:** Acetoacetyl-CoA (ketogenic)

## Clinical Implication
- **Purely ketogenic:** Cannot contribute to gluconeogenesis
- **Leucine and mTOR:** Key anabolic signal
- **Ketogenic diets:** High leucine supports muscle preservation
- **METHAP relevance:** Leucine is key BCAA for muscle protein synthesis; ketogenic amino acids support ketosis
