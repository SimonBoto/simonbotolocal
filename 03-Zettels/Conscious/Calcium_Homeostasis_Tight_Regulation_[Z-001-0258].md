# Calcium Homeostasis — Tight Physiological Regulation

> Serum calcium is maintained within the narrowest physiological range of any electrolyte (±2%) through precise regulation by PTH, vitamin D, and calcitonin, reflecting its critical importance for life-sustaining functions.

## Normal Values

**Total Serum Calcium:**
- **Normal:** 8.5-10.5 mg/dL (2.12-2.62 mmol/L)
- **Ionized:** 4.65-5.25 mg/dL (1.16-1.31 mmol/L)
- **Regulation precision:** ±0.2 mg/dL

**Circadian Variation:**
- Lowest: Early morning
- Highest: Late afternoon
- Variation: ~0.5 mg/dL

## Critical Functions Requiring Tight Control

### 1. Neuromuscular Excitability
**Mechanism:**
- Calcium modulates sodium channel opening
- Extracellular Ca²⁺ inversely related to excitability
- Low Ca²⁺ → Hyperexcitability (tetany)
- High Ca²⁺ → Hypoexcitability (weakness)

### 2. Cardiac Contractility
- Calcium triggers excitation-contraction coupling
- Essential for cardiac muscle contraction
- Abnormal levels → Arrhythmias

### 3. Blood Clotting
- Calcium required for multiple clotting factors
- Factor IV in coagulation cascade
- Essential for hemostasis

### 4. Bone Integrity
- Hydroxyapatite: Ca₁₀(PO₄)₆(OH)₂
- Provides skeletal strength
- Reservoir for calcium homeostasis

### 5. Intracellular Signaling
- Second messenger for hormones
- Neurotransmitter release
- Muscle contraction
- Enzyme regulation

## Consequences of Dysregulation

### Hypocalcemia (<8.5 mg/dL)
**Acute:**
- Paresthesias (perioral, fingers)
- Muscle cramps, tetany
- Carpopedal spasm
- Laryngospasm
- Seizures

**Chronic:**
- Cataracts
- Dermatitis
- Dental abnormalities
- Osteomalacia

### Hypercalcemia (>10.5 mg/dL)
**Mild (10.5-11.5):**
- Often asymptomatic
- Constipation
- Fatigue

**Moderate (11.5-13):**
- Polyuria, polydipsia
- Nausea, vomiting
- Abdominal pain
- Depression, confusion

**Severe (>13):**
- Cardiac arrhythmias
- Coma
- Death if untreated

## Regulatory Systems

**PTH:** Rapid (minutes)
**Vitamin D:** Slow (hours-days)
**Calcitonin:** Minor role (humans)
