## Mechanism

**The liver produces ketone bodies from fatty acid β-oxidation during low-insulin states (fasting, ketogenic diet) — acetyl-CoA condenses to acetoacetate via HMG-CoA, which is then reduced to BHB (the major circulating ketone) or spontaneously decarboxylates to acetone** — HMG-CoA synthase is rate-limiting and induced by glucagon/cortisol, while insulin inhibits ketogenesis via malonyl-CoA-mediated CPT-I inhibition.

## Mechanism

**The liver produces ketone bodies from fatty acid β-oxidation during low-insulin states (fasting, ketogenic diet) — acetyl-CoA condenses to acetoacetate via HMG-CoA, which is then reduced to BHB (the major circulating ketone) or spontaneously decarboxylates to acetone** — HMG-CoA synthase is rate-limiting and induced by glucagon/cortisol, while insulin inhibits ketogenesis via malonyl-CoA-mediated CPT-I inhibition.

## Evidence

### Trigger Conditions
| Condition | Mechanism |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Low carbohydrate** | Depleted glycogen, reduced insulin |
| **Low insulin** | Permits lipolysis, activates hormone-sensitive lipase |
| **High fatty acid oxidation** | Excess acetyl-CoA exceeds Krebs capacity |

### Hepatic Synthesis Pathway
```
Fatty acid → Acetyl-CoA (β-oxidation)
     ↓
2 Acetyl-CoA → Acetoacetyl-CoA (thiolase)
     ↓
+ Acetyl-CoA → HMG-CoA (HMG-CoA synthase — rate-limiting)
     ↓
HMG-CoA → Acetoacetate (HMG-CoA lyase)
     ↓
Acetoacetate → BHB (β-OHBDH, NADH-dependent)
```

### Key Regulatory Enzymes
| Enzyme | Regulation |
|--------|------------|
| **CPT-I** | Inhibited by malonyl-CoA (insulin signal) |
| **HMG-CoA synthase** | Induced by fasting/glucagon |
| **β-OHBDH** | NADH/NAD⁺ ratio drives BHB formation |

### Hormonal Control
| Hormone | Effect | Mechanism |
|---------|--------|-----------|
| **Insulin** | ↓ Ketogenesis | Inhibits lipolysis, increases malonyl-CoA |
| **Glucagon** | ↑ Ketogenesis | Activates lipolysis, induces HMG-CoA synthase |
| **Cortisol** | ↑ Ketogenesis | Permissive effect on lipolysis |

## Clinical Implication
- **Therapeutic ketosis:** 1-3 mM BHB, safe and beneficial
- **Diabetic ketoacidosis:** >10-25 mM, life-threatening acidosis
- **CPT-I deficiency:** Impaired ketogenesis, hypoketotic hypoglycemia
- **METHAP relevance:** Mild nutritional ketosis may enhance metabolic flexibility; HMG-CoA pathway is distinct from cholesterol synthesis pathway
