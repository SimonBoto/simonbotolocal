## Core Principle

Ethics committees may not respond promptly. Systematic follow-up protects your timeline while maintaining professional relationships.

## Response Time Expectations

| Committee Type | Normal Response | Follow-Up Threshold |
|----------------|-----------------|---------------------|
| University IRB | 4-8 weeks | 10 weeks |
| Hospital ethics | 6-12 weeks | 14 weeks |
| National committee | 3-6 months | 7 months |
| Multi-site review | 6-12 months | 14 months |

## Core Principle

Ethics committees may not respond promptly. Systematic follow-up protects your timeline while maintaining professional relationships.

## Follow-Up Sequence

### Week 1–2: Initial Submission
- [ ] Confirm receipt (email read receipt or phone call)
- [ ] Note submission date and assigned reference number
- [ ] Identify committee meeting schedule

### Week 4–6: First Follow-Up
- [ ] Email to administrative contact
- [ ] Reference submission date and number
- [ ] Request status update politely

### Week 8–10: Second Follow-Up
- [ ] Email to committee chair
- [ ] Copy supervisor and department
- [ ] Express concern about timeline impact

### Week 12+: Escalation
- [ ] Phone call to chair
- [ ] Department head involvement
- [ ] Consider institutional ombudsman
- [ ] Document all attempts

## Core Principle

Ethics committees may not respond promptly. Systematic follow-up protects your timeline while maintaining professional relationships.

## Documentation Requirements

Keep records of:
- Submission date and method
- All follow-up dates and responses
- Names of contacts
- Reference numbers
- Meeting dates (if known)

## Core Principle

Ethics committees may not respond promptly. Systematic follow-up protects your timeline while maintaining professional relationships.

## Red Flags

Non-response may indicate:
- **Substantive concerns** — Protocol requires major revision
- **Administrative backlog** — Committee overwhelmed
- **Political issues** — Supervisor conflicts, institutional problems
- **Missing information** — Your application incomplete

## When to Worry

| Timeline | Action |
|----------|--------|
| 2x normal response | Standard follow-up |
| 3x normal response | Escalate to chair |
| 4x normal response | Involve department |
| 6x normal response | Consider formal complaint |
