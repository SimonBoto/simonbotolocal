## Core Claim
**Glutathione (GSH) is the most abundant intracellular antioxidant and detoxification agent, synthesized from cysteine (rate-limiting), glutamate, and glycine** — its GSH:GSSG redox ratio (~100:1 in health) serves as the primary indicator of cellular oxidative stress, and its synthesis depends on methionine-derived cysteine via transsulfuration.

## Evidence

### Structure and Synthesis
| Component | Source | Role |
|-----------|--------|------|
| **γ-Glutamate** | Diet/metabolism | Peptide bond (γ-linkage) |
| **Cysteine** | Transsulfuration | **Rate-limiting** — provides thiol (-SH) |
| **Glycine** | Diet/metabolism | Completes tripeptide |

**Two-Step Synthesis:**
1. **GCL enzyme:** Glutamate + Cysteine → γ-Glutamylcysteine
2. **GSS enzyme:** γ-Glutamylcysteine + Glycine → Glutathione

### Functions
| Function | Mechanism |
|----------|-----------|
| **Antioxidant** | 2 GSH + H₂O₂ → GSSG + 2 H₂O |
| **Regeneration** | Recycles vitamins C and E |
| **Detoxification** | Conjugates toxins (Phase II) |
| **Heavy metals** | Chelates mercury, lead, arsenic |

### Redox Status
- **GSH:GSSG ratio:** ~100:1 in healthy cells
- **Declining ratio** indicates oxidative stress
- **Intracellular concentration:** 1-10 mM (highest of any antioxidant)

## Clinical Implication
- **Cysteine availability is rate-limiting** — methionine/B6 adequacy determines glutathione synthesis capacity
- **Depleted in:** chronic disease, aging, toxin exposure, intense exercise
- **NAC (N-acetylcysteine)** provides cysteine precursor to boost glutathione
- **METHAP relevance:** B6 supports transsulfuration → cysteine → glutathione; magnesium required for GCL enzyme
