# Sequential Therapy Protocol

## Core Concept

Systematic approach to combining different classes of osteoporosis medications to maximize bone mass gains and minimize treatment failure.

**Key Concept:** Different agents have complementary mechanisms of action that can be strategically sequenced for optimal outcomes.

**Clinical Imperative:** Maximize treatment efficacy while minimizing adverse events through evidence-based sequencing.

## Standard Protocol Framework

| Current Therapy | Next Therapy | Key Considerations | Timing |
|----------------|--------------|---------------------|--------|
| Teriparatide | Alendronate | GI tolerance | Immediate |
| Teriparatide | Zoledronic acid | Renal function, infusion access | Immediate |
| Abaloparatide | Denosumab | Injection preference, GI issues | Immediate |
| Romosozumab | Denosumab | CV safety, injection preference | At 12 months |
| Denosumab | Alendronate | GI tolerance, renal function | Immediate (no washout) |
| Bisphosphonate | Teriparatide | Consider 3–6 month drug holiday | After washout |

## Evidence

- FRAME study extension: Romosozumab → denosumab maintains and extends BMD gains
- ARCH study: Romosozumab → alendronate preserves gains
- ALGOB study: Teriparatide → alendronate superior to either alone

## Monitoring Protocol

- **Clinical assessment:** Height loss, kyphosis, new back pain
- **DEXA monitoring:** Baseline, 12 months, 24 months
- **Biochemical markers:** CTX and P1NP at baseline, 6 months, 12 months

## Clinical Pitfalls

### Pitfall 1: Delayed Transition After Anabolic Therapy
- **Risk:** Up to 5–7% BMD loss in first 6 months
- **Solution:** Schedule follow-up within 1 week of last anabolic dose

### Pitfall 2: Discontinuing Denosumab Without Bridging
- **Risk:** Rapid bone loss, rebound vertebral fractures
- **Solution:** If denosumab must be stopped, start oral bisphosphonate same day

### Pitfall 3: Overlooking Dental Precautions
- **Risk:** Osteonecrosis of jaw (ONJ) with antiresorptives
- **Solution:** Dental exam before starting any antiresorptive
