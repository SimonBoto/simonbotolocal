## Core Claim
**Organic acids are carboxylic acid metabolites that serve as diagnostic biomarkers in organic acids testing (OAT)—elevated levels reveal specific metabolic blocks, cofactor deficiencies, or mitochondrial dysfunction** — pattern recognition enables targeted nutritional intervention.

## Evidence

### Krebs Cycle Organic Acids (OAT Markers)
| Acid | Structure | Clinical Significance |
|------|-----------|----------------------|
| **Citrate** | 6C tricarboxylic | Aconitase inhibition (oxidative stress, B3) |
| **Alpha-ketoglutarate** | 5C keto acid | B-vitamin cofactor deficiency |
| **Succinate** | 4C dicarboxylic | Complex II dysfunction, hypoxia, B2 |

### Glycolysis-Related
| Acid | OAT Marker | Indicates |
|------|------------|-----------|
| **Pyruvate** | Pyruvate | B1 deficiency, PDH block, mitochondrial dysfunction |
| **Lactate** | Lactate | Hypoxia, mitochondrial dysfunction, B1/B3 |

### Fatty Acid Oxidation Markers
| Acid | OAT Marker | Indicates |
|------|------------|-----------|
| **Suberate** | Suberate | Medium-chain FAO impairment, carnitine deficiency |
| **Adipate** | Adipate | General FAO stress |
| **Ethylmalonate** | Ethylmalonate | Multiple FAO deficiency, B2 |

### B-Vitamin Deficiency Markers
| Acid | Related To | Deficiency |
|------|------------|------------|
| **Methylmalonate** | Val, Ile, Met, B12 | B12 deficiency |
| **FIGLU** | Histidine, folate | Folate deficiency |
| **Kynurenate/Xanthurenate** | Tryptophan, B6 | B6 deficiency |

## Clinical Implication
- **OAT provides functional assessment** — reveals metabolic blocks before clinical symptoms
- **Pattern recognition** more valuable than single markers
- **Methylmalonate** — most clinically validated B12 marker
- **METHAP relevance:** OAT guides personalized nutrient dosing; baseline and follow-up testing validates protocol efficacy
