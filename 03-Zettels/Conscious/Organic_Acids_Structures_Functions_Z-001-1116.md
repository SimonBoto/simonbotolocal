# Organic Acids Structures Functions

## Overview
Reference table of key organic acids in —their structures, sources, and clinical significance in organic acids testing (OAT).

## Krebs Cycle Organic Acids

| Acid | Structure | OAT Marker | Clinical Significance |
|------|-----------|------------|----------------------|
| **Citrate** | 6C tricarboxylic | Citrate | Aconitase inhibition (oxidative stress) |
| **Isocitrate** | 6C, -OH added | Not measured | Intermediate only |
| **Alpha-ketoglutarate** | 5C, keto acid | α-KG | B-vitamin cofactor deficiency |
| **Succinate** | 4C dicarboxylic | Succinate | Complex II dysfunction, hypoxia |
| **Fumarate** | 4C, trans-ene | Not measured | Hydration product |
| **Malate** | 4C, -OH added | Not measured | Dehydrogenation product |
| **Oxaloacetate** | 4C keto acid | Not stable | Key intermediate |

## Glycolysis-Related Organic Acids

| Acid | Structure | OAT Marker | Clinical Significance |
|------|-----------|------------|----------------------|
| **Pyruvate** | 3C keto acid | Pyruvate | B1 deficiency, PDH block |
| **Lactate** | 3C, -OH replaces keto | Lactate | Hypoxia, mitochondrial dysfunction |

## Fatty Acid Oxidation Markers

| Acid | Structure | OAT Marker | Clinical Significance |
|------|-----------|------------|----------------------|
| **Suberate** | 8C dicarboxylic | Suberate | Medium-chain FAO impairment |
| **Adipate** | 6C dicarboxylic | Adipate | General FAO stress |
| **Ethylmalonate** | 5C, ethyl branch | Ethylmalonate | Multiple FAO deficiency |

## B-ependent Markers

| Acid | Related AA/Vitamin | OAT Marker | Deficiency Indicated |
|------|-------------------|------------|---------------------|
| **Methylmalonate** | Val, Ile, Met, B12 | Methylmalonate | B12 deficiency |
| **FIGLU** | Histidine, folate | FIGLU | Folate deficiency |
| **Kynurenate** | Tryptophan, B6 | Kynurenate | B6 deficiency |
| **Xanthurenate** | Tryptophan, B6 | Xanthurenate | B6 deficiency |

## Other Significant Organic Acids

### Oxalate Family
| Acid | Source | Clinical |
|------|--------|----------|
| **Oxalate** | Diet, ascorbate, glyoxylate | Kidney stones, toxicity |
| **Glyoxylate** | Glycine metabolism | Primary hyperoxaluria |

### Aromatic Metabolites
| Acid | Source | OAT Marker |
|------|--------|------------|
| **Hippurate** | Glycine + benzoate | Gut bacteria activity |
| **Phenylacetate** | Phenylalanine | Bacterial overgrowth |
| **Indican** | Tryptophan | Gut permeability |

### Krebs Related
| Acid | Relationship | Clinical |
|------|--------------|----------|
| **Pyroglutamate** | Glutathione metabolism | Glutathione deficiency |
| **Orotate** | Pyrimidine synthesis | OTC deficiency marker |

## OAT Pattern Recognition

### Mitochondrial Dysfunction Pattern
- Elevated: Lactate, pyruvate, succinate, citrate
- Indicates: ETC impairment, oxidative stress

### B-Vitamin Deficiency Pattern
- Elevated: Pyruvate, α-KG, methylmalonate, FIGLU
- Indicates: Multiple cofactor deficiencies

### Gut Dysbiosis Pattern
- Elevated: Hippurate (low), phenylacetate, indican
- Indicates: Bacterial imbalance

## Structural Features Summary

| Feature | Significance |
|---------|-------------|
| **Keto group (C=O)** | Indicates deamination state |
| **Carboxyl groups (-COOH)** | Acidic properties, urine excretion |
| **Hydroxyl groups (-OH)** | Reduction state, metabolic intermediates |
| **Branching** | Often indicates amino acid origin |
