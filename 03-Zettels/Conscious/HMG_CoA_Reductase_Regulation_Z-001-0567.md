## Core Claim
**HMG-CoA reductase — the rate-limiting enzyme of cholesterol synthesis — is controlled through multiple regulatory layers including SREBP-2 transcriptional control, AMPK phosphorylation-mediated inhibition, and cholesterol-dependent degradation, making it the primary target of statin drugs and a central node in metabolic homeostasis.**

## Evidence

### Regulatory Mechanisms

#### 1. Transcriptional Control (SREBP-2)
| Condition | SREBP-2 Activity | HMG-CoA Reductase Expression |
|-----------|------------------|------------------------------|
| **Low cholesterol** | Active (cleaved, nuclear) | ↑ Increased transcription |
| **High cholesterol** | Inactive (ER membrane) | ↓ Decreased transcription |
| **Insulin** | Activates SREBP-2 | ↑ Increased expression |
| **Fasting/glucagon** | Inhibits SREBP-2 | ↓ Decreased expression |

#### 2. Post-Translational Control (AMPK)
| State | AMPK Activity | HMG-CoA Reductase | Effect |
|-------|---------------|-------------------|--------|
| **High AMP/ATP** | Active | Phosphorylated | **INHIBITED** |
| **Low AMP/ATP** | Inactive | Dephosphorylated | **ACTIVE** |
| **Exercise** | AMPK activated | Inhibited | Energy conservation |
| **Metformin** | AMPK activated | Inhibited | Mimics energy stress |

#### 3. Protein Degradation
| Condition | Mechanism | Result |
|-----------|-----------|--------|
| **High cholesterol** | Ubiquitination → proteasome | Enzyme degradation |
| **Low cholesterol** | Stabilized | Prolonged half-life |
| **Statin treatment** | Compensatory upregulation | More enzyme produced |

### Statin Mechanism
| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Target** | HMG-CoA reductase active site |
| **Mechanism** | Competitive inhibition |
| **Mimicry** | Structurally similar to HMG-CoA |
| **Result** | ↓ Mevalonate → ↓ Cholesterol |
| **Compensatory response** | ↑ HMG-CoA reductase expression |

### Statin Side Effect Pathway
```
Statin → HMG-CoA reductase inhibition
    ↓
↓ Mevalonate synthesis
    ↓
↓ Isopentenyl pyrophosphate (IPP)
    ↓
↓ CoQ10 + Dolichol + Isoprenoid proteins
    ↓
Mitochondrial dysfunction + Myopathy
```

### Clinical Correlations
| Observation | Mechanism | Management |
|-------------|-----------|------------|
| **Statin myopathy** | CoQ10 depletion | CoQ10 supplementation |
| **Statin resistance** | Upregulated HMG-CoA reductase | Higher dose or alternative |
| **Exercise intolerance** | AMPK + statin double inhibition | Timing of dose |
| **Diabetes risk** | SREBP-2 → insulin resistance | Monitor glucose |

### Energy Sensing Integration
| Pathway | Regulator | Effect on Cholesterol Synthesis |
|---------|-----------|--------------------------------|
| **AMPK** | Energy stress | Inhibits |
| **mTOR** | Nutrient abundance | Promotes (via SREBP) |
| **Insulin** | Fed state | Promotes |
| **Glucagon** | Fasted state | Inhibits |

## Clinical Implication
- **Primary statin target:** Understanding mechanism explains efficacy and side effects
- **CoQ10 depletion:** Universal effect requiring supplementation
- **Compensatory upregulation:** Explains dose escalation needs
- **Energy sensing:** Links cholesterol metabolism to overall metabolic state
- **METHAP relevance:** Rational statin use requires CoQ10 support; metabolic syndrome alters pathway regulation
