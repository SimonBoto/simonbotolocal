# Nucleoside Formation and Phosphorylation Pathways

## Core Claim
**Nucleotide synthesis from bases occurs via two sequential steps: nucleoside formation (base + ribose) followed by progressive phosphorylation (NMP → NDP → NTP), with each phosphate addition requiring ATP investment** — this energy investment creates the high-energy phosphate bonds that power cellular work.

## Evidence

### Step 1: Nucleoside Formation
| Reaction | Enzyme | Context |
|----------|--------|---------|
| Base + Ribose-1-P → Nucleoside | Nucleoside phosphorylase | Salvage pathway |
| Nucleoside + ATP → NMP + ADP | Nucleoside kinase | Phosphorylation |

### Step 2: Progressive Phosphorylation
| Step | Reaction | Enzyme | ATP Cost |
|------|----------|--------|----------|
| **Nucleoside → NMP** | + Pi | Nucleoside kinase | 1 ATP |
| **NMP → NDP** | + Pi | NMP kinase | 1 ATP |
| **NDP → NTP** | + Pi | NDP kinase | 1 ATP |

### Nucleotide Hierarchy
```
Base (adenine) → Nucleoside (adenosine) → NMP (AMP) → NDP (ADP) → NTP (ATP)
     ↓                    ↓                    ↓           ↓           ↓
  No energy           Low energy          Low energy   Medium     High energy
```

## Clinical Implication
- **ATP synthesis requires ATP investment** — metabolic bootstrap
- **Nucleoside analog drugs** (e.g., AZT) exploit phosphorylation pathway
- **Mitochondrial dysfunction** impairs NDP→NTP conversion
- **METHAP relevance:** Robust mitochondrial ATP production supports nucleotide synthesis for high-turnover tissues
