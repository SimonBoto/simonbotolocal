## Core Claim
**Weeks 1-2 of metabolomics training reformat the clinician's cognitive operating system — shifting from viewing metabolism as isolated facts to understanding it as information flow, with mitochondria as cellular decision-makers rather than mere energy furnaces.**

## Evidence

### Cognitive Transformation
| Before | After |
|--------|-------|
| Metabolism as memorized pathways | Metabolism as information flow |
| Mitochondria as power plants | Mitochondria as decision centers |
| Isolated markers | Interconnected systems |
| Rote recall | Pattern recognition |

### Core Concepts Established
| Concept | Understanding |
|---------|--------------|
| **Pathway megastructure** | How major pathways interconnect |
| **5 failure patterns** | Common metabolic dysfunction modes |
| **Metabolism as language** | Markers communicate status |
| **Energy-first thinking** | Cellular energy as organizing principle |

### Training Methods
| Method | Purpose |
|--------|---------|
| **Interactive diagrams** | Visual pathway integration |
| **Micro-quizzes** | Immediate feedback |
| **Thought puzzles** | Reasoning development |
| **Oral exams** | Articulation practice |

### Deliverables
- Pathway Atlas (mental and physical)
- 5 failure pattern recognition
- Biochemical inference capability
- Foundation for subsequent phases

## Clinical Implication
- **Framework for interpretation:** All subsequent OAT analysis builds on this foundation
- **Pattern recognition:** Early exposure to failure modes enables faster clinical correlation
- **Cognitive efficiency:** Structured mental models reduce cognitive load
- **METHAP relevance:** Research assistants require similar foundation — understanding metabolic pathways before analyzing data prevents misinterpretation
