## Core Claim
**Even minimal physical activity frequency provides substantial health benefits** — 8,000 steps just 1-2 times per week achieves >50% mortality risk reduction.

## Significance
The study is particularly significant because:
- Even those who **cannot walk daily** achieve impressive health benefits
- 8,000 steps just **once or twice weekly** provides >50% mortality reduction
- Removes barrier of "daily exercise requirement" that discourages many people

## Minimum Effective Dose
| Frequency | Benefit |
|-----------|---------|
| **1-2 days/week** | Substantial benefit (foundation) |
| **3-7 days/week** | Additional incremental benefit |

## Comparison with Other Interventions
| Intervention | Mortality Reduction |
|--------------|---------------------|
| Walking 8,000 steps | >50% |
| Most drugs | Much lower |
| Walking is **free** and **accessible** to all |
| **No side effects** unlike medications |

## Practical Implementation
- Start with **1-2 days/week** if daily walking not achievable
- 8,000 steps = approximately **6-7 km** or **45-60 minutes**
- Can be **accumulated throughout the day**

## Clinical Implication
- **"Some walking"** vastly better than **"no walking"**
- Remove perfectionism barrier — 1-2x/week still transformative
- Focus on **consistency over intensity**
