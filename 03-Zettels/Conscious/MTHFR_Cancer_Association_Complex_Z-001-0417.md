## Core Principle

> MTHFR polymorphisms have complex, context-dependent associations with cancer risk that vary by cancer type, folate status, alcohol intake, and interaction with other genetic factors.

## Protective Associations (High Folate)

**Colorectal Cancer:**
- Variant genotypes protective with adequate folate
- : Maintains DNA methylation
- Stability of genome

**Mechanism:**
- Slower folate  = more 5,10-methylene-THF
- Enhanced thymidylate synthesis
- Reduced uracil misincorporation
- DNA stability

## Core Principle

> MTHFR polymorphisms have complex, context-dependent associations with cancer risk that vary by cancer type, folate status, alcohol intake, and interaction with other genetic factors.

## Risk Associations (Low Folate)

**Gastric Cancer:**
- Variant genotypes at risk with low folate
- Cervical dysplasia
- Acute lymphoblastic leukemia

**Mechanism:**
- Reduced 5-MTHF
- Hypomethylation
- Genomic instability
- Oncogene activation

## Core Principle

> MTHFR polymorphisms have complex, context-dependent associations with cancer risk that vary by cancer type, folate status, alcohol intake, and interaction with other genetic factors.

## Context-Dependent

**Folate Status Matters:**
- High folate: Variants may be protective
- Low folate: Variants increase risk
- Critical window: Pre-cancerous vs. established tumor

**Alcohol Interaction:**
- Alcohol interferes with folate metabolism
- Amplifies variant effects
- Multiplicative risk

**Not Associated:**
- Multiple myeloma (per CGPT-0264)
- No direct causation established
- Part of multifactorial risk

## Core Principle

> MTHFR polymorphisms have complex, context-dependent associations with cancer risk that vary by cancer type, folate status, alcohol intake, and interaction with other genetic factors.

## Clinical Implication

**Nutrition Over Genetics:**
- Folate status dominates effect
- Adequate folate mitigates variant risk
- Dietary intervention key
