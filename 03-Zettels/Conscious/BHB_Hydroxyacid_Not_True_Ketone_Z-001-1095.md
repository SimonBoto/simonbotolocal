# BHB — Hydroxyacid, Not True Ketone

## Core Claim
**Despite being called a "ketone body," beta-hydroxybutyrate (BHB) is chemically a hydroxyacid (reduced form of acetoacetate), not a true ketone — this distinction is critical because BHB's hydroxyl group enables unique signaling functions beyond energy metabolism** — BHB is the most abundant "ketone body" during ketosis.

## Evidence

### Chemical Structure Comparison
| Feature | Acetoacetate (True Ketone) | BHB (Hydroxyacid) |
|---------|---------------------------|-------------------|
| **Functional group** | Ketone (C=O at C3) | Hydroxyl (-OH at C3) |
| **Chemical class** | Ketoacid | Hydroxyacid |
| **Redox state** | Oxidized | Reduced |
| **Interconversion** | NADH + H⁺ → BHB | NAD⁺ → Acetoacetate |

### Why the Misnomer?
- **Historical grouping** by metabolic origin (liver) and function (alternative fuel)
- **Functional similarity** — all three "ketone bodies" serve similar roles
- **Terminology stuck** despite chemical inaccuracy

### Unique BHB Properties
| Property | Significance |
|----------|--------------|
| **Stability** | More stable than acetoacetate |
| **Transport** | Major circulating form during ketosis |
| **Signaling** | HDAC inhibitor, HCAR2 agonist |
| **Energy yield** | Same as acetoacetate when oxidized |

## Clinical Implication
- **BHB is the preferred form** for measurement (stable, abundant)
- **Signaling functions** — BHB inhibits histone deacetylases, modulates inflammation
- **Therapeutic ketosis** — BHB elevation correlates with seizure control (epilepsy), neuroprotection
- **METHAP relevance:** Mild ketosis may provide signaling benefits; BHB supplements available but natural ketogenesis preferred
