## Core Principle
Humanized monoclonal antibody against **sclerostin** that uniquely stimulates bone formation while simultaneously inhibiting bone resorption.

## Sclerostin Biology

### Source and Function
- **Produced by:** Osteocytes (embedded bone cells)
- **Target:** Osteoblast precursors and osteocytes
- **:** Inhibits Wnt/β-catenin signaling pathway
- **Effect:** Suppresses osteoblast proliferation and differentiation

### Physiological Role
- **Negative regulator:** Limits excessive bone formation
- **Mechanosensor:** Decreased with mechanical loading, increased with disuse
- **Coupling mediator:** Links osteocyte activity to surface remodeling

## Dual Mechanism of Action

### 1. Increased Formation (Anabolic)
> "Blocking sclerostin → Wnt pathway activation → osteoblastogenesis"

**Pathway:**
- Sclerostin normally binds LRP5/6 (Wnt co-receptors) → blocks Wnt signaling
- Romosozumab binds sclerostin → prevents LRP5/6 inhibition
- Wnt signaling proceeds → β-catenin accumulation → osteoblast gene transcription
- **Result:** Increased osteoblast number and activity

### 2. Decreased Resorption (Antiresorptive)
> "Sclerostin inhibition reduces RANKL expression by osteocytes"

**Pathway:**
- Osteocytes produce RANKL (promotes osteoclast formation)
- Sclerostin signaling increases RANKL expression
- Anti-sclerostin antibody → reduced RANKL → decreased osteoclastogenesis
- **Result:** Suppressed bone resorption

**Temporal Pattern:**
- Early (0–6 months): Formation stimulation dominant
- Late (6–12 months): Resorption suppression more apparent

##  Pharmacology

### Administration
- **Dose:** 210 mg monthly
- **Route:** Two subcutaneous injections (deliver full dose)
- **Duration:** 12 months typically
- **Convenience:** Monthly vs. daily (PTH-based therapies)

### Bone Mineral Density Effects
| Site | Increase at 12 months |
|------|----------------------|
| **Lumbar spine** | 12–15% |
| **Femoral neck** | 5–7% |
| **Total hip** | 6–7% |

**Speed:** Rapid onset — significant BMD gain by 6 months

### Fracture Risk Reduction
- **Vertebral fractures:** 73% reduction (vs. placebo)
- **Non-vertebral fractures:** 25% reduction (nonsignificant in some trials)
- **Clinical fractures:** Significant reduction in high-risk populations

## Cardiovascular Safety Signal

### The ARCH Trial Finding
- **Observation:** Increased cardiovascular events (MI, stroke)
- **Incidence:** ~2.5% vs. 1.9% in alendronate group
- **Mechanism:** Unknown — possibly Wnt pathway effects on vascular tissue

### Regulatory Response
- **FDA:** Approved with cardiovascular warning
- **Contraindications:** History of MI or stroke within preceding year
- **Boxed warning:** CV risk highlighted

### Clinical Approach
- Screen CV history before initiating
- Consider alternatives (teriparatide, abaloparatide) in high CV risk patients
- Monitor for CV symptoms during treatment

## Duration and Transition

### 12-Month Design
- **Rationale:** Rapid BMD gain, then preserve with antiresorptive
- **Evidence:** ARCH trial protocol — 12 months romosozumab → alendronate
- **Results:** Superior to alendronate alone; gains maintained

### Sequencing Strategy
1. **Months 0–12:** Romosozumab monthly (dual action, rapid gain)
2. **Months 12+:** Antiresorptive (denosumab or bisphosphonate)
   - Denosumab: Potent preservation, but rebound risk if stopped
   - Bisphosphonate: Slower loss after discontinuation

## Indications

### FDA-Approved
- Postmenopausal osteoporosis with high fracture risk
- History of osteoporotic fracture or multiple risk factors

### Ideal Candidates
- Severe osteoporosis requiring rapid BMD increase
- Multiple vertebral fractures
- T-score < -3.0 with fracture history
- Low CV risk profile

### Avoid In
- Recent MI or stroke (< 12 months)
- High CV risk uncontrolled
- Patient preference for established therapies

## Adverse Effects

### Common
- **Injection site reactions:** 6–8%
- **Arthralgia:** 8–9%
- **Headache:** 6%
- **Muscle spasms:** 5%

### Serious
- **Cardiovascular events:** Increased risk (see above)
- **Hypocalcemia:** Rare, especially with severe  deficiency

## Monitoring
| Parameter | Timing |
|-----------|--------|
| **CV history/assessment** | Baseline (critical) |
| **Calcium, vitamin D** | Baseline, correct deficiency |
| **DEXA** | Baseline, 12 months (at transition) |
| **Adverse events** | Each injection visit |

## Comparison to PTH-Based Anabolics

| Feature | Romosozumab | Teriparatide/Abaloparatide |
|---------|-------------|---------------------------|
| **Mechanism** | Anti-sclerostin | PTH/PTHrP |
| **Frequency** | Monthly | Daily |
| **Dual action** | Yes (formation + resorption) | Primarily formation |
| **Speed** | Fastest | Fast |
| **CV risk** | Signal | None identified |
| **Duration** | 12 months | 24 months |
| **Injection burden** | Lower | Higher |
