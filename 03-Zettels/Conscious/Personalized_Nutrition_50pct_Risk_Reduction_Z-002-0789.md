## Core Claim
**Personalized vitamin D supplementation with dosing adjusted every 3 months to maintain blood levels of 40-80 ng/mL reduces heart attack risk by 52%** compared to standard care in adults with established cardiovascular disease.

## The Evidence

### TARGET-D Study Design
| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Population** | Adults with established CVD |
| **Intervention** | Personalized vitamin D dosing |
| **Target range** | 40-80 ng/mL |
| **Adjustment** | Every 3 months based on blood levels |
| **Outcome** | 52% lower MI risk over ~4 years |

### Key Findings
- **85% started below 40 ng/mL** — despite "adequate" levels by conventional standards
- **Personalized approach superior** — one-size-fits-all dosing fails many patients
- **Significant risk reduction** — 52% lower heart attack risk with individualized protocol

## Clinical Implications
- Standard vitamin D recommendations may be insufficient for high-risk populations
- Blood testing and dose adjustment are essential for optimal outcomes
- Personalized nutrition approaches can achieve dramatic risk reductions
