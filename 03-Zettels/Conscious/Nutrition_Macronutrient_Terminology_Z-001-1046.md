# Nutrition Macronutrient Terminology

Dietary biochemistry terms for metabolomics practice: Macronutrients, Micronutrients, Essential Fatty Acids, Omega-6/3 Ratio, Metabolic Flexibility. These 12 terms define nutritional metabolism.

## Evidence
> "Nutrition is not just fuel—it's information that programs metabolism. Every meal is a metabolic signal." (Nutritional biochemistry)

## Core Definitions

### Macronutrients
**Definition:** Nutrients required in large amounts: carbohydrates, proteins, fats.

| Macronutrient | Energy | Primary Functions |
|---------------|--------|-------------------|
| **Carbohydrates** | 4 kcal/g | Quick energy, fiber, glycoproteins |
| **Proteins** | 4 kcal/g | Structure, enzymes, signaling |
| **Fats** | 9 kcal/g | Energy storage, membranes, hormones |

**Metabolic fate:**
- Excess carbs → glycogen → fat
- Excess protein → amino acid pool → glucose or fat
- Excess fat → adipose storage

**Clinical relevance:** Macronutrient balance affects metabolic health

### Micronutrients
**Definition:** Nutrients required in small amounts: vitamins, minerals.

**Categories:**
| Category | Examples | Function |
|----------|----------|----------|
| **Water-soluble vitamins** | B-complex, C | Cofactors, antioxidants |
| **Fat-soluble vitamins** | A, D, E, K | Signaling, structure |
| **Major minerals** | Ca, Mg, Na, K | Structure, electrolytes |
| **Trace minerals** | Zn, Se, Fe, Cu | Enzyme cofactors |

**Clinical relevance:** Deficiencies impair metabolism; OAT can detect some

### Essential Fatty Acids
**Definition:** Fatty acids that cannot be synthesized and must be obtained from diet.

**Two families:**
| Family | Parent | Derivatives |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| **Omega-6** | Linoleic acid (LA) | GLA, DGLA, arachidonic acid (AA) |
| **Omega-3** | Alpha-linolenic acid (ALA) | EPA, DHA |

**Functions:**
- Cell membrane structure
- Eicosanoid precursors (signaling)
- Brain development (DHA)
- Anti-inflammatory (EPA/DHA)

**Clinical relevance:** Balance is critical; modern diets are omega-6 heavy

### Omega-6:3 Ratio
**Definition:** Ratio of omega-6 to omega-3 fatty acids in diet/tissues.

**Evolutionary context:**
- Hunter-gatherers: ~1-2:1
- Modern Western diet: 15-20:1
- Target: < 4:1

**Health implications:**
| Ratio | Status |
|-------|--------|
| **1-2:1** | Optimal |
| **2-4:1** | Good |
| **4-10:1** | Elevated |
| **> 10:1** | Pro-inflammatory |

**Clinical relevance:** High ratio associated with inflammation, CVD

### Metabolic Flexibility
**Definition:** Ability to switch between glucose and fat oxidation based on availability.

**Indicators:**
| State | Primary Fuel |
|-------|--------------|
| **Fed** | Glucose |
| **Fasting (12-24h)** | Fatty acids |
| **Fasting (> 24h)** | Ketones |
| **Exercise** | Mixed |

**Metabolically inflexible:**
- Cannot burn fat efficiently
- Hypoglycemia with fasting
- Poor endurance
- Weight loss resistance

**Clinical relevance:** Marker of metabolic health; improves with training

## Additional Key Terms

### Glycemic Index
**Definition:** How quickly a food raises blood glucose relative to glucose.

### Glycemic Load
**Definition:** Glycemic index × carbohydrate amount; more clinically relevant.

### Nutrient Density
**Definition:** Nutrient content per calorie; measure of food quality.

### Bioavailability
**Definition:** Proportion of nutrient absorbed and utilized.

### Antinutrients
**Definition:** Compounds that reduce nutrient absorption (phytates, lectins, oxalates).

### Phytonutrients
**Definition:** Plant compounds with health benefits beyond basic nutrition.

### Fermentation
**Definition:** Microbial breakdown of food; produces beneficial metabolites.

### Resistant Starch
**Definition:** Starch resisting digestion; feeds gut bacteria.

### Ketogenic Diet
**Definition:** Very low carbohydrate, high fat diet inducing ketosis.

### Mediterranean Diet
**Definition:** Diet pattern associated with health: olive oil, fish, vegetables, legumes.

### Intermittent Fasting
**Definition:** Cycling between periods of eating and fasting.

## Clinical Integration

### Diet Assessment Framework
| Factor | Assessment |
|--------|------------|
| **Macronutrient balance** | Carbs:Protein:Fat ratio |
| **Micronutrient adequacy** | Diverse, colorful foods |
| **Fatty acid balance** | Omega-6:3 ratio |
| **Metabolic flexibility** | Response to fasting |
| **Gut health** | Fiber, fermented foods |

### Metabolomics-Diet Connection
| Diet Pattern | Metabolic Signature |
|--------------|---------------------|
| **High processed food** | ↑ Oxidative stress, ↓ antioxidants |
| **Mediterranean** | ↑ Omega-3, ↓ inflammation |
| **Ketogenic** | ↑ Ketones, ↓ glucose |
| **High sugar** | ↑ Glycolysis markers |
