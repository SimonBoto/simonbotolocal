## Core Claim
**Twelve nutritional terms — Macronutrients, Micronutrients, Essential Fatty Acids, Omega-6/3 Ratio, and Metabolic Flexibility — define the dietary substrates that fuel metabolism, with metabolic flexibility representing the capacity to switch between fuel sources as the hallmark of metabolic health.**

## Evidence

### Core Definitions (12 Terms)
| Term | Definition | Clinical Relevance |
|------|------------|-------------------|
| **Macronutrients** | Carbohydrates, proteins, fats | Energy and structural substrates |
| **Micronutrients** | Vitamins, minerals, cofactors | Enzymatic function, regulation |
| **Essential Fatty Acids** | Linoleic, alpha-linolenic | Cannot be synthesized, must be consumed |
| **Omega-6/3 Ratio** | Balance of PUFA families | Inflammation regulation |
| **Metabolic Flexibility** | Fuel switching capacity | Glucose ↔ fatty acid oxidation |

### Macronutrient Functions
| Macronutrient | Primary Role | Metabolic Fate |
|---------------|--------------|----------------|
| **Carbohydrates** | Quick energy | Glucose → glycogen or fat |
| **Proteins** | Structure, enzymes | Amino acids → protein synthesis |
| **Fats** | Energy storage, membranes | Fatty acids → energy or storage |

### Essential Nutrients
| Category | Examples | Deficiency Consequences |
|----------|----------|------------------------|
| **Essential fatty acids** | LA, ALA | Skin issues, growth problems |
| **Essential amino acids** | 9 total | Protein deficiency, wasting |
| **Vitamins** | A, B, C, D, E, K | Various deficiency diseases |
| **Minerals** | Fe, Zn, Mg, Se | Enzyme dysfunction, anemia |

### Metabolic Flexibility
| State | Characteristics | Assessment |
|-------|-----------------|------------|
| **Flexible** | Easy fuel switching | Normal glucose, ketone response |
| **Inflexible** | Stuck on glucose | High insulin, impaired ketogenesis |
| **Keto-adapted** | Efficient fat burning | Elevated ketones, stable glucose |

### Omega-6/3 Balance
| Source | Omega-6 | Omega-3 |
|--------|---------|---------|
| **Seed oils** | High (corn, soy) | Low |
| **Fish** | Low | High (EPA/DHA) |
| **Grass-fed meat** | Moderate | Moderate (ALA) |
| **Nuts/seeds** | Variable | Variable |

## Clinical Implication
- **Dietary assessment:** Macronutrient balance affects metabolic outcomes
- **Metabolic disease:** Inflexibility associated with insulin resistance
- **Inflammation:** Omega-6/3 ratio modulates inflammatory state
- **Performance:** Metabolic flexibility enhances endurance
- **METHAP relevance:** Dietary composition is intervention target in metabolic syndrome research; metabolic flexibility is key outcome measure
