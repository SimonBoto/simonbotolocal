## Core Claim
**Glutamine serves as the primary nitrogen shuttle between tissues — it transports ammonia in non-toxic form from peripheral tissues to the liver and kidney, where glutaminase releases glutamate and ammonia for urea synthesis or urinary excretion** — glutamine is also the preferred fuel for enterocytes and immune cells, and critical for acid-base balance in the kidney.

## Evidence

### The Cycle
| Step | Reaction | Location | Function |
|------|----------|----------|----------|
| **Glutamine synthesis** | Glutamate + NH₄⁺ + ATP → Glutamine | Muscle, liver | Ammonia detoxification |
| **Transport** | Glutamine in blood | Circulation | Nitrogen shuttle |
| **Glutamine breakdown** | Glutamine + H₂O → Glutamate + NH₄⁺ | Liver, kidney | Ammonia release |

### Enzymes
| Enzyme | Reaction | Regulation |
|--------|----------|------------|
| **Glutamine synthetase** | Glutamate → Glutamine | Inhibited by glutamine, alanine, glycine |
| **Glutaminase** | Glutamine → Glutamate | Activated by phosphate, inhibited by glutamate |

### Tissue-Specific Roles
| Tissue | Role |
|--------|------|
| **Muscle** | Glutamine synthesis, nitrogen export |
| **Liver** | Urea synthesis from glutamine nitrogen |
| **Kidney** | Acid excretion (NH₄⁺), gluconeogenesis |
| **Intestine** | Preferred fuel for enterocytes |
| **Immune cells** | Energy and biosynthesis |
| **Brain** | Neurotransmitter precursor |

### Kidney Acid Excretion
```
Glutamine → Glutamate + NH₄⁺ (glutaminase)
NH₄⁺ excreted in urine → H⁺ buffering
```

## Clinical Implication
- **Critical illness:** Glutamine depletion → supplementation may help
- **Kidney disease:** Impaired ammonia excretion → metabolic acidosis
- **Cancer:** Tumors consume glutamine ("glutamine addiction")
- **METHAP relevance:** Glutamine is conditionally essential during stress; supports gut barrier and immune function
