# Male Hormone Profile - B-011-0001

## CRITICAL PRINCIPLE

> **"For men: We want VERY HIGH free testosterone. Fix the metabolic, nutritional, lifestyle foundations — testosterone follows."
>
> **For women: Don't mess with hormones. Fix insulin resistance, nutrition, stress, thyroid — hormones balance naturally. Never give hormones unless absolutely necessary.**

## 1. IDENTIFICATION

| Marker | Full Name | What It Measures |
|--------|-----------|------------------|
| **Total T** | Total Testosterone | All testosterone in blood |
| **Free T** | Free Testosterone | Unbound, biologically active |
| **SHBG** | Sex Hormone Binding Globulin | Binds testosterone |
| **E2** | Estradiol | Estrogen (aromatization) |
| **DHT** | Dihydrotestosterone | Active androgen in tissues |
| **LH** | Luteinizing Hormone | Pituitary signal to testes |
| **FSH** | Follicle Stimulating Hormone | Spermatogenesis |

**Specimen:** Serum (morning, 8-10 AM — peak testosterone)
**Collection:** Fasting preferred

## 2. SIMOS TARGETS FOR MEN

### Free Testosterone - pg/mL (or ng/dL)
| Range | Standard | Simos Target | Interpretation |
|-------|----------|--------------|----------------|
| **<50** | Low | Low | Hypogonadal symptoms |
| **50-100** | Low-normal | Suboptimal | May have symptoms |
| **100-150** | Normal | **MINIMUM** | Adequate |
| **150-250** | High-normal | **OPTIMAL** | **Target range** |
| **>250** | High | **VERY HIGH** | **Ideal if asymptomatic** |

**Simos Rule: Free T should be 150-250+ (VERY HIGH)**

**Why Free T Matters More Than Total T:**
- **SHBG binds 60% of testosterone** (not available)
- **Albumin binds 38%** (weakly, partially available)
- **Only 2% is FREE** (biologically active)
- High SHBG = low free T even with normal total T
- **Free T is what tissues see**

### Total Testosterone - ng/dL
| Range | Interpretation |
|-------|----------------|
| **<300** | Low (hypogonadal) |
| **300-500** | Low-normal |
| **500-800** | Normal |
| **800-1200** | Optimal |
| **>1200** | High (check E2, hematocrit) |

**Note:** Total T less important than free T

### SHBG - nmol/L
| Range | Interpretation |
|-------|----------------|
| **<20** | Low (more free T) |
| **20-40** | Normal |
| **40-60** | High-normal |
| **>60** | High (less free T) |

**Factors Increasing SHBG:**
- Aging
- Liver disease
- Hyperthyroidism
- Estrogen
- Certain medications

**Factors Decreasing SHBG:**
- Insulin resistance
- Obesity
- Hypothyroidism
- Androgens

### Estradiol (E2) - pg/mL
| Range | Interpretation |
|-------|----------------|
| **<10** | Low (bone risk) |
| **10-30** | **OPTIMAL** |
| **30-40** | High-normal |
| **>40** | High (aromatization) |

**Simos Target: E2 10-30 pg/mL**

**High E2 in Men:**
- Aromatization (fat tissue converts T → E2)
- Symptoms: Gynecomastia, water retention, mood changes
- **Fix: Lose body fat, reduce insulin, zinc, DIM**

### DHT - ng/dL
| Range | Interpretation |
|-------|----------------|
| **<10** | Low |
| **10-30** | Normal |
| **30-50** | Optimal |
| **>50** | High (hair loss, prostate concern) |

**DHT is the active androgen in:**
- Prostate
- Skin (sebaceous glands)
- Hair follicles

### LH and FSH - mIU/mL
| Pattern | LH | FSH | Interpretation |
|---------|-----|-----|----------------|
| Primary hypogonadism | High | High | Testicular failure |
| Secondary hypogonadism | Low | Low | Pituitary/hypothalamic |
| Normal | Normal | Normal | Intact axis |

## 3. TESTOSTERONE PHYSIOLOGY

### Production
```
Hypothalamus (GnRH) → Pituitary (LH) → Testes (Leydig cells) → Testosterone
```

### Daily Rhythm
- **Peak:** 8-10 AM
- **Nadir:** 8-10 PM
- **Variation:** 20-30% throughout day
- **Always test morning**

### Age-Related Decline
- Peak: 20s (800-1200 ng/dL total)
- Decline: 1-2% per year after 30
- Free T declines faster (SHBG rises with age)
- **Not "normal" to have low T at any age — fixable**

## 4. CAUSES OF LOW TESTOSTERONE

### Primary (Testicular)
- Aging
- Varicocele
- Testicular trauma/surgery
- Chemotherapy/radiation
- Klinefelter syndrome
- **LH high, FSH high**

### Secondary (Pituitary/Hypothalamic)
- Obesity/insulin resistance
- Sleep deprivation
- Chronic stress (high cortisol)
- Opioids, steroids
- Pituitary tumor
- Head trauma
- **LH low, FSH low**

### Functional (Most Common — Reversible)
| Cause | Mechanism | Fix |
|-------|-----------|-----|
| **Insulin resistance** | ↓ SHBG, aromatization | Low-carb, exercise |
| **Obesity** | ↑ Aromatase (fat) | Weight loss |
| **Sleep deprivation** | ↓ Nocturnal T surge | Sleep 7-9h |
| **Chronic stress** | Cortisol ↓ T | Stress management |
| **Nutritional deficiency** | Zinc, vitamin D, magnesium | Replete |
| **Alcohol** | Liver toxicity, ↓ T | Eliminate |

## 5. NATURAL OPTIMIZATION (NO EXOGENOUS T)

### Priority 1: Fix Metabolic (Biggest Impact)
| Intervention | Effect on T |
|--------------|-------------|
| **Lose body fat** | ↓ Aromatization, ↑ SHBG |
| **Low-carb/keto** | ↓ Insulin, ↑ SHBG |
| **Resistance training** | ↑ T production, ↑ receptor sensitivity |
| **Sleep 7-9h** | ↑ Nocturnal T surge |
| **Stress reduction** | ↓ Cortisol competition |

### Priority 2: Nutritional Support
| Nutrient | Dose | Mechanism |
|----------|------|-----------|
| **Zinc** | 15-30 mg | T synthesis (5α-reductase) |
| **Vitamin D** | 4000-10,000 IU | T production |
| **Magnesium** | 400-600 mg | SHBG reduction, T production |
| **Boron** | 3-10 mg | ↓ SHBG, ↑ free T |
| **DHEA** | 25-50 mg | T precursor (if low) |

### Priority 3: Lifestyle
| Factor | Recommendation |
|--------|----------------|
| **Exercise** | Heavy compound lifts (squat, deadlift) |
| **Avoid** | Endurance cardio (cortisol), alcohol |
| **Sunlight** | 15-20 min daily (vitamin D) |
| **Cold exposure** | May boost T (theoretical) |

### Herbs (Modest Effect)
| Herb | Evidence |
|------|----------|
| **Ashwagandha** | Moderate T increase, stress reduction |
| **Tongkat Ali** | Moderate T increase |
| **Fenugreek** | Modest effect |
| **Tribulus** | Weak evidence |

## 6. WHEN TO CONSIDER TRT (Testosterone Replacement)

### Criteria
- Free T <100 (symptomatic)
- Total T <300 (symptomatic)
- Failed natural optimization 6+ months
- Symptoms: Fatigue, low libido, depression, low muscle mass

### Before TRT, Rule Out:
- Sleep apnea (TRT can worsen)
- Prostate issues (PSA, DRE)
- Hematocrit (TRT increases — stroke risk)
- Fertility desires (TRT suppresses spermatogenesis)

### TRT Monitoring
| Timeline | Check |
|----------|-------|
| 3 months | T, E2, hematocrit, PSA |
| 6 months | Lipids, liver |
| Ongoing | Every 6 months |

## 7. RED FLAGS

🚩 **Critical:**
- Testicular mass
- Galactorrhea (prolactinoma)
- Severe depression with low T
- Osteoporosis in young male

⚠️ **Urgent:**
- Rapid T decline
- New erectile dysfunction
- Infertility workup

## 8. WOMEN — DO NOT MESS WITH HORMONES

### Principle
> **"Fix insulin resistance, nutrition, stress, thyroid — hormones balance naturally. Never give hormones unless absolutely necessary."

### Why:
- Women's hormones fluctuate cyclically
- External hormones disrupt natural rhythm
- Metabolic fixes resolve most "hormonal" symptoms
- HRT has risks (clots, cancer)

### What to Fix Instead:
1. **Insulin resistance** → PCOS, irregular cycles
2. **Thyroid** → Metabolism, cycle regulation
3. **Stress/cortisol** → Cycle disruption
4. **Nutrition** → Amenorrhea, PMS
5. **Sleep** → All hormone regulation
6. **Body composition** → Estrogen balance

### Exception:
- Menopausal symptoms: Consider bioidentical HRT short-term
- Always lowest dose, shortest time
- Transdermal preferred (less clot risk)
