# Bilirubin — Liver Function & Bile Pigment

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**Bilirubin:** From Latin "bilis" (bile) + "ruber" (red)

**Named for:** Red pigment in bile

**Also called:**
- Total bilirubin
- Direct (conjugated) bilirubin
- Indirect (unconjugated) bilirubin

---

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**Heme breakdown product:**
- Hemoglobin → heme → biliverdin → bilirubin
- Transported to liver bound to albumin (unconjugated)
- Liver conjugates with glucuronic acid (conjugated)
- Excreted in bile → gut → feces (stercobilin = brown color)

**Types:**
| Type | Form | Solubility |
|------|------|------------|
| **Unconjugated (indirect)** | Lipid-soluble, albumin-bound | Water-insoluble |
| **Conjugated (direct)** | Water-soluble | Water-soluble |

---

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### Bilirubin Levels

| Level (mg/dL) | Status | Interpretation |
|---------------|--------|----------------|
| **<1.0** | Normal | Adequate clearance |
| **1.0-2.5** | Borderline | Mild elevation |
| **2.5-5.0** | Elevated | Jaundice likely |
| **>5.0** | High | Clinical jaundice |
| **>15.0** | Very high | Risk of kernicterus (newborns) |

### Causes of Elevated Bilirubin

**Unconjugated (indirect) elevation:**
| Cause | Mechanism |
|-------|-----------|
| **Hemolysis** | Increased RBC breakdown |
| **Gilbert's syndrome** | Reduced conjugation enzyme |
| **Crigler-Najjar** | Severe conjugation defect (rare) |
| **Newborn jaundice** | Immature liver enzymes |

**Conjugated (direct) elevation:**
| Cause | Mechanism |
|-------|-----------|
| **Liver disease** | Impaired excretion (hepatitis, cirrhosis) |
| **Bile duct obstruction** | Blocked flow (gallstones, tumor) |
| **Dubin-Johnson** | Impaired hepatic excretion (rare) |
| **Rotor syndrome** | Storage defect (rare) |

**Mixed elevation:**
- Severe liver disease

---

## 4. COFACTORS

### Bilirubin Metabolism

| Factor | Role |
|--------|------|
| **Albumin** | Transport of unconjugated bilirubin |
| **UGT enzyme** | Conjugation in liver |
| **Bile flow** | Excretion |
| **Gut bacteria** | Conversion to urobilinogen |

---

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### For Elevated Bilirubin

**Depends on cause:**

**Hemolysis:**
- Treat underlying cause
- Folate supplementation
- Transfusion if severe

**Gilbert's:**
- Benign, no treatment needed
- Fasting/exercise may elevate

**Liver disease:**
- Treat hepatitis
- Support liver function
- Avoid alcohol, hepatotoxins

**Obstruction:**
- ERCP to remove stones
- Surgery if tumor

**Newborn jaundice:**
- Phototherapy
- Exchange transfusion if severe

---

## 6. CONNECTED BIOMARKERS

| Biomarker | Relationship | Pattern |
|-----------|--------------|---------|
| **ALT/AST** | Liver enzymes | Elevated in hepatocellular disease |
| **ALP/GGT** | Cholestatic enzymes | Elevated in obstruction |
| **LDH** | Hemolysis marker | Elevated in hemolysis |
| **Haptoglobin** | Hemolysis marker | Low in hemolysis |
| **CBC** | RBC count | Low in hemolysis |

### Clinical Patterns

**Hemolytic Jaundice:**
- Bilirubin: Elevated (unconjugated)
- LDH: Elevated
- Haptoglobin: Low
- CBC: Anemia
- **Increased production**

**Hepatocellular Jaundice:**
- Bilirubin: Elevated (mixed)
- ALT/AST: Very high
- ALP: Mildly elevated
- **Liver cell damage**

**Obstructive Jaundice:**
- Bilirubin: Elevated (conjugated)
- ALP/GGT: Very high
- ALT/AST: Mildly elevated
- **Bile flow blocked**

**Gilbert's Syndrome:**
- Bilirubin: Mildly elevated (unconjugated)
- All other LFTs: Normal
- **Benign, common (5-10% population)**

## 7. OPTIMAL RANGE

| Population | Optimal |
|------------|---------|
| **Total bilirubin** | 0.2-1.0 mg/dL |
| **Direct** | 0-0.3 mg/dL |
| **Indirect** | 0.2-0.8 mg/dL |

---

## 8. ACCEPTED RANGE

| Category | Range |
|----------|-------|
| **Total** | 0.2-1.2 mg/dL |
| **Direct** | 0-0.4 mg/dL |

---

## 9. PROBLEMATIC RANGE

| Level | Concern | Action |
|-------|---------|--------|
| **>2.5 mg/dL** | Elevated | Investigate cause |
| **>5.0 mg/dL** | Jaundice | Urgent evaluation |
| **>15 mg/dL** | Severe | Risk of complications |
| **>20 mg/dL (newborn)** | Critical | Exchange transfusion |

---

## 10. CLINICAL WEIGHT

**Significance: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (5/5 — Critical)**

**Primary jaundice marker**

**Differentiates liver vs hemolysis vs obstruction**

**Always with:**
- Direct/indirect fractionation
- Liver enzymes (ALT, AST, ALP, GGT)
- LDH, haptoglobin (if hemolysis suspected)
