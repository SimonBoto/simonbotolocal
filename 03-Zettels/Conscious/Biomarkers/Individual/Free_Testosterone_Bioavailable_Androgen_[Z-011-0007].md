# Free Testosterone — Bioavailable Androgen

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**Free testosterone:** Unbound, biologically active fraction

**Bioavailable testosterone:** Free + albumin-bound (weakly bound)

---

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**Active testosterone:**
- ~2-3% of total testosterone is free
- ~30-40% is albumin-bound (bioavailable)
- ~60-65% is SHBG-bound (inactive)

**Why free T matters:**
| Fraction | Activity | Clinical Relevance |
|----------|----------|-------------------|
| **Free T** | Fully active | Best marker of androgen status |
| **Albumin-bound** | Weakly bound, active | Bioavailable |
| **SHBG-bound** | Tightly bound, inactive | Inactive reservoir |

**Total T can be misleading:**
- High SHBG → high total T, low free T (symptomatic)
- Low SHBG → low total T, normal free T (asymptomatic)

---

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### Free Testosterone Levels

| Level (pg/mL) | Status | Interpretation |
|---------------|--------|----------------|
| **50-200** | Normal | Adequate androgen activity |
| **<50** | Low | Androgen deficiency |
| **<30** | Very low | Significant deficiency |

### Clinical Significance

**Low free T symptoms (men):**
- Low libido
- Erectile dysfunction
- Fatigue
- Depression
- Reduced muscle mass
- Increased body fat
- Osteoporosis

**Causes of low free T:**
| Cause | Mechanism |
|-------|-----------|
| **Low total T** | Primary or secondary hypogonadism |
| **High SHBG** | Aging, liver disease, hyperthyroidism, estrogen |
| **Obesity** | Low SHBG, but also low total T |

---

## 4. COFACTORS

### Free T Determinants

| Factor | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| **Total T** | Primary determinant |
| **SHBG** | Inverse relationship |
| **Albumin** | Modest effect |

---

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### For Low Free Testosterone

**Address underlying cause:**
- Primary hypogonadism: TRT
- Secondary hypogonadism: Treat pituitary/hypothalamic cause
- High SHBG: Address cause (liver, thyroid)

**TRT (Testosterone Replacement Therapy):**
- Monitor free T, not just total T
- Target free T in upper normal range

---

## 6. CONNECTED BIOMARKERS

| Biomarker | Relationship | Pattern |
|-----------|--------------|---------|
| **Total testosterone** | Precursor | Free T derived from total |
| **SHBG** | Binding protein | High SHBG → low free T |
| **Albumin** | Weak binding | Modest effect on bioavailable |
| **LH/FSH** | Regulation | Indicates primary vs secondary |

### Clinical Patterns

**High SHBG, Low Free T:**
- Total T: Normal/high
- SHBG: High
- Free T: Low
- **Symptomatic despite normal total T**

**Obesity, Low Free T:**
- Total T: Low
- SHBG: Low
- Free T: Low
- **Both fractions reduced**

**Aging Male:**
- Total T: Borderline/low
- SHBG: High
- Free T: Low
- **SHBG rises with age**
