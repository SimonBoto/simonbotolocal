# WBC — White Blood Cell Count, Leukocytes

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**White Blood Cells:** Named for appearance (white vs red blood cells)

**Leukocytes:** From Greek "leukos" (white) + "kytos" (cell)

**Also called:**
- WBC
- Leukocytes
- White cells

---

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**Immune system cells:**
| Type | Percentage | Function |
|------|------------|----------|
| **Neutrophils** | 40-70% | Bacterial/phagocytic defense |
| **Lymphocytes** | 20-40% | Adaptive immunity (B, T, NK cells) |
| **Monocytes** | 2-8% | Phagocytosis, antigen presentation |
| **Eosinophils** | 1-4% | Allergy, parasite defense |
| **Basophils** | 0.5-1% | Allergic response |

**Production:**
- Bone marrow (hematopoiesis)
- Regulated by cytokines (G-CSF, GM-CSF)

---

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### WBC Levels

| Level (K/μL) | Status | Interpretation |
|--------------|--------|----------------|
| **4.5-8.0** | Optimal | Normal immune function |
| **4.5-10.0** | Normal | Standard reference |
| **10.0-11.0** | Borderline high | Mild stress/inflammation |
| **>11.0** | Elevated | Leukocytosis |
| **<4.5** | Low | Leukopenia |
| **<3.5** | Significantly low | Immunosuppression risk |

### Causes of Elevated WBC (Leukocytosis)

| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| **Infection** | Bacterial (most common), viral, fungal |
| **Inflammation** | Autoimmune, tissue injury |
| **Stress** | Exercise, emotional, surgery |
| **Medications** | Corticosteroids, lithium |
| **Hematologic** | Leukemia (very high) |

### Causes of Low WBC (Leukopenia)

| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| **Viral infection** | Common cause |
| **Bone marrow suppression** | Chemotherapy, radiation |
| **Autoimmune** | Lupus, RA |
| **Nutritional** | B12, folate deficiency |
| **Medications** | Immunosuppressants |

---

## 4. COFACTORS

### WBC Production Requirements

| Nutrient | Role | Deficiency Effect |
|----------|------|-------------------|
| **B12** | DNA synthesis | Megaloblastic changes, low WBC |
| **Folate** | DNA synthesis | Impaired cell division |
| **Iron** | Hemoglobin (related) | Anemia with leukopenia |
| **Protein** | Cell structure | Leukopenia |
| **Zinc** | Immune function | Impaired WBC function |
| **Copper** | Hematopoiesis | Neutropenia |

---

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### For Elevated WBC

**Address underlying cause:**
- Treat infection
- Manage inflammation
- Reduce stress
- Review medications

### For Low WBC

**Nutritional support:**
- B12: 1000 mcg/day
- Folate: 400-800 mcg/day
- Zinc: 15-30 mg/day
- Copper: 1-2 mg/day (if deficient)

**Medical:**
- Treat underlying condition
- G-CSF (filgrastim) if severe neutropenia

---

## 6. CONNECTED BIOMARKERS

| Biomarker | Relationship | Pattern |
|-----------|--------------|---------|
| **Neutrophils** | Major component | Elevated in bacterial infection |
| **Lymphocytes** | Major component | Elevated in viral infection |
| **CRP** | Inflammation | Often elevated with high WBC |
| **ESR** | Inflammation | Often elevated with high WBC |
| **Ferritin** | Acute phase | Elevated with inflammation |

### Clinical Patterns

**Bacterial Infection:**
- WBC: Elevated
- Neutrophils: Elevated (left shift)
- Lymphocytes: Normal/low
- **Neutrophilic leukocytosis**

**Viral Infection:**
- WBC: Normal or low
- Lymphocytes: Elevated (atypical)
- Neutrophils: Normal or low
- **Lymphocytosis**

**Leukemia:**
- WBC: Very high (or low in some types)
- Immature cells present
- **Blasts on smear**

## 9. PROBLEMATIC RANGE

| Level | Concern | Action |
|-------|---------|--------|
| **>11.0 K/μL** | Elevated | Evaluate for infection/inflammation |
| **>15.0 K/μL** | High | Urgent evaluation |
| **<4.5 K/μL** | Low | Evaluate cause |
| **<3.5 K/μL** | Significantly low | Immunocompromised risk |
| **<2.0 K/μL** | Critical | Neutropenic precautions |
