# Cortisol — Primary Stress Hormone

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**Cortisol:** From "cortex" (adrenal cortex) + "-ol" (steroid suffix)

**Also called:**
- Hydrocortisone
- Glucocorticoid
- "Stress hormone"

**Discovery:** 1936 by Edward Kendall and Tadeusz Reichstein (Nobel Prize 1950)

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**Primary glucocorticoid:**
- Produced in adrenal cortex (zona fasciculata)
- Essential for life
- Regulated by HPA axis

**Functions:**
| System | Function |
|--------|----------|
| **Metabolism** | Gluconeogenesis, lipolysis, protein catabolism |
| **Immune** | Anti-inflammatory, immunosuppression |
| **Cardiovascular** | Vascular tone, catecholamine sensitivity |
| **Brain** | Mood, cognition, memory |
| **Bone** | Remodeling (excess causes osteoporosis) |
| **Stress response** | Primary mediator |

### Diurnal Rhythm

**Normal pattern:**
- **Peak:** 30-45 minutes after waking (8-10 AM)
- **Nadir:** Midnight
- **Pattern:** 50% drop by noon, continues declining

**Measurement timing critical:**
- Morning: Highest
- Evening: Lowest
- 24-hour urine or salivary profiles show rhythm

### Regulation

**HPA Axis:**
```
Hypothalamus (CRH) → Pituitary (ACTH) → Adrenals (Cortisol)
                              ↑___________________________|
                                    (Negative feedback)
```

**Stress activation:**
- Acute stress → cortisol surge
- Chronic stress → dysregulated rhythm

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### Cortisol Levels

| Level (mcg/dL) | Status | Interpretation |
|----------------|--------|----------------|
| **Morning: 10-20** | Optimal | Normal peak |
| **Morning: 5-10** | Low-normal | Possible adrenal fatigue |
| **Morning: <5** | Low | Adrenal insufficiency |
| **Morning: >20** | Elevated | Chronic stress, Cushing's |
| **Evening: <2** | Optimal | Normal nadir |
| **Evening: >5** | Elevated | Disrupted rhythm, chronic stress |

### Clinical Patterns

**Chronic Stress:**
- Morning: Normal or elevated
- Evening: Elevated (should be low)
- Flattened rhythm
- **HPA axis dysregulation**

**Adrenal Fatigue (Controversial):**
- Morning: Low
- Evening: Low
- **Not universally accepted diagnosis**

**Cushing's Syndrome:**
- Cortisol: Elevated throughout day
- Loss of diurnal rhythm
- Symptoms: Weight gain, striae, hypertension, diabetes

**Addison's Disease:**
- Cortisol: Low
- ACTH: High
- Symptoms: Fatigue, weight loss, hypotension, hyperpigmentation

## 4. COFACTORS

### Cortisol Metabolism

| Factor | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| **Stress** | Acute increase |
| **Sleep deprivation** | Elevates and flattens rhythm |
| **Exercise** | Acute increase |
| **Caffeine** | May elevate |
| **Blood sugar** | Low glucose stimulates cortisol |
| **Medications** | Steroids suppress ACTH; some drugs affect metabolism |

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### For Elevated Cortisol (Chronic Stress)

**Lifestyle:**
- Stress reduction (meditation, yoga, breathing)
- Sleep optimization (7-9 hours, regular schedule)
- Exercise (moderate, not excessive)
- Nature exposure
- Social connection

**Nutritional:**
- Phosphatidylserine: 100-300 mg
- Ashwagandha: 300-600 mg
- Magnesium: 400-600 mg
- Omega-3: 2-4g EPA+DHA
- Avoid excess caffeine

**Adaptogens:**
- Rhodiola
- Holy basil
- Ginseng

### For Low Cortisol

**Address cause:**
- Adrenal insufficiency → hydrocortisone replacement
- Pituitary dysfunction → address underlying
- Chronic fatigue → comprehensive approach

## 6. CONNECTED BIOMARKERS

| Biomarker | Relationship | Pattern |
|-----------|--------------|---------|
| **ACTH** | Pituitary signal | High ACTH + low cortisol = primary adrenal insufficiency |
| **DHEA-S** | Adrenal androgen | Often low when cortisol dysregulated |
| **Blood glucose** | Metabolic effect | Cortisol raises glucose |
| **Insulin** | Counter-regulatory | Chronic high cortisol → insulin resistance |

### Clinical Patterns

**Primary Adrenal Insufficiency:**
- Cortisol: Low
- ACTH: High
- DHEA-S: Low
- **Addison's disease**

**Secondary Adrenal Insufficiency:**
- Cortisol: Low
- ACTH: Low
- **Pituitary problem**

**Cushing's:**
- Cortisol: High
- ACTH: Variable (high if pituitary, low if adrenal)

| **Noon** | 5-10 mcg/dL |
| **Evening (6 PM)** | 2-5 mcg/dL |
| **Midnight** | <2 mcg/dL |
