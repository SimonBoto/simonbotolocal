# eGFR — Estimated Glomerular Filtration Rate

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**eGFR:** estimated Glomerular Filtration Rate

**Glomerular:** From Latin "glomerulus" (small ball) — referring to renal glomerulus

**Filtration:** Process of filtering blood

**Clinical significance:** Best overall measure of kidney function

---

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### What eGFR Measures

**Kidney filtration capacity:**
- Volume of blood filtered per minute
- Normal: ~90-120 mL/min/1.73m²
- Adjusted for body surface area

**Calculation:**
Based on serum creatinine using equations:
- **CKD-EPI equation** (most accurate, current standard)
- **MDRD equation** (older, less accurate at normal GFR)
- **Cockcroft-Gault** (older, used for drug dosing)

### CKD-EPI Equation Factors
- Serum creatinine
- Age
- Gender
- Race (African American higher GFR at same creatinine)

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### eGFR Stages (CKD Classification)

| Stage | eGFR (mL/min/1.73m²) | Description |
|-------|----------------------|-------------|
| **G1** | ≥90 | Normal or high (with other kidney damage) |
| **G2** | 60-89 | Mildly decreased |
| **G3a** | 45-59 | Mildly to moderately decreased |
| **G3b** | 30-44 | Moderately to severely decreased |
| **G4** | 15-29 | Severely decreased |
| **G5** | <15 | Kidney failure (dialysis/transplant) |

### Clinical Significance

**eGFR >90:**
- Normal kidney function
- If other markers normal, no concern
- If albuminuria present, still CKD

**eGFR 60-89:**
- Mildly reduced
- Common with aging
- Monitor trends

**eGFR <60:**
- Chronic kidney disease (if persistent >3 months)
- Requires evaluation and management

**eGFR <30:**
- Severe CKD
- Nephrology referral
- Prepare for renal replacement

## 4. COFACTORS

### Factors Affecting eGFR Accuracy

| Factor | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| **Muscle mass** | Low mass → overestimates GFR |
| **Age** | Decreases with age (normal) |
| **Race** | African Americans have higher GFR |
| **Pregnancy** | Increases (physiologic) |
| **Diet** | High meat → underestimates |
| **Medications** | Some affect creatinine secretion |

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### For Reduced eGFR

**Slow progression:**
- Blood pressure control (ACE inhibitors/ARBs preferred)
- Blood sugar control (if diabetic)
- Avoid nephrotoxins
- Smoking cessation
- Weight management
- Protein moderation (0.8g/kg/day in CKD)

**Monitor:**
- eGFR trends
- Urine albumin
- Electrolytes
- Blood pressure

### When to Refer

**Nephrology referral:**
- eGFR <30
- Rapid decline
- Persistent albuminuria
- Hematuria
- Refractory hypertension
- Electrolyte abnormalities

## 6. CONNECTED BIOMARKERS

| Biomarker | Relationship | Pattern |
|-----------|--------------|---------|
| **Creatinine** | Used in calculation | Inverse relationship |
| **Cystatin C** | Alternative calculation | Less muscle-dependent |
| **Urine albumin** | Kidney damage marker | Albuminuria + low eGFR = CKD |
| **BUN** | Another waste product | BUN:Creatinine ratio helpful |
| **Blood pressure** | CKD cause and effect | Hypertension accelerates CKD |

### Clinical Patterns

**CKD with Albuminuria:**
- eGFR: Any level
- Urine albumin: Elevated
- **Highest cardiovascular risk**

**CKD without Albuminuria:**
- eGFR: <60
- Urine albumin: Normal
- May be non-diabetic, age-related

**Isolated Albuminuria:**
- eGFR: Normal
- Urine albumin: Elevated
- Early kidney disease

## 10. CLINICAL WEIGHT

**Significance: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (5/5 — Critical)**

**Best overall kidney function assessment**

**Advantages over creatinine alone:**
- Adjusts for age, gender, race
- Better reflects true function
- Standardized staging

**Limitations:**
- Estimation, not direct measurement
- Less accurate at extremes of muscle mass
- Race adjustment controversial

**Always with:**
- Creatinine
- Urine albumin
- Blood pressure
