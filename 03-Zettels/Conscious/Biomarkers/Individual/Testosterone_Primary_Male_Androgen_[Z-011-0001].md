# Testosterone — Primary Male Androgen

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**Testosterone:** From "testis" (testicle) + "sterone" (steroid hormone)

**Also called:**
- Total testosterone
- Free testosterone (unbound, active)
- Bioavailable testosterone

**Discovery:** 1935 by Ernst Laqueur — isolated from bull testes

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**Primary male sex hormone:**
- Produced in testes (Leydig cells)
- Small amount from adrenal glands
- Dihydrotestosterone (DHT) is active form in many tissues

**Functions in men:**
| System | Function |
|--------|----------|
| **Sexual** | Libido, erectile function, spermatogenesis |
| **Muscle** | Mass, strength, protein synthesis |
| **Bone** | Density, mineralization |
| **Fat** | Distribution, metabolism |
| **Red blood cells** | Stimulation of erythropoiesis |
| **Mood** | Well-being, cognitive function |
| **Cardiovascular** | Vascular function |

**Functions in women:**
- Libido
- Bone density
- Muscle mass
- Mood
- Produced in ovaries and adrenals

### Regulation

**Hypothalamic-pituitary-gonadal axis:**
```
Hypothalamus (GnRH) → Pituitary (LH/FSH) → Testes (Testosterone)
                              ↑___________________________|
                                    (Negative feedback)
```

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### Testosterone Levels (Men)

| Level (ng/dL) | Status | Interpretation |
|---------------|--------|----------------|
| **>700** | Optimal | Ideal young adult levels |
| **300-700** | Normal | Standard range |
| **200-300** | Low-normal | Possible symptoms |
| **<300** | Low | Hypogonadism |
| **<200** | Very low | Severe deficiency |

### Age-Related Decline

**Normal decrease with age:**
- Peak: Late teens/early 20s
- Decline: ~1% per year after 30
- By 70: 30-50% of peak levels

**Not all decline is "normal":**
- Chronic disease accelerates decline
- Obesity lowers testosterone
- Sleep apnea lowers testosterone
- Medications lower testosterone

### Symptoms of Low Testosterone

**Physical:**
- Reduced libido
- Erectile dysfunction
- Fatigue
- Decreased muscle mass/strength
- Increased body fat
- Reduced bone density
- Hot flashes (severe deficiency)

**Psychological:**
- Depression
- Irritability
- Poor concentration
- Reduced motivation

## 4. COFACTORS

### Testosterone Production Requirements

| Factor | Role | Deficiency Effect |
|----------|------|-------------------|
| **Zinc** | Enzyme cofactor | Reduced synthesis |
| **Vitamin D** | Leydig cell function | Low levels associated with low T |
| **Magnesium** | SHBG reduction | More free T available |
| **Cholesterol** | Steroid precursor | Very low fat diets reduce T |
| **Sleep** | Peak production during sleep | Sleep deprivation lowers T |

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### For Low Testosterone

**Address underlying causes:**
- Obesity → weight loss
- Sleep apnea → CPAP
- Medications → review and adjust
- Chronic disease → treat

**Lifestyle:**
- Resistance exercise
- Adequate sleep (7-9 hours)
- Stress reduction
- Zinc: 30-50 mg/day
- Vitamin D: optimize

**Testosterone Replacement Therapy (TRT):**
- Indicated if symptomatic + low levels
- Forms: Injections, gels, patches, pellets
- Monitor: Hematocrit, PSA, lipids
- Contraindications: Prostate cancer, severe sleep apnea, high hematocrit

### For High Testosterone

**Causes:**
- TRT overdose
- Steroid abuse
- Adrenal/ovarian tumors (rare)

**Treatment:**
- Reduce dose or discontinue
- Evaluate for underlying cause

## 6. CONNECTED BIOMARKERS

| Biomarker | Relationship | Pattern |
|-----------|--------------|---------|
| **Free testosterone** | Active fraction | May be low when total normal (high SHBG) |
| **SHBG** | Binding protein | High SHBG → lower free T |
| **LH** | Pituitary signal | High LH + low T = primary hypogonadism |
| **Estradiol** | Aromatization | High E2 + low T = increased conversion |
| **PSA** | Prostate marker | Monitor if on TRT |
| **Hematocrit** | RBC production | Monitor if on TRT (risk of polycythemia) |

### Clinical Patterns

**Primary Hypogonadism (Testicular failure):**
- Testosterone: Low
- LH: Elevated
- **Testes not responding**

**Secondary Hypogonadism (Pituitary/hypothalamic):**
- Testosterone: Low
- LH: Low or inappropriately normal
- **Brain not signaling**

**High SHBG:**
- Total T: Normal
- Free T: Low
- SHBG: Elevated
- **Liver disease, aging, thyroid**
