# LDL Cholesterol — Low-Density Lipoprotein

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**LDL:** Low-Density Lipoprotein

**Density classification:** Separated by ultracentrifugation based on density
- LDL: 1.019-1.063 g/mL
- Lower density than HDL, higher than VLDL

**Also called:**
- "Bad cholesterol" (oversimplified)
- Beta-lipoprotein

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**Primary cholesterol transporter to tissues:**
- Delivers cholesterol to cells for:
  - Membrane synthesis
  - [[Testosterone_Primary_Male_Androgen_[Z-011-0001]|Steroid hormone]] production
  - Bile acid synthesis

**LDL particle structure:**
- Core: Cholesterol esters, triglycerides
- Surface: Phospholipids, free cholesterol
- Protein: [[ApoB_Apolipoprotein_B_Atherogenic_Particles_[Z-006-0005]|ApoB-100]] (one per particle)

### LDL Receptor Pathway

**Normal metabolism:**
1. LDL circulates in blood
2. Binds LDL receptor on cells
3. Internalized via endocytosis
4. Cholesterol released for cellular use
5. LDL receptor recycled

**Regulation:**
- High intracellular cholesterol → downregulates LDL receptors
- High LDL in blood → continues circulating

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### LDL Cholesterol Levels

| Level (mg/dL) | Status | Interpretation |
|---------------|--------|----------------|
| **<70** | Optimal | Very low cardiovascular risk |
| **70-100** | Near optimal | Low risk |
| **100-130** | Borderline | Moderate risk |
| **130-160** | High | Increased risk |
| **160-190** | Very high | Significant risk |
| **>190** | Extremely high | High risk, possible familial |

### The LDL Controversy

**Traditional view:**
- Higher LDL = higher cardiovascular risk
- Linear relationship
- Lower is better

**Modern understanding:**
- LDL particle number (LDL-P) more predictive than LDL-C
- Small dense LDL more atherogenic than large buoyant
- Oxidized LDL is the truly pathogenic form
- Context matters (insulin resistance, inflammation)

### LDL Particle Size

| Pattern | Description | Risk |
|---------|-------------|------|
| **Pattern A** | Large, buoyant LDL | Lower risk |
| **Pattern B** | Small, dense LDL | Higher risk |

**Pattern B associated with:**
- Insulin resistance
- High triglycerides
- Low HDL
- Metabolic syndrome

## 4. COFACTORS

### LDL Metabolism Factors

| Factor | Effect on LDL |
|--------|---------------|
| **Saturated fat intake** | Increases LDL |
| **Dietary cholesterol** | Modest increase (variable) |
| **Fiber** | Decreases LDL |
| **Thyroid function** | Hypothyroidism increases LDL |
| **Insulin resistance** | Increases small dense LDL |
| **Genetics** | Familial hypercholesterolemia |

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### For Elevated LDL

**Tier 1: Lifestyle**
- Reduce saturated fat (<7% calories)
- Increase soluble fiber (10-25g/day)
- Plant sterols/stanols (2g/day)
- Exercise
- Weight management

**Tier 2: Supplements**
| Supplement | Dose | Effect |
|------------|------|--------|
| **Red yeast rice** | 1200-2400mg | Natural statin (monacolin K) |
| **Berberine** | 500mg 2-3x/day | LDL reduction |
| **Plant sterols** | 2g/day | Blocks absorption |
| **Psyllium** | 10g/day | Bile acid binding |
| **Niacin** | 1-3g | LDL reduction (flushing) |

**Tier 3: Medications**
- Statins (first-line)
- Ezetimibe
- PCSK9 inhibitors
- Bile acid sequestrants

### Target Goals

| Risk Category | LDL Target |
|---------------|------------|
| **Very high risk** | <70 mg/dL |
| **High risk** | <100 mg/dL |
| **Moderate risk** | <130 mg/dL |
| **Low risk** | <160 mg/dL |

## 6. CONNECTED BIOMARKERS

| Biomarker | Relationship | Pattern |
|-----------|--------------|---------|
| **HDL** | Inverse relationship | High LDL + low HDL = high risk |
| **Triglycerides** | Metabolic partner | Both elevated = metabolic syndrome |
| **Total cholesterol** | Includes LDL | Less predictive than individual fractions |
| **ApoB** | LDL particle count | More predictive than LDL-C |
| **LDL-P** | Particle number | Better predictor than LDL-C |
| **Insulin** | Metabolic link | High insulin + high LDL = small dense pattern |

### Clinical Patterns

**Atherogenic Dyslipidemia:**
- LDL: Elevated or normal
- HDL: Low (<40 men, <50 women)
- Triglycerides: High (>150)
- **High cardiovascular risk**

**Familial Hypercholesterolemia:**
- LDL: >190 mg/dL
- Family history of early heart disease
- Xanthomas possible
- **Requires aggressive treatment**
