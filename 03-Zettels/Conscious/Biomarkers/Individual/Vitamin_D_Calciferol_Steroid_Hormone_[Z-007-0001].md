# Vitamin D — Calciferol, Steroid Hormone

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**Vitamin D:** Fourth vitamin discovered (A, B, C, then D)

**Calciferol:** From "calci-" (calcium) + "-fer" (bearing/carrying)

**Forms:**
- **D3 (cholecalciferol):** Animal form, skin synthesis
- **D2 (ergocalciferol):** Plant form

**Discovery:** 1922 by Elmer McCollum — cured rickets

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**Actually a steroid hormone, not a vitamin:**
- Synthesized in skin (with UVB exposure)
- Activated in liver and kidneys
- Nuclear receptor (VDR) in most tissues

**Primary functions:**
| System | Function |
|--------|----------|
| **Bone** | Calcium absorption, mineralization |
| **Immune** | Modulation, antimicrobial peptides |
| **Muscle** | Strength, function |
| **Cardiovascular** | Blood pressure regulation |
| **Metabolic** | Insulin sensitivity |
| **Neurological** | Neuroprotection, mood |

### Activation Pathway

```
Skin (UVB) → Cholecalciferol (D3)
    ↓
Liver (25-hydroxylase) → 25(OH)D (storage form)
    ↓
Kidney (1α-hydroxylase) → 1,25(OH)2D (active form)
```

**Measured clinically:** 25(OH)D (25-hydroxyvitamin D)

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### 25(OH)D Levels

| Level (ng/mL) | Status | Interpretation |
|---------------|--------|----------------|
| **<20** | Deficient | Rickets/osteomalacia risk |
| **20-30** | Insufficient | Suboptimal |
| **30-50** | Sufficient | Standard target |
| **50-80** | Optimal | Ideal range |
| **>100** | High | Potential toxicity |
| **>150** | Toxic | Hypercalcemia risk |

### Clinical Significance

**Deficiency (<20 ng/mL) associated with:**
- Rickets (children), osteomalacia (adults)
- Increased fracture risk
- Muscle weakness, pain
- Immune dysfunction
- Depression
- Cardiovascular disease
- Autoimmune disease risk
- Cancer risk (some types)

**Insufficiency (20-30 ng/mL):**
- Suboptimal immune function
- Bone loss acceleration
- Increased disease risk

**Optimal (50-80 ng/mL):**
- Best immune function
- Optimal bone health
- Reduced disease risk

## 4. COFACTORS

### Vitamin D Metabolism Requirements

| Nutrient | Role | Deficiency Effect |
|----------|------|-------------------|
| **Magnesium** | Cofactor for hydroxylases | Impaired activation |
| **Vitamin K2** | Directs calcium to bone | Arterial calcification risk |
| **Vitamin A** | Balances D action | Toxicity risk if excessive |
| **Zinc** | VDR function | Impaired signaling |
| **Boron** | Extends D half-life | Faster degradation |

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### For Deficiency (<20 ng/mL)

**D3 supplementation:**
| Severity | Dose | Duration |
|----------|------|----------|
| **Severe (<10)** | 10,000 IU/day | 8-12 weeks |
| **Moderate (10-20)** | 5,000 IU/day | 8-12 weeks |
| **Maintenance** | 2,000-5,000 IU/day | Ongoing |

**Supportive nutrients:**
- Magnesium: 400-600 mg/day
- Vitamin K2 (MK-7): 100-200 mcg/day
- Zinc: 15-30 mg/day

**Retest:** 8-12 weeks
**Target:** 50-80 ng/mL

### For Insufficiency (20-30 ng/mL)

**D3:** 3,000-5,000 IU/day
**Retest:** 12 weeks

### Sources

**Sunlight:**
- 10-30 minutes midday sun (arms/legs)
- Season, latitude, skin tone affect synthesis
- UVB required (not through glass)

**Food:**
- Fatty fish (salmon, mackerel)
- Egg yolks
- Fortified foods
- Limited amounts

**Supplements:**
- D3 preferred over D2
- Oil-based better absorbed
- Take with fat-containing meal

## 6. CONNECTED BIOMARKERS

| Biomarker | Relationship | Pattern |
|-----------|--------------|---------|
| **PTH** | Inverse relationship | Low D → elevated PTH (secondary hyperparathyroidism) |
| **Calcium** | Absorption | Low D → low calcium |
| **Phosphate** | Metabolism | Low D → low phosphate |
| **ALP** | Bone marker | Elevated in rickets/osteomalacia |

### Clinical Patterns

**Vitamin D Deficiency:**
- 25(OH)D: <20 ng/mL
- PTH: Elevated
- Calcium: Low or normal
- **Bone loss, muscle weakness**

**Secondary Hyperparathyroidism:**
- 25(OH)D: Low
- PTH: Elevated
- Calcium: Normal or low
- **Bone resorption increased**

## 10. CLINICAL WEIGHT

**Significance: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (5/5 — Critical)**

**Hormone with widespread effects**

**High prevalence of deficiency:**
- 40-50% of population insufficient
- Higher in elderly, dark skin, limited sun

**Always with:**
- PTH (if bone concerns)
- Calcium
- Magnesium status
