# Anti-Tg — Thyroglobulin Antibodies

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**Anti-Tg:** Antibodies against Thyroglobulin

**Thyroglobulin:** Glycoprotein synthesized in thyroid follicular cells
- Precursor for T3 and T4 synthesis
- Stored in colloid

---

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**Autoantibodies:**
- Attack thyroglobulin protein
- Marker of autoimmune thyroid disease
- **Not normally present**

**Thyroglobulin function:**
- Tyrosine residues iodinated
- Coupled to form T3/T4
- Essential for thyroid hormone synthesis

### Conditions Associated

**Hashimoto's thyroiditis:**
- 60-80% have elevated Anti-Tg
- Often with Anti-TPO

**Graves' disease:**
- 50-70% have elevated Anti-Tg

**Postpartum thyroiditis:**
- 30-50% have elevated Anti-Tg

**Thyroid cancer (post-treatment):**
- Thyroglobulin used as tumor marker
- Anti-Tg interferes with thyroglobulin assay

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### Anti-Tg Levels

| Level (IU/mL) | Status | Interpretation |
|---------------|--------|----------------|
| **<20** | Negative | No autoimmune thyroid disease |
| **20-40** | Borderline | Possible early autoimmune process |
| **>40** | Positive | Autoimmune thyroid disease likely |

### Clinical Significance

**Positive Anti-Tg indicates:**
- Autoimmune thyroid disease present or developing
- Often co-occurs with Anti-TPO
- If Anti-TPO negative, Anti-Tg confirms autoimmunity

**Negative Anti-Tg:**
- Does not rule out autoimmune disease (check Anti-TPO)
- ~20% of Hashimoto's have only Anti-TPO

**Anti-Tg vs. Anti-TPO:**
| Marker | Hashimoto's | Graves' |
|--------|-------------|---------|
| **Anti-TPO** | 90-95% | 70-80% |
| **Anti-Tg** | 60-80% | 50-70% |
| **Both** | Common | Common |

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### For Elevated Anti-Tg

**No specific treatment to lower antibodies:**
- Same as Anti-TPO
- Focus on thyroid function management
- Monitor TSH, Free T4
- Treat hypothyroidism if develops

**Thyroid cancer monitoring:**
- Anti-Tg interferes with thyroglobulin measurement
- Must use special assays or correct for interference
- Trend more important than absolute value

## 6. CONNECTED BIOMARKERS

| Biomarker | Relationship | Pattern |
|-----------|--------------|---------|
| **Anti-TPO** | Partner antibody | Often both positive |
| **TSH** | Thyroid function | May be normal early |
| **Free T4** | Thyroid output | Low if hypothyroidism |
| **Thyroglobulin** | Tumor marker | Anti-Tg interferes with assay |

### Clinical Patterns

**Hashimoto's Thyroiditis:**
- Anti-Tg: Positive (60-80%)
- Anti-TPO: Positive (90-95%)
- TSH: Elevated (or normal early)
- **Chronic autoimmune destruction**

**Autoimmune Thyroiditis with Isolated Anti-Tg:**
- Anti-Tg: Positive
- Anti-TPO: Negative
- Less common but still autoimmune
