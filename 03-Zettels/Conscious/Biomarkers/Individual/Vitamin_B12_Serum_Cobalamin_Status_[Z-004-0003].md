# Vitamin B12 (Serum) — Cobalamin Status

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**B12:** Vitamin discovered in 1948 (last B vitamin identified)

**Cobalamin:** Contains cobalt atom at center of corrin ring

**Also called:**
- Cyanocobalamin (synthetic form)
- Methylcobalamin (active form)
- Hydroxocobalamin (injectable form)

**Discovery:** 1948 by Karl Folkers and Alexander Todd (Nobel Prize)

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**Essential for:**
| Function | Mechanism |
|----------|-----------|
| **Methylation** | Methyl donor for homocysteine → methionine |
| **DNA synthesis** | Required for cell division |
| **Myelin synthesis** | Neurological function |
| **Red blood cells** | Maturation (with folate) |
| **Energy metabolism** | Mitochondrial function |

### Absorption

**Complex process:**
1. Food B12 bound to R-protein (saliva)
2. Pancreatic enzymes release B12
3. B12 binds intrinsic factor (IF) in ileum
4. IF-B12 complex absorbed in terminal ileum
5. Transported via transcobalamin

**Causes of malabsorption:**
- Pernicious anemia (no IF)
- Gastritis/atrophic gastritis
- PPI use (reduced acid)
- Metformin
- Ileal disease/resection
- Vegan diet (no intake)

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### Serum B12 Levels

| Level (pg/mL) | Status | Interpretation |
|---------------|--------|----------------|
| **>400** | Optimal | Adequate status |
| **200-400** | Borderline | Possible functional deficiency |
| **150-200** | Low | Deficiency likely |
| **<150** | Deficient | Clear deficiency |
| **<100** | Severe | High risk of neurological damage |

### Limitations of Serum B12

**Not perfect marker:**
- Measures total B12 (active + inactive analogs)
- Late indicator (tissue depletion precedes low serum)
- Normal range too broad (200-900 pg/mL)
- Functional deficiency possible with "normal" levels

**Better functional markers:**
- Methylmalonic acid (MMA)
- Homocysteine
- Holotranscobalamin (active B12)

### Clinical Significance

**Deficiency symptoms:**
- Fatigue, weakness
- Megaloblastic anemia
- Neurological symptoms (paresthesias, ataxia, dementia)
- Glossitis
- Depression

**High risk groups:**
- Vegans/vegetarians (no animal products)
- Elderly (reduced absorption)
- Pernicious anemia
- Gastric surgery
- Metformin users
- PPI users

## 4. COFACTORS

### B12 Metabolism

| Nutrient | Role | Deficiency Effect |
|----------|------|-------------------|
| **Folate** | Works with B12 | Masked B12 deficiency |
| **Iron** | Hemoglobin synthesis | Combined anemia |
| **Intrinsic factor** | Absorption required | Pernicious anemia |
| **Calcium** | IF-B12 absorption | Reduced absorption |

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### For Deficiency (<200 pg/mL or symptomatic)

**Oral supplementation:**
- Methylcobalamin: 1000-2000 mcg/day
- Sublingual or oral
- Effective even without IF

**Intramuscular injections:**
- Cyanocobalamin or hydroxocobalamin
- 1000 mcg weekly × 4-8 weeks
- Then monthly maintenance
- For severe deficiency or malabsorption

**Monitoring:**
- Recheck B12, MMA, homocysteine in 8-12 weeks
- Target: >400 pg/mL
- Lifelong supplementation if pernicious anemia

### Prevention

**High-risk groups:**
- Vegans: Supplement 250-500 mcg/day
- Elderly: 500-1000 mcg/day
- Metformin users: 1000 mcg/day

## 6. CONNECTED BIOMARKERS

| Biomarker | Relationship | Pattern |
|-----------|--------------|---------|
| **MMA** | Functional marker | Elevated when B12 deficient (more sensitive) |
| **Homocysteine** | Methylation marker | Elevated in B12 deficiency |
| **MCV** | RBC size | Elevated in B12 deficiency anemia |
| **Folate** | Partner vitamin | Must be adequate for B12 function |

### Clinical Patterns

**B12 Deficiency:**
- Serum B12: <200 pg/mL
- MMA: Elevated
- Homocysteine: Elevated
- MCV: Elevated (if anemia)

**Folate-B12 Interaction:**
- High folate + low B12 = dangerous
- Folate corrects anemia but not neurological damage
- **Always check B12 before folate supplementation**
