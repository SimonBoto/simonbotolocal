# HDL Cholesterol — High-Density Lipoprotein

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**HDL:** High-Density Lipoprotein

**Density:** 1.063-1.210 g/mL (highest density lipoprotein)

**Also called:**
- "Good cholesterol" (oversimplified)
- Alpha-lipoprotein

---

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**Reverse cholesterol transport:**
- Removes cholesterol from peripheral tissues
- Transports to liver for excretion (bile)
- Anti-atherogenic effect

**Additional protective functions:**
- Antioxidant (paraoxonase activity)
- Anti-inflammatory
- Anti-thrombotic
- Endothelial protection

### HDL Particle Structure

- **Small, dense particles**
- **ApoA-I** primary protein (2 per particle)
- Rich in phospholipids
- Cholesterol content variable

### HDL Subclasses

| Class | Size | Function |
|-------|------|----------|
| **HDL2** | Larger | More protective |
| **HDL3** | Smaller | Less protective |
| **Pre-beta-HDL** | Smallest | Cholesterol acceptors |

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### HDL Cholesterol Levels

| Level (mg/dL) | Status | Risk |
|---------------|--------|------|
| **<40 (men), <50 (women)** | Low | Increased cardiovascular risk |
| **40-59** | Moderate | Acceptable |
| **60+** | High | Protective |
| **>80** | Very high | Optimal |

### The HDL Paradox

**Higher is better... to a point:**
- HDL <40: Increased risk
- HDL 40-60: Moderate protection
- HDL 60-80: Good protection
- HDL >80: Excellent protection

**But:**
- Extremely high HDL (>100) may not add additional benefit
- HDL function matters more than absolute level
- "Dysfunctional HDL" can be pro-inflammatory

### HDL Functionality

**Cholesterol efflux capacity:**
- How well HDL removes cholesterol from cells
- Better predictor than HDL-C level
- Affected by inflammation, oxidation

## 4. COFACTORS

### Factors Affecting HDL

| Factor | Effect on HDL |
|--------|---------------|
| **Exercise** | Increases |
| **Alcohol (moderate)** | Increases |
| **Smoking** | Decreases |
| **Trans fats** | Decreases |
| **Insulin resistance** | Decreases |
| **Obesity** | Decreases |
| **Niacin** | Increases significantly |

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### For Low HDL

**Tier 1: Lifestyle**
- Regular exercise (aerobic especially)
- Smoking cessation
- Weight loss (if overweight)
- Moderate alcohol (if appropriate)
- Eliminate trans fats

**Tier 2: Supplements/Medications**
| Intervention | Effect |
|--------------|--------|
| **Niacin** | Most effective HDL-raising |
| **Fibrates** | Moderate increase |
| **Statins** | Modest increase |

**Target:**
- Men: >40 mg/dL
- Women: >50 mg/dL
- Optimal: >60 mg/dL

### Caution: Raising HDL Doesn't Always Reduce Risk

**Clinical trials:**
- CETP inhibitors raise HDL but didn't reduce events
- Niacin raises HDL but outcome benefit debated

**Conclusion:** HDL function > HDL quantity

## 6. CONNECTED BIOMARKERS

| Biomarker | Relationship | Pattern |
|-----------|--------------|---------|
| **LDL** | Inverse relationship | High LDL + low HDL = high risk |
| **Triglycerides** | Inverse relationship | High TG + low HDL = metabolic syndrome |
| **Total cholesterol** | Includes HDL | Less predictive than ratio |
| **TG:HDL ratio** | Important ratio | <2 optimal, >4 high risk |
| **ApoA-I** | HDL protein | Better measure of HDL particles |

### Clinical Patterns

**Protective Pattern:**
- HDL: >60 mg/dL
- TG: <100 mg/dL
- **Low cardiovascular risk**

**Atherogenic Pattern:**
- HDL: <40 mg/dL (men), <50 mg/dL (women)
- TG: >150 mg/dL
- **High cardiovascular risk**
