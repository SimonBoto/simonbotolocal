# Eosinophils — Allergy & Parasite Defense

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**Eosinophil:** From Greek "eos" (dawn/red) + "philein" (to love)

**Named for:** Stains bright red-orange with eosin dye

**Characteristic:** Granules contain major basic protein (MBP)

---

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**1-4% of WBCs:**
- Granulocytic white blood cells
- Bilobed nucleus
- Large cytoplasmic granules

**Primary functions:**
| Function | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Parasite defense** | Attack helminths (worms) |
| **Allergy modulation** | Release mediators |
| **Tissue repair** | Remodeling |
| **Immune regulation** | Modulate inflammation |

### Granule Contents

- **Major basic protein (MBP):** Toxic to parasites
- **Eosinophil cationic protein (ECP):** Antimicrobial
- **Eosinophil peroxidase:** Oxidative burst
- **Cytokines:** IL-3, IL-5, GM-CSF

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### Eosinophil Levels

| Level (%) | Status | Interpretation |
|-----------|--------|----------------|
| **<1** | Low | Normal (can be zero) |
| **1-4** | Normal | Typical range |
| **4-10** | Elevated | Mild eosinophilia |
| **>10** | High | Eosinophilia |
| **>20** | Very high | Severe eosinophilia |

### Causes of Eosinophilia

**Allergic diseases:**
- Asthma
- Allergic rhinitis
- Atopic dermatitis
- Drug reactions

**Parasitic infections:**
- Helminths (worms)
- Protozoa (some)

**Skin diseases:**
- Psoriasis
- Pemphigus
- Dermatitis herpetiformis

**Autoimmune:**
- Eosinophilic granulomatosis with polyangiitis (EGPA/Churg-Strauss)
- Inflammatory bowel disease

**Hematologic:**
- Eosinophilic leukemia (rare)
- Lymphomas
- Myeloproliferative disorders

**Other:**
- Addison's disease
- Radiation
- Toxins

### Clinical Significance

**Elevated eosinophils suggest:**
- Allergic condition
- Parasitic infection
- Drug reaction
- Autoimmune vasculitis

## 4. COFACTORS

### Eosinophil Regulation

| Factor | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| **IL-5** | Primary growth factor |
| **IL-3** | Growth factor |
| **GM-CSF** | Survival factor |
| **Corticosteroids** | Reduce eosinophils |

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### For Elevated Eosinophils

**Identify cause:**
- Allergy testing
- Parasite screening (stool O&P, serology)
- Drug history
- Autoimmune workup if indicated

**Treatment:**
- Address underlying cause
- Corticosteroids (if indicated)
