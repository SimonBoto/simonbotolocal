# ALT — Alanine Aminotransferase

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**ALT:** Alanine Aminotransferase

**Formerly:** SGPT (Serum Glutamic-Pyruvic Transaminase)

**Enzyme class:** Aminotransferase (transaminase)

**Discovered:** 1950s — recognition as liver enzyme

source-litnote: Synthesized from clinical practice
---

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**Intracellular enzyme:**
- Catalyzes: Alanine + α-ketoglutarate ↔ Pyruvate + Glutamate
- Role in amino acid metabolism and gluconeogenesis
- Found primarily in liver cytosol

**Tissue distribution:**
- **Liver:** Highest concentration (cytoplasmic)
- **Kidney:** Moderate
- **Heart:** Low
- **Muscle:** Low
- **Red blood cells:** Present

### Release into Blood

**Hepatocellular injury:**
- Cell membrane damage → ALT leaks into circulation
- Elevates with any liver cell injury
- More specific to liver than AST

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### ALT Levels

| Level (U/L) | Status | Interpretation |
|-------------|--------|----------------|
| **<20 (women), <30 (men)** | Optimal | Minimal liver stress |
| **<40** | Normal | Standard reference |
| **40-80** | Mild elevation | Fatty liver, mild inflammation |
| **80-200** | Moderate elevation | Hepatitis, significant fatty liver |
| **200-1000** | High elevation | Acute hepatitis, drug injury |
| **>1000** | Very high | Severe acute injury, ischemia |

### Causes of Elevation

**Common causes:**
| Cause | Mechanism |
|-------|-----------|
| **Fatty liver (NAFLD)** | Most common cause in Western world |
| **Alcohol** | Direct hepatotoxicity |
| **Medications** | Statins, acetaminophen, antibiotics, antifungals |
| **Viral hepatitis** | Hepatocyte destruction |
| **Autoimmune hepatitis** | Immune-mediated injury |
| **Hemolysis** | RBC ALT release |
| **Muscle injury** | Minor contribution |

**Mild elevation (<3x ULN):**
- Fatty liver
- Alcohol
- Medications
- Hemolysis

**Moderate elevation (3-10x ULN):**
- Active hepatitis
- Drug-induced injury
- Biliary obstruction (early)

**Severe elevation (>10x ULN):**
- Acute viral hepatitis
- Ischemic hepatitis (shock liver)
- Acetaminophen toxicity
- Autoimmune hepatitis flare

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### For Elevated ALT

**Identify and treat cause:**

**Fatty liver (most common):**
- Weight loss (7-10% if overweight)
- Reduce sugar/fructose
- Exercise
- Mediterranean diet
- Coffee (2-3 cups/day protective)

**Alcohol:**
- Abstinence or reduction

**Medications:**
- Review and adjust if possible
- Statins: usually continue unless >3x ULN

**Supplements (evidence variable):**
- Milk thistle (silymarin)
- NAC
- Vitamin E (non-diabetic NASH)

### Monitoring

**Frequency:**
- Mild elevation: Recheck in 3-6 months
- Moderate: 1-3 months
- Severe: Immediate evaluation

**Target:** Normalization or <2x ULN

## 6. CONNECTED BIOMARKERS

| Biomarker | Relationship | Pattern |
|-----------|--------------|---------|
| **AST** | Paired liver enzyme | AST:ALT ratio diagnostic |
| **ALP** | Cholestatic enzyme | Elevated ALP + normal ALT = cholestasis |
| **GGT** | Inducible enzyme | Elevated GGT + elevated ALT = alcohol/medication |
| **Bilirubin** | Excretory function | Elevated bilirubin + elevated ALT = significant dysfunction |
| **Albumin** | Synthetic function | Low albumin + high ALT = chronic liver disease |

### Clinical Patterns

**Hepatocellular Pattern:**
- ALT: Elevated (>AST)
- ALP: Normal or mildly elevated
- **Hepatocyte injury**

**Cholestatic Pattern:**
- ALT: Normal or mildly elevated
- ALP: Elevated
- **Biliary obstruction**

**AST:ALT Ratio:**
- **<1:** Typical of fatty liver, viral hepatitis
- **>2:** Suggests alcoholic liver disease
- **>3:** Highly suggestive of alcohol
