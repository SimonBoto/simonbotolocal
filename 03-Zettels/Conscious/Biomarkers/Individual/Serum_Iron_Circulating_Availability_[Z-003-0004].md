# Serum Iron — Circulating Iron Availability

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**Serum Iron:** Iron concentration in blood serum

**Also called:**
- Plasma iron
- Circulating iron
- Free iron (misleading — it's bound to transferrin)

**Note:** "Free iron" is toxic; all serum iron is protein-bound

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### What Serum Iron Measures

**Iron in circulation bound to [[TIBC_Total_Iron_Binding_Capacity_[Z-003-0002]|transferrin]]:**
- Not "free" iron (would be toxic)
- Represents immediate delivery pool
- ~0.1% of total body iron
- Dynamic — fluctuates throughout day

### Diurnal Variation

| Time | Serum Iron | Reason |
|------|------------|--------|
| **Morning (8 AM)** | Highest | Overnight release |
| **Afternoon** | Lower | Tissue uptake |
| **Evening** | Lowest | Distribution to tissues |

**Variation:** Can be 30-50% different within same day

### Factors Affecting Serum Iron

| Factor | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| **Time of day** | Morning higher |
| **Meals** | Postprandial increase |
| **Menstruation** | Lower in women |
| **Exercise** | Acute increase (cell damage) |
| **Inflammation** | Decreased (hepcidin) |
| **Supplements** | Increased |

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### Limitations of Serum Iron

**NOT a reliable standalone marker:**
- High variability (diurnal, dietary)
- Acute phase reactant (decreases in inflammation)
- Represents only transport pool, not stores

**Best used with:**
- Ferritin (stores)
- TIBC (transport capacity)
- Transferrin saturation (functional)

### Patterns

**Iron Deficiency:**
- Serum iron: Low
- Ferritin: Low
- TIBC: High
- Transferrin sat: Low

**Inflammatory Block:**
- Serum iron: Low
- Ferritin: Normal/high
- TIBC: Low/normal
- CRP: Elevated
- **Iron trapped in macrophages**

**Iron Overload:**
- Serum iron: High
- Ferritin: Very high
- Transferrin sat: High
- TIBC: Normal/low

## 4. COFACTORS

### Measurement Considerations

| Factor | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| **Fasting** | More consistent |
| **Morning** | Peak level |
| **Recent meal** | May elevate |
| **Supplements** | Recent iron increases level |
| **Inflammation** | Decreases |

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### For Low Serum Iron

**Address underlying cause:**
- Iron deficiency → supplementation
- Inflammation → treat underlying
- Malabsorption → investigate GI

### For High Serum Iron

**Investigate:**
- Iron overload
- Recent supplementation
- Hemolysis
- Liver disease
