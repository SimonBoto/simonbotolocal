# GGT — Gamma-Glutamyl Transferase

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**GGT:** Gamma-Glutamyl Transferase

**Formerly:** GGTP (Gamma-Glutamyl Transpeptidase)

**Function:** Transfers gamma-glutamyl groups between molecules

**Discovery:** 1950s — recognition as liver enzyme

---

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**Cellular location:**
- Cell membranes (hepatocytes, biliary epithelium, kidney, pancreas)
- Not normally in blood

**Role in glutathione metabolism:**
- Transfers gamma-glutamyl moiety
- Part of gamma-glutamyl cycle
- Involved in amino acid transport

### Tissue Distribution

| Tissue | Relative Amount |
|--------|-----------------|
| **Kidney** | Highest |
| **Liver** | High |
| **Pancreas** | Moderate |
| **Prostate** | Moderate |
| **Brain** | Low |

**Note:** Kidney has highest GGT, but serum GGT is primarily hepatic (kidney GGT not released into blood)

### Inducible Enzyme

**Key characteristic:** GGT is **inducible**
- Alcohol induces GGT synthesis
- Drugs induce GGT (phenytoin, barbiturates)
- Bile acids induce GGT

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### GGT Levels

| Level (U/L) | Status | Interpretation |
|-------------|--------|----------------|
| **<20 (women), <30 (men)** | Optimal | Minimal exposure |
| **<40** | Normal | Standard reference |
| **40-100** | Mild elevation | Alcohol, medications, fatty liver |
| **100-300** | Moderate elevation | Significant alcohol, cholestasis |
| **>300** | High elevation | Severe alcohol use, biliary obstruction |

### Causes of Elevation

**Alcohol-related (most common):**
- Chronic alcohol consumption
- Even moderate drinking elevates GGT
- Sensitive marker of alcohol use
- **Elevates before other liver enzymes**

**Medications (inducers):**
- Phenytoin
- Barbiturates
- Carbamazepine
- Rifampin
- Statins (mild)

**Liver/biliary causes:**
- Cholestasis (any cause)
- Fatty liver
- Hepatitis
- Biliary obstruction

**Other causes:**
- Obesity (associated with fatty liver)
- Diabetes/insulin resistance
- Smoking
- Pancreatic disease

### GGT as Alcohol Marker

**Sensitivity:**
- Detects chronic alcohol use
- Elevates with regular consumption
- **Not specific** (other causes exist)

**Clinical use:**
- Screening for alcohol use
- Monitoring abstinence
- Life insurance screening

**False positives:**
- Medications (as above)
- Obesity
- Diabetes

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### For Elevated GGT

**Identify cause:**

**Alcohol:**
- Abstinence or reduction
- Recheck in 4-8 weeks
- Should normalize with abstinence

**Medications:**
- Review necessity
- Consider alternatives if possible

**Fatty liver/metabolic:**
- Weight loss
- Exercise
- Diabetes management

### Monitoring

**Recheck:** 4-8 weeks after intervention
**Target:** Normalization or <2x ULN

## 6. CONNECTED BIOMARKERS

| Biomarker | Relationship | Pattern |
|-----------|--------------|---------|
| **ALP** | Confirms liver source | Elevated GGT + elevated ALP = liver |
| **ALT/AST** | Hepatocellular enzymes | GGT elevates earlier than ALT in alcohol |
| **MCV** | Alcohol marker | Elevated MCV + elevated GGT = alcohol |
| **CDT** | Specific alcohol marker | More specific than GGT |

### Clinical Patterns

**Alcoholic Liver Disease:**
- GGT: Elevated (often first)
- AST: Elevated
- ALT: Elevated (less than AST)
- MCV: Elevated
- **AST:ALT ratio >2**

**Non-Alcoholic Fatty Liver:**
- GGT: Mildly elevated
- ALT: Elevated
- Metabolic syndrome features
- **Normal MCV**

**Cholestasis:**
- GGT: Elevated
- ALP: Elevated
- Bilirubin: May be elevated

## 10. CLINICAL WEIGHT

**Significance: ⭐⭐⭐ (3/5 — Moderate)**

**Sensitive but not specific**

**Best for:**
- Alcohol screening
- Confirming liver source of elevated ALP
- Early detection of liver stress

**Limitations:**
- Many non-alcohol causes
- Inducible (medications, obesity)

**Always with:**
- ALP (to confirm liver source)
- ALT/AST
- Clinical history (alcohol, medications)
