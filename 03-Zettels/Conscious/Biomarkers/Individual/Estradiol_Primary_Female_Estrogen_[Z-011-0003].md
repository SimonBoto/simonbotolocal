# Estradiol — Primary Female Estrogen

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**Estradiol:** From Greek "oistros" (frenzy/passion) + "-diol" (two hydroxyl groups)

**Also called:**
- E2 (estradiol)
- 17β-estradiol (active form)

**Discovery:** 1935 by Adolf Butenandt

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**Primary female sex hormone:**
- Produced in ovaries (follicles, corpus luteum)
- Small amount from adrenal glands, adipose tissue
- Most potent natural estrogen

**Functions in women:**
| System | Function |
|--------|----------|
| **Reproductive** | Endometrial proliferation, fertility |
| **Secondary sex characteristics** | Breast development, body composition |
| **Bone** | Density, remodeling |
| **Cardiovascular** | Vascular protection |
| **Brain** | Mood, cognition, neuroprotection |
| **Skin** | Collagen, elasticity |

**Functions in men:**
- Bone health
- Brain function
- Libido
- Produced via testosterone aromatization

### Menstrual Cycle Variation

| Phase | Estradiol Range (pg/mL) |
|-------|------------------------|
| **Early follicular** | 25-75 |
| **Mid-cycle peak** | 200-500 |
| **Luteal** | 50-300 |
| **Postmenopausal** | <20 |

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### Estradiol Levels (Women)

| Status | Premenopausal | Postmenopausal |
|--------|---------------|----------------|
| **Optimal** | Cycle-dependent | 20-40 (HRT) |
| **Follicular** | 30-100 pg/mL | <20 |
| **Ovulatory peak** | 200-500 pg/mL | N/A |
| **Luteal** | 50-300 pg/mL | N/A |
| **Low** | <30 | <10 |

### Men (pg/mL)
- **Normal:** 10-40
- **Optimal:** 20-30

### Clinical Significance

**Low estradiol:**
- Menopause
- Ovarian failure
- Hypothalamic amenorrhea
- Athletic amenorrhea
- Eating disorders

**High estradiol:**
- Estrogen dominance
- PCOS (relative to progesterone)
- Obesity (adipose aromatization)
- Estrogen-secreting tumors (rare)
- Exogenous estrogen

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### For Low Estradiol (Menopause)

**Hormone replacement therapy (HRT):**
- Systemic (pills, patches, gels)
- Local (vaginal for urogenital symptoms)
- Benefits: Symptom relief, bone protection
- Risks: Thromboembolism, breast cancer (with long-term use)

**Bioidentical hormones:**
- 17β-estradiol
- Estriol
- Compounded preparations

**Non-hormonal:**
- SSRIs (hot flashes)
- Gabapentin
- Lifestyle modifications

### For Estrogen Dominance

**Address cause:**
- Weight loss (reduce adipose aromatization)
- Progesterone supplementation
- Reduce xenoestrogen exposure
- Support liver detoxification
