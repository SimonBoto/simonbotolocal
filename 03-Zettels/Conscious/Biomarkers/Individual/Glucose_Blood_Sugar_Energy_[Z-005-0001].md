# Glucose — Blood Sugar Energy Currency

## 1. ETYMOLOGY

**Glucose:** From Greek "gleukos" (sweet wine, must)

**Also called:**
- Dextrose (D-glucose)
- Blood sugar
- Grape sugar

**Discovery:** Isolated from raisins by Andreas Marggraf in 1747

## 2. PHYSIOLOGY

### Biological Function

**Primary energy source:**
- Brain fuel (120g/day required)
- Red blood cells (only fuel)
- Muscle energy (anaerobic and aerobic)

**Regulation:**
- **High glucose** → Insulin release → glucose uptake
- **Low glucose** → Glucagon release → glycogenolysis, gluconeogenesis

### Normal Kinetics

**Fasting glucose:**
- Morning: 70-90 mg/dL (optimal)
- Maintained by hepatic glucose output

**Postprandial:**
- Peak at 30-60 minutes
- Return to baseline by 2 hours

**Diurnal variation:**
- Dawn phenomenon: slight morning elevation
- Lowest: late afternoon/early evening

## 3. METABOLIC INTERPRETATION

### Glucose Levels

| Level (mg/dL) | Status | Interpretation |
|---------------|--------|----------------|
| **<70** | Hypoglycemia | Low energy, symptoms |
| **70-85** | Optimal | Ideal metabolic health |
| **86-99** | Normal | Acceptable |
| **100-125** | Prediabetes | Insulin resistance developing |
| **>126 (fasting)** | Diabetes | Diagnostic threshold |

### Patterns

**Fasting Hyperglycemia:**
- Hepatic insulin resistance
- Dawn phenomenon
- Insufficient overnight insulin

**Postprandial Hyperglycemia:**
- Peripheral insulin resistance
- First-phase insulin response impaired
- Carbohydrate intolerance

**Reactive Hypoglycemia:**
- Excessive insulin response
- 2-4 hours post-meal
- Adrenergic symptoms

## 5. INTERVENTIONS

### For Elevated Glucose

**Tier 1: Lifestyle**
- Carbohydrate reduction/quality
- Time-restricted eating
- Exercise (aerobic + resistance)
- Sleep optimization
- Stress management

**Tier 2: Supplements**
| Nutrient | Dose | Effect |
|----------|------|--------|
| **Chromium** | 200-400 mcg | Glucose tolerance |
| **Magnesium** | 400-600 mg | Insulin sensitivity |
| **Berberine** | 500mg 2-3x/day | Glucose-lowering |
| **Alpha-lipoic acid** | 300-600 mg | Insulin sensitivity |
| **Cinnamon** | 1-3g | Mild glucose reduction |

**Tier 3: Medications**
- Metformin (first-line)
- Other hypoglycemics as needed

### For Hypoglycemia

**Reactive:**
- Reduce simple carbohydrates
- Increase protein/fat at meals
- Smaller, more frequent meals
- Chromium supplementation

## 6. CONNECTED BIOMARKERS

| Biomarker | Relationship | Pattern |
|-----------|--------------|---------|
| **Insulin** | Regulatory hormone | High glucose + high insulin = insulin resistance |
| **HbA1c** | 3-month average | Confirms chronic elevation |
| **HOMA-IR** | Insulin resistance index | Calculated from glucose + insulin |
| **Triglycerides** | Metabolic partner | Both elevated = metabolic syndrome |
| **HDL** | Inverse relationship | Low HDL + high glucose = insulin resistance |

### Clinical Patterns

**Insulin Resistance:**
- Fasting glucose: 100-125 mg/dL
- Fasting insulin: >10 μIU/mL
- HOMA-IR: >2.5
- Triglycerides: Elevated
- HDL: Low

**Diabetes:**
- Fasting glucose: >126 mg/dL (confirmed)
- HbA1c: >6.5%
- Symptoms: Polyuria, polydipsia, weight loss

**Metabolic Syndrome:**
- Glucose: >100 mg/dL
- Plus 2 of: high BP, high TG, low HDL, central obesity
