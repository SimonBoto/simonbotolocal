# Propionate — Sources and Metabolic Fate

> Propionate (propionic acid) is a short-chain fatty acid produced by gut bacteria from dietary fiber and by catabolism of certain amino acids, serving as a substrate for hepatic gluconeogenesis and energy production.

## Sources

### Gut Microbial Production
- **Fermentation:** Colonic bacteria metabolize dietary fiber
- **SCFA ratio:** Acetate:Propionate:Butyrate (~60:20:20)
- **Diet dependence:** Higher fiber = more propionate

### Amino Acid Catabolism
- Isoleucine, methionine, threonine, valine degradation
- Produces propionyl-CoA → converted to propionate

### Odd-Chain 
- 15:0 and 17:0 fatty acid oxidation
- Terminal three carbons become propionyl-CoA

## Absorption and Transport

- Absorbed from colon into bloodstream
- Transported to liver
- Metabolized primarily by hepatocytes

## Metabolic Fates

### Gluconeogenesis
- Propionate → Succinyl-CoA → Oxaloacetate → Glucose
- Net glucose production (energy storage)

###  Entry
- Via succinyl-CoA
- Anaplerotic substrate

### Gut Signaling
- Local effects on colonocytes
- Gut hormone regulation
- Metabolic health marker
