# Microbiome Gut Brain Terminology

Gut ecosystem concepts for metabolomics practice: Microbiome, Dysbiosis, Leaky Gut, SIBO, Neurotransmitters. These 8 terms define the gut-brain-metabolism axis.

## Evidence
> "The gut microbiome is a metabolic organ—its dysfunction appears in urine before symptoms appear in the patient." (Microbiome research)

## Core Definitions

### Microbiome
**Definition:** Community of microorganisms living in and on the body, especially the gut.

**Composition:**
| Domain | Examples | Function |
|--------|----------|----------|
| **Bacteria** | Bacteroides, Firmicutes | Primary metabolic activity |
| **Archaea** | Methanobrevibacter | Methane production |
| **Viruses** | Bacteriophages | Regulate bacterial populations |
| **Fungi** | Candida | Opportunistic, normally low |

**Key functions:**
- Fermentation of fiber → short-chain fatty acids (SCFAs)
- Vitamin synthesis (K, B12, biotin)
- Immune system modulation
- Protection against pathogens
- Neurotransmitter production (95% serotonin)

**Clinical relevance:** Dysbiosis linked to metabolic disease, autoimmunity, mood disorders

### Dysbiosis
**Definition:** Imbalance in microbiome composition or function.

**Types:**
| Type | Description | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **Loss of diversity** | Reduced species richness | Post-antibiotic |
| **Overgrowth** | Excessive beneficial or pathogenic | SIBO |
| **Pathobiont expansion** | Potentially harmful species dominate | C. difficile |
| **Functional dysbiosis** | Metabolic dysfunction despite normal composition | Low SCFA production |

**Causes:**
- Antibiotics
- Poor diet (low fiber, high sugar)
- Stress
- Infections
- Medications (PPIs, NSAIDs)

**Clinical relevance:** OAT markers (indican, HPHPA) reveal dysbiosis

### Leaky Gut (Intestinal Permeability)
**Definition:** Increased intestinal permeability allowing substances to pass into bloodstream.

**Mechanism:**
- Tight junction disruption
- Mucosal barrier damage
- Inflammation

**Consequences:**
| Effect | Mechanism |
|--------|-----------|
| **Endotoxemia** | LPS translocation |
| **Immune activation** | Systemic inflammation |
| **Autoimmunity** | Molecular mimicry |
| **Food sensitivities** | Antigen exposure |

**Clinical relevance:** Central to autoimmune disease, metabolic endotoxemia

### SIBO (Small Intestinal Bacterial Overgrowth)
**Definition:** Excessive bacteria in small intestine where they shouldn't be.

**Risk factors:**
- Low stomach acid
- Motility disorders
- PPI use
- IBS
- Diabetes (autonomic neuropathy)

**Symptoms:**
- Bloating (within 30-60 min of eating)
- Gas
- Diarrhea or constipation
- Nutrient malabsorption

**Testing:**
- Breath testing (glucose, lactulose)
- OAT markers (elevated bacterial metabolites)
- Culture (limited utility)

**Clinical relevance:** Common cause of IBS symptoms, responds to treatment

### Neurotransmitters
**Definition:** Chemical messengers; many produced in gut by microbiome.

**Gut-produced neurotransmitters:**
| Neurotransmitter | Gut Production | Function |
|------------------|----------------|----------|
| **Serotonin** | 95% | Mood, motility |
| **GABA** | Significant | Calming |
| **Dopamine** | Moderate | Motivation, reward |
| **Acetylcholine** | Moderate | Cognition, motility |

**Clinical relevance:** Gut-brain axis explains GI-mood connections

## Additional Key Terms

### Short-Chain Fatty Acids (SCFAs)
**Definition:** Butyrate, propionate, acetate—products of fiber fermentation.

### Prebiotics
**Definition:** Substances that feed beneficial bacteria (fiber, resistant starch).

### Probiotics
**Definition:** Live beneficial microorganisms consumed for health benefits.

### Postbiotics
**Definition:** Metabolic products of probiotics (SCFAs, enzymes, peptides).

### Endotoxin (LPS)
**Definition:** Lipopolysaccharide from gram-negative bacteria; triggers inflammation.

### Gut-Brain Axis
**Definition:** Bidirectional communication between gut and central nervous system.

### Enteric Nervous System
**Definition:** "Second brain"—neural network controlling GI function.

## Clinical Integration

### OAT Markers of Gut Dysfunction
| Marker | Source | Interpretation |
|--------|--------|----------------|
| **Indican** | Tryptophan metabolism | Protein putrefaction |
| **HPHPA** | Clostridia species | Neuropsychiatric link |
| **Phenylacetate** | Bacterial metabolism | Dysbiosis |
| **Hippurate** | Polyphenol metabolism | Beneficial bacteria |

### Gut-Metabolic Connection
| Gut Factor | Metabolic Effect |
|------------|------------------|
| **Dysbiosis** | Insulin resistance |
| **Leaky gut** | Systemic inflammation |
| **Low SCFA** | Poor metabolic health |
| **Endotoxemia** | Obesity, diabetes |
