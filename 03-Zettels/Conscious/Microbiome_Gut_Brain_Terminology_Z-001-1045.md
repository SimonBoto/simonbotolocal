## Core Claim
**Eight gut-related terms — Microbiome, Dysbiosis, Leaky Gut, SIBO, and Neurotransmitters — describe the gut ecosystem and its bidirectional communication with the brain, where intestinal dysbiosis can drive systemic inflammation, metabolic dysfunction, and neuropsychiatric symptoms.**

## Evidence

### Core Definitions (8 Terms)
| Term | Definition | Clinical Relevance |
|------|------------|-------------------|
| **Microbiome** | Gut microbial ecosystem | 10¹⁴ organisms, metabolic activity |
| **Dysbiosis** | Microbial imbalance | Pathobiont overgrowth, inflammation |
| **Leaky Gut** | Increased intestinal permeability | Endotoxin entry, immune activation |
| **SIBO** | Small intestinal bacterial overgrowth | Bloating, malabsorption, systemic effects |
| **Neurotransmitters** | Gut-produced signaling molecules | 90% serotonin made in gut |

### Microbiome Functions
| Function | Mechanism | Clinical Impact |
|----------|-----------|---------------|
| **Fermentation** | Fiber → SCFAs | Butyrate for colonocyte fuel |
| **Vitamin synthesis** | K, B12, biotin | Nutrient contribution |
| **Immune training** | Gut-associated lymphoid tissue | Immune system development |
| **Xenobiotic metabolism** | Drug and toxin processing | Bioavailability, detoxification |
| **Neurotransmitter production** | Tryptophan → serotonin | Mood, sleep, cognition |

### Dysbiosis Patterns
| Pattern | Cause | Consequence |
|---------|-------|-------------|
| **Loss of diversity** | Antibiotics, poor diet | Resilience loss |
| **Pathobiont overgrowth** | Dysbiosis, inflammation | Toxin production |
| **Fungal overgrowth** | Antibiotics, sugar | Candida issues |
| **Parasitic infection** | Travel, contaminated food | Malabsorption |

### Gut-Brain Axis Communication
| Route | Mechanism | Example |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| **Vagus nerve** | Direct neural signaling | Stress → gut symptoms |
| **Immune mediators** | Cytokine signaling | Inflammation → depression |
| **Metabolites** | SCFAs, neurotransmitters | Butyrate → brain function |
| **Endocrine** | Gut hormones | Ghrelin, leptin |

## Clinical Implication
- **Mental health:** Gut health directly impacts mood and cognition
- **Autoimmunity:** Leaky gut contributes to immune dysregulation
- **Metabolic disease:** Dysbiosis associated with obesity and T2DM
- **Chronic symptoms:** SIBO explains IBS, bloating, fatigue
- **METHAP relevance:** Gut microbiome is emerging factor in metabolic syndrome; SCFA production links to insulin sensitivity
