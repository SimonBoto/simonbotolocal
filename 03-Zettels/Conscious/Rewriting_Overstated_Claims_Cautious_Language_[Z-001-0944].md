# Rewriting Overstated Claims Using Cautious Scientific Language

## Purpose
Convert **premature conclusions** to **appropriately calibrated statements** that match evidence strength while maintaining scientific significance.

## The Overstatement Spectrum

| Evidence Level | Inappropriate Language | Appropriate Language |
|----------------|----------------------|---------------------|
| **Exploratory** (n<20, no validation) | "Validated " | "Candidate metabolite requiring validation" |
| **Associational** (observational) | "Proven to cause" | "Associated with" |
| **Underpowered** | "Significant difference" | "Trend toward difference" |
| **Unreplicated** | "Established mechanism" | "Proposed mechanism" |
| **No clinical testing** | "Diagnostic tool" | "Potential diagnostic indicator" |

## Common Overstatements and Fixes

### 1. The Validation Claim
**Original:**
> "Two potential , DHEAS and L-TA, were successfully identified and validated."

**Problem:** "Validated" implies external confirmation; "successfully" presupposes utility

**Revision:**
> "DHEAS and L-TA showed statistically significant differences between groups and warrant evaluation as candidate biomarkers in independent cohorts."

**Key Changes:**
- "Successfully identified and validated" → "showed statistically significant differences"
- "Biomarkers" → "candidate biomarkers"
- Added requirement: "independent cohorts"

---

### 2. The Clinical Utility Claim
**Original:**
> "This method may provide a valuable tool for the early diagnosis and clinical risk assessment."

**Problem:** "Valuable tool" assumes utility; "diagnosis" requires sensitivity/specificity data not provided

**Revision:**
> "This analytical method enables simultaneous quantification of DHEAS and L-TA. Future studies should assess diagnostic performance (sensitivity, specificity, AUC) in prospective cohorts."

**Key Changes:**
- "Valuable tool" → "analytical method"
- "For diagnosis" → "Future studies should assess diagnostic performance"
- Added specific metrics needed

---

### 3. The Mechanism Claim
**Original:**
> "These abnormalities in arginine  indicate disruption of the urea cycle in gastric cancer progression."

**Problem:** "Indicate disruption" implies causation/mechanism from correlation

**Revision:**
> "Altered arginine metabolite levels are consistent with urea cycle dysfunction, though whether this represents cause, consequence, or epiphenomenon of gastric cancer requires mechanistic investigation."

**Key Changes:**
- "Indicate disruption" → "are consistent with"
- Added uncertainty: "cause, consequence, or epiphenomenon"
- Added requirement: "mechanistic investigation"

---

### 4. The Generalization Claim
**Original:**
> "In patients with gastric cancer, DHEAS levels are reduced."

**Problem:** "In patients" implies universal finding; study had 9 GC patients from one site

**Revision:**
> "In this cohort of 9 gastric cancer patients from [location], DHEAS levels were lower than controls. Replication in diverse populations is needed."

**Key Changes:**
- Added specificity: "this cohort of 9"
- Added location context
- Added limitation: "Replication needed"

---

## Language Substitution Guide

### Remove → Replace
| Remove (Strong) | Replace (Calibrated) |
|-----------------|---------------------|
| Proven | Suggested by evidence |
| Validated | Candidate (pending validation) |
| Successfully | [Describe what was actually done] |
| Clearly demonstrates | Is consistent with |
| Established | Proposed |
| Diagnostic tool | Potential diagnostic indicator |
| Therapeutic target | Molecule of interest for investigation |
| Causative factor | Associated factor |
| Significant improvement | Trend toward improvement (p=0.04, n=10) |

### Hedging Language (Use Appropriately)
**Appropriate hedges:**
- "suggests"
- "is consistent with"
- "may indicate"
- "warrants further investigation"
- "generates the hypothesis that"

**Excessive hedging (avoid):**
- "might possibly suggest"
- "could potentially perhaps indicate"

## Context-Dependent Calibration

### Exploratory Studies (n<30, no validation)
**Claim type:** Generate hypotheses
**Language:** "We identified candidate metabolites that may warrant future investigation"
**Avoid:** Validation, clinical utility, mechanism claims

### Discovery Studies (n=30-100, internal validation)
**Claim type:** Prioritize candidates
**Language:** "These findings prioritize X for validation in independent cohorts"
**Avoid:** Clinical recommendations, definitive mechanisms

### Validation Studies (n>100, external replication)
**Claim type:** Confirm utility
**Language:** "Independent replication supports X as a robust marker"
**Permitted:** Performance metrics, clinical potential (not definitive utility)

### Clinical Utility Studies (prospective, diagnostic metrics)
**Claim type:** Enable clinical use
**Language:** "X demonstrates diagnostic performance of [specificity, sensitivity] suitable for [specific clinical context]"
**Permitted:** Clinical recommendations within tested context

## Revision Exercise

### Original Paragraph (CGPT-0328)
> "The present study successfully identified and validated DHEAS and L-TA as biomarkers for gastric cancer progression. The developed method provides a valuable diagnostic tool for early detection. These findings establish the importance of steroid hormone metabolism in gastric carcinogenesis."

### Revised Paragraph
> "This exploratory study identified DHEAS and L-TA as candidate metabolites differing between gastric disease stages in a small cohort (n=57). While analytical methods were developed for simultaneous quantification, diagnostic performance (sensitivity, specificity) remains to be assessed in prospective studies. These findings generate the hypothesis that steroid hormone metabolism may be altered in gastric carcinogenesis, requiring mechanistic investigation."

### Changes Made
1. "Successfully identified and validated" → "exploratory study identified"
2. "Biomarkers" → "candidate metabolites"
3. "Valuable diagnostic tool" → "analytical methods" + pending assessment
4. "Establish the importance" → "generate the hypothesis"
5. Added specific limitations: sample size, lack of prospective testing

## Quality Check

### Final Calibration Questions
1. Could someone replicate this claim with the data provided?
2. Does the claim require assumptions not tested in the study?
3. Would a skeptical expert find the claim justified?
4. Does the claim match the limitation statements?

If any answer is "no," revise to weaker claim.
