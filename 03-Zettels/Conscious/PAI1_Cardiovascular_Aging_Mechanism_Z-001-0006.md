# PAI1_Cardiovascular_Aging_Mechanism

## Source
[[L-001-0654_Vaughan2017_PAI1_Aging_L-001-0654]]

## Core Claim
PAI-1 increases cardiovascular aging by impairing clot and plaque clearance, causing chronic inflammation, arterial stiffness, fibrosis, and reduced nitric oxide bioavailability.

## Key Details
- PAI-1 increases → harder to clear clots/plaques
- Causes chronic inflammation, arterial stiffness, fibrosis, ↓NO
- Spreads aging systemically
- Elevated levels accelerate aging + CVD risk
- "Evolutionary leftover" — helps reach reproductive age, harms later

## Evidence
- [[Vaughan_2017_Arterioscler_Thromb_Vasc_Biol_R-001-0006]] — Key vascular aging evidence
- [[Eren_2014_PNAS_R-001-0007]] — Senescence contribution to vascular aging
- [[Jiang_2025_Ageing_Res_Rev_R-001-0008]] — Systemic aging effects

## Significance
PAI-1 is a central mediator linking cellular senescence to vascular aging, representing a key therapeutic target for age-related cardiovascular disease.

## Clinical Significance
- Elevated PAI-1 levels serve as a biomarker for increased cardiovascular risk in aging populations
- Therapeutic targeting of PAI-1 may slow age-related cardiovascular decline
- PAI-1 inhibition strategies could complement existing CVD prevention approaches
