# Health Philosophy Core Concepts

Foundational concepts for metabolomics practice: Health, Disease, Homeostasis, Adaptation, Self-regulation, Allostatic Load, Oxidative Stress. These 14 terms form the philosophical foundation of clinical metabolomics.

## Evidence
> "Before understanding mechanisms, one must understand what health is—and what it is not." (Philosophy of medicine)

## Core Definitions

### Health
**Definition:** Not merely absence of disease, but optimal function and adaptability.

**Metabolomics perspective:**
- Efficient energy production
- Balanced redox state
- Resilient stress response
- Adaptive capacity maintained

**Clinical application:** Health is measured by function, not just lab ranges.

### Disease
**Definition:** Disruption of homeostasis beyond adaptive capacity.

**Metabolomics perspective:**
- Metabolic pathways impaired
- Compensation mechanisms exhausted
- Function compromised
- Symptoms manifest

**Clinical application:** Disease is a spectrum, not binary.

### Homeostasis
**Definition:** Dynamic maintenance of internal stability despite external changes.

**Key aspects:**
- Not static—constantly adjusting
- Multiple overlapping systems
- Priority hierarchy exists
- Energy-intensive process

**Clinical application:** Support homeostatic mechanisms rather than override them.

### Adaptation
**Definition:** Adjustment to environmental demands to maintain function.

**Types:**
| Type | Example | Timeframe |
|------|---------|-----------|
| **Physiological** | Heart rate increase | Seconds |
| **Metabolic** | Ketone production | Days |
| **Structural** | Muscle hypertrophy | Weeks |
| **Epigenetic** | Gene expression changes | Months |

**Clinical application:** Adaptation capacity indicates health reserve.

### Self-Regulation
**Definition:** Innate capacity to maintain optimal function without conscious control.

**Examples:**
- Body temperature regulation
- Blood glucose control
- pH buffering
- Fluid balance

**Clinical application:** Support self-regulation; avoid disrupting it.

### Allostatic Load
**Definition:** Cumulative wear and tear from chronic stress responses.

**Sources:**
- Chronic psychological stress
- Poor sleep
- Nutrient deficiencies
- Toxin exposure
- Inflammation

**Clinical markers:**
- Elevated cortisol
- Dysregulated HPA axis
- Oxidative stress
- Mitochondrial dysfunction

**Clinical application:** Reduce allostatic load to restore resilience.

### Oxidative Stress
**Definition:** Imbalance between reactive oxygen species (ROS) production and antioxidant defense.

**Sources:**
| Source | Example |
|--------|---------|
| **Mitochondrial** | Leaky electron transport |
| **Immune** | Respiratory burst |
| **Environmental** | Pollution, radiation |
| **Lifestyle** | Smoking, poor diet |

**Consequences:**
- DNA damage
- Protein oxidation
- Lipid peroxidation
- Mitochondrial damage

**Clinical application:** Measure (8-OHdG), address causes, support antioxidants.

## Additional Key Terms

### Resilience
**Definition:** Capacity to recover from stress and maintain function.

### Reserve Capacity
**Definition:** Functional capacity beyond baseline needs.

### Metabolic Reserve
**Definition:** Capacity to produce energy under demand.

### Biological Age vs. Chronological Age
**Definition:** Functional age vs. calendar age; metabolomics can distinguish.

### Systems Biology
**Definition:** Understanding organisms as integrated systems, not isolated parts.

### Functional Medicine
**Definition:** Approach that addresses root causes and restores function.

### Precision Medicine
**Definition:** Tailoring treatment to individual metabolic profile.

## Clinical Integration

### The Health-Disease Spectrum
```
Optimal Health ←→ Suboptimal ←→ Dysfunction ←→ Disease ←→ Crisis
   (resilient)    (compensating)  (decompensating)  (failed)
```

**Metabolomics role:** Detect suboptimal function before disease manifests.

### Assessment Framework
| Level | Question |
|-------|----------|
| **Philosophy** | What is health for this patient? |
| **Mechanism** | Which pathways are impaired? |
| **Intervention** | How do we restore function? |
| **Monitoring** | Are we improving resilience? |
