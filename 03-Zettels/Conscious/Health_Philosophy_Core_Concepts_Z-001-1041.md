## Core Claim
**Clinical metabolomics rests on 14 foundational philosophical concepts — Health, Disease, Homeostasis, Adaptation, Self-regulation, Allostatic Load, and Oxidative Stress — that define health not as absence of disease but as optimal function and adaptive capacity.**

## Evidence

### Core Definitions (14 Terms)
| Term | Definition | Metabolomics Perspective |
|------|------------|-------------------------|
| **Health** | Optimal function and adaptability | Efficient energy, balanced redox, resilient stress response |
| **Disease** | Disruption beyond adaptive capacity | Pathways impaired, compensation exhausted |
| **Homeostasis** | Dynamic equilibrium maintenance | Metabolic set points regulated |
| **Adaptation** | Adjusting to maintain function | Metabolic flexibility, stress response |
| **Self-regulation** | Internal control systems | Feedback loops, enzyme regulation |
| **Allostatic Load** | Cumulative wear from stress | Chronic cortisol, HPA dysfunction |
| **Oxidative Stress** | ROS imbalance | Antioxidant depletion, damage markers |

### Health vs Disease Spectrum
| Health | Subclinical | Disease |
|--------|-------------|---------|
| Full adaptive capacity | Reduced reserve | Compensation failed |
| Optimal markers | Borderline abnormalities | Clear pathology |
| Resilient | Vulnerable | Decompensated |

### Clinical Application
| Concept | Assessment | Intervention |
|---------|------------|--------------|
| **Homeostasis** | Baseline markers | Support regulatory systems |
| **Adaptation** | Stress response testing | Build resilience |
| **Allostatic load** | Cortisol rhythm, HRV | Stress reduction |
| **Oxidative stress** | 8-OHdG, lipid peroxides | Antioxidant support |

## Clinical Implication
- **Prevention focus:** Health defined functionally enables early intervention
- **Patient education:** Concepts explain why "normal" labs may not mean healthy
- **Treatment goals:** Aim for optimal function, not just disease absence
- **Assessment framework:** Philosophical concepts guide test selection
- **METHAP relevance:** Research framework — studying metabolic health requires defining it beyond binary disease classification
