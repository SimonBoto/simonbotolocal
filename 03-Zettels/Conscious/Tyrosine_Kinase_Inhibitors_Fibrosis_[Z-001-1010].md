# Tyrosine Kinase Inhibitors — FGFR, VEGFR, PDGFR Blockade in Fibrosis

## Overview

Tyrosine kinase inhibitors (TKIs) block receptor tyrosine kinases (RTKs) that drive cell proliferation, migration, and survival. In fibrotic diseases, multi-targeted TKIs like nintedanib slow progression by inhibiting pro-fibrotic signaling.

## Receptor Tyrosine Kinase Families

### FGFR (Fibroblast Growth Factor Receptors)

**Structure:**
- Single-pass transmembrane receptors
- Intracellular tyrosine kinase domain
- Extracellular ligand-binding domain

**Isoforms:**
- **FGFR1:** Widespread, embryonic development
- **FGFR2:** Epithelial tissues, bone
- **FGFR3:** Cartilage, CNS
- **FGFR4:** Liver, muscle, pituitary

**Ligands:**
- FGFs (fibroblast growth factors) 1-23
- Heparan sulfate proteoglycans (co-receptors)

**Signaling Pathway:**
```
FGF + FGFR + HSPG → Receptor dimerization
    ↓
Autophosphorylation (tyrosine kinase activation)
    ↓
FRS2 recruitment
    ↓
Ras-Raf-MEK-ERK (MAPK pathway) → Proliferation
PI3K-Akt → Survival, metabolism
PLCγ → Calcium signaling
```

**Role in Fibrosis:**
- Fibroblast proliferation
- Migration to injury sites
- Differentiation to myofibroblasts
- ECM production

### VEGFR (Vascular Endothelial Growth Factor Receptors)

**Structure:**
- Seven Ig-like domains extracellularly
- Split kinase domain intracellularly

**Isoforms:**
- **VEGFR1 (Flt-1):** Decoy receptor, modulates VEGF-A
- **VEGFR2 (KDR/Flk-1):** Primary mediator of angiogenesis
- **VEGFR3 (Flt-4):** Lymphangiogenesis

**Ligands:**
- VEGF-A, VEGF-B (VEGFR1, VEGFR2)
- VEGF-C, VEGF-D (VEGFR2, VEGFR3)
- Placental growth factor (PlGF)

**Signaling Pathway:**
```
VEGF + VEGFR2 → Dimerization
    ↓
Autophosphorylation (Tyr951, Tyr1054, Tyr1059, Tyr1175, Tyr1214)
    ↓
PLCγ → PKC → ERK (proliferation)
PI3K → Akt (survival, permeability)
FAK/paxillin → Migration
```

**Role in Fibrosis:**
- Pathological angiogenesis
- Vascular remodeling
- Vascular leakiness (edema)
- Endothelial-mesenchymal transition

### PDGFR (Platelet-Derived Growth Factor Receptors)

**Structure:**
- Five Ig-like domains extracellularly
- Split kinase domain

**Isoforms:**
- **PDGFRα:** Binds A-chain, B-chain
- **PDGFRβ:** Binds B-chain only
- Homodimers (αα, ββ) and heterodimers (αβ)

**Ligands:**
- PDGF-AA (αα receptor)
- PDGF-BB (all receptors)
- PDGF-AB (αα, αβ)
- PDGF-CC, PDGF-DD (specific isoforms)

**Signaling Pathway:**
```
PDGF + PDGFR → Dimerization
    ↓
Autophosphorylation
    ↓
PI3K-Akt (survival, chemotaxis)
Ras-Raf-MEK-ERK (proliferation)
PLCγ-PKC (migration)
JAK-STAT (transcription)
Src family (cytoskeleton)
```

**Role in Fibrosis:**
- Potent fibroblast chemoattractant
- Fibroblast proliferation
- Myofibroblast differentiation
- Collagen synthesis

## Nintedanib: Multi-Targeted TKI

### Inhibition Profile

| Receptor | IC50 | Clinical Relevance |
|----------|------|-------------------|
| **FGFR1** | ~69 nM | Anti-fibrotic |
| **FGFR2** | ~37 nM | Anti-fibrotic |
| **FGFR3** | ~108 nM | Anti-fibrotic |
| **VEGFR1** | ~34 nM | Anti-angiogenic |
| **VEGFR2** | ~13 nM | Anti-angiogenic |
| **VEGFR3** | ~16 nM | Anti-angiogenic |
| **PDGFRα** | ~59 nM | Anti-fibrotic |
| **PDGFRβ** | ~65 nM | Anti-fibrotic |

**Additional Targets:**
- FLT-3
- Src family kinases
- Lck, Lyn
- RET

### Mechanism in IPF

**Pathophysiology of IPF:**
```
Alveolar epithelial injury
    ↓
Dysregulated repair
    ↓
Myofibroblast accumulation
    ↓
ECM deposition (scar tissue)
    ↓
Progressive fibrosis, loss of function
```

**Nintedanib Intervention:**
```
Inhibit FGFR → ↓ fibroblast proliferation
Inhibit PDGFR → ↓ fibroblast recruitment/activation
Inhibit VEGFR → ↓ pathological angiogenesis
    ↓
Slowed fibrosis progression
```

**Note:** Does not reverse existing fibrosis

## Other TKIs in Fibrosis

### Pirfenidone (Esbriet)

**Mechanism:**
- Not a kinase inhibitor (exact mechanism unclear)
- Downregulates TGF-β
- Inhibits fibroblast proliferation
- Anti-inflammatory

**Comparison:**
| Feature | Nintedanib | Pirfenidone |
|---------|------------|-------------|
| Class | Multi-TKI | Small molecule |
| Targets | FGFR, VEGFR, PDGFR | TGF-β, fibroblasts |
| Efficacy | Similar | Similar |
| Side effects | Diarrhea, LFTs | Photosensitivity, GI |

### Other TKIs (Cancer)

**Sunitinib:**
- VEGFR, PDGFR, c-Kit, FLT-3
- Used in renal cell carcinoma, GIST

**Sorafenib:**
- Raf, VEGFR, PDGFR, c-Kit
- HCC, renal cell carcinoma

**Imatinib:**
- BCR-ABL, c-Kit, PDGFR
- CML, GIST

## Clinical Efficacy

### IPF Trials

**INPULSIS-1 and INPULSIS-2:**
- Nintedanib 150 mg bid vs placebo
- Primary endpoint: Annual rate of FVC decline
- Result: ~50% reduction in FVC decline
- No significant mortality benefit

**INPULSIS-ON (Open-label extension):**
- Long-term safety
- Continued efficacy
- Manageable side effect profile

### SSc-ILD

**SENSCIS Trial:**
- Nintedanib in systemic sclerosis ILD
- Reduced FVC decline vs placebo
- Benefit in skin fibrosis limited

## Adverse Effects

### Mechanism-Based

**GI Toxicity (FGFR/VEGFR):**
- Diarrhea (most common, ~60%)
- Nausea, vomiting
- Mechanism: Kinase inhibition in gut epithelium
- Management: Anti-diarrheals, dose reduction

**Hepatotoxicity (VEGFR):**
- Elevated ALT/AST
- Bilirubin elevation
- Mechanism: VEGF protection of hepatocytes
- Management: Monitoring, dose adjustment

**Bleeding (VEGFR):**
- Epistaxis, bruising
- Mechanism: VEGF role in endothelial integrity
- Caution with anticoagulants

**Cardiovascular:**
- Myocardial infarction (rare)
- Mechanism: Unknown
- Monitor in high-risk patients

## Drug Interactions

### Metabolism-Based

**UGT Inhibitors/Inducers:**
- May affect nintedanib levels
- Monitor closely

**CYP3A4:**
- Minor pathway
- Less significant

**P-gp:**
- Substrate
- P-gp inhibitors increase exposure

## Patient Selection

### Appropriate Candidates

- Confirmed IPF diagnosis
- FVC > 35% predicted (traditional criteria)
- No major bleeding risk
- Normal or mild LFT abnormalities

### Caution/Contraindications

- Child-Pugh C cirrhosis
- High bleeding risk
- Recent surgery
- Concomitant anticoagulation (relative)

## Summary

**Key Points:**
1. Nintedanib is multi-targeted TKI (FGFR, VEGFR, PDGFR)
2. Blocks multiple pro-fibrotic pathways simultaneously
3. Slows IPF progression by ~50%
4. Does not reverse fibrosis
5. Side effects manageable with monitoring
6. Safe in patients with cancer history
7. Alternative to pirfenidone

**Clinical Pearl:** In IPF, nintedanib and pirfenidone are both effective. Choice depends on side effect profile and patient preference. Neither reverses fibrosis, so early initiation is key.
