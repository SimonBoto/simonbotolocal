## Core Claim
**Vitamin K2 (menaquinones) directs calcium into bone and away from soft tissues by activating matrix Gla protein (MGP) and osteocalcin — with inadequate K2 leading to vascular calcification despite adequate calcium and vitamin D intake, representing a critical but often overlooked factor in cardiovascular and bone health.**

## Evidence

### Vitamin K2 Forms
| Form | Source | Half-life | Activity |
|------|--------|-----------|----------|
| **MK-4** | Animal products, synthesis | ~1 hour | Tissue-specific |
| **MK-7** | Fermented foods (natto) | ~3 days | Long-acting, preferred |
| **MK-9** | Cheese | Variable | Intermediate |

### K2-Dependent Proteins
| Protein | Function | K2 Role |
|---------|----------|---------|
| **Matrix Gla Protein (MGP)** | Inhibits vascular calcification | Carboxylation required for activity |
| **Osteocalcin** | Binds calcium in bone | Carboxylation for bone mineralization |
| **Protein S** | Anticoagulant | Carboxylation for function |
| **Gas6** | Cell signaling | Carboxylation |

### The Calcium Paradox
| Scenario | D + Ca Without K2 | D + Ca + K2 |
|----------|-------------------|-------------|
| **Bone** | Some mineralization | Optimal mineralization |
| **Vessels** | Calcification risk | Protected |
| **Kidneys** | Stone risk | Reduced risk |
| **Heart valves** | Calcification | Protected |

### MGP Mechanism
```
Inactive MGP (uncarboxylated) → Vitamin K-dependent carboxylation → Active MGP (carboxylated)
                                                                     ↓
                                                         Binds calcium in vessel wall
                                                                     ↓
                                                         Prevents calcification
```

### Clinical Evidence
| Outcome | K2 Intervention | Result |
|---------|-----------------|--------|
| **Vascular calcification** | MK-7 180 mcg/day | Reduced progression |
| **Bone density** | MK-4 45 mg/day | Improved BMD |
| **Cardiovascular risk** | High K2 intake | Reduced CV events |
| **Warfarin users** | K2 antagonized | Increased calcification |

### Supplementation Guidelines
| Goal | Form | Dose | Notes |
|------|------|------|-------|
| **General health** | MK-7 | 100-200 mcg/day | With fat meal |
| **Osteoporosis** | MK-4 | 45 mg/day | Japanese protocol |
| **Vascular protection** | MK-7 | 180-360 mcg/day | Higher doses studied |

### K2 and D Synergy
| Nutrient | Role | Combined Effect |
|----------|------|-----------------|
| **Vitamin D** | Increases calcium absorption | More calcium available |
| **Vitamin K2** | Directs calcium to bone | Proper calcium utilization |
| **Magnesium** | Cofactor for both | Optimizes function |

## Clinical Implication
- **Calcium supplementation:** Without K2 may increase vascular risk
- **Warfarin therapy:** Antagonizes K2, increases calcification
- **Osteoporosis:** K2 improves bone quality, not just density
- **Cardiovascular:** K2 status predicts vascular calcification
- **METHAP relevance:** Vascular calcification is complication of metabolic syndrome and diabetes; K2 may offer protection
