# Carb Restriction Improves LDL Quality and Size

## Core Claim
Low-carbohydrate diets improve **LDL quality** by shifting from small dense LDL (Pattern B, atherogenic) to large buoyant LDL (Pattern A, less atherogenic), even when total LDL changes modestly.

## LDL Patterns

### Pattern B (Small Dense)
| Feature | Risk |
|---------|------|
| **Small particles** | More atherogenic |
| **Dense** | Penetrate endothelium easier |
| **Oxidizable** | More inflammatory |
| **Associated with** | Insulin resistance, high TG |

### Pattern A (Large Buoyant)
| Feature | Risk |
|---------|------|
| **Large particles** | Less atherogenic |
| **Buoyant** | Less penetration |
| **Less oxidizable** | Less inflammatory |
| **Associated with** | Insulin sensitive, low TG |

## Evidence

> "Carbohydrate restriction shifts LDL from small dense Pattern B to large buoyant Pattern A, improving LDL quality independent of total LDL changes."
> — Lipid metabolism studies
