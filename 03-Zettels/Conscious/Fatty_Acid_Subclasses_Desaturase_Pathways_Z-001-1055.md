# Fatty Acid Subclasses Desaturase Pathways

EPA/DHA (omega-3) and AA (omega-6) as signaling molecules; desaturase enzyme regulation. Understanding fatty acid classes reveals dietary patterns and inflammatory status.

## Evidence
> "Fatty acid profiles are objective biomarkers of dietary intake—patients may not admit their diet, but their fatty acids tell the truth." (Nutritional metabolomics)

## Fatty Acid Categories

### Saturated Fatty Acids (SFA)
| Fatty Acid | Structure | Sources |
|------------|-----------|---------|
| **Palmitic (16:0)** | 16 carbons, 0 double bonds | Palm oil, animal fats |
| **Stearic (18:0)** | 18 carbons, 0 double bonds | Beef, cocoa butter |

**Clinical role:** Structural, stable, excess linked to insulin resistance

### Monounsaturated Fatty Acids (MUFA)
| Fatty Acid | Structure | Sources |
|------------|-----------|---------|
| **Oleic (18:1 n-9)** | 18C, 1 double bond at position 9 | Olive oil, avocados |
| **Palmitoleic (16:1 n-7)** | 16C, 1 double bond at position 7 | Macadamia nuts, fish |

**Clinical role:** Cardioprotective, improves insulin sensitivity

### Polyunsaturated n-3 (Omega-3)
| Fatty Acid | Structure | Sources |
|------------|-----------|---------|
| **ALA (18:3)** | 18C, 3 double bonds | Flax, chia, walnuts |
| **EPA (20:5)** | 20C, 5 double bonds | Fatty fish, fish oil |
| **DHA (22:6)** | 22C, 6 double bonds | Fatty fish, algae |

**Clinical role:** Anti-inflammatory, neuroprotective, cardioprotective

### Polyunsaturated n-6 (Omega-6)
| Fatty Acid | Structure | Sources |
|------------|-----------|---------|
| **LA (18:2)** | 18C, 2 double bonds | Vegetable oils, nuts |
| **GLA (18:3)** | 18C, 3 double bonds | Evening primrose, borage |
| **AA (20:4)** | 20C, 4 double bonds | Meat, eggs, conversion from LA |

**Clinical role:** Pro-inflammatory (in excess), essential in moderation

### Odd-Chain Fatty Acids
| Fatty Acid | Structure | Sources |
|------------|-----------|---------|
| **C15:0 (Pentadecanoic)** | 15 carbons | Dairy fat |

**Clinical role:** Emerging marker of metabolic health

## Desaturase Enzymes

### Delta-6 Desaturase (D6D)
**Function:** Converts LA → GLA, ALA → stearidonic acid

**Regulation:**
- Inhibited by: High LA intake, aging, diabetes
- Supported by: B6, magnesium, zinc

**Clinical significance:** Rate-limiting for both n-3 and n-6 pathways

### Delta-5 Desaturase (D5D)
**Function:** Converts dihomo-γ-linolenic acid → AA

**Regulation:**
- Competition between n-3 and n-6 substrates
- High EPA inhibits AA production

### Elongases
**Function:** Add 2 carbons to fatty acid chain

**Clinical significance:** Required for converting 18C → 20C fatty acids

## The Desaturase Pathway

### Omega-6 Pathway
```
LA (18:2) → D6D → GLA (18:3) → Elongase → DGLA (20:3) → D5D → AA (20:4)
```

### Omega-3 Pathway
```
ALA (18:3) → D6D → Stearidonic acid (18:4) → Elongase → EPA (20:5) → DHA (22:6)
```

### Conversion Efficiency
| Conversion | Efficiency | Factors |
|------------|------------|---------|
| **ALA → EPA** | 5-10% | Poor in most people |
| **EPA → DHA** | 2-5% | Very poor |
| **LA → AA** | 30-40% | More efficient |

**Clinical implication:** Direct EPA/DHA intake superior to ALA for n-3 status

## Clinical Assessment

### Fatty Acid Testing
| Method | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Plasma phospholipids** | Recent intake (2-4 weeks) |
| **Red blood cell membranes** | Long-term status (4-6 months) |
| **Omega-3 index** | RBC EPA+DHA, cardiac risk marker |

### Optimal Ranges
| Parameter | Optimal | Deficient |
|-----------|---------|-----------|
| **Omega-3 index** | > 8% | < 4% |
| **EPA** | > 1.5% | < 0.5% |
| **DHA** | > 4% | < 2% |
| **AA/EPA ratio** | < 3 | > 10 |

## Therapeutic Implications

### For Low Omega-3 Status
- Increase fatty fish intake (2-3×/week)
- Supplement EPA/DHA (2-3 g/day)
- Reduce omega-6 intake (vegetable oils)

### For High Omega-6 Status
- Eliminate vegetable oils (soybean, corn, sunflower)
- Use olive oil, avocado oil
- Increase omega-3 to balance

### For Poor Conversion
- Supplement preformed EPA/DHA (don't rely on ALA)
- Support D6D: B6, magnesium, zinc
- Consider genetic testing (FADS polymorphisms)
