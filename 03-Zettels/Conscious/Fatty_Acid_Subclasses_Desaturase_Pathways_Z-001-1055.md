## Core Claim
**Omega-3 (n-3) and omega-6 (n-6) fatty acids are essential polyunsaturated fatty acids that compete for the same desaturase and elongase enzymes — with dietary imbalance favoring n-6 producing pro-inflammatory eicosanoids, while adequate n-3 supports anti-inflammatory signaling and membrane fluidity.**

## Evidence

### Essential Fatty Acid Families
| Family | Parent | Key Derivatives | Function |
|--------|--------|-----------------|----------|
| **Omega-3 (n-3)** | ALA (18:3) | EPA (20:5), DHA (22:6) | Anti-inflammatory, brain, heart |
| **Omega-6 (n-6)** | LA (18:2) | GLA (18:3), AA (20:4) | Pro-inflammatory (in excess), structural |

### Desaturase Pathway
```
LA (18:2 n-6) ──Δ6D──→ GLA (18:3 n-6) ──E──→ DGLA (20:3 n-6) ──Δ5D──→ AA (20:4 n-6)
                                                          ↓
                                                   Pro-inflammatory
                                                   eicosanoids

ALA (18:3 n-3) ──Δ6D──→ EPA (20:5 n-3) ──E──→ DPA (22:5 n-3) ──Δ6D──→ DHA (22:6 n-3)
                                                          ↓
                                                   Anti-inflammatory
                                                   eicosanoids, resolvins
```

### Enzyme Competition
| Enzyme | Substrates | Rate-Limiting | Inhibited By |
|--------|------------|---------------|--------------|
| **Delta-6-desaturase** | LA, ALA | Yes | High n-6 intake |
| **Delta-5-desaturase** | DGLA, EPA | Yes | Insulin, dietary factors |
| **Elongase** | All | No | — |

### Conversion Efficiency
| Conversion | Efficiency | Factors |
|------------|------------|---------|
| **ALA → EPA** | ~5-10% | Gender, genetics, diet |
| **EPA → DHA** | ~2-5% | Limited |
| **LA → AA** | ~30-40% | More efficient |

### Clinical Implications of Imbalance
| Pattern | Consequence | Intervention |
|---------|-------------|--------------|
| High n-6, low n-3 | Pro-inflammatory state | Reduce seed oils, increase fish |
| Low DHA | Cognitive impairment | DHA supplementation |
| High AA | Thrombotic risk | EPA/DHA to compete |
| Delta-6 deficiency | Atopic dermatitis | GLA supplementation |

## Clinical Implication
- **Dietary assessment:** Fatty acid profiles reveal true intake
- **Inflammation management:** Balance determines eicosanoid production
- **Brain health:** DHA essential for neuronal membranes
- **Cardiovascular:** EPA competes with AA for thromboxane synthesis
- **METHAP relevance:** Fatty acid desaturase activity and n-6/n-3 balance are key variables in metabolic syndrome and inflammation research
