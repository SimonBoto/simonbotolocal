# Resorption Phase — Osteoclast Acid and Protease Activity

## Core Principle
Mature osteoclasts create a **sealed acidic microenvironment** that dissolves bone mineral and enzymatically degrades collagen matrix.

## The Resorption Lacuna

### Structural Formation
- Osteoclast attaches to bone surface via integrins (αvβ3)
- Forms **sealed zone** — tight attachment creating isolated compartment
- Ruffled border formed — highly folded plasma membrane facing bone
- Resorption lacuna: Isolated space where degradation occurs

### Acidification 
| Component | Function |
|-----------|----------|
| **Carbonic anhydrase II** | Generates H+ from CO₂ + H₂O |
| **Vacuolar H+-ATPase** | Pumps protons into lacuna |
| **Chloride channel 7 (ClC-7)** | Provides Cl- counterion |
| **Acidic microenvironment** | pH ~4.5 (optimal for enzymes) |

**Result:** Hydroxyapatite dissolution releases Ca²⁺, PO₄³⁻ into lacuna

## Enzymatic Degradation

### Cathepsin K
- Cysteine protease highly expressed in osteoclasts
- Optimal activity at acidic pH
- Degrades type I collagen (major bone matrix protein)
- Degrades osteonectin, osteopontin
- **Genetic deficiency:** Pycnodysostosis (dense bone disease)

### Matrix Metalloproteinase-9 (MMP9/Gelatinase B)
- Degrades denatured collagen (gelatin)
- Facilitates osteoclast migration through matrix
- Works synergistically with cathepsin K

## Resorption Depth and Duration
- **Duration:** 2–4 weeks per remodeling unit
- **Depth:** Typically 40–60 μm (can exceed 100 μm in high turnover)
- **Rate:** ~50 μm/day in active resorption

##  Correlates
| Condition | Resorption Characteristic |
|-----------|---------------------------|
| **Postmenopausal osteoporosis** | Increased resorption depth |
| **Paget's disease** | Massive osteoclasts, chaotic resorption |
| **Osteopetrosis** | Failed resorption (lack of acidification) |

## Therapeutic Targets
- **Bisphosphonates:** Internalized by osteoclasts → inhibit FPPS → induce apoptosis
- **Cathepsin K inhibitors** (Odanacatib — discontinued): Block collagen degradation
