# D-K2 Co-Supplementation Evidence Overview

> Despite limited high-quality RCTs, existing studies on vitamin D and K2 co-supplementation consistently show metabolic and cardiovascular benefits exceeding those of either nutrient alone, establishing proof-of-concept for synergistic therapeutic potential.

## Current Evidence Landscape

**Quantity:**
- Fewer than 10 high-quality RCTs
- Mostly small to moderate sample sizes
- Predominantly postmenopausal women
- Limited metabolic syndrome data

**Quality:**
- Heterogeneous designs
- Variable dosing regimens
- Short to moderate duration (3 months - 3 years)
- Different outcome measures

**Consistency:**
- Direction of benefit: Consistent
- Co-supplementation > individual: Consistent
- Safety profile: Favorable
- Effect sizes: Modest to moderate

## Why Evidence Is Limited

**Historical Factors:**
- D and K2 studied separately for decades
- Nutrient interaction research recent
- Pharma funding lacking (non-patentable)

**Scientific Factors:**
- Complex interaction mechanisms
- Multiple outcome variables
- Long latency to clinical outcomes

**Funding Factors:**
- Limited commercial interest
- Academic research prioritized
- METHAP addresses this gap

## Pattern of Evidence

Despite limitations, consistent findings:

| Outcome | D Alone | K2 Alone | D+K2 Together |
|---------|---------|----------|---------------|
| **Vascular calcification** | Variable | Protection | Best protection |
| **Arterial stiffness** | Modest | Modest | Greatest reduction |
| **Insulin sensitivity** | Some benefit | Some benefit | Synergistic |
| **Inflammation** | Reduction | Reduction | Enhanced |

**Interpretation:**
- Co-supplementation consistently superior
- Suggests true synergy
- Justifies combined protocols

## METHAP Position

**Addresses Evidence Gaps:**
- Metabolic syndrome population
- High-dose D + K2 + Mg
- Systematic 12-week study
- Multiple metabolic endpoints

**Contribution:**
- Adds to limited evidence base
- Tests high-dose hypothesis
- Establishes safety in MetS
- Informs clinical guidelines
