# Lipid Metabolism & Fatty Acid Insulin Resistance Markers

## Core Claim
**Fatty acid metabolism and desaturase activity generate specific biomarker patterns** — omega-3/omega-6 ratios, desaturase indices, and eicosanoid precursors that predict insulin resistance and inflammatory status.

## The Omega-6: Omega-3 Ratio

> **Patterson et al. (2012)** — Health implications of high dietary omega-6 polyunsaturated fatty acids

### Ratio Significance
| Ratio | Population | Health Implication |
|-------|-----------|-------------------|
| **1:1 to 2:1** | Evolutionary/hunter-gatherer | Anti-inflammatory baseline |
| **4:1** | Recommended optimal | Reduced CVD risk |
| **10:1 to 20:1** | Modern Western diet | Pro-inflammatory, chronic disease risk |
| **>20:1** | High processed food intake | Elevated IR, metabolic syndrome risk |

### METHAP Context
High omega-6:3 ratio → pro-inflammatory eicosanoids → insulin resistance → adipic acid elevation

## Desaturase Enzymes: Δ5 and Δ6

> **Kröger & Schulze (2012)** — Relation of Δ5 desaturase and Δ6 desaturase activity to T2D development

### Enzyme Functions
| Enzyme | Substrate | Product | Function |
|--------|-----------|---------|----------|
| **Δ6 desaturase** | Linoleic acid (18:2n-6) | γ-linolenic acid (18:3n-6) | Rate-limiting for omega-6 pathway |
| **Δ5 desaturase** | Dihomo-γ-linolenic acid (20:3n-6) | Arachidonic acid (20:4n-6) | Pro-inflammatory precursor production |
| **Δ6 desaturase** | α-linolenic acid (18:3n-3) | Stearidonic acid (18:4n-3) | Omega-3 pathway initiation |

### Desaturase Activity as IR Predictor
| Desaturase | Activity Change in IR | Mechanism |
|------------|----------------------|-----------|
| **Δ5 desaturase** | ↑ Activity (compensatory) | Increased AA production |
| **Δ6 desaturase** | ↓ Activity | Impaired essential FA conversion |
| **Δ5/Δ6 ratio** | Altered | Dysregulated inflammation |

### Product-to-Precursor Ratios as Biomarkers
- 20:4n-6/20:3n-6 (Δ5 desaturase index)
- 18:3n-6/18:2n-6 (Δ6 desaturase index)
- **↑ Ratio = ↑ Desaturase activity = ↑ IR risk**

## Specific Fatty Acid IR Associations

> **Kurotani et al. (2012)** — High stearic acid, palmitoleic acid, dihomo-γ-linolenic acid and low linoleic acid associated with metabolic risk

### Pro-IR Fatty Acid Pattern
| Fatty Acid | Change in IR | Mechanism |
|------------|--------------|-----------|
| **Stearic acid (18:0)** | ↑ | SFA, lipotoxicity |
| **Palmitoleic acid (16:1n-7)** | ↑ | Lipogenesis marker |
| **Dihomo-γ-linolenic (20:3n-6)** | ↑ | Pro-inflammatory precursor |
| **Linoleic acid (18:2n-6)** | ↓ | Essential FA deficiency |

### Anti-IR Fatty Acid Pattern
| Fatty Acid | Change in IR | Mechanism |
|------------|--------------|-----------|
| **EPA (20:5n-3)** | ↓ | Insufficient omega-3 intake |
| **DHA (22:6n-3)** | ↓ | Insufficient omega-3 intake |
| **Adiponectin correlation** | ↓ with SFA | Anti-inflammatory adipokine |

## Eicosanoid Inflammatory Mediators

### From Arachidonic Acid (AA, 20:4n-6)
- **Prostaglandin E2 (PGE2)** — Inflammation, pain, fever
- **Leukotriene B4 (LTB4)** — Neutrophil chemotaxis
- **Thromboxane A2 (TXA2)** — Platelet aggregation, vasoconstriction

### From EPA (20:5n-3)
- **Prostaglandin E3 (PGE3)** — Less inflammatory than PGE2
- **Resolvins, protectins** — Pro-resolution, anti-inflammatory

### Balance Hypothesis
High omega-6:3 ratio → AA dominance → pro-inflammatory eicosanoids → chronic inflammation → insulin resistance

## Vitamin D Connection

### VitD-Fatty Acid Interactions
| Mechanism | Effect |
|-----------|--------|
| **Anti-inflammatory** | Reduces AA-derived eicosanoids |
| **Gene regulation** | Modulates desaturase expression |
| **Calcium homeostasis** | Affects membrane fatty acid composition |
| **Adiponectin** | Increases anti-inflammatory adipokine |

### METHAP Hypothesis
High-dose VitD → improved omega-6:3 balance (via inflammation reduction) → improved insulin sensitivity → reduced adipic acid

## Clinical Application

### Fatty Acid Panel for IR Assessment
| Biomarker | Interpretation | Action |
|-----------|---------------|--------|
| Omega-6:3 ratio >10:1 | Pro-inflammatory | Increase omega-3 intake |
| Δ5 desaturase index ↑ | High AA production | Reduce omega-6, increase omega-3 |
| Stearic acid ↑ | High SFA intake | Reduce saturated fat |
| EPA+DHA ↓ | Omega-3 deficiency | Supplement EPA/DHA |
