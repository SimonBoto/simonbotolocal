# Vitamin C Supplementation Risk Assessment

## Clinical Risk Framework

### Risk Categories
| Risk Level | Definition | Clinical Indicators |
|------------|------------|---------------------|
| **Low risk** | Healthy renal function, no stone history | Normal eGFR, no prior stones |
| **Moderate risk** | Mild renal impairment, family history | eGFR 60-89, stone history in relatives |
| **High risk** | Moderate-severe renal impairment, prior stones | eGFR <60, documented calcium oxalate stones |

### Risk Assessment Parameters
| Parameter | Measurement | Risk Threshold |
|-----------|-------------|----------------|
| **eGFR** | Estimated glomerular filtration rate | <90 mL/min/1.73m² |
| **24h urine oxalate** | Urinary oxalate excretion | >45 mg/24h |
| **Calcium:oxalate ratio** | Urinary calcium/oxalate | <10:1 (increased crystallization risk) |
| **Citrate level** | Urinary citrate | <320 mg/24h (reduced inhibition) |

## Supplementation Guidelines

### Dose-Dependent Risk
| Daily Dose | Oxalate Excretion | Stone Formation Risk | Clinical Recommendation |
|------------|-------------------|----------------------|--------------------------|
| **<250mg** | Baseline (10-45mg/24h) | Negligible | Safe for all populations |
| **250-1000mg** | 15-60mg/24h | Low-moderate | Monitor urinary oxalate in at-risk patients |
| **>1000mg** | 25-75mg/24h | Moderate-high | Contraindicated in stone formers, renal impairment |

### Population-Specific Recommendations

#### General Population
- **UL:** 2,000mg/day (NIH)
- **Primary concern:** Gastrointestinal upset, not oxalate toxicity
- **Monitoring:** Not required for healthy individuals

#### Kidney Stone Formers
- **Recommended max:** 200mg/day
- **Evidence:** Penniston et al. (2008) guideline recommendation
- **Rationale:** Prevents supersaturation of urine with oxalate

#### Renal Impairment Patients
- **Stage 3+ (eGFR <60):** ≤100mg/day
- **Stage 4-5 (eGFR <30):** Avoid supplementation
- **Rationale:** Impaired oxalate clearance → systemic accumulation

## Clinical Monitoring Protocol

### Baseline Assessment
- **Renal function:** eGFR, serum creatinine
- **Urinary analysis:** 24h urine oxalate, calcium, citrate, pH
- **Stone analysis:** If available, calcium oxalate composition

### Follow-up Monitoring
| Frequency | Parameters | Indications |
|-----------|------------|-------------|
| **3 months** | Urinary oxalate, eGFR | High-dose supplementation (>500mg/day) |
| **6 months** | 24h urine analysis | Moderate-dose supplementation (250-500mg/day) |
| **Annual** | Renal function, stone history | Low-dose supplementation (<250mg/day) |

## Risk Mitigation Strategies

### Dietary Modifications
| Strategy | Mechanism | Effect |
|----------|-----------|--------|
| **Calcium co-administration** | Binds oxalate in GI tract | ↓ Absorption by 30-50% |
| **Hydration increase** | Dilutes urinary oxalate | ↓ Supersaturation risk |
| **Citrate supplementation** | Inhibits calcium oxalate crystallization | ↓ Crystal growth and aggregation |

### Alternative Approaches
- **Liposomal vitamin C:** May reduce GI side effects, but oxalate risk remains
- **Food-based sources:** Citrus fruits, bell peppers, broccoli - lower risk than supplements
- **Ascorbyl palmitate:** Fat-soluble form, different metabolism pathway
