# HPGA Aging Decline

The hypothalamic-pituitary-gonadal axis undergoes characteristic age-related changes in both sexes, with distinct patterns of decline that impact clinical management.

## Male Patterns (Andropause)

- **Testosterone decline**: ~1% per year after age 30
- **SHBG increase**: Age-related rise in sex hormone-binding globulin
- **Free testosterone decline**: More significant than total testosterone decline
- **Gonadotropin changes**: Gradual LH/FSH increase as feedback sensitivity declines

## Female Patterns (Menopause)

- **Ovarian reserve depletion**: Leads to rising FSH and declining inhibin B
- **Estradiol decline**: Gradual decrease during perimenopause, rapid decline at menopause
- **Follicular phase shortening**: Early sign of ovarian aging
- **FSH rise**: Primary marker of ovarian aging and reserve depletion

## Clinical Implications

- **Diagnosis**: Differentiate age-related decline from pathological causes
- **HRT considerations**: Timing, formulations, and risk-benefit analysis
- **Bone health**: HPGA decline is major contributor to osteoporosis
- **Cardiovascular health**: Hormonal changes impact vascular function

## Key Research Findings

- Testosterone decline in men is associated with increased adiposity and decreased muscle mass
- The menopausal transition takes an average of 4-8 years
- Ovarian aging begins in the third decade, decades before menopause
