# Pentose Phosphate Pathway — Ribose and NADPH

## Core Claim
**The pentose phosphate pathway (PPP) generates ribose-5-phosphate for nucleotide synthesis and NADPH for biosynthesis and antioxidant defense—its two phases (oxidative and non-oxidative) provide metabolic flexibility to balance cellular needs for reducing power, biosynthetic precursors, and energy** — G6PD deficiency is the most common enzyme deficiency worldwide.

## Evidence

### Two Phases
| Phase | Reactions | Products | Regulation |
|-------|-----------|----------|------------|
| **Oxidative** | Irreversible | 2 NADPH + Ribulose-5-P + CO₂ | Inhibited by NADPH |
| **Non-oxidative** | Reversible | Ribose-5-P, glycolytic intermediates | Demand-driven |

### Key Enzymes
| Enzyme | Function | Clinical Relevance |
|--------|----------|-------------------|
| **G6PD** | Rate-limiting, produces NADPH | Deficiency → hemolytic anemia |
| **Transketolase** | 2-carbon transfers | Requires thiamine (B1) |
| **Transaldolase** | 3-carbon transfers | Connects PPP to glycolysis |

### NADPH Functions
| Function | Example | Tissue |
|----------|---------|--------|
| **Biosynthesis** | Fatty acid, cholesterol synthesis | Liver, adipose |
| **Antioxidant** | Glutathione reduction | All tissues |
| **Immune** | Respiratory burst | Phagocytes |
| **NO synthesis** | Vasodilation | Endothelium |

### G6PD Deficiency
| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Prevalence** | 400M+ people (most common enzyme deficiency) |
| **Inheritance** | X-linked |
| **Triggers** | Oxidative stress (fava beans, sulfa drugs, infection) |
| **Mechanism** | Insufficient NADPH → glutathione depletion → RBC lysis |
| **Advantage** | Malaria resistance (heterozygote) |

## Clinical Implication
- **PPP is essential for RBC survival** — only source of NADPH in mature erythrocytes
- **Rapidly dividing tissues** (bone marrow, gut, skin) have high PPP activity
- **Cancer cells upregulate PPP** — meet biosynthetic and redox demands
- **METHAP relevance:** B-vitamins (B1, B2, B3) support PPP enzymes; high metabolic demand requires robust NADPH generation
