# Hematological Overlap — B12 Deficiency vs MM

> Both B12 deficiency and multiple myeloma cause anemia, elevated LDH, and bone marrow abnormalities, but the anemia morphology and mechanism differ critically.

## B12 Deficiency Hematology

**Peripheral Blood:**
- **Macrocytic anemia:** MCV >100 fL
- **Hypersegmented neutrophils:** >5 lobes
- **Pancytopenia:** Severe cases
- **LDH:** Elevated (ineffective erythropoiesis)

**Mechanism:**
- Impaired DNA synthesis
- Megaloblastic maturation
- Cell death in marrow (ineffective)

## Multiple Myeloma Hematology

**Peripheral Blood:**
- **Anemia:** Usually normocytic
- **Rouleaux formation:** Protein effect
- **Plasma cells:** In blood (advanced)
- **LDH:** Elevated (tumor burden)

**Mechanism:**
- Bone marrow infiltration
- Cytokine suppression
- Chronic disease pattern

## Key Differences

| Feature | B12 Deficiency | MM |
|---------|---------------|-----|
| **MCV** | High (>100) | Normal |
| **Neutrophils** | Hypersegmented | Normal |
| **Pathophysiology** | Maturation defect | Infiltration |
| **Reversibility** | Rapid with B12 | Progressive |
