# Osteocalcin — Metabolic Hormone Function

## Overview

Osteocalcin is a vitamin K-dependent protein produced by osteoblasts. Once thought to be only a bone matrix protein, it's now recognized as a metabolic hormone regulating [[KEYWORD-Insulin|insulin]] sensitivity, energy [[KEYWORD-Metabolism|metabolism]], and male fertility.

---

## Bone and Metabolic Functions

### In Bone (Classical Function)
- Binds hydroxyapatite crystals
- Regulates bone mineralization
- Marker of bone formation

### In Metabolism (Endocrine Function)
**Undercarboxylated osteocalcin (ucOC)** acts as hormone:

**Targets:**
| Tissue | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| **Pancreas** | ↑ Insulin production (β-cell proliferation) |
| **Adipose** | ↑ Adiponectin → ↑ Insulin sensitivity |
| **Muscle** | ↑ Glucose uptake |
| **Testis** | ↑ Testosterone synthesis |

---

## [[KEYWORD-Vitamin_D|vitamin d]] and K2 Regulation

### Vitamin D
- **Effect:** Increases osteocalcin gene expression
- **Result:** More osteocalcin produced by osteoblasts

### Vitamin K2
- **Effect:** Carboxylates osteocalcin
- **Result:** Modulates binding to bone matrix

**Key Insight:**
- Carboxylated: Stays in bone (structural)
- Undercarboxylated: Released into circulation (hormonal)

**Vitamin K2 balance determines metabolic vs. skeletal role**

---

## Metabolic Syndrome Relevance

### Insulin Resistance
**Osteocalcin improves:**
- β-cell function
- Peripheral insulin sensitivity
- Glucose tolerance

### Adiponectin Connection
```
ucOC → Adipose tissue → ↑ Adiponectin secretion
    ↓
↑ Insulin sensitivity in liver and muscle
    ↓
Improved glucose metabolism
```

### Clinical Observations
- Low osteocalcin associated with MetS, T2DM, obesity
- Higher ucOC correlates with better metabolic health
- Aging decreases osteocalcin (links to age-related metabolic decline)

---

## Supplementation Effects

### Vitamin K2 (MK-7)
**Complex effects:**
- Increases carboxylation → more bone-bound osteocalcin
- But optimal K2 may increase total osteocalcin production
- Net effect: Improved metabolic function with adequate D+K2

### Exercise
- Mechanical loading increases osteocalcin
- Explains part of exercise's metabolic benefits
