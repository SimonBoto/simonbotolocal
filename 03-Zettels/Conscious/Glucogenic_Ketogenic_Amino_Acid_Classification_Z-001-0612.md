# Glucogenic vs Ketogenic Amino Acid Classification

## The Fundamental Division

Amino acids are classified by the metabolic fate of their carbon skeletons:
- **Glucogenic:** Can be converted to glucose
- **Ketogenic:** Can be converted to ketone bodies or 
- **Both:** Have dual metabolic fates

## Complete Classification

### Purely Ketogenic (2)
> "Leucine and Lysine are the only purely ketogenic amino acids."

| Amino Acid | Product | Notes |
|------------|---------|-------|
| **Leucine** | Acetyl-CoA + Acetoacetyl-CoA | Branched-chain, HMG-CoA pathway |
| **Lysine** | Acetyl-CoA | Strictly ketogenic, complex catabolism |

**Key point:** These two CANNOT be used for net glucose synthesis.

### Both Glucogenic and Ketogenic (5)
| Amino Acid | Glucogenic Product | Ketogenic Product |
|------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| **Isoleucine** | Propionyl-CoA → Succinyl-CoA | Acetyl-CoA |
| **Phenylalanine** | Fumarate | Acetoacetate |
| **Tyrosine** | Fumarate | Acetoacetate |
| **Tryptophan** | Pyruvate / Alanine | Acetyl-CoA |
| **Threonine** | Propionyl-CoA → Succinyl-CoA | Acetyl-CoA |

### Purely Glucogenic (13)
All other amino acids feed into gluconeogenic precursors:

| Amino Acid | Gluconeogenic Precursor |
|------------|------------------------|
| Alanine | Pyruvate |
| Arginine | α-Ketoglutarate |
| Asparagine | Oxaloacetate |
| Aspartate | Oxaloacetate |
| Cysteine | Pyruvate |
| Glutamate | α-Ketoglutarate |
| Glutamine | α-Ketoglutarate |
| Glycine | Pyruvate |
| Histidine | α-Ketoglutarate |
| Methionine | Succinyl-CoA |
| Proline | α-Ketoglutarate |
| Serine | Pyruvate |
| Valine | Succinyl-CoA |

## Clinical and Physiological Significance

### During Fasting
- **Glucogenic amino acids:** Sustained blood glucose via gluconeogenesis
- **Ketogenic amino acids:** Spare glucose by providing alternative fuel
- **Muscle protein breakdown:** Releases both types; liver coordinates fate

### Protein Quality and Diet Design
- **Ketogenic diets:** Higher leucine/lysine content matters less for ketosis
- **Glucose management:** Glucogenic amino acids raise  more
- **Complete proteins:** Must contain adequate amounts of all classes

### Metabolic Disease

| Condition | Implication |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Diabetes** | Glucogenic amino acids contribute to hyperglycemia |
| **Ketogenic diet** | Ketogenic amino acids support ketogenesis |
| **Starvation** | Both classes utilized; glucogenic for glucose, ketogenic for fuel |
| **Liver disease** | Impaired amino acid  affects both pathways |

## Memory Aid: PITTT

- **P**henylalanine
