# Statin Intolerance — Myopathy Management

> Statin intolerance affects 5-10% of patients, with myopathy representing the most common concern requiring systematic evaluation and management.

## Myopathy Spectrum

| Condition | CK | Symptoms | Incidence |
|-----------|----|----------|-----------|
| **Myalgia** | Normal | Pain, no weakness | 5-10% |
| **Myositis** | 3-10x ULN | Pain + weakness | <1% |
| **Rhabdomyolysis** | >10x ULN | Severe, myoglobinuria | <0.1% |

## Risk Factors

**Drug:** High-dose, lipophilic statins, interactions
**Patient:** Age >80, renal impairment, hypothyroidism, low weight, Asian ancestry

## Interactions

- CYP3A4 inhibitors (macrolides, azoles, grapefruit)
- Gemfibrozil (avoid), fenofibrate (safer)
- Amiodarone, verapamil

## 

- CoQ10 depletion
- l dysfunction
- Cell membrane instability

## Management

**Myalgia:**
1. Reassure (often transient)
2. Switch to hydrophilic (pravastatin, rosuvastatin)
3. Reduce dose
4. Alternate-day dosing
5. Add ezetimibe
6. CoQ10 supplementation (limited evidence)

**True Intolerance:**
- Non-statin options (ezetimibe, PCSK9, bempedoic acid)
- Lifestyle intensification
