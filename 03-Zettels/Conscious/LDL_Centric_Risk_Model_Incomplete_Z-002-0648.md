## Core Claim
The medical paradigm of using **LDL cholesterol as the primary target** for cardiovascular risk assessment and intervention is **incomplete** and may lead to over-treatment, as LDL alone fails to predict mortality in healthy populations.

## The LDL-Centric Model

### Current Paradigm
```
Elevated LDL → Atherosclerosis → CVD Events → Death
     ↓
  [Target for intervention]
     ↓
Statins / PCSK9 inhibitors / Ezetimibe
```

### Problems with the Model
| Issue | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| **Incomplete** | Ignores inflammation, insulin resistance, oxidative stress |
| **Over-simplified** | Treats cholesterol transport as root cause |
| **Extrapolated** | Secondary prevention evidence applied to healthy people |
| **Incomplete risk prediction** | Many CVD events occur with "normal" LDL |

## Why the Model Fails in Primary Prevention

### The Disconnect
| Observation | Implication |
|-------------|-------------|
| High LDL + no CVD = same mortality as low LDL | LDL not the driver in healthy people |
| Statins reduce events less than expected from LDL reduction | Pleiotropic effects (inflammation) matter more |
| Residual risk persists after LDL lowering | Other factors unaddressed |

### Missing Pieces
| Factor | Role in CVD | Current Status in Guidelines |
|--------|-------------|------------------------------|
| **Inflammation (hs-CRP)** | Direct atherogenic role | Underemphasized |
| **Insulin resistance** | Root of metabolic dysfunction | Not directly targeted |
| **Oxidative stress** | LDL oxidation required for pathology | Not measured clinically |
| **Endothelial function** | Actual site of disease initiation | Rarely assessed |
| **Lp(a)** | Independent risk factor | Often not measured |

## A More Complete Model

### Proposed Framework
```
Metabolic Dysfunction → Inflammation → Endothelial Dysfunction
        ↓                        ↓
   Insulin resistance      Oxidative stress
        ↓                        ↓
   Dyslipidemia (including    LDL oxidation
   elevated LDL in context)         ↓
        ↓                        ↓
        └──────→ Atherosclerosis ←──────┘
```

### Key Insight
- **LDL is a carrier**, not a toxin
- **Context matters:** LDL in insulin-sensitive vs. insulin-resistant states
- **Oxidized LDL** is the problem, not native LDL

## Evidence

> "When LDL is not associated with mortality in primary prevention, other factors (inflammation, insulin resistance, metabolic health) must be more predictive of outcomes than isolated lipid values."
> — Kip et al., 2024

## Clinical Implications

### Risk Assessment Evolution
| Current | Proposed |
|---------|----------|
| LDL-centric | Multi-factorial |
| Lipid panel | Lipids + metabolic markers + inflammation |
| Treat numbers | Treat pathophysiology |

### For Practitioners
- **Don't treat LDL in isolation**
- **Assess metabolic health** (insulin, glucose, waist circumference)
- **Measure inflammation** (hs-CRP)
- **Consider Lp(a)** for additional risk stratification
