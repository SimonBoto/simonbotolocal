## Core Claim
**Effective metabolomics training requires matching teaching style to trainee personality and clinic needs — choosing between Military Instructor (structured, high-intensity), Socratic Mentor (guided discovery), or Hybrid Precision (adaptive combination) to optimize learning outcomes.**

## Evidence

### Three Teaching Styles
| Style | Approach | Best For | Risks |
|-------|----------|----------|-------|
| **Military Instructor** | Structured, high-intensity, clear expectations | Trainees needing discipline, fast learners | Burnout if too rigid |
| **Socratic Mentor** | Guided discovery, questioning, patient | Self-motivated learners, deep thinkers | Too slow for some |
| **Hybrid Precision** | Adaptive, context-dependent, flexible | Most trainees, varied backgrounds | Requires skilled instructor |

### Selection Criteria
| Trainee Characteristic | Recommended Style |
|------------------------|-------------------|
| Needs structure | Military Instructor |
| Self-directed | Socratic Mentor |
| Mixed background | Hybrid Precision |
| Time-constrained | Military Instructor |
| Deep thinker | Socratic Mentor |
| Variable readiness | Hybrid Precision |

### Implementation Examples
| Scenario | Style Application |
|----------|-------------------|
| **Foundation phase** | Military — establish discipline |
| **Pattern recognition** | Socratic — develop reasoning |
| **Integration phase** | Hybrid — adapt to competence |
| **Remediation** | Socratic — understand errors |

### Instructor Competency
Effective instructors must:
- Assess trainee learning style
- Adapt approach in real-time
- Maintain standards while being flexible
- Recognize when to switch styles

## Clinical Implication
- **Training efficiency:** Matched styles accelerate learning
- **Retention:** Appropriate challenge improves retention
- **Satisfaction:** Trainees thrive with compatible approach
- **Outcomes:** Style matching affects clinical competence
- **METHAP relevance:** Research supervision similarly benefits from style matching — some RAs need structure, others thrive with autonomy
