# Aspirin as Cancer Immunomodulator (Not Just Anti-Platelet)

## The Old View

| Use | Mechanism |
|-----|-----------|
| Cardiovascular | Anti-platelet (prevents clotting) |
| Pain/Fever | COX inhibition (reduces prostaglandins) |
| Inflammation | Anti-inflammatory |

## The New View

**Aspirin = Cancer Immunomodulator**

- Enhances anti-tumor immunity
- Releases T-cells from platelet-mediated suppression
- Prevents metastasis through immune mechanism

## Why This Matters

### Clinical
- Low-cost cancer prevention/adjuvant therapy
- Widely available globally
- Well-established safety profile (at low doses)

### Research
- Opens combination therapy possibilities
- Suggests immune biomarkers for response (HLA-I)
- Reframes aspirin from "anti-platelet" to "immune enhancer"

## Mechanism Distinction

| Traditional | New Understanding |
|-------------|-------------------|
| Anti-platelet (bleeding prevention) | Immune modulation (cancer prevention) |
| COX-1 inhibition → ↓ TXA2 → ↓ platelet aggregation | COX-1 inhibition → ↓ TXA2 → ↑ T-cell function |
| Same molecular target, different physiological outcome |

## Significance

| Same molecular target, different physiological outcome |
