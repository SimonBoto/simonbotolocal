## Core Claim
**Saturated fats are called "saturated" because all their carbon bonds are occupied (saturated with hydrogen), meaning they contain no double bonds that can react with oxygen** — this makes them chemically stable and resistant to oxidation.

## Chemical Structure
| Feature | Result |
|---------|--------|
| **All bonds saturated** | No double bonds to react with oxygen |
| **Chemically stable** | Resistant to oxidation |
| **Like iron that doesn't rust** | No oxidative damage |

## Stability In Practice
| Environment | Behavior |
|-------------|----------|
| **Cooking** | Stable at high heat |
| **In the body** | Doesn't form harmful oxidized compounds |
| **Storage** | Long shelf life |

## Comparison: Double Bonds and Oxidation Risk
| Fat Type | Double Bonds | Oxidation Risk |
|----------|--------------|----------------|
| **Saturated** | 0 | **Lowest** — most stable |
| **Monounsaturated** | 1 | Moderate |
| **Polyunsaturated** | Multiple | **Highest** |

## Clinical Implication
Saturated fats are **safe for high-heat cooking** and don't contribute to **oxidative stress** in the body.
