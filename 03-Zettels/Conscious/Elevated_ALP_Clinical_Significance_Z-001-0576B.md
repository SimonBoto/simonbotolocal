# Elevated ALP Clinical Significance

> Elevated serum alkaline phosphatase (ALP) requires differentiation between hepatobiliary disease and bone turnover disorders, guiding appropriate diagnostic workup.

## Core Concept

**Key Process:**
```
Elevated ALP: Hepatobiliary disease vs bone turnover disorders
```

## Mechanism

1. **Hepatobiliary causes:** Cholestasis (bile duct obstruction), hepatitis, cirrhosis
2. **Bone causes:** Paget's disease, osteomalacia, healing fractures, metastases
3. **Physiological causes:** Children (bone growth), pregnancy (placental ALP)

## Clinical Significance

Elevated ALP interpretation guides diagnostic approach:
- Hepatobiliary disease: Evaluate with LFTs, imaging, viral serology
- Bone disorders: Evaluate with bone markers, imaging, clinical exam
- Physiological causes: Consider age and clinical context
