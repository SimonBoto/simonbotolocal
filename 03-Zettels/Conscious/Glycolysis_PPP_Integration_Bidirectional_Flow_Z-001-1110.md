## Core Claim
**The pentose phosphate pathway (PPP) branches from glycolysis at glucose-6-phosphate and can feed back into glycolysis via transketolase/transaldolase reactions, creating bidirectional metabolic flexibility that allocates carbon between energy (glycolysis) and biosynthesis (NADPH/ribose)** — this integration is regulated by cellular redox state and biosynthetic demand.

## Evidence

### Branch Point Regulation
| Condition | Glycolysis Favored | PPP Favored |
|-----------|-------------------|-------------|
| **High NAD⁺/NADH** | ✅ | — |
| **High NADP⁺/NADPH need** | — | ✅ |
| **High ATP demand** | ✅ | — |
| **High ribose need** | — | ✅ |
| **Insulin** | ✅ | ✅ (both stimulated) |

### Bidirectional Flow
| Direction | When Active | Purpose |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| **Glycolysis → PPP** | High NADPH/ribose demand | Biosynthesis support |
| **PPP → Glycolysis** | Excess ribose, energy need | ATP production |

### Key Enzymes
| Enzyme | Reaction | Role |
|--------|----------|------|
| **G6PD** | Glucose-6-P → 6-phosphogluconolactone | PPP entry, rate-limiting |
| **Transketolase** | 2-carbon transfer | Reversible interconversion |
| **Transaldolase** | 3-carbon transfer | Reversible interconversion |

## Clinical Implication
- **G6PD deficiency** — most common enzyme deficiency; hemolytic anemia with oxidative stress
- **Cancer cells** — upregulate PPP for NADPH (redox) and ribose (proliferation)
- **Insulin stimulates both pathways** — supports anabolic needs after glucose uptake
- **METHAP relevance:** B-vitamins (B1 for transketolase) support pathway integration; metabolic flexibility depends on both pathways functioning
