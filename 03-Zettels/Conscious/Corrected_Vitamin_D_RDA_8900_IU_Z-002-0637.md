# Corrected Vitamin D RDA ~8,900 IU

## Core Claim
Proper statistical analysis using the 97.5th percentile approach shows that **~8,900 IU/day** of vitamin D is required to achieve serum 25(OH)D >20 ng/mL in 97.5% of the population — approximately **15× higher** than the IOM's 600 IU/day recommendation.

## The Calculation

### Methodological Correction
| Parameter | IOM 2011 (Wrong) | Veugelers & Ekwaru 2014 (Correct) |
|-----------|------------------|-----------------------------------|
| Statistical measure | Mean | 97.5th percentile |
| Population coverage | ~50% | 97.5% |
| Required intake | 600 IU/day | ~8,900 IU/day |
| Ratio | 1× | 14.8× |

### Dose-Response Relationship
- Serum 25(OH)D increases ~0.5-1.0 ng/mL per 100 IU/day
- Large inter-individual variation (genetics, BMI, baseline status)
- 97.5th percentile accounts for "non-responders"

## Clinical Significance

### Physiological Context
- **Sun exposure:** 15-20 minutes midday sun → 10,000-20,000 IU synthesized
- **Evolutionary precedent:** Human physiology adapted to higher intakes
- **Safety:** 10,000 IU/day for 5 months showed no toxicity (Heaney 2003)

### Current Guidelines vs. Reality
| Guideline | Recommendation | Actual Need |
|-----------|----------------|-------------|
| IOM RDA | 600 IU/day | ~8,900 IU/day |
| IOM Upper Limit | 4,000 IU/day | Within normal range |
| Endocrine Society | 1,500-2,000 IU/day | Closer but still insufficient |

## Evidence

> "When using the correct 97.5th percentile approach, the RDA should be approximately 8,895 IU/day to ensure 97.5% of the population reaches 20 ng/mL."
> — Veugelers & Ekwaru, 2014

## Implications

### For Patients
- Current RDAs leave most people deficient
- Supplementation needs often underestimated by clinicians
- Testing 25(OH)D levels essential (target: 40-60 ng/mL optimal)

### For Policy
- Public health guidelines require urgent revision
- "Upper limits" based on flawed calculations
- Cost of deficiency far exceeds supplementation cost
