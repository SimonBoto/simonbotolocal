## Core Claim
Optimal vitamin D levels and supplementation requirements vary **2-3 fold between individuals** due to genetic polymorphisms, body composition, baseline status, and disease state — necessitating personalized approaches rather than universal RDAs.

## Sources of Variation

### 1. Genetic Polymorphisms
| Gene | Function | Effect on Requirements |
|------|----------|------------------------|
| **VDR** (Vitamin D Receptor) | Mediates genomic effects | Variants affect sensitivity |
| **DBP** (Vitamin D Binding Protein) | Transport protein | Affects bioavailability |
| **CYP2R1** | 25-hydroxylase (liver) | Affects conversion to 25(OH)D |
| **CYP27B1** | 1α-hydroxylase (kidney/tissues) | Affects activation to calcitriol |
| **GC** | Group-specific component | Affects circulating levels |

### 2. Body Composition
- **BMI:** Higher adiposity → vitamin D sequestration in fat → higher needs
- **Body surface area:** Larger individuals need more for same serum level

### 3. Baseline Status
- **Deficient individuals:** Require higher doses to reach repletion
- **Seasonal variation:** Winter depletion requires higher supplementation

### 4. Age
- **Elderly:** Reduced skin synthesis capacity (7-dehydrocholesterol decline)
- **Infants:** Limited sun exposure → reliance on supplementation

### 5. Skin Pigmentation
- **Melanin:** Reduces UVB penetration → lower synthesis per minute of sun
- **Darker skin:** 5-10× more sun exposure needed for same synthesis

## Clinical Variation by Condition

### Population-Specific Needs
| Population | Target Level | Rationale |
|------------|--------------|-----------|
| General adults | 30-50 ng/mL | Optimal for most outcomes |
| Children | ~30 ng/mL | Adequate for growth/immunity |
| Elderly | 40-60 ng/mL | Compensate for reduced synthesis |
| Autoimmune patients | 50-80 ng/mL | Immunomodulation requires higher levels |
| Dark skin (low sun) | 40-60 ng/mL | Compensate for reduced synthesis |
| Obese individuals | 40-60 ng/mL | Compensate for fat sequestration |

### Evidence for Higher Needs in Autoimmunity
- Multiple sclerosis: Higher levels associated with reduced relapse
- Rheumatoid arthritis: Inverse correlation with disease activity
- Type 1 diabetes: Higher maternal levels reduce offspring risk

## Implications for Practice

### Universal RDA Problems
- Based on "average" person who doesn't exist
- Ignores 2-3 fold inter-individual variation
- Results in suboptimal levels for many

### Personalized Approach
1. **Measure baseline** 25(OH)D
2. **Assess factors:** BMI, skin tone, age, health status
3. **Set individualized target** (30-80 ng/mL depending on condition)
4. **Calculate dose** needed based on current vs. target
5. **Re-test in 3 months** and adjust

### Dosing Formula (Approximate)
- **To increase 10 ng/mL:** ~1,000 IU/day for 3 months
- **Individual adjustment:** ±50% based on genetic/body factors
