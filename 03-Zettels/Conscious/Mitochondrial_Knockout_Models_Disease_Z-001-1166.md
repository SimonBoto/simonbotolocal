# l Knockout Models — Disease Mechanisms

## Why Knockout Models Matter

 genes are essential for life. Knockout studies reveal:
- Which functions are absolutely required
- Pathophysiology of mitochondrial disorders
- Tissue-specific vulnerabilities
- Therapeutic targets

---

## POLG Knockout — The Replication Catastrophe

**Gene Function:** mtDNA polymerase — replicates and repairs mitochondrial DNA

### Knockout Phenotypes

| Genotype | Outcome | Timeline |
|----------|---------|----------|
| **POLG-/- (homozygous)** | Embryonic lethal | Before implantation |
| **POLG+/- (heterozygous)** | Progressive phenotype | Months to years |

### Heterozygous Phenotype (POLG+/-)
- Progressive neurodegeneration
- Muscle weakness and atrophy
- Premature aging features
- Reduced lifespan

**Mechanism:** 50% enzyme activity → gradual mtDNA depletion over time → cumulative energy failure

### Human POLG Diseases
- **Alpers syndrome:** Childhood-onset encephalopathy, liver failure, seizures
- **CPEO:** Chronic progressive external ophthalmoplegia (eye muscle weakness)
- **Ataxia-neuropathy:** SANDO, MIRAS syndromes

---

## TFAM Knockout — Transcription Collapse

**Gene Function:** Mitochondrial transcription factor A — binds mtDNA, enables transcription

### Tissue-Specific Consequences

**Heart (Cardiac-Specific KO):**
- Dilated cardiomyopathy
- Heart failure
- Early lethality
- **Why severe:** Cardiac muscle has highest mitochondrial density and energy demand

**Skeletal Muscle:**
- Exercise intolerance
- Ragged red fibers on biopsy
- Lactic acidosis

**Brain:**
- Seizures
- Neurodegeneration
- Developmental defects
- **Why vulnerable:** Neurons are post-mitotic and energy-dependent

**Liver:**
- Hepatic steatosis
- Dysfunction
- Impaired gluconeogenesis

**Mechanism:** No TFAM → no mtDNA transcription → no OXPHOS proteins → ATP crisis

---

## Essential vs. Tolerated Loss

### Absolutely Essential (Embryonic Lethal)
- POLG (homozygous)
- TFAM (homozygous)
- Most respiratory chain subunits

### Tolerated (Phenotype with Reduced Function)
- POLG+/- (heterozygous)
- Tissue-specific knockouts
- Conditional knockouts (inducible)

### Implications
- Complete mitochondrial failure is incompatible with life
- Partial dysfunction causes disease
- Tissue specificity determines phenotype

## Clinical Correlates

| Mouse Model | Human Equivalent | Clinical Features |
|-------------|------------------|-------------------|
| POLG+/- | POLG-related disorders | Neurodegeneration, myopathy |
| TFAM heart KO | Cardiomyopathy with mtDNA depletion | Heart failure |
| TFAM brain KO | Mitochondrial encephalopathy | Seizures, neurodegeneration |

## Research Applications

**Disease Modeling:**
- Recapitulate human mitochondrial disorders
- Test therapeutic interventions
- Understand pathophysiology

**Drug Development:**
- Screen for mtDNA protective compounds
- Test mitochondrial biogenesis inducers
- Evaluate gene therapy approaches

**Aging Research:**
- POLG mutator mouse: accelerated mtDNA mutations
- Premature aging phenotype
- Tests mitochondrial theory of aging
