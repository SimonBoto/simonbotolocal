# Health Foundations — Diet, Movement, and Sleep as Core Pillars

## Core Claim
**Diet, physical movement, and sleep are the three foundational pillars of health** — these core lifestyle factors have the strongest evidence base for preventing chronic disease, extending lifespan, and improving quality of life. Master these before optimizing other factors.

## The Three Pillars

### 1. Diet — Fuel Quality
| Principle | Implementation |
|-----------|----------------|
| **Eliminate UPF** | No packaged, industrial foods |
| **Whole foods** | Meat, fish, eggs, vegetables, fruit |
| **Limit sugar** | <25g/day added sugar |
| **Stable fats** | Olive oil, butter, tallow |
| **Adequate protein** | 1.2-1.6g/kg body weight |

### 2. Movement — Physical Activity
| Type | Frequency | Benefit |
|------|-----------|---------|
| **Daily walking** | 7,000-10,000 steps | Cardiovascular, metabolic |
| **Resistance training** | 2-3x/week | Muscle, bone, insulin |
| **Zone 2 cardio** | 2-3x/week | Mitochondrial health |
| **Mobility** | Daily | Joint health, injury prevention |

### 3. Sleep — Recovery
| Factor | Target | Why |
|--------|--------|-----|
| **Duration** | 7-9 hours | Hormonal regulation |
| **Consistency** | Same times | Circadian rhythm |
| **Quality** | Uninterrupted | Deep/REM cycles |
| **Timing** | 10pm-6am optimal | Melatonin, cortisol |

## Why These Three

### Evidence Strength
| Pillar | Evidence Quality |
|--------|------------------|
| **Diet** | Strongest — direct metabolic impact |
| **Movement** | Strong — dose-response clear |
| **Sleep** | Strong — disruption causes disease |

### Synergy
| Combination | Effect |
|-------------|--------|
| **Diet + Movement** | Better body composition |
| **Movement + Sleep** | Better recovery |
| **Sleep + Diet** | Better appetite control |
| **All three** | Optimal health |

## Implementation

### Week 1-2: Diet
| Action | Task |
|--------|------|
| **Clean out pantry** | Remove UPF, seed oils |
| **Shop whole foods** | Meat, vegetables, eggs |
| **Cook at home** | Most meals |

### Week 3-4: Movement
| Action | Task |
|--------|------|
| **Track steps** | Baseline, then increase |
| **Schedule walks** | Daily habit |
| **Add resistance** | 2x/week minimum |

### Week 5-6: Sleep
| Action | Task |
|--------|------|
| **Fix schedule** | Consistent bedtime |
| **Optimize environment** | Dark, cool, quiet |
| **Wind-down routine** | No screens 1 hour before |

## Common Mistakes

### Diet
| Mistake | Correction |
|---------|------------|
| **Perfectionism** | Progress, not perfection |
| **Ignoring quality** | Whole foods over macros |
| **Too restrictive** | Sustainable changes |

### Movement
| Mistake | Correction |
|---------|------------|
| **All or nothing** | Some movement > none |
| **Only cardio** | Add resistance |
| **Ignoring recovery** | Rest days essential |

### Sleep
| Mistake | Correction |
|---------|------------|
| **Inconsistent schedule** | Same times daily |
| **Screens before bed** | Blue light blocking |
| **Caffeine late** | Cut off 2pm |

## Significance

**Diet, physical movement, and sleep are the three foundational pillars of health** — these core lifestyle factors have the strongest evidence base for preventing chronic disease, extending lifespan, and improving quality of life. Master these before optimizing other factors.
