# CD28-Negative T Cells — Exhausted vs. Senescent

## CD28: The Co-Stimulatory Molecule

**Function:**
- Binds CD80/CD86 on antigen-presenting cells
- Provides "Signal 2" for full T-cell activation
- Required for IL-2 production, survival, proliferation

**Loss of CD28:**
- Marker of chronic activation
- Associated with aging and autoimmunity
- Indicates functional impairment

---

## CD8+CD28(lo/-) T Cells in Autoimmunity

### Expanded Population
**Observed in:**
- Systemic lupus erythematosus (SLE)
- Rheumatoid arthritis (RA)
- Other chronic inflammatory conditions

**Frequency:**
- Healthy young: <10% of CD8+ T cells
- Autoimmune patients: 20-50%
- Elderly: Also increased

---

## The Paradox: Proliferating but Dysfunctional

### Telomerase-Positive
**Surprising Finding:**
- Express active telomerase
- Capable of proliferation
- Not truly "senescent" in classical sense

**Yet Functionally Impaired:**
- Reduced proliferative response to stimuli
- Altered cytokine profile
- Resistant to apoptosis

### Classification Debate
**"Replicatively Exhausted":**
- Proliferated extensively
- Telomeres maintained by telomerase
- But functionally worn out

**Distinct from:**
- **Senescence:** Telomere-induced cell cycle arrest
- **Anergy:** Functional unresponsiveness
- **Deletion:** Cell death

---

## Pathogenic Potential

### Pro-Inflammatory Phenotype
**Cytokine Production:**
- IFN-γ (Th1)
- TNF-α (pro-inflammatory)
- Cytotoxic molecules (perforin, granzyme B)

**Tissue Damage:**
- Can kill target cells
- Contribute to organ damage
- Resistant to immunosuppression

### Resistance to Therapy
**Challenges:**
- Standard immunosuppressants less effective
- Persist in chronic 
- Re-expand after partial control

---

## Senescence vs. Exhaustion Markers

| Feature | Replicative Senescence | T-Cell Exhaustion | CD28-Negative |
|---------|------------------------|-------------------|---------------|
| **Telomere length** | Very short | Variable | Maintained |
| **Telomerase** | Absent | Variable | Active |
| **Proliferation** | Arrested | Limited | Possible |
| **PD-1 expression** | Low | High | Variable |
| **Function** | None | Impaired | Partial |
| **Cytokines** | SASP | Limited | Pro-inflammatory |

**SASP** = Senescence-Associated Secretory Phenotype (IL-6, IL-8, etc.)

---

## Clinical Significance

### Disease Activity
- Higher percentages = more severe disease
- Correlate with organ damage
- Predict treatment resistance

###  Potential
- Track disease progression
- Monitor treatment response
- Identify high-risk patients

---

## Therapeutic Considerations

### Targeting Strategies
**Challenges:**
- Eliminating these cells may compromise immunity
- They serve (dysfunctional) function
- Markers not unique (overlap with other subsets)

**Potential Approaches:**
- Prevent formation (reduce chronic activation)
- Enhance apoptosis (sensitization)
- Functional modulation (cytokine blockade)
