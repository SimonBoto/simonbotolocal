# Glucose Discovery Historical Timeline

## Key Milestones in Glucose Discovery

### 1747: First Isolation by Andreas Marggraf
> "Glucose was first isolated in 1747 by the German chemist Andreas Marggraf."

- German chemist (1709-1782)
- Isolated glucose from raisins
- Demonstrated it was a type of sugar
- Limited understanding of significance at time

### Early Understanding
- Recognized as "sweet principle"
- Chemical nature unclear
- Physiological role unknown
- Connection to diabetes not yet made

### 19th Century: Chemical Identification
- Molecular formula: C6H12O6 established
- Isomerism discovered (glucose vs. fructose)
- Carbohydrate classification created
- Stereochemistry elucidated (Fischer projections)

### 1850s: Claude Bernard's Glycogen Discovery
- French physiologist (1813-1878)
- Discovered glycogen ("sugar-former") in liver
- Demonstrated liver's role in glucose homeostasis
- Concept of internal secretion (precursor to hormone concept)

### 1902: Emil Fischer's Nobel Prize
- Nobel Prize in Chemistry for glucose stereochemistry
- Fischer projections (still used today)
- Synthesized glucose from glycerol

### 1921: Insulin Discovery Integration
- Banting and Best discover insulin
- Clarifies crucial role of glucose in metabolic processes
- Integration of glucose chemistry with clinical medicine

## Historical Significance
The understanding of glucose evolved from chemical curiosity to central metabolic molecule over 175+ years. Marggraf's isolation (1747) predated any physiological understanding by a century. Bernard's glycogen discovery (1850s) established the liver's role in glucose homeostasis. Fischer's chemical work (1900s) enabled precise understanding. The insulin discovery (1921) finally integrated glucose chemistry with clinical medicine. This progression illustrates how fundamental biochemistry enables clinical breakthroughs.
