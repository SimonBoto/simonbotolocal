# Sirolimus Impairs GLUT4 Translocation Causing Peripheral [[KEYWORD-Insulin|insulin]] Resistance

**Core Mechanism:** mTORC1 is downstream of Akt in insulin signaling and facilitates GLUT4 vesicle translocation. Sirolimus disrupts this pathway, reducing glucose uptake.

## Normal Insulin-GLUT4 Pathway

**Signal Cascade:**
```
Insulin → Insulin Receptor (autophosphorylation)
              ↓
        IRS-1/2 phosphorylation
              ↓
        PI3K activation
              ↓
        PIP3 generation
              ↓
        PDK1 + mTORC2 → Akt activation
              ↓
        ┌─────────────┴─────────────┐
        ↓                           ↓
    AS160 phosphorylation      mTORC1 activation
    (Rab-GAP inhibition)            ↓
        ↓                    Protein synthesis,
    GLUT4 vesicle          [[KEYWORD-Metabolism|metabolism]] regulation
    translocation
        ↓
    GLUT4 on membrane
        ↓
    Glucose uptake
```

**GLUT4 Translocation Steps:**
1. Insulin binds receptor
2. Signaling cascade activates Akt
3. Akt phosphorylates AS160 (inactivates Rab-GAP)
4. Active Rab proteins promote vesicle trafficking
5. GLUT4 vesicles fuse with plasma membrane
6. Glucose enters cell

## Sirolimus Disruption

**Direct mTORC1 Inhibition:**
- Sirolimus binds FKBP12
- Complex inhibits mTORC1 kinase activity
- Downstream signaling blocked

**Consequences for GLUT4:**
- Impaired AS160 phosphorylation (partial)
- Reduced Rab protein activation
- Defective vesicle trafficking
- Decreased GLUT4 on cell surface

## Tissue-Specific Effects

### Skeletal Muscle (Major Site)

**Normal:**
- ~80% of insulin-stimulated glucose disposal
- High GLUT4 expression
- Primary target for glucose homeostasis

**Sirolimus Effect:**
- Reduced glucose uptake
- Peripheral insulin resistance
- Post-prandial hyperglycemia

### Adipose Tissue

**Normal:**
- Glucose uptake for lipogenesis
- GLUT4-dependent insulin sensitivity

**Sirolimus Effect:**
- Impaired glucose uptake
- Altered adipokine production
- Lipotoxicity from increased FFA release

### Cardiac Muscle

**Normal:**
- High GLUT4 expression
- Metabolic flexibility (glucose/fatty acids)

**Sirolimus Effect:**
- Cardiac insulin resistance
- Reduced metabolic flexibility
- Potential cardiomyopathy

## Quantitative Impact

**Glucose Uptake Reduction:**
- 30-50% decrease in insulin-stimulated glucose uptake
- Fasting glucose: +15-30 mg/dL typical
- Post-prandial glucose: more pronounced elevation

**HOMA-IR Increase:**
- 20-50% increase in insulin resistance index
- Indicates reduced insulin sensitivity

## Distinction from Type 2 Diabetes

**Typical T2DM:**
- Multiple mechanisms (IR, β-cell failure, [[KEYWORD-Inflammation|inflammation]])
- Gradual onset
- Often associated with obesity

**Sirolimus-Induced:**
- Primarily peripheral IR from GLUT4 defect
- Faster onset (weeks to months)
- Can occur without obesity
- Reversible with dose reduction/discontinuation
