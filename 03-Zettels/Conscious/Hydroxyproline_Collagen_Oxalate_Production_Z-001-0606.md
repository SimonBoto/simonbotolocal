## Hydroxyproline Structure

**Modified amino acid:** Proline + hydroxyl group  
**Location:** Primarily in collagen (10% of collagen amino acids)  
**Function:** Stabilizes collagen triple helix structure

## Metabolic Pathway to Oxalate

**Collagen turnover releases hydroxyproline:**
```
Hydroxyproline → 4-Hydroxy-2-oxoglutarate → Glyoxylate → Oxalate
```

**Key enzymes:**
- Hydroxyproline oxidase
- 4-hydroxy-2-oxoglutarate aldolase
- Glyoxylate reductase/oxidase

## Quantitative Significance

**Endogenous oxalate production:**
- ~10-20% from hydroxyproline 
- Increases with high collagen turnover:
  - Growth periods
  - Wound healing
  - High meat intake (gelatin/collagen)
  - Collagen supplement use

## Dietary Sources of Hydroxyproline

| Source | Hydroxyproline Content |
|--------|------------------------|
| **Collagen supplements** | Very high |
| **Gelatin** | Very high |
| **Bone broth** | High |
| **Meat with connective tissue** | Moderate |
| **Skin, tendons, cartilage** | High |

##  Relevance

**High collagen intake:**
- Bone broth popularity
- Collagen peptide supplements
- May increase oxalate burden
- Relevant for kidney stone formers

**Primary hyperoxaluria Type 2 (PH2):**
- GRHPR gene defect (glyoxylate reductase/hydroxypyruvate reductase)
- Impaired glyoxylate → glycolate
- Glyoxylate shunted to oxalate

## Comparison to Glycine

| Source | % Endogenous Oxalate | Dietary Impact |
|--------|---------------------|----------------|
| **Glycine** | ~50% | High glycine intake |
| **Hydroxyproline** | ~10-20% | Collagen supplements |
| **Vitamin C** | ~10-50% | High-dose supplements |
