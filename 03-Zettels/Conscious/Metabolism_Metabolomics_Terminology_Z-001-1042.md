# Metabolism Metabolomics Terminology

Core methodology terms for metabolomics practice: Metabolism, Metabolites, Metabolomics, Biochemical Pathways, Redox Balance, Energy Flow. These 9 terms define the field.

## Evidence
> "Precise terminology enables precise thinking. In metabolomics, definitions are not academic—they guide clinical decisions." (Metabolomics practice)

## Core Definitions

### Metabolism
**Definition:** The sum of all chemical reactions in the body that maintain life.

**Two categories:**
| Type | Definition | Examples |
|------|------------|----------|
| **Catabolism** | Breaking down molecules for energy | Glycolysis, β-oxidation |
| **Anabolism** | Building molecules for structure | Protein synthesis, glycogenesis |

**Clinical relevance:** Metabolic rate determines energy needs; metabolic flexibility determines adaptability.

### Metabolites
**Definition:** Small molecules involved in metabolism—intermediates and products of metabolic reactions.

**Categories:**
| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| **Amino acids** | Building blocks of proteins |
| **Organic acids** | Krebs cycle intermediates |
| **Fatty acids** | Lipid components, energy source |
| **Sugars** | Glucose, fructose, lactate |
| **Nucleotides** | DNA/RNA building blocks |

**Clinical relevance:** Metabolite levels reveal pathway function.

### Metabolomics
**Definition:** The comprehensive study of metabolites in biological systems.

**Types:**
| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Targeted** | Measures specific, known metabolites (e.g., OAT) |
| **Untargeted** | Measures all detectable metabolites |
| **Fluxomics** | Measures metabolic pathway rates |

**Clinical application:** OAT (organic acids test) is targeted metabolomics for clinical practice.

### Biochemical Pathways
**Definition:** Series of enzyme-catalyzed reactions that transform substrates into products.

**Key pathways in metabolomics:**
| Pathway | Function |
|---------|----------|
| **Glycolysis** | Glucose → Pyruvate |
| **Krebs cycle** | Acetyl-CoA → Energy + CO₂ |
| **β-oxidation** | Fatty acids → Acetyl-CoA |
| **Pentose phosphate** | Glucose → Ribose + NADPH |
| **Urea cycle** | Ammonia → Urea |

**Clinical relevance:** Pathway blockages appear as metabolite elevations.

### Redox Balance
**Definition:** Balance between oxidation (electron loss) and reduction (electron gain).

**Key molecules:**
| Oxidized | Reduced | Function |
|----------|---------|----------|
| NAD⁺ | NADH | Energy transfer |
| FAD | FADH₂ | Energy transfer |
| GSSG | GSH | Antioxidant defense |

**Clinical relevance:** Redox imbalance → oxidative stress → cellular damage.

### Energy Flow
**Definition:** Movement of energy through metabolic pathways to produce ATP.

**Flow diagram:**
```
Food → Digestion → Absorption → Metabolism → ATP → Work
```

**Efficiency factors:**
- Mitochondrial function
- Nutrient availability
- Oxygen supply
- Enzyme activity

**Clinical relevance:** Energy flow disruptions cause fatigue, dysfunction.

## Additional Key Terms

### Substrate
**Definition:** Molecule acted upon by an enzyme; starting material for a reaction.

### Enzyme
**Definition:** Protein catalyst that speeds biochemical reactions.

### Cofactor
**Definition:** Non-protein molecule required for enzyme activity (e.g., B-vitamins, minerals).

### Intermediate
**Definition:** Metabolite formed in one reaction and consumed in another.

### End Product
**Definition:** Final metabolite of a pathway (e.g., CO₂, urea, ATP).

### Analyte
**Definition:** Substance being measured in a laboratory test.

### Reference Range
**Definition:** Range of values for a healthy population; not necessarily optimal.

## Clinical Integration

### Reading an OAT
| Metabolite | Pathway | Interpretation |
|------------|---------|----------------|
| **Lactate** | Glycolysis | Anaerobic metabolism |
| **Citrate** | Krebs cycle | Mitochondrial function |
| **Methylmalonate** | B12-dependent | B12 status |
| **Indican** | Gut bacteria | Protein digestion |

### Pattern Recognition
| Pattern | Metabolites | Interpretation |
|---------|-------------|----------------|
| **Mitochondrial** | ↑ Lactate, pyruvate | Energy dysfunction |
| **B-vitamin** | ↑ MMA, FIGLU | Cofactor deficiency |
| **Gut** | ↑ Indican, HPHPA | Dysbiosis |
