## Core Principle

The hypothalamic-pituitary-gonadal axis in females orchestrates the menstrual cycle through precisely timed hormonal fluctuations that prepare the body for potential pregnancy.

## Cycle Phases

- **Follicular phase**: Rising FSH stimulates follicular development; estradiol increases
- **Ovulation**: Estradiol positive feedback triggers LH surge
- **Luteal phase**: Corpus luteum produces progesterone and estradiol
- **Menstruation**: Hormone withdrawal triggers endometrial shedding

## Hormonal Dynamics

- **Estradiol**: Rises during follicular phase, triggers LH surge, maintains endometrium
- **Progesterone**: Dominant in luteal phase, prepares endometrium, suppresses further ovulation
- **LH**: Surge triggers ovulation; luteinizing action maintains corpus luteum
- **FSH**: Stimulates follicular development; suppressed during luteal phase

## Clinical Assessment

- **Cycle tracking**: Timing of ovulation, luteal phase length
- **Hormone testing**: Day 3 (FSH, LH, estradiol), mid-luteal (progesterone)
- **Ovulation confirmation**: Progesterone level >3 ng/mL
- **Cycle disorders**: PCOS, hypothalamic amenorrhea, premature ovarian insufficiency

## Key Research Findings

- The LH surge requires sustained estradiol levels >200 pg/mL for >48 hours
- Progesterone production by the corpus luteum is essential for endometrial receptivity
- Follicular phase length is more variable than luteal phase length (fixed at ~14 days)
