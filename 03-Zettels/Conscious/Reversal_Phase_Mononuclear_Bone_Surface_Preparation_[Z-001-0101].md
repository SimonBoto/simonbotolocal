# Reversal Phase — Mononuclear Cells Prepare Bone Surface

## Core Principle
The **reversal phase** bridges bone resorption and formation, with mononuclear cells clearing debris and preparing the surface for new osteoid deposition.

## Cellular Components

### Mononuclear Cells Present
1. **Pre-osteoblasts** — Mesenchymal-derived, recruited to site
2. **Macrophages** — Clear apoptotic osteoclasts and matrix debris
3. **Reverse cells** — Specialized cells of unclear lineage

### Functions
| Function |  |
|----------|-----------|
| **Debris clearance** | Phagocytosis of collagen fragments, dead osteoclasts |
| **Surface preparation** | Demineralization of residual mineral |
| **Cement line deposition** | Glycoprotein-rich boundary layer formation |
| **Coupling signaling** | Release factors linking resorption to formation |

## The Cement Line

### Composition
- Rich in osteopontin, bone sialoprotein, proteoglycans
- No collagen (unlike lamellar bone)
- Glycosylated proteins create boundary layer

### Function
- **Mechanical:** Interface between old and new bone
- **Adhesive:** Site for osteoblast attachment
- **Barrier:** Prevents hypermineralization of old bone edge

## Coupling Mechanism (Resorption → Formation)

### Signal Release from Resorption
Factors released during bone degradation signal formation to begin:

| Factor | Source | Effect on Formation |
|--------|--------|---------------------|
| **TGF-β** | Bone matrix (latent) | Released by acid, recruits osteoblast precursors |
| **IGF-1** | Bone matrix | Stimulates osteoblast proliferation |
| **BMPs** | Degraded matrix | Induce osteoblast differentiation |
| **S1P (Sphingosine-1-phosphate)** | Osteoclasts | Promotes precursor migration |
| **Semaphorins** | Osteoclasts | Regulate osteoblast differentiation |

###  Significance of Coupling
- **Normal coupling:** Resorption balanced by equivalent formation
- **Uncoupling in osteoporosis:** High resorption, inadequate formation response
- **Anabolic therapy target:** Enhance formation signal independent of resorption

## Timeline
- **Duration:** Days to 1 week
- **Critical transition:** Must complete before formation can initiate
- **Rate-limiting:** Coupling signals determine formation magnitude
