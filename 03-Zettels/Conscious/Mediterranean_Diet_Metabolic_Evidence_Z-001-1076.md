## Core Claim
**The Mediterranean diet — characterized by high olive oil, nuts, fish, fruits, vegetables, and whole grains with moderate wine consumption — has demonstrated significant cardiovascular and metabolic health benefits in randomized controlled trials including PREDIMED, providing evidence-based dietary recommendations for metabolic syndrome and cardiovascular risk reduction.**

## Evidence

### PREDIMED Trial Results
| Outcome | Mediterranean Diet | Control | Hazard Ratio |
|---------|-------------------|---------|--------------|
| **Major CV events** | 3.8% | 4.4% | 0.70 (30% reduction) |
| **Myocardial infarction** | 1.5% | 2.1% | 0.69 |
| **Stroke** | 1.7% | 2.0% | 0.61 |
| **CV death** | 1.2% | 1.6% | 0.71 |
| **All-cause mortality** | 2.7% | 3.4% | 0.73 |

### Mediterranean Diet Components
| Component | Frequency | Metabolic Impact |
|-----------|-----------|-----------------|
| **Olive oil (EVOO)** | Daily (>4 tbsp) | MUFA, polyphenols |
| **Nuts** | 3+ servings/week | Healthy fats, fiber |
| **Fatty fish** | 2-3 servings/week | Omega-3s, protein |
| **Fruits** | 3+ servings/day | Antioxidants, fiber |
| **Vegetables** | 2+ servings/day | Fiber, micronutrients |
| **Whole grains** | Daily | Fiber, B-vitamins |
| **Legumes** | 3+ servings/week | Fiber, plant protein |
| **Wine** | Moderate (1-2 glasses/day) | Polyphenols |

### Metabolic Mechanisms
| Mechanism | Effect | Evidence |
|-----------|--------|----------|
| **Improved insulin sensitivity** | Lower HOMA-IR | Metabolic studies |
| **Reduced inflammation** | Lower CRP, IL-6 | PREDIMED subanalysis |
| **Improved lipid profile** | ↑ HDL, ↓ TG | Consistent finding |
| **Blood pressure reduction** | ↓ systolic/diastolic | RCT evidence |
| **Endothelial function** | ↑ flow-mediated dilation | Mechanistic studies |

### Special Population Studies
| Population | Intervention | Outcome | Evidence |
|------------|-------------|---------|----------|
| **Pediatric asthma** | Mediterranean + fatty fish | Improved lung function | Papamichael 2018 |
| **Metabolic syndrome** | Mediterranean diet | Reversal rates | Multiple RCTs |
| **T2DM** | Mediterranean diet | Improved glycemic control | Strong evidence |
| **NAFLD** | Mediterranean diet | Reduced hepatic fat | Emerging |

### Dietary Pattern vs. Single Nutrient
| Approach | Outcome | Evidence |
|----------|---------|----------|
| **Dietary pattern** | Synergistic effects | PREDIMED superiority |
| **Single nutrient** | Isolated effects | Limited impact |
| **Food synergy** | Combined benefits | Mechanistic studies |

## Clinical Implication
- **CVD prevention:** 30% reduction in major cardiovascular events
- **Metabolic health:** Improves insulin sensitivity, lipids, BP
- **Sustainability:** Culturally adaptable, enjoyable diet
- **Evidence base:** Strong RCT support
- **METHAP relevance:** Mediterranean diet is evidence-based intervention for metabolic syndrome; serves as control or comparator arm in research
