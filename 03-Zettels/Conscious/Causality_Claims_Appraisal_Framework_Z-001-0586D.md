# Causality Claims Appraisal Framework

> **Core Claim:** Hierarchy of evidence and Bradford Hill criteria prevent overinterpretation of observational studies.

## Evidence Hierarchy (for Causality)

| Rank | Design | Causal Strength |
|------|--------|-----------------|
| 1 | RCTs | Strongest |
| 2 | Cohort studies | Moderate |
| 3 | Case-control | Weak-moderate |
| 4 | Cross-sectional | Weakest |

## Bradford Hill Criteria

| Criterion | Question |
|-----------|----------|
| **Temporal relationship** | Does cause precede effect? |
| **Dose-response** | Greater exposure → greater effect? |
| **Biological plausibility** | Mechanism makes sense? |
| **Consistency** | Replicated across studies? |
| **Specificity** | One cause → one effect? |
| **Experiment** | Does intervention change outcome? |

## Common Pitfall

> **Observational data cannot prove causation** — only association.

## Critical Appraisal Questions

- Is the study design appropriate for causal claims?
- Are confounders adequately addressed?
- Do the authors overstate their conclusions?
