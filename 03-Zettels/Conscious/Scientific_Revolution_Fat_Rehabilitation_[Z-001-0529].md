# Scientific R — Fat Rehabilitation

> Starting in the 2000s, accumulating evidence challenged the diet-heart hypothesis, rehabilitating dietary fat and shifting focus to carbohydrate quality.

## Key Evidence

**WHI (2006):**
- 48,835 women, 8.1 years
- Low-fat diet: **No CVD benefit**

**Meta-Analyses:**
- Siri-Tarino (2010): No SFA-CHD association
- Chowdhury (2014): No total SFA-CVD link
- Skeaff & Miller: No fat reduction benefit

## Saturated Fat Rehabilitation

**Dairy Fat:**
- Full-fat not linked to CVD
- Fermented dairy beneficial

**Coconut Oil:**
- MCTs different 
- Raises HDL

**Red Meat:**
- Processed vs. unprocessed differs
- Whole pattern matters

## Carbohydrate Shift

**Glycemic Index:**
- Refined carbs problematic
- May matter more than fat

**Sugar Exposed:**
- Linked to CVD independent of fat
- Industry influence revealed

**Low-Carb Validated:**
- Ketogenic for metabolic disease
- Carb restriction effective

## PREDIMED (2013)

**Result:** Mediterranean + olive oil/nuts
- **30% CVD reduction**
- **Fat quality > quantity**
