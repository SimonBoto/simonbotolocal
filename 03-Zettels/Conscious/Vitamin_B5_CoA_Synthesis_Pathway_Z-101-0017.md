## Five-Step Biosynthesis

### Step 1: Phosphorylation (Rate-Limiting)
**Pantothenic acid + ATP → 4'-phosphopantothenate + ADP**
- **Enzyme:** Pantothenate kinase (PanK)
- **Significance:** Regulatory step — inhibited by CoA and acyl-CoAs
- **Location:** Cytosol

### Step 2: Cysteine Addition
**4'-phosphopantothenate + Cysteine + ATP → 4'-phosphopantothenoylcysteine + ADP + Pi**
- **Enzyme:** Phosphopantothenoylcysteine synthetase (PPCS)
- **Significance:** Introduces sulfhydryl group (becomes reactive thiol in CoA)

### Step 3: Decarboxylation
**4'-phosphopantothenoylcysteine → 4'-phosphopantetheine + CO₂**
- **Enzyme:** Phosphopanthenoylcysteine decarboxylase (PPCDC)
- **Cofactor:** Mg²⁺
- **Significance:** Removes carboxyl, creates pantetheine backbone

### Step 4: Adenylation
**4'-phosphopantetheine + ATP → dephospho-CoA + PPi**
- **Enzyme:** Pantetheine phosphate adenylyltransferase (PPAT)
- **Significance:** Adds adenosine moiety (from ATP)

### Step 5: Phosphorylation
**dephospho-CoA + ATP → CoA + ADP**
- **Enzyme:** Dephospho-CoA kinase (DPCK)
- **Significance:** Final phosphate addition completes CoA structure

## CoA Structure

CoA consists of:
1. **Pantothenic acid** (vitamin B5) — core
2. **Cysteamine** (from cysteine) — provides reactive thiol (-SH)
3. **3'-phosphate ADP** — energy handle

**Reactive site:** Terminal thiol group (-SH) forms thioester bonds with acyl groups

##  Disorders

### PKAN (Pantothenate Kinase-Associated Neurodegeneration)
- **Gene:** PANK2 mutations
- **Defect:** Step 1 enzyme deficiency
- **Features:** Iron brain accumulation, dystonia, "eye of tiger" MRI
- **Note:** Not dietary deficiency — enzymatic defect

### Dietary B5 Deficiency
- **Extremely rare** (widespread in foods)
- **Symptoms:** Fatigue, paresthesias, GI issues
- **Treatment:** Oral pantothenic acid
