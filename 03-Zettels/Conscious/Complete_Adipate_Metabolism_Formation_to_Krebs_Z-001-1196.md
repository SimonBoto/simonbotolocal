## Core Claim
**Adipate (C6 dicarboxylic acid) undergoes complete metabolism through two cycles of β-oxidation — first to glutaryl-CoA (C5) releasing acetyl-CoA, then to succinyl-CoA (C4) releasing a second acetyl-CoA — with succinyl-CoA directly entering the Krebs cycle, demonstrating that adipate is not merely a waste product but an energy-yielding intermediate when metabolic capacity is not overwhelmed.**

## Evidence

### Metabolic Pathway Overview
| Stage | Input | Output | Location |
|-------|-------|--------|----------|
| **Formation** | Palmitate (impaired β-ox) | Adipate | ER (ω-oxidation) |
| **Activation** | Adipate | Adipyl-CoA | Mitochondria |
| **First β-ox cycle** | Adipyl-CoA (C6) | Glutaryl-CoA (C5) + Acetyl-CoA | Mitochondria |
| **Second β-ox cycle** | Glutaryl-CoA (C5) | Succinyl-CoA (C4) + Acetyl-CoA | Mitochondria |
| **Krebs entry** | Succinyl-CoA | Energy (GTP, NADH, FADH2) | Mitochondria |

### Step-by-Step Metabolism
**First β-Oxidation Cycle (C6 → C5):**
```
Adipyl-CoA + FAD → Trans-enoyl-adipyl-CoA + FADH2
↓ + H2O
L-3-Hydroxyadipyl-CoA
↓ + NAD+
3-Ketoadipyl-CoA
↓ + CoA
Glutaryl-CoA (C5) + Acetyl-CoA
```

**Second β-Oxidation Cycle (C5 → C4):**
```
Glutaryl-CoA + FAD → Trans-enoyl-glutaryl-CoA + FADH2
↓ + H2O
L-3-Hydroxyglutaryl-CoA
↓ + NAD+
3-Ketoglutaryl-CoA
↓ + CoA
Succinyl-CoA (C4) + Acetyl-CoA
```

### Energy Yield
| Product | Quantity | ATP Equivalent |
|---------|----------|----------------|
| Succinyl-CoA | 1 | ~12 ATP (through Krebs) |
| Acetyl-CoA | 2 | ~20 ATP total |
| FADH2 | 2 | ~3 ATP |
| NADH | 2 | ~5 ATP |
| **Total** | — | **~32-40 ATP** |

### Why Adipate Appears in Urine
| Scenario | Metabolic Capacity | Urinary Adipate |
|----------|-------------------|-----------------|
| **Normal** | Fully metabolized | Not elevated |
| **Mild stress** | Partially metabolized | Low elevation |
| **Severe impairment** | Overwhelmed | High elevation |

## Clinical Implication
- **Not just waste:** Adipate is metabolically active
- **Capacity indicator:** Urinary levels reflect metabolic reserve
- **Energy recovery:** ~80% of theoretical maximum recovered
- **Intervention target:** Supporting metabolism reduces adipate excretion
- **METHAP relevance:** Adipate metabolism capacity indicates metabolic flexibility
