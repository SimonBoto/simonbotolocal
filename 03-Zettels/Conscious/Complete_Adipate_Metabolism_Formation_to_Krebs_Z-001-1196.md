# Complete Adipate  — Formation to Krebs Entry

## Overview

Adipate is not just a waste product—it's a metabolically active intermediate. This zettel traces the complete journey from fatty acid to energy production.

---

## Part 1: Formation (When β-Oxidation Impaired)

```
Palmitate (C16) → [β-oxidation impairment]
          ↓
Hexanoyl-CoA (C6) accumulation
          ↓
[ω-oxidation in ER]
          ↓
Adipate (C6 dicarboxylic acid)
```

**Key Point:** Formation indicates metabolic stress, not endpoint

---

## Part 2: Activation

```
Adipate + CoA + ATP → Adipyl-CoA + AMP + PPi
```

- Location: l matrix or peroxisomes
- Enzyme: Broad-specificity acyl-CoA synthetase
- Requires: Coenzyme A (B5-dependent)

---

## Part 3: β-Oxidation of Adipate

### First Cycle
```
Adipyl-CoA (C6) → Glutaryl-CoA (C5) + Acetyl-CoA
```

Products:
- 1 FADH₂ (~1.5 ATP)
- 1 NADH (~2.5 ATP)
- 1 Acetyl-CoA (~10 ATP via Krebs)

### Second Cycle
```
Glutaryl-CoA (C5) → Succinyl-CoA (C4) + Acetyl-CoA
```

Products:
- 1 FADH₂ (~1.5 ATP)
- 1 NADH (~2.5 ATP)
- 1 Acetyl-CoA (~10 ATP via Krebs)

---

## Part 4: Entry into Krebs Cycle

### Succinyl-CoA → Succinate
```
Succinyl-CoA + GDP + Pi → Succinate + GTP + CoA
```

- GTP = ATP equivalent
- Direct Krebs cycle intermediate

### Remaining Krebs Cycle
```
Succinate → Fumarate → Malate → Oxaloacetate
          ↓
Citrate → Isocitrate → α-ketoglutarate → [back to succinyl-CoA]
```

Produces:
- 1 FADH₂ (from succinate dehydrogenase)
- 1 NADH (from malate dehydrogenase)

---

## Part 5: Complete Oxidation

### Total ATP from One Adipate

| Source | ATP Yield |
|--------|-----------|
| 2 rounds β-oxidation | 8 ATP (2 NADH + 2 FADH₂) |
| 2 Acetyl-CoA | 20 ATP (× 2 via Krebs) |
| Succinyl-CoA → complete Krebs | 12 ATP |
| **Total** | **~40 ATP** |

### Comparison
- **Glucose (C6):** ~30-32 ATP
- **Adipate (C6):** ~40 ATP
- **Efficiency:** Dicarboxylic pathway yields MORE energy!

---

##  Implications

### Why Adipate Accumulates
If adipate produces energy, why does it appear in urine?

**Answer:** Capacity limitation

```
Production Rate > Metabolism Capacity
          ↓
Overflow to urine
```

### Urinary Level Interpretation
| Level | Interpretation |
|-------|----------------|
| Normal (< 20 mg/g) | Balance maintained |
| Mild elevation | Early stress, compensation working |
| High | Overwhelmed system |
| Very high | Severe genetic or metabolic disorder |

---

## The Complete Picture

```
PALMITATE (C16)
      ↓ [β-oxidation]
HEXANOYL-CoA (C6) ————→ If impaired → [ω-oxidation]
      ↓                              ↓
Complete oxidation              ADIPATE (C6)
(to CO₂)                            ↓
                              [dicarboxylic pathway]
                                    ↓
                              SUCCINYL-CoA
                                    ↓
                              KREBS CYCLE
                                    ↓
                              CO₂ + H₂O + ATP
```

**Key Insight:** Same endpoint, different route based on metabolic health
