# Erectile Dysfunction Hormonal Workup — Diagnostic Algorithm

## Overview

Erectile dysfunction (ED) affects approximately 40% of men by age 40, with prevalence increasing with age. While psychological and vascular factors are common, hormonal evaluation is essential in the workup.

## When to Evaluate Hormones in ED

### Indications for Hormonal Testing

**All men with ED should have:**
- Testosterone (morning sample)
- Consider prolactin

**Additional testing if:**
- Low libido present
- Other hypogonadal symptoms (fatigue, decreased muscle mass)
- Gynecomastia
- Infertility
- Signs of pituitary disease (headaches, visual changes)

## Essential Laboratory Tests

### Tier 1: Basic Evaluation (All Patients)

**1. Total Testosterone:**
- **Timing:** Morning (7-11 AM), fasting preferred
- **Why:** Diurnal variation (highest morning)
- **Repeat:** If low, repeat to confirm

**2. Prolactin:**
- **Why:** Hyperprolactinemia causes ED via hypogonadism
- **When:** All men with ED + low libido, or unexplained ED

**3. Fasting Glucose/HbA1c:**
- **Why:**  major cause of ED (vascular + neuropathy)
- **Prevalence:** 50% of diabetic men have ED

**4. Lipid Panel:**
- **Why:** Atherosclerosis affects penile vessels
- **Cardiovascular risk stratification**

### Tier 2: Extended Evaluation (Selected Patients)

**5. Free or Bioavailable Testosterone:**
- **When:** Total T borderline or discordant with symptoms
- **SHBG-dependent:** Obesity lowers SHBG

**6. FSH and LH:**
- **When:** Low testosterone confirmed
- **Purpose:** Distinguish primary vs secondary hypogonadism

**7. Thyroid Function (TSH):**
- **When:** Symptoms suggest thyroid dysfunction
- **Both hyper- and hypothyroidism can cause ED**

**8. Estradiol:**
- **When:** Gynecomastia, obesity, suspected aromatase excess

### Tier 3: Specialized Testing (Specific Indications)

**9. DHEA-S:**
- **When:** Suspected adrenal insufficiency, adrenal tumor

**10. ACTH Stimulation Test:**
- **When:** Suspected adrenal insufficiency
- **Not routine for ED**

**11. Nocturnal Penile Tumescence (NPT):**
- **When:** Distinguish psychogenic vs organic
- **Not blood test — physiological measurement**

**12. Penile Doppler Ultrasound:**
- **When:** Suspected vascular insufficiency
- **Specialized testing**

## Interpretation Guide

### Testosterone Patterns

| Total T (ng/dL) | Interpretation | Action |
|-----------------|----------------|--------|
| **>400** | Normal | Unlikely cause of ED |
| **300-400** | Borderline | Repeat; check symptoms; consider free T |
| **200-300** | Low | Confirm with repeat; check FSH/LH |
| **<200** | Deficient | Hypogonadism likely; full workup |

**Note:** Symptomatic threshold varies individually

### Prolactin Patterns

| Prolactin (ng/mL) | Interpretation | Action |
|-------------------|----------------|--------|
| **<20** | Normal | No action |
| **20-100** | Mild elevation | Rule out drugs, hypothyroidism; recheck |
| **100-200** | Moderate | Pituitary MRI if persistent |
| **>200** | High | Strongly suggests prolactinoma; MRI |

### FSH/LH Patterns in Low Testosterone

| Pattern | FSH/LH | Diagnosis | Next Steps |
|---------|--------|-----------|------------|
| **High FSH/LH** | Elevated | Primary hypogonadism | Karyotype if young; testicular evaluation |
| **Low/normal FSH/LH** | Low/normal | Secondary hypogonadism | Prolactin, MRI pituitary, iron studies |

## Special Populations

### Young Men (<40) with ED

**Higher suspicion for:**
- Hypogonadism (primary or secondary)
- Pituitary tumors
- Klinefelter syndrome

**Evaluation:**
- Full hormonal workup even if isolated ED
- Karyotype if hypogonadism confirmed
- Consider genetic testing

### Older Men (>65) with ED

**Common causes:**
- Vascular disease
- Diabetes
- Medications
- Age-related testosterone decline

**Evaluation:**
- Basic labs sufficient initially
- Cardiovascular risk assessment
- Medication review critical

### Men with Low Libido

**Hormonal cause likely if:**
- Low testosterone
- High prolactin
- Both libido and ED present

**Non-hormonal if:**
- Normal hormones
- ED but preserved libido (more likely vascular)

## Treatment Based on Hormonal Findings

### Low Testosterone (Hypogonadism)

**Testosterone Replacement Therapy (TRT):**
- Indicated if symptomatic + confirmed low T
- Benefits: Improved libido, erectile function (partial), energy, muscle
- Limitations: May not fully resolve ED (often multifactorial)

**Response to TRT:**
- Libido improves in 3-6 weeks
- Erectile function: 40-60% improvement
- Often need PDE5 inhibitors concurrently

### Hyperprolactinemia

**Treatment:**
- Dopamine agonists (cabergoline preferred)
- Testosterone normalizes with prolactin reduction
- ED usually resolves

### Normal Hormones

**Management:**
- PDE5 inhibitors (sildenafil, tadalafil, vardenafil)
- Address vascular risk factors
- Lifestyle modification
- Psychotherapy if psychogenic component

## Key Clinical Pearls

1. **Morning testosterone:** Always check 7-11 AM
2. **Repeat low values:** One low testosterone requires confirmation
3. **Prolactin in low libido:** Don't miss prolactinoma
4. **Check glucose:** Diabetes is common reversible cause
5. **SHBG in obesity:** Free T may be normal despite low total
6. **TRT not panacea:** Many men need PDE5 inhibitors even with TRT
7. **Address cardiovascular risk:** ED often early marker of CVD

## Summary

**Essential ED Workup:**
1. Morning total testosterone
2. Prolactin
3. Glucose/HbA1c
4. Lipid panel

**Extended if T low:**
- FSH, LH (differentiate primary/secondary)
- Free T if SHBG altered

**Specialized:**
- Thyroid, DHEA-S (selected cases)
- Imaging (vascular assessment if indicated)

**Clinical Pearl:** ED + low libido → Check prolactin. Normal hormones doesn't exclude vascular or psychogenic ED.
