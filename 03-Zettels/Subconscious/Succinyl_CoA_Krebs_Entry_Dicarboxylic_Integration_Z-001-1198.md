# Succinyl-CoA Krebs Entry — Dicarboxylic Acid Integration

## Core Claim
**Succinyl-CoA — produced from adipate through two cycles of β-oxidation — enters the Krebs cycle at the succinyl-CoA synthetase step, generating GTP (substrate-level phosphorylation) and continuing through the remaining cycle steps to produce additional NADH and FADH2, fully integrating dicarboxylic acid metabolism into mitochondrial energy production.**

## Evidence

### Succinyl-CoA in the Krebs Cycle
| Step | Reaction | Enzyme | Product |
|------|----------|--------|---------|
| **Entry** | Succinyl-CoA → Succinate | Succinyl-CoA synthetase | GTP + CoA |
| **Oxidation** | Succinate → Fumarate | Succinate dehydrogenase | FADH2 |
| **Hydration** | Fumarate → Malate | Fumarase | — |
| **Oxidation** | Malate → Oxaloacetate | Malate dehydrogenase | NADH |

### Energy from Succinyl-CoA Entry
| Product | ATP Equivalent | Source |
|---------|----------------|--------|
| **GTP** | 1 ATP | Substrate-level phosphorylation |
| **FADH2** | ~1.5 ATP | Succinate dehydrogenase (Complex II) |
| **NADH** | ~2.5 ATP | Malate dehydrogenase |
| **Total** | **~5 ATP** | Per succinyl-CoA |

### Integration with Dicarboxylic Acid Pathway
| Source | Succinyl-CoA Production | Context |
|--------|------------------------|---------|
| **Adipate** | Via β-oxidation of C6 dicarboxylic acid | Impaired β-ox compensation |
| **Odd-chain fatty acids** | Propionyl-CoA → Succinyl-CoA | Normal metabolism |
| **Amino acids** | Isoleucine, methionine, valine | Protein catabolism |
| **Krebs cycle** | α-ketoglutarate → Succinyl-CoA | Normal cycle |

### Anaplerotic Role
| Function | Significance |
|----------|--------------|
| **Cycle replenishment** | Succinyl-CoA is anaplerotic (refills Krebs intermediates) |
| **Glucose synthesis** | Succinyl-CoA → oxaloacetate → gluconeogenesis |
| **Heme synthesis** | Succinyl-CoA + glycine → δ-aminolevulinic acid |

## Clinical Implication
- **Full integration:** Dicarboxylic acids complete energy metabolism
- **Anaplerotic support:** Succinyl-CoA helps maintain Krebs cycle function
- **Multiple sources:** Dietary, metabolic, and compensatory pathways converge
- **Metabolic flexibility:** Ability to use multiple substrates indicates health
- **METHAP relevance:** Succinyl-CoA integration demonstrates metabolic pathway redundancy
