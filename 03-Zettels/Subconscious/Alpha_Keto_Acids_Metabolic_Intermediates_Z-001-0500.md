# Alpha-Keto Acids as Metabolic Intermediates

## Core Claim
**α-Keto acids (2-oxo acids) are the carbon skeletons remaining after amino acid deamination — they serve as entry points to central metabolism, with pyruvate, oxaloacetate, and α-ketoglutarate being the primary gateways connecting amino acid catabolism to glycolysis, gluconeogenesis, and the Krebs cycle** — transamination reactions (all B6-dependent) interconvert amino acids and α-keto acids reversibly.

## Evidence

### Key α-Keto Acids
| α-Keto Acid | From Amino Acid | Krebs Entry | Classification |
|-------------|-----------------|-------------|----------------|
| **Pyruvate** | Alanine | Pyruvate (→ OAA or acetyl-CoA) | Glucogenic |
| **Oxaloacetate** | Aspartate | Krebs cycle | Glucogenic |
| **α-Ketoglutarate** | Glutamate | Krebs cycle | Glucogenic |
| **α-Ketoisocaproate** | Leucine | → HMG-CoA | Ketogenic |
| **α-Ketoisovalerate** | Valine | → Succinyl-CoA | Glucogenic |
| **α-Keto-β-methylvalerate** | Isoleucine | → Succinyl-CoA + acetyl-CoA | Both |
| **Phenylpyruvate** | Phenylalanine | → Fumarate + acetoacetate | Both |

### Transamination: The Exchange
```
Amino acid₁ + α-keto acid₂ ↔ α-keto acid₁ + Amino acid₂
```
| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Enzymes** | Transaminases (aminotransferases) |
| **Cofactor** | Vitamin B6 (pyridoxal phosphate, PLP) |
| **Reversibility** | Fully reversible (equilibrium) |
| **No net nitrogen loss** | Just exchange |

### Metabolic Integration
- **Glucogenic α-keto acids:** Enter at pyruvate, OAA, α-KG, succinyl-CoA, fumarate
- **Ketogenic α-keto acids:** Yield acetyl-CoA or acetoacetyl-CoA

## Clinical Implication
- **B6 deficiency:** Impairs all transaminations
- **Maple syrup urine disease:** Defect in branched-chain α-keto acid dehydrogenase
- **α-Keto acid supplements:** Used in renal failure to reduce nitrogen load
- **METHAP relevance:** Understanding α-keto acids explains how amino acids enter energy metabolism; B6 is critical for this interconversion
