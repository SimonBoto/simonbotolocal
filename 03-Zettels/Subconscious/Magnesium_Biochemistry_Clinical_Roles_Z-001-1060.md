# Magnesium Biochemistry Clinical Roles

## Core Claim
**Magnesium serves as an essential cofactor for over 300 enzymatic reactions — including all ATP-dependent processes, DNA repair, muscle relaxation, and nerve conduction — with deficiency producing widespread clinical manifestations from cardiac arrhythmias to seizures and metabolic dysfunction.**

## Evidence

### Magnesium Biochemistry
| Function | Mechanism | Clinical Relevance |
|----------|-----------|-------------------|
| **ATP function** | Mg-ATP complex is true substrate | All energy metabolism |
| **Enzyme cofactor** | >300 enzymes require Mg²⁺ | Glycolysis, TCA, replication |
| **DNA stability** | Stabilizes DNA structure | Repair, replication |
| **Membrane stability** | Regulates ion channels | Nerve conduction, muscle |
| **Calcium antagonist** | Competes with Ca²⁺ | Vascular tone, contraction |

### Key Mg-Dependent Enzymes
| Pathway | Enzyme | Mg²⁺ Role |
|---------|--------|-----------|
| **Glycolysis** | Phosphofructokinase, pyruvate kinase | Catalytic |
| **TCA cycle** | Isocitrate dehydrogenase, α-KGDH | Cofactor |
| **Mitochondria** | ATP synthase | Structural (ATP-Mg) |
| **DNA synthesis** | DNA polymerases | Catalytic |
| **Protein synthesis** | Ribosomal function | Structural |

### Magnesium and Vitamin D
| Interaction | Mechanism | Clinical Impact |
|-------------|-----------|-----------------|
| **D activation** | Mg required for 25-hydroxylation | Low Mg → impaired D status |
| **PTH secretion** | Mg required for PTH release | Severe hypomagnesemia → hypocalcemia |
| **D receptor function** | Mg affects VDR affinity | Combined deficiency common |

### Clinical Deficiency Manifestations
| System | Symptoms | Mechanism |
|--------|----------|-----------|
| **Cardiac** | Arrhythmias, QT prolongation | K⁺/Ca²⁺ channel dysfunction |
| **Neuromuscular** | Tremor, tetany, seizures | NMDA receptor, Ca²⁺ regulation |
| **Metabolic** | Insulin resistance, dyslipidemia | Glucose transport, enzyme function |
| **Psychiatric** | Anxiety, depression, irritability | NMDA, GABA function |
| **Musculoskeletal** | Cramps, weakness | Muscle contraction/relaxation |

### Assessment and Supplementation
| Parameter | Normal Range | Considerations |
|-----------|--------------|----------------|
| **Serum Mg** | 1.7-2.2 mg/dL | Poor tissue status indicator |
| **RBC Mg** | 4.2-6.8 mg/dL | Better tissue status |
| **Ionized Mg** | 0.55-0.75 mmol/L | Physiologically active |
| **Oral supplement** | 200-400 mg elemental | Glycinate, citrate preferred |

## Clinical Implication
- **Ubiquitous need:** Mg involved in virtually all metabolic processes
- **Deficiency common:** Dietary insufficiency, medications (PPIs, diuretics)
- **Cardiac risk:** Arrhythmias with severe deficiency
- **Metabolic health:** Mg improves insulin sensitivity
- **METHAP relevance:** Magnesium status affects glucose metabolism and insulin sensitivity; supplementation is low-cost intervention for metabolic syndrome
