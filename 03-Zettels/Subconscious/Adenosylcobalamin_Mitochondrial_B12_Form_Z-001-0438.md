# Adenosylcobalamin — The Mitochondrial B12 Form

## Core Claim
**Adenosylcobalamin (5'-deoxyadenosylcobalamin) is the mitochondrial form of vitamin B12, characterized by a unique cobalt-carbon bond that enables radical chemistry for methylmalonyl-CoA mutase** — this distinguishes it from methylcobalamin (cytosolic, methylation) and makes it essential for energy metabolism.

## Evidence

### Structure
| Feature | Significance |
|---------|--------------|
| **Corrin ring** | Cobalt coordination (similar to heme) |
| **Co-C bond** | **Unique** — only B12 form with covalent cobalt-carbon bond |
| **5'-Deoxyadenosyl** | Source of radical for catalysis |

### Synthesis
```
Hydroxocobalamin → [mitochondria] → Adenosylcobalamin
```
- **Enzyme:** ATP:corrinoid adenosyltransferase
- **Requires:** ATP
- **Location:** Mitochondrial matrix

### Function
- **Cofactor for:** Methylmalonyl-CoA mutase
- **Mechanism:** Radical-based carbon skeleton rearrangement
- **Substrate:** L-methylmalonyl-CoA → succinyl-CoA

### Distinction from Methylcobalamin
| Feature | Adenosylcobalamin | Methylcobalamin |
|---------|-------------------|-----------------|
| **Location** | Mitochondrial | Cytosolic |
| **Enzyme** | Methylmalonyl-CoA mutase | Methionine synthase |
| **Chemistry** | Radical mechanism | Methyl transfer |
| **Function** | Energy metabolism | Methylation |

## Clinical Implication
- **Adenosylcobalamin deficiency** → methylmalonic acidemia → impaired energy from propionate
- **MMA (methylmalonic acid)** is the functional marker of adenosylcobalamin status
- **Some B12 supplements** provide only methylcobalamin — may not correct mitochondrial deficiency
- **METHAP relevance:** Both B12 forms needed; adenosylcobalamin for energy, methylcobalamin for methylation
