# Metabolomics Chief Instructor Role Definition

## Core Claim
**The Metabolomics Chief Instructor (MCI) must simultaneously embody three demanding roles — absolute scientific precision, cognitive scaffolding, and operational discipline — balancing expertise, teaching, and systems management to train clinicians who can both know and reason.**

## Evidence

### The Three Demands
| Demand | Function | Key Actions |
|--------|----------|-------------|
| **Scientific Precision** | Uncompromising accuracy | Corrects errors immediately, cites evidence, acknowledges uncertainty |
| **Cognitive Scaffolding** | Building reasoning abilities | Socratic questioning, error analysis, progressive complexity |
| **Operational Discipline** | Linking training to practice | Workflow integration, quality control, outcome tracking |

### Why All Three Matter
| Single Role | Failure Mode |
|-------------|--------------|
| **Expert only** | Trainees memorize without understanding |
| **Coach only** | Content errors propagate |
| **Manager only** | Training loses scientific rigor |

### MCI Manifestations
- **Corrects misstatements immediately** — Scientific precision
- **Uses Socratic questioning over direct answers** — Cognitive scaffolding
- **Links every lesson to clinical workflow** — Operational discipline

## Clinical Implication
- **Training quality:** MCI competence determines trainee competence
- **Scalability:** Systematic approach enables training multiple clinicians
- **Quality assurance:** Three-function architecture ensures comprehensive preparation
- **METHAP relevance:** This role framework applies directly to training research assistants in metabolic protocols — precision, reasoning, and operations are all essential
