# Carbohydrates Are Not Essential Nutrient

## Core Claim
**Carbohydrates are not essential nutrients** — no deficiency symptoms occur from complete carbohydrate absence, as the body can produce required glucose via gluconeogenesis.

## Essential vs Non-Essential
| Nutrient | Essential | Reason |
|----------|-----------|--------|
| **Proteins** | ✅ YES | Cannot synthesize all amino acids |
| **Fats** | ✅ YES | Cannot synthesize essential fatty acids |
| **Carbohydrates** | ❌ NO | Can synthesize glucose |

## Gluconeogenesis
| Organ | Function |
|-------|----------|
| **Liver** | Primary glucose production |
| **Kidneys** | Secondary production |
| **Substrates** | Protein, glycerol, lactate |

### Tissues That Need Glucose
| Tissue | Alternative Fuel |
|--------|------------------|
| **Brain** | Ketones (60-70% after adaptation) |
| **RBCs** | No mitochondria — glucose only |
| **Eyes** | Glucose preferred |

## Clinical Implication
| Approach | Validity |
|----------|----------|
| **Zero-carb diets** | Biologically possible |
| **Ketogenic diets** | Sustainable long-term |
| **Carb "requirements"** | No minimum intake needed |

## Evidence

> "No deficiency symptoms from complete carbohydrate absence. Brain, RBCs, eyes can get glucose via gluconeogenesis. Proteins and fats ARE essential; carbs are not."
> — Metabolic physiology
