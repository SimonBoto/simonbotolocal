# Five Modifiable Risk Factors Determine 11-14 Extra Years of Life

## Core Claim
The absence of five major modifiable risk factors — **high blood pressure, elevated cholesterol, abnormal body weight, diabetes, and smoking** — is associated with **14.5 additional years of life for women and 11.8 additional years for men**, quantifying the massive impact of lifestyle and dietary interventions on longevity.

## The Five Factors

| Risk Factor | Modification Strategy |
|-------------|----------------------|
| **High blood pressure** | Diet (DASH, low sodium), exercise, weight loss |
| **Elevated cholesterol** | Diet (low carb for metabolic), exercise |
| **Abnormal body weight** | Caloric balance, macronutrient quality |
| **Diabetes** | Carbohydrate restriction, weight loss, exercise |
| **Smoking** | Cessation programs, behavioral support |

## The Longevity Gain

### NEJM 2025 Findings
| Population | Extra Years | Healthy Years |
|------------|-------------|---------------|
| **Women** | +14.5 years | +13.3 years |
| **Men** | +11.8 years | +10.6 years |

### Context
- **Study size:** 2 million people
- **Countries:** 39
- **Follow-up:** Lifetime estimates
- **Key insight:** All factors are modifiable through lifestyle

## Clinical Implications

### Prevention Priority
| Rank | Factor | Impact |
|------|--------|--------|
| 1 | **Smoking cessation** | Greatest single benefit |
| 2 | **Blood pressure control** | Second greatest |
| 3 | **Weight normalization** | Major impact |
| 4 | **Diabetes prevention/remission** | Significant |
| 5 | **Cholesterol** | Modest independent effect |

### The Cumulative Effect
| Factors Addressed | Approximate Benefit |
|-------------------|---------------------|
| 1 factor | 2-3 years |
| 2 factors | 4-6 years |
| 3 factors | 7-9 years |
| 4-5 factors | 11-14 years |

## Evidence

> "NEJM 2025 analysis of 2 million people across 39 countries demonstrates that these five factors, all modifiable through diet and lifestyle, account for the largest preventable burden of disease and mortality; smoking cessation provides greatest benefit, followed by blood pressure control."
> — Global Burden of Disease, 2025
