# Cofactor Deficiency OAT Signatures

## Core Claim
**Organic acid testing reveals specific B-vitamin and mineral deficiencies through characteristic metabolite elevations — pyruvate for B1, methylmalonate for B12, FIGLU for folate — enabling targeted supplementation based on biochemical evidence rather than empiric guessing.**

## Evidence

### OAT Markers and Cofactor Needs
| Marker | Elevated Indicates | Cofactor Needed | Enzyme Affected |
|--------|-------------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| **Pyruvate** | PDH dysfunction | B1 (TPP) | Pyruvate dehydrogenase |
| **Lactate** | Anaerobic shift | B1, B3, Mg²⁺ | LDH, PDH |
| **α-Ketoglutarate** | α-KGDH block | B1, B2, B3, B5, Mg²⁺ | α-KG dehydrogenase |
| **Methylmalonate** | MUT dysfunction | B12 (adenosylcobalamin) | Methylmalonyl-CoA mutase |
| **FIGLU** | Formiminotransferase block | Folate (THF) | FIGLU transferase |
| **Succinate** | Complex II dysfunction | B2 (FAD) | Succinate dehydrogenase |

### Pyruvate Dehydrogenase Complexity
PDH requires 5 cofactors:
| Cofactor | Vitamin Form | Function |
|----------|--------------|----------|
| **TPP** | B1 | Decarboxylation |
| **FAD** | B2 | Electron transfer |
| **NAD+** | B3 | Electron acceptor |
| **CoA** | B5 | Acetyl transfer |
| **Mg²⁺** | Mineral | Cofactor |

**Clinical implication:** Elevated pyruvate suggests B-complex + magnesium need.

### Methylmalonate and B12
| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Enzyme** | Methylmalonyl-CoA mutase |
| **Cofactor** | Adenosylcobalamin (B12) |
| **Elevation indicates** | Functional B12 deficiency |
| **Clinical significance** | Neurological risk, odd-chain FA oxidation impaired |

### FIGLU and Folate
| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Enzyme** | Formiminotransferase |
| **Cofactor** | Tetrahydrofolate (THF) |
| **Elevation indicates** | Folate deficiency |
| **Clinical significance** | Methylation impairment, anemia risk |

### Pattern Recognition
| Pattern | Interpretation | Intervention |
|---------|---------------|--------------|
| Pyruvate + α-KG ↑ | B1 deficiency likely | Thiamine high-dose |
| Methylmalonate + FIGLU ↑ | B12 + folate needed | Methylated B vitamins |
| Succinate ↑ alone | B2/CoQ10 consideration | Riboflavin, CoQ10 |
| Multiple markers ↑ | Comprehensive B-complex | Multi-B + Mg |

## Clinical Implication
- **Targeted supplementation:** OAT guides specific cofactor replacement
- **Dose optimization:** Severity of elevation guides dosing
- **Monitoring:** Repeat OAT verifies repletion
- **Cost-effectiveness:** Avoid unnecessary broad supplementation
- **METHAP relevance:** Biomarker-guided supplementation is research-validated approach; OAT patterns can serve as outcome measures in intervention studies
