# Dicarboxylic Acid Energy Yield — ATP Production Comparison

## Core Claim
**Dicarboxylic acids (adipate C6, suberate C8, sebacate C10) yield approximately 80% of the ATP that would be produced from complete β-oxidation of their precursor fatty acids — with adipate generating ~32-40 ATP (vs ~50 for 3 acetyl-CoA direct), representing significant energy recovery through the dicarboxylic acid pathway when mitochondrial β-oxidation is impaired.**

## Evidence

### ATP Yield Comparison
| Substrate | Pathway | ATP Yield | Efficiency |
|-----------|---------|-----------|------------|
| **Palmitate (C16)** | Complete β-oxidation | ~106 ATP | 100% (reference) |
| **Adipate (C6)** | Dicarboxylic pathway | ~32-40 ATP | ~80% of C6 potential |
| **Suberate (C8)** | Dicarboxylic pathway | ~45-55 ATP | ~80% of C8 potential |
| **Sebacate (C10)** | Dicarboxylic pathway | ~60-70 ATP | ~80% of C10 potential |

### Adipate Energy Calculation
| Step | Product | ATP Equivalent |
|------|---------|----------------|
| Activation | Adipyl-CoA | -2 ATP (cost) |
| First β-ox cycle | Acetyl-CoA + FADH2 + NADH | ~15 ATP |
| Second β-ox cycle | Acetyl-CoA + FADH2 + NADH | ~15 ATP |
| Succinyl-CoA entry | GTP + Krebs completion | ~12 ATP |
| **Net yield** | — | **~32-40 ATP** |

### Why Efficiency is Lower
| Factor | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| **ω-oxidation cost** | NADPH consumption |
| **Activation cost** | 2 ATP for CoA attachment |
| **Incomplete oxidation** | Some carbon lost as CO2 |
| **Transport costs** | ER to mitochondria transfer |

### Comparative Metabolic Efficiency
| Pathway | Efficiency | When Active |
|---------|-----------|-------------|
| **Complete β-oxidation** | 100% | Normal mitochondria |
| **Dicarboxylic pathway** | ~80% | Impaired β-oxidation |
| **Ketogenesis** | ~90% | Hepatic, fasting state |
| **Lipogenesis** | ~75% | Energy storage |

## Clinical Implication
- **Significant recovery:** 80% efficiency is metabolically meaningful
- **Not just waste:** Dicarboxylic acids provide energy
- **Capacity indicator:** High urinary levels = overwhelmed system
- **Intervention target:** Improving β-oxidation restores efficiency
- **METHAP relevance:** Energy yield calculations inform metabolic support dosing
