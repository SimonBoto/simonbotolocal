# Vigorous Activity More Efficient Than Moderate

## Core Claim
**Vigorous physical activity** (high intensity) provides **greater mortality reduction per minute** than moderate activity, making it more time-efficient for busy individuals seeking health benefits from minimal exercise investment.

## The Intensity Advantage

### Time Equivalence
| Vigorous | Moderate | Benefit |
|----------|----------|---------|
| **15 min/week** | ~75 min/week | Similar mortality reduction |
| **25 min/week** | ~125 min/week | 46% lower all-cause mortality |
| **1 minute** | ~2-3 minutes | Vigorous more efficient |

**Rule of thumb:** 1 minute vigorous ≈ 2-3 minutes moderate

### Why Vigorous Works Better
| Mechanism | Vigorous Effect | Moderate Effect |
|-----------|-----------------|-----------------|
| **VO2 max improvement** | Greater per minute | Less per minute |
| **Mitochondrial stress** | Stronger stimulus | Weaker stimulus |
| **Hormonal response** | Larger GH, catecholamine release | Smaller release |
| **EPOC (afterburn)** | Higher, longer | Lower, shorter |
| **Cardiac adaptation** | Greater stroke volume improvement | Less improvement |

## Defining Intensity

### Vigorous Activity Examples
| Activity | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Running/jogging** | Can't hold conversation easily |
| **Cycling fast** | >20 km/h or hills |
| **Swimming laps** | Continuous, breathless |
| **HIIT/burpees** | High heart rate, intervals |
| **Sprinting** | Maximum effort bursts |
| **Heavy lifting** | Compound movements, near-max |

### Moderate Activity Examples
| Activity | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Brisk walking** | Can talk but not sing |
| **Light cycling** | <20 km/h, flat |
| **Easy swimming** | Leisurely pace |
| **Yoga/Pilates** | Controlled movements |
| **Gardening** | Light to moderate effort |

## The Time-Efficiency Equation

### For Busy Individuals
| Constraint | Vigorous Solution | Moderate Equivalent |
|------------|-------------------|---------------------|
| **10 min available** | 10 min HIIT | 20-30 min walking |
| **2×/week only** | 2× 10 min sprints | 2× 30 min jogging |
| **Lunch break** | 15 min bodyweight circuit | 45 min walk |

### Clinical Recommendation
| Patient Type | Prescription |
|--------------|--------------|
| **Time-constrained** | Vigorous 15-20 min/week |
| **Enjoys longer sessions** | Moderate 75-150 min/week |
| **Mixed preference** | Combination approach |

## Evidence

> "The 15-20 minute weekly threshold refers to vigorous activity; achieving equivalent benefits through moderate activity would require substantially more time, highlighting intensity as a key variable for time-constrained populations."
> — Polo-López et al., 2024

## Safety Considerations

### Who Should Start Vigorous?
| Population | Guidance |
|------------|----------|
| **Healthy, sedentary** | Gradual progression to vigorous |
| **Cardiac risk factors** | Medical clearance first |
| **Known CVD** | Supervised cardiac rehab |
| **Older adults** | Build base fitness first |

### Progression Protocol
| Week | Activity | Intensity |
|------|----------|-----------|
| 1-2 | Walking | Moderate |
| 3-4 | Brisk walking + short intervals | Moderate-vigorous |
| 5-6 | Intervals | Vigorous |
| 7+ | Full vigorous sessions | Vigorous |

## Practical Implementation

### Minimal Vigorous Program (15-20 min/week)
| Day | Exercise | Duration |
|-----|----------|----------|
| **Monday** | Tabata (20s work/10s rest × 8) | 4 min |
| **Thursday** | Sprint intervals (30s sprint/60s rest × 6) | 9 min |
| **Total** | — | ~15 min |

**Equipment needed:** None (bodyweight)
**Location:** Home, park, anywhere
**Time investment:** Minimal
**Benefit:** 16-40% mortality reduction
