# Carnitine Shuttle — Fatty Acid Transport System

## Core Claim
**The carnitine shuttle transports long-chain fatty acids across the inner mitochondrial membrane via CPT1 (outer membrane), carnitine-acylcarnitine translocase, and CPT2 (inner membrane) — with CPT1 being the rate-limiting step and primary regulatory point, and defects causing impaired β-oxidation, dicarboxylic acid accumulation, and hypoketotic hypoglycemia.**

## Evidence

### Transport Steps
| Step | Enzyme/Transporter | Location | Reaction |
|------|-------------------|----------|----------|
| **1** | CPT1 (Carnitine palmitoyltransferase 1) | Outer mitochondrial membrane | Acyl-CoA + Carnitine → Acylcarnitine + CoA |
| **2** | CACT (Carnitine-acylcarnitine translocase) | Inner mitochondrial membrane | Acylcarnitine in, free carnitine out |
| **3** | CPT2 (Carnitine palmitoyltransferase 2) | Inner mitochondrial membrane | Acylcarnitine + CoA → Acyl-CoA + Carnitine |

### CPT1 Regulation
| Regulator | Effect | Mechanism |
|-----------|--------|-----------|
| **Malonyl-CoA** | Inhibition | Product of fatty acid synthesis |
| **Insulin** | Indirect inhibition | ↑ Malonyl-CoA |
| **Glucagon** | Activation | ↓ Malonyl-CoA |
| **Fasting** | Activation | ↓ Malonyl-CoA |
| **High fat diet** | Increased expression | Adaptive upregulation |

### Carnitine Sources
| Source | Contribution | Notes |
|--------|--------------|-------|
| **Diet** | ~75% | Primarily meat, fish, dairy |
| **Synthesis** | ~25% | Lysine + methionine (liver, kidney) |
| **Total body pool** | ~20-25g | 98% in muscle |

### Clinical Deficiencies
| Deficiency | Effect | Presentation |
|------------|--------|--------------|
| **Primary carnitine deficiency** | Transport failure | Hypoketotic hypoglycemia, cardiomyopathy |
| **CPT1 deficiency** | Cannot enter mitochondria | Hepatic encephalopathy, hypoglycemia |
| **CPT2 deficiency** | Cannot reactivate in matrix | Muscle weakness, rhabdomyolysis |
| **CACT deficiency** | Translocase failure | Severe neonatal hypoglycemia |

### Acylcarnitine Profile in Defects
| Condition | Elevated Acylcarnitines | Pattern |
|-----------|------------------------|---------|
| **CPT1 deficiency** | Long-chain (C16-C18) | Cannot enter mitochondria |
| **MCAD deficiency** | Medium-chain (C8-C10) | Block in β-oxidation |
| **Carnitine deficiency** | Mixed | Global transport impairment |

## Clinical Implication
- **Rate-limiting step:** CPT1 controls fatty acid oxidation flux
- **Malonyl-CoA switch:** Coordinates synthesis vs oxidation
- **Deficiency consequences:** Hypoketotic hypoglycemia, organ damage
- **Supplementation:** L-carnitine for primary deficiency
- **METHAP relevance:** Carnitine status affects metabolic flexibility; secondary deficiency common in insulin resistance
