Good morning! Glutathione is a tripeptide composed of three amino acids: glutamine, cysteine, and glycine. Its production involves two major enzymatic steps:

1. **γ-Glutamylcysteine Synthesis**: This step is catalyzed by the enzyme γ-glutamylcysteine synthetase (also known as glutamate-cysteine ligase). It combines glutamate and cysteine to form γ-glutamylcysteine.

2. **Glutathione Synthesis**: The enzyme glutathione synthetase then adds glycine to γ-glutamylcysteine to form glutathione.

The major pathway required for its production is the **γ-glutamyl cycle**, which involves the above two enzymatic reactions. Glutathione plays a crucial role in cellular redox homeostasis, detoxification, and the maintenance of the thiol status of proteins.
