GLP-1, or Glucagon-like Peptide-1, is an incretin hormone primarily produced in the small intestine. It has several important functions in the human body:

1. **Regulation of Blood Glucose Levels**: GLP-1 enhances insulin secretion from the pancreatic beta cells in a glucose-dependent manner, which helps lower blood glucose levels. It also inhibits glucagon release from alpha cells, reducing hepatic glucose production.

2. **Appetite and Weight Regulation**: GLP-1 slows gastric emptying and promotes satiety, leading to reduced food intake and potentially aiding in weight management.

3. **Cardiovascular Effects**: GLP-1 has been shown to have cardioprotective effects, including improving endothelial function, reducing blood pressure, and exerting anti-inflammatory effects.

4. **Neuroprotective Functions**: Emerging research suggests that GLP-1 may have neuroprotective effects, potentially benefiting conditions like Alzheimer's disease by enhancing neuronal survival and function.

Due to its significant role in glucose homeostasis and appetite regulation, GLP-1 and its analogs are used in the treatment of type 2 diabetes and obesity. Medications such as exenatide and liraglutide mimic the effects of GLP-1, providing therapeutic benefits for these conditions.
