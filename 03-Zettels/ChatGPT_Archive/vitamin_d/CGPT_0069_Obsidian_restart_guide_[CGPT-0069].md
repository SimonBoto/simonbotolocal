# 🧭 The Core Rules of Obsidian (Zettelkasten + Productivity Style)

### 1. **Atomic Notes**  
- Each note should capture **one idea, one concept, one source**.  
- Short, focused, linkable.  
- Example: Instead of a big file called *Vitamin D*, have separate notes like *Vitamin D – Metabolic Pathways*, *Vitamin D – Clinical Trials in IR*, *Vitamin D – Interaction with K2*.

---

### 2. **Linking is More Valuable than Filing**  
- Use **`[[links]]`** to connect notes; don’t rely too much on strict folder hierarchy.  
- Your brain works by association — the graph view and backlinks replicate that.  
- Example: In your PhD, *Insulin Resistance* should link to *Organic Acids*, *FFA Ratios*, *Vitamin D deficiency*, etc.

---

### 3. **Capture > Clarify > Connect**  
This is your workflow:
1. **Capture** fleeting notes (raw thoughts, highlights, patient insights).  
2. **Clarify** by rewriting them in your own words (literature notes, case summaries).  
3. **Connect** by creating permanent notes with links across fields (your Zettelkasten).  

---

### 4. **Templates Keep You Consistent**  
- Use Templater to reduce friction.  
- Examples:  
  - **Literature Note Template**: citation, abstract, key findings, my comments, tags.  
  - **Daily Note Template**: intentions, tasks, reflections.  
  - **Patient Protocol Template**: condition, supplements, diet, follow-up.  

---

### 5. **Write to Think, Not to Store**  
- Obsidian isn’t a filing cabinet — it’s a **thinking partner**.  
- Notes evolve: you revisit, rewrite, and connect them.  
- Example: Your metabolomics notes aren’t “done” until you’ve rephrased them into your **own permanent knowledge base**.

---

### 6. **Resurface & Review Regularly**  
- Use **Daily/Weekly Notes** to resurface connections.  
- Periodically scan backlinks and tags.  
- Example: A weekly review of your *Insulin Resistance* cluster will help you see missing links for your PhD.

# 🔗 Optimising Backlinks

### 1. **Prefer Links Over Tags for Concepts**
- Always create `[[note links]]` for *concepts, people, molecules, conditions, etc.*  
- Example: Instead of `#vitaminD`, make `[[Vitamin D]]`.  
- Why: links create **backlinks + graph connections**, whereas tags only group loosely.

---

### 2. **Use Links to Build “Conversations”**
- Every note should answer: *What other ideas does this connect to?*  
- Example:  
  - *Vitamin D – Insulin Sensitivity* links to → `[[Magnesium]]`, `[[Metabolic Syndrome]]`, `[[Fatty Acids]]`.  
  - This creates a **cluster** of related knowledge.

---

### 3. **Make Index / MOC (Map of Content) Pages**
- Create special hub notes like *Insulin Resistance MOC*.  
- These are curated lists of links (not tags) — essentially your personal Wikipedia.  
- Example snippet:  
  `‌`‌`
  ## Insulin Resistance – Map of Content
  - [[Pathophysiology]]
  - [[Metabolomics Biomarkers]]
  - [[Clinical Trials]]
  - [[Therapeutic Nutrition]]
  `‌`‌`

---

### 4. **Backlink Gardening**
- Once a week:  
  - Open the **backlinks panel** for a major note.  
  - Re-link orphan mentions → if you wrote “D3” somewhere, link it to `[[Vitamin D]]`.  
  - Add clarifying notes: “See also [[Magnesium]]” at the bottom.  
- This is how you keep the web alive.

---

# 🏷 Optimising Tags

Tags are for **broad classification**, not concepts. Use them sparingly.  

### 1. **Use Tags for States, Types, or Status**
- Good tag examples:  
  - `#fleeting`, `#literature`, `#permanent` (note status)  
  - `#patient-case`, `#protocol`, `#draft` (note type)  
  - `#todo`, `#review` (workflow)  

### 2. **Keep Tags Flat (No Hierarchies)**
- Avoid `#research/vitaminD` → instead just `#research`.  
- If something deserves hierarchy, make it a **note link** instead.  

### 3. **Create a Tag Index Note**
- One note listing all your tags with explanations.  
- Example:  
  `‌`‌`
  ## Tag Index
  - #fleeting → raw capture, to be processed
  - #literature → notes from a paper/book
  - #permanent → finalized atomic note
  - #protocol → clinical guideline
  - #draft → manuscript in progress
  `‌`‌`

---

- **Concepts = Links.**  
- **Categories / Status = Tags.**  
- **Hubs = Maps of Content.**  
- **Weekly backlink review = vault hygiene.**
