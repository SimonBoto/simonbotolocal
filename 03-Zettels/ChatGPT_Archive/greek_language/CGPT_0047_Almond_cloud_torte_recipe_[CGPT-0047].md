## METHOD

### 1️⃣ Make the almond crust
1. Roast almonds 8–10 min at ~160°C until aromatic.
2. Grind medium-fine (not powder — we want texture).
3. Mix with melted butter + pinch of salt + sweetener/caramel if used.
4. Press firmly into the base of a springform pan.
5. Refrigerate to set.

### 2️⃣ Prepare the honey-cream bavaroise
1. Warm coconut cream + heavy cream together gently (do NOT boil).
2. Beat cream cheese until smooth separately.
3. Temper eggs: in a bowl, whisk eggs + yolks + honey/xylitol, then slowly stream in warm cream mixture to avoid scrambling.  
4. Return everything to low heat and cook to ~78–82°C until **nappe** consistency (coats the spoon).  
   *(This creates a custard base, just like a classic crème anglaise.)*
5. Remove from heat.  
6. Add softened gelatin (dissolved into warm custard). Mix gently until uniform.
7. Fold in cream cheese until smooth and homogeneous. Let cool to *lukewarm/thickening but not stiff*.
8. Pour over almond crust.

→ Refrigerate 3–4 hours (or overnight) to fully set.

### 3️⃣ Top with marmalade glaze
1. Warm marmalade slightly with a tiny splash of water.
2. Spread a thin but glossy layer over the cold set cream.
3. Chill another 30 mins.

## 🔥 Technique: Honey–Glazed Almond Praline (Soft Crunch Version)

### Ingredients
- 200 g roasted almonds (already prepared)
- 1–1.5 tbsp honey
- 1 tsp butter (helps coating & prevents brittleness)
- Pinch of salt (optional but enhances depth)

---

## STEP-BY-STEP

### 1️⃣ Lightly warm the honey
In a **small pan**, low heat:
- Add honey
- Let it bubble just *slightly* (5–10 seconds)
- Do **not** cook to dark amber — we only want a light reduction

### 2️⃣ Add butter
- Stir in the butter until glossy  
This lowers honey’s glass-transition hardness → prevents rock-hard caramel later

### 3️⃣ Coat the almonds
- Add roasted almonds into pan
- Toss to coat fully
- Keep on low heat **30–60 seconds max**
This step infuses honey INTO the micro-cracks of roasted almonds.

### 4️⃣ Spread to partial-set
- Transfer to parchment/baking paper in a single layer
- Let cool 5 minutes (not fully hardened)

→ At this stage, they are **soft-crunchy**, not brittle.

---

## 5️⃣ Crushing to the correct texture

Once 5 minutes have passed (still slightly warm, slightly tacky):
- Pulse in food processor **only 2–3 pulses**
We want:
- Some fine crumb (to bind)
- Some coarse bits (to crunch)

**If you wait until fully set** → they become harder → more praline-like.
**By pulsing warm** → we keep that tender crunch.

---

## Why this method works

| Component | Role |
|----------|------|
| minimal heating | prevents bitterness & glycation off-notes |
| butter + honey | creates soft “elastic” caramel |
| warm pulsing | integrated crunch without brittleness |
| salt | deepens flavor, traditional pastry effect |

- Let it cool to **lukewarm** (important: not hot).

- Beat **250 g cream cheese** until smooth (room temperature helps).
- Add the **2 egg yolks** and mix until silky.
- Add **40–50 g honey** and a pinch of salt.
- Add a little **coconut cream** (spoon by spoon) until smooth.
- Now mix in the **melted gelatin mixture**.

This is your *flavor + structure base*.

---

### 4️⃣ Add whipped cream
- Gently fold the whipped heavy cream into the yolk/cheese mixture.
- Do this in **two additions** (first loosens, second brings volume).
- You should now have a thick, glossy, mousse-like base.

### 5️⃣ Whip egg whites
Separately:
- Whip the **2 egg whites** ONLY to *medium peaks*.

- Use a wide spatula, folding **from bottom to top** to avoid deflation.
