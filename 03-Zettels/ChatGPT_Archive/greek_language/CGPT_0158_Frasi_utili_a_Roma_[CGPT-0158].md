## Basic Greetings and Polite Expressions

1. **Buongiorno** \[bwon-JOR-no\]  
   - **English:** Good morning / Good day.  
   - **When to use:** Typically used in the morning and early afternoon.

2. **Buonasera** \[bwon-a-SAY-ra\]  
   - **English:** Good evening.  
   - **When to use:** Used from late afternoon/evening onward.

3. **Ciao** \[chow\]  
   - **English:** Hi / Bye.  
   - **Note:** Informal; best suited among friends or younger people. In more formal settings, greet with “Buongiorno” or “Buonasera.”

4. **Arrivederci** \[a-ree-veh-DER-chee\]  
   - **English:** Goodbye (formal).  

5. **Per favore / Per piacere** \[per fa-VO-re / per pya-CHE-re\]  
   - **English:** Please.  

6. **Grazie** \[GRA-tsyeh\]  
   - **English:** Thank you.  

7. **Prego** \[PREH-go\]  
   - **English:** You’re welcome / Please (in the sense of inviting someone to do something).  

8. **Mi scusi** \[mee SCOO-zee\]  
   - **English:** Excuse me (formal), or I’m sorry to interrupt.  

9. **Scusa** \[SCOO-za\]  
   - **English:** Sorry (informal).

## Directions and Transportation

1. **Dov’è … ?** \[doh-VEH\]  
   - **English:** Where is … ?  

2. **Dov’è il bagno?** \[doh-VEH eel BAN-yo\]  
   - **English:** Where is the bathroom?

3. **Dov’è la stazione?** \[doh-VEH la sta-TZYO-ne\]  
   - **English:** Where is the station?  
   - **Note:** Can be adapted to “autobus” (bus) or “metro” (subway) station.

4. **Vorrei un taxi, per favore.** \[vor-RAY oon TAX-ee, per fa-VO-re\]  
   - **English:** I would like a taxi, please.  

5. **Quanto costa il biglietto?** \[KWAN-to CO-sta eel bee-LYET-to\]  
   - **English:** How much does the ticket cost?

6. **Un biglietto per favore.** \[oon bee-LYET-to per fa-VO-re\]  
   - **English:** One ticket, please.

## Eating and Ordering

1. **Un tavolo per due (tre, quattro), per favore.** \[oon TA-vo-lo per DOO-eh, (TREH, KWAT-tro) per fa-VO-re\]  
   - **English:** A table for two (three, four), please.  

2. **Il menù, per favore.** \[eel meh-NOO, per fa-VO-re\]  
   - **English:** The menu, please.  

3. **Vorrei ordinare …** \[vor-RAY or-dee-NA-re\]  
   - **English:** I would like to order …  

4. **Vorrei un bicchiere d’acqua.** \[vor-RAY oon bee-KYEH-reh DA-kwa\]  
   - **English:** I would like a glass of water.  

5. **Il conto, per favore.** \[eel CON-to, per fa-VO-re\]  
   - **English:** The check, please.  

6. **Posso avere … ?** \[POS-so a-VEH-re\]  
   - **English:** May I have … ?

## Shopping and Money

1. **Quanto costa?** \[KWAN-to CO-sta\]  
   - **English:** How much does it cost?  

2. **È troppo caro / cara!** \[eh TROP-po CA-ro / CA-ra\]  
   - **English:** It’s too expensive!  
   - **Note:** “Caro” refers to a masculine noun, “cara” to feminine.  

3. **Posso pagare con la carta?** \[POS-so pa-GA-re con la CAR-ta\]  
   - **English:** Can I pay by card?  

4. **Posso pagare in contanti?** \[POS-so pa-GA-re in con-TAN-tee\]  
   - **English:** Can I pay in cash?

## Emergencies and Practicalities

1. **Aiuto!** \[a-YOO-to\]  
   - **English:** Help!  

2. **Chiamate un’ambulanza!** \[kyah-MAH-te oon am-bu-LAN-za\]  
   - **English:** Call an ambulance!

3. **Dov’è l’ospedale?** \[doh-VEH lo-spe-DA-le\]  
   - **English:** Where is the hospital?

4. **Ho bisogno di un medico.** \[oh bee-SO-nyo dee oon MEH-dee-co\]  
   - **English:** I need a doctor.

5. **Mi sono perso / persa.** \[mee SO-no PER-so (m) / PER-sa (f)\]  
   - **English:** I am lost.  

6. **Non parlo molto italiano.** \[non PAR-lo MOL-to ee-ta-LYA-no\]  
   - **English:** I don’t speak much Italian.

7. **Parla inglese?** \[PAR-la een-GLAY-zeh\]  
   - **English:** Do you speak English?

## Useful Courtesy Phrases

1. **Può ripetere, per favore?** \[pwo ree-PEH-te-re, per fa-VO-re\]  
   - **English:** Could you repeat that, please?

2. **Più lentamente, per favore.** \[pyoo len-ta-MEN-te, per fa-VO-re\]  
   - **English:** More slowly, please.  

3. **Grazie mille.** \[GRA-tsyeh MEEL-leh\]  
   - **English:** Thank you very much.

4. **Prego, si figuri.** \[PREH-go, see fee-GOO-ree\]  
   - **English:** You’re welcome, don’t mention it. (Polite form)

## Tips for Pronunciation and Usage

- **Stress** often falls on the second-to-last syllable in Italian words. For example, in “Buongiorno” (bwon-JOR-no), the stressed syllable is “JOR.”
- When asking for directions or help, **always open with a polite greeting** or “Scusi,” which is more culturally fitting.
- **Body language and gestures** are common in Italian communication. Don’t be shy to use a friendly smile or polite gestures to help convey meaning.
