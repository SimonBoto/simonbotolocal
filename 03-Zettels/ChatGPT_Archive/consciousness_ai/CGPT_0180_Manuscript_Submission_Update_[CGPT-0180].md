GC-MS stands for **Gas Chromatography-Mass Spectrometry**, a powerful analytical technique used for identifying and quantifying compounds in complex mixtures. 

- **Gas Chromatography (GC):** Separates volatile components of a sample based on their interaction with a stationary phase and their boiling points.
- **Mass Spectrometry (MS):** Identifies the chemical structure of the separated compounds by analyzing their mass-to-charge ratio (m/z).  

GC-MS is widely used in fields such as metabolomics, pharmacology, environmental analysis, and food safety.
