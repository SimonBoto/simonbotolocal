### **How People Normally Cast Hexagrams:**

There are **three primary traditional methods** for casting a hexagram. Each has symbolic and ritual depth.

---

### **1. Three-Coin Method** *(Simplest and Most Common Today)*  
- **Materials**: 3 coins (identical, preferably metal)
- **Procedure**:
  - Toss the 3 coins **six times**, recording the result each time.
  - Each toss gives one line, from **bottom to top** (the first toss is the bottom line).
  - **Heads = 3**, **Tails = 2**:
    - 6 (three tails): **Old Yin** (broken, changing)
    - 7 (two tails, one head): **Young Yang** (solid)
    - 8 (two heads, one tail): **Young Yin** (broken)
    - 9 (three heads): **Old Yang** (solid, changing)
- After 6 throws, you build a hexagram from bottom to top.
- **If there are changing lines (6 or 9), a second hexagram** is formed by transforming those lines into their opposites.

---

### **2. Yarrow Stalk Method** *(Traditional, Meditative, Complex)*  
- Ancient method involving 50 stalks (e.g., broom straw or yarrow), taking ~20 minutes.
- More spiritually involved and favored by Confucius and later scholars.
- Produces slightly different probabilities, emphasizing Yin.

---

- Often uses events, time, or visual intuition rather than coins or stalks.

---

### **Most Modern Readers Use**:
The **three-coin method**, either physically or digitally, due to its simplicity and symbolic balance.

### **Why the Lines Are Read Bottom to Top**

In traditional Chinese cosmology, everything begins **from the ground upward**—mirroring how seeds grow into plants, how energy (qi) rises from the Earth, and how human life unfolds from foundations to emergence. The hexagram reflects this logic.

---

### **Metaphysical and Structural Reasons:**

1. **Hexagrams Are Built From the Bottom Up**:
   - The **1st line (bottom)** is the beginning of the situation.
   - The **6th line (top)** represents culmination, transformation, or transcendence.
   - Each line has a symbolic meaning according to its position:
     - Line 1 = **Initiation**
     - Line 2 = **Early development**
     - Line 3 = **Crisis or struggle**
     - Line 4 = **Resolution rising**
     - Line 5 = **Power and clarity**
     - Line 6 = **Excess or spiritual detachment**

2. **Yi Jing is a Dynamic System**:
   - The changes begin **at the base**, like energy moving through the chakras or kundalini up the spine.
   - Reading from the bottom reveals the **story of transformation** unfolding upward.

3. **Canonical Tradition**:
   - The earliest commentaries—such as those by Confucius (Ten Wings)—interpret the lines bottom to top.
   - Every major English translation (Wilhelm/Baynes, Legge, Huang) follows this structure.

---

### **But… You Can Read Top-Down for Intuition**
While the orthodox approach is bottom-up, in modern psychological or intuitive readings, some practitioners do read top-down to **mirror present consciousness** (what is visible) and then dig into the **roots** (what is emerging from the unseen).

But this is interpretive liberty, not classical form.

---

### In Summary:

> **Hexagram lines are written from bottom to top because they represent the process of emergence—from foundational forces to culmination.**  
> Each line has positional meaning, and the reading follows the same upward logic: transformation begins from below and rises.
