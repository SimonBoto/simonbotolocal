   \[
   \text{pH} = -\log_{10} [H^+]
   \]
   For a pH of 1:
   \[
   1 = -\log_{10} [H^+]
   \]
   \[
   [H^+] = 10^{-1} \text{ mol/L} = 0.1 \text{ mol/L}
   \]

2. **Calculate final \([H^+]\) concentration:**
   For a pH of 6:
   \[
   6 = -\log_{10} [H^+]
   \]
   \[
   [H^+] = 10^{-6} \text{ mol/L}
   \]

3. **Determine the dilution factor:**
   The dilution factor (DF) is the ratio of the initial concentration to the final concentration:
   \[
   \text{DF} = \frac{[H^+]_{\text{initial}}}{[H^+]_{\text{final}}}
   \]
   \[
   \text{DF} = \frac{0.1}{10^{-6}} = 10^{5} = 100,000
   \]

Thus, to dilute a solution from a pH of 1 to a pH of 6, you need to dilute it by a factor of 100,000. This means you would need to add 999,999 parts of water to 1 part of the original solution.
