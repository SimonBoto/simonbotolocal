1. Introduction

Telomerase is a ribonucleoprotein complex with reverse transcriptase activity that adds telomeric tandem simple sequence repeats to the chromosomal ends by copying a template sequence within the RNA component of the enzyme [1]. In this way telomerase retains its title as the ultimate key-molecule for the maintenance of telomere integrity and therefore contributing to the enhancement of the stability of linear eukaryotic chromosomes, which plays a crucial role in the regulation of cellular senescence and cell division. The interaction between telomerase and shelterin and/or the formation of telomeric DNA/telomerase RNA hybrid retain telomerase at the telomere, resulting in processive extension telomere [2]. Telomerase has a preferential option of targeting shorter telomeres for extension, thus ensuring a uniform average telomere length throughout the chromosome [3]. Telomerase activity can be detected in tissues from the fetus to the newborn, but after birth, the telomerase activity in somatic cells is lost [4]. 

There are 6 types of telomerase regulators, the intervention in the regulation mechanism of which can essentially contribute to the selection and utilization of the proper therapeutic/treatment agents for various age‑related diseases. These are categorized as synthetic direct telomerase inhibitors, synthetic indirect telomerase inhibitors, synthetic telomerase activators, natural direct telomerase activators, natural telomerase inhibitors and natural indirect telomerase activators, according to their origin and their activity [5]. In the present study, we are going to focus on non-synthetic-natural direct and indirect telomerase activators. 

Telomerase activators have attracted the interest of the scientific community [6*], as their synthesis and development have been used as a guiding principle in order to create a wide variety of therapeutic agents against age-related diseases, as well as diseases associated with telomere dysfunction and increased shortening rate. A typical example of the latter category of diseases mentioned, is GRN510, a relatively new small molecule activator that appears to be an effective therapeutic agent in limiting bleomycin-induced pulmonary fibrosis, which enhances telomerase activity up to 2-4 times, as revealed by experiments performed both in vitro and in vivo, in heterozygous TERT mice.[7*] Another intriguing finding from in vivo investigations was that senescence was also suppressed in mouse lung cells, indicating the potential use of small molecule telomerase activators in treatments for idiopathic pulmonary fibrosis. [6]. A prime example of a natural direct telomerase activator is hydrogen sulfide (H2S), which has been shown to possess strong antiaging properties, protecting against replicative aging.[8*] Specifically, a reduced amount of the enzyme and consequently the expressed gene of H2S appears to be produced in adult human dermal fibroblasts undergoing replicative senescence, while simultaneously the expression of p16, p21 and the ribonucleotide-diphosphate reductase subunit M2B appears to be enhanced, features directly linked to replicative aging. On the other hand, the levels of hTERT, sirtuin 1 (SIRT1), nicotinamide phosphoribosyltransferase (NAMPT) and the nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide (NAD)/NADH ratio appear to decrease, but external addition of exogenous H2S was observed to restore this imbalance, maintaining hTERT expression in a NAMPT- and SIR T1-dependent manner, delaying the onset of replicative senescence [7].

A variety of natural compounds are used as positive telomerase regulators to halt the development of age-related diseases and to restore the normal function of defective telomeres. [10*,11*] In this category are included factors which, although are not directly linked to the telomerase enzyme, have shown an affinity for telomerase and telomeric DNA. A typical example of such agents are curcuminoids, which have been found to enhance telomerase activity in an in vitro TRAP assay through interaction of their curcuminoid core with at least one n-pentylpyridine side chain [8].

Another natural indirect telomerase activator is cycloastragenol (CAG, GRN 665 or TA65), a triterpenoid saponin compound and a hydrolytic product of the main active ingredient of Astragalus membranaceus, which safely maintains telomeric DNA integrity and lengthens telomeres, while at the same time it has a wide range of applications [9]. Furthermore, regarding the effect on human dermal fibroblasts (HDF), CAG interferes with hTERT mRNA splicing and inhibits telomerase activity [10].

A third typical example of a natural indirect telomerase activator is resveratrol, a polyphenol that has been tested as a potential promoter of WRN helicase and telomerase in people with WS in patients with premature aging. Its action can be summarized in DNA repair and telomere maintenance, through a pathway that results in an increase in of WRN promoter activity and its gene and protein expression is accompanied by upregulation of telomerase in HeLa S3 cells, without affecting cell viability. [11,12].

Finally, Centella asiatica extract (08AGTLF) has been shown to induce telomerase activation in peripheral blood mononuclear cells from healthy donors. Previous research has shown that rats treated with Centella asiatica had enhanced cognitive ability and higher expression of mitochondrial and antioxidant genes in the brain and liver and positively contributed to cognitive improvement and motor activity and significantly reduced stress in a dose-dependent manner in a study with aged rats, after administration of Centella asiatica contained in supplement (Reverse™) for a period of 3 months. In parallel, TERT expression was restored in the brains of middle-aged rats [13,14].	

In the present study we are investigating whether non-synthetic supplements and their various combinations induce telomerase activation in human peripheral blood mononuclear cells (PBMCs) to determine whether synergism occurs, and, if so, to assess the extent to which this occurs. The study is based on previous in vitro studies that tested singly plant-derived compounds on telomerase activity and showed positive results. The research group aims to move forward and tested some of the compounds for potent synergistic effect, as these supplements have not previously been tested as a whole product.
