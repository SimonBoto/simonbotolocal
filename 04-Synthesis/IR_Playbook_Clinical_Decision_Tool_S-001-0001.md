# Insulin Resistance Playbook - Clinical Decision Tool

## Executive Summary

**Purpose:** Rapid clinical assessment and staging of insulin resistance using metabolomics, organic acids, and standard biomarkers

**Target Audience:** 
- Functional medicine practitioners
- Metabolomics clinicians
- Precision medicine specialists

**Core Output:** 
- Staged diagnosis (Early → Compensatory → Overt)
- Mechanism-specific intervention protocol
- Monitoring schedule with decision points

**Key Innovation:** Integrates adipic acid (early marker) with HOMA-IR and metabolic flexibility assessment for pre-diabetic detection

---

## Section 1: Pathophysiology Foundation

### 1.1 Normal Insulin Signaling
The insulin signaling cascade begins at the receptor and propagates through multiple nodes:

**Step 1: Receptor Activation**
- [[Insulin_Receptor_Structure_Z-001-0001]] — Tyrosine kinase receptor, α2β2 structure
- [[INSR_Autophosphorylation_Mechanism_Z-001-0003]] — Ligand binding triggers trans-autophosphorylation
- [[INSR_Mutations_Donohue_Syndrome_Z-001-0004]] — Extreme insulin resistance phenotype

**Step 2: IRS Recruitment**
- [[IRS_Protein_Recruitment_Z-001-0005]] — IRS1/IRS2 as adaptor proteins
- [[IRS1_vs_IRS2_Tissue_Distribution_Z-001-0006]] — Tissue-specific roles
- [[IRS_Serine_Phosphorylation_Causes_IR_Z-001-0008]] — Key mechanism of resistance

**Step 3: PI3K-Akt Pathway**
- [[PI3K_Activation_Mechanism_Z-001-0007]] — Lipid kinase generates PIP3
- [[Akt_Recruitment_by_PIP3_Z-001-0009]] — Pleckstrin homology domain binding
- [[Akt_Substrate_Phosphorylation_Z-001-0010]] — Multiple downstream targets

**Step 4: GLUT4 Translocation**
- [[GLUT4_Vesicle_Translocation_Z-001-0011]] — Insulin-stimulated glucose uptake
- [[GSK3_Inhibition_Glycogen_Synthesis_Z-001-0012]] — Glycogen storage activation

**Step 5: mTOR Integration**
- [[mTORC2_Akt_Insulin_Signaling_Z-001-0925]] — Feedback regulation
- [[Cbl_CAP_Lipid_Raft_Pathway_Z-001-0017]] — Alternative signaling route

### 1.2 Mechanisms of Resistance

**Lipotoxicity Pathway**
- [[Lipotoxicity_Pathway_IR_Z-001-0029]] — Ceramide and DAG accumulation
- [[Lipotoxicity_Ceramide_Induced_IR_Z-001-0013]] — Direct inhibition of Akt
- [[ER_Stress_Induced_IR_Z-001-0014]] — Unfolded protein response activation
- [[ER_Stress_UPR_IR_Z-001-0033]] — JNK-mediated serine phosphorylation

**Mitochondrial Dysfunction**
- [[Mitochondrial_Dysfunction_IR_Z-001-0015]] — Reduced oxidative capacity
- [[Mitochondrial_Dysfunction_IR_Z-001-0030]] — Incomplete fatty acid oxidation
- [[Beta_Oxidation_Clinical_Mechanisms_Z-001-1054]] — Adipic acid as marker

**Inflammatory Signaling**
- [[Inflammatory_Signaling_IR_Z-001-0031]] — Cytokine-mediated resistance
- [[Gut_Derived_Mechanisms_IR_Z-001-0032]] — LPS translocation
- [[Vicious_Cycle_Inflammation_IR_Z-001-0038]] — Self-amplifying loop

**Hormonal/Metabolic**
- [[Vicious_Cycle_IR_Obesity_Z-001-0037]] — Adipokine dysregulation
- [[Vicious_Cycle_Stress_IR_Z-001-0039]] — Cortisol-mediated glucose elevation

## Section 3: Staging Algorithm

### 3.1 Stage 0: Normal Metabolic Health
**Criteria:**
- HOMA-IR <2.0
- Fasting glucose <90 mg/dL
- HbA1c <5.5%
- Adipic acid <15 (OAT)
- TG/HDL <1.5

**Action:** Maintenance protocol
- Mediterranean diet pattern
- 150 min/week moderate exercise
- Sleep optimization
- Stress management

### 3.2 Stage 1: Early Insulin Resistance
**Criteria:**
- HOMA-IR 2.0-2.5
- Fasting glucose 90-100 mg/dL
- Adipic acid 15-30 (early marker!)
- BHB 0.05-0.1 (reduced metabolic flexibility)
- TG/HDL 1.5-2.0

**Mechanism Focus:** [[Lipotoxicity_Pathway_IR_Z-001-0029]]

**Intervention Protocol:**
1. **Diet:** Low-carbohydrate or ketogenic (patient preference)
2. **Exercise:** Resistance training 3x/week + HIIT 2x/week
3. **Supplements:** 
   - Magnesium 400mg ([[Magnesium_Insulin_Resistance_Mechanisms_Z-001-0211]])
   - Omega-3 2-4g EPA+DHA
   - Vitamin D to 80-150 nmol/L
4. **Monitoring:** Repeat OAT + HOMA-IR in 8 weeks

### 3.3 Stage 2: Compensatory Hyperinsulinemia
**Criteria:**
- HOMA-IR 2.5-4.5
- Fasting insulin 10-20 μU/mL
- HOMA-β 100-150% (compensating)
- HbA1c 5.7-6.4%
- Adipic acid 30-60
- BHB <0.05 (impaired ketogenesis)

**Mechanism Focus:** [[Compensatory_IR_Stage_Z-001-0034]], [[Beta_Cell_Exhaustion_Stage_Z-001-0035]]

**Intervention Protocol:**
1. **Diet:** Strict ketogenic (<30g carbs) or carnivore (if autoimmune)
2. **Exercise:** Daily movement + resistance training priority
3. **Supplements (Aggressive):**
   - Berberine 500mg 2-3x/day
   - Alpha-lipoic acid 600mg
   - Chromium 1000mcg
   - CoQ10 200mg ([[CoQ10_Mitochondrial_Support_Z-001-1217]])
4. **Monitoring:** Monthly HOMA-IR, quarterly OAT

### 3.4 Stage 3: Overt Type 2 Diabetes
**Criteria:**
- HOMA-IR >4.5
- Fasting glucose >125 mg/dL
- HbA1c >6.4%
- HOMA-β <80% (failing) or >200% (struggling)
- Multiple OAT markers elevated

**Mechanism Focus:** [[Overt_Diabetes_Stage_Z-001-0036]], [[Beta_Cell_Exhaustion_Stage_Z-001-0035]]

**Intervention Protocol:**
1. **Medical Management:** Coordinate with physician (metformin, SGLT2-i, GLP-1 RA)
2. **Diet:** Very low-carb or ketogenic, time-restricted eating
3. **Exercise:** Supervised program, muscle-centric
4. **Advanced Supplements:**
   - All Stage 2 supplements
   - Mitochondrial support ([[Mitochondrial_Supplementation_Protocol_Z-001-0539]])
   - B-vitamins for neuropathy prevention
5. **Monitoring:** Weekly glucose, monthly comprehensive panel

## Section 4: Mechanism-Specific Interventions

### 4.1 Lipotoxicity Protocol
**Markers:** Elevated NEFA, elevated ceramides, hepatic steatosis
**Zettels:** [[Lipotoxicity_Pathway_IR_Z-001-0029]], [[Lipotoxicity_Ceramide_Induced_IR_Z-001-0013]]

**Interventions:**
- Ketogenic diet (reduces hepatic lipid)
- Intermittent fasting (16:8 or 20:4)
- Exercise (increases fatty acid oxidation)
- Supplements: MCT oil, carnitine, choline

### 4.2 Mitochondrial Dysfunction Protocol
**Markers:** Elevated adipic/suberic, low BHB, elevated lactate
**Zettels:** [[Mitochondrial_Dysfunction_IR_Z-001-0015]], [[Mitochondrial_Dysfunction_IR_Z-001-0030]]

**Interventions:**
- [[Mitochondrial_Supplementation_Protocol_Z-001-0539]]
- B-vitamins (B1, B2, B3, B5)
- CoQ10, alpha-lipoic acid
- Ketogenic therapy ([[Ketogenic_Therapy_Metabolic_Inflexibility_Z-001-1122]])

### 4.3 Inflammatory Protocol
**Markers:** Elevated CRP, IL-6, TNF-α; gut dysbiosis
**Zettels:** [[Inflammatory_Signaling_IR_Z-001-0031]], [[Gut_Derived_Mechanisms_IR_Z-001-0032]]

**Interventions:**
- Omega-3 high-dose (4g EPA+DHA)
- Curcumin, resveratrol
- Gut healing protocol ([[Glutamine_Intestinal_Repair_Protocol_Z-001-1224]])
- Probiotics, prebiotics

### 4.4 Stress/Cortisol Protocol
**Markers:** Elevated cortisol, disrupted sleep, abdominal obesity
**Zettels:** [[Vicious_Cycle_Stress_IR_Z-001-0039]], [[Cortisol_SelfTolerance_Breach_Z-001-0052]]

**Interventions:**
- Sleep optimization (target 7-9 hours)
- Stress reduction (meditation, breathwork)
- Phosphatidylserine, ashwagandha
- Morning light exposure

## Section 5: Clinical Decision Trees

### 5.1 Initial Assessment Flowchart

```
Patient presents with metabolic concerns
        ↓
[Order fasting panel: glucose, insulin, lipids, HbA1c]
        ↓
Calculate HOMA-IR + TyG Index
        ↓
┌─────────────────┬─────────────────┬─────────────────┐
│   HOMA-IR <2.0  │  HOMA-IR 2.0-4.5│   HOMA-IR >4.5  │
│   (Normal)      │  (Early/Comp)   │   (Overt)       │
└────────┬────────┴────────┬────────┴────────┬────────┘
         ↓                 ↓                 ↓
   [Maintenance]    [Order OAT]        [Aggressive]
   Protocol         [Assess BHB]       Intervention
                          ↓
                   [Adipic elevated?]
                          ↓
              ┌───────────┴───────────┐
              │         Yes           │
              ↓                       ↓
        [Early IR         [Compensatory
         Protocol]        Protocol]
```

### 5.2 Monitoring Schedule

| Stage | Follow-up | Tests | Decision Point |
|-------|-----------|-------|----------------|
| 0 | Annual | Fasting panel | Escalate if HOMA-IR >2.0 |
| 1 | 8 weeks | HOMA-IR, OAT | Advance if no improvement |
| 2 | 4 weeks | Full panel | Advance if HOMA-IR >4.0 |
| 3 | 2 weeks | Glucose log | Coordinate medical care |

### 5.3 Reversal Criteria

**Metabolic Health Restored (all must be met):**
- [ ] HOMA-IR <2.0 for 3+ months
- [ ] HbA1c <5.7% (if previously elevated)
- [ ] Normal OAT pattern (adipic <15)
- [ ] Fasting BHB >0.1 (metabolic flexibility restored)
- [ ] TG/HDL <1.5
- [ ] Patient symptom resolution

## Section 8: Open Questions & Research Agenda

### 8.1 Unresolved Clinical Questions

1. **Adipic Acid Cutoffs**
   - Current: <15 normal, 15-30 early, 30-60 moderate, >60 severe
   - Need: Population-specific validation, age/gender adjustments
   - Zettel: [[Adipic_Acid_Early_IR_Biomarker_Z-001-0041]]

2. **BHB Supplementation Impact**
   - Question: Does exogenous BHB (ketone esters) improve IR staging?
   - Hypothesis: May provide metabolic flexibility bridge
   - Need: Clinical trial data

3. **TyG Index vs HOMA-IR**
   - TyG = ln[fasting TG (mg/dL) × fasting glucose (mg/dL)/2]
   - May be superior in certain populations
   - Need: Head-to-head comparison study

4. **Optimal Carbohydrate Threshold**
   - Varies by individual (genetics, microbiome, activity)
   - Need: Personalized carb tolerance testing protocol

5. **Exercise Type & Timing**
   - Resistance vs aerobic vs HIIT for different stages
   - Morning vs evening for circadian optimization

### 8.2 METHAP Integration

**Research Opportunity:**
- Use METHAP study to validate adipic acid cutoffs
- Correlate OAT patterns with HOMA-IR progression
