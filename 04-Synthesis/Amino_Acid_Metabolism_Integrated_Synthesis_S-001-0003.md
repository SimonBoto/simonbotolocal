## The Glutamate Hub (L-001-0046B Core)

### Central Position
**Glutamate receives amino groups from most amino acids:**
- Direct: Alanine, aspartate (transamination)
- Indirect: Arginine, proline, histidine (via glutamate)

### Glutamate ↔ α-Ketoglutarate
```
Glutamate + NAD⁺ + H₂O ↔ α-ketoglutarate + NH₄⁺ + NADH
```
**Enzyme:** Glutamate dehydrogenase (GDH)
- **Activated by:** ADP, leucine
- **Inhibited by:** ATP, GTP, NADH

### Nitrogen Disposal
1. **Urea cycle (liver):** Glutamate → urea
2. **Glutamine synthesis (brain/muscle):** Glutamate + NH₃ → glutamine

## Glycogenic vs Ketogenic Classification (L-001-0603 Core)

### The Classification

| Category | Amino Acids | Metabolic Fate |
|----------|-------------|----------------|
| **Purely glucogenic** | 14 AAs (Ala, Arg, Asn, Asp, Cys, Glu, Gln, Gly, His, Met, Pro, Ser, Thr, Val) | Glucose via gluconeogenesis |
| **Purely ketogenic** | Leucine, Lysine | Ketone bodies, fatty acids |
| **Both** | Ile, Phe, Tyr, Trp | Context-dependent |

### Clinical Significance

**Fasting/Starvation:**
- Glycogenic AAs → maintain blood glucose
- Ketogenic AAs → support ketosis when fat stores low
- Muscle protein catabolism provides both

**Ketogenic Diet:**
- High leucine/lysine supports ketosis
- BCAA metabolism critical

## Complete Metabolic Mapping (L-001-0060B Core)

### Master Table: Krebs Entry Points

| Krebs Metabolite | Amino Acids | Notes |
|------------------|-------------|-------|
| **Acetyl-CoA** | Leu, Ile, Trp | Ketogenic fate |
| **α-Ketoglutarate** | Glu, Pro, Arg, His | Glutamate hub |
| **Succinyl-CoA** | Met, Ile, Val, Thr | **B12-dependent** (methylmalonyl-CoA mutase) |
| **Fumarate** | Phe, Tyr | Via phenylalanine hydroxylase |
| **Oxaloacetate** | Asp | Direct transamination |

### Critical BCAA-Succinyl-CoA Pathway

```
Methionine/Isoleucine/Valine/Threonine
    ↓
Propionyl-CoA
    ↓
Methylmalonyl-CoA
    ↓ (methylmalonyl-CoA mutase — B12 required)
Succinyl-CoA → Krebs cycle
```

**Clinical:** B12 deficiency → impaired BCAA metabolism → methylmalonic acidemia

### Threonine: Unique Dual Metabolism
- **Glucogenic:** Propionyl-CoA → succinyl-CoA → glucose
- **Ketogenic:** Can also produce acetyl-CoA

## Synthesis: The Integrated Framework

### The Complete Picture

```
AMINO ACID INPUT
       ↓
[TRANSAMINATION] — B6-dependent
       ↓
α-KETO ACIDS (carbon skeletons)
       ↓
┌─────────────────┬─────────────────┐
│   GLUCOGENIC    │   KETOGENIC     │
│   (14 AAs)      │   (Leu, Lys)    │
│                 │   + dual (4)    │
└────────┬────────┴────────┬────────┘
         ↓                 ↓
   KREBS CYCLE        ACETYL-CoA
   (entry points)     → ketogenesis
         ↓                 ↓
   GLUCONEOGENESIS    FATTY ACIDS
   (glucose output)   (storage)
```

### Key Enzymes and Cofactors

| Process | Enzyme | Cofactor |
|---------|--------|----------|
| Transamination | Aminotransferases | Vitamin B6 (PLP) |
| Oxidative deamination | Glutamate dehydrogenase | NAD⁺/NADP⁺ |
| BCAA catabolism | Methylmalonyl-CoA mutase | **Vitamin B12** |
| Phenylalanine metabolism | Phenylalanine hydroxylase | Iron, tetrahydrobiopterin |

## Clinical Applications

### 1. Maple Syrup Urine Disease (MSUD)
**Defect:** Branched-chain α-keto acid dehydrogenase (BCKDH)
**Accumulation:** α-ketoisocaproate, α-ketoisovalerate, α-keto-β-methylvalerate
**Screening:** Elevated BCAAs, characteristic odor

### 2. B12 Deficiency
**Impaired:** BCAA → succinyl-CoA pathway
**Marker:** Methylmalonic acid elevation
**Consequence:** Metabolic block, neurological symptoms

### 3. Fasting Adaptation
**Glycogenic AAs:** Maintain euglycemia via gluconeogenesis
**Ketogenic AAs:** Support ketone production
**Source:** Muscle protein catabolism (regulated)

### 4. Ketogenic Diet Optimization
**High leucine/lysine:** Support ketosis
**Monitor:** BCAA intake, B12 status
**Balance:** Glucogenic vs ketogenic amino acids
