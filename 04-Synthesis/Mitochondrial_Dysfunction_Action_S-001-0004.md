# Mitochondrial Dysfunction Clinical Action Protocol
## Testing, Interpretation, and Targeted Intervention

---

## Executive Summary

**Purpose:** Systematic approach to identifying and treating mitochondrial dysfunction in clinical practice

**Target Conditions:**
- Chronic fatigue syndrome / ME
- Fibromyalgia
- Metabolic syndrome and insulin resistance
- Neurodegenerative diseases (early stages)
- Autoimmune diseases
- Aging-related decline
- Post-viral fatigue (including post-COVID)

**Core Principle:** Mitochondrial dysfunction is not a diagnosis but a mechanism — identify the pattern, target the intervention

**Evidence Base:** 24+ mitochondria-related zettels, clinical trials, mechanistic studies

---

## Section 1: Mitochondrial Biology for Clinicians

### 1.1 Mitochondrial Structure and Function

**Anatomy:**
- [[Mitochondrial_Membrane_Architecture_Z-001-1214]
- **Outer membrane:** Permeable to small molecules
- **Inner membrane:** Impermeable, contains ETC complexes
- **Cristae:** Folds increasing surface area for ATP synthesis
- **Matrix:** Contains Krebs cycle enzymes, mitochondrial DNA

**Key Functions:**
1. **ATP Production:** Oxidative phosphorylation
2. **Metabolism:** Fatty acid oxidation, Krebs cycle
3. **Calcium Homeostasis:** Buffering intracellular calcium
4. **Apoptosis Regulation:** Cytochrome c release
5. **Reactive Oxygen Species:** Both production and detoxification

### 1.2 Energy Metabolism Overview

**ATP Production Pathways:**

| Pathway | Location | ATP Yield | Clinical Relevance |
|---------|----------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Glycolysis** | Cytoplasm | 2 ATP | Anaerobic; rapid but inefficient |
| **Krebs Cycle** | Mitochondrial matrix | 2 ATP (direct) | Generates reducing equivalents |
| **β-Oxidation** | Mitochondrial matrix | N/A | Produces acetyl-CoA for Krebs |
| **ETC/OXPHOS** | Inner membrane | ~28-34 ATP | Primary ATP source; oxygen-dependent |

**Source:** [[ATP_Yield_Comparison_Z-001-1258]

### 1.3 Mitochondrial Dynamics

**Fusion and Fission:**
- [[Mitochondrial_Dynamics_Fusion_Fission_Z-001-1168]
- **Fusion:** Joining mitochondria (health, recovery)
- **Fission:** Dividing mitochondria (quality control, apoptosis)
- Imbalance → dysfunction

**Mitophagy:**
- Selective degradation of damaged mitochondria
- PINK1/Parkin pathway
- Critical for quality control

**Biogenesis:**
- Creation of new mitochondria
- PGC-1α master regulator
- Stimulated by exercise, cold exposure, fasting
- [[Mitochondrial_Biogenesis_Exercise_PGC1a_Z-001-0555]

### 1.4 Mitochondrial DNA (mtDNA)

**Unique Characteristics:**
- Maternal inheritance
- No introns
- No histone protection
- Higher mutation rate than nuclear DNA
- [[Mitochondrial_DNA_Mutations_Cancer_Z-001-0989]

**Clinical Significance:**
- mtDNA deletions → aging, disease
- Heteroplasmy → variable expression
- [[Mitochondrial_Gene_Knockout_Models_Z-001-0988]

## Section 2: Testing for Mitochondrial Dysfunction

### 2.1 Organic Acids Test (OAT) - Primary Tool

**Collection:** First morning urine
**Method:** GC-MS
**Advantage:** Non-invasive, comprehensive, clinically accessible

**Mitochondrial Markers on OAT:**

| Marker | Normal | Elevated Indicates | Clinical Correlation |
|--------|--------|-------------------|---------------------|
| **Lactic acid** | <20 | Anaerobic metabolism, ETC dysfunction | Fatigue, exercise intolerance |
| **Pyruvic acid** | <10 | B1 deficiency, hypoxia | Neurological symptoms |
| **Citric acid** | Variable | Krebs cycle backup | Metabolic stress |
| **Isocitric acid** | <5 | Impaired isocitrate dehydrogenase | Iron-sulfur cluster issues |
| **α-Ketoglutaric acid** | <10 | B-vitamin deficiency | Cognitive dysfunction |
| **Succinic acid** | <10 | Complex II dysfunction, B2 deficiency | Muscle weakness |
| **Fumaric acid** | <5 | Krebs cycle impairment | Fatigue |
| **Malic acid** | <10 | Mitochondrial stress | Exercise intolerance |
| **Adipic acid** | <15 | Impaired β-oxidation | Early IR, fatigue |
| **Suberic acid** | <10 | Partial β-oxidation | Mitochondrial stress |
| **Ethylmalonic acid** | <2 | Complex III dysfunction | ETC impairment |
| **Methylsuccinic acid** | <2 | B12 deficiency, odd-chain FA oxidation | Neuropathy |

**Source:** [[OAT_Mitochondrial_Patterns_Z-001-1067], [[Organic_Acid_Krebs_Cycle_Markers_Z-001-0537]

### 2.2 Plasma/Serum Markers

**Lactate and Pyruvate:**
- **Lactate:** <2.0 mmol/L (fasting)
- **Pyruvate:** <0.15 mmol/L
- **Lactate:Pyruvate ratio:** <20:1
- Elevated ratio → ETC dysfunction, hypoxia

**BHB (Beta-Hydroxybutyrate):**
- **Fasting:** 0.1-0.5 mmol/L
- **Low:** <0.1 mmol/L indicates impaired ketogenesis
- [[BHB_Hydroxy_Acid_Not_Ketone_Z-001-0483]

**Acylcarnitine Profile:**
- Identifies specific fatty acid oxidation defects
- Requires specialized testing

### 2.3 Functional Assessments

**Exercise Testing:**
- Cardiopulmonary exercise testing (CPET)
- Low VO2 max → mitochondrial limitation
- Early lactate threshold → impaired oxidative capacity

**Metabolic Flexibility:**
- RER (Respiratory Exchange Ratio)
- RER >0.85 at rest → carbohydrate-dependent
- Target: RER 0.70-0.75 in fasting ketosis
- [[Metabolic_Flexibility_RER_Z-001-1012]

**Questionnaire-Based:**
- Fatigue Severity Scale
- Mitochondrial Disease Criteria (modified)

### 2.4 Advanced Testing (Specialized)

**Muscle Biopsy:**
- Gold standard for mitochondrial disease diagnosis
- Ragged red fibers, COX-negative fibers
- Invasive; reserved for severe cases

**Genetic Testing:**
- mtDNA analysis (deletions, mutations)
- Nuclear DNA (mitochondrial genes)
- Consider if: early onset, family history, multisystem involvement

**Mitochondrial Function Assays:**
- Oxygen consumption rate (OCR)
- ATP production rate
- Research use primarily

### 2.5 Pattern Recognition

**Pattern 1: Pure Mitochondrial Dysfunction**
- Elevated lactate, pyruvate
- Multiple Krebs cycle intermediates elevated
- Normal adipic acid
- **Implication:** ETC or Krebs cycle defect

**Pattern 2: Beta-Oxidation Impairment**
- Elevated adipic, suberic, ethylmalonic
- Normal or low BHB
- **Implication:** Fatty acid oxidation defect

**Pattern 3: Combined Dysfunction**
- Elevated markers across categories
- Low BHB
- **Implication:** Severe mitochondrial stress

**Pattern 4: Nutritional Deficiency**
- Elevated pyruvate (B1)
- Elevated succinate (B2)
- Elevated methylmalonic (B12)
- **Implication:** Cofactor deficiency

## Section 3: Clinical Assessment

### 3.1 History-Taking Focus

**Symptoms Suggesting Mitochondrial Dysfunction:**

| System | Symptoms |
|--------|----------|
| **Energy** | Fatigue, exercise intolerance, post-exertional malaise |
| **Musculoskeletal** | Muscle weakness, myalgia, cramping |
| **Neurological** | Brain fog, cognitive decline, headaches, neuropathy |
| **Cardiac** | Palpitations, exercise intolerance |
| **GI** | IBS, constipation, motility issues |
| **Sensory** | Hearing loss, vision changes |
| **Metabolic** | Weight gain, glucose intolerance, temperature dysregulation |

**Red Flags for Primary Mitochondrial Disease:**
- Early onset (<20 years)
- Multisystem involvement
- Progressive course
- Family history
- Neurological + myopathic features

### 3.2 Physical Examination

**Key Findings:**
- Proximal muscle weakness
- Ptosis, ophthalmoplegia
- Short stature
- Hearing loss
- Cardiac arrhythmia

### 3.3 Severity Stratification

**Mild (Functional):**
- 1-2 OAT markers elevated (<2× upper limit)
- Fatigue with exertion
- Normal daily function

**Moderate (Limiting):**
- 3-4 OAT markers elevated (2-3× upper limit)
- Activity limitation
- Cognitive symptoms

**Severe (Disabling):**
- 5+ OAT markers elevated (>3× upper limit)
- Significant disability
- Multiple system involvement

## Section 4: Intervention Protocols

### 4.1 Foundation Protocol (All Patients)

**B-Vitamins (Mitochondrial Cofactors):**

| Vitamin | Dose | Rationale |
|---------|------|-----------|
| **B1 (Thiamine)** | 100-300 mg | Pyruvate dehydrogenase cofactor |
| **B2 (Riboflavin)** | 100-400 mg | FAD precursor, Complex I & II |
| **B3 (Niacin)** | 50-100 mg | NAD+ precursor |
| **B5 (Pantothenate)** | 100-500 mg | CoA synthesis |
| **B6 (Pyridoxine)** | 50-100 mg | Neurotransmitter synthesis |

**Source:** [[B_Vitamin_Cofactors_Summary_Z-001-1202], [[Cofactor_Deficiency_OAT_Signatures_Z-001-1105]

**Minerals:**
- **Magnesium:** 400-600 mg (ATP-Mg complex)
- **Zinc:** 15-30 mg (dehydrogenases)
- **Selenium:** 100-200 mcg (antioxidant defense)
- [[Magnesium_Metabolic_Cofactor_ATP_Function_Z-001-1104]

**Antioxidants:**
- **CoQ10 (Ubiquinol):** 200-400 mg — [[CoQ10_Mitochondrial_Support_Z-001-1217]
- **Alpha-lipoic acid:** 300-600 mg
- **Vitamin E:** 400 IU (mixed tocopherols)
- **Vitamin C:** 1-2 g

**Source:** [[Mitochondrial_Supplementation_Protocol_Z-001-0539]

### 4.2 Targeted Interventions by Pattern

**Pattern 1: ETC Dysfunction (Elevated Lactate)**

**Primary Interventions:**
- CoQ10: 400-600 mg (ubiquinol preferred)
- NAD+ precursors:
  - Nicotinamide riboside: 300-500 mg
  - or NMN: 250-500 mg
- PQQ (Pyrroloquinoline quinone): 10-20 mg
- [[CoQ10_Mitochondrial_Support_Z-001-1217]

**Supporting:**
- B-vitamins (especially B2, B3)
- Magnesium
- Exercise (graded, see below)

**Pattern 2: Beta-Oxidation Defect (Elevated Adipic, Suberic)**

**Primary Interventions:**
- L-Carnitine: 1-3 g
- MCT Oil: 1-2 tablespoons daily
- Ribose: 5 g twice daily
- [[Carnitine_Fatty_Acid_Transport_Z-001-0913]

**Supporting:**
- B-vitamins
- Magnesium
- Ketogenic diet (see below)

**Pattern 3: Krebs Cycle Impairment (Multiple Elevations)**

**Primary Interventions:**
- B-vitamins (high dose)
- Magnesium (high dose)
- Alpha-ketoglutarate: 300-600 mg
- Malic acid: 1-2 g

**Supporting:**
- CoQ10
- Antioxidants

**Pattern 4: Nutritional Deficiency Pattern**

**Targeted Replacement:**
- B1 (if elevated pyruvate): 300-600 mg
- B2 (if elevated succinate): 200-400 mg
- B12 (if elevated methylmalonic): 1000 mcg (methylcobalamin)
- Iron (if isocitrate elevated): Only if deficiency confirmed

### 4.3 Ketogenic Metabolic Therapy

**Rationale:**
- Bypasses impaired glucose metabolism
- Provides alternative fuel (ketones)
- Reduces ROS production
- [[Ketogenic_Therapy_Metabolic_Inflexibility_Z-001-1122]

**Indications:**
- Moderate to severe mitochondrial dysfunction
- Beta-oxidation defects
- Neurological symptoms
- Failed standard interventions

**Protocol:**
- **Standard Ketogenic:** <30g net carbs/day
- **Modified Atkins:** 10-20g carbs/day (easier compliance)
- **Duration:** Minimum 3 months
- **Monitoring:** BHB, lipid panel, kidney function

**Contraindications:**
- Pyruvate carboxylase deficiency
- Primary carnitine deficiency (untreated)
- Porphyria

### 4.4 Exercise Prescription

**Principle:** Stimulate mitochondrial biogenesis without overtaxing

**Mild Dysfunction:**
- Moderate aerobic exercise: 30 min, 5×/week
- Target heart rate: 60-70% max
- Progress gradually

**Moderate Dysfunction:**
- Graded exercise therapy
- Start with 10-15 min low-intensity
- Increase by 10% per week
- Stop before symptom exacerbation

**Severe Dysfunction:**
- Very gentle movement (walking, stretching)
- Focus on not deconditioning
- Consider physical therapy
- Avoid post-exertional malaise

**Key:**
- [[Mitochondrial_Biogenesis_Exercise_PGC1a_Z-001-0555]
- Consistency over intensity
- Monitor for symptom flare

### 4.5 Advanced Interventions

**For Severe/Resistant Cases:**

**IV Nutrient Therapy:**
- Myers' Cocktail (modified)
- High-dose vitamin C
- Glutathione
- **Caution:** Requires medical supervision

**Ozone Therapy:**
- Autohemotherapy
- Stimulates antioxidant response
- Controversial; limited evidence

**Hyperbaric Oxygen:**
- Increases tissue oxygenation
- May improve ETC function
- Expensive; limited access

**Peptides (Research):**
- SS-31 (elamipretide)
- Targets cardiolipin
- Clinical trials ongoing

## Section 5: Monitoring and Follow-up

### 5.1 Initial Follow-up (4-6 weeks)

**Assessment:**
- Symptom improvement (patient report)
- Tolerance of supplements
- Adherence check
- Side effects

**Testing (if available):**
- Repeat OAT (optional; often premature)
- BHB (if ketogenic)

**Adjustments:**
- Increase doses if tolerated
- Add targeted interventions based on response
- Address adherence barriers

### 5.2 Three-Month Follow-up

**Assessment:**
- Comprehensive symptom review
- Functional status
- Quality of life

**Testing:**
- Repeat OAT (primary)
- Lactate/pyruvate (if previously elevated)
- BHB (metabolic flexibility)

**Success Criteria:**
- 50% reduction in OAT markers
- Improved energy/function
- Tolerating interventions

### 5.3 Long-term Monitoring

**Every 6 Months:**
- OAT repeat
- Symptom assessment
- Protocol adjustment

**Annual:**
- Comprehensive metabolic panel
- Lipid panel
- Consider genetic testing if indicated

### 5.4 When to Escalate

**Refer to Specialist:**
- Primary mitochondrial disease suspected
- Progressive neurological decline
- Cardiac involvement
- Pediatric cases

**Consider IV Therapy:**
- Severe dysfunction not responding to oral
- Significant disability
- Medical supervision available

## Section 6: Special Populations

### 6.1 Chronic Fatigue Syndrome / ME

**Characteristics:**
- Post-exertional malaise (hallmark)
- Exercise intolerance
- Often normal basic labs

**Approach:**
- Very gradual exercise introduction
- Pacing critical
- Higher dose mitochondrial support
- Address sleep, stress

### 6.2 Post-COVID Syndrome

**Characteristics:**
- Persistent fatigue
- Exercise intolerance
- Cognitive impairment
- Often mitochondrial involvement

**Approach:**
- Anti-inflammatory support
- Gradual rehabilitation
- Monitor for autoimmunity

### 6.3 Aging

**Characteristics:**
- Declining mitochondrial function
- Reduced biogenesis
- Accumulated mtDNA damage

**Approach:**
- Emphasize biogenesis stimulation
- Exercise critical
- Higher antioxidant support
- NAD+ precursors

### 6.4 Athletes

**Characteristics:**
- High mitochondrial demand
- Performance limited by mitochondrial capacity

**Approach:**
- Optimize for performance
- Periodization of support
- Monitor for overtraining

## Section 7: Integration with Other Protocols

### 7.1 Insulin Resistance

**Overlap:**
- Mitochondrial dysfunction common in IR
- Adipic acid marker shared

**Integration:**
- Use [[IR_Playbook_Clinical_Decision_Tool_S-001-0001]
- Add mitochondrial support to IR protocol
- Monitor both OAT and HOMA-IR

### 7.2 Autoimmune Disease

**Overlap:**
- Mitochondrial dysfunction in immune cells
- Energy failure in affected tissues

**Integration:**
- Mitochondrial support + autoimmune protocol
- Higher antioxidant doses
- Address gut (common trigger)

### 7.3 Neurodegenerative Disease

**Overlap:**
- Brain highly dependent on mitochondrial function
- Early mitochondrial dysfunction precedes symptoms

**Integration:**
- Aggressive mitochondrial support
- Ketogenic diet often beneficial
- Address other risk factors

## Section 8: Evidence Summary

### 8.1 Supplementation Evidence

**CoQ10:**
- Multiple trials in mitochondrial disease
- Modest benefit in fatigue
- Dose: 300-1200 mg/day
- [[CoQ10_Mitochondrial_Support_Z-001-1217]

**B-Vitamins:**
- Case series in mitochondrial dysfunction
- Safe, low cost
- Dose: High-dose B-complex

**Ketogenic Diet:**
- Established in epilepsy
- Emerging in neurodegeneration
- Case reports in mitochondrial disease

**Exercise:**
- Stimulates biogenesis
- Improves function
- Must be graded

### 8.2 Gaps in Evidence

- Limited RCTs in "secondary" mitochondrial dysfunction
- Optimal dosing not established
- Long-term outcomes unclear
- Individual variation poorly characterized
