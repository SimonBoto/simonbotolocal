# METHAP Protocol
## Metabolomics of High-Dose Vitamin D in Adipic Acid Pathways

---

## Executive Summary

| Element | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Study Title** | Metabolomics of High-Dose Vitamin D in Adipic Acid Pathways (METHAP) |
| **Study Type** | Single-arm, open-label, before-after intervention study |
| **Duration** | 12 weeks intervention + 4 weeks follow-up |
| **Sample Size** | 40 participants (target) |
| **Primary Endpoint** | Change in urinary adipic acid levels |
| **Secondary Endpoints** | Metabolic flexibility, insulin sensitivity, inflammatory markers |
| **Intervention** | Vitamin D3 10,000 IU daily + Vitamin K2 (MK-7) 200 mcg |
| **Phase** | Pre-IRB submission |
| **Status** | Ethics framework complete, protocol finalized |

**Research Question:** Does high-dose vitamin D3 supplementation improve mitochondrial fatty acid oxidation, as evidenced by reduced urinary adipic acid and improved metabolic flexibility in adults with suboptimal vitamin D status?

### 1.2 Knowledge Gap

Current evidence demonstrates:
1. Vitamin D deficiency associated with insulin resistance
2. Adipic acid elevation indicates impaired fatty acid oxidation
3. Metabolic flexibility predicts metabolic health outcomes

**Gap:** No prospective study has examined whether vitamin D optimization improves mitochondrial fatty acid oxidation markers (adipic acid) and metabolic flexibility.

### 1.3 Hypothesis

**Primary Hypothesis:** 12 weeks of high-dose vitamin D3 (10,000 IU/day) will significantly reduce urinary adipic acid levels, indicating improved mitochondrial β-oxidation capacity.

**Secondary Hypotheses:**
- Vitamin D supplementation will improve metabolic flexibility (BHB response to fasting)
- Vitamin D supplementation will reduce inflammatory markers (CRP, IL-6)
- Vitamin D supplementation will improve insulin sensitivity (HOMA-IR)

---

## Section 2: Study Objectives

### 2.1 Primary Objective
To evaluate the effect of 12-week high-dose vitamin D3 supplementation on urinary adipic acid levels in adults with suboptimal vitamin D status.

### 2.2 Secondary Objectives
1. Assess changes in metabolic flexibility (fasting BHB, RER if available)
2. Evaluate changes in insulin sensitivity (HOMA-IR, fasting glucose/insulin)
3. Measure changes in inflammatory markers (CRP, IL-6, TNF-α)
4. Assess changes in lipid profile (TG, HDL, LDL, TG/HDL ratio)
5. Evaluate safety and tolerability of high-dose vitamin D
6. Correlate vitamin D status changes with metabolic outcomes

### 2.3 Exploratory Objectives
1. Identify metabolomic signatures responsive to vitamin D supplementation
2. Examine gut microbiome changes (if funding permits)
3. Assess quality of life changes (SF-36 questionnaire)

## Section 3: Study Design

### 3.1 Design Overview

**Type:** Single-arm, open-label, before-after intervention study

**Rationale for Single-Arm Design:**
- Primary outcome (adipic acid) is objective biomarker
- Each participant serves as own control (before-after)
- Ethical considerations: Withholding vitamin D from deficient participants problematic
- Vitamin D supplementation standard of care for deficiency

**Timeline:**
```
Week -2: Screening, baseline assessment
Week 0: Randomization (if applicable), intervention start
Week 4: Follow-up visit 1
Week 8: Follow-up visit 2
Week 12: End of intervention, primary endpoint
Week 16: Follow-up assessment (4 weeks post-intervention)
```

### 3.2 Study Population

**Inclusion Criteria:**
- Age 25-65 years
- BMI 25-35 kg/m² (overweight/obese)
- 25(OH)D level 30-75 nmol/L (insufficient to suboptimal)
- Fasting glucose <126 mg/dL (no diabetes diagnosis)
- Willing to maintain stable diet and exercise habits
- Able to provide informed consent

**Exclusion Criteria:**
- [[METHAP_Inclusion_Exclusion_Criteria_[Z-002-0618]]]
- Known hypercalcemia or hyperparathyroidism
- Kidney disease (eGFR <60)
- Liver disease (ALT/AST >3x ULN)
- Malabsorption disorders
- Pregnancy or lactation
- Use of thiazide diuretics or digoxin
- Use of anticoagulants (warfarin — K2 interaction)
- Current use of vitamin D >2,000 IU/day
- Recent (within 3 months) participation in other intervention studies

**Recruitment Strategy:**
- [[METHAP_Participant_Selection_Sample_Size_[Z-002-0617]]]
- [[METHAP_Population_Recruitment_Strategy_[Z-001-1141]]]
- Clinic patient database
- Social media advertising
- Community partnerships

### 3.3 Sample Size Justification

**Primary Endpoint:** Change in adipic acid (continuous variable)

**Assumptions:**
- Baseline adipic acid: mean 35 μmol/mmol creatinine (SD 15)
- Expected reduction: 20% (7 μmol/mmol creatinine)
- SD of change: 12
- Alpha: 0.05 (two-tailed)
- Power: 80%
- Dropout rate: 20%

**Calculation:**
- Required per group: 32
- With dropout: 40 participants

**Software:** G*Power 3.1.9.7 — [[Power_Analysis_Sample_Size_METHAP_[Z-001-1149]]]

## Section 4: Intervention

### 4.1 Study Supplements

**Vitamin D3 (Cholecalciferol):**
- Dose: 10,000 IU (250 mcg) daily
- Form: Softgel capsules
- Timing: Morning with fat-containing meal
- Duration: 12 weeks
- Source: [To be specified — pharmaceutical grade]

**Vitamin K2 (MK-7):**
- Dose: 200 mcg daily
- Rationale: [[Vitamin_K2_Vascular_Calcification_MGP_[Z-001-0228]]]
- Prevents vascular calcification from high-dose D
- Synergistic effect on metabolic outcomes
- Timing: Same as D3

### 4.2 Dosing Rationale

**Why 10,000 IU?**
- [[Vitamin_D_RDA_10x_Underestimation_[Z-001-0059]]]
- [[Vitamin_D_Optimal_Range_80_150_[Z-002-0004]]]
- Safe upper limit: 10,000 IU/day (Endocrine Society)
- Required to raise 25(OH)D to optimal range (80-150 nmol/L)
- Lower doses insufficient for metabolic effects

**Safety Monitoring:**
- [[High_Dose_Vitamin_D_Monitoring_Protocol_[Z-001-0075]]]
- [[Vitamin_D_Safety_Research_Summary_[Z-001-1075]]]
- Calcium levels at weeks 4, 8, 12
- 25(OH)D at weeks 4, 8, 12
- Discontinue if 25(OH)D >250 nmol/L or calcium >10.5 mg/dL

### 4.3 Compliance Monitoring

- Pill count at each visit
- Participant diary (daily intake)
- 25(OH)D level as objective compliance marker
- <80% compliance = per-protocol exclusion

## Section 6: Statistical Analysis Plan

### 6.1 Analysis Populations

**Intention-to-Treat (ITT):** All randomized participants
**Per-Protocol (PP):** Completed intervention with >80% compliance
**Safety:** All participants who took at least one dose

### 6.2 Primary Analysis

**Change in Adipic Acid (Week 12 vs Week 0):**
- Paired t-test (if normally distributed)
- Wilcoxon signed-rank test (if non-normal)
- Report: Mean change (95% CI), p-value
- Effect size: Cohen's d

### 6.3 Secondary Analyses

**Continuous Outcomes (Week 12 vs Week 0):**
- Paired tests for all secondary endpoints
- Bonferroni correction for multiple comparisons

**Correlation Analyses:**
- Change in 25(OH)D vs change in adipic acid
- Baseline 25(OH)D vs treatment response
- [[Multivariate_Analysis_Pitfalls_[Z-001-0517]]]

**Subgroup Analyses (exploratory):**
- Baseline vitamin D status (insufficient vs insufficient)
- BMI category (overweight vs obese)
- Age (<50 vs ≥50 years)

### 6.4 Missing Data

- Last observation carried forward (LOCF) for primary analysis
- Sensitivity analysis with multiple imputation

### 6.5 Software

- R version 4.x or SPSS
- G*Power for sample size
- MetaboAnalyst for metabolomics (if applicable)

## Section 8: Data Safety and Monitoring

### 8.1 Safety Assessments

**Hypervitaminosis D Monitoring:**
- 25(OH)D at weeks 4, 8, 12
- Serum calcium at weeks 4, 8, 12
- PTH if calcium elevated
- [[Vitamin_D_Toxicity_Myth_Persistence_[Z-001-0057]]]

**Stopping Rules:**
- 25(OH)D >250 nmol/L
- Serum calcium >10.5 mg/dL
- Symptomatic hypercalcemia
- Participant request
- Investigator discretion

### 8.2 Adverse Event Reporting

**Definition:** Any untoward medical occurrence

**Classification:**
- Mild: No intervention required
- Moderate: Intervention required
- Severe: Hospitalization or disability
- Serious: Life-threatening or fatal

**Reporting:**
- Immediate: Serious adverse events (24 hours to IRB)
- Periodic: All adverse events (quarterly)

### 8.3 Data Safety Monitoring

**DSMB:** Independent physician (if multi-site) or PI monitoring (single-site)

**Review Schedule:**
- Interim analysis at 50% enrollment
- Safety review at each adverse event
- Final safety report
