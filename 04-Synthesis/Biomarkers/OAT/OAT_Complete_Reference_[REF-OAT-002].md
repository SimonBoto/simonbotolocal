## 1. KREBS CYCLE — Energy Production

| Metabolite | Optimal Range | Unit | Key Interpretation |
|------------|---------------|------|-------------------|
| **Citric** | 67.0 – 171.0 | mmol/mol Crea | Metabolic flexibility indicator; low = nutrient deficiency, high = block |
| **Aconitic** | 6.0 – 22.0 | mmol/mol Crea | Aconitase checkpoint; citrate:aconitic ratio <20% difference |
| **Isocitric** | 3.0 – 6.5 | mmol/mol Crea | Oxidative stress marker |
| **2-Ketoglutaric (α-KG)** | 2.7 – 10.5 | mmol/mol Crea | Nitrogen metabolism hub |
| **Succinic** | 1.4 – 10.0 | mmol/mol Crea | Complex II/SDH, hypoxia marker |
| **Fumaric** | Not detectable | mmol/mol Crea | Any detection = stress |
| **Malic** | Not detectable | mmol/mol Crea | Any detection = stress |

### Critical Pattern: Aconitase Block
**Finding:** Low citrate + very high aconitate  
**Action:** STOP — do not interpret downstream metabolites  
**Fix:** Antioxidants, glutamine, iron if deficient  
**Retest:** 6-8 weeks

## 2. CARBOHYDRATE METABOLISM

| Metabolite | Optimal Range | Unit | Key Interpretation |
|------------|---------------|------|-------------------|
| **Lactic** | < 25.0 | mmol/mol Crea | Anaerobic metabolism; high = hypoxia, mitochondrial dysfunction |
| **Pyruvic** | < 15.0 | mmol/mol Crea | Glycolysis endpoint; high with low citrate = PDH impairment, IR |
| **3-Hydroxybutyric** | Not detectable | mmol/mol Crea | Ketone body; detectable = ketosis (fasting or carb excess/IR) |

### Patterns
- **Easy metabolism:** Low lactic, low pyruvic, no ketones
- **Difficulty:** High 3-hydroxybutyric = excess carbs, insulin resistance, lipoic acid/B-vitamin deficiency
- **Pyruvate block:** High pyruvate + low citrate = impaired Krebs entry, fat accumulation

## 5. NEUROTRANSMITTER METABOLITES

| Metabolite | Optimal Range | Unit | Neurotransmitter | Function |
|------------|---------------|------|------------------|----------|
| **HVA (Homovanillic)** | 1.2 – 3.6 | mmol/mol Crea | Dopamine | Reward, euphoria, sympathetic (alertness, action) |
| **5-HIAA** | 1.0 – 4.0 | mmol/mol Crea | Serotonin | Thinking, introspection, parasympathetic (sleep, digestion) |
| **VMA (Vanillylmandelic)** | 1.0 – 1.6 | mmol/mol Crea | Adrenaline/Noradrenaline | Stress response, energy compensation |
| **2-Hydroxyisobutyric** | < 5.0 | mmol/mol Crea | GABA | Emotion control, pain, gut motility |

### Critical Patterns

#### HVA:5-HIAA Ratio
- **Ideal:** HVA slightly higher than 5-HIAA
- **Disturbed ratio:** Excessive mental work (computer, reading), need outdoor activity + B-vitamins
- **Both elevated:** High mental activity, stress

#### VMA + Citrate Clinical Pairing
| Citrate | VMA | Symptom | Interpretation |
|---------|-----|---------|---------------|
| Low | High | Hunger | Adrenal compensation working — energy okay |
| Low | Low | Fatigue | **Adrenal fatigue** — no compensation, mitochondria failing |
| Low | Normal | Variable | Intermediate — partial compensation |

**Key insight:** VMA shows if the body is responding to mitochondrial dysfunction (citrate low) with adrenal activation, or if both systems are failing.
