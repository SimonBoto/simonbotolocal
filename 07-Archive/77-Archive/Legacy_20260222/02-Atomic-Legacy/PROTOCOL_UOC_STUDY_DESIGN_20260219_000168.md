# UoC PhD Study Design — Metabolomics Approach to IR

## Overview

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Design** | Case-control comparative study |
| **Groups** | 2 distinct groups |
| **Primary Analysis** | GC-MS metabolomics |
| **Secondary Analysis** | Statistical modeling (PCA, multivariate, network) |

## Study Population

### Group 1: Healthy Controls
- **Inclusion:** No insulin resistance, normal metabolic parameters
- **Matching:** Age, sex matched to cases

### Group 2: Insulin Resistant Cases
- **Inclusion:** Established insulin resistance (by HOMA-IR or clinical criteria)
- **Exclusion:** Diabetes mellitus (to focus on pre-diabetic IR)

## Biomaterials

| Sample Type | Biomarkers | Analysis Method |
|-------------|------------|-----------------|
| **Urine** | Organic acids | GC-MS |
| **Plasma** | Free fatty acids | GC-MS |
| **Blood** | Glucose, insulin, HbA1c | Standard clinical chemistry |

## Intervention Arm (Secondary)

**Dietary Modifications:**
- Mediterranean diet optimization
- Omega-3 supplementation
- Vitamin D supplementation (if deficient)
- Magnesium supplementation

**Duration:** Not specified in protocol (assumed 12 weeks based on standard designs)

## Analytical Methods

### GC-MS Metabolomics
- **Platform:** Gas Chromatography-Mass Spectrometry
- **Target:** Comprehensive metabolic profiling
- **Data Volume:** Large dataset requiring advanced statistical handling

### Statistical Analysis
1. **PCA** (Principal Component Analysis) — Dimensionality reduction, pattern detection
2. **Multivariate Analysis** — Regression modeling, biomarker identification
3. **Network Analysis** — Metabolic pathway interactions

## Expected Deliverables

1. **Metabolomic Signatures:** Distinctive patterns differentiating IR from controls
2. **Early Biomarkers:** Markers that change before conventional diagnostics
3. **Intervention Response:** Metabolic changes with diet/supplements
4. **Clinical Algorithm:** Decision tree for early IR detection
