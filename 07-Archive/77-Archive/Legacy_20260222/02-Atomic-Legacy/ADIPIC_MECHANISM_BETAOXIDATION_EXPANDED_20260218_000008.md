# Adipic Acid: Mitochondrial Beta-Oxidation Mechanism

## Core Mechanism

Adipic acid (hexanedioic acid) accumulates when mitochondrial fatty acid beta-oxidation is impaired. Under normal conditions, fatty acids undergo beta-oxidation in mitochondria, producing acetyl-CoA for energy. When this pathway is blocked:

```
Fatty Acids → [Blocked Beta-Oxidation] → 
→ Omega-Oxidation (Cytochrome P450) → 
→ Dicarboxylic Acids (Adipic, Suberic, Sebacic)
```

## Why It Matters for IR

1. **Carnitine Shuttle Defect**: Insufficient carnitine prevents fatty acid transport into mitochondria
2. **Mitochondrial Dysfunction**: Damaged or overloaded mitochondria cannot process fatty acids
3. **Compensatory Pathway**: Omega-oxidation in peroxisomes produces dicarboxylic acids as alternative endpoint
