# Ref: Clayton et al. (2006) — Pharmaco-Metabonomic Phenotyping

## TL;DR
Landmark study demonstrating that pre-dose metabolite profiles can predict drug metabolism and toxicity, establishing the foundation for pharmacometabolomics and personalized drug treatment based on metabolic phenotype.

## Citation
Clayton, T. A. et al. (2006) 'Pharmaco-metabonomic phenotyping and personalized drug treatment', Nature, 440(7087), pp. 1073–1077. doi: 10.1038/nature04648.

## Key Findings

| Finding | Evidence | Relevance to METHAP |
|---------|----------|---------------------|
| Pre-dose metabolome predicts drug fate | Rat paracetamol study | Metabolic profiling enables personalized dosing |
| Metabolic phenotype > genotype for drug response | Urinary metabolite patterns | VD response may depend on baseline metabolic state |
| Toxicity prediction possible | Liver toxicity biomarkers | Safety monitoring via metabolomics |
| Individual variability quantifiable | Multivariate statistical models | Protocol for IR variability assessment |

## Methods
| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Design** | Pre-clinical rat study + proof-of-concept human analysis |
| **Model** | Paracetamol (acetaminophen) metabolism |
| **Analysis** | NMR spectroscopy + pattern recognition |
| **Outcomes** | Drug metabolism, toxicity prediction |

## Relevance to PhD

**METHAP Trial:**
- **Biomarker potential:** Pre-intervention metabolome predicts response
- **Mechanism insight:** Baseline metabolic state determines VD efficacy
- **Study design:** Justifies stratification by baseline metabolome
- **Safety:** Metabolomics for toxicity/adverse event monitoring

**Key Quote**
> "The metabolic profile of an individual can predict their response to drug treatment before administration, enabling personalized therapeutic decisions."
