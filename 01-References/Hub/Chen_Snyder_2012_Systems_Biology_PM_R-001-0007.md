# Ref: Chen & Snyder (2012) — Systems Biology and Personalized Medicine Future

## TL;DR
Foundational review exploring how systems biology approaches will transform medicine from reactive to predictive, preventive, personalized, and participatory (P4 medicine).

## Citation
Chen R. and Snyder M (2012), 'Systems biology: personalized medicine for the future?', Current Opinion in Pharmacology, 12(5) pp 623-628. doi: 10.1016/j.coph.2012.07.002

## Key Findings

| Finding | Evidence | Relevance to METHAP |
|---------|----------|---------------------|
| P4 medicine paradigm | Predictive, Preventive, Personalized, Participatory | METHAP framework: predict IR risk, prevent progression |
| Multi-omics integration essential | iPOP (integrative Personal Omics Profile) study | Longitudinal metabolomics approach |
| Dynamic monitoring superior | Single timepoint insufficient | METHAP: multiple timepoints (baseline, 3mo, 6mo) |
| Personal baselines required | Population reference ranges inadequate | Individual metabolite trajectories |

## Relevance to PhD

**METHAP Trial Design:**
- **Predictive:** Baseline metabolome predicts IR progression
- **Preventive:** VD intervention before overt diabetes
- **Personalized:** Dose based on metabolic phenotype
- **Participatory:** Patient engagement with metabolite feedback
