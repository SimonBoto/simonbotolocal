# Reaven 1988 — Banting Lecture

**The single most important takeaway:** Gerald Reaven introduced "Syndrome X" (now metabolic syndrome), establishing insulin resistance as the central pathophysiological feature connecting obesity, hypertension, dyslipidemia, and glucose intolerance — not as separate diseases but as manifestations of a common underlying defect.

---

## Abstract

Reaven's Banting Lecture synthesized evidence that insulin resistance is the fundamental defect in what he termed "Syndrome X." He demonstrated that:
1. Insulin resistance precedes and predicts type 2 diabetes
2. Multiple metabolic abnormalities cluster in insulin-resistant individuals
3. Compensatory hyperinsulinemia drives the associated cardiovascular risk factors
4. The syndrome has genetic underpinnings but is amplified by environmental factors

This lecture established the modern understanding of metabolic syndrome as an insulin resistance syndrome.

## Key Findings

| Finding | Evidence | Relevance |
|---------|----------|-----------|
| Insulin resistance is primary | Euglycemic clamp studies | Foundation for all subsequent IR research |
| Hyperinsulinemia compensates | Cross-sectional population data | Explains "normal" glucose in early IR |
| Clustering of risk factors | Factor analysis of risk variables | Metabolic syndrome concept born |
| Genetic + environmental | Family studies, migration studies | Nature vs nurture in IR |
| CVD risk independent of glucose | Prospective data | IR dangerous even before diabetes |
