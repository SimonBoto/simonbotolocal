# Ref: Savage, Petersen & Shulman (2007) — Cellular Mechanisms of IR

## TL;DR
Molecular mechanisms linking lipid accumulation to insulin resistance: diacylglycerol (DAG) activates novel PKCs causing IRS-1 serine phosphorylation, while ceramide promotes apoptosis and inflammation. Demonstrates causality between ectopic lipid and insulin signaling defects.

## Citation
Savage, D. B., Petersen, K. F. and Shulman, G. I. (2007) 'Disordered lipid metabolism and the pathogenesis of insulin resistance', Physiological Reviews, 87(2), pp. 507–520. doi: 10.1152/physrev.00024.2006.

## Key Findings

| Finding | Evidence | Relevance to METHAP |
|---------|----------|---------------------|
| DAG → PKC → IRS-1 Ser307 | Cell culture, rodent models | Core lipotoxicity mechanism |
| Ceramide induces IR | Pharmacological elevation | Apoptosis link to beta-cell failure |
| Ectopic lipid causality | MRS lipid quantification + clamp | Adipic acid as proxy for ectopic lipid |
| Muscle lipid correlates with IR | In vivo human studies | T2D pathogenesis mechanism |
