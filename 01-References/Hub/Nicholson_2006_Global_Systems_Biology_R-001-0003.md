# Ref: Nicholson (2006) — Global Systems Biology & Personalized Medicine

## TL;DR
Foundational framework connecting systems biology approaches to personalized medicine and population health (molecular epidemiology), with metabolomics as the key bridge between individual physiology and environmental exposures.

## Citation
Nicholson, J. K. (2006) 'Global systems biology, personalized medicine and molecular epidemiology.', Molecular systems biology, p. 52. doi: 10.1038/msb4100095.

## Key Findings

| Finding | Evidence | Relevance to METHAP |
|---------|----------|---------------------|
| Systems biology enables personalized medicine | Integration of multi-omics data | METHAP framework for individualized VD therapy |
| Metabolomics bridges individual & population | Molecular epidemiology approach | Scale from individual IR to population patterns |
| Environmental exposure mapping | Metabolome captures exposures | VD status as environmental factor |
| Dynamic phenotype monitoring | Longitudinal metabolomic changes | Trial outcome measurement strategy |

## Relevance to PhD

**METHAP Trial:**
- **Biomarker potential:** Metabolites as dynamic health indicators
- **Mechanism insight:** Systems approach to IR pathophysiology

**Key Quote**
> "Systems biology provides the conceptual and technological framework for translating molecular information into personalized clinical decisions at the population level."
