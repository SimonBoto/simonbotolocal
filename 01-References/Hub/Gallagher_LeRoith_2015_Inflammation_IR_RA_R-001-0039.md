# Ref: Gallagher & LeRoith (2015) — Inflammation and IR in RA

## TL;DR
Chronic inflammation in rheumatoid arthritis promotes insulin resistance through cytokine-mediated serine phosphorylation of IRS-1. TNF-α, IL-6, and IL-1β directly interfere with insulin signaling, creating a mechanistic link between autoimmune inflammation and metabolic disease.

## Citation
Gallagher, E. J. and LeRoith, D. (2015) 'Inflammation and insulin resistance: New targets for rheumatoid arthritis therapy', Cell Metabolism, 22(3), pp. 361–363. doi: 10.1016/j.cmet.2015.08.003.

## Key Findings

| Finding | Evidence | Relevance to METHAP |
|---------|----------|---------------------|
| TNF-α induces IR | RA patients + TNF inhibitors | Inflammatory cytokine mechanism |
| IL-6 blocks insulin signaling | Human cell studies | CRP correlation with IR |
| IL-1β promotes IRS degradation | Macrophage experiments | Inflammasome connection |
| Anti-TNF improves insulin sensitivity | Clinical trials in RA | Treating inflammation restores sensitivity |
