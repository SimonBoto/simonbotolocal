# 5 Modifiable Risk Factors Add 14.5 Years of Life — NEJM 2025

## Source
- **Original:** 5 Παράγοντες Κινδύνου που Μπορούν να Τροποποιηθούν και Συνδέονται με έως 14,5 Επιπλέον Χρόνια Ζωής
- **English:** "5 Modifiable Risk Factors Linked to Up to 14.5 Extra Years of Life"
- **Study:** NEJM, March 30, 2025 — Global Cardiovascular Risk Consortium
- **Scale:** 2 million people, 39 countries

## The Five Factors

| Factor | Modifiable? |
|--------|-------------|
| **High blood pressure** | ✅ Yes |
| **Cholesterol** | ✅ Yes (context-dependent) |
| **Abnormal body weight** (very low or high) | ✅ Yes |
| **Diabetes** | ✅ Yes |
| **Smoking** | ✅ Yes |

## Longevity Impact

### With All 5 Factors vs. None
| Sex | Extra Years | Healthy Years |
|-----|-------------|---------------|
| **Women** | ~14 years | 13.3 years |
| **Men** | ~12 years | 10.6 years |

### CVD Risk with All 5 Factors
| Sex | Lifetime CVD Risk |
|-----|-------------------|
| **Women** | 1 in 4 (25%) |
| **Men** | 1 in 3 (33%) |

## Hierarchy of Impact

| Rank | Factor | Impact on Longevity |
|------|--------|---------------------|
| **1** | **Smoking cessation** | Greatest benefit |
| **2** | **Hypertension control** | Second greatest |
| **3-5** | Diabetes, weight, cholesterol | Significant but less |

## Surprising Findings

### Cholesterol
| Finding | Explanation |
|---------|-------------|
| **No clear difference** | Both very low and very high associated with risk |
| **U-shaped curve** | Optimal range matters |
| **Context-dependent** | Less important when other factors controlled |

### Body Weight
| Finding | Explanation |
|---------|-------------|
| **No significant difference alone** | Often accompanied by diabetes and hypertension |
| **Confounded** | Other factors affect risk more directly |

## Modification Pathways

| Factor | Intervention |
|--------|--------------|
| **Hypertension** | Lifestyle, diet, micronutrient repletion |
| **Diabetes** | Carbohydrate restriction, weight loss |
| **Weight** | Dietary changes, metabolic optimization |
| **Smoking** | Cessation programs, metabolic support |
| **Cholesterol** | Address when other factors uncontrolled |

## Key Insight

> "When the other four factors are controlled, cholesterol does not appear to have particular significance on its own."

## Evidence

> "Study of 2 million people across 39 countries found that those without any of the five risk factors lived ~14 extra years for women and ~12 for men. Smoking cessation showed the greatest benefit, followed by hypertension control."
> — NEJM, March 2025
