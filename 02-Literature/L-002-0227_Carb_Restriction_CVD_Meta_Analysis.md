# Carbohydrate Restriction Reduces CVD Risk — Meta-Analysis of 174 RCTs

## Source
- **Original:** Ο Περιορισμός των Υδατανθράκων Μειώνει τον Καρδιαγγειακό Κίνδυνο
- **English:** "Carbohydrate Restriction Reduces Cardiovascular Risk"
- **Study:** Largest meta-analysis to date — 174 randomized controlled trials

## Key Findings

### Metabolic Benefits
| Marker | Change |
|--------|--------|
| **Triglycerides** | ↓ Reduced |
| **Systolic BP** | ↓ Reduced |
| **Diastolic BP** | ↓ Reduced |
| **TG/HDL ratio** | ↓ Improved |
| **LDL/HDL ratio** | ↓ Improved |
| **ApoB/ApoA1 ratio** | ↓ Improved |
| **Inflammation (CRP, TNF-α, IL-6)** | ↓ Reduced |
| **HDL cholesterol** | ↑ Increased |

### Lipid Changes
| Marker | Change | Interpretation |
|--------|--------|----------------|
| **LDL cholesterol** | ↑ +5 mg/dL | Minor, context matters |
| **Total cholesterol** | ↑ +4 mg/dL | Minor, outweighed by benefits |

> These increases do **not negate overall metabolic benefits** when accompanied by improvement in other markers.

### Body Composition
| Measure | Change |
|---------|--------|
| **Body weight** | ↓ Reduced |
| **Body fat** | ↓ Reduced |
| **BMI** | ↓ Reduced |
| **Waist circumference** | ↓ Reduced |

## Carbohydrate Restriction Categories

| Category | Carb Intake |
|----------|-------------|
| **Moderate reduction** | 26-45% of calories |
| **Low-carb** | 10-26% of calories |
| **Ketogenic** | <10% of calories |

### Ketogenic Specific Effects
| Outcome | Result |
|---------|--------|
| **Weight loss** | Greater than moderate reduction |
| **LDL increase** | Larger than other categories |
| **Overall CVD risk** | Not increased (despite higher LDL) |

## Critical Insight: Carb Replacement

| Replacement | Benefit |
|-------------|---------|
| **Carbs → Fats** | Largest benefits |
| **Carbs → Protein** | Significant benefits |

## Mechanism

Improved metabolic markers (TG, HDL, BP, inflammation) outweigh minor LDL increases, resulting in **net CVD risk reduction**.

## Evidence

> "Low-carbohydrate diets showed significant benefits for metabolic health. When LDL increase is accompanied by improvement in TG/HDL, BP, and inflammation, it is not associated with increased cardiovascular risk."
> — Meta-analysis of 174 RCTs
