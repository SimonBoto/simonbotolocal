## Core Concepts Extracted

### 1. Insulin Receptor Activation
> "Once the tyrosine kinase of insulin receptor is activated, it promotes autophosphorylation of the β subunit, where phosphorylation of three tyrosine residues (Tyr-1158, Tyr-1162, and Tyr-1163) is required for amplification of the kinase activity."

**Zettel extracted:** [[Z_MECH_INSR_AUTOPHOS_003]]

### 2. IRS Recruitment
> "The insulin receptor tyrosine kinase phosphorylates the IRS proteins, and phosphotyrosine residues on IRS proteins become good targets for the p85 regulatory subunit of PI3-kinase."

**Zettel extracted:** [[Z_IRS_RECRUITMENT_005]]

### 3. PI3K-Akt Pathway
> "Most of the metabolic and antiapoptotic effects of insulin are mediated by the signaling pathway involving the phosphorylation of the insulin receptor substrate (IRS) proteins, and the activation of the phosphatidylinositol (PI) 3-kinase, Akt..."

**Zettels extracted:** [[Z_MECH_PI3K_ACTIVATION_007]], [[Z_AKT_RECRUITMENT_PIP3_009]], [[Z_AKT_SUBSTRATE_PHOS_010]]

### 4. Mechanisms of Insulin Resistance
**Four major mechanisms discussed:**
1. **Serine phosphorylation of IRS** — inflammatory kinases
2. **Lipotoxicity** — ceramide and DAG accumulation
3. **ER stress** — unfolded protein response
4. **Mitochondrial dysfunction** — incomplete oxidation

**Zettels extracted:** [[Z_CLAIM_IRS_SERINEPHOS_IR_008]], [[Z_LIPOTOXICITY_CERAMIDE_IR_013]], [[Z_ER_STRESS_IR_014]], [[Z_MITOCHONDRIAL_DYSFUNCTION_IR_015]]
