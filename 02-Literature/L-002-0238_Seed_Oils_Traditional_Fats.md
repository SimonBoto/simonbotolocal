# Seed Oils Oxidation and Traditional Fats for Cooking

## Source
- **Original:** Σπορέλαια και Παραδοσιακά Λιπαρά
- **English:** "Seed Oils and Traditional Fats"

## The Problem with Seed Oils

### Industrial Processing Damage
| Issue | Result |
|-------|--------|
| **Extensive processing** | Lost nutrients and antioxidants |
| **High oxidation** | Damaged lipids |
| **Air exposure** | Worsens oxidation |
| **Cooking/frying** | Intensifies damage further |

### Historical Context
| Aspect | Reality |
|--------|---------|
| **Modern seed oils** | Industrial products |
| **Human diet history** | Not part of traditional diets |
| **Introduction** | Last 100 years only |
| **Not to be confused with** | Virgin olive oil, nut fats, traditionally produced oils |

## Traditional Fats Recommended

### Primary Recommendations
| Fat | Use |
|-----|-----|
| **Extra virgin olive oil** | Cooking, salads |
| **Goat butter** | Cooking, baking |
| **Sheep butter** | Cooking, baking |
| **Cold-pressed sesame oil** | Flavoring |

### Other Safe Traditional Fats
| Fat | Origin |
|-----|--------|
| **Avocado oil** | Traditional use |
| **Ghee** | Indian tradition |
| **Coconut oil** | Tropical traditions |
| **Macadamia oil** | Traditional use |

## Clinical Evidence (20 Years of Metabolomic Analysis)

Removing processed foods and seed oils + using traditional fats associated with:

| Improvement | Mechanism |
|-------------|-----------|
| **Mitochondrial energy production** | Better fuel quality |
| **Immune system function** | Reduced inflammation |
| **Insulin sensitivity** | Metabolic optimization |
| **Chronic inflammatory diseases** | Reduced disease activity |
| **Oxidation** | Reduced |
| **Chronic inflammation** | Reduced |
| **Fatigue** | Reduced |
| **Mood swings** | Stabilized |

### Conditions Improved
- Autoimmune diseases
- Cardiovascular disease
- Diabetes
- Asthma

## Key Insight

> "The removal of processed foods and seed oils from the diet, combined with the use of traditional fats, is associated with substantial health improvement."
