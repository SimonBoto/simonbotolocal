# Glucose vs Fructose: Aldoses vs Ketoses

## Chemical Classification

### Aldoses vs Ketoses

| Feature | **Aldoses** | **Ketoses** |
|---------|-------------|-------------|
| **Carbonyl group** | Aldehyde (-CHO) at C1 | Ketone (C=O) at C2 |
| **Location** | End of chain | Internal carbon |
| **Example** | Glucose | Fructose |
| **Ring form** | Pyranose (6-membered) | Furanose (5-membered) |

### Glucose: The Aldose
- **Structure:** Aldehyde at C1, hydroxyl groups on carbons
- **Ring:** Forms 6-membered pyranose ring
- **Most stable:** Chair conformation

### Fructose: The Ketose
- **Structure:** Ketone at C2 (internal carbon)
- **Ring:** Forms 5-membered furanose ring
- ** sweeter:** ~1.7× sweeter than glucose

## Metabolic Pathways

### Glucose Metabolism
**Universal utilization:**
- **All cells** can metabolize glucose
- **Glycolysis:** Primary pathway
- **Regulated:** PFK-1 is key control point
- **Insulin-dependent:** Glucose uptake via GLUT4 (muscle/adipose)

### Fructose Metabolism
**Liver-centric:**
- **~90%** metabolized by liver
- **Bypasses PFK-1:** No rate-limiting step
- **Rapid phosphorylation:** Fructose → Fructose-1-phosphate
- **Lipogenic:** Rapid conversion to fat

**Fructose pathway:**
```
Fructose → Fructose-1-P → DHAP + Glyceraldehyde
→ Glycolysis/gluconeogenesis/lipogenesis
```

## Metabolic Consequences

### Insulin Response
| Sugar | Insulin Response | GLP-1/GIP |
|-------|------------------|-----------|
| **Glucose** | Strong | Stimulated |
| **Fructose** | Minimal | Minimal |

**Implication:** Fructose doesn't trigger satiety signals effectively.

### Lipogenesis
**Fructose drives fat synthesis:**
- High flux through liver
- Excess converted to triglycerides
- VLDL secretion → elevated blood lipids

### Glycogen Replenishment
- **Glucose:** Replenishes muscle and liver glycogen
- **Fructose:** Preferentially liver glycogen

## Evolutionary Perspective

### Glucose: Ancient Energy Source
- **Glycolysis:** ~3.5 billion years old
- **Universal:** All life uses glucose
- **Stability:** Reliable energy source

### Fructose: Plant Signaling Molecule
- **Found in:** Fruits, honey, some vegetables
- **Role in nature:** Attracts animals for seed dispersal
- **Less ancient:** Specialized pathway

**Key distinction:**
- Glucose in glycolysis (as F6P intermediate) ≠ dietary fructose metabolism
- Evolutionary confusion: Fructose as intermediate vs fructose as sugar

## Health Implications

### High Fructose Consumption
**Metabolic syndrome associations:**
- Insulin resistance
- Non-alcoholic fatty liver disease (NAFLD)
- Hypertriglyceridemia
- Visceral adiposity

**Mechanism:**
- Unregulated hepatic uptake
- Excess lipogenesis
- Uric acid production (from AMP degradation)

### Glucose-Fructose Mixtures
**Sucrose (table sugar):**
- 50% glucose + 50% fructose
- Both monosaccharides released by sucrase

**High fructose corn syrup (HFCS):**
- 55% fructose + 42% glucose
- Similar metabolic fate as sucrose
