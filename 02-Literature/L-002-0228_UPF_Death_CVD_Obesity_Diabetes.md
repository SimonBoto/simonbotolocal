# Ultra-Processed Foods Increase Death, CVD, Obesity, Diabetes

## Source
- **Original:** Η Κατανάλωση Υψηλά Επεξεργασμένων Τροφών Αυξάνει τον Κίνδυνο
- **English:** "Ultra-Processed Food Consumption Increases Risk"
- **Study:** Umbrella review of 45 clinical studies, BMJ 2024

## Key Findings

### Health Risks Associated with UPF
| Outcome | Risk Increase |
|---------|---------------|
| **All-cause mortality** | ↑ Increased |
| **CVD mortality** | ↑ Increased |
| **Mental health disorders** | ↑ Increased |
| **Body weight** | ↑ Increased |
| **Obesity** | ↑ Increased |
| **Type 2 diabetes** | ↑ Increased |

### What Are Ultra-Processed Foods?

Beyond soda and chips, UPF includes:
- Cookies, cornflakes, toast bread
- Ice cream, sweets, mayonnaise
- Seed oils, ready-made sauces
- Restaurant sauces (with seed oils)
- French fries in seed oils
- Margarine, packaged snacks
- Instant pasta, ready meals

### Industrial Processing Characteristics
| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Main ingredients** | Chemically modified food substances |
| **Additives** | For taste, texture, appearance, shelf-life |
| **Whole foods** | Minimal or none |

## Why UPF Is Harmful

| Mechanism | Effect |
|-----------|--------|
| **High calories, low nutrients** | Energy excess, deficiency states |
| **Disrupts satiety regulation** | Overconsumption |
| **Vitamin/mineral deficiencies** | Micronutrient gaps |
| **Insulin resistance** | Metabolic dysfunction |
| **Gut microbiome disruption** | Dysbiosis |
| **Chronic inflammation** | Systemic inflammation |

## The Awareness Gap

> Most people **overestimate their diet quality** and believe they eat healthy while consuming industrial processed foods much more frequently than they realize.

## Minimally Processed Alternatives

| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| **Proteins** | Meat, fish, seafood, eggs |
| **Dairy** | Cheese, yogurt (full-fat) |
| **Fats** | Olive oil, olives |
| **Nuts/seeds** | Natural, unsweetened |
| **Plants** | Fruits, legumes, vegetables |

## Processing Principle

> Any degree of processing **removes nutrients** and **increases harmful content**.

## Evidence

> "Ultra-processed food consumption is associated with increased risk of death from all causes, increased CVD mortality, mental health disorders, body weight, obesity, and type 2 diabetes."
> — Umbrella review of 45 studies, BMJ 2024
