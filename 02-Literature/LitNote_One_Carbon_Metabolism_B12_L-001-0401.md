## Core Concepts Extracted

### 1. One-Carbon Metabolism — The Core Pathway
> "One-carbon metabolism involves the transfer of one-carbon units for DNA synthesis, methylation reactions, and amino acid metabolism... folate and vitamin B12 are essential cofactors."

**Definition:**
- Network of biochemical reactions involving the transfer of single carbon groups (methyl, methylene, formyl)
- Essential for: DNA synthesis, cell division, methylation reactions, amino acid metabolism
- Hub of nucleotide metabolism and epigenetic regulation

**The Folate Cycle:**
```
Dietary Folate → Dihydrofolate (DHF) → Tetrahydrofolate (THF)
    ↓
5,10-Methylene-THF → 5-Methyl-THF (5-MTHF) ← ACTIVE FORM
    ↓
Donates methyl group to B12 → Methionine synthesis
```

**Key Intermediates:**
| Form | Carbon Unit | Function |
|------|-------------|----------|
| **5-MTHF** | Methyl (-CH₃) | Homocysteine remethylation |
| **5,10-Methylene-THF** | Methylene (-CH₂-) | Thymidylate synthesis |
| **10-Formyl-THF** | Formyl (-CHO) | Purine synthesis |

### 2. Vitamin B12 — The Cobalamin Cofactor
> "Vitamin B12 serves as a cofactor for two critical enzymes: methionine synthase and methylmalonyl-CoA mutase."

**Biochemical Forms:**
- **Methylcobalamin:** Cofactor for methionine synthase (cytosol)
- **Adenosylcobalamin:** Cofactor for methylmalonyl-CoA mutase (mitochondria)
- **Cyanocobalamin:** Synthetic form (requires conversion)
- **Hydroxocobalamin:** Storage form, long-acting

**Enzyme 1: Methionine Synthase**
- **Reaction:** Homocysteine + 5-MTHF → Methionine + THF
- **Cofactor:** Methylcobalamin
- **Location:** Cytosol
- **Function:** Regenerates methionine, maintains methylation capacity

**Enzyme 2: Methylmalonyl-CoA Mutase**
- **Reaction:** L-methylmalonyl-CoA → Succinyl-CoA
- **Cofactor:** Adenosylcobalamin
- **Location:** Mitochondria
- **Function:** Odd-chain fatty acid metabolism

### 3. B12 Absorption and Transport
> "Vitamin B12 absorption requires intrinsic factor and occurs in the terminal ileum."

**Absorption Steps:**
1. **Gastric:** B12 released from food, binds haptocorrin
2. **Duodenal:** Pancreatic enzymes release B12, binds intrinsic factor
3. **Ileal:** IF-B12 complex absorbed via cubilin receptor

**Transport:**
- **TC II:** Active transport to cells
- **Storage:** Liver (2-5 mg, 2-5 year reserve)
- **Daily requirement:** 2.4 mcg

**Zettels extracted:** [[B12_Absorption_Transport_Z-001-0403]]

### 4. The Methyl Trap
> "B12 deficiency leads to functional folate deficiency as 5-MTHF accumulates and cannot be converted back to THF."

**Mechanism:**
- B12 deficient → 5-MTHF cannot donate methyl group
- 5-MTHF accumulates (trapped)
- THF not regenerated
- DNA synthesis impaired → Megaloblastic anemia

**Clinical Pearl:**
- Folate can mask B12 deficiency anemia
- But neurological damage continues
- Always check B12 before folate supplementation

**Zettels extracted:** [[Methyl_Trap_B12_Folate_Z-001-0404]]

### 5. Homocysteine Metabolism
> "Homocysteine sits at a metabolic crossroads: remethylation or transsulfuration."

**Remethylation:** B12/folate-dependent
- Homocysteine + 5-MTHF → Methionine

**Transsulfuration:** B6-dependent
- Homocysteine → Cystathionine → Cysteine

**Clinical:** Elevated homocysteine = CVD risk factor

**Zettels extracted:** [[Homocysteine_Metabolism_Z-001-0405]]

### 6. B12 Deficiency Manifestations
> "Severe B12 deficiency causes megaloblastic anemia, neurological damage, and may mimic hematological malignancies."

**Hematological:** Megaloblastic anemia, pancytopenia
**Neurological:** Subacute combined degeneration (irreversible if untreated)
**Mimics:** Bone marrow changes may resemble MM (but no clonal plasma cells)

**Diagnosis:** Low serum B12, elevated MMA (specific), elevated homocysteine
