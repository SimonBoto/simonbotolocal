## Trioses: The Simplest Sugars

### Glyceraldehyde and Dihydroxyacetone
**Trioses:** 3-carbon monosaccharides — the simplest sugars in metabolism.

| Triose | Type | Formula | Key Feature |
|--------|------|---------|-------------|
| **Glyceraldehyde** | Aldotriose | C₃H₆O₃ | Aldehyde group |
| **Dihydroxyacetone** | Ketotriose | C₃H₆O₃ | Ketone group |

**Isomeric pair:** Same formula, different structure.

## Glycolysis: The 10-Step Pathway

### Overview
```
Glucose (6C) → 2 Pyruvate (3C) + 2 ATP + 2 NADH
```

**Universal:** Found in archaea, bacteria, eukaryotes (~3.5 billion years old).

### Investment Phase (Steps 1-5)

| Step | Reaction | Enzyme | Energy Cost |
|------|----------|--------|-------------|
| 1 | Glucose → Glucose-6-phosphate | **Hexokinase** | -1 ATP |
| 2 | G6P → Fructose-6-phosphate | Phosphoglucose isomerase | - |
| 3 | F6P → Fructose-1,6-bisphosphate | **Phosphofructokinase-1 (PFK-1)** | -1 ATP |
| 4 | F1,6BP → DHAP + G3P | **Aldolase** | - |
| 5 | DHAP ↔ G3P | Triose phosphate isomerase | - |

**Net investment:** 2 ATP

### Payoff Phase (Steps 6-10)
- G3P oxidation → yields 2 ATP per G3P
- **Total yield:** 4 ATP - 2 ATP = **2 ATP per glucose**

## The Fructose Intermediary

### Why Fructose in Glycolysis?
```
Glucose-6-P → Fructose-6-P (isomerization)
```

**Evolutionary insight:**
- Fructose as metabolic intermediate ≠ fructose as dietary sugar
- Isomerization enables aldol cleavage (splitting 6C → 2×3C)
- **Glycolysis evolved to process glucose**, not fructose as free molecule

### Fructose-1,6-bisphosphate
- **Key intermediate:** Split by aldolase into two trioses
- **Regulatory significance:** PFK-1 commits glucose to glycolysis

---

## Evolutionary Significance

### Why Trioses?
**Efficiency advantage:**
- Splitting 6C glucose → 2×3C trioses
- Each triose yields ATP independently
- More energy extracted per glucose molecule

### Ancient Pathway
- **~3.5 billion years old**
- Precedes oxygenic photosynthesis
- Anaerobic (works without O₂)
- Universal across all life domains

**Evidence for antiquity:**
- Enzymes conserved in archaea/bacteria/eukaryotes
- No oxygen requirement
- Central to energy metabolism

## Pyruvate: The Metabolic Crossroads

### From Triose to Pyruvate
```
G3P → ... → Pyruvate (3C α-keto acid)
```

### Metabolic Fates of Pyruvate
| Condition | Pathway | Product |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| **Aerobic** | Mitochondrial oxidation | Acetyl-CoA → Krebs cycle |
| **Anaerobic** | Lactate fermentation | Lactate |
| **Yeast** | Alcoholic fermentation | Ethanol + CO₂ |

### Central Role
Pyruvate connects:
- Glycolysis (carbohydrate metabolism)
- Krebs cycle (aerobic energy)
- Gluconeogenesis (glucose synthesis)
- Amino acid metabolism (transamination)

## Clinical Relevance

### Glycolytic Enzyme Deficiencies
**Pyruvate kinase deficiency:**
- Most common glycolytic enzyme defect
- Hemolytic anemia (RBCs dependent on glycolysis)
- Chronic hemolysis, jaundice

**Phosphofructokinase deficiency (Tarui disease):**
- Glycogen storage disease type VII
- Exercise intolerance, hemolysis

### Metabolic Flexibility
Cells can switch between:
- Aerobic glycolysis (Warburg effect in cancer)
- Anaerobic glycolysis (rapid energy, hypoxia)
