# High-Fat Dairy Reduces Dementia Risk — 25-Year Cohort Study

## Source
- **Original:** Τυριά Υψηλών Λιπαρών και Μακροπρόθεσμος Κίνδυνος Άνοιας
- **English:** "High-Fat Dairy and Long-Term Risk of Dementia"
- **Study:** Neurology, December 2025
- **Duration:** 25-year prospective cohort

## Key Findings

### High-Fat Cheese
| Outcome | Association |
|---------|-------------|
| **Overall dementia risk** | ↓ Lower |
| **Alzheimer's disease** | ↓ Lower |
| **Vascular dementia** | ↓ Lower |

### Cream and Full-Fat Dairy
| Outcome | Association |
|---------|-------------|
| **Overall dementia** | ↓ Lower risk |
| **Alzheimer's** | ↓ Lower risk |
| **Vascular dementia** | ↓ Lower risk |

### Low-Fat Dairy
| Product | Association |
|---------|-------------|
| **Low-fat cheese** | No clear relationship |
| **Low-fat cream** | No clear relationship |
| **Milk** | No clear relationship |
| **Yogurt** | No clear relationship |
| **Butter** | No clear relationship |

| **Parmesan** | Aged, concentrated nutrients |

## Key Insight

> "Increased intake of animal fats not only does not burden health, but is associated with positive outcomes."

## Evidence

> "Individuals who consumed more high-fat cheeses had lower risk of overall dementia, Alzheimer's disease, and vascular dementia compared to those who ate less."
> — Neurology 2025, 25-year cohort
