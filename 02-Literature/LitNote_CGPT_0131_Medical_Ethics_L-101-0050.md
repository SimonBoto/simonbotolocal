## Emanuel's Seven Requirements for Ethical Research (2000)

### The Framework
Informed consent alone is **necessary but not sufficient**. All seven requirements must be satisfied:

| # | Requirement | Core Principle |
|---|-------------|----------------|
| 1 | **Social/Clinical Value** | Research answers important question; benefits justify risks |
| 2 | **Scientific Validity** | Methodologically sound; incompetent research is unethical |
| 3 | **Fair Subject Selection** | Scientific objectives drive selection; avoid exploiting vulnerable |
| 4 | **Favorable Risk-Benefit Ratio** | Benefits outweigh risks; minimize harm |
| 5 | **Independent Review** | IRB/ethics committee evaluates impartially |
| 6 | **Informed Consent** | Voluntary participation with full disclosure |
| 7 | **Respect for Enrolled Subjects** | Ongoing protection of privacy, confidentiality, well-being |

### Detailed Principles

#### 1. Social and Clinical Value
- Must answer **specific, well-defined question**
- Addresses **important clinical or social issue**
- **Both positive and negative results** must be published
- Prevents wasteful, exploitative, or redundant studies

**Examples upheld:**
- Salk polio vaccine trial (1954) — 1.8M children, eradicated polio
- WHI hormone therapy study — published negative results, saved millions
- COVID-19 vaccine trials — diverse populations, transparent data

**Violations:**
- Tuskegee syphilis study — no treatment offered, exploitation continued

#### 2. Scientific Validity
- Methodologically rigorous design
- Conducted by competent professionals
- Poor-quality research = unethical (risk without reliable outcome)

#### 3. Fair Subject Selection
- Selection criteria **scientifically justified**
- Equitable distribution of risks and benefits
- Protect vulnerable populations from exploitation

#### 4. Favorable Risk-Benefit Ratio
- Systematic assessment of risks vs. benefits
- Minimize risks; maximize benefits
- Continuous monitoring throughout study

#### 5. Independent Review
- Neutral body (IRB/ethics committee) evaluates
- Ensures unbiased judgment
- Accountability and transparency

#### 6. Informed Consent
- **Voluntary** agreement after complete disclosure
- Understandable information about:
  - Purpose, methods, risks, benefits
  - Alternatives, right to withdraw
- **Competence** to consent required

#### 7. Respect for Enrolled Subjects
- Protection continues **after enrollment**
- Privacy and confidentiality safeguards
- Data protection throughout and after study
- Right to withdraw without penalty

## Historical Ethical Frameworks

### Foundational Documents
| Document | Year | Source | Key Contribution |
|----------|------|--------|------------------|
| **Nuremberg Code** | 1947 | Nuremberg Tribunal | Voluntary consent, avoid unnecessary harm, right to withdraw |
| **Declaration of Helsinki** | 1964 | World Medical Association | International standard; therapeutic vs. non-therapeutic distinction |
| **Belmont Report** | 1979 | US National Commission | Three principles: respect for persons, beneficence, justice |
| **CIOMS Guidelines** | 1982 | CIOMS/WHO | Global standards; vulnerable populations focus |

### Regulatory Frameworks
| Framework | Year | Scope |
|-----------|------|-------|
| 45 CFR 46 (Common Rule) | 1981; 1991 | US federal research funding |
| WHO GCP Guidelines | 1995 | Global pharmaceutical standards |
| ICH E6 GCP | 1996 | Harmonized EU/US/Japan standards |
| European Convention on Human Rights and Biomedicine | 1997 | European bioethical treaty |

## Notable Ethics Cases

### Violations (Lessons in What Not to Do)
| Case | Years | Violation | Outcome |
|------|-------|-----------|---------|
| **Tuskegee Syphilis Study** | 1932-1972 | No treatment offered; deception; racial exploitation | Public outcry; Belmont Report; strengthened regulations |
| **Willowbrook Hepatitis Study** | 1956-1980 | Intentional infection of disabled children | Consent controversies; vulnerable population protections |
| **Nazi Medical Experiments** | 1940s | Unconsented, horrific experimentation | Nuremberg Code; crimes against humanity trials |
| **Henrietta Lacks (HeLa)** | 1951 | Cells taken without consent; commercialization without family benefit | Informed consent evolution; genetic privacy discussions |
