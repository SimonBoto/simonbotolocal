# Coffee Reduces Dementia Risk — 37-Year Study

## Source
- **Original:** Ο καφές μειώνει τον κίνδυνο άνοιας
- **English:** "Coffee Reduces Dementia Risk"
- **Study:** 132,000 participants, 37-year follow-up

## Key Findings

### Study Design
| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Participants** | 132,000 |
| **Follow-up** | 37 years |
| **Dementia cases** | 11,000 |

### Coffee Effect
| Finding | Result |
|---------|--------|
| **Higher caffeinated coffee** | Significantly lower dementia risk |
| **Optimal dose (coffee)** | 2-3 cups/day |
| **Optimal dose (tea)** | 1-2 cups/day |

### Caffeine Specificity
| Beverage | Effect |
|----------|--------|
| **Caffeinated coffee** | ✅ Protective |
| **Caffeinated tea** | ✅ Protective |
| **Decaffeinated** | ❌ No benefit observed |

## Mechanisms (Hypothesized)

| Pathway | Effect |
|---------|--------|
| **Adenosine receptor antagonism** | Neuroprotection |
| **Antioxidant effects** | Reduced oxidative stress |
| **Improved insulin sensitivity** | Metabolic brain health |
| **Reduced inflammation** | Neuroinflammation suppression |

## Dose-Response

| Intake | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| **None** | Baseline risk |
| **1 cup/day** | Some benefit |
| **2-3 cups/day** | Optimal protection |
| **>4 cups/day** | Benefit may plateau |

## Clinical Implication

| Recommendation | Evidence |
|----------------|----------|
| **Moderate coffee consumption** | Supported for dementia prevention |
| **Caffeine appears key** | Decaf doesn't show same benefit |
| **Both coffee and tea** | Effective sources |

## Evidence

> "The highest consumption of caffeinated coffee was associated with significantly lower dementia risk. The benefit was observed only with caffeinated coffee or tea and was most pronounced at around 2-3 cups per day for coffee and 1-2 for tea."
> — 37-year cohort study
