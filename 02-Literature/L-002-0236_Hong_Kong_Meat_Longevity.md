# Hong Kong: Highest Meat Consumption, Longest Life Expectancy

## Source
- **Original:** Ο πληθυσμός με το υψηλότερο προσδόκιμο ζωής παγκοσμίως είναι το Χονγκ Κονγκ
- **English:** "Hong Kong Has World's Longest Life Expectancy"

## The Paradox

| Metric | Hong Kong |
|--------|-----------|
| **Life expectancy** | **Highest globally** |
| **Meat consumption** | **Highest globally** (per capita) |
| **Annual meat per person** | ~145 kg |

## Impact Assessment

| Observation | Conclusion |
|-------------|------------|
| **High meat intake** | **No negative impact** on health or longevity |
| **Coexistence** | High meat + longest life, not mutually exclusive |

## Contributing Factors (Beyond Meat)

| Factor | Contribution |
|--------|--------------|
| **High fish/seafood consumption** | Omega-3s, micronutrients |
| **Low smoking rates** | Reduced CVD/cancer risk |
| **Efficient healthcare system** | Early intervention |
| **Lifestyle factors** | Activity, social connections |

## The Key Insight

> "The increased meat intake does not appear to reduce this population's lifespan. On the contrary, it coexists with the highest life expectancy globally."

## Challenging Assumptions

| Old Belief | Evidence |
|------------|----------|
| "Meat reduces lifespan" | Not supported in Hong Kong data |
| "Plant-based only for longevity" | Contradicted by population data |
| "Meat is harmful" | Context matters |

## Context Matters

| Aspect | Hong Kong Pattern |
|--------|-------------------|
| **Meat quality** | Likely high |
| **Preparation** | Traditional methods |
| **Overall diet** | Balanced with seafood, vegetables |
| **Metabolic health** | Better insulin sensitivity |

## Evidence

> "Hong Kong has world's longest life expectancy. Hong Kong also has highest meat consumption per capita globally. Each resident consumes approximately 145 kg of meat annually — and this does not appear to have negative impact on health or life expectancy."
> — Health & Protection 2025, Our World in Data
