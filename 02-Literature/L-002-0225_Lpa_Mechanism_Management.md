# Lipoprotein(a) — Mechanism, Risk Levels, and Management

## Source
- **Original:** Λιποπρωτεΐνη Άλφα ή Lp(a), τι Είναι και τι Μπορούμε να Κάνουμε
- **English:** "Lipoprotein(a) or Lp(a): What It Is and What We Can Do"

## What is Lp(a)?

**Lp(a)** is a cardiovascular risk factor that increases thrombosis (clot formation) and consequently heart attack and stroke risk.

### Structure
| Component | Function |
|-----------|----------|
| **LDL-like particle** | Cholesterol delivery |
| **Apolipoprotein(a)** | Extra piece promoting clot formation |
| **Result** | Thrombosis → heart attacks, strokes |

## Evolutionary Origin

| Era | Function |
|-----|----------|
| **Prehistoric** | Faster bleeding cessation after injury |
| **Survival advantage** | Reduced trauma mortality |
| **Modern** | Liability with longer lifespans, less trauma |

> The same mechanism that increased survival now acts as a risk factor.

## Risk Level Interpretation

| Level | Risk | Interpretation |
|-------|------|----------------|
| **<30 mg/dL** | Not increased | Normal |
| **30–50 mg/dL** | Moderate increase | Borderline high |
| **>50 mg/dL** | Significant increase | 50% or greater risk elevation |

### Dose-Response
| Increase | Risk Change |
|----------|-------------|
| +20 mg/dL | ~10% higher risk |
| +40 mg/dL | ~20% higher risk |

## Multiplicative Effect

Lp(a) acts **multiplicatively** on existing risk:

| Base Risk | Lp(a) Effect |
|-----------|--------------|
| **Low overall CVD risk** | Small absolute increase |
| **High base risk** | Significant contribution |

## Current Treatment Landscape

| Aspect | Status |
|--------|--------|
| **Available drugs** | None currently |
| **Clinical trials** | Active (reduce Lp(a) up to 95%) |
| **Outcome data** | Insufficient — more data expected |
| **Timeline** | Results in coming years |

## Base Risk Factors to Control

While awaiting Lp(a)-specific therapies, aggressively manage:
- Hypertension
- Diabetes
- Body weight
- Insulin resistance
- Smoking
- sdLDL
- Triglycerides
- HDL
- Processed food consumption
- Sedentary lifestyle
