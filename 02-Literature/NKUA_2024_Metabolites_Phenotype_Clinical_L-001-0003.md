## 📝 Section Summaries

### Introduction
Autoimmune and chronic diseases have multifactorial causes, complicating early diagnosis and effective treatment. Metabolic disorders are largely responsible for their manifestation. Metabolomics serves as the decryption tool for these disorders through phenotype mapping.

**Key statistic:** Non-communicable diseases are primarily due to modifiable factors and metabolic disorders. Genetic background accounts for only a small percentage — **non-genetic modifiable factors constitute 80% of causative factors** (WHO data).

Omics sciences evaluate the impact of modifiable factors on disease manifestation, evolution, and treatment.

### Subsection 1: Factors Affecting Phenotype and Disease Manifestation

#### The 3 Axes of Metabolomics Application (Figure 1)
Metabolomics maps phenotypes including:
- Disease presence (patient vs healthy)
- Disease risk (high risk vs low risk)
- Treatment response (responders vs non-responders)
- Specific states: dysbiosis, insulin resistance

#### Six Major Factor Categories

**1. Micronutrient Deficiencies (1.1)**
Micronutrients = vitamins, minerals, trace elements (vs macronutrients: proteins, carbs, lipids)

Four categories:
- Water-soluble vitamins (B1, B2, B3, B5, B6, B9, B12, C)
- Fat-soluble vitamins (A, D, E, K)
- Macrominerals
- Trace elements
