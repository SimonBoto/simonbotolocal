# Lit: Historical Ethics Guidelines — From Nuremberg to ICH-GCP

## Source
- **Full citation:** Multiple international guidelines (Nuremberg Code 1947, Declaration of Helsinki 1964+, Belmont Report 1979, CIOMS 1982+, ICH-GCP 1996)
- **Source:** ChatGPT Archive CGPT-0131 (Medical Ethics Assistance)
- **Context:** Evolution of research ethics frameworks post-WWII to present

## Core Concepts Extracted

### 1. Nuremberg Code (1947)
> "The voluntary consent of the human subject is absolutely essential."

Established in response to Nazi medical atrocities during the Doctors' Trial. Introduced foundational principles: voluntary informed consent, avoidance of unnecessary harm, scientific validity, and right to withdraw.

**Zettel extracted:** [[Nuremberg_Code_Research_Ethics_1947_[Z-002-0608].md]]

### 2. Declaration of Helsinki (1964, revised 1975, 1983, 1989, 1996, 2000, 2008, 2013)
> "The health of my patient will be my first consideration."

World Medical Association's international standard distinguishing therapeutic from non-therapeutic research. Emphasizes informed consent, independent ethical review, and patient welfare prioritization.

**Zettel extracted:** [[Declaration_Helsinki_Research_Ethics_[Z-002-0609].md]]

### 3. Belmont Report (1979)
> "Respect for persons, beneficence, and justice."

U.S. National Commission's foundational document establishing three core principles. Led to IRB establishment and 45 CFR 46 (Common Rule) regulations for federally funded research.

**Zettel extracted:** [[Belmont_Report_Ethical_Principles_1979_[Z-002-0610].md]]

### 4. CIOMS International Ethical Guidelines (1982, revised 1993, 2002, 2016)
> "Ethical guidelines for biomedical research involving human subjects in resource-limited settings."

Council for International Organizations of Medical Sciences and WHO collaboration. Addresses vulnerable populations, international research ethics, and capacity building in developing countries.

**Zettel extracted:** [[CIOMS_International_Research_Ethics_[Z-002-0611].md]]

### 5. ICH Good Clinical Practice (1996)
> "A standard for the design, conduct, performance, monitoring, auditing, recording, analyses, and reporting of clinical trials."

International Conference on Harmonization unified European, American, and Japanese standards. Now the global standard for pharmaceutical trials with enforceable regulatory requirements.

**Zettel extracted:** [[ICH_Good_Clinical_Practice_GCP_[Z-002-0612].md]]

## Evolution Themes

1. **From physician-centered to participant-centered:** Early codes emphasized physician judgment; modern frameworks prioritize participant protection
2. **From national to international:** Growing recognition of global research ethics
3. **From principles to practice:** Increasing specificity in implementation requirements
4. **From therapeutic to research distinction:** Clear separation of clinical care from research participation

## Application to METHAP

METHAP must comply with:
- **Declaration of Helsinki** — International standard for medical research
- **ICH-GCP principles** — Where applicable to non-pharmaceutical trials
- **CIOMS guidelines** — If involving international collaboration
- **Belmont principles** — Respect for persons, beneficence, justice
- **National regulations** — Greek ethics committee requirements
